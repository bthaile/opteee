# Put Spread

A put spread is a multi-leg options strategy that involves buying and selling put options at different strike prices to define risk and reduce the cost of entry compared to buying a single put outright. Put spreads are used to express bearish or neutral directional views while limiting both maximum loss and maximum profit, making them suitable for traders seeking to isolate directional exposure from volatility or to establish relative-value trades across different assets. The strategy requires active management and clear risk parameters, particularly in volatile or fast-moving markets where gamma and theta dynamics can create unexpected outcomes.
