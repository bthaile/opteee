# Market Making

Market making is the practice of providing liquidity by simultaneously quoting buy and sell prices, a role that institutional market makers perform with regulatory advantages unavailable to retail traders. While retail traders cannot legally replicate full market-making strategies due to exemptions from short-locate and position-limit rules, they can adopt market-making principles such as disciplined execution, thinking in terms of liquidity provision, and monitoring strike-volatility relationships to improve their trading within their actual constraints. Success in any trading approach—whether market-making or retail—depends fundamentally on identifying genuine edge, systematic decision-making, and proper position sizing rather than complex strategies or emotional discipline alone.
