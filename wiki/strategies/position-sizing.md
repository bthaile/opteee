# Position Sizing

Position sizing is the allocation of capital to individual trades and is the only element of risk a trader can control before execution occurs. Proper position sizing prevents ruin even when a trading strategy has positive expectancy, while oversizing leads to account destruction regardless of edge quality. The Kelly criterion provides an objective mathematical framework for determining position size based on win rate and payoff ratios, though most traders use fractional Kelly (half or quarter Kelly) rather than full Kelly to reduce volatility and account drawdown. Disciplined position sizing enables profitable strategies to compound reliably over time and is foundational to trading as a sustainable business rather than gambling.
