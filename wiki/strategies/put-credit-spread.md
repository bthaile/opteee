# Put Credit Spread

A put credit spread is a multi-leg options strategy in which a trader sells a put at one strike and buys a put at a lower strike, collecting net premium upfront while capping maximum loss. The strategy is used to capture theta decay and volatility risk premium in sideways or moderately bullish market conditions, with the short put generating income and the long put serving as protection. Position adjustments to put credit spreads are driven by changes in P&L targets, time decay dynamics, Greek profile drift, or portfolio fit, and may involve rolling strikes, closing legs, or adding offsetting positions rather than simply defending or repairing the trade.
