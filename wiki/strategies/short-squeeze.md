# Short Squeeze

A short squeeze occurs when a heavily shorted stock experiences rapid price appreciation, forcing short sellers to buy back shares at higher prices and thereby amplifying upward price movement. The 2021 GameStop squeeze exemplified this dynamic, though discussions distinguish between pure short squeezes and gamma squeezes (driven by options mechanics), and examine evidence that short exposure may persist through derivatives structures like total return swaps rather than traditional short positions. Structural factors including naked short selling, settlement mechanics, and market maker conflicts of interest are cited as enabling conditions for squeezes, while technical patterns such as options expiration cycles and ETF reconstitution create periodic volatility spikes that may suppress or release price pressure.
