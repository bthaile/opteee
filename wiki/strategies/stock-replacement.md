# Stock Replacement

Stock Replacement is a strategy that uses deep-in-the-money options—particularly put or call options—as an alternative to holding shares outright, allowing traders to acquire or maintain exposure to a stock while preserving capital and portfolio flexibility. The strategy is commonly executed by closing a stock position at a loss and immediately rolling into short deep-in-the-money puts or long deep-in-the-money calls at a chosen strike, effectively replacing the share position with leveraged option exposure. This approach is useful for basis reduction, managing adverse price moves, and maintaining the ability to make further adjustments without tying up as much capital as a full share position would require.
