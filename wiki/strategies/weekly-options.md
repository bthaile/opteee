# Weekly Options

Weekly options are shorter-dated derivatives contracts that expire within a week, offering traders access to volatility and theta decay dynamics on compressed timescales compared to standard monthly expirations. According to professional traders in the corpus, weeklies provide a meaningful edge in volatility terms while maintaining significantly lower transaction costs than zero-days-to-expiration (0DTE) options, making them a more practical vehicle for capturing short-term volatility and time decay. The strategy is particularly relevant for analyzing concentrated positioning in front-month expirations, where retail and institutional traders often establish directional bets ahead of catalysts or earnings events.
