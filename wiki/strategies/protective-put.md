# Protective Put

A protective put is a hedging strategy in which an investor buys a put option on an underlying position to limit downside risk while retaining upside potential. Like all hedges, it functions as insurance with an explicit cost that must be weighed against the protection it provides, and its effectiveness depends on strike selection and the investor's risk tolerance. The strategy isolates downside risk rather than eliminating it entirely, and practitioners must consider both the direct cost of the put premium and the opportunity cost of capital tied up in the hedge when deciding whether and how to implement it.
