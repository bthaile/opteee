# Ratio Put Diagonal

A ratio put diagonal is a multi-leg options strategy that combines puts at different strike prices and expiration dates to manage directional risk and extract premium in a declining market environment. The strategy involves selling near-term puts at a lower strike while buying longer-dated puts at a higher strike, creating a defined-risk structure that benefits from time decay and volatility changes across the two expirations. Traders use ratio put diagonals when they have conviction in sector weakness or downside moves but want to control risk through the diagonal spread structure and manage Greeks across multiple expiration cycles. The strategy requires active monitoring and adjustment as expirations approach, particularly when market conditions shift or the underlying position moves significantly.
