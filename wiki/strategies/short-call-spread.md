# Short Call Spread

A short call spread is a vertical spread strategy where a trader sells a call option at one strike price and buys a call option at a higher strike price, creating a defined-risk, defined-reward directional bet that profits if the underlying asset stays below the sold call strike. Rather than a volatility play, it is fundamentally a bearish directional position that benefits from time decay and declining price movement, though it carries negative expected value due to transaction costs and the inherent structure of vertical spreads. Success with short call spreads requires a strong directional thesis validated through backtesting and market structure analysis rather than relying solely on probability-based entry signals, and traders must carefully manage position sizing and trading friction to achieve long-term profitability.
