# Directional Trading

Directional trading in options is a profit mechanism based on predicting and capitalizing on price movement of an underlying asset, where success depends critically on aligning strike selection, time-to-expiration, and implied volatility with the directional thesis rather than simply choosing strikes near price targets. The same directional move can produce vastly different outcomes across different option structures—from substantial gains to total loss—because delta, gamma, theta, and vega interact differently depending on how quickly the move occurs, how volatility changes, and how much time remains until expiration. Effective directional options trading requires identifying the profit mechanism first, then selecting or constructing specific option structures (long calls, synthetic shorts, put spreads, etc.) that align speed, leverage, and time decay with the expected market behavior and portfolio objectives.
