# Risk Reversal

A risk reversal is an options strategy that pairs a long call with a short put (or vice versa) to express a directional market view while managing costs through the premium collected from the short leg offsetting the long leg's cost. In the context of the Hull Tactical ETF discussed in the corpus, risk reversals are employed as a skew-harvesting mechanism overlaid on an equity portfolio to capture volatility edge. The strategy functions as a way to gain directional exposure or hedge while reducing net capital outlay, though like all options strategies it requires careful consideration of the profit mechanism and portfolio alignment rather than mechanical application.
