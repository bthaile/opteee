# Delta Neutral

Delta neutral is a hedging approach where a trader offsets the directional exposure of an options position by adjusting holdings in the underlying asset, creating a position with near-zero sensitivity to small price movements. This technique is central to gamma scalping, where traders maintain a long-gamma base position (such as a long straddle) and repeatedly rehedge deltas at predetermined price thresholds to harvest realized volatility and convert gamma exposure into profits that offset theta decay. By neutralizing delta, traders isolate their exposure to gamma and volatility rather than directional price movement, allowing them to profit from the convex, non-linear nature of options P&L regardless of which direction the underlying moves.
