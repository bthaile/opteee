# Long Volatility

Long volatility is a strategy that profits when implied volatility rises or when realized volatility exceeds the implied volatility priced into options, making it a core component of volatility-focused trading approaches. Retail traders employ long volatility positions by identifying mispricings between implied and realized volatility through coherent measurement frameworks and observation-driven edge discovery rather than competing on scale with institutional traders. The strategy generates returns by capitalizing on favorable volatility regimes and understanding market mechanics, and it has become increasingly important as traditional market-on-close effects have declined, requiring traders to develop more sophisticated approaches to volatility trading.
