# Risk Units - An Easy Tool to Size your Portfolio

## Summary

Risk units provide a simple, volatility-normalized framework for sizing positions across different securities. By dividing the volatility of a target asset by the volatility of a base asset (typically SPY), traders can determine the relative position size needed to equalize risk exposure across their portfolio.

## Key takeaways

- **Risk unit formula** [00:00]: Divide the volatility of the asset you want to position in by the volatility of your base asset (e.g., SPY). A ratio of 1.67 means your base position should be 1.67× larger than the alternative to carry equivalent risk.
- **Practical application** [00:18]: If Tesla has 50 vol and SPY has 30 vol, an SPY position needs to be 1.67× the size of a Tesla position to achieve the same risk profile.
- **Portfolio-level tool**: Risk units normalize across different volatility regimes, enabling consistent risk allocation regardless of which securities you hold.
