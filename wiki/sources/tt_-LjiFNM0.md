# Managing Long IBIT Shares During a 50% Drop #optionstrading

## Summary

Eric discusses tactical adjustments to a long [[ibit]] position that has declined approximately 50% from entry. Rather than exit, he demonstrates how to use short puts to scale into the position, gradually lowering cost basis while managing capital constraints and opportunity cost in smaller accounts.

## Key takeaways

- **Position conviction vs. capital constraints** [00:00] — Even when bullish on an asset long-term, smaller accounts face real opportunity cost; capital tied up in underwater positions cannot be deployed elsewhere.
- **Scaling-in via short puts** [00:21] — Original position was 20 contracts at ~52 strike; added 4 contracts to bring total to 24, lowering average basis from 52 to 50.
- **Grinding basis lower incrementally** [00:46] — Each adjustment adds modest size rather than averaging down aggressively, preserving dry powder and managing risk.
