# Creating Edge in Trading | Outlier Options Trading Basics Ep5

## Summary

This episode concludes the Outlier Options Trading Basics series by teaching practitioners how to build sustainable edge through systematic research and testing. Rather than chasing exotic strategies, the host demonstrates a repeatable process: identify profit mechanisms via academic research (SSRN), validate them against historical data, and test multiple option structures to understand performance across market regimes. The session walks through a live momentum-based sector rotation strategy, showing how to construct a trading log, track Greeks, and compare results against buy-and-hold benchmarks.

## Key takeaways

- **Edge = positive expected value over time** [10:21]. It's not alpha (market outperformance), but the ability to generate consistent returns through a repeatable process.
- **Start with observation, not exotic strategies** [13:34]. The foundation is looking at what markets actually do (e.g., long-term uptrends), not inventing novel ideas without evidence.
- **Market beta (momentum) is a legitimate edge** [16:11]. Buying winners and selling losers via index rebalancing is a proven edge accessible to all traders; don't dismiss it.
- **Use SSRN and academic research to find ideas** [19:00]. Search for published trading strategies, read papers critically (noting author bias), and use AI to summarize findings if needed.
- **Build a process with feedback loops** [32:54]. Observe → Study profit mechanism → Outline structures (different option configurations) → Test across multiple expirations and deltas → Analyze results.
- **Test manually or via backtest, but transact at bid/ask, not mid** [43:54]. Realistic fills matter; if your edge disappears at realistic spreads, you have a bigger problem.
- **Track performance in a trading log with Greeks and P&L** [47:09]. Record entry date, spot price, strike, premium, delta, and exit prices to see how different DTEs and deltas perform under the same move.
- **Compare against buy-and-hold and benchmark returns** [01:19:20]. Normalize portfolio performance by account size and compare to the underlying's return to see if your strategy adds value.
- **Avoid economic rabbit holes** [31:10]. Macro indicators (GDP, M1/M2) are disconnected from trading edge; focus on price action and proven factors instead.
- **Cherry-pick periods to understand regime dependence** [01:29:06]. Test the same strategy in both drawdown and bullish periods to see how it behaves under different market conditions.
- **This is the unglamorous work that separates traders from speculators** [01:26:26]. Finding edge requires sitting down, reading research, and testing systematically—not watching videos about vertical spreads.

## Notable quotes

> "Edge boils down to this idea of positive expectancy. What is positive expectancy? This is the artifact that you make money over time if you do the things or thing or combination thereof over time." [10:21]

> "You have to start with observations. You have to start with stuff that you can see and contextualize. Just like in jiu-jitsu when you first start, you spend a lot of time working on very basic, seemingly boring positions, but those provide the foundation for you to be able to do cooler stuff." [15:00]

> "If your edge is like, 'Oh, I saw a video about vertical spreads. I want to try them.' That's just not going to work. You have to understand with a better degree of certainty what you're attempting to extract and why it may or may not be there." [01:25:17]
