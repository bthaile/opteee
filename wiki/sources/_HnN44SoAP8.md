# Market update – Visa antitrust lawsuit & Unity unusual options activity

## Summary

The Justice Department filed an antitrust lawsuit against Visa alleging it unlawfully stifles competition in debit card processing, controlling over 60% of the market and collecting ~$7B in annual fees. Separately, Unity saw massive call volume (262k calls vs 37k puts) with several 5,000-lot blocks at multiple strikes and expirations, sparking speculation but requiring careful fingerprinting against historical patterns.

## Key takeaways

### Visa antitrust event
- **[00:00–03:51]** DOJ alleges Visa uses market power to extract fees exceeding competitive levels; merchants pass costs to consumers; lawsuit follows failed 2020 attempt to block Plaid acquisition.
- **[03:51–04:53]** Stock down ~5.5% on news; implied volatility percentile jumped to 81%; event offers tradable opportunity but requires monitoring for buy-the-rumor/sell-the-news dynamics.
- **[04:53–05:57]** Put volume expanded (22% of activity); mostly 20 DTE; call skew historically bullish for Visa but beginning to shift; notable 1,700–1,800 contract adds at 285 puts suggest informed positioning.

### Unity unusual activity
- **[07:07–08:24]** Massive call volume (262k calls, 37k puts); 36% of calls transacted at offer or above; multiple 5,000-lot blocks across different strikes and expirations (Jan 24, Jan 25).
- **[08:24–10:41]** Dollar value of largest blocks reaches several million; appears to be at least two separate positions building across 15-strike and 20-strike calls; activity spans multiple days (23rd onward).
- **[10:41–12:43]** Speculation ties 5,000 blocks to Roaring Kitty, but fingerprinting analysis (ongoing GameStop series) suggests different behavior pattern; RK typically layers into single strikes, not across multiple expirations/strikes simultaneously.
- **[12:43–13:49]** Round-number strikes (20, 25, 30) naturally attract higher open interest due to human tendency to use round lot sizes; 5,000-lot blocks are standard sizing for multi-million-dollar positions, not unique to any single trader.
