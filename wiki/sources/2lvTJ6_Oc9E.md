# Win Rate and Profits #optionstrading

## Summary

This video explores the fundamental trade-off between win rate and profit per trade in options strategies. High win-rate strategies (65%+) like short premium tend to have capped upside and lower average profits, while lower win-rate strategies offer larger per-trade payoffs. The key insight is that probability of success and expected return are inversely related in most trading setups.

## Key takeaways

- **High win-rate strategies cluster around short premium** [00:00] — selling options (e.g., short puts) naturally produce 65%+ win rates because you collect premium on a wide buffer.
- **Break-even visualization on short puts** [00:26] — the red hash mark on the blue line shows where the position breaks even at expiration; this buffer is your margin of safety.
- **Capped upside is the cost of high probability** [00:26–00:52] — when you sell a 30-delta put, your P&L is capped at the premium collected, while your loss is theoretically unlimited (or large).
- **Inverse relationship: higher win rate → lower average profit** [00:52] — strategies with higher probability of winning generally produce lower average dollar amounts per trade; this is a fundamental market mechanic, not a flaw.

## Notable quotes

> "If we're looking at things that have a higher probability of winning, in general, you're going to expect to make a lower average amount of money."
