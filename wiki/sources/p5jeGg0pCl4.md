# Trade Updates XLF, XLE, KR - Small Stacks Pt10

## Summary

Eric closes the [[xlf]] short-put position at ~50% max profit and replaces it with a [[xle]] covered-strangle entry, collecting 72 cents on the 30 October 29 puts. He also adjusts the [[kr]] ratio-call-diagonal by rolling the 23 October 35 puts up to 35.5 strike, maintaining capital efficiency while preserving upside optionality. The account realizes $668 in P&L with $124 outstanding credit (2.2% of account), demonstrating disciplined [[profit-management]] and [[process-over-outcome]] execution.

## Key takeaways

- **XLF exit & replacement logic** [00:26–01:43]: Closed 22.5 short puts at 24 cents (from 54-cent sale) for ~50% max profit. Before exiting, identified a suitable replacement trade ([[xle]]) to redeploy capital, avoiding idle cash and ensuring the replacement premium exceeded remaining profit potential (24 cents).
- **XLE covered-strangle entry** [02:32–04:04]: Sold one 30 October 29 puts for 72 cents (~2% return on capital for 30 days). Positioned to convert to [[covered-strangle]] if assigned; energy sector near 3-year lows (23 support), making assignment at 29 acceptable. Willing to hold covered call if assigned rather than exit.
- **KR ratio-call-diagonal adjustment** [04:32–06:59]: Rolled 23 October 35 puts from 62 cents (sold) to 40 cents (closed), then sold one 6 November 35.5 call for 52 cents. Moved short strike up 50 cents to create more room; intentionally sold only one call to preserve upside optionality if price rallies (out-of-the-money calls trading 90 cents–$1).
- **Capital allocation & margin discipline** [07:30–10:14]: Account at $668 realized P&L, $124 outstanding credit (2.2% utilization). ~75% of $5,000 deployed; holding ~$700 cash reserve. Avoiding margin ahead of election; waiting for KR upside move to unwind a leap and free capital for next diagonal entry.
- **Process focus over dollar growth** [07:54–08:42]: Emphasizes that small-stacks account is a skill-building exercise, not wealth generation. Goal is to refine repeatable 2% monthly process that scales with account size (e.g., 2% on $500k vs. $5k).
