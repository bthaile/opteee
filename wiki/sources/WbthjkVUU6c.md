# Trading earnings with options

## Summary

Options provide a dual-return mechanism: traders can profit from directional price movement or from changes in implied volatility independent of direction. Earnings events create predictable volatility patterns—expansion into the release and sharp contraction post-announcement—that can be systematically traded via options strategies.

## Key takeaways

- **Volatility as a tradeable asset** [00:00] — Options allow you to isolate and trade volatility behavior separately from directional price movement, creating an additional source of returns when directional bets underperform.
- **Earnings volatility expansion pattern** [00:39] — Implied volatility typically begins expanding approximately two weeks before an earnings release, affecting both short-term and long-term volatility curves.
- **Post-earnings IV crush** [00:39] — After earnings release, implied volatility contracts sharply ("catastrophically"), creating a distinct profit opportunity for short-volatility positions.
- **Dual-trade opportunity** [00:39] — Traders can profit from the volatility expansion into earnings, then reverse or adjust positions to capture the contraction post-release.
