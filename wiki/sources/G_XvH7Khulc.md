# GME Options Update

## Summary

A technical and options-chain analysis of GameStop consolidation as of early July 2024. The video identifies light volume with persistent upside price action as anomalous and potentially bullish, then dissects the options structure: very low implied volatility, heavy call open interest (750k calls vs. 456k puts), and a notable $200k+ position in deep-in-the-money 12 July $10 calls suggesting informed directional conviction to the upside.

## Key takeaways

- **Consolidation with anomalous volume behavior** [02:04–02:56]: Price has held a range (swing low ~$22.31 on July 1) despite predominantly sell-side volume and light overall volume—unusual for a heavily-shorted stock and potentially signaling a catalyst building.
- **Implied volatility at multi-week lows** [03:38–04:00]: Expiration volatility is among the lowest observed in the past month, despite a holiday-week setup that would normally see expansion; this compression is noteworthy.
- **Call-heavy open interest skew toward 19 July** [04:31–05:23]: Post-Opex residual OI shows 750k calls vs. 456k puts; most OI is in 19 July (~270k calls) rather than the nearer 12 July, but volume is inverted (88k calls in 12 July vs. 41k in 19 July), suggesting near-term speculation on an imminent event.
- **Large directional position in deep-ITM 12 July $10 calls** [08:34–11:36]: Multiple tranches (~150–160 contracts total, ~$200k notional) of $10 calls (essentially one-delta, deep in-the-money) accumulated in early morning trading (06:30–06:33 PT), phased in a manner consistent with informed scaling; minimal extrinsic value bleed and pure directional leverage to the upside.
- **Put-skew volatility smile persists** [12:02–12:24]: Volatility surface shows puts trading higher than calls on both near-term expirations, consistent with recent patterns; no excessive call-side vol pricing suggesting market is not pricing in an outsized upside move.

## Notable quotes
