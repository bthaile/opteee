# Market Update: Tesla Trade Breakdown & GameStop Earnings + Bitcoin Treasury

## Summary

Eric reviews two key market moves: a short-term gamma-scalping trade in Tesla using near-term and longer-dated calls, capturing a double-bottom setup; and GameStop's earnings beat (30¢ vs. 9¢ estimate) coupled with the board's approval to add Bitcoin as a treasury reserve asset via convertible senior notes. The video emphasizes position sizing, time-frame alignment, and the strategic implications of GameStop's pivot toward cryptocurrency holdings.

## Key takeaways

- **Tesla volatility and sector context** [00:00–02:29]: Volatility remains suppressed across markets; tech is lagging communication services; Tesla faces near-term sentiment headwinds (tariff concerns, Elon-related controversy) but tariff impact is limited since most Tesla production is in Texas.

- **Tesla trade structure: kicker + base** [04:45–06:53]: Used two expiration legs—17 April 2025 calls (22 DTE, "kicker" options for gamma) and 16 Jan 2026 calls (296 DTE, base position for time). Kicker: 100 × 15 calls @ ~12¢ avg cost ($1,200 risk) → sold for ~$3,200 (167% ROIC). Base: 25 × calls @ ~$1.48 avg cost ($3,700) → sold for ~$4,500 (22% ROIC). Demonstrates pot-odds approach on speculative, time-frame-aligned positions.

- **GameStop earnings surprise** [08:07–09:34]: EPS beat of 30¢ vs. 9¢ estimate; net sales declined YoY (negative signal); net income $131M driven largely by cash interest (not sustainable). Cash position: $4.775B. Company divested Italy, winding down Germany. Post-earnings drift likely.

- **Bitcoin treasury strategy** [11:04–15:04]: Board approved convertible senior notes ($1.3–1.5B) maturing April 2030, zero coupon, to fund Bitcoin purchases. Settlement options: cash, new shares, treasury shares, or mix. No upfront dilution; conversion upside tied to stock appreciation. Branding play + institutional positioning + Bitcoin appreciation optionality if corporate adoption trend continues.

- **Options flow and positioning** [16:18–18:46]: GameStop options chain quiet (690K calls vs. 209K puts); call premiums elevated on relative basis post-earnings; largest single trade ~$1.1M; dispersion into further expirations suggests positional (not speculative) interest.

## Notable quotes

> "This is a $1,200 position as it relates to the portfolio. This is a fraction. It's literally less than a percent way less than a percent. And it's either going to work or it's not going to work. So the way that the risk is defined on that leg is literally by the size of the trade."

> "If you can raise money using other people's money without diluting the stock up front with the expectation of increasing the value of the stock so that those people can get paid back, then you can add to the balance sheet without drawing down your cash."
