# So You Want to Be an Options Trader? Watch This First

## Summary

This foundational education video deconstructs the nine core functional roles a retail options trader must master to succeed: educator, researcher, data analyst, strategy designer, trader, portfolio manager, risk manager, program manager, and psychologist. Rather than focusing on quick-win narratives, the host emphasizes that profitable trading requires systematic skill-building across research, backtesting, position management, and emotional discipline—a multi-year investment in yourself that compounds over time.

## Key takeaways

- **The nine hats framework** [01:52–02:28]: Educator, researcher, data analyst, strategy designer, trader, portfolio manager, risk manager, program manager, psychologist. Retail traders must wear all of them; institutional traders specialize.

- **Educator role: foundational learning is non-negotiable** [02:55–04:06]: Start with how markets operate, then learn derivatives/options mechanics, then study statistics and research methodology. This is not a quick YouTube course; it's a deliberate, long-term investment in yourself.

- **Researcher role: edges exist but require rigorous analysis** [04:53–06:08]: Learning options is table stakes, not an edge. Theta is the cost of gamma, not an edge itself. You must learn to read research papers critically—understand methodology, institutional bias, and how findings apply to your trading world.

- **Data analyst: deconstruct your trades** [07:24–07:51]: After each trade, analyze P&L by Greek to verify you isolated the profit mechanism you intended. This feedback loop is essential for iteration.

- **Strategy designer: profit mechanisms must be well-researched** [08:15]: Only implement strategies grounded in documented, tested profit mechanisms—not hunches.

- **Trader: execution is the easiest part** [08:15–08:39]: Clicking the button is straightforward; the hard work is everything before it. Avoid execution errors (e.g., adding an extra zero to order size).

- **Portfolio manager: ensure positions align with risk-reward preferences** [08:39–09:08]: Individual trades must work together and reflect your longer-term objectives.

- **Risk manager: ruthlessly enforce your risk framework** [09:08–10:00]: This role became prestigious at institutions when funds started blowing up. Align your total portfolio to both risk and reward constraints.

- **Program manager: optimize your process** [10:20–10:49]: The faster you iterate through research, testing, and analysis, the more ideas you'll find. Use audit processes (weekly/monthly/annual after-action reviews) to monitor regime changes and strategy fit.

- **Psychologist: know yourself ruthlessly** [11:14–12:47]: Identify behavioral gaps (e.g., impatience leading to missed earnings checks) and build guard rails (e.g., trade checklists). Smooth your emotional cycle—be indifferent to wins and losses; focus on process and data collection instead.

- **Scarcity mindset and risk aversion** [13:58]: Understand your own relationship with money. The host acknowledges his scarcity-driven risk aversion and actively controls for it.
