# focusing on the RIGHT problems in options trading

## Summary

New options traders typically waste time optimizing entry signals (RSI, technical indicators) that don't meaningfully improve outcomes, then blame the signal when losses occur. The core issue is problem selection: traders should shift focus from low-impact signal-hunting to identifying genuine market inefficiencies and structural edges that actually drive profitability.

## Key takeaways

- **Entry-signal obsession is a trap** [00:00–00:28]: Spending 20+ hours researching RSI oversold conditions or similar indicators creates false confidence; when the trade fails, the trader blames the signal rather than recognizing the signal was never the real problem.
- **Reframe the problem** [00:28–00:56]: Stop optimizing components that don't matter (indicator tuning, chart patterns). Redirect effort toward identifying genuine market effects and structural edges that have real edge.
- **Process over outcome**: The psychological pattern of perpetually solving the wrong problem is a hallmark of traders who lack a coherent edge-first framework.
