# Does Your Trade Make Sense?

## Summary

This video distinguishes thesis management from profit-and-loss management, arguing that a trade can be fundamentally wrong even if it hasn't hit your predefined loss target. The core insight is that invalidation of your original idea—independent of price targets—should trigger a reassessment of whether you should remain in the position.

## Key takeaways

- **Thesis vs. P&L are separate concerns** [00:00–00:30]: Your idea can be disproven even if price hasn't reached your stop-loss. A trade that moves against your thesis but hasn't hit your loss target is still a failed trade.
- **Example: SPY short thesis** [00:00–00:30]: If you expected SPY to rise but it falls without hitting your loss level, your thesis is invalidated—the trade should be questioned regardless of P&L mechanics.
- **Invalidation triggers reassessment** [00:30]: When evidence contradicts your original thesis, that is the signal to exit, not the price level you initially set.

## Notable quotes

> "My idea was still completely wrong even though I didn't hit my loss. That would call into question whether or not I should still be in that trade."
