# Huge NVDA Earnings Ahead - Analyzing the Options Chain

## Summary

With NVIDIA reporting earnings on November 20 after market close, this analysis examines the options chain positioning, historical earnings behavior, and volatility structure to assess risk/reward for directional plays. The stock sits at all-time highs with narrowing earnings surprises; the options market shows meaningful call skew and put-side IV richness, suggesting balanced but slightly downside-hedged positioning despite recent upside gaps post-earnings.

## Key takeaways

- **Earnings surprise trend narrowing** [01:02]: NVIDIA's EPS beats have compressed from 26.7% (Q1'23) → 16.7% (Q1'24) → 13.7% (Q4'24) → 8.8% (Q3'24), signaling tightening analyst expectations even as the stock hits all-time highs.

- **Historical post-earnings gaps favor upside** [03:39]: Recent earnings releases show consistent upside gaps (e.g., 207→230, 305→370, 674→785, 949→1037) with smaller downside drift, suggesting asymmetric risk/reward to the long side.

- **IV vs. realized vol: selling volatility looks unfavorable** [04:44]: Implied volatility is not significantly elevated relative to historical volatility; prior profitable short-straddle setups (e.g., 49 IV → 37 post-earnings) are not present here, making short premium plays less attractive.

- **Post-earnings announcement drift (PEAD) conditional** [05:54]: PEAD exists when earnings beat is large enough; NVIDIA has exhibited it after strong beats (e.g., $6.12 vs. $5.14 expected), but mega-cap mean reversion can suppress it.

- **Options chain: call skew in 22-Nov expiry, put skew in 20-Dec** [08:10]: The earnings expiry (22-Nov) shows meaningful call skew; the 20-Dec primary expiry shows huge call skew, indicating longer-dated traders expect upside. Put-side IV is richer than call-side, a normal (not hyper-bullish) structure.

- **Open interest distribution** [08:10]: ~1M calls vs. 850k puts in 22-Nov; call dominance persists even accounting for hedging by long shareholders, suggesting net bullish positioning.

- **Positioning for earnings** [11:04]: Longer-term positions should be held based on conviction; shorter-dated plays depend on final options structure at market open on earnings day.
