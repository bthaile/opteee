# Quadruple Witching Explained

## Summary

Quadruple witching occurs when stock options, stock index futures, and single stock futures all expire on the same day—the third Friday of March, June, September, and December. The event drives institutional hedging and position-closing activity, resulting in elevated volume and volatility, though historical data shows the direct market impact has diminished as markets have become more efficient.

## Key takeaways

- **Definition & timing** [00:00–01:10]: Quadruple witching is the simultaneous expiration of multiple derivative classes on the third Friday of March, June, September, and December. Triple witching is now legacy terminology; single stock futures expirations made it "quadruple."
- **Institutional mechanics** [01:32–01:55]: Market makers hedge expiring positions while institutional speculators close and reopen positions further out in time, creating arbitrage opportunities for sophisticated traders.
- **Market impact: volume & volatility** [02:15–02:39]: Volume typically increases and volatility spikes, though the effect depends on broader market conditions. Higher probability of volatility events around the expiration date.
- **S&P 500 performance data** [03:02–04:28]: Over 10 years, the S&P 500 shows ~71% positive days in the 5 days before expiration (attributable partly to positive drift), but ~0.38% average negative move on the day itself, with slight negative skew 5 days after.
- **VIX behavior** [05:18–05:40]: VIX tends to decline 5 days before (aligned with S&P 500 strength), and contracts on expiration day itself despite S&P 500 weakness—suggesting the market prices in heightened risk pre-event but sees limited realized volatility.
- **Practical trader implications** [06:06–06:47]: Be aware of the event; expect wider bid-ask spreads due to increased volatility; avoid heavy trading on expiration day if possible. The impact has diminished over time as markets became more efficient and expirations proliferated.

Data spans 10 years of quadruple witching cycles (2023 dates cited: March 17, June 16, September 15, December 15). The video was published June 20, 2024, one day after the June 2024 quadruple witching expiration (June 21). Historical analysis shows declining impact relative to earlier decades, reflecting market structure evolution and increased derivative expirations throughout the year.
