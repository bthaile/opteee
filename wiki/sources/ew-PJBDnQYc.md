# Options Max Pain Theory Explained

## Summary

Max pain is the strike price at which the greatest dollar value of options expire worthless at a given expiration cycle. The theory posits that market makers, who are net sellers of options, have incentive and ability to move stock prices toward max pain at expiration to minimize their losses. Recent peer-reviewed research confirms observable stock return predictability at option expiration that reverses post-expiration, particularly in smaller, less-liquid stocks.

## Key takeaways

- **What max pain is not** [00:18]–[00:38]: Not the video game character or portfolio drawdown feeling; it's a specific strike price with measurable market mechanics.

- **Core definition and market maker incentive** [01:02]–[01:49]: Max pain is the strike where the most options expire worthless. Market makers, as net sellers, prefer this outcome because it eliminates inventory risk and weekend exposure.

- **Calculation method** [02:49]–[03:43]: Sum the intrinsic value × open interest for all strikes in an expiration cycle; the strike with the lowest total series value is max pain. Use thinkorswim's "Product Depth Details" chart with intrinsic value overlay to visualize it.

- **Peer-reviewed evidence** [04:27]–[05:48]: A November 2022 SSRN study found that a spread portfolio buying high max-pain stocks and selling low max-pain stocks generates statistically significant positive returns and alpha. Effect is strongest in small, illiquid stocks; weaker in large indices like SPX and SPY.

- **Max pain is dynamic, not static** [08:21]–[08:59]: Options prices and open interest shift throughout the day, so max pain recalculates continuously. ~60% of options are traded before expiration, making it a moving target.

- **Practical application: not a silver bullet** [09:18]–[10:17]: Use max pain as an additive input, not the center of your trade thesis. For zero-DTE SPX short straddles or iron flies, check max pain later in the day and adjust strikes or timing if price is a few points away; wait for confirmation of directional movement toward it.

- **Research methodology** [09:40]–[09:58]: Track max pain on SPY/SPX over several weeks (daily expirations available) to build your own data sample and test whether price actually trends toward it in your market conditions.

## Notable quotes

> "Market makers are the vast majority of sellers out there on the open marketplace; they would prefer their options to expire worthless so come expiration day they are then incentivized to quite literally manipulate the price of the stock to land at maximum pain." [01:49]–[02:12]
