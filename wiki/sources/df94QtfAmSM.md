# Dollar Cost Averaging Options #optionstrading

## Summary

Eric demonstrates how to apply dollar-cost-averaging principles to options trading by systematically adding contracts to existing positions while managing total capital deployment. The strategy involves accepting incremental risk on each addition while reducing average cost basis, allowing remaining capital to be deployed more efficiently across subsequent entries.

## Key takeaways

- **Systematic entry discipline** [00:00]: Adding contracts in tranches rather than all at once; each addition carries explicit risk that must be accepted upfront.
- **Cost-basis reduction via averaging** [00:28]: By adding three more contracts while decreasing basis by ~3 points, total capital requirement expands by only ~$6,000 instead of the full notional risk, demonstrating capital efficiency.
- **Danger of adding to losers** [00:28]: Dollar-cost averaging into underwater positions is inherently risky and only appropriate for strategies explicitly designed around it; unsuitable for most other approaches.
- **Capital leverage through averaging** [00:28]: The core mechanic is squeezing remaining capital further with each entry by improving the blended cost basis, allowing more efficient deployment of the total risk budget.
