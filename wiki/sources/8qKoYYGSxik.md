# SPY vs TSLA: Same PnL, Different Risk

## Summary

A comparative analysis of expected volatility and P&L impact between SPY and TSLA over a 30-day horizon. Despite similar absolute dollar moves (~$57–$57), the two securities exhibit vastly different percentage volatility due to their price levels, resulting in materially different portfolio impact for equal dollar allocations.

## Key takeaways

- **Dollar move equivalence masks percentage divergence** [00:00–00:33]: SPY and TSLA both move ~$57 in one standard deviation over 30 days, but SPY's 8.6% move vs. TSLA's 14.3% move reflects their different price bases (SPY ~$672 vs. TSLA ~$396).
- **Equal dollar allocation ≠ equal risk exposure** [00:33]: Investing $10,000 equally split between the two will produce materially higher P&L volatility in TSLA due to its higher percentage volatility, despite identical notional sizing.
- **Deeper analysis required for position-sizing decisions**: Understanding the specific volatility differential allows traders to right-size positions and manage portfolio-level risk more precisely.
