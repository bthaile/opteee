# Objective analysis #options #trading #optionstrading

## Summary

A foundational lesson on removing personal bias and preference from trading decisions. The core principle: what you *need* to happen is irrelevant; what matters is objective analysis of what *is* happening and your ability to respond to it. Traders who impose their expectations onto the market (e.g., "this stock is too high, it must fall") often get run over by continued adverse price action.

## Key takeaways

- **Separate need from reality** [00:00] — Your personal profit targets or expectations do not dictate market behavior. Focus on what the market is actually doing, not what you think should happen.
- **Avoid belief-driven trading** [00:27] — Saying "I can't believe this stock is up this high" and shorting it based on that disbelief is a recipe for losses. The market will continue to move against you if your thesis is rooted in emotion rather than analysis.
- **Analyze, then trade** [00:50] — The correct sequence is: (1) objectively analyze the scenario, (2) determine what best fits current conditions, (3) execute for profit. Do not reverse the order by deciding what you want first and then trying to force the market to comply.

## Notable quotes

- "What matters is what's happening and then your ability to interact with it."
- "Our job as traders is to analyze things, figure out what best fits the scenario, and then to make money—not to come up with what you think you want to happen and then try to trade that in front of the market."
