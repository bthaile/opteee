# Trade Profit and Loss Management

## Summary

This video addresses the core mechanics of profit and loss management: when to take profits on winning trades and when to exit losing positions. The session focuses on overcoming the disposition effect—the psychological bias to lock in winners quickly while holding losers—and establishing a systematic approach to position exits based on plan rather than emotion.

## Key takeaways

- **Disposition effect defined** [00:00–00:31]: Traders tend to take winning trades off quickly (because we enjoy winning) while holding losers longer (to avoid realizing losses). This behavioral bias undermines long-term profitability.
- **Profit-taking framework** [00:00]: When a thesis plays out (e.g., SPY declines as expected), establish clear rules for *when* to exit rather than relying on emotional satisfaction.
- **Loss management parallel** [00:31]: The same discipline required for winners applies to losers—define exit criteria *before* entering, not in real time.
- **Process over outcome**: Separate the quality of your decision-making from the emotional reward/pain of the trade result.
