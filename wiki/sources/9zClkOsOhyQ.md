# After Action Reviews - Trading Like A Marine #optionstrading

## Summary

Eric discusses the after-action review (AAR) process—a structured feedback loop borrowed from military discipline—as a core mechanism for iterative trading improvement. Weekly, monthly, and annual AARs enable traders to audit execution quality, identify operational gaps, and compound learning over time. External feedback and community accountability are essential to the process.

## Key takeaways

- **AAR cadence**: conduct reviews weekly, monthly, and annually to maintain continuous feedback loops [00:00–00:23]
- **Execution audit**: use AARs to assess whether you are executing your plan and identify operational opportunities [00:23–00:48]
- **External feedback critical**: seek peer review or community participation (e.g., Outlier free community) to gain objective perspective, mirroring how military training relies on external correction [00:48]
- **ROI justification**: the discipline required for structured review compounds into significant returns (multiples of annual salary) [00:23]
- **Iterative process**: all trading processes are dynamic and evolve over time; AARs are the mechanism that drives that evolution [00:00]
