# GME & CHWY Options: Inside the Big Trades

## Summary

Analysis of unusual options activity in GameStop and Chewy, focusing on a large 496-contract deep-in-the-money call trade in GME at 07:44 PT interpreted as a share acquisition method, and systematic review of month-long Chewy call accumulation centered on the 19 July expiration. The video emphasizes position structure, expiration-cycle liquidity, and the distinction between lottery-style low-probability trades and sustainable long-term edge.

## Key takeaways

- **GME 128 call expiration** [00:36]: 67,341 contracts expired worthless—cautionary example of low-probability lotto plays that undermine long-term profitability.
- **Deep-ITM call trade as share acquisition** [01:42–02:47]: 496 contracts of 11 calls (49,600 shares notional) transacted at 07:44 PT on 0-DTE, landing near the ask; interpreted as a method to enter a large position while avoiding market impact, despite paying a premium via options rather than direct equity purchase.
- **Front-expiration concentration in GME** [03:32–04:22]: ~350,000 calls and 130,000 puts in the front weekly; next major cycle (19 July) holds 217,000 calls and 131,000 puts. Liquidity drives this clustering.
- **Volatility compression into short week** [06:12–06:36]: GME IV at 227% for today's expiration drops to 111% for 5 July (short week due to July 4th), then rebounds to 120s+ for 19 July. Market not pricing in massive move for the short week.
- **Position-structure principle** [05:11–05:29]: Establishing positions further out in time costs more upfront but allows positions to work; front-expiration positions are sensitive to spot moves but bleed quickly if thesis fails.
- **Chewy call accumulation (month-long data)** [08:09–09:14]: 19 July expiration shows concentrated open interest at 30, 35, 37.5, and 45 strikes; significantly higher volume than other expirations for the month.
- **Chewy large block trades** [10:19–11:09]: Multiple 5,000+ contract blocks and several million-dollar positions in 30 and 35 calls; phased entry across multiple days suggests deliberate position building.
- **Chewy 19 July current activity** [11:31–11:51]: 30 strike has 15,000 OI with ~10,000 traded today; 35 strike has 16,700 OI with 5,000 traded today. Volatility not elevated; standard volatility smile (not GME-style call skew).
- **Volatility skew contrast** [12:12–12:32]: GME shows extreme call-side IV (fear of upside); Chewy displays standard downside-heavy smile with some tradable kinks. Chewy activity appears slower than GME despite large block trades.
