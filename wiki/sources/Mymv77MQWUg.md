# Setting goals #optionstrading #trading #stockmarket

## Summary

Goal-setting for traders must be grounded in demonstrated skill and track record, not wishful thinking. Beginners should prioritize learning and positive returns over specific return targets; only after building a trading history can you refine goals to match your actual edge and capabilities.

## Key takeaways

- **First two years: target positive returns, not specific percentages** [00:20–00:38]. Without a track record, any specific return target is speculation about your skill level.
- **Your goal must align with your current skill set, not your desires** [00:20]. A 20% return target is only valid if your demonstrated edge supports it.
- **Refine goals iteratively as you build data** [00:38]. Once you have trading history, you can set more specific targets based on what you've actually achieved.
- **Learning comes before optimization** [00:38]. Early trading is about building process and edge, not chasing arbitrary return numbers.
