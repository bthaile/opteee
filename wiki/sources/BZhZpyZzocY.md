# David Hunter: Final Leg of Melt Up - What's Coming Next | The Outlier Podcast

## Summary

David Hunter returns to discuss the final leg of a 43-year secular bull market that began in 1982. He maintains aggressive upside targets (S&P 9,500, NASDAQ 32,000, Russell 3,800) and expects the market to move parabolic in the next 3–6 months, driven by Fed liquidity, falling bond yields, dollar weakness, and Trump administration deregulation. He forecasts a global bust to follow the top, but emphasizes timing is uncertain; his framework prioritizes targets and sentiment over precise entry/exit dates.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Melt-up definition:** Unusually strong market with each leg steeper than the last; S&P doubled from 3,500 (Oct 2022) to ~7,000 (Jan 2026); NASDAQ more than doubled from 10,100 [22:07]
- **Wall of worry sustains rally:** Institutions kept "one foot out the door" throughout; every 3–5% pullback rebuilds bearish sentiment, providing fuel for next leg [11:16], [12:30]
- **Fed liquidity unprecedented:** $5 trillion injected 2020–2021 (pandemic response); balance sheet peaked at $9 trillion, now ~6.5 trillion; money still flowing through system [08:51], [10:07]
- **Technical setup:** S&P currently ~4 standard deviations above 30-year mean; parabolic phase likely underway [20:46]
- **Sentiment as primary driver:** Hunter uses CNN Fear/Greed Index, Bank of America fund manager survey, Investor's Intelligence strategist gauge; extremes signal inflection points [42:57], [44:06]
- **Tariff reaction temporary:** Court rulings or geopolitical shocks may trigger days-long selloffs, not weeks; Trump administration has "Plan B, C, D" [31:57]
- **Oil supply/demand:** Belongs in $50–60 range near-term (geopolitical spikes to $70 possible); $30s in bust; $500/barrel by early 2030s post-bust [34:33], [35:44], [36:57]

- **Gold accumulation thesis:** Central banks, BRICS, Eastern Europe buying for currency competition and inflation hedge; demand exceeds supply [40:33], [41:43]
- **Timing vs. targets:** Hunter prioritizes targets and sentiment over precise dates; "three to six months" is rolling estimate, not trading call; cycle determined by market, not forecaster [16:12], [17:18]
