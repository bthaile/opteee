# You're MESSING UP Covered Calls #optionstrading

## Summary

A critique of the covered-call strategy when applied to bullish positions. The core argument: selling call premium against equity you expect to appreciate caps your upside for insufficient compensation, sacrificing the right-tail profit potential you bought the stock for in exchange for near-term premium that feels material but is actually a pittance relative to the opportunity cost.

## Key takeaways

- **The upside-cap trap** [00:00–00:26]: The short call leg is not the most important part of a covered-call trade; it's a distraction that forces you to choose between holding equity and capping gains.
- **Right-tail sacrifice** [00:26–00:46]: If you hold equity because you expect it to rise significantly, selling calls surrenders that right-tail profit potential for near-term premium—a poor trade-off.
- **Ratio selling as an alternative** [00:26]: If you must sell calls, consider selling at a ratio (requires >100 shares) rather than 1:1, preserving some upside exposure.
- **Premium illusion** [00:46]: Near-term call premium can psychologically distort your decision-making; don't let it override your conviction in the underlying's upside.
