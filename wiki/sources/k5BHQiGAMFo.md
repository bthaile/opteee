# Market Update: RKLB, GME, AMC — Technical & Options Analysis

## Summary

Quick market recap covering three meme-adjacent names: [[rklb]] showing strong breakout continuation with bullish call skew and volume support; [[gme]] consolidating sideways in a quiet period with options activity shifting to longer-dated expirations; and [[amc]] exhibiting downtrend continuation with a notable spike in call volume (63k calls traded, 52% bought) concentrated in longer-dated strikes. All three show reduced near-term volume and options positioning favoring longer-term plays over front-month concentration.

## Key takeaways

### RKLB
- **Breakout continuation pattern** [00:59–02:01]: Classic breakout-consolidation-breakout cycle; volume on upmoves is significant and broadly supported, indicating conviction.
- **Bullish call skew** [02:01–03:00]: 354k calls vs. 170k puts; majority of calls bought at offer, majority of puts sold at bid. Earnings on Nov 6 creates kink in volatility smile.
- **Trend-following entry** [02:01]: Using options for leverage; stop set below recent consolidation; next intermediate alert around prior resistance.
- **Longer-dated positioning** [04:15]: Unusual activity shows large trades in 15-Jan-27 and 17-Jan-25 expirations—market playing longer-term upside.

### GME
- **Range-bound consolidation** [05:15–06:13]: Trading sideways; volume node about to shift lower; floor estimated around $19–20 range (Peruvian Bull opinion ~$20; speaker slightly more conservative at high $19s).
- **Volume dried up** [06:13–07:14]: Down from 7M to 5.2–5.3M daily; quiet period supports consolidation over choppiness.
- **Options shift to longer expirations** [07:14–08:19]: Put/call ratio near 2:1 but no longer concentrated in front month; longer-dated positioning makes sense given price consolidation and low volume.
- **Volatility surface anomaly** [08:19–09:23]: 11-Oct expiration shows unusually low at-the-money volatility relative to other expirations—potential relative-value opportunity, but not a misprice.
- **Breakout levels** [11:24–12:27]: Watching for breaks above or below consolidation range; concerned that recent relative high is lower than prior high and recent low is lower than prior low (potential curve turning over).

### AMC

- **Downtrend continuation** [14:41–15:46]: Lower highs on 3-month chart; standard deviation channels negative-sloping; price starting to level out around value area.
