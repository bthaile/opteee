# Trading the election outcome

## Summary

Post-election market dislocations present sector-rotation opportunities driven by policy expectations: financials rallying on deregulation hopes, energy rising on oil-friendly sentiment, tech recovering from suppression, and real estate cratering on higher-for-longer rate expectations. The key is to focus on profit mechanisms rather than political sentiment.

## Key takeaways

- **Financials outperforming** [00:00–00:21] — deregulation expectations under Trump administration lifting the sector; concrete opportunity in [[xlf]] positioning.
- **Energy sector turning up** [00:21–00:40] — expectation of increased oil production and favorable energy policy driving [[crude-oil]] and energy sector strength.
- **Tech regaining footing** [00:21–00:40] — approaching 3-month highs after suppression; deregulation narrative supporting [[xlk]] recovery.
- **Real estate cratering** [00:40] — 30-year mortgage rates spiking on expectation of higher federal funds rate persisting due to inflationary policy pressures; [[xlv]] (real estate proxy) under pressure.
- **Regime shift driver** [00:00] — [[sector-rotation]] mechanics activated by policy-driven [[market-regimes]] change; focus on [[profit-mechanism]] over political outcome.
