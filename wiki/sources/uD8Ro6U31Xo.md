# Ratio Call Diagonal Option Trade Explained

## Summary

Eric walks through a live ratio call diagonal trade on Apple that returned 28.8% on invested capital over ~2.5 months. The strategy combined long deep-in-the-money calls (190 DTE) with short calls sold at a 2:1 ratio, allowing leverage while collecting upfront premium. He adjusts the position dynamically as the stock rallies, downsizing the short ratio to let profits run, and compares the outcome to pure long calls and share ownership.

## Key takeaways

- **Entry thesis [00:55–01:32]**: Bought strength after earnings gap-up, targeting near-term resistance highs rather than bargain-hunting lows; entered 190-DTE deep-ITM calls at 86 delta, sold 2:1 short calls against them.
- **Position sizing [02:33–02:53]**: Used near-term support (~162) as hard stop; allocated ~$10k to the proxy position, keeping loss to ~$22.50 per contract if stopped out.
- **Dynamic adjustment [03:13–03:46]**: As stock rallied, closed short calls at small profit despite being ITM (theta decay still favored credit), then downsized from 2:1 to 1:1 ratio to reduce short deltas and let upside run.
- **Trailing stop discipline [05:11–06:23]**: Slid stops up with each new resistance level (162 → 170 → 181); would have exited if price closed below 9-day MA and touched 22-day MA; forced exit on 28 July when short calls expired.
- **Leverage comparison [07:16–07:55]**: Same $10k capital controlled ~200 shares via options vs. ~57 shares via stock; 28.8% return on ratio diagonal vs. 13.1% on shares, vs. 35–38% on pure long calls (hindsight).
- **Trade-off analysis [06:39–07:16]**: Selling short premium cost 6–10 percentage points vs. pure long calls, but that required knowing the future; ratio diagonal balanced leverage with defined risk.
