# DEEP In the Money Put Options Management || IWM Covered Strangle

## Summary

Eric walks through live management of a deep in-the-money short put position on IWM that has moved against him by ~16% since entry. He evaluates three primary adjustment paths—rolling out, taking assignment and selling covered calls, or increasing size to roll down—and settles on scaling in slightly to reduce basis and create more flexibility for future assignment and covered-call sales.

## Key takeaways

- **Entry & first roll context** [00:22–01:05]: Sold 6× IWM 202 puts at $3.38 on 29 March; rolled to 6× 17 June 200 puts at $1.33 on 28 April after an ~11% down move, reducing basis by 1% while maintaining size.

- **Deep ITM put management threshold** [06:11–06:34]: When extrinsic value drops below ~$0.10 (currently at $0.125 on 80-delta puts with 22 DTE), early assignment risk rises sharply; time to act before it trades at $0.04–$0.05.

- **Rolling out alone is insufficient** [06:56–07:19]: Rolling 42 DTE at same strike (200) yields only ~$0.24 credit—not enough to justify the move; rolling out and down to 199 yields ~$0.23, still inadequate.

- **Covered-call alternative underwhelming** [07:19–07:37]: Selling 42-DTE covered calls at 200 (or slightly OTM for upside) yields only ~$0.40–$0.50, insufficient compensation for cost of carry.

- **Extended roll-down requires 92 DTE** [08:48–09:17]: To achieve net credit rolling down to 199 or 198, must extend to 15 July (57 DTE) or August (92 DTE); not preferred at this time.

- **Preferred adjustment: scale in & roll down** [09:36–10:23]: Increase from 6 to 8 contracts, targeting 195 strike (5-point reduction in basis); this allows more meaningful credit collection and flexibility for eventual assignment at a lower cost basis.

- **Covered-call ratio strategy post-assignment** [10:45–11:27]: If assigned 800 shares at 195, sell 6× 198 calls (18 delta, 57 DTE) for ~$0.60 credit, leaving 200 shares uncapped for upside—a favored asymmetric structure.

- **Capital allocation & risk sizing** [02:41–03:04]: Maintains ≤20% portfolio utilization year-to-date due to low market trust; sizing is primary risk-management lever; position sizing drives all adjustment decisions.

- **Annualized return perspective** [05:25–05:45]: Despite unrealized loss, net P&L of $24.48 on capital invested yields 14.6% annualized return—acceptable enough to remain patient and scale in rather than panic-close.

- **Extrinsic value extraction & patience** [12:28–13:06]: Waits for puts to drop below $0.10 to maximize time decay benefit; monitors technical support levels (175, 182, 189) to inform whether to scale in further or hold.
