### Evergreen mechanics

- **Option P&L calculation:** Profit = (closing intrinsic value × contracts × 100) − (opening cost × contracts × 100); Greeks are irrelevant at expiration [13:15–14:45]
- **Currency hedging logic:** US importer paying in foreign currency 6 months forward should buy calls on that currency to hedge upside risk in its value; reduces risk of paying more dollars for same goods [27:35–30:26]
- **Volatility term structure:** plots implied volatility across different expiration dates; shows where volatility is relatively expensive or cheap; distinct from volatility surface (which adds strike dimension) [42:24–49:08]
- **Volatility skew:** describes the shape of IV across strikes within a single expiration; call skew (higher IV on calls) common in meme stocks due to squeeze fear; put skew (higher IV on puts) more typical in broad market [50:15–55:41]
- **Market maker mechanics:** Market makers price options via volatility estimates, not to "rig" retail; competitive pressure forces accuracy; they manage risk by adjusting bid/offer and IV, not by turning off quotes [56:06–01:01:55]
- **Order types & fills:** Market orders prioritize speed over price (dangerous); limit orders allow walking the bid/ask by cancel-replace; trailing stops work by subtracting a fixed amount from the highest price reached [09:36–01:18:55]
- **Relative value trades:** When IV kinks appear (e.g., 17.5 calls more expensive than 17 calls), can trade the relationship without betting on absolute direction; calendar spreads exploit term structure differences [01:08:04–01:09:14]
