# VIX vs VXX

## Summary

The VIX and VXX are both volatility-tracking products tied to S&P 500 implied volatility, but they differ fundamentally in structure and mechanics. The VIX is a cash-settled index with no underlying stock, while the VXX is a futures-based ETN with an underlying that experiences contango drag. Understanding these differences is critical for selecting appropriate strategies and managing decay risk.

## Key takeaways

- **Similarities** [00:48]: Both have tradable options, track S&P 500 volatility, are mean-reverting, and exhibit rapid parabolic moves during volatility spikes (e.g., VIX moved from ~14 to 85).

- **VIX structure** [01:50]: No underlying stock; cash-settled on Wednesdays; tracks current S&P 500 implied volatility (e.g., VIX at 28.22 matched SPX volatility at 28.13).

- **VXX structure** [03:21]: Has an underlying ETN with bid/ask/volume; futures-based product using front-month and next-month /VX futures contracts; forward-looking projection of VIX.

- **Contango drag** [04:26]: VXX suffers monthly roll drag when further-dated futures are more expensive (contango). Rolling from May to June futures at higher prices erodes product value over time, necessitating reverse splits.

- **Volatility dampening** [06:33]: VXX exhibits slightly lower volatility than VIX because it blends two expiration months, reducing gamma and vega risk compared to front-month-only exposure.

- **Decay mechanics** [07:20]: Never hold VXX shares naked long-term; monthly rolling and contango decay erode value even if volatility eventually spikes. Timing a spike does not guarantee profit after accounting for decay.

- **Risk management** [08:00]: Always use spreads (e.g., short call spreads) when shorting volatility products. Naked short positions expose you to binary moves that can trigger margin calls and forced liquidation at losses.

- **Preferred approach** [09:25]: Eric prefers trading VXX over VIX because of better liquidity, the presence of an underlying, and the ability to exploit predictable contango drag to inform mean-reversion timing.

## Notable quotes

- "Both of them have options available to trade on them, they both track in some capacity the volatility of the S&P 500, they both are mean reverting, and they both find expansion with heightened volatility." [00:48]

- "The VXX is a forward-looking projection of the VIX… it's a futures-based product so that means every single month it'll look at two different futures." [03:50]

- "I would never ever just be long shares of VXX for any period of time simply because… every month when we're rolling you'll just see those futures rolling out causing decay eroding the value of the position." [07:20]
