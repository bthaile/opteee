# -254% LOSS #trading #optionstrading #stockmarket

## Summary

Eric documents a significant loss on a Dash earnings trade, capturing the reality that individual losing trades are inevitable in systematic trading. The key insight is that system-level profitability and positive expected value matter more than the outcome of any single trade, even a large drawdown.

## Key takeaways

- **Earnings trades carry event risk:** A single earnings play can produce outsized losses (−254% in this case) despite a sound process [00:00]
- **System performance trumps individual outcomes:** The broad trading system remained profitable for the week despite the knockback, demonstrating the importance of [[process-over-outcome]] [00:00–00:26]
- **Positive expected value is the metric:** As long as the system continues to perform with positive expected value, individual losing trades are acceptable and part of the game [00:26]
- **Transparency about losses:** Acknowledging and accepting losses publicly reinforces that real trading involves drawdowns, not a perfect win rate [00:00]

## Notable quotes
