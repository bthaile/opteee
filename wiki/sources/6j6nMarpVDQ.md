# Is this the biggest mistake in options trading? #optionstrading

## Summary

The video examines a critical pitfall in options trading: over-hedging short volatility positions to the point where risk mitigation destroys profitability. Using an earnings example where expected move was ~16% but realized move was ~57%, the host illustrates how traders become "intoxicated" by hedging and lose sight of the fact that they are fundamentally paid to take risk. The core mistake is laying off too much risk when the edge lies in accepting it.

## Key takeaways

- **Earnings vol mismatch as a teaching example** [00:00–00:24]: When expected move significantly underestimates realized move (16% expected vs. 57% actual), naked short options positions suffer severe losses; this is where risk mitigation appears necessary.
- **The hedging addiction trap** [00:24–00:47]: Traders become psychologically addicted to hedging and risk-laying, especially in zero-DTE strategies, losing sight of the fundamental principle that options premiums exist *because* you are paid to take risk.
- **Profitability paradox** [00:47]: Over-hedging erodes the edge; the biggest mistake is laying off so much risk that you can no longer make money from the positions you entered.

## Notable quotes

> "The issue is very frequently you are paid to take risk. So, if you get too intoxicated by the fact of trying to lay off your risk, it becomes really difficult to make money."
