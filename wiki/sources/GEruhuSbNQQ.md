# AVERAGE #trading #stockmarket #outlier

## Summary

A brief mental-model lesson on the danger of using average performance as a personal benchmark. Eric argues that comparing yourself to average outcomes—whether in fitness or trading—is a trap that locks you into mediocre results; instead, upgrade your reference point to match your actual capability level and aspirations.

## Key takeaways

- **Benchmark against your own level, not the average** [00:00–00:24]: If you know you won't perform at average, don't use average as your reference. The average American is obese; if you're not, that's not your benchmark.
- **Listening to average finance voices projects their limitations onto you** [00:24]: Spending too much time absorbing what average market participants say will anchor you to their capability ceiling, not yours.
- **Upgrade your benchmarks to play on a different level** [00:24]: Conscious benchmark selection is a prerequisite for outlier performance.

## Notable quotes

> "If you spend too much time listening to what the average person in finance says you're going to hear their projection of their capabilities onto you whereas you might be playing on a completely different level."
