# Using Unusual Options Activity (UOA) to Trade

## Summary

Eric demonstrates a CCJ (Cameco) trade that returned ~200% on the option and ~160% on invested capital by combining unusual options activity (UOA) monitoring with a thematic nuclear-energy conviction play. The trade used long calls (50 lots of 19 May $60 calls at $2.52, 37 delta) entered on 14 May, with partial profit-taking on 27 May after a faster-than-expected move. The core insight: options positioning (massive call-premium skew and open-interest buildup) served as an alert signal that informed traders possessed advance information about upcoming nuclear policy announcements.

## Key takeaways

- **Thematic setup + UOA confluence [00:00–03:23]**: Eric monitors broad themes (nuclear energy policy under Trump) and uses unusual options activity as a trigger to enter when conviction aligns with positioning signals. Neither alone drives the trade; both together do.

- **Position sizing as risk definition [04:34–07:01]**: The $12,600 entry (50 lots, 2% of model portfolio) was sized larger than typical speculative trades (~1%) due to higher conviction. Risk was defined by position size, not a hard stop, allowing planned management of the remainder.

- **Call-premium skew as positioning alert [07:01–09:21]**: On 27 May (after the move began), call premium dominated: 1.7M calls vs. 236K puts across expirations. This skew had been building since the 14 May entry. Percent-change in open interest also showed pronounced buildup—both signals of informed positioning.

- **Options flow as information leak [09:21–10:40]**: The 60-strike calls were being heavily bought on 14 May. The subsequent massive volume spike and rapid move to 5775+ suggests traders had advance knowledge of Trump's 23 May nuclear executive orders, which were not yet public.

- **Profit-taking and trend management [10:40–12:56]**: Partial exit on 27 May realized $20,720 P&L; remaining 10 lots held with defined risk ($2,520) and profit target at ~5775. Trade was managed by price movement and trend, not time decay, because the options flow indicated shorter-term directional conviction.

## Notable quotes

> "The reason why I always take the time to define risk, whether it's going to be by size or by a hard stop, is because it allows me to then plan successfully for the rest of the trade." [07:01]

> "Seeing that kind of options activity and then just this massive explosion in volume following, it's telling that a lot of times people get pieces of information before it's fully released." [12:00]
