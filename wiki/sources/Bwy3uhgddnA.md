# Start simple #options #trading

## Summary

A foundational overview of entry points for options traders transitioning from stock trading. The video presents three accessible strategies—the wheel, covered strangle, and long calls as stock replacement—that allow traders to build complexity gradually based on their goals and risk tolerance.

## Key takeaways

- **The wheel strategy** [00:22]: Start with [[short-put|cash-secured puts]], get assigned, buy the underlying, then sell [[covered-call|calls]] against it for income.
- **Covered strangle variant** [00:22]: A preferred alternative to the wheel with similar mechanics but additional nuances for managing assignment and premium collection.
- **Long calls as stock replacement** [00:46]: Use [[long-call|long calls]] further out in time and deeper in the money to replicate stock upside with [[leverage]] and reduced capital outlay.
- **Progression path** [00:00]: Stock traders can transition into options by matching their existing directional thesis (momentum, value) to appropriate option strategies rather than jumping to complex multi-leg positions.
