# Covered Strangle Adding to Losers

## Summary

This segment explores the mechanics of adding risk via a covered strangle structure when a position moves against you. The strategy combines a long equity position with a ratio covered call above and a cash-secured put below, allowing disciplined re-entry on downside moves while maintaining defined risk parameters.

## Key takeaways

- **Full coverage strangle structure** [00:00]: Long equity + ratio covered call (upside) + cash-secured put (downside) creates a complete risk-defined framework for adding to losing positions.
- **Patience is critical** [00:00]: The strategy only works if you can wait for the downside trigger; adding risk on the way down requires the psychological discipline that the strangle structure provides.
- **Scenario-dependent risk addition** [00:00]: This is the only scenario where the trader feels comfortable adding risk on a downside move—the structure itself enforces that discipline.

## Notable quotes

> "There's no other scenario that I would feel comfortable adding risk on the way down but in this scenario I do feel comfortable—you just have to be patient."
