# How to Actually Make Money Trading Options with Market Effects & Profit Mechanisms

## Summary

This video reframes options trading away from technical indicators and strategy selection toward identifying and monetizing **market effects** and **profit mechanisms**. The core thesis: most retail traders focus on the wrong problems (RSI, DTE, strike selection) when they should first identify a reliable, explainable market pattern, then design an options strategy to extract it. The outlier strategy process places profit-mechanism research in stages 1–2, before any options structure is chosen.

## Key takeaways

- **Market effects vs. profit mechanisms** [02:39]: A market effect is a pattern or feature that impacts the market; a profit mechanism is a market effect that can be monetized by retail traders. Not all market effects are equally accessible.
- **Wrong focus kills edge** [00:00–01:19]: Spending time on RSI, strike selection, DTE, or "best stock under $100" is misdirected effort. These questions come *after* identifying a profit mechanism.
- **Post-earnings announcement drift (PEAD)** [05:20, 08:05–10:38]: Stocks often continue in the direction of earnings surprise; this is driven by systematic investor underreaction. Researchable via backtesting across EPS beat/miss, gap behavior, and multi-week follow-through.
- **Variance risk premium (VRP)** [05:20–06:39]: Implied volatility often trades above realized volatility (e.g., in [[iwm]]); this premium is not constant—periods exist where it's more or less favorable. Detecting these periods is "the entire game."
- **Research process** [08:05–09:18]: Use Google and SSRN to find studies on a candidate effect; upload them to an AI tool to correlate findings; conduct backtests, live tests, and forward tests to quantify the effect across multiple securities and time horizons.
- **Logical explanation required** [10:38]: Free money doesn't exist. You must explain *why* a profit mechanism persists (e.g., PEAD exists because investors underreact to information). This reasoning validates the edge.
- **Options strategy comes last** [08:05]: Once you understand the profit mechanism, choose an options structure (short put, long call, call spread, etc.) that maximally extracts it. The structure is a *vehicle*, not the foundation.
- **Outlier strategy process** [07:20–08:05]: Stages 1–2 are profit-mechanism identification and signal analysis; options selection is stage 3+. This inverts the typical retail approach.

## Notable quotes

> "It doesn't matter if you know how to trade a put credit spread. If this stock doesn't go the direction that you want and if you have no reliable method to determine probabilistically a direction that it might go specific to that, you don't always have to pick direction." [05:20]

> "This is the entire game of being a trader. It's not spending all of your time obsessing over what DTE is best." [06:39]

> "The purpose of an option strategy is to figure out how to maximally extract a profit mechanism. But you have to identify and understand what a profit mechanism is first." [08:05]
