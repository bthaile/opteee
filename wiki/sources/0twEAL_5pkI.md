# Avoid Random Trade Management #optionstrading

## Summary

This video critiques generic trade-management rules (e.g., "sell at 50% profit," "never risk more than 1% per trade," "always roll for credit") as context-blind advice. The core argument: effective trade management must align with the specific profit mechanism you're trading, not follow universal heuristics.

## Key takeaways

- **Generic rules are random** [00:00–00:24]: Common trade-management maxims (50% exit, 1% risk cap, half-sell strategies, credit-only rolls) are often heard but lack specificity to your actual strategy.
- **Profit mechanism drives management** [00:24]: The reason generic advice fails is that different profit mechanisms behave differently; your trade management must be tailored to what you're actually trying to capture.
- **Context matters** [00:00]: Any single rule might work in isolation, but applying unrelated rules together creates incoherent decision-making.

## Notable quotes

> "All of this is advice that you probably heard in different places that is completely random. In reality, the reason why this doesn't make sense is the specific profit mechanism that you're trying to trade behaves differently."
