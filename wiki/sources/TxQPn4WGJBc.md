# Kris Abdelmessih: How to Profit Using Volatility in Options Trading | The Outlier Podcast

## Summary

Kris Abdelmessih, founder of Moon Tower AI, discusses his evolution from SIG proprietary trader to independent volatility specialist. The conversation centers on how to identify mispricings in options markets through coherent measurement frameworks, the critical distinction between implied and realized volatility, and practical approaches for retail traders to extract edge from the options surface without competing on scale.

## Key takeaways

### Institutional foundations & risk philosophy
- **Measurement, not prediction** [10:13]: The core principle is normalizing all market observations to a common scale so anomalies become visible. For example, divide every price move by its implied volatility to see which moves are truly outsized.
- **Respect for market signals** [04:00–05:06]: SIG's eight-year curriculum teaches that bids and offers contain information; traders must be humble about what they know while maintaining conviction to act.
- **Two-tier risk management** [23:22–26:51]: Separate local risk (daily VAR-style metrics) from shock risk (what happens if correlations spike to 1.0 or oil crashes 50%). Gross exposure matters when assumptions break.

### Path dependency & feedback loops
- **Getting kicked in the face** [17:59–21:15]: Losses become pattern libraries. The 2020–2021 SoftBank call-buying and meme-stock coordination taught market makers that their shared frameworks can be exploited; this pattern is now part of the risk library.
- **Attribution & interim feedback** [30:05–33:16]: For discretionary traders, normalize P&L to a benchmark (e.g., daily close delta hedging) and construct vega-neutral baskets to isolate stock-picking skill without waiting for 15,000 trades.

### Volatility surface as information
- **IV ÷ 16 = expected daily move** [52:32]: A simple rule to size positions relative to realized variance expectations.
- **Term structure signals events** [53:39]: If VIX term structure is ascending (normal) but a stock's is descending, an event (earnings, conference) is likely priced in.
- **Skew reveals stress direction** [53:39–55:54]: Inverted skew (calls richer than puts) signals upside stress; normal skew signals downside stress. Meme stocks show inverted skew because the market doesn't know how high they can go.
- **Put spread pricing as sentiment** [55:54–57:00]: Expensive put spreads mean the market expects the stock to be lower in one year; cheap put spreads mean it expects stability or upside.

### Retail-specific optionality
- **Synthetic futures via options** [47:07–50:19]: A one-year synthetic short (sell call, buy put) on USO collects 3% instead of paying 6% to short shares. No delta hedging needed; the market maker's funding rate is embedded in the option price.
- **Volatility is not a directional bet** [01:15:34–01:23:17]: Low IV does not mean "buy" and high IV does not mean "sell." Implied volatility is forward-looking and mean-reverting; realized volatility is backward-looking and mean-reverting in opposite direction. Low IV often has negative carry (theta decay works against you); high IV often has positive carry when sold.
- **Contrarian framework** [01:21:13–01:22:16]: When everyone sees a low number and buys, consider selling—but only after walking through the arithmetic of the trade structure.

### Optionality as a concept
- **Kinks in the curve** [01:10:01]: Look for situations where payoff changes discontinuously (e.g., coal-switching support in natural gas, M&A bids in stocks). These embedded options are often sold cheap by natural hedgers.
- **Refinery as an option** [01:07:40–01:08:52]: A refiner has optionality to run more crude when spreads are wide and less when they're tight—a put-like payoff that's often underpriced because refiners are natural sellers.

### Open interest & gamma
- **Concentration matters more than totals** [59:06–01:00:07]: Track where chunky trades landed and how concentrated OI is on strikes, especially near expiration when dollar gamma becomes large. Six months out, OI concentration is less predictive.
- **Vanna/Charm complexity** [01:01:09–01:03:09]: These flows are real but overlapping with skew signals. Market makers running dispersion books (long single-stock gamma, short index gamma) mean the full ledger is more complex than published gamma alone suggests.

## Notable quotes
