# Post Earnings Announcement Drift #optionstrading

## Summary

Post-earnings announcement drift (PEAD) is the tendency for stock prices to continue moving in the direction of an earnings surprise for days or weeks after the announcement. The key driver is not the reported EPS number itself, but forward guidance—the market's true unknown. Understanding this mechanic allows traders to structure options positions that capture drift moves beyond the initial earnings volatility event.

## Key takeaways

- **EPS vs. forward guidance:** The reported EPS number is only a small part of the equation; forward guidance is the true unknown that drives post-announcement price action [00:00–00:27].
- **One-time expenses don't move markets:** A company can miss EPS due to a one-time charge, but if the underlying business is sound, the market may ignore it [00:27].
- **Weak guidance kills rallies:** Strong EPS can be completely negated by weak forward guidance, causing sharp reversals [00:27].
- **PEAD as a trading edge:** The drift phase after earnings offers a distinct window for options strategies that capture continuation moves beyond the initial IV crush.

## Notable quotes
