# Massive GME Option Volatility

## Summary

GameStop's near-term option chain exhibits extreme call-side implied volatility skew (272% IV on 21 June expiration vs. 235% Friday close), pricing in significant upside expectation despite the catalyst being a shareholder meeting rather than earnings. The video breaks down why far out-of-the-money options appear cheap but are structurally difficult to profit from due to rapid theta decay, low delta, and the need for large, fast moves.

## Key takeaways

- **Unusual IV structure for non-earnings event** [01:34–02:00]: GME's 21 June expiration jumped from ~235% IV Friday to ~272% Sunday, an anomalous move for a shareholders meeting (typically seen in biotech FDA events, not equity meetings).

- **Massive call skew vs. normal put skew** [02:43–04:30]: GME shows inverted volatility skew—out-of-the-money calls trade much higher IV than OTM puts. SPX and normal markets show the opposite (puts higher IV), reflecting typical downside-risk premium. This call skew signals market expectation of upside move this week.

- **Roaring Kitty exercise speculation** [05:37–06:13]: Based on extrinsic value remaining (~$3–4M loss if exercised), likely did not exercise his calls; no clear massive catalyst visible yet, though squeeze dynamics can build over time.

- **Far OTM options are a trap for retail** [07:38–12:17]: Buying cheap far-OTM calls (e.g., $0.28 premium, 5 DTE) requires massive fast moves; theta decay is ~$0.15/day (>50% of premium), cutting profit probability sharply. Theta decay accelerates within 60 days to expiration.

- **Delta increases with moneyness** [14:28–15:10]: Far OTM $42 calls have ~0.24 delta (24¢ move per $1 underlying move); ATM calls ~0.50 delta; ITM calls ~0.80 delta. Paying more for higher delta reduces leverage but improves probability of profit per dollar move.

- **Sophisticated approach: layered deltas** [15:29–16:06]: Mix slightly ITM calls (higher delta, better theta profile) with a few kicker OTM calls (0.20 delta) for upside optionality; manage actively rather than hold to expiration.
