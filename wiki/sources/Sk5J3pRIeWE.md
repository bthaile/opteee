# Do NOT Repeat Mistakes #investing #stockmarket #trading

## Summary
A foundational risk-management lesson: mistakes are inevitable in trading, but repeating them or allowing a single loss to blow up an account is not. The core discipline is to exit positions when losses become too large, assess what went wrong, and return to trading with lessons learned. Retail traders have no excuse for account destruction other than inattention.

## Key takeaways

- **Exit before catastrophic loss** [00:00] — Do not allow any single mistake to blow up your account; the market will extract money from inattentive traders every time.
- **Assess and reset** [00:16] — When losses become too large, exit the position, analyze what happened, and come back to trading with a clear head.
- **Accountability over blame** [00:00] — Mistakes are part of trading life; the discipline is in not repeating them and maintaining position-sizing discipline.
