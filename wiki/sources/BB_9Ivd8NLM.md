# The GME Options Trade — How I'm Playing Ryan Cohen's eBay Bid

## Summary

Eric walks through a catalyst-driven short-term long-call trade in GameStop following news of Ryan Cohen's eBay bid, executed on a 2.3 standard-deviation breakout. He details position construction (500 × 15 May $30 calls at ~$0.28, exited at ~$0.59 for ~111% return), stop-loss placement, and multi-timeframe management. He then pivots to variance risk premium plays—short straddles and short strangles in both GME and eBay—exploiting elevated implied volatility and mean-reverting vol behavior across the catalyst event.

## Key takeaways

- **Catalyst-driven entry via alerts** [00:57–01:43]: A 2.3 standard-deviation move triggered automated unusual-options-activity alerts; Eric had no prior knowledge of the eBay bid news but recognized the signal pattern in a name he tracks.

- **Position structure: low-delta OTM calls for gamma** [05:37–06:17]: Built 500 × 15 May $30 calls (delta ~15–20) at ~$0.28 avg cost (~$14k total) to capture big moves while minimizing extrinsic decay over a weekend. Avoided ATM to reduce theta bleed on an uncertain duration.

- **Dual soft stops + hard stop via sizing** [08:11–09:01]: First soft stop at $25.25 (meaningful return below breakout); second at $25.00 (close below pre-move consolidation). Hard stop is position size (~0.5% of portfolio). Exited at 12:50 PT (~$0.59) when 5-minute moving averages violated, yielding ~$15k profit.

- **Multi-timeframe management: 1-min composition, 5-min consolidation** [06:38–11:57]: Used 1-minute bars to read move composition but relied on 5-minute for stop placement and consolidation decisions to avoid noise. Trailed stops up as price advanced.

- **Variance risk premium: short vol in elevated IV environment** [15:00–17:24]: GME and eBay both show elevated IV percentile post-catalyst. Eric plays short straddles/strangles in the front (9–16 DTE, not 43 DTE) to capture mean-reverting vol while limiting exposure to announcement risk. Acknowledges high gamma management burden in front month.

- **Directional vs. volatility paradigm** [17:50–18:10]: Acquirer (eBay) typically declines; target (GME) typically rises. Eric blends directional plays with vol-premium harvesting because both edges exist simultaneously in M&A events.

- **Low hit rate, asymmetric sizing** [04:30–05:13]: This strategy fails ~40–60% of the time; recent 2-month hit rate ~65%. Sizing is tiny relative to portfolio to accommodate the low win rate while capturing asymmetric upside on winners.

## Notable quotes
