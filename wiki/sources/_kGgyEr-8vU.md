# Retail Vol Adjusted Sizing HACK

## Summary

This video introduces volatility-capped position sizing, a technique borrowed from institutional trading to help retail traders manage portfolio risk. By setting a target volatility cap (e.g., 40% expected move) and sizing each individual trade to respect that cap, traders can maintain consistent risk exposure across their portfolio without over-leveraging on high-conviction positions.

## Key takeaways

- **Set a target volatility cap** [00:00] — Choose a portfolio-wide volatility target (e.g., 40%) based on your risk tolerance, plan, and approach; this becomes your sizing constraint.
- **Size individual trades to the cap** [00:25] — Each trade is sized so that its expected P&L volatility over its life does not exceed the target; this prevents any single position from dominating portfolio risk.
- **Portfolio-level alternative** [00:48] — An advanced variant applies the same logic across the entire portfolio simultaneously, requiring existing positions to be sized down as new trades are added; this is more complex but tighter.
- **Emphasis on individual trades** [00:25] — The retail-friendly approach focuses on capping each trade independently, avoiding the need to rebalance the entire portfolio on every new entry.
