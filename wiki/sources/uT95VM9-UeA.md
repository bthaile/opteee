# REDUNDANCY #investing #optionstrading #stockmarket

## Summary

Eric discusses the importance of building financial redundancy beyond a single skill set. Rather than relying solely on trading expertise, he emphasizes diversifying income sources—including commercial real estate—to reduce long-term risk and ensure sustained wealth generation across multiple decades.

## Key takeaways

- **Don't conflate craft with purpose** [00:25]: The goal is to make money and reduce risk, not to remain attached to any single skill or method. Trading is a tool, not an identity.
- **Build redundancies in your system** [00:43]: Once you've created wealth, establish multiple income streams to hedge against skill obsolescence or market regime changes.
- **Skill sets have expiration dates** [00:25]: Even well-developed trading expertise may be phased out by technological or market shifts over a 60-year horizon; plan accordingly.
- **Portfolio-first thinking** [00:00]: Allocate capital to the highest-return, lowest-risk opportunities available—whether that's trading, real estate, or other vehicles—rather than defaulting to your strongest existing skill.
