# Fed Rate Cut Implications | Stock Market News

## Summary

The Federal Reserve cut rates by 50 basis points on September 18, 2024—a significant acceleration from the market's prior expectation of 25 bps. While inflation metrics appear controlled and unemployment remains strong, the late-week pivot to a larger cut signals underlying economic concern and raises recession probability questions. The market exhibited sharp intraday volatility, rallying hard on the announcement then retracing nearly to pre-cut levels, reflecting conflicted sentiment about the path forward.

## Key takeaways

- **50 bps cut signals caution despite strong data** [00:27–01:24]: Core PCE and unemployment data suggest inflation is under control and the economy is strong, yet the Fed's sudden pivot from 25 bps to 50 bps—inverted in probability only ~1 week prior—indicates the Fed may be responding to market stress or hidden economic weakness rather than following a pre-planned path.

- **Market euphoria followed by sharp retracement** [03:03–04:18]: Russell 2000 rallied from ~2219.5 to ~2287 on the announcement, then retraced nearly to pre-cut levels intraday. This conflicted price action suggests the market is simultaneously pricing in rate relief and grappling with recession risk.

- **Recession probability remains material** [04:42–05:29]: JP Morgan (as of mid-August) estimated ~45% probability of recession by end of 2025; separate data shows ~62% probability by August 2025. These are not negligible odds, and the soft-landing narrative may be overstated.

- **No historical precedent for current scenario** [05:29–05:51]: With rates having been near 0% for an extended period, there is no clean historical comparison for how equity markets typically behave after rate-cut campaigns in this regime, making forward guidance speculative.

- **Tactical approach: scaling and stop discipline** [06:16–07:00]: Eric scaled into a Russell position ahead of the announcement and ran stops to manage intraday retracement risk. He anticipated some pullback but was surprised by the near-full retracement, underscoring the importance of position sizing and stop placement in volatile macro events.

- **Earnings season and bank guidance next focus** [08:05–08:23]: October earnings, particularly from banks in the second week, will be critical for understanding what corporate America expects in a lower-rate, higher-recession-risk environment.
