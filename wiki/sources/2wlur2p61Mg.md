# GameStop Weekly Update – Store Closures, Analyst Estimates & Options Flow

## Summary

GameStop is consolidating sideways after a multi-stage decline, showing relative strength versus small caps and the broader market despite a ~10% move. Options flow remains bullish with elevated net call premium and strong call skew; most positioning is in near-term expirations (7 March) and longer-dated 2026 contracts. Recent news on store closures, international divestment, and negative analyst EPS estimates for FY2425 reflect the company's restructuring, though improving price-to-sales ratios suggest emerging value.

## Key takeaways

- **Price consolidation & relative strength** [00:00–02:07]: GME has declined ~10% from ~$27 to ~$25 but is outperforming small caps (IWM) and large caps (SPX) over the same period; the decline is occurring in stages (down, consolidation, sideways drift) rather than parabolic, indicating market support.
- **Volatility stabilization despite broader market weakness** [02:07–03:19]: While S&P 500 ATR has nearly doubled (1.16 → 2.3 over 90 days), GME's 20-day ATR (5%) and 5-day ATR (4.7%) show relative stability, suggesting GME volatility is not accelerating as sharply as the broader market.
- **Bullish options flow & net call premium spike** [03:19–05:47]: Net call premium spiked today to levels not seen since late April; 72,000 calls vs. 17,000 puts traded, with 44% bought at ask or above (vs. typical 40% midpoint), indicating strong upside speculation.
- **Longer-dated positioning dominates** [04:30–05:47]: Most open interest is in 2026 expirations and 28 March (earnings cycle), not short-term front-month plays; this reflects expectations of company value growth rather than short-term price distortions.
- **Earnings volatility & IV elevation** [04:30]: 28 March expiration shows elevated implied volatility ahead of earnings (slated 25 March after close); 28,000 of 72,000 call volume is in 7 March front month.
- **Store closure & international divestment strategy** [07:03–08:18]: GameStop is closing unprofitable stores across multiple states and divesting international operations (Canada ~5% of sales, France); this reflects disciplined capital reallocation toward profitable segments.
- **Analyst estimates & valuation** [08:18–09:43]: Most analysts project negative EPS for FY2425, but some are eyeing profitability expansion toward 2025; price-to-sales ratios are beginning to show value opportunity despite revenue contraction.
