# RISK #stockmarket #investing #trading

## Summary

A brief motivational piece on the necessity of embracing calculated risk-taking to build wealth. The core argument: fear of loss prevents traders and investors from capitalizing on genuine opportunities, and the ability to generate future capital makes present risk-taking rational.

## Key takeaways

- **Embrace risk as a prerequisite for wealth building** [00:00] — excessive caution leaves money on the table and prevents participation in outsized opportunities.
- **Opportunity cost dominates regret** [00:10] — missing a high-conviction trade is often costlier than taking a calculated loss; capital can be regenerated, but equivalent opportunities may not recur.
- **Reframe fear through regenerative capacity** [00:15] — remind yourself that you can always make more money; this mental model unlocks the ability to act when conviction is high.

## Notable quotes

> "If you are too afraid to take a risk you're gonna have a really hard time getting rich."

> "Sometimes there's a big opportunity you got to take advantage of it while it's there even though you might be a little bit scared."
