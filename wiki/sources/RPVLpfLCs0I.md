# Build a Robust Trading Plan

## Summary

A trading plan must be grounded in real analysis and reasoning, not superficial rules. The primary value of a trading plan lies not in the document itself but in the disciplined planning exercise—the process of thinking through your approach end-to-end before entering positions.

## Key takeaways

- **Plans built on hollow reasoning collapse under pressure** [00:27] — if you cannot articulate *why* you exit at 50% profit or any other rule, your plan is fragile.
- **The planning exercise is the primary purpose** [00:51] — the act of building an end-to-end analysis of your trading approach is more valuable than the final document.
- **Robust plans require real analysis** [00:27] — avoid superficial slogans like "trade your plan, plan your trade" unless backed by genuine edge and reasoning.

## Notable quotes

> "If you build something that's made out of papier-mâché, don't be surprised if it crumbles under the slightest pressure."
