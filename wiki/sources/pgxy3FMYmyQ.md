# theta gang

## Summary

Eric critiques the "Theta Gang" approach—selling premium exclusively via strategies like the wheel and covered strangle—as myopic and opportunity-limiting, especially for active traders. While passive, hands-off premium selling has merit, professional traders who restrict themselves to one side of the market (short volatility only) leave significant edge on the table, particularly during rallies when long-dated calls get run over.

## Key takeaways

- **The core flaw of Theta Gang:** selling premium exclusively misses binary events and directional opportunities [00:00–00:22]
- **Covered call and covered strangle risks:** during market rallies, short calls get assigned or capped, limiting upside participation [00:00–00:22]
- **Passive vs. active trade-off:** hands-off strategies like the wheel work for lower-activity traders, but come with explicit opportunity cost [00:22–00:44]
- **Professional traders need optionality:** restricting to one side of the market (short vol only) leaves "way too many opportunities on the table" [00:44]
- **Context matters:** strategy choice depends on activity level and professional vs. retail intent [00:22–00:44]

## Notable quotes

> "one of the fatal flaws of a place like Theta Gang is they're myopic. they will only sell premium and like anything that becomes binary you tend to miss a lot of opportunities"
