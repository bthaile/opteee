# Selling Vol & Delta Neutrality #optionstrading

## Summary

When selling options into high implied volatility, traders face two dominant forces: delta (directional movement) and vega (volatility movement). Simply selling and holding until expiration does not isolate volatility exposure; instead, the position becomes enslaved to delta risk. Effective volatility selling requires active delta management to truly capture the volatility premium.

## Key takeaways

- **Two dominant forces in short-vol trades** [00:00]: Delta (directional) and vega (volatility) are the primary Greeks affecting short-option positions; ignoring one leaves you exposed to the other.
- **Passive short-vol is delta-dependent** [00:00–00:31]: Selling options and waiting until expiration without rehedging does not isolate volatility; you remain exposed to whatever directional movement occurs.
- **Volatility movement is secondary** [00:31]: Whether IV rises or falls during the trade is manageable, but delta exposure is the constraint that forces active management.
