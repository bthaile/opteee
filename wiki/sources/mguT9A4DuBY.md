# The BEST 2 Option Strategies for SMALL Accounts

## Summary

For accounts below $150k–$200k, capital preservation and skill development take priority over raw returns. Eric presents two core strategies: the [[covered-strangle]] for building diversified core positions in index/sector ETFs, and the [[ratio-call-diagonal]] for directional, capital-efficient trades. The video emphasizes that avoiding [[drawdown|drawdowns]] is as important as generating returns, and that small accounts must prioritize [[position-sizing]] and [[sector-rotation]] to find tradeable products.

## Key takeaways

- **Capital preservation > returns for small accounts** [01:51–02:40]: A 30% return on a $5,500 account yields only $1,650; even a 10% return is just $550. The real priority is saving money and avoiding losses, not chasing outsized percentage gains.

- **Drawdowns are catastrophic at small scale** [03:21–04:09]: A 4% drawdown requires a 66.7% gain to break even. Portfolio 1 outperformed Portfolio 3 despite lower average returns, because it avoided a 5% loss in year three—demonstrating that [[sequence-of-returns]] matters more than average return.

- **Covered strangle: core-building, forgiving, diversified** [05:11–06:47]: Structure is [[cash-secured-put]] → assignment → [[covered-call]] + additional puts. Works best on index/sector ETFs (e.g., [[xlf]]) or strong S&P 500 stocks. Forgiving because assignment of a quality asset is acceptable; offers inherent diversification. Drawback: capital-intensive and slow to work.

- **Ratio diagonal: directional, capital-efficient, defined risk** [05:11–08:01]: Long call/put + short call/put, typically deep ITM and 3+ months out to limit [[theta]] decay. Requires correct direction *and* timing. Allocate up to 75% of portfolio (vs. 100% for covered strangle) until you prove directional skill.

- **Sector rotation + stock screening for covered strangle** [09:00–09:40]: Identify top-performing sectors (past 3 months), then scan S&P 500 for stocks <$50 with EPS/revenue growth. Example: Wells Fargo in financial sector.

- **ETFs solve small-account diversification problem** [10:18–10:57]: Sector ETFs like [[xlf]] ($30) offer exposure to entire sectors without centralizing risk in one stock. Scan for ETFs <$50 trading in top 20% of category; Spiders ETFs are ideal for small accounts.

- **Ratio diagonal doesn't require product hunting** [11:13]: Many products are approachable for small accounts with ratio diagonals; the constraint is directional skill, not capital.

## Notable quotes

> "You can win in trading via three things: create better returns, which is very difficult but doable up to a certain degree, and avoiding drawdowns. Those two things combined are actually a really efficacious way to make a lot of money trading." [04:32]
