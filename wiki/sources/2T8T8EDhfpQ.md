# Rebalance Your Deltas in a Covered Call #optionstrading

## Summary

When selling covered calls, traders often neglect to rebalance their delta exposure as the underlying price moves. By establishing a delta band around your initial short strike (e.g., 30–40 delta) and rebalancing when the position drifts 10–15 deltas outside that range, you preserve the income-generation effect of the short call and avoid letting the position become too far out-of-the-money, where additional downside moves yield minimal P&L.

## Key takeaways

- **Delta decay in covered calls** [00:00–00:22]: When you sell a 27-delta call and the underlying drops, your delta exposure shrinks (e.g., to 17 delta). This means each additional dollar of downside generates less profit, eroding the short call's protective income effect.
- **Create a delta band** [00:40]: Establish a rebalancing range around your target delta (e.g., 30–40 delta). When the position drifts 10–15 deltas outside that band, rebalance by rolling or adjusting.
- **Proactive management vs. passive decay** [00:40]: Rather than sitting idle while deltas drift far out-of-the-money, actively manage the position to maintain consistent income generation and hedge effectiveness.

## Notable quotes

> "If you typically sell a 30 delta, 40 delta, and you see it start moving, you know, 10 deltas, 15 deltas outside that range, then you can rebalance it."
