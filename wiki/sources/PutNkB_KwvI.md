# Analyzing Roaring Kitty's Trades Pt3 | GameStop Analysis Live Ep11

## Summary

Eric conducts a detailed forensic analysis of Roaring Kitty's (Keith Gill's) options trading activity throughout January 2021, tracking position entries, exits, and cash flows across his account. The session reveals patterns of profit-taking on longer-dated calls, unusual position management decisions (holding out-of-the-money calls while exiting in-the-money ones), and sustained large cash balances. Eric emphasizes that replicating Gill's approach is impractical due to his anomalous psychology and risk tolerance, while acknowledging both skill and luck in his trading outcomes.

## Key takeaways

- **Position tracking methodology** [08:36]–[09:06]: Eric is analyzing each of Gill's trade updates chronologically to identify management patterns, particularly around position exits and time-based decision-making rather than price targets.

- **Suboptimal options management on 5 Jan 2021** [14:29]–[15:46]: Gill exited 1,000 15-Jan-21 $10 calls (higher delta, lower theta decay) while keeping 1,000 $20 calls (lower delta, higher theta decay at ~$6K/day vs. $3K/day). This decision was counterintuitive across all risk/reward metrics.

- **Theta decay impact quantified** [25:41]–[26:17]: Between 31 Dec 2020 and 11 Jan 2021, the $20 calls lost ~$150K in value despite GameStop trading at nearly identical prices, demonstrating pure theta erosion.

- **Profit-taking pattern emerges** [39:17]–[40:44]: On 25 Jan 2021 (81 DTE remaining), Gill scaled out 200 of 1,000 16-April-21 $12 calls during an uptrend—the first clear evidence of profit-taking on longer-dated options rather than time-decay management.

- **Scaling out behavior** [51:59]–[52:34]: Between 25–29 Jan 2021, Gill withdrew small amounts ($10K–$20K) multiple times while reducing call positions by 300 contracts total, suggesting disciplined partial profit-taking.

- **Large cash positions maintained** [42:52]–[43:15]: Gill held $2.6M+ in cash throughout January despite having sufficient capital to deploy, indicating either optionality preservation or lack of immediate deployment thesis.

- **Why replication fails** [32:34]–[36:02]: Gill's psychology is anomalous—he tolerates concentrated risk, holds through 50%+ portfolio drawdowns, uses no stops, and appears indifferent to money beyond family security. Copying his trades without adopting his entire worldview will fail.

- **Luck vs. skill in 15-Jan-21 $20 calls** [44:17]–[45:34]: Gill held these calls to 1 DTE, realizing $1M+ profit. However, on 11 Jan they showed only $122K gain; the $900K swing came from a fortuitous price spike two days before expiration—demonstrating the role of timing luck.

- **Dark pool order analysis** [02:05:43]–[02:15:39]: RC Ventures' purchases (36,847 shares on 10 June 2024 and 253,000 on 9 June 2023) were broken into multiple fills rather than single blocks, indicating no evidence of internalized order flow suppression.

- **Volume distribution patterns** [02:16:05]–[02:16:47]: Pre- and post-market volume spikes are normal institutional behavior; intraday volume typically dips midday. Triple witching effects are less significant now due to consolidated expirations.
