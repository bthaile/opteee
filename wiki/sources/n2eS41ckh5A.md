# GME Option Expiration and BBBY Case

## Summary

Eric reviews GameStop's options expiration on August 16, 2024, noting hundreds of thousands of out-of-the-money calls expired worthless. He also previews recent Bed Bath & Beyond litigation documents featuring Ryan Cohen's deposition testimony. The stock trades sideways on light volume with notably depressed implied volatility in the earnings expiration cycle relative to subsequent weeks—a potential trading opportunity.

## Key takeaways

- **Options expiration recap** [00:23]: Hundreds of thousands of OTM calls expired worthless on August 16; volume in the next expiration (Aug 23) is significantly lighter than typical post-expiration rollover activity.
- **GME price action** [07:30]: Stock consolidating sideways; volume contracted to ~3.6M vs. 20-day average of ~7.3M; small-cap index showing positive momentum provides tailwind.
- **IV anomaly as trade setup** [10:55]: August 23 expiration (earnings cycle) trading at ~84% IV vs. September 13 at ~92% IV—a ~10-point spread that is historically anomalous and tradable; Eric expects IV expansion.
- **Aug 23 call volume** [09:08]: Largest single trade was 5,273 contracts; total OTM call volume ~150K expired; replacement volume in Aug 23 is minimal, suggesting limited new positioning.
- **BBBY litigation update** [01:02]: Cohen deposition released; key moment: when asked if consultants (BCG) delivered on contractual obligations, Cohen responded they "drove the company off a cliff" and "get an A+ for that"—a strategically smart answer that sidesteps contractual liability by focusing on outcome.
- **Deposition tactics explained** [04:34]: Attorneys use repetitive questioning and document contradictions to box witnesses into locked-in testimony before trial; high-profile cases involve elite law-firm attorneys with millions in experience.
