# BIG Call Buying in GME

## Summary

Market update covering macro conditions (CPI, PPI, Fed rate-cut probabilities) and detailed GME options activity on expiration day. Large call purchases across multiple expirations (16 Aug, 16 Jan 26) signal bullish positioning; open interest and volume patterns show sustained call-side accumulation despite elevated implied volatility (~140% on 19 July expiration).

## Key takeaways

- **Macro context** [00:45–02:46]: PPI came in hot at 2.6% (vs. 2.3% forecast); Michigan sentiment light at 66 (vs. 68.5 expected). Fed Watch Tool shows 18 September cut probability has "skyrocketed." Russell 2000 rallied ~127 points in 3 days (2036→2163); TLT similarly strong—watch for small-cap and bond rotation if cuts materialize.

- **GME price structure** [03:06–05:09]: Positive drift continues from 1 July low; volume died off today but price held floor (no capitulation selling). Weekly expirations typically quiet; next week's IV jumped to ~140% vs. surrounding cycles at ~121—market pricing larger move expectation for 19 July expiration.

- **Large call transactions** [07:02–08:49]:
  - 16 Aug 24.30 calls: 998 contracts sold at bid (263¢), ~$262k notional—likely covered calls.
  - 16 Aug 20 calls: 546 contracts, ~$368k.
  - 16 Jan 26 20 calls: 483 contracts at 1330¢, ~$642k position.
  - Pattern: money flowing into 20-strike calls across multiple expirations (Jan 25, Jan 26, Aug 16).

- **Open interest & volume** [10:33–12:29]: Call OI expanded ~94k contracts vs. put OI rise of ~25k—widening call-lean bias. 19 July 30 calls: 3,469 volume with 60k OI; 25 calls: 28k OI. 125 calls (far OTM): 56k OI, 5,600 volume today, +2,700 net new positions. Persistent accumulation despite zero extrinsic value if expired OTM.

- **Trader positioning** [13:12–13:29]: Host reduced 16 Aug exposure (took profit on decent price), maintaining share exposure, rolling out to 20 September and 18 October for longer-dated upside.

- **Mindset note** [03:28–04:29]: Price action remains relevant even in heavily-manipulated names; balance skepticism with information gathering rather than binary dismissal.
