# Failure rate #optionstrading

## Summary

Eric discusses why the commonly cited 95% trader failure rate is misleading and what actually separates successful traders from the rest. Success in trading requires treating it with appropriate respect, putting in sustained work over years, and doing things differently than the majority—it is simple in concept but difficult in execution.

## Key takeaways

- **The 95% failure metric is deceiving** [00:00] — most traders enter expecting fast, easy money without understanding the actual time and effort required.
- **Respect the craft** [00:25] — one of the healthiest approaches is to treat trading with the seriousness it deserves; this alone improves odds of success.
- **Become an outlier** [00:47] — to succeed, you must do things differently than other people and invest enough work to achieve different outcomes.
- **Simple but not easy** [00:47] — the principles are straightforward, but execution and discipline over years (Eric's 18 years cited) separate winners from the 95%.

## Notable quotes

> "In order to be successful as a Trader you literally have to be an outlier—it means that you have to do things differently than other people and it means that you have to put in enough work to have a different outcome from other people."
