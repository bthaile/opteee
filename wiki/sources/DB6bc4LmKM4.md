# GameStop Stock Overview and Trade Adjustments

## Summary

Eric reviews GameStop's recent sharp selloff, analyzing technical support levels, trend breaks, and the collapse in options volatility. He then walks through his live covered-strangle trade adjustments—reducing share basis, consolidating short puts, and maintaining capital efficiency to preserve upside participation while managing downside risk.

## Key takeaways

- **Technical breakdown [00:58–02:14]**: GME broke its prior uptrend line; a new steep downtrend is now in play. Key support sits at the bottom of the large volume node; below that is a significant gap with light volume. RSI is below 50 and negatively sloped—bearish setup, though GME frequently violates standard technical analysis at key inflection points.

- **Volatility collapse [02:14–03:20]**: Front-month expiration (24 Jan) trading at ~87% IV; 7 Feb showing slight uptick to 86.5% IV, suggesting market positioning for potential event. No confirmed news catalyst identified.

- **Options chain structure [04:25–06:36]**: Heavy call skew (523k calls vs. 172k puts). Bulk concentrated in 24 Jan (~91k) and 31 Jan (~70k); secondary concentrations at 17 April and 20 June. Unusual options activity shows small-to-medium trades; no major institutional flow detected.

- **Trade adjustment rationale [07:42–08:49]**: Reduced equity position from 300 to 200 shares (sold 100 at $28.90, basis $24.31, realized $460 P&L) to lighten exposure during uncertain downtrend. Consolidated short puts to regain spot-price sensitivity as 28-strike approached ITM and 26-strike neared ATM.

- **Position structure [09:51–11:04]**: Now holding 200 shares (basis $28.27) + short 1× 21 Feb 35-call (delta 35, premium $1.93) + 5× 21 Feb 25-puts (sold for $0.06 each). Total realized P&L $1,070; total P&L including open options $1,633. Capital utilization only 72.6%, preserving dry powder to scale on further moves.

- **Compounding via realized gains [11:04]**: Realized capital ($1,070) can be redeployed to buy more GME without fresh cash injection—key to scaling the position sustainably.
