# Selling Options With High IV - What Could Go Wrong?

## Summary

A cautionary exploration of the common options-selling maxim "sell when IV is high." While elevated implied volatility does offer higher premiums and appears to offer mean-reversion edge, the transcript warns that high IV does not guarantee further compression—volatility can spike further, invalidating the assumed edge and exposing sellers to outsized losses.

## Key takeaways

- **The two-part pitch for selling high IV:** higher premiums collected, and the expectation that IV will mean-revert [00:00–00:20]
- **IV can continue to rise even when already elevated:** S&P volatility reached 63% on Nov 4, then climbed to 90.7% IV percentile within a month—demonstrating that "high" is not a ceiling [00:20–00:49]
- **Just because volatility is high doesn't mean it can't go higher:** the core risk of selling premium into what feels like an extreme [00:49]
