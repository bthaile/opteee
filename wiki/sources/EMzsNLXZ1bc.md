# Chris from projectfinance Pt2 | Money Talks

## Summary

Chris discusses his channel rebrand from Project Option to Project Finance, expanding beyond options trading into broader investing and personal finance topics. The conversation centers on the current investment environment where low-risk assets offer negative real returns, forcing capital into higher-risk assets (stocks, real estate, crypto). Chris shares his core strategy of buying far out-of-the-money long calls on conviction names, converting profitable positions into risk-free call spreads by shorting higher strikes, and managing these positions with patience and proper position sizing.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2021-04-27)

- **Negative real returns in low-risk assets** [05:29–08:28]: Cash and bond yields are below inflation expectations (~2%), creating negative real returns. Wealthfront dropped high-yield savings from 0.35% to 0.1%; 10-year bonds yield <2%; 30-year bonds ~2.25%. This forces investors into higher-risk assets.
- **Massive money supply expansion** [08:54–09:20]: Money supply has increased dramatically (described as "straight up line" on charts), diluting currency value and creating inflation fears of 3–5%+ rather than the Fed's 2% target.
- **Asset price inflation across the board** [10:30–12:35]: S&P 500 is 20%+ higher than pre-COVID despite incomplete economic reopening. Real estate prices up 12% year-over-year (Jan 2020–Jan 2021) vs. 6% the prior year. Properties in Denver selling 50–75k over asking within 5 days.
- **Bullish stance on risk assets** [12:35–13:31]: Given easy monetary policy and low rates, Chris is bullish on stocks, real estate, and crypto—anything not cash-denominated. Inflation erodes debt, making strategic leverage attractive.
- **Fed consistency and policy path** [15:31–18:26]: Fed has been consistent for ~8 quarters in executing stated policy. No viable alternatives exist; reversing course would shock the financial system. Fed minutes show no identified alternatives.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Long-call strategy with far expirations** [19:30–23:40]: Buy out-of-the-money calls 600–750 days out on conviction names (Tesla, Apple, Nvidia, SPY). Accept 100% loss risk but size positions at ~10% of account to preserve psychology and allow patience. Far expirations minimize theta impact and reduce pressure to be right immediately.
- **Converting to risk-free spreads** [23:40–26:12]: Once long calls become profitable, short a higher-strike call for equal or greater premium, creating a risk-free or profitable spread. Example: bought 900-strike Tesla calls for $100, shorted 1500-strike calls for $230, netting $130 credit.
- **Trading the short leg dynamically** [26:12–29:13]: After shorting the higher strike, you can trade in and out of that short call. If stock crashes, buy back the short for profit, reducing cost basis on the long call. If stock rallies, the spread appreciates. This preserves optionality with 600+ days remaining.
- **Spread width and time decay** [38:23–39:06]: Wider spreads (e.g., 900/1500 call spread) are preferable with 600+ days to expiration. Narrow spreads (900/950) are effectively just closing the original position and waste remaining time value.
- **Stock selection** [33:32–37:23]: Choose well-known, highly liquid names (Tesla, Apple, Nvidia, Square, Visa, SPY, QQQ, IWM). Avoid exotic or low-liquidity stocks. Conviction should span 1–2 years; don't expect immediate moves.
- **Volatility product trades** [42:16–46:00]: VXX (long-dated calls as portfolio insurance) and SVXY (inverse volatility, now reduced leverage post-volpocalypse). Recent VXX trade: bought 100 contracts of 10-strike puts at $0.17 when VIX was 20 but near-term futures at 25, expecting convergence. Closed for $1.00+, capturing the term structure collapse.
- **Bitcoin conviction and holding** [48:42–53:03]: Bought Bitcoin at $9–15k post-COVID crash with long-term conviction. Accepts total loss risk but believes Bitcoin has highest probability of being "next Amazon" over decades. Holds despite volatility; partial profit-taking not part of plan.
- **Bitcoin vs. gold comparison** [54:07–01:02:33]: Bitcoin has all gold's characteristics plus improvements (divisibility, portability, no physical use case needed). Gold down YTD while Bitcoin up 84% YTD; suggests Bitcoin is "eating gold's market share." Bitcoin custody via hardware wallet superior to physical gold storage; settlement speed and liquidity far exceed gold.
