# Measuring the Greek Charm

## Summary

Charm is a second-order Greek that measures the rate of change of delta with respect to the passage of time, analogous to how gamma measures delta's sensitivity to underlying price movement. As expiration approaches, in-the-money options gain delta while out-of-the-money options lose it, with the delta curve steepening toward the binary payoff at expiration.

## Key takeaways

- **Charm as second-order Greek** [00:00]: Charm measures delta's decay over time, just as gamma measures delta's change per point move in the underlying.
- **Delta behavior across time decay** [00:20]: In-the-money options gain delta as time passes and expiration approaches; the delta curve steepens progressively from ~325 days to ~31 days to expiration.
- **Binary convergence at expiration** [00:53]: At expiration, all in-the-money options converge to delta of 1.0 and all out-of-the-money options converge to delta of 0.0, regardless of how far out-of-the-money they are.
