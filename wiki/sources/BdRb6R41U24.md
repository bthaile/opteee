# Are You Choosing Your Trades Correctly? #optionstrading

## Summary

Eric discusses the principle of "stacking strength"—a trade-selection framework that prioritizes finding setups with aligned technical signals rather than forcing trades into marginal opportunities. The core thesis: the question is never "Can I trade this?" but always "Is this the right place for my money?" This approach emphasizes opportunity cost and selective entry over activity.

## Key takeaways

- **Stacking strength principle** [00:00–00:22]: Don't trade mixed-signal setups (e.g., "I want to be long S&P but getting conflicting indicators"). Instead, move to another underlying with clearer directional alignment.
- **Opportunity cost framing** [00:22]: The decision gate is not "Could I possibly place a trade?" but "Is this the right place for my capital right now?"
- **Selective entry over activity**: If short-term price-action signals don't objectively stack in your favor, skip the trade and hunt for a better setup elsewhere.
- **Applies across timeframes**: Relevant for both swing trades and intraday price-action plays seeking upside exposure.

## Notable quotes

> "The question is always not, 'Could I possibly place a trade in this?' The question instead should always be, 'Is this the right place for my money?'"
