# BAC adjustment and path forward - Small Stacks Pt15

## Summary

Eric closes the short BAC 24 puts for a nickel profit after a gap-up move, demonstrating disciplined profit-taking. The portfolio maintains diagonal positions in Kroger and Intel while managing capital efficiently at ~24% utilization, targeting a ~19% annualized return through longer-dated premium-selling strategies rather than swing trading or scalping.

## Key takeaways

- **BAC short-put closure** [00:23]: Closed short 24 puts opened at $1.22 for a nickel, exiting after gap-up and pullback to lock in profit rather than hold to expiration.
- **Diagonal strategy rationale** [01:12]: Ride premium decay as long as possible on range-bound positions; accept occasional adverse moves as part of the structure.
- **Kroger position management** [02:00]: Short 35 calls on two long LEAPs but only one short call active, maintaining uncovered ratio to capture additional upside; waiting for 90%+ profit to close or rebound to add premium.
- **Intel diagonal health** [03:08]: Long LEAP at cost basis ~$10.35, short 47 call at parity; position benefits from range-bound behavior and theta bleed; strong basis for adding another short call if rebound occurs.
- **Portfolio metrics** [04:54]: $11.56k profit (~19% return) on $61.56k total capital; only 23.92% utilization; diagonal strategy outperforming on capital efficiency.
- **Deployment philosophy** [05:16]: Deliberately avoiding verticals, swing trades, and scalps; prioritizing skill development and disciplined shot selection over chasing returns in a small account.
- **Scaling mindset** [07:26]: Current focus is practicing repeatable process; scaling to larger accounts (e.g., $1M) would amplify returns meaningfully and require different risk management.
