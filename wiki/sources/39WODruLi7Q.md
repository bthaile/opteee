# Trading vs Regular Jobs - The Real Costs

## Summary

A foundational comparison of the hidden costs and structural differences between W2 employment and full-time trading. The discussion covers income consistency, tax burden, healthcare, and the opportunity cost of time commitment required to maintain a profitable trading process.

## Key takeaways

- **Income stability trade-off** [00:00]: W2 employment provides predictable bi-weekly or semi-monthly paychecks; trading income is inherently inconsistent and requires capital risk borne by the trader, not an employer.
- **Tax and benefits burden** [00:23]: Self-employed traders must self-fund healthcare and manage higher tax complexity; these costs must be explicitly factored into profitability calculations.
- **Time commitment and travel cost** [00:39]: A trading process that requires full-time screen presence creates direct opportunity costs if you want flexibility or travel; stepping away from the desk directly reduces income potential.
