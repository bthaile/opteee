# Covered Calls For Income - Three Mistakes To Avoid

## Summary

This video identifies three critical mistakes traders make when selling covered calls for income: treating premium collection as income rather than capital allocation, overweighting the short call mechanics at the expense of the underlying stock position, and chasing high implied volatility without regard to opportunity cost and capped upside. The host argues that the covered-call framework itself is fundamentally flawed when approached as an income strategy.

## Key takeaways

- **Mistake #1: Collecting premium as income [00:00]** — Premium is not income; it represents capital deployed and opportunity cost. Treating it as "free money" obscures the true risk-return profile of the position.
- **Mistake #2: Overemphasizing the short call [00:33]** — Traders focus on the premium leg while ignoring that the underlying stock position dominates the P&L. The short call is a secondary consideration.
- **Mistake #3: Prioritizing high implied volatility [00:33]** — Chasing elevated IV for premium collection caps upside and locks in opportunity cost without adequate compensation for the risk taken.
