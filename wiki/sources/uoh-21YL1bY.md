# DON'T Be an Options Trader #investing #trading #optionstrading

## Summary

Options are not always the optimal vehicle for every trade hypothesis. While options traders often default to their preferred product, successful trading requires matching the trade idea to the right instrument—whether that's equities, futures, or options. The key is to evaluate the directional, volatility, and time assumptions required by each product and choose accordingly.

## Key takeaways

- **Options add multiple layers of correctness** [00:00–00:18]: To win with options, you must be right on direction, volatility assumption, and time frame simultaneously. Equities require only directional correctness.
- **Don't get attached to the product** [00:18–00:36]: Identify your trade hypothesis first, then select the instrument that best fits it—not the other way around.
- **Think like a trader, not an "options trader"** [00:00]: Self-identification as an "options trader" can create bias toward using options even when they're suboptimal.

## Notable quotes

> "When you trade options there are a handful of things you got to be right on: you have to be correct in terms of your direction, you have to be correct in terms of your volatility assumption, you have to be correct in a certain amount of time."
