# Understanding VIX vs VXX

## Summary

VIX is a 30-day implied volatility index calculated from S&P 500 options with no underlying tradable asset, while VXX is an exchange-traded note backed by volatility futures contracts that track two different terms of the volatility curve. The key distinction is that VIX can only be traded via options and futures (/VX), whereas VXX has a direct underlying with bid-ask spreads and volume like a stock.

## Key takeaways

- **VIX is a calculation, not a tradable asset** [00:00] — it represents 30-day implied volatility of S&P 500 options; you cannot buy or sell VIX directly, only trade options on it or /VX futures.
- **VXX is an ETN with an underlying** [00:37] — it tracks two different terms of volatility futures and has a bid, offer, size, and volume like a stock or ETF.
- **VIX has no bid-ask spread or volume** [00:37] — because there is no underlying asset to trade; VXX does have both because it is backed by futures contracts.
- **VXX options and futures exist** [00:37] — in addition to the note itself, providing multiple ways to trade volatility exposure.

## Notable quotes

> "VIX is a 30-day volatility calculation. It is an index which is 30-day IV in S&P 500 options."

> "VXX is an exchange-traded note and it tracks two different terms of volatility. It's not a calculation, but it's actually two different futures."
