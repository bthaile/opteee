# HOW to Trade Meme Stocks with Options

## Summary

This video outlines a systematic framework for trading meme stocks using options, emphasizing the importance of identifying profit mechanisms (price movement and volatility) before selecting option structures. Rather than buying cheap out-of-the-money calls, the host advocates for longer-dated, slightly in-the-money calls combined with ratio call diagonals and kicker options, paired with disciplined position management and stop-loss discipline to avoid theta decay erosion.

## Key takeaways

### Profit mechanism first
- **[00:43–01:23]** Define your profit mechanism before choosing an option structure. For meme stocks, the primary drivers are price movement and volatility changes, not dividends or correlations.
- **[01:45–02:26]** Measure and quantify the profit mechanism: understand when it occurs, how long moves persist, and what signals precede them.

### Meme stock traits
- **[04:06–04:45]** Meme stocks exhibit severe, explosive price moves interspersed with quiet periods; volume spikes are highly correlated with >10% price moves.
- **[06:08–06:29]** 5-day average volume relative to current volume is a strong signal; RSI with 5-day MA is less informative.
- **[06:54–07:15]** After a 15% move, duration above that threshold is typically short; this directly informs position duration choices.

### Why not cheap OTM calls
- **[11:37–12:15]** Cheap, far-OTM calls decay rapidly (theta ~3 cents per day as percentage of premium). While they offer higher notional profit on big moves, they require repeated cycles of buying and losing money.
- **[12:58–13:19]** Theta decay accelerates within 30 days to expiration; cheap options force you to cycle through many losing trades before a big move hits.

### Preferred structure: ratio call diagonal
- **[10:36–11:17]** Buy longer-dated calls (60+ DTE) that are slightly in-the-money (higher delta, better dollar-for-dollar movement with underlying).
- **[14:15–15:15]** Sell near-term short calls against the long position at a ratio (e.g., 5 long / 2 short) to collect theta and subsidize cost.
- **[15:33–15:54]** Add kicker options (further OTM, 20–30 DTE) when volume signals confirm the move is starting, capturing upside without capped profit.

### Downside management
- **[16:30–17:06]** Set price-level alerts and stop losses; don't hold options to expiration. Define a loss threshold and exit period; if price recovers, re-enter.
- **[09:04–09:23]** Prefer long premium strategies (long calls, long puts) over short premium when expecting large moves, to avoid capped profit and painful drawdowns.

## Notable quotes

> "If you don't have a defined profit mechanism, doesn't matter what option structure you pick—you won't make money."

> "The problem with cheap options is they will have lower gamma being further out in time, so if gamma is too low and you have a low delta, your options won't compound."

## Regime / context

Recorded October 2024. The framework applies to any high-volatility, mean-reverting security with explosive intraday/intraweek moves; meme stocks (GME, AMC, SOFI, UNG) serve as primary examples. The emphasis on longer-dated, slightly ITM calls with ratio short calls reflects a preference for capital preservation and compounding over lottery-ticket leverage.
