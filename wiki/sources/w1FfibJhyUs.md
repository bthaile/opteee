# GME Options Flow Update

## Summary

Quick snapshot of [[gme]] options positioning ahead of the 5 July expiration, with focus on call/put open-interest flattening and retail participation in far out-of-the-money strikes. Price has drifted modestly upward (from ~$20.33 to ~$24.37 over two days) on anemic volume, and the majority of new flow is concentrating in 19 July and 16 August expirations rather than rolling into near-term series.

## Key takeaways

- **Price action & volume context** [00:51–01:38]: [[gme]] has consolidated in a tight range (~$22–$29) since mid-June, with volume notably light. This low-volume base suggests limited institutional shorting resistance.
- **5 July expiration risk** [05:54–06:20]: ~192k calls are set to expire; most positions below current price (~$24.37) will likely be eviscerated. Open interest is not yet building meaningfully in follow-on expirations (12 July only ~70k calls).
- **Retail lotto buying in 125 strikes** [02:52–04:10]: Massive volume in 19 July $125 calls, but predominantly small lot sizes (1–10 contracts), indicating retail participation rather than institutional positioning. Dollar values remain modest (~$39k per larger trade).
- **Call/put OI flattening** [09:08]: Call open interest has compressed from ~2.5× put OI down to ~8.5:5.2k ratio, suggesting put buying is accelerating or call buying is slowing.
- **Net positive flow in standard strikes** [07:40–08:04]: 19 July $25 and $30 calls show net positive flows over the prior day (~3,500 contracts opened), though volume remains subdued.
- **Structural positioning** [08:27–08:45]: 355k contracts remain in far out-of-the-money strikes; host notes this is a low-probability, high-payoff structure typical of retail speculation. Host's own positioning is in 18 October, reflecting longer-dated, lower-leverage approach.
