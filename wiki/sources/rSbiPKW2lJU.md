# What trading returns are possible? #options #trading

## Summary

Eric addresses a fundamental question in options trading: whether specific return targets are achievable. The core insight is that return possibilities are highly individual and dependent on the trader's own track record and capabilities. Asking "is X return possible?" before you have trading experience is a category error—the answer becomes knowable only after you've built a documented trading history.

## Key takeaways

- **The question itself is the answer** [00:00–00:27]: If you have to ask whether a specific return is possible, the answer is no—not because it's impossible in general, but because you lack the data to know your own capability.
- **Returns are individual, not universal** [00:27–00:46]: Someone else's achievable return does not express your reality. Averages can inform, but they don't predict your outcome.
- **Track record is the only truth** [00:46]: Once you have a documented trading history, you know what *you* are capable of. Until then, you have no idea—and that's the uncomfortable truth.
- **Build before you predict** [00:00]: The path forward is to trade, log results, and let your own data answer the question, not to seek external validation of targets.
