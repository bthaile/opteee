# David Hunter on Why Gold is Skyrocketing Right Now | The Outlier Podcast

## Summary

David Hunter discusses the final leg of a 43-year secular bull market, with the S&P 500 up ~46% since April 2024. He raises his year-end targets significantly (S&P to 9,500, NASDAQ to 32,000, Russell 2000 to 3,800) and explains gold's strength as driven by dollar weakness and technical momentum rather than imminent crisis signals. Hunter maintains his longer-term thesis of a global bust in 2025–2026, driven by unprecedented leverage and policy reluctance to intervene at scale.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2025-10-24)

- **Parabolic final leg**: S&P 500 up ~46% since April 2024; Hunter raised targets from July (8,700) to October (9,500) and expects 30–40% further upside [02:26]
- **Institutional catch-up**: Institutions missed the double from 2022 lows and are only now warming to equities; sentiment remains skeptical despite all-time highs, unusual for a bull peak [05:19, 06:40]
- **Gold breakout**: Gold broke out technically and is being driven by dollar weakness, geopolitical uncertainty, and momentum buying; Hunter raised gold target from 4,500 to 5,000 (pre-bust timeframe) and silver from 75 to 100 [09:24, 10:39]
- **Recent correction**: Steep rally correction in metals and equities (one to two days) likely complete; expect continued strength in next 3–6 months [10:39, 11:57]
- **Government shutdown**: Shutdown ongoing but not yet systemic risk; Democrats lack leverage; Republicans may use it to cut government spending; market impact minimal so far [19:15, 24:33]
- **Regional bank fraud**: Zion Bank fraud incident caused sharp but contained selloff; no technical damage to banking charts; not systemic yet [25:42, 27:02]

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Volatility regime shift**: Realized volatility has increased substantially over the past decade due to faster information flow, retail access to trading tools, and late-cycle dynamics; VIX moved up alongside equities (unusual) [11:57, 13:17, 14:32]
- **Correlation breakdown**: Stocks, metals, bonds, and gold all rallying together (not inverse); driven by Fed easing cycle and falling rates [16:53, 18:03]
- **Institutional nervousness as bullish signal**: Heavy institutional skepticism and one-foot-out-the-door positioning at all-time highs is atypical and suggests room to run; sentiment still bearish despite rally [28:15, 29:32]
- **Cycle timing vs. precision**: Hunter distinguishes between "could happen by" (scenario planning) and "will happen by" (false precision); 43-year bull market unprecedented in magnitude; five trillion dollars pumped 2020–2021 extended cycle beyond historical norms [34:44, 35:56, 37:15]
- **Leverage and policy reluctance**: Global bust thesis hinges on unprecedented leverage (~330 trillion debt, quadrillions in derivatives notional), combined with policymakers' stated reluctance to repeat 2008–2009 interventions; delays in response will amplify unwind [30:42, 41:05, 42:08]
- **Roaring Twenties parallel**: Current environment mirrors 1920s—party on surface, fragility underneath; have/have-not economy with ~50% of population barely getting by despite 3.8% GDP estimates [32:03, 33:24]
