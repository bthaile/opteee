# A Common Mistake with Covered Call Profits #optionstrading

## Summary

A covered call's primary profit driver is appreciation of the underlying long shares, not the short premium collected. The short call premium is supplementary income; the max profit occurs above the short strike when shares appreciate. Misunderstanding this relationship leads traders to over-weight the premium side and under-weight directional conviction.

## Key takeaways

- **Primary profit source is share appreciation, not premium** [00:00–00:28]: The covered call structure (long shares + short calls) makes money mainly from the long position moving up in value. The short premium is secondary.
- **Max profit sits above the short strike** [00:00]: Your capped upside is defined by the strike you sold calls against; all gains above that strike are forfeited.
- **Short premium is supplementary, not the main edge** [00:28]: Treating the call premium as the primary income source is a common mistake that distorts position sizing and directional bias.

## Notable quotes

> "Your primary way that you're going to make money from a covered call is not from the short calls you sell against your long shares. It's from your long shares appreciating in value. The short premium side of the equation is supplementary."
