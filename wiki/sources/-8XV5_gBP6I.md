# WHAT do the Markets Looking Like in 2022? Fed Policy, Market Regulation, & Blockchain w/ Dave Lauer

## Summary

Eric interviews Dave Lauer on the state of markets amid unprecedented Fed policy, geopolitical tensions (Russia–Ukraine), and structural market issues. The discussion spans transitory inflation, the mechanics of payment for order flow, algorithmic accountability in social media, and blockchain applications to clearing and settlement. Lauer advocates for competition-driven market structure reform and discusses his upcoming fintech platform.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2022-02-16)

- **Fed tightening cycle imminent**: Rate hike expected mid-March; asset purchases still ongoing despite inflation concerns [01:00–01:21]
- **Regime shift underway**: "Bad news is now bad news again"—markets transitioning from bull-market dynamics where bad news was good news [05:34–06:01]
- **Oil near multi-year highs**: Geopolitical risk (Russia–Ukraine) driving crude-oil prices; potential supply shock if sanctions escalate [07:33–08:12]
- **China positioning critical**: NATO alignment may force China to choose sides; economic decoupling from China is a priority but practically difficult [08:32–10:28]
- **Market resilience surprising**: Despite Fed tightening and geopolitical uncertainty, equities holding up better than expected [06:42–07:05]

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Transitory inflation definition**: Supply-side inflation impulse (2–3 quarters) vs. structural demand-side inflation; peak likely reached, normalization expected [16:09–17:38]
- **Supply-chain stress**: COVID-era hoarding and port congestion created unprecedented dislocation; normalization will take quarters [18:01–18:47]
- **Payment for order flow (PFOF) harms retail**: Dutch study shows retail orders routed to internalizers (Citadel, Virtu) do not receive best execution; transparent venue competition would tighten spreads by ~25 bps [36:11–43:39]
- **Best execution duty**: Brokers legally obligated to achieve best price for retail; PFOF inducements create conflict of interest [37:18–38:13]
- **Algorithmic accountability**: Facebook's engagement-maximizing algorithms create filter bubbles, polarization, and outrage; regulatory guardrails (Algorithmic Accountability Act) require impact assessments [28:42–32:31]
- **Blockchain in clearing**: Paxos's decentralized smart-contract settlement reduces capital lock-up and counterparty risk vs. centralized NSCC/DTCC model; first new clearing agency in 10+ years [45:11–49:18]
- **Insider trading appearance**: Congressional trading (e.g., Pelosi) erodes public trust; appearance of corruption sufficient to justify reform, regardless of intent [51:09–56:46]

## Notable quotes

> "I remain team transitory... when you pull out used cars it drops dramatically... I think we're gonna start to see inflation slow down significantly."

> "Bad news is now bad news again... that's a big difference you see generally more in a bear market than a bull market."

> "If you believe in capitalism and competition then you're gonna say a thousand firms [competing for order flow] right... that's what I push for: open competition."
