# HUGE Option Volume in GME and CHWY & Roaring Kitty Tweet

## Summary

GameStop saw over 400,000 call options trade on 2024-06-27 with massive call skew across expirations, while Roaring Kitty's dog emoji tweet (with green eyes matching Chewy brand colors) coincided with unusual volume in Chewy. The host analyzes order flow, strike selection anomalies, and retail positioning in both names, emphasizing the distinction between activity and actionable signal.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2024-06-27)

- **GME call volume spike** [02:30]: Over 400,000 calls traded vs. ~200,000 yesterday; put volume doubled but remains dwarfed by calls.
- **Roaring Kitty tweet timing** [00:00]: Dog emoji with green eyes (matching Chewy hex code) posted at 10:00 a.m.; Chewy stock began moving immediately after.
- **Chewy 2-minute chart** [12:37]: Stock flew off at 10:00 a.m. on the tweet; host entered long calls around 10:04 and exited on the pullback.
- **GME 26.5-strike anomaly** [05:32]: ~2,400 call contracts (≈$456k notional) bought at 26.5 strike expiring 5 July; unusual half-strike selection for a large institutional-sized position.
- **GME 28 June 30-strike** [10:53]: 28,000 OI with 52,000 transacted; 128-strike calls now trading at 0.005 (down from 1.5 yesterday).
- **Chewy 45-strike** [16:03]: 29,000 calls transacted at 0.04 delta; theta decay ~10 cents/day; likely worthless by expiration unless major catalyst.
- **Chewy XRT theory** [16:53]: Chewy is only 1.77% of XRT holdings; dilution makes shell-game hypothesis difficult to execute.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Bid-ask spread on fractional strikes** [06:15]: Half-strikes and quarter-strikes consistently show 2–3× wider spreads than whole strikes; 26.5 calls bid 115/ask 135 vs. 26 calls at 6 cents wide.
- **Order-flow interpretation** [08:47]: Trades executed at the offer indicate buyer aggression; time-of-sales chunking (same timestamp, multiple fills) suggests coordinated position building.
- **Moneyness and theta decay** [16:03]: Far OTM calls (0.04 delta) decay ~10 cents/day; retail often buys these hoping for lottery-like moves rather than directional conviction.
- **Call skew oscillation** [10:00]: GME shows alternating call/put skew across expirations (front months heavily call-skewed, October puts-skewed, January calls again), suggesting different trader cohorts at different time horizons.
- **Volume vs. open interest** [10:53]: High transacted volume with lower OI indicates day-trading and quick exits; persistent OI would suggest conviction holds.
