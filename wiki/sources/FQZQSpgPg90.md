# Building True Confidence in Trading

## Summary

True confidence in trading is built exclusively through demonstrated skill and repeated successful outcomes, not through positive self-talk or wishful thinking. The distinction between genuine confidence (earned through deliberate practice and proven results) and false confidence (belief without evidence) is fundamental to sustainable trading performance.

## Key takeaways

- **Confidence requires demonstrated skill, not belief** [00:00–00:26]: Confidence is built only through practicing a skill set and seeing desired outcomes materialize repeatedly. Positive thinking alone cannot create genuine confidence.
- **Repetition and stakes alignment matter** [00:26–00:49]: A novice basketball player facing a high-stakes free throw has low confidence because of limited practice; Kobe Bryant's supreme confidence came from thousands of repetitions. The same principle applies to trading.
- **Fake confidence is not confidence** [00:49]: Distinguishing between actual confidence (earned through evidence) and false confidence (belief without foundation) is critical for traders.

## Notable quotes

> "There is one and only one way to build confidence in anything, including trading, and it's through the demonstration of a skill set and seeing the outcome being what you want it to be."
