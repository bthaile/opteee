# 26% Return in 3 Months - Small Stacks Finale Pt16

## Summary

Eric closes out the three-month Small Stacks portfolio experiment with a 26.65% return (~$1,817 profit on $5,000 initial capital), driven primarily by diagonal spreads on [[ko|Kroger]] and Pfizer. The finale emphasizes that small accounts are skill-development laboratories, not wealth-generation vehicles, and that consistent execution, aggressive saving, and realistic position-sizing are the true drivers of long-term trading success.

## Key takeaways

- **Account performance:** Started September 1 with $5,000; closed at $6,817.17 (26.65% return) [02:02]
- **Strategy breakdown:** Diagonals (primarily [[ko|Kroger]] and Pfizer) generated the bulk of returns; [[covered-strangle|covered strangles]] contributed only ~$225 but served portfolio diversification [02:34]
- **Market context:** SPX moved sideways with dips and rallies over the three-month period—volatility helped strategy execution [03:23]
- **Small account reality:** $5–50k accounts cannot be your wealth source; they are skill-building sandboxes [04:06]
- **Primary goal is skill, not money:** Focus on developing repeatable process and [[consistency]] while aggressively saving external capital [05:44]
- **Less is more:** Avoid daily scalping, earnings plays, and high-variance strategies in small accounts; they cannot absorb large drawdowns [06:31]
- **Commission drag is real:** Smaller accounts suffer disproportionately from transaction costs; limit trade frequency [07:17]
- **Patience and long-term thinking:** Resist the urge to turn a small account into a car payment; focus on the 10+ year horizon [07:38]
- **Risk control is constrained:** Small accounts will run 70–80% invested (vs. ~20% margin utilization in larger accounts); acknowledge this and be selective with entry points [08:42]
- **Consistency compounds:** A 26% return on $5k is meaningless ($1.6k); but the same execution on a $50k account ($13k) or $500k account ($130k) becomes transformative [09:54]
- **Future plan:** Eric intends to run a similar experiment on a larger account ($500k–$1M) to show how approach changes at scale [01:36]

## Notable quotes

> "Your five thousand, ten thousand, twenty thousand, fifty thousand dollar account is not going to be the source of your future wealth. The sooner we can get over that, the better off we're going to be able to trade." [04:32]

> "The primary purpose of trading in a small account is not to make money. The primary purpose is to make sure that you are developing the skill set." [05:44]

> "Focus on consistency with these smaller accounts. As your account grows in value, that consistency is going to become invaluable." [10:47]
