# Market Outlook: Week Ahead – Sector Rotation, Fed Rate Path, and Earnings Season

## Summary

Eric reviews broad market positioning following the Fed's recent rate cut, noting a significant shift away from tech-led gains toward strength in utilities, financials, and other sectors. With key economic data (PMI, consumer confidence, GDP, jobless claims, PCE) and Fed speakers scheduled for the week, the market is attempting to gauge the probability of a soft landing and the trajectory of future rate cuts. Upcoming bank earnings will serve as a critical indicator of economic health and corporate expectations.

## Key takeaways

- **Sector rotation underway** [00:45–01:05]: Large caps remain relatively strong, but the S&P 500's gains are now driven by XLU, XLF, XLC, XLB, and XLI rather than technology. The Nasdaq is ~20 points off highs; small caps (Russell 2000) are ~7 points off highs, signaling tech weakness.
- **Consumer health mixed signals** [02:23–03:24]: Credit card rates and consumer loan charge-offs are rising; personal savings are contracting. Real estate may strengthen as rates fall, but the consumer is not in a "rock solid position" — watch for lagged effects on consumer spending.
- **Critical economic calendar this week** [01:05–02:23]: Services/manufacturing PMI (23rd), consumer confidence (24th), GDP and jobless claims (26th), Jay Powell speech (26th morning), PCE and University of Michigan sentiment (27th).
- **Fed rate path increasingly aggressive** [04:24–05:33]: Fed Watch Tool shows ~51% probability of another 50 basis point cut by November 7th meeting. Probability of no change is "essentially null." Market is pricing in a path to 2.75–3% by September 2025, with the first expected pause later than historical norms.
- **Bank earnings as canary in coal mine** [04:24–05:33]: October bank earnings will be crucial for understanding how financial institutions are adapting to the rate-cut environment and their forecasts for the remainder of the year.
