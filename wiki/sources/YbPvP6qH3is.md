# Illusion of Short-term Trading Performance

## Summary

New options traders frequently mistake short-term success for sustainable edge, projecting near-term wins into future expectations without adequate sample size. The core problem is insufficient data collection and analysis—traders don't know what they don't know, and early wins create dangerous confirmation bias that prevents proper adjustment and learning.

## Key takeaways

- **Sample size is foundational** [00:25]: Trading longevity requires exposure to many different market regimes; small sample sizes from early success are statistically meaningless and lead to false generalizations.
- **Early wins create false confidence** [00:00]: New traders see near-term success and immediately project it forward as a reliable future expectation, a classic mental error.
- **Broad assumptions from limited data** [00:00]: Traders make sweeping conclusions based on a handful of winning trades, then fail to adjust when conditions change.
- **Continuous data collection is essential** [00:25]: Collecting good information over time allows for proper analysis and necessary adjustments; without it, early success will not persist.

## Notable quotes

> "New traders are at a point where they really don't know what they don't know yet and then when they start seeing some near-term success they kind of make broad assumptions based on that and then they kind of project that forward as a future expectation."
