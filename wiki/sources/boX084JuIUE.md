# Decrease Trading Commissions & Lower Options Fees

## Summary

A practical guide to reducing trading costs by negotiating directly with your broker. The video covers two primary negotiation tactics: requesting volume-based discounts on options contracts and leveraging competitive offers from alternative brokers to secure rate reductions.

## Key takeaways

- **Volume-based negotiation** [00:00–00:22]: Contact your broker and request a discount based on your monthly options contract volume. Frame it as a straightforward business request tied to your trading activity.
- **Competitive leverage** [00:22–00:37]: Mention that you are shopping around and have received competitive commission offers from other brokers. Use this as negotiating power without committing to a move.
- **Anchoring tactic** [00:37–00:55]: Let the broker propose a number first, then use that as your anchor point for further negotiation. Standard negotiation principles apply.
- **No downside risk** [00:55]: The worst outcome is a "no"—there is no penalty for asking. If they decline and you prefer to stay, you remain in your current position.
