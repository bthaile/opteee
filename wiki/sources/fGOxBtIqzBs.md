# Analyzing Gamestop's Recent Surge

## Summary

GameStop experienced a significant intraday surge on 2024-09-20 with volume exceeding 60 million shares—roughly 3–5× the 5- and 20-day averages. The move was driven by large block trades (many executed at average price), concentrated options activity in the front October expiration (18 OCT), and a bullish skew in the options market (calls bought at the offer, puts sold at the bid). Implied volatility spiked from sub-80 to over 100, and open interest in the October calls expanded materially, signaling institutional or coordinated positioning.

## Key takeaways

- **Volume explosion [00:55–01:25]**: 61.8M shares traded vs. 18M 5-day average and 13M 20-day average; exceeding the full-day average intraday is a strong signal of genuine volume pickup.
- **Dark pool and block activity [01:51–03:28]**: Multiple large trades ($92M, $19M, $16M premiums) executed at average price, indicating institutional-sized positions; these still hit the consolidated tape despite dark pool routing.
- **Front-month options concentration [04:22–04:46]**: Three largest options trades of the day were all in 18 OCT expiration; $500K trade in 21 calls (2,000 contracts) and $250K trade in same expiration within an hour.
- **Bullish options skew [05:15–05:40]**: 56K calls vs. 107K puts; calls bought at the offer (bullish), puts sold at the bid (bullish); majority of calls were out-of-the-money but benefited from the 12% intraday move.
- **IV spike and OI buildup [06:36–07:28]**: Implied volatility jumped from sub-80 to over 100; October call open interest expanded significantly with large yellow boxes (big positions) appearing across the chain, especially at-the-money and slightly out-of-the-money strikes.
- **Scan trigger [07:47–08:09]**: Volume exceeded both 5-day and 20-day averages intraday, a reliable signal that volume is picking up meaningfully; the question now is whether it sustains.
