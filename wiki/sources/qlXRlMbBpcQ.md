# The Quirks of Theta and Option Moneyness

## Summary

This video explores the counterintuitive relationship between theta decay and option moneyness, demonstrating that absolute theta values can be misleading when comparing options at different strike prices. By examining theta as a percentage of remaining premium rather than in absolute terms, traders gain a more accurate picture of time decay's impact across the moneyness spectrum.

## Key takeaways

- **Absolute theta ≠ relative decay rate** [00:00] — A 10-delta 60-day option showing 19 cents of theta appears cheaper than higher-delta options, but this ignores the context of remaining premium.
- **Theta as percentage of premium** [00:30] — The same 19 cents represents 30% of the remaining premium in the out-of-the-money option, revealing aggressive decay relative to what's left to lose.
- **Moneyness distorts perception** — Out-of-the-money options decay faster as a percentage of their value, even when absolute theta is lower, creating a hidden cost for directional bets.
