# What's Going on with GME? | Meme Stock Saturday Ep59

## Summary

A deep-dive analysis of GameStop's current fundamental and technical position, examining the warrant structure, earnings expectations, and options chain dynamics ahead of Q4 FY25 earnings (24 March). The host uses Claude to synthesize recent developments—including the $8.8B cash position, 16% short interest, and Cohen's capital allocation optionality—while maintaining that near-term MOASS probability remains low despite persistent volatility opportunities for options traders.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2026-03-22)

- **Warrant mechanics & expiration risk** [13:14–24:20]: 59M warrants distributed in 2025 at $32 strike, expiring 30 Oct 2026. Current warrant price ~$3.94; probability of touching $32 strike before expiration is 35.23%. Theta decay will accelerate as expiration approaches; warrant holders should monitor holding costs. Full exercise would raise ~$1.9B and dilute by ~13%.

- **Short interest & squeeze probability** [04:01–10:32]: Short interest reported at 16% of float (historically low). Host identifies multiple prior squeezes but argues MOASS probability is low in near term due to sufficient liquidity from prior offerings and dilution events. Warrant exercise by shorts could provide their own liquidity, reducing squeeze fuel.

- **Earnings expectations (24 March after close)** [32:42–44:27]: Holiday quarter typically strong. Consensus EPS ~$0.37 vs. $0.30 YoY; revenue expected lighter. Core retail (physical, collectibles, accessories) now cash-flow positive post-footprint optimization. Operating cash flow $583M; gross profit $1.17B. Host expects solid beat given cost discipline and seasonal strength.

- **Implied volatility into earnings** [49:08–51:41]: IV has been relatively flat coming into recent earnings (69.5% currently). Realized vol has normalized relative to implied; straddle priced at 9.2% expected move. Historical pattern shows 4 consecutive quarters of flat IV into release, with normalized post-earnings vol behavior.

- **Options chain positioning** [52:43–56:29]: Mostly call-heavy premium on front months (27 Mar, 17 Apr); heavier put accumulation in 10 Apr (22s, 21.5s). Longer-dated (15 Jan 27) shows mostly sold calls at lower strikes, bought calls at higher strikes (moonshot plays). Host's covered strangle on 17 Apr 22s sold at 35 delta for $0.78 (44.62% annualized ROIC).

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Cash as optionality driver** [06:32–07:25]: Cohen's primary value lever is the $8.8B cash pile and its deployment optionality. Once deployed, that optionality evaporates; current volatility partly reflects uncertainty around capital allocation decisions. "Store of the future" modernization efforts did not materially improve P&L vs. baseline stores.

- **Dilution vs. squeeze trade-off** [10:58–12:03]: Shareholder dilution from capital raises (warrants, offerings) directly reduces MOASS probability but strengthens the underlying business. Cohen has been disciplined, raising capital at peaks; this is self-serving for squeeze proponents but beneficial for the company long-term.

- **Warrant as leveraged call surrogate** [14:38–15:25]: Warrants trade independently with distinct volatility vs. commensurate call options. Host previously scalped the vol spread between warrant and call moves; effect now muted but illustrates market-making opportunity in the vol relationship.

- **Theta decay on warrants** [21:00–24:20]: With 35% probability of touch, warrants lose ~$0.08/day currently. As expiration approaches (150 days out), 32 strike calls decay from ~$0.36 to ~$0.30, mirroring warrant decay. Warrant holders should actively manage position to avoid slow bleed.

- **Volatility persistence despite price stagnation** [27:33–29:45]: GameStop exhibits fat risk premiums (7-day and 30-day) relative to realized vol, driven by historical volatility and retail sentiment. Even without directional conviction, vol isolation strategies remain lucrative. Year-to-date return on covered strangle: +1.3% (underlying down 10%).

- **Swaps as red herring** [25:13–58:18]: Host disputes the GameStop community's focus on swaps as a causal mechanism for bearish moves. Swaps are not novel; they predate the 2021 squeeze. Correlation analysis between swap dates/positions and price behavior shows weak evidence. Host remains open to contrary evidence but has not found compelling data.

- **Bayesian analysis & conviction** [28:52–29:15]: Strong convictions loosely held—maintain research-backed conclusions but abandon them immediately upon material new information. Current MOASS thesis does not look probable near-term, but future regime changes could alter that assessment.
