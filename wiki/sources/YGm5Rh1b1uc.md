# Becoming a Daytrader w/ @HumbledTraderOfficial | The Outlier Podcast Ep71

## Summary

Shay from Humbled Trader discusses her decade-long evolution from penny-stock chat-room follower to disciplined day trader focused on mid- and large-cap stocks. She emphasizes the critical importance of building your own watchlist, pre-market planning, rigorous journaling, and pattern recognition through chart observation—and reveals she is now experimenting with swing trading to capture larger moves on parabolic setups.

## Key takeaways

- **Chat-room alerts are a trap** [07:13–07:30]: Following text alerts from trading rooms creates dependency and poor execution; you end up buying late and selling at the wrong time. Building your own watchlist is the foundational shift from follower to trader.

- **Pre-market prep is non-negotiable** [11:15–11:31]: Wake up at least one hour before market open to scan, plan levels, and identify risk per trade. This single habit separates self-sufficient traders from those chasing alerts.

- **Risk management must be intentional** [12:12–12:32]: Plan your risk *before* entering—if you risk $20 per trade, that determines your share size. Most beginners skip this step entirely.

- **Focus on one thing deeply** [13:57–14:40]: Jumping between strategies (day trading, swing trading, options) causes you to abandon your craft and restart. Master one approach before branching out.

- **Parabolic daily charts signal short-squeeze and reversal** [44:30–47:10]: Consecutive green candles with gaps indicate short covering and irrational buying. Trade the trend long on days 3–4 of the move; short the first red day when the rubber band snaps. This pattern repeats across small and large caps.

- **Entry filter: today's open and low must exceed yesterday's high** [49:33–49:57]: This simple rule screens out weak setups and ensures you're trading genuine breakouts, not noise.

- **Journaling and chart collection are data** [36:26–37:29]: Save charts, track outcomes in spreadsheets, and collect at least 50 samples before claiming a setup works. Without recorded data, you're not actually testing—you're guessing.

- **Seasonal and cyclical patterns matter** [37:50–38:08]: Track success rates by month; small-cap gap-up fades have different probabilities in summer vs. winter. Quantify what you observe.

- **Day traders leave money on the table** [53:52–54:32]: Certain parabolic moves justify swing trading a portion of the position into the next 2–3 days. She is now opening a separate swing account to maximize these high-probability continuations.

- **Candlestick *trends* matter more than individual patterns** [40:18–40:41]: Rather than memorizing "shooting star" or "hammer" names, observe the bigger picture—higher lows, trend direction, and the shape of the entire day's candle.
