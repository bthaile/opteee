# Market Update - CRWD & GME

## Summary

Eric reviews two high-volatility securities: CrowdStrike (CRWD), which gapped down sharply on bad news with overwhelming sell-side volume, and GameStop (GME), which remains range-bound with mixed options activity. He examines unusual options flow—particularly large long-dated call sales against shares and significant put volume—to assess positioning and trader intent.

## Key takeaways

- **CRWD gap-down thesis** [00:20–01:38]: Bad news + gap down + 90% sell-side volume on the candle indicated capitulation; however, Eric set alerts both directions, believing the move may be overdone once the market fully synthesizes the severity of the outage.

- **GME range-bound structure** [02:22–03:12]: Price oscillates between ~23.25 (body low) and ~28.50 (body high); today's candle showed 48% sell volume with slight upward pressure; CCI crossed above zero but technical analysis has limited predictive power in a meme stock with non-fundamental drivers.

- **GME options volume: heavy but light** [04:55–05:25]: ~200k total contracts (relatively light for GME) with 200k calls vs. 46k puts—massive call skew; majority concentrated in Friday expiration; unusual for a "quiet period."

- **Large long-dated call sales (Jan 2025)** [09:46–13:10]: Identified ~463k shares' worth of short calls sold at the 20 strike (in-the-money) across multiple tranches, likely against a large long stock position; theta decay is minimal (~2¢/day) but meaningful on size; suggests a shareholder willing to sell at $20 if assigned.

- **Competing put and call flow** [07:06–08:38]: Time-and-sales analysis shows mixed put activity—some large sales (e.g., 847 contracts at 25 strike) but also significant buying; no conclusive evidence of one-directional put accumulation; unusual-whales data reveals bigger picture is long-dated call selling.

- **Premium transacted at scale** [13:58–14:15]: Top of options chain now dominated by positions >$100k premium; call-buying is slower; pattern suggests position settling rather than short-term directional bets.

## Notable quotes

> "It's always tomorrow" — reflecting on the community saying that quiet periods in GME can flip instantly.

> "If you have a really big position and you want to hedge some of the downside, you can sell those calls in the money because if they fall out of the money you've made a ton of money."
