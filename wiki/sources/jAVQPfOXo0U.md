# No Share Lockup - SpaceX IPO

## Summary

Eric discusses the mechanics and market implications of SpaceX's direct share program exemption from standard IPO lockup periods. A subset of shareholders will have immediate liquidity access to life-changing wealth, creating potential selling pressure, though the magnitude of impact remains uncertain given the anticipated demand for the offering.

## Key takeaways

- **Direct share program exemption** [00:00]: A small but notable group of shareholders are exempt from lockup restrictions, allowing immediate share sales post-IPO.
- **Behavioral incentive** [00:00]: Holders facing generational wealth overnight will face strong psychological pressure to realize gains, though not all will necessarily act.
- **Pricing impact uncertain** [00:25]: While selling pressure is expected to be factored into pricing, the sheer interest in the IPO may absorb supply without causing a "massive shift."
- **Supply vs. demand dynamic** [00:25]: The question hinges on whether demand depth is sufficient to absorb early lockup-exempt selling.
