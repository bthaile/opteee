# Position Adjustments - Small Stacks Pt7

## Summary

Eric reviews portfolio adjustments across the small-stacks account, closing a profitable short-put position in XLF, managing a diagonal spread in Pfizer that moved favorably, scaling into Kroger after a post-earnings dip, and monitoring Zynga. The account is at ~60% capital utilization with 3.6% realized return and 4.3% additional credit pending, demonstrating disciplined position management without over-adjustment.

## Key takeaways

- **XLF short puts closed early** [00:00–00:22]: Closed October 24 short puts for $0.26 after collecting $0.50, realizing $24 profit. Acknowledged leaving ~$50 on the table but prioritized capital release.
- **Pfizer diagonal: let it run** [01:18–02:36]: Stock moved up on vaccine news; holding the short 36 calls without adjustment. Position is at ~two-thirds max profit; will monitor extrinsic value of short calls to determine early exit, but no incentive to adjust while stock rises.
- **Kroger post-earnings adjustment** [02:58–04:27]: Took profit on short calls ($1.16 → $0.03 close), then scaled in by adding a second long call (April 2021, 29 strike, 72 delta) to reduce basis to $6.79 total. Deliberately did not re-establish short calls yet; waiting to see if the post-earnings dip is temporary or structural.
- **Portfolio metrics** [04:56–05:45]: ~$3,060 deployed (60%+ utilization), $179 realized P&L (3.6% return since Sept 1), $215 additional credit pending (4.3% upside). Planning to rebalance toward two short-put and two LEAP positions.
- **Zynga monitoring** [06:28–07:14]: Short 8.5 calls; stock at ~$8.40. Watching 9-day MA dropping below 50-day and 200-day MA treatment; no major adjustments yet while stock trends sideways.

## Notable quotes

> "The best case scenario is just like a covered call—that's why I use that analogy. More people are familiar with that than diagonals. But in a covered-call situation, a lot of people see their short strike fall in the money and they're like, 'Oh well, crap, now I have to adjust this.' Realistically, you shouldn't trade these unless you're willing to flatten a position."
