# 0DTE Option Revolution - VIX1D Explained

## Summary

The CBOE launched VIX1D (one-day volatility index) on April 24, 2023, to address explosive growth in zero-DTE SPX options trading—now over 40% of SPX volume. Unlike the standard VIX (which measures 30-day implied volatility), VIX1D provides intraday volatility measurement via a weighted aggregate of same-day and next-day expiration SPX options. The product is non-tradable (observational only) and requires careful observation before deployment in trading decisions.

## Key takeaways

- **Why VIX1D exists**: Zero-DTE SPX volume has grown from ~2–3% of total SPX volume in 2011 to over 40% in Q1 2023; the standard VIX's 30-day lookback loses sensitivity to this short-term demand [00:43–01:48]
- **Product mechanics**: VIX1D is a non-tradable index (like VIX1Y) calculated from weighted prices of SPX options expiring same-day and next-day; no bid/ask, no options on the index itself [02:15–03:45]
- **Calculation**: Aggregate of weighted prices from zero and one-day expiration SPX calls and puts; methodology published in CBOE index paper [03:25–03:45]
- **Early observation phase**: On launch day (April 24), VIX1D behavior departed from expected patterns; SPX intraday move was 0.6% (inside day), suggesting the index is still calibrating [05:11–06:22]
- **Recommended use**: Do not trade on VIX1D signals yet; instead, observe correlations over 1–2 weeks between VIX1D, SPX, Russell 2000, and individual S&P 500 stocks to build pairs-trade ideas [06:45–07:02]

## Regime / context

Launched April 24, 2023. This is the inaugural day of VIX1D; the product is in early observation phase and behavior may not yet be stable. The explosive growth in zero-DTE options reflects structural shifts in retail and institutional trading post-COVID. Traders should treat this as a new tool requiring calibration before live deployment.
