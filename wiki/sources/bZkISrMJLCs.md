# GameStop Analysis: Cohen Lawsuit, $2B Buyback | Ep63

## Summary

Eric analyzes two major GameStop developments: Ryan Cohen's $2 billion share buyback authorization and a Delaware class-action lawsuit challenging Cohen's $35 billion compensation package. The buyback appears strategically incongruous with Cohen's stated eBay acquisition ambitions, while the lawsuit alleges procedural irregularities in shareholder voting mechanics. Options positioning shows muted near-term implied volatility despite elevated failed-to-deliver counts and short interest.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2026-06-21)

- **Cohen's eBay bid escalation** [12:04–13:16]: Cohen has moved from a 5% stake (February) to 7.8% ownership through derivatives and equities, with interviews reiterating his intent to acquire eBay as an owner-operator and eliminate "entrenched management." The structure is half cash, half stock, with $9B on GameStop's balance sheet and $20B in available debt financing.
- **$2B buyback timing paradox** [11:23–25:42]: GameStop authorized a $2B share repurchase through June 2026, effective immediately. Eric flags this as strategically odd—deploying capital on buybacks while pursuing a multi-billion-dollar acquisition typically requires cash preservation. The buyback price (~$21–22) is reasonable but not exceptional.
- **Shareholder vote lawsuit** [29:36–32:57]: A Delaware class action filed Monday seeks to block the July 7 shareholder vote on Cohen's $35B compensation package (triggered at $100B market cap and $10B EBIT). The complaint alleges the board reversed course on voting procedures, allowing Cohen's 9.3% stake to determine outcome with only ~15% support from unaffiliated shareholders—a potential violation of Delaware law.
- **Options chain: muted front-end vol** [35:27–45:24]: Out-of-the-money call premium totals ~308 contracts (295 OTM); 25 June expiry shows only 7,200 at $25 strike, 3,700 at $27, insufficient to trigger a gamma squeeze even with $2B buyback. IV percentile at 6 DTE is 7%, indicating historically low relative volatility despite elevated skew in the front.
- **Failed-to-deliver tracking** [48:58–49:21]: FTDs are elevated and rising; short volume ratios remain high; short interest remains elevated. No extreme yet, but sustained short pressure beneath the surface.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Dealer gamma and positioning** [40:14–41:02]: Max pain was not hit in the last term; dealer gamma positioning requires further decomposition by delta and gamma dollars to clarify net short/long exposure.
- **Volatility term structure collapse** [44:02–44:55]: Front-end vol (5 DTE) collapsed from ~36 five days prior to current levels; term structure flattens sharply beyond 5 DTE, indicating low near-term event expectation despite tail skew remaining expensive.
- **Dispersion vs. concentration** [48:21–48:58]: 17 July expiry shows abnormally high volume and open interest concentration (relative to GameStop norms), suggesting dispersion in positioning rather than concentrated near-term gamma ramp.
- **Dark pool activity anomaly** [45:24–45:51]: Large block transactions on 20 June evening (~8 PM Pacific) are atypical; no clear catalyst identified.
