# Fed Cuts 25bps, Market Rotation & GameStop Context

## Summary

The Fed executed an expected 25 basis-point cut, but signaled a likely pause in the cutting cycle, triggering a significant intraday selloff in equities (SPY down ~3%, small caps hit harder). The market had been rotating internally for weeks; this announcement crystallized existing weakness across sectors. GameStop showed relative resilience despite the broad decline, holding key price levels around the high $20s.

## Key takeaways

- **Fed messaging mismatch** [03:19]: The 25bps cut was priced in; the market reacted to the Fed signaling a pause in cuts, not the cut itself. Probability models now show overwhelming likelihood of a pause at the January meeting.
- **Internal rotation preceded today** [02:10]: The speaker had been tracking advancing/declining issues and moving averages across timeframes for ~10 days, warning of underlying weakness. Today's move was not a surprise in direction, only magnitude.
- **Bayesian risk reassessment** [02:10]: Position sizing and risk reduction decisions made the day before were based on updating probabilities of up vs. down moves as the market reached parabolic highs—not a prediction of today's specific event.
- **Broad sector weakness** [05:49]: Energy at 3-month lows, financials rolling over, industrials down, discretionary showing weakness. Few pockets of strength; IT and healthcare were the main gainers.
- **Options positioning looks normal** [07:04]: SPX put skew elevated on near-term (20-day) expiration but not excessive on 17-Jan and 21-Feb. Volume not extreme, suggesting market had some anticipation of volatility.
- **GameStop relative strength** [10:23]: GME held above key price levels (high $20s) despite broad selloff. Call open interest remains very heavy (~840k calls vs. 286k puts), mostly in 17-Jan expiration. Put side saw some unwinding from earlier large positions.
- **Meme-stock cohort resilience** [12:33]: PLTR down only 4%, RKLB down 13%, IONQ down but not catastrophic. Growth names showing less damage than expected, suggesting this is not a capitulation event yet.
- **Futures stabilizing** [08:14]: Futures up slightly post-close, which is a positive signal (reversals after big down days often precede further weakness; acceleration after close signals real panic).
