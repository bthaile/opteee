# Market Melt Up into Global Bust | David Hunter

## Summary

David Hunter presents a contrarian macro thesis: despite widespread bearish sentiment, the S&P 500 is poised for a parabolic rally from ~4,000 to 4,600+ in the near term, followed by a global deflationary bust later in 2023. The Fed has tightened too aggressively and will be forced to pause or pivot as economic weakness becomes undeniable; this recognition will trigger a capitulation rally as institutional capital reallocates from bonds back into equities. Precious metals and commodities are positioned to outperform as the dollar weakens and real rates compress.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2023-01-23)

- **Fed tightening cycle is nearly complete** [02:38–03:22]: The speed of rate increases from near-zero to ~5% is the real damage, not the absolute level. Economic weakness is already visible in PPI, CPI, and commodity prices but the Fed is managing policy month-to-month and will be caught flat-footed.
- **S&P 500 technical setup favors breakout** [20:06–21:23]: Market bottomed October 13, 2022 (CPI reversal). Currently consolidating around the 200-day moving average (~4,000). Hunter expects a decisive break above 4,000 → 4,300 → 4,600 in the next 1–3 months, with new all-time highs triggering the parabolic phase.
- **Bond market is leading; Fed is lagging** [25:42–28:27]: The 10-year yield has fallen ~100 bps from 4.35% to 3.35%, signaling the market has already priced in peak rates and economic weakness. The Fed continues hawkish messaging despite this signal, creating a dangerous policy lag.
- **Precious metals and commodities are early leaders** [37:30–39:57]: Gold and silver have already begun their bull move; Hunter targets $3,000 gold and $50–60 silver (more likely $60). Copper at $6, miners outperforming, and broad commodity strength suggest risk-on rotation is underway.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Market discounting vs. Fed backward-looking data** [11:12–13:21]: Economists and the Fed focus on month-to-month data and miss inflection points. Markets are forward-looking and discount 6–9 months ahead. Retail traders often chase short-term noise while missing the forest; professional strategists focus on longer-term trends.
- **Allocation reversion as the primary driver** [22:31–23:20]: Over 9 months in 2022, institutions reallocated away from equities into bonds. Hunter expects this to reverse in 3–5 months, creating a concentrated, illiquid rally with outsized moves.
- **Fed credibility and policy error** [30:28–33:14]: Powell was burned by the "transitory" call and is now overcorrecting. The Fed is targeting financial conditions (stock market weakness) rather than economic data, which is backwards. A pause—not a pivot—will be the inflection point the market needs.
- **Commodity prices as leading indicators** [05:28–06:09]: Lumber, copper, steel, and oil have already collapsed from 2022 peaks, signaling demand destruction ahead. This is the "leads and lags" framework: commodity weakness precedes equity weakness by months.

## Notable quotes

> "Things happen slowly until they don't, and then all of a sudden they break in the other direction."

> "The bond market is a much better predictor of the future than any economist or the Fed. In the aggregate, the market is a discounting mechanism and has a good track record of seeing the future better than any forecaster."

> "If the Fed pauses, you can have an awful lot of people on the bear side reach the same conclusion at once: 'Fed tightening may be over, this is the time to buy.' That's when you'll see how liquid this market is."

## Regime / context

**Date:** January 23, 2023. This is the fifth quarterly check-in between Eric and David Hunter. The S&P 500 is consolidating near 3,900–4,000 after a ~20% rally from the October 2022 lows; the Fed has paused its rate-hike cycle at 4.33% but continues hawkish forward guidance. Inflation (CPI/PPI) is rolling over faster than consensus expected, but the Fed is not yet acknowledging the policy error. This is a critical inflection point in Hunter's macro thesis: the setup for a melt-up rally into a later-year deflationary bust.
