# 0DTE SPX Short Straddle Option Strategy

## Summary

Eric walks through a live short straddle trade on SPX expiring same-day (16 May), sold at the 4125 strike for ~$18,080 premium on a 10-lot. The trade capitalized on elevated implied volatility relative to realized volatility ahead of a catalyst-heavy economic calendar (retail sales, Fed speakers). The position was held into expiration, netting ~$3,130 profit (0.53% ROI on ~$594k margin), demonstrating a repeatable volatility-overstatement hypothesis executed with active risk management via ATR-based exit thresholds.

## Key takeaways

- **Catalyst + IV skew = entry signal** [00:39–01:01]: Short straddles work best when implied volatility is elevated relative to realized volatility *and* there is a known catalyst (economic data, Fed speakers) that justifies the elevated premium.
- **Why SPX over SPY** [01:36–02:21]: SPX is cash-settled, eliminating round-trip friction on assignment; this matters for holding into expiration. SPY is viable for smaller accounts (1/10th the notional).
- **IV vs. realized volatility comparison** [04:46–06:20]: On 15 May, 30-day IV was 17.61%, 7-day IV was 18.01%, but 5-day realized volatility was only 6%. This overstatement justified the short premium.
- **Position sizing and margin efficiency** [06:39–07:51]: 10-lot collected $18,080 on $594k portfolio margin = 3% ROI in one day. Annualized, this is ~589% (not a daily repeatable claim, but illustrates capital efficiency).
- **Active monitoring, not set-and-forget** [09:32–10:14]: Risk plan: exit if price moves beyond 5-day average true range (ATR) or approaches 5× loss threshold. Emotional discipline and real-time observation are non-negotiable.
- **Context matters: market regime** [11:15–11:37]: Since February, SPX has shown large intraday trending candles; this informed the decision to give the trade room to move rather than use tight percentage-based stops.
- **Sample size and aggregate performance** [13:17–14:01]: One winning trade is not a strategy. Short straddles require a decent sample size to prove edge; many will be small losses when IV pricing is accurate.
- **Avoid undefined loss without guardrails** [14:24–15:00]: Short straddles have theoretically unlimited loss. Mitigate by (a) active monitoring, (b) ATR/support-resistance exit thresholds, (c) position sizing relative to account.

## Regime / context

**Date:** 16 May 2023 (0DTE expiration). **Market regime:** SPX had been trading sideways with large intraday trending candles since February; this informed the decision to use ATR-based exits rather than tight percentage stops. **Catalyst:** Busy economic calendar (retail sales, industrial production, capacity utilization, business inventories, housing index, six Fed speakers) drove elevated IV. **Portfolio margin:** Trade required ~$594k margin; retail traders should scale down to SPY or reduce lot size to adapt the hypothesis to their account.
