# Market Reaction to Economic Data & GME Analysis

## Summary

Broad markets declined on Jackson Hole Fed commentary and mixed economic data, with tech (QQQ) showing relative weakness and small caps (IWM) lagging. GME consolidated around the $22 handle with light options volume; volatility term structure showed notable mispricing between September and October expirations. Fed funds futures shifted toward a 25 bp cut probability, signaling less acute economic stress.

## Key takeaways

- **Broad market structure [00:16–01:45]**: SPX near previous highs with moderate decline; QQQ ~80% recovered to near-term highs before rejection; IWM ~60% recovered with continued pressure. Tech showed one-directional selling (minimal wicks); NYSE breadth remained resilient (136 net highs vs. 142 prior day).
- **Breadth deterioration [01:45–02:14]**: QQQ net highs/lows contracted sharply from 112 to 62; NYSE net highs stable at 136–142, indicating small-cap weakness persists while large-cap breadth holds.
- **Volatility and bonds [02:36–02:57]**: VIX cracked above 17.2; TLT declined to ~124; author holds small long VIX futures position as hedge, not expecting material acceleration.
- **Economic data mixed [03:17–03:45]**: Jobless claims on consensus; Global manufacturing PMI light; services PMI above expectations. Market awaiting Powell Jackson Hole speech for rate-cut clarity.
- **Fed funds shift [04:11–04:32]**: Probability of 25 bp cut increased significantly (vs. prior 50 bp lean), suggesting market pricing less acute economic deterioration.
- **GME consolidation [06:04–06:51]**: Down 1.9% on heavy market day; volume light (3.3M total, 150K buy vs. 3.2M sell); consolidating around $22 consensus value; not rejecting to new lows.
- **GME options structure [07:17–07:40]**: Call/put OI scattered across expirations; put OI concentrated in Jan25, Oct24, Sep24; call OI mixed. Total options volume 65K calls / 15K puts (light).
- **Volatility anomalies [08:18–09:00]**: Six-step (Sep) expiration IV at 83% (up from mid-70s), tracking earnings cycle; Sep27–Oct4 IV cut in half (80.91% → 41.8%), potential mispricing; Oct18 IV back to 88.2%. Author trading IV rather than delta given lack of directional conviction.
