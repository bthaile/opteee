# VIX Isn't the Whole Story

## Summary

VIX measures S&P 500 implied volatility only and does not represent volatility across the entire market. Even when VIX is at historically low levels (14), individual sectors and securities can exhibit significantly different implied volatility percentiles, creating trading opportunities and risk management considerations that a single index volatility metric cannot capture.

## Key takeaways

- **VIX is index-specific, not market-wide** [00:00] — VIX reflects only S&P 500 volatility; low VIX does not mean low volatility everywhere in the market.
- **Sector IV percentiles vary widely** [00:26] — Gold at 56% IV percentile, materials at 33%, healthcare at 17%, staples at 23%, while S&P IV percentile is near zero.
- **High volatility exists somewhere when VIX is low** [00:54] — Traders should scan across sectors and securities rather than relying on a single volatility metric for portfolio construction or hedging decisions.

## Notable quotes

> "VIX does not equal all volatility. VIX is the S&P 500 volatility."

> "Even though implied volatility percentile in S&P is literally zero, that does not mean everything is low V."
