# GameStop Deep Dive: Options Chains, Roaring Kitty's Strategy & Settlement Cycles

## Summary

Eric analyzes historical options chains from the January 2021 GameStop squeeze, reconstructs Roaring Kitty's trading approach from public records, and examines settlement cycle theories circulating in the ape community. He concludes that Roaring Kitty's strategy was fundamentally valuation-driven with extreme leverage and conviction, not timing-based, and reviews research on systemic leverage risks and exit strategies for retail traders.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2021-12-28)

- **Options chain forensics** [07:36]: Eric maintains full historical options chains downloaded directly from Cboe, enabling deep analysis of volume and open-interest shifts. The January 2021 chain showed focal heavy strikes but no anomalies suggesting pre-planned coordination.
- **Roaring Kitty's core thesis** [11:12]: Valuation-based entry with extreme leverage applied via long-dated calls; not optimized for theta decay or volatility plays, but for unit building and capital amplification.
- **Risk tolerance & capital structure** [17:26]: Roaring Kitty segregated his $100k GameStop bet into a dedicated account, implying other positions elsewhere. This mirrors professional risk discipline—treating speculative capital as already lost.
- **Settlement cycle skepticism** [23:16]: Eric rejects the theory that Roaring Kitty was trading 35-day settlement cycles. Portfolio volatility (swings from $47M to $17M) contradicts reliable cycle exploitation; longer-dated options don't align with 35-day cycles.
- **Margin call mechanics** [54:26]: Margin calls prevent default; they don't trigger it. Successive margin calls can siphon liquidity, but a single call is a risk-management tool, not a catastrophe signal.
- **Archegos case study** [59:36]: Five times leverage at Archegos caused ~$10B in losses across Credit Suisse, Nomura, UBS, and Morgan Stanley—material but not systemic. CS still earned $19.9B revenue in 2022; MS earned $59B. Leverage abuse is endemic but contained.
- **Exit strategy research** [01:08:11]: Community DD on scaling out and stop-market orders (vs. stop-limit) highlighted; prior move analysis recommended for position unwinding.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Full options chain data as edge**: Maintaining historical chains enables pattern detection unavailable to retail; volume and open-interest ramps are early signals.
- **Valuation + leverage as a durable model**: Simple thesis + extreme conviction + capital discipline = outsized returns; no hidden timing code required.
- **Confirmation bias in pattern-seeking**: Ambiguous communication (memes, tweets) invites retrofitting; community analysis can be creative but not necessarily predictive.
- **Leverage as a double-edged tool**: Informed speculators use it; risk management separates winners from blowups. Archegos and Nomura losses were absorbed by larger portfolios.
- **Fear-driven narratives**: Negative research gains traction; consciously filter doomsday framing to avoid subconscious priming toward panic exits.
