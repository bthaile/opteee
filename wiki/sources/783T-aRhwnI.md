# GameStop Technical Analysis & Naked Short Selling Deep Dive with Lucy Commissar

## Summary

Eric conducts a detailed technical analysis of GameStop's recent 10% single-day decline with no news catalyst, examining price action across multiple timeframes and the options chain. The stream pivots to a deep historical investigation of naked short selling through Lucy Commissar's research, tracing coordinated offshore manipulation schemes (Sedona Software case study) and their structural parallels to modern market dysfunction. The session emphasizes the distinction between short-term downtrend and long-term uptrend, while exploring systemic failures in SEC enforcement and media capture.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Multi-timeframe divergence as capitulation signal**: When short-term downtrend diverges from long-term uptrend, watch for either breakdown below key levels or consolidation/base-out pattern [21:02]
- **Roaring Kitty post-reversal tradability**: Correlation exists between posts and stock movement; reversal component within several days is also tradable; requires quantitative analysis to confirm [17:14]
- **Volume node identification**: Zones of heavy transacting become support/resistance; breaks below volume nodes signal trend continuation [13:16]
- **Naked short selling mechanics**: Coordinated offshore schemes use electronic communications networks (ECNs) to hide seller identity; brokers execute aggressive short sales across multiple entities to suppress price; settlement failures enable phantom shares [56:53–01:01:32]
- **Death spiral financing**: Convertible debt structures where conversion price depends on stock price at conversion date; if price suppressed via naked shorting, debt converts to excessive share dilution, enabling control transfer [58:42]
- **Regulatory arbitrage**: SEC Reg SHO (2005) included massive loopholes; trades routed through non-reporting jurisdictions (Canada, dark pools, internalization) escape disclosure; offshore funds registered in Panama/Zurich coordinate with US brokers [01:16:08]
- **Extradition gaps**: Perpetrators fleeing to non-treaty countries (Austria) avoid prosecution but face lifetime restrictions on US travel/assets; not "free" but delayed justice [01:17:18]
- **Media capture mechanism**: Large corporations sponsor/donate to news outlets with implicit quid pro quo: no negative coverage in exchange for "partnership" branding; journalists paid via prepaid cards to write negative articles on target stocks [01:20:16–01:21:46]
- **State court discovery advantage**: Federal courts may dismiss on jurisdictional grounds (SEC-only); state courts allow discovery, exposing manipulation evidence [01:18:37]
