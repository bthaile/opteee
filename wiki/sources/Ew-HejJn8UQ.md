# Why You're Not Sizing Your Portfolio Correctly Instead Use Vol

## Summary

Position sizing should be calibrated to realized volatility and expected move magnitude, not notional capital alone. A $10,000 position in [[spy]] and [[tsla]] will experience dramatically different P&L swings (~$860 vs ~$1,433 respectively) due to volatility differences, requiring proportional downsizing of higher-volatility names to maintain consistent risk across a portfolio.

## Key takeaways

- **Volatility-adjusted position sizing** [00:00–00:31]: Plot expected moves against fixed capital to reveal P&L variance; [[spy]] and [[tsla]] show ~40% variance in dollar risk for identical notional positions.
- **Risk parity across names** [00:31]: When comparing two securities, size down the higher-volatility position (e.g., [[tsla]]) relative to lower-volatility peers (e.g., [[spy]]) to equalize dollar risk exposure.
- **Management plan precision** [00:00–00:31]: Volatility-aware sizing improves [[trading-plan]] execution and [[stop-loss]] placement by anchoring them to realistic P&L ranges rather than arbitrary percentages.
