# 6 Things I've Learned over 16 Years of Trading

## Summary

Eric reflects on 16 years of trading experience, distilling six nuanced lessons beyond basic risk management. The core themes are: markets are indifferent to individual traders; theoretical brilliance without practical application fails; ego removal is non-negotiable; probabilistic thinking beats conviction; iterative process beats static thesis; and structured documentation (trading plan + log) is the foundation of sustainable profitability.

## Key takeaways

- **Markets are indifferent** [00:49–01:38]: The market does not care about you, your convenience, or your desired outcomes. This is not cruelty—it is indifference. Small accounts are structurally disadvantaged (like being short in professional basketball), but disadvantage is not impossibility; you must find alternative paths.

- **Theory ≠ Practice** [04:18–05:09]: Brilliant people often struggle in trading because they over-explain and over-commit to narrative. What matters is price action, not whether your thesis was theoretically correct. Trading is practical application, not intellectual purity.

- **Ego removal is non-negotiable** [05:53–06:39]: Prestigious professionals (doctors, lawyers) often trade poorly due to ego and over-conviction in their own summaries. The difference between prediction (high certainty) and forecast (probabilistic idea) matters; trading requires balance.

- **Think probabilistically** [06:39–07:24]: View directional forecasts as probabilistic outcomes (e.g., 60% chance of happening, 40% chance of not). A single losing trade does not kill you; overemphasizing individual positions kills you. Profits come from systematic process applied over time.

- **Iterate and test, don't overfit** [08:17–09:24]: Your trading plan evolves. In 2023, Eric noticed near-term variance risk premium behavior diverging from longer-term patterns, tested alternatives, and switched to a better structure. Maintain paper vs. live comparison to detect overfitting; adjust for market conditions without driving forward looking in the rearview mirror.

- **Ignore noise; be autonomous** [09:47–11:21]: Do not follow pundits, Twitter predictions, or others' market forecasts. Gather information (news, economist views) but do not let it override your own skill application. Most market moves are not discreetly explained by any single narrative; acknowledge the unknown.

- **Structure and documentation are foundational** [11:41–13:13]: Without a trading plan and trading log, you are flying blind. These documents enable hypothesis testing (e.g., testing 12, 21, 30, 40 DTE for covered strangles) and kill ego by replacing conviction with data. This is where all the work and all the benefit of trading reside.
