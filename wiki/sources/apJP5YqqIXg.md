# UFC Fighter Turned Trader - Nik Lentz | The Outlier Podcast Ep69

## Summary

Nik Lentz, a former UFC fighter with nearly 30 professional bouts, discusses his unconventional path from childhood adversity through elite combat sports into quantitative macro trading. He details how he built a systematic approach combining quantitative asset-spread analysis, technical trend-following, and macro fundamentals across uncorrelated markets—primarily futures and options—while maintaining strict risk discipline and a multi-timeframe strategy spanning 1–3 weeks and 3–6 months.

## Key takeaways

### Background & psychology
- **Childhood adversity as foundation** [03:55–04:42]: Grew up in a chaotic household with substance abuse; was removed and placed with his father at age 13, then struggled with unprocessed trauma during college wrestling, leading to depression and eventual dropout.
- **Reframing failure as learning** [09:00–09:47]: After crashing out of college wrestling, he "went off the rails" for a year but used that period to process trauma and rebuild self-worth through fighting, eventually becoming a ranked UFC contender.
- **Obsessive personality as asset and liability** [06:01–07:19]: Recognized his tendency to overtrain and overwork; deliberately sought markets as a positive distraction to channel obsessive energy away from fighting injuries while maintaining mental engagement.

### Trading origin & evolution
- **Entry point: post-GFC fear** [21:18–21:58]: Began accumulating capital during UFC career but was traumatized by 2008 financial crisis; avoided equities initially despite perfect timing for entry.
- **One-year structured study** [23:57–24:36]: Committed 8 hours daily for a year to study stocks, bonds, and market mechanics before opening a trading account; initial success came from buying the Christmas Eve 2017 crash.
- **Pivot from stocks to macro** [31:25–32:30]: Realized stocks are "horrible macro assets" that don't respond predictably to macro events; shifted focus to bonds, currencies, volatility spreads, and SOFR curves for better edge and lower stress.
- **Mentorship model** [39:55–40:34]: Reached out to world-class traders on Twitter, demonstrated serious intent by mastering their teachings, and built a network of 4–5 exceptional mentors.

### System architecture
- **Three-pillar approach** [40:54–42:30]: Combines quantitative scoring (asset spreads, relative performance models), technical analysis (trend-following, moving averages, fractal time measurements), and macro fundamentals; only trades when all three align ("wind at your back").
- **Uncorrelated asset streams** [51:44–53:09]: Core goal is to identify and trade 10–15 uncorrelated asset relationships; acknowledges this is "almost impossible" but essential for portfolio resilience; notes that correlations shift constantly (e.g., stocks–bonds, gold–TIPS now 70% correlated).
- **Product selection: Futures over options** [43:40–44:41]: Prefers futures for control over exit mechanics and lack of theta drag; uses options on ETF equivalents when liquidity is superior; spread-trades futures against each other.

### Time-frame strategy
- **Two discrete buckets** [45:40–46:43]: Trades 1–3 week positions and 3–6 month positions; avoids the 45-day "middle ground" where he lacks edge; uses 30–40 day options expecting a pop within 1–2 weeks.
- **Trade-specific duration** [48:02–49:37]: Example: short USD/JPY intervention trade held in two tranches (August and November expirations) because timing a carry-trade blow-up within days is unrealistic; lets the thesis dictate holding period.
- **Separate accounts by timeframe** [47:45–48:02]: Maintains distinct accounts for short and long positions; evaluates daily which regime is active and allocates accordingly.

### Risk & psychology
- **Stress as signal** [35:02–36:07]: If a trade causes stress, it's a sign the thesis is weak; when properly positioned with quantitative + technical + macro alignment, losses don't trigger emotional response.
- **Drawdown tolerance** [36:40–37:20]: Lost ~75% of gains in a multi-week stock-trading drawdown; realized he couldn't trade that way; shifted to systems where he has quantifiable edge and can weather drawdowns without panic.
- **Compounding over volatility** [01:00:24–01:01:52]: Advises against chasing unrealistic short-term returns (10% per month); emphasizes that consistent, boring execution over years compounds into extraordinary wealth; early career volatility smooths into exponential growth.

### Long-term vision
- **10-year mastery goal** [54:43–56:25]: Plans to be "one of the best traders in the world" within a decade; success defined not by fame but by ability to support family, eliminate financial stress, and acquire deep market wisdom.
- **Success redefined** [56:45–58:25]: Rejects external validation; measures success by freedom and family security, not public recognition; would have fought in underground pits if it paid the same.

## Notable quotes

> "I will be one of the best traders in the world in 10 years. I have the capability, the support structure, and the backdrop. The failure is a real option—I've failed plenty of times—but every year I've exponentially gotten better."

> "Relax on the shorter timeframe, don't be so stressed about making money now, but don't underestimate what's possible. If you do the right things, one day you wake up and you have a ton of money and a ton of freedom."

## Regime / context

Recorded July 2024. Lentz discusses his evolution across multiple market regimes (post-GFC recovery, 2017–2018 volatility, 2022 bear market, 2023 rally). His system is designed to be regime-agnostic through uncorrelated asset selection and macro-driven entry signals. The interview emphasizes long-term compounding and psychological resilience rather than regime-specific tactics.
