# Contango and Backwardation Explained

## Summary

Contango and backwardation describe the shape of the volatility term structure across different expiration cycles. Contango (normal state) occurs when longer-dated expirations carry higher implied volatility than near-term; backwardation occurs when front-month volatility is priced richer than further-out cycles, typically during elevated volatility regimes.

## Key takeaways

- **Contango (normal):** longer-dated options/futures have higher IV than near-term; the curve slopes up and to the right [00:57]
- **Backwardation:** front-month or spot volatility is priced higher than future expirations; occurs during elevated volatility periods [01:35]
- **VIX futures as visualization:** the VIX futures curve cleanly illustrates these relationships across expiration cycles [00:00]
- **Practical application:** understanding term structure shapes helps traders anticipate volatility mean reversion and position accordingly across different time horizons [00:29]
