# Scanning for Unusual Options and Speculative Positions

## Summary

This video walks through a systematic approach to scanning for unusual options activity, focusing on volume-over-open-interest metrics and premium thresholds to isolate directional speculation from hedging or stock-replication trades. The key insight is filtering for out-of-the-money positions with near-term expiration, which signals genuine directional conviction rather than synthetic equity positioning.

## Key takeaways

- **Volume-over-OI scan with premium floor** [00:00]: Start with scans for unusual relative volume or OI changes; apply a minimum premium threshold (e.g., $50,000) to filter out noise and small retail trades.
- **Out-of-the-money bias for directional trades** [00:00]: In-the-money options are typically used for stock replication or hedging; out-of-the-money positions indicate directional speculation.
- **Near-term expiration as conviction signal** [00:23]: Out-of-the-money calls expiring in ~30 days suggest the trader expects directional movement within that window—a stronger signal than longer-dated positions.
