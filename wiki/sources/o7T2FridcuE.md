# Profit Mechanisms in Unusual Options

## Summary

This segment outlines three core profit mechanisms available when trading unusual options activity: directional continuation of the expected move, reversal trades after aggressive positioning, and volatility arbitrage between individual options. The critical insight is that term structure—whether unusual activity occurs weeks or months out—fundamentally changes the inference and impact of the signal.

## Key takeaways

- **Three profit mechanisms from unusual options:** continuation of expected move, reversal after aggressive transactions, or volatility trading relative to other options [00:00–00:24]
- **Term structure determines signal weight:** large trades expiring in 2 weeks carry far greater impact than identical trades expiring a year out [00:24–00:48]
- **Volatility arbitrage:** when an option is bid up massively, you can trade the relative IV of that option versus others in the same chain [00:24]
- **Trade alignment:** once you identify which profit mechanism to target, build positions that align with your directional or volatility expectation [00:48]
