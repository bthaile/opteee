# Trading is Easy, Just Stick to The Process

## Summary

The core of successful trading lies not in the mechanics of clicking the button, but in the disciplined pre-trade process of answering three critical questions: profit-taking rules, loss-exit criteria, and position duration. Once those logical answers are established, execution becomes straightforward—the hard work is done before entry.

## Key takeaways

- **Thesis management vs. P&L management are separate concerns** [00:00]. Your thesis can remain valid even if profit or loss targets aren't hit; invalidation occurs only when the underlying idea is contradicted by new information.
- **Duration management is foundational** [00:26]. Know upfront how long you expect the trade to take; this drives all downstream decisions.
- **Answer three questions before you trade** [00:26]:
  1. When do I take profits?
  2. When do I exit if it goes against me?
  3. How long do I expect this to take?
- **The actual trading process is simple once the plan is solid** [00:50]. All difficulty is in the pre-trade logic; execution is mechanical.
- **Trading psychology is about trusting a pre-built plan, not improvising at the button** [00:26]. Avoid decisions based on "fairy dust"—use logical, repeatable criteria.
