# The Myth of Complex Options Strategies #optionstrading

## Summary

Options strategies sold to retail traders are often fundamentally flawed because they violate linearity: you cannot combine losing trades to create a winning outcome. If a multi-leg strategy is profitable, at least one leg must be profitable; the others are likely drains on capital. The practical takeaway is to isolate and trade the profitable leg directly while eliminating the losing components and managing risk more effectively.

## Key takeaways

- **Linearity principle** [00:00–00:25]: Options strategies are linear operations. You cannot sum two losing trades to produce a winning trade. If a complex strategy is profitable, at least one leg must carry the edge.
- **Identify the profitable leg** [00:25–00:46]: In a multi-leg strategy, isolate which component is actually making money. Likely only one leg is profitable; the others are losing money and should be eliminated.
- **Simplify and manage risk** [00:46]: Rather than executing a complex strategy with multiple losing legs, trade the profitable component directly and apply better risk management to improve overall returns.
