# Finding Option Trades in Low Volatility Markets

## Summary

This video addresses how to identify and trade opportunities in low-volatility market environments, emphasizing that suppressed VIX levels (12–14) are historically normal and can persist. Eric demonstrates that while broad-market volatility (measured by VIX and large-cap indices) is muted, volatility exists in pockets—individual stocks, sectors, and event-driven catalysts—and provides practical scans to locate tradable opportunities across directional, premium-selling, and variance-premium strategies.

## Key takeaways

- **Volatility is not uniformly low** [00:47–04:52]: VIX at ~13 is historically common (>30% of days since 1990 fall below 15) and not an extreme. Volatility clusters and can remain suppressed for extended periods; mean reversion alone is insufficient justification for VIX calls. Large-cap indices (SPX, NDX, IWM) show low IV percentiles (10–15%), but this does not mean all equities are low-vol.

- **Distinguish systematic from unsystematic volatility** [03:41–04:52]: VIX reflects S&P 500 large-caps only. Russell 2000 and individual stocks frequently exhibit elevated volatility even when broad indices are calm. Relying solely on VIX to assess market-wide opportunity is a critical mistake.

- **Scan sectors and exchanges for pockets of volatility** [06:02–07:49]: Compare net highs/lows across NASDAQ and NYSE; examine sector rotation (energy at 22% IV percentile vs. financials at 4%). Muted price action and centered sector positioning indicate dampening; downside acceleration signals higher volatility.

- **Identify catalysts for volatility** [08:32–09:50]: Earnings releases, biotech trial announcements, FOMC statements, CPI/PPI data, sector rotations (e.g., AI), M&A news, and commodity moves all generate tradable volatility. These are perpetual sources; even slower months have opportunities.

- **Use practical scans to locate opportunities** [13:20–15:45]:
  - Filter for optionable stocks with weeklies and IV percentile ≥40 (206+ names in example).
  - Scan for IV expansion >15% over recent bars (19 names in example).
  - Identify stocks down ≥10% over 10 days to trade directional or volatility plays (65 names in example).

- **Broaden your skill set beyond short volatility** [10:12–11:40]: When broad-market volatility is suppressed, deploy ratio diagonals, pairs trades, variance-risk-premium plays, leveraged ETF strategies (e.g., TQQQ), zero-DTE, and earnings plays. Eric's 2024 YTD return is ~10.9% from speculative allocation, with core holdings contributing the bulk of capital but lower returns.

- **Earnings are a perpetual return source** [11:58–12:41]: Stock-market calendars show earnings are never completely absent; filter for weeklies to ensure optionability. Earnings volatility plays are a variance-premium strategy.

## Notable quotes

> "Volatility clusters. It can stay in a specific area for quite some time."

> "If you're using VIX to then assume all stocks across the entire stock market are now no longer volatile, you're grossly mistaken and you're really cutting yourself off from finding a lot of great opportunities."

> "Your job as a trader is to identify what the market is doing and then to fit what you're doing to what the market is doing."
