# Avoid Back Testing Pitfalls #optionstrading

## Summary

Backtesting is essential for long-term trading success, but most traders fail to recognize the statistical and cognitive pitfalls embedded in the process. The video addresses three critical biases: availability bias (easy-to-access information is widely known and unlikely to contain edge), desired-outcome bias (confirmation bias that poisons decision-making), and the broader failure to apply rigorous statistical methodology to backtest design and interpretation.

## Key takeaways

- **Backtesting is necessary but dangerous** [00:00] — most traders feel productive while backtesting but don't understand how to apply basic statistical rigor to the process.
- **Availability bias erodes edge** [00:24] — if information is easy to access, it is widely known; easy-to-access trading signals are unlikely to contain meaningful edge.
- **Desired-outcome bias poisons the entire analysis** [00:46] — entering a backtest with a predetermined outcome in mind corrupts all downstream decision-making and invalidates the results.
- **Statistical literacy is foundational** [00:00] — traders must understand statistical approaches to backtesting; without this foundation, biases go unrecognized.
