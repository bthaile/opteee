# NO HIGH FIVES with Tom Sosnoff #optionstrading

## Summary

Tom Sosnoff discusses the "no high fives" rule—a core principle of his trading philosophy that rejects complacency and self-congratulation. The rule emphasizes that traders should never become impressed with their own performance, maintain focus on process rather than outcome, and avoid building businesses with predetermined exit strategies. Success is treated as a baseline expectation, not an achievement to celebrate.

## Key takeaways

- **The no high fives rule** [00:00]: Never become impressed with trading results; treat success as a baseline expectation, not an accomplishment worthy of celebration.
- **Exit strategy paradox** [00:18]: Building a business with a predetermined exit strategy signals misalignment of focus; successful traders listen to acquisition offers opportunistically rather than planning exits in advance.
- **Process over outcome** [00:00]: The rule enforces discipline by keeping traders focused on execution and edge rather than celebrating wins, which can breed complacency.
