# Rate Cuts & GME | Stock Market News

## Summary

Eric provides a broad market update ahead of the Fed's expected 25–50 basis point rate cut on September 18, noting that a 25 bp cut has already been largely priced in while a 50 bp cut remains less than 50% probable and could move markets more meaningfully. He reviews sector rotation (Tech contracting, Industrials and Financials expanding), bond positioning, and the Russell 2000's lag, then pivots to GameStop's post-earnings consolidation above $20 with elevated volume and quiet options flow.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2024-09-13)

- **S&P 500 near all-time highs; Tech lagging; Russell 2000 significantly behind** [00:45] — SPY has rallied back to near all-time highs, but QQQ is still away from recent highs and IWM is lagging considerably.
- **VIX down ~16.5; NYSE net lows evaporated** [01:11] — Broad market strength reflected in NYSE breadth; NASDAQ still gaining but not as dramatically.
- **Fed rate cut probability shift: 25 bp largely priced in; 50 bp now ~50% probable** [03:32] — Bond futures volume dying down, suggesting the 25 bp cut was priced weeks ago; the revival of 50 bp probability is the new market variable.
- **Bond positioning already captured; late arrivals face higher risk** [05:06] — 30-year, 10-year, and 2-year bonds all running below 5-day and 20-day average volume; the bond rally has already happened.
- **Sector rotation: Tech contracting (since Q2 2023), Industrials and Financials expanding** [06:47] — Institutional exposure shows Tech concentration declining quarter-over-quarter; Industrials and Financial Services consuming more portfolio transactions.
- **Recession probability underestimated; bond-equity correlation normalizing but fragile** [02:45] — Goldman Sachs 20% recession probability seems low; bond-equity relationship broke down 2020–present and is only now normalizing, making risk-off hedges unreliable.
- **Trading tight stops in indices; long e-mini QQQ and Russell positions with resting exit orders** [09:24] — Using tight stops and resting orders to exit if market reverses; not fully committed to the upside move.

### GameStop post-earnings (2024-09-13)

- **GME holding above $20 on inside day; 1.23% up; elevated volume persists** [10:36] — Today 8M shares, yesterday 92M; prior quiet period averaged 1.5–4.5M, so current volume still 2–6× baseline.
- **Options flow: 172k calls vs. 49k puts; mostly rolling into Sept 20 expiration** [11:42] — Sept 13 calls closing, Sept 20 calls opening; no massive concentration suggesting directional conviction.
- **Open interest ~730k calls, 361k puts; healthy distribution across expirations** [12:09] — No single strike or expiration showing unusual concentration.
- **Notable trade: 287k Sept 18 $15 calls; 174k 2025-dated calls** [12:57] — Larger trades but not exceptional by GME standards; future-dated positioning suggests patience.
- **Dark pool: 5,000-share blocks ebbing and flowing; 30–35+ on Sept 13, massive on Sept 12** [14:15] — Tracking 5k-share dark pool transactions as a proxy for institutional activity; no monopoly evident but pattern worth monitoring.
- **Options chain quiet; stock quiet; waiting for catalyst** [15:50] — No unusual options activity; consolidation phase until next move.
