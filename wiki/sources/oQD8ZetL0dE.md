# Selling Earnings Straddles #optionstrading

## Summary

This video demonstrates a short-straddle strategy applied across earnings releases, exploiting the historical tendency for implied volatility to overprice realized volatility through an earnings cycle. The presenter sells at-the-money straddles into earnings and shows backtested results indicating profitability on average, though with expected drawdowns and a mix of small winners and occasional losers.

## Key takeaways

- **Core edge:** implied volatility systematically overprices realized volatility around earnings; selling straddles captures this premium [00:00]
- **Execution:** sell at-the-money straddles into the close before earnings [00:32]
- **Expected outcome profile:** small, frequent winners with occasional larger losers—typical of short-volatility strategies [00:32]
- **No filtering applied:** the backtest used raw earnings straddle sales with no additional entry logic or risk filters [00:52]
- **Single sample caveat:** one backtest result is insufficient to draw robust conclusions; the general concept (IV > RV at earnings) is the takeaway, not the specific P&L [00:52]
