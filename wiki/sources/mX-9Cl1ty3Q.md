# Expected Return in Options Trading

## Summary

High probability of winning does not equal profitability in options trading. A trade with 95% win probability but negative expected value will lose money over the long run. Traders must shift focus from win rate and profit potential to expected return—the only metric that determines long-term survival.

## Key takeaways

- **Expected value trumps win rate** [00:00–00:26]: A 95% chance of making $50 with a 5% chance of losing $1,000 has negative expected return and will lose money systematically, despite the high probability.
- **Abandon the profitability illusion** [00:26]: The "good feeling" of high win rates is meaningless if the strategy loses money; traders must focus on expected return, not probability of winning.
- **Expected return is the survival metric** [00:26]: Long-term success in trading depends entirely on expected return, not on the frequency of wins or the size of individual profits.

## Notable quotes

> "High probability trades does not mean profitable—this is a massively commonly misunderstood concept specifically for option sellers."

> "You got to abandon this hyperfocus on profit potential and high probability of winning and you have to focus way more on the expected return—it's the only way to survive in this business long term."
