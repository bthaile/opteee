# Tracking Trading Signals

## Summary

Effective trading requires systematic documentation of signal performance rather than relying on memory or intuition. By recording when specific technical signals trigger (e.g., Bollinger Band touches) and their outcomes in a spreadsheet, traders can objectively measure signal efficacy and their own ability to execute them. No universal signal works for all traders; individual skill and fit determine which signals become reliable edges.

## Key takeaways

- **Document everything in a trading log** [00:00–00:21]: Write down when signals trigger, your thesis, and the actual outcome. This transforms vague recollection ("Bollinger Bands seem to work") into measurable data.
- **Measure both signal and execution quality** [00:21–00:40]: Track not just whether a signal is theoretically sound, but whether *you* can use it effectively. Some signals will naturally suit your style better than others.
- **No magic signal exists** [00:40]: The absence of a universal signal that works for everyone underscores the importance of personal testing and fit. Edge comes from matching signal to trader, not from the signal alone.
