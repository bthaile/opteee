# GameStop Covered Strangle Pt2

## Summary

Eric walks through live position updates on a covered strangle in GameStop, demonstrating tactical adjustments to allocation ($15k → $25k), short put placement at multiple deltas (44Δ and 25Δ across two expirations), and plans to add a second short call to increase capital efficiency. The position is tracking ~$3k P&L on $25k deployed, with emphasis on exploiting elevated implied volatility (117%, IV rank 58%) while managing early-assignment risk via ratio management rather than 1:1 hedging.

## Key takeaways

- **Technical setup**: GME posting higher highs and higher lows on a steep 6-month trend; 5-day average daily range 6.47%, 20-day 6.6%, 90-day 5.98% — near-term volatility elevated relative to longer-term baseline [01:13–02:20]
- **Volatility signal**: 17 Jan expiration IV at 140.6 vol vs. 123 vol on 24 Jan — market is pricing something undisclosed; call skew extreme (896k calls vs. 278k puts, ~3× variance) with 17 Jan holding >50% of open interest [03:39–05:56]
- **Position structure**: 300 shares at $26.95 cost basis; two short 28-strike puts (21 Feb, 52 DTE, 25Δ) and one short 32-strike put (10 Jan, 3 DTE, 44Δ) — short-term put at ATM with assignment intent [09:41–10:57]
- **Capital allocation**: Bumped allocation from $15k to $25k based on community poll; 67.5% utilization; clarified that initial 200 shares came from existing holdings, all future adds/subtracts are new market activity [07:13–08:37]
- **Planned adjustment**: Will add second short call to move from 3:1 (shares:calls) to 2:1 or 3:2 ratio, managing via aggressive rebalancing to avoid full assignment while capturing volatility premium [12:04–13:06]
- **P&L tracking**: Current total P&L ~$3k on $25k deployed; return on invested capital strong; baseline entry price for year-to-date tracking established [13:06–14:14]
- **Risk management**: Early-exercise risk acknowledged if GME squeezes; ratio structure (not 1:1) designed to allow partial unwinding rather than full position liquidation [12:04–13:06]
