# Market Volatility, Bond Auctions & Options Activity — May 21, 2025

## Summary

On May 21, 2025, the market experienced significant volatility driven by weak demand at a 20-year Treasury bond auction and Moody's credit rating downgrade, causing a sharp selloff in equities and a steepening of the yield curve. The session featured zero-DTE trading opportunities, notable options positioning in healthcare (UNH), technology (GOOGL), and meme stocks (RDDT), with analysis of how to compare leveraged ETF positions and manage premium-selling strategies across different account sizes.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2025-05-21)

- **Bond auction weakness drove the selloff** [08:41–10:08]: A weak 20-year Treasury auction (16 billion in demand) sent the 10-year yield up ~10 basis points to 4.59%, triggering broad equity losses and a one-week low. This was a textbook supply-and-demand dislocation.
- **Yield curve steepened; long end hit hardest** [11:42–13:17]: ZB (30-year), ZN (10-year), and ZT (2-year) futures all fell sharply. The long end of the curve saw the largest moves, signaling weak demand for longer-duration debt and geopolitical/fiscal concerns.
- **UNH crashed on Medicaid fraud investigation** [17:45–20:48]: United Healthcare fell ~5 years of gains on a single day following news of a Medicaid fraud investigation. Options showed massive put buying and short call volume, with insiders stepping in at lows—a contrarian signal.
- **GOOGL surged on AI announcements** [28:58–31:43]: Google saw huge call volume (2.1M calls vs. 1.3M puts) and near-term IV expansion, reflecting bullish positioning around new AI model rollout.
- **RDDT earnings beat but stock sold off** [36:45–42:41]: Reddit reported 61% YoY revenue growth and 29.4% EBITDA margin, yet closed lower. The stock broke below prior support and entered a confirmed downtrend, making it unsuitable for short-put selling.
- **TLT (bond ETF) saw doubled call volume** [31:43–35:02]: Call volume on TLT doubled intraday; many were penny options (133 strike, Jan 2026), indicating retail speculation on a bond bounce despite near-term downside bias.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Zero-DTE entry timing** [06:04]: Premium collection on zero-DTE trades can compensate for risk if entry occurs early in the range before intraday chaos. Days like May 21 are hard to predict but can be positioned for as they unfold.
- **Options market reveals collective positioning** [20:48–22:20]: Unusual options activity (put/call ratios, open interest shifts, insider buying) often contradicts short-term price action and signals longer-term sentiment. Use options data to avoid guessing.
- **Bid-ask spread analysis on leveraged ETFs** [54:08–01:12:33]: Wider spreads on leveraged ETFs (e.g., TQQQ vs. QQQ) are offset by capital efficiency and leverage factors. For small accounts, the spread cost is trivial; for large accounts, the notional exposure advantage can outweigh spread friction.
- **Cash-secured put return calculation** [45:23–49:43]: Annualized return on short puts = (credit / cash secured) × (365 / days to expiration). High-IV, low-price stocks (e.g., BOIL at 11 strike, 90¢ premium) can yield 100%+ annualized returns, but require strict risk management and wings.
- **Vertical spreads cap risk on volatile names** [49:43–52:44]: When selling premium on highly volatile stocks with earnings, buy wings (e.g., 5¢ for a 5-strike-wide put spread) to cap max loss at ~50% of naked put risk while sacrificing only 3¢ of credit.
- **Short strangle on range-bound names** [42:41–44:10]: RDDT's wide range (130–80 handle) and defined support/resistance make it a candidate for short strangles, rolled as price moves, rather than directional short puts.
- **Leverage factor in notional exposure** [01:07:02–01:11:21]: When comparing TQQQ (3× leverage) to QQQ, the notional value of a position must account for the leverage multiplier. A smaller TQQQ position can deliver equivalent delta exposure to a larger QQQ position, freeing up capital for risk-free rate returns.
