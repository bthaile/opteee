# Delta's Expiration Dilemma #optionstrading

## Summary

This video explores the binary nature of delta at option expiration: every option must resolve to either a delta of 1 (in-the-money, retaining intrinsic value) or a delta of 0 (out-of-the-money, expiring worthless). The segment clarifies that while in-the-money options avoid worthlessness, profitability depends on factors beyond intrinsic value alone.

## Key takeaways

- **Delta convergence at expiration** [00:00]: As options approach expiration, delta converges to either 1 (ITM) or 0 (OTM); there is no middle ground at the expiration boundary.
- **Intrinsic value preservation** [00:31]: Options with delta of 1 at expiration retain intrinsic value and do not expire worthless, though this does not guarantee profit.
- **Zero-delta worthlessness** [00:00]: Options with delta approaching 0 expire worthless with no intrinsic value.
