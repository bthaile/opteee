# Finding Opportunities in Low Volatility

## Summary

Even when broad-market volatility is depressed, individual securities can exhibit extreme moves driven by company-specific catalysts. The key is to actively search for outlier volatility rather than passively accepting a low-vol regime. This video demonstrates how to identify high-volatility opportunities by scanning for unusual price action and confirming with implied volatility percentile readings.

## Key takeaways

- **Catalyst hunting as edge:** Look for stocks with unusual price moves decoupled from broad indices; a 44% single-day move followed by a 10% continuation signals something material is happening [00:00–00:28].
- **IV percentile as confirmation:** Use implied volatility percentile (98% in the example) to validate that the move reflects genuine uncertainty, not just noise [00:28].
- **Avoid lazy vol assumptions:** Don't assume low broad-market volatility means no opportunities exist; the mistake is treating low VIX as a reason to sit idle rather than a reason to dig deeper [00:49].
- **Sector/security-level variance:** Even when the S&P 500 trades sideways, individual names can experience significant volatility tied to business-specific news or events [00:00–00:28].

## Notable quotes

> "There's absolutely volatility to be found here. The thing you have to not do is just assume that V is low. There's opportunities—you're just being lazy if you're not looking."
