# Managing Risk Using Vol Instead of Dollars

## Summary

This video explains how to size positions using volatility rather than dollar amounts to ensure consistent risk exposure across different securities. By matching P&L volatility across positions instead of notional capital, traders can maintain logical risk sizing regardless of the underlying asset's price or volatility profile.

## Key takeaways

- **Volatility-based sizing equalizes risk:** Two positions sized by volatility will experience approximately the same P&L swings over a given period, even if the underlying securities have different prices or volatility profiles [00:00–00:29].
- **Dollar-amount sizing creates hidden risk disparity:** Equal dollar allocations to different securities result in materially different actual risk exposures due to differences in volatility and price action [00:29].
- **Cross-curve consistency:** Volatility-based sizing ensures that position sizing is logical and consistent when comparing positions across different securities or asset classes.
