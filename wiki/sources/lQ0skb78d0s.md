# More Than Just Theta Decay - Charm Exists

## Summary

Duration management—the deliberate control of how long a trade is held—is critical for options traders because every contract has an expiration date. Beyond theta decay, **charm** (a higher-order Greek) causes directional delta drift: in-the-money options gain delta over time while out-of-the-money options lose delta, creating significant P&L swings if duration is mismanaged.

## Key takeaways

- **Duration is a first-order risk lever** [00:00–00:21]: Options traders must actively decide how long they expect a thesis to play out; allowing idle time without price movement creates hidden P&L drag.
- **Charm drives delta drift independent of theta** [00:42]: If price stays flat, in-the-money options *gain* delta (become more directional), while out-of-the-money options *lose* delta—this is charm, not theta decay.
- **Moneyness determines charm direction** [00:42]: The sign of charm depends on whether you hold ITM or OTM contracts; this asymmetry is why duration management prevents surprise delta exposure.

## Notable quotes

> "If I'm holding an option that's in the money, I'm actually going to gain delta. And if I'm holding an option in this context that's out of the money, I'm going to lose delta."
