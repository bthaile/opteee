# How to Start Investing or Trading

## Summary

A foundational guide for beginning traders and investors covering essential prerequisites, market structure, and a five-step onboarding process. Eric outlines the minimal viable product for successful market entry: establishing clear goals, securing adequate savings, opening a brokerage account, paper-trading options strategies while maintaining a buy-and-hold portfolio, and validating results before deploying real capital.

## Key takeaways

- **Define goals and secure savings first** [01:17–02:04]. Before trading, clarify your objectives and ensure you have 6+ months of emergency expenses saved. Do not trade money you need within 3–5 years.
- **Begin with the end in mind** [02:04–03:31]. Establish a trading plan as a living document that prevents analysis paralysis and keeps you aligned with your actual objectives, not random rabbit holes (e.g., implied volatility surface calculations).
- **Choose a robust brokerage** [09:14–10:05]. Avoid gamified platforms like Robinhood; select established firms with strong market support during volatility. Bid-ask spreads and liquidity matter when you need them most.
- **Risk management is job one** [10:25–11:29]. Your primary role is risk manager, not money-maker. Risk and reward are intimately tied; skepticism toward "too good to be true" opportunities is essential.
- **Run a buy-and-hold portfolio in parallel** [11:56–12:40]. Set up a passive portfolio (outperforming the market) while paper-trading to learn. If you cannot beat buy-and-hold via active trading, you are making money slower, not faster.
- **Paper trading builds discipline** [12:40–13:25]. Treat paper trading seriously; inability to do so signals a discipline problem that will plague your entire trading career. Emotions and plan adherence matter more than strategy complexity.
- **Retail traders are small fish** [13:43–14:11]. Institutions (banks, hedge funds, pensions) dominate order flow, but inefficiencies remain for retail to exploit via edge development.
- **Market efficiency is imperfect** [14:35–14:59]. Markets are not purely efficient; edge exists but changes over time. Continuous adaptation is required.
- **Use both technical and fundamental analysis** [15:27–16:18]. Avoid the false debate between technical and fundamental analysis. As a retail trader, use everything at your disposal; weight them differently based on your strategy.
- **Start with three option strategies** [17:07–17:28]. Begin with covered strangle, diagonals, and naked short put (short calls added later). Video links in description provide specifics on scanning and execution.
- **Five-step onboarding process** [17:52–18:59]:
  1. Save capital [17:52]
  2. Define objectives and research using provided resources [17:52]
  3. Open a brokerage account (margin account; consider IRAs) [18:12]
  4. Fund account and set up buy-and-hold portfolio [18:12]
  5. Paper-trade options strategies while building trading logs and plan [18:33]
  6. **Validate results before going live** [18:33–18:59]: confirm you can beat your buy-and-hold baseline, or pivot to income-focused strategies.

## Notable quotes

> "Begin with the end in mind" — Stephen Covey principle applied to trading to prevent distraction and maintain alignment with actual objectives.
