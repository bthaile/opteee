# GME Analysis Gamma Squeeze and Option Volatility

## Summary

Eric analyzes the GameStop options chain across multiple expiration cycles (21–23 June 2024), tracking volatility expansion and skew patterns to understand market-maker positioning and potential gamma-squeeze mechanics. He identifies significant call-side implied-volatility skew in far out-of-the-money strikes and discusses how price acceleration could force market makers into rapid hedging, creating buying pressure—the core driver of gamma squeezes distinct from short squeezes.

## Key takeaways

- **Volatility tracking across expirations** [01:58–02:43]: Eric is building a model comparing IV changes day-to-day across the full options chain, with the hypothesis that market makers already price in cycle dynamics before retail traders perceive them.

- **28 June expiration shows outsized IV variance** [04:43–05:10]: The 28 June cycle shows 27.7 IV points (5.55% per day) change from 21st to 23rd—far above the sub-1% daily moves in other expirations, signaling concentrated near-term activity.

- **Extreme call-side skew in far OTM strikes** [05:10–05:51]: Call IV at the 128 strike (~580 IV) vs. put-side cap (~487–488 IV) reflects market makers pricing inventory risk for potential explosive upside; retail traders buying these far OTM calls are likely overpaying.

- **Gamma squeeze mechanics: delta-hedging cascade** [08:04–09:27]: As OTM calls move closer to ATM (rising delta), market makers must accumulate shares to hedge unless they internalize orders. This forced buying is the gamma squeeze—distinct from short squeezes because it's driven by options-market hedging, not short covering.

- **Front-month volatility smile expansion expected** [10:36–10:59]: Eric expects put-side IV to expand into expiration (similar to prior week), creating a tradable event for options traders who can capture relative IV expansion between put and call sides.

- **Order-flow monitoring for anomalies** [11:17–11:42]: Eric has alerts set for large orders and total flow to detect unusual purchasing activity or pricing dislocations that might signal cycle initiation.

## Notable quotes

> "A gamma squeeze is driven by market maker activities—they're just trying to hedge their position to capture the bid-ask spread and go home with the money that they made on the spread."

> "If GameStop starts flying up, this creates massive buying demand from market makers because as these get closer to at the money, the market makers need to accumulate more shares."
