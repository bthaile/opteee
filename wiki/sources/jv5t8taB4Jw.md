# Trading Earning Releases And Post Earnings Announcement Drift

## Summary

Post-earnings announcement drift (PEAD) is a measurable continuation of price movement in the days and weeks following an earnings release, distinct from the immediate gap. The phenomenon is predictable under specific conditions: gap thresholds and EPS-beat magnitude correlate with drift magnitude. Trading earnings requires large sample sizes because you are trading an aggregate statistical effect, not a single-event outcome.

## Key takeaways

- **PEAD definition and observation** [00:00–00:26]: After an earnings gap, price often continues moving in the same direction over the following quarter. A stock that gapped up 64% on earnings guidance may continue higher despite recent pullbacks—this continuation is PEAD.
- **Predictive criteria** [00:26]: Gap size and EPS-vs-actual beat magnitude are measurable thresholds that positively predict drift magnitude. These are starting points; individual research is required.
- **Sample-size imperative** [00:26]: Earnings trading is an aggregate effect. You must trade it with large sample sizes, not single-event conviction. This is a process-over-outcome framework.
- **Research foundation** [00:26]: SSRN hosts extensive peer-reviewed research blocks on PEAD. Serious earnings traders should review the academic literature before building a strategy.
