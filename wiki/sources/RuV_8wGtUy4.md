# Learn from, DON'T copy other traders

## Summary
The distinction between learning from successful traders and blindly copying their strategies is fundamental to developing sustainable edge. Many traders attempt to replicate what appears successful without understanding the underlying mechanics, risk management, or personal fit required to deploy those strategies effectively.

## Key takeaways

- **Learn the mechanics, not the trade.** [00:00] Understand *why* a strategy works, not just *what* the entry and exit look like. Copying without comprehension leads to poor execution and losses.
- **Develop your own process.** [00:00] Take time to learn your own approach rather than chasing others' apparent success. Personal fit, risk tolerance, and timeframe alignment matter as much as the strategy itself.
- **Recognize the gap between observation and execution.** [00:00] Seeing a successful trade is not the same as having the discipline, capital allocation, and emotional control to execute it consistently.
