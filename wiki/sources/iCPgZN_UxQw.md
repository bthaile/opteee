# Finding the NEXT Market Leaders - Matt Carusso | The Outlier Podcast Ep 52

## Summary

Matt Carusso discusses his transition from day trading to position trading and growth investing, his thesis on building an AI-driven stock-screening system to identify market leaders, and his analysis of the current market regime. He argues that mega-cap tech has become a "bond proxy" due to institutional flight from bonds during inflation, creating an anomalous market structure. Carusso expects a significant rotation once inflation fully subsides and the Fed begins cutting rates, with small-cap and mid-tier growth stocks positioned for outsized returns.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2023-11-01)

- **Mega-cap tech as bond proxy** [33:37–36:50]: The NASDAQ 100 is up ~30% while the S&P 500 is up ~16% and small caps are negative; this disconnect reflects institutional investors treating Apple, Microsoft, and Google as inflation-protected bond substitutes rather than growth stocks. This dynamic is historically unprecedented.
- **Rotation thesis** [35:44–36:34]: Once inflation peaks and bonds become attractive again, capital will rotate out of mega-cap tech into small caps and mid-tier growth, creating a period where the NASDAQ stalls but broader market rallies.
- **Fed pivot likely bullish** [52:11–54:47]: Unlike 2008 (when rate cuts signaled economic collapse), current rate cuts would signal the end of an inflationary cycle. Carusso assigns ~90% probability to a significant rally once cuts begin, contingent on the Fed not "overcooking" the tightening cycle.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Stock selection framework** [06:33–08:21]: Building a quantitative system to narrow ~10,000 stocks to a shortlist of 20–30 high-probability leaders by identifying predictive variables (relative strength, price action, fundamentals, earnings growth) and their optimal combinations for each stock.
- **Market regime classification** [18:42–21:55]: Distinguish between "normal" growth periods (2–4 years of rising markets driven by Fed inflation target + productivity gains + credit expansion) and "crisis" periods (6–9 months of deleveraging). Use VIX and breadth metrics to identify which regime is active.
- **Periodicity and overfitting** [27:16–30:41]: Use longer historical datasets (100+ years of monetary history) to identify enduring principles (e.g., Fed behavior, credit cycles) while avoiding overfitting to recent data. Keep variables minimal; simpler models generalize better across regime changes.
- **Avoiding negative bias at bottoms** [59:31–01:02:05]: Market bottoms always feel terrible; the best opportunities come when sentiment is worst. Protect downside with technical stops, but avoid freezing into inaction due to macro pessimism.
- **Concentration vs. diversification tradeoff** [10:33–15:25]: William O'Neal achieved 200%+ returns via extreme concentration; Carusso seeks to replicate his stock-picking skill while using 10–20 positions to reduce volatility and enable scaling to other investors' capital.
