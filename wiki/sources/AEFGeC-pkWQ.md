# Win Rate vs Expected Returns

## Summary

Win rate alone is a meaningless metric for trading profitability. A strategy with a 35% win rate can be massively profitable if average winners are large relative to average losers. What matters is positive expected value: the product of win probability, average win size, and loss probability, average loss size.

## Key takeaways

- **Win rate ≠ profitability** [00:00–00:29]: A 35% win rate with $1,500 average winners and $600 average losers across 50 trades yields strong net positive returns, despite the low hit rate.
- **Expected value is the metric** [00:29–00:52]: Focus on positive expected return, not win rate. A high win rate strategy can still lose money if losses exceed wins in magnitude.
- **Size matters more than frequency** [00:00–00:52]: Asymmetric payoff structures (big wins, small losses) create profitability regardless of how often you're right.

## Notable quotes

> "Do not ever take somebody that says, 'Oh, I have a high win rate. This is a high win rate strategy.' That means nothing. It can still lose money. What you care about is positive expected [value]."
