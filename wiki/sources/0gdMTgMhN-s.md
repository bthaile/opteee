# Selling Calls Is A BAD Hedge #optionstrading

## Summary

Selling calls does not function as an effective hedge despite common misconception. The premium received provides only a small buffer (approximately 1% in the example shown) against downside risk, making it an inadequate risk-management tool. The strategy may still have merit on its own terms, but framing it as a hedge misrepresents its actual risk profile.

## Key takeaways

- **Downside protection is minimal** [00:27]: When you sell calls, your downside buffer equals your basis minus the premium received—often only ~1% of position value, which is insufficient to call it a hedge.
- **Distinguish strategy intent from outcome** [00:49]: Short calls may be profitable or appropriate for other reasons, but they should not be mentally categorized as a hedging tool.
- **Risk profile clarity matters** [00:00]: Understanding your actual downside exposure (where it begins, where it shifts) is essential before claiming any position is hedged.

## Notable quotes

> "I wouldn't in any way, shape, or form think of short calls as a hedge. Again, it doesn't mean that the short calls are bad or not to do it, but that's a wrong way to think about it." [00:49]
