# Rocket Lab's 20% Earnings Pop & HEAVY Call Buying in GME

## Summary

Rocket Lab (RKLB) posted solid earnings and rallied ~20%, but encountered heavy selling pressure at resistance, closing with an inverted hammer candle despite 120M shares traded. GameStop (GME) is holding a multi-day rally with unusual large block call buying (3,000+ contracts at the 40 strike, Jan 17 expiration) that mirrors historical patterns, suggesting deliberate conviction positioning rather than short-term speculation.

## Key takeaways

- **RKLB earnings & price action** [00:59–02:10]: Stock broke above $20 (5x from earlier support at ~$4.66) but faced rejection and heavy selling; 95% of 120M shares traded were sells, yet price held, suggesting profit-taking by early holders rather than capitulation.
- **RKLB options positioning** [03:17]: Pre-earnings call skew was 2:1 calls-to-puts; 308k calls traded post-earnings with 22% at bid or below (profit-taking), 30% opening, 46% mid-market.
- **Sector divergence** [04:20]: Tech and communications remain strong; consumer staples hitting new highs while discretionary trends negative (unusual); healthcare/pharma weak post-election; utilities and real estate struggling.
- **GME large block call buying** [06:24–09:44]: 3,000-lot of 40-strike Jan 17 calls bought on Nov 12 (~$1M notional); followed by 1,400, 2,000, 1,000, and 800-lot purchases on same and following days. No sales despite 10% overnight gains, indicating conviction for larger move rather than quick scalp.
- **GME technical hold** [06:24]: Inverted hammer candle with heavy volume; RSI momentum slowing but price holding support—early to call a rollover.
- **Pattern alignment** [07:34–08:33]: Call strike selection (40s, Jan 17 expiration) and multi-day accumulation pattern consistent with historical large-trader positioning; deliberate, not speculative.
