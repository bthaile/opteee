# indicators dont matter

## Summary

Indicators are contextual tools, not trading foundations. The core of profitable options trading rests on identifying and capturing **profit mechanisms**—the actual market effects that generate returns, such as directional price movement or variance risk premium. Spending time optimizing indicator settings diverts focus from understanding the fundamental drivers of profitability.

## Key takeaways

- **Focus on profit mechanisms, not indicators** [00:00–00:26]: Price direction (up or down) and [[volatility-risk-premium]] (implied volatility trending over realized volatility) are concrete profit mechanisms; indicators are secondary tools for contextualization.
- **Research the mechanism first** [00:26–00:49]: Deep research into what actually makes money—the profit mechanism itself—leads to better assumptions and entry strategies than optimizing MACD settings or other signal parameters.
- **Indicators are useful but secondary** [00:49]: Technical tools can help measure and contextualize, but they must serve the primary goal of capturing a known profit mechanism.
