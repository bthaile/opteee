# Buying 0DTE Options - Charm and Gamma

## Summary

Zero-DTE (same-day expiration) options experience rapid delta decay—called charm—that erodes position delta faster than gamma can compensate. This creates a mechanical disadvantage: as delta decays, the underlying must move even further in your favor just to break even, making 0DTE long options a capital-inefficient trade structure despite their low nominal cost.

## Key takeaways

- **Charm (delta decay) dominates 0DTE mechanics** [00:00–00:33]: Delta erodes continuously intraday even without underlying price movement; this is the primary risk for long 0DTE buyers.
- **Gamma expansion fails to offset delta loss** [00:33]: As delta decays, gamma does not expand fast enough to compensate, forcing the underlying to move larger distances to recover break-even.
- **Break-even threshold rises as time passes** [00:33]: Each hour of decay increases the required move magnitude, compounding the difficulty of profitability.
- **Low nominal cost masks true capital inefficiency** [00:33]: 0DTE options appear cheap but are an expensive way to lose capital due to the charm/gamma mismatch.
