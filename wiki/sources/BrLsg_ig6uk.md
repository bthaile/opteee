# Finding Strength w/ Matt Caruso | The Outlier Podcast Ep75

## Summary

Matt Caruso discusses market regime shifts in 2024, focusing on the K-shaped recovery driven by inflation and AI concentration in mega-cap stocks. He analyzes the August 1–5 volatility spike (Yen carry trade unwind, recession fears), the subsequent rebound mechanics, and positioning dynamics between large and small caps. Caruso emphasizes relative strength as a guard rail, the importance of mental discipline and meditation in trading longevity, and opportunities in mid-cap names that held up during the correction.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2024-08-21)

- **K-shaped recovery distorts normal market mechanics** [13:29]: Inflation and stimulus have broken traditional 60/40 portfolio relationships; large caps with pricing power have led while small caps lagged, exacerbated by AI concentration in mega-cap names.
- **August 1–5 volatility was a positioning unwind, not a fundamental shift** [19:28]: The Yen carry trade collapse and short squeeze in large-cap Tech (long large-cap, short small-cap crowded trade) caused rapid reversals; Nikkei fell ~30% in two weeks—extreme positioning always precedes violent corrections.
- **VIX spike to ~65 marked capitulation; rebound driven by Bank of Japan backstop and low PPI expectations** [22:44]: Initial bounce off the lows was vacuum-driven; Caruso expects a retest before confirming a sustained uptrend (V-recoveries to all-time highs are historically rare).
- **Large caps rebounding parabolic; small caps consolidating** [24:24]: Semis fell ~30% from highs into the drop; funds are re-establishing the large-cap/Tech trade, but this is a bear-market rally bounce, not a new uptrend—pullbacks will reveal true conviction.
- **Expect small/mid-cap catchup once rate-cut cycle begins** [30:32]: Small caps were hit twice (no pricing power during inflation, recession-sensitive); once inflation normalizes and Fed cuts, mid/small caps should outperform significantly.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Portfolio sizing for vacation/reduced attention** [02:33]: Caruso sizes down to ~30–50% exposure when away; checks markets at regular intervals (e.g., every 30 minutes or hourly, per *Reminiscence of a Stock Operator*) to catch outlier moves without constant screen time; avoids full investment to limit damage if connectivity fails.
- **Relative strength is the primary guard rail** [40:18]: Stocks that don't fall when the market drops 10–15% signal institutional demand; this signal is more reliable than earnings or news. Caruso uses this to avoid chasing prior-cycle winners (e.g., Micron) and identify new leaders (e.g., Palantir).
- **Meditation and mental centering separate long-term winners from burnouts** [08:01]: Ray Dalio attributes success to meditation; Caruso agrees that managing mind state is critical during drawdowns. Markets are a 3–4 month cycle game; staying centered through lag periods prevents panic changes to strategy.
- **Simplicity and discipline over complexity** [09:16]: Successful traders boil approaches down to core concepts (e.g., "slow is smooth, smooth is fast"). Quantify what you can; use gut feel to calibrate aggression, not to override system.
- **Guard rails prevent repeating cycle mistakes** [38:52]: After each cycle, identify the biggest mistake and create a rule to prevent it. Example: Caruso focuses on small/mid-caps with relative strength; he skipped Nvidia in 2023 (trillion-dollar company, not his focus) but now uses "won't fall in a 10–15% market drop" as a filter.
- **Timing is separate from thesis correctness** [32:01]: Being early on a good idea (e.g., Russell 2000 in early 2024) is not failure if you manage risk and stay positioned. Markets move in 3–4 month cycles; patience and reduced sizing allow you to catch the move without blowing up.
- **Election uncertainty creates hedging demand and positioning flux** [44:06]: Trump policies (tax cuts, deregulation) are seen as faster, more market-positive; Harris policies (status quo, more regulation) are seen as slower. Options market shows long-term hedges being put on earlier than usual; positioning will remain fluid until clarity emerges.

## Regime / context

**Date of recording:** 2024-08-21 (one week after the August 5 market bottom; VIX spike to ~65, Yen carry trade unwind, Nikkei correction ~30%).

**Market state:** Post-correction rebound phase; large-cap Tech re-establishing dominance; small-cap consolidation; Fed rate-cut cycle expected to begin in September 2024. Inflation normalizing; recession concerns elevated but not consensus. U.S. election (Trump vs. Harris) adds policy uncertainty; options market pricing in hedges.

**Caruso's positioning:** Cashed up going into the drop; light exposure (~30–50%) post-rebound; watching relative-strength leaders (Palantir, Meta, Cava) that held up during the correction; avoiding full re-investment until market confirms a retest and higher-low structure.

**Key macro backdrop:** K-shaped recovery (large caps with pricing power and AI exposure leading; small caps lagging); inflation still elevated but trending down; Fed behind the curve; potential for multi-year bull market if rate cuts + AI productivity + tax cuts (Trump scenario) align.
