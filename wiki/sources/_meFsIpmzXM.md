# How to Trade Earnings with Options

## Summary

Earnings releases are binary catalysts that create four distinct profit mechanisms: implied volatility expansion, variance risk premium extraction, intraday post-earnings repricing, and post-earnings announcement drift. The video breaks down each mechanism with specific entry/exit frameworks, position-sizing guidance, and common retail mistakes to avoid when trading options around earnings.

## Key takeaways

- **Four profit mechanisms in earnings:** [03:52] implied volatility expansion into the release, variance risk premium through the release, immediate post-earnings gap/reversal moves, and post-earnings announcement drift over the following quarter.

- **IV expansion trade (long volatility):** [07:31] Enter ~2 weeks before earnings with long straddles or strangles, delta-hedge continuously, and gamma-scalp the convexity. Subsidize holding cost through active rebalancing.

- **Variance risk premium trade (short volatility):** [08:34] Sell straddles or strangles ~15 minutes before market close on earnings day. Use circuit breakers based on tested move magnitude thresholds rather than arbitrary max loss. Hold into the second half of the next trading day to capture theta bleed.

- **Intraday repricing (day-of move):** [12:08] Trade the gap and intraday extension on the day of release. Smaller-cap or higher-volatility names show larger same-day moves; mega-cap names with high analyst coverage show smaller moves.

- **Post-earnings announcement drift (PEAD):** [14:33] Trade the continuation move over the following quarter using long calls (upside) or ratio put diagonals (downside). Requires specific criteria: gap thresholds and EPS beat/miss magnitude.

- **Position sizing:** [16:52] Start with 0.5–1% of portfolio per earnings play. For short volatility plays, target at least 5 per day. Paper-trade for at least 2 quarters before risking real capital.

- **Common mistakes:** [17:54] Confusing theta collection with the actual effect (implied vs. realized vol); sizing too large based on historical performance; abandoning strategy after bad paths due to insufficient sample size; over-weighting liquidity concerns and missing less-liquid opportunities; capping risk with iron condors when you're already selling overpriced vol.

- **Entry screening:** [20:10] Compare implied vs. realized volatility over the last 4, 6, and 8 earnings. If implied consistently exceeds realized, it's a candidate for short volatility. If realized exceeds implied, avoid the trade.

- **Forward guidance > EPS:** [02:42] The stock can beat EPS and fall, or miss EPS and rise. The true unknown is forward guidance, not the headline earnings number.

- **Hedge activity effect:** [01:17] Long equity holders systematically hedge through earnings, creating embedded variance risk premium in options markets.
