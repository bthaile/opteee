# GameStop Tuesday Update: Signs of Life, Coverage Strangle Position, Bitcoin Speculation

## Summary

GameStop is showing early signs of stabilization after a prolonged institutional unwinding, finding support at the 200-day moving average and rallying through a descending channel. Despite implied volatility percentile remaining depressed at 6%, absolute IV of ~77% still offers attractive premium-selling opportunities relative to SPY. The host maintains a [[covered-strangle]] position (28% shares, 50% short puts) and discusses accumulation opportunities as volatility contracts toward realized levels.

## Key takeaways

- **Technical stabilization** [02:12–04:31]: GME fell through 26.5 and 25.5 support levels into a low-volume "no man's land," but found firm support on the 200-day MA and rallied ~27 on light news, suggesting institutional sponsorship. Near-term downtrend is decelerating; channels widening.
- **Volatility context** [05:40–08:08]: IV percentile at 6% (very low for GME), but absolute IV of 77% still elevated. A 0.27-delta short put yields 4.1% return on capital vs. 0.99% on SPY's equivalent delta—a 3.24% relative advantage justifying premium-selling despite low IV percentile.
- **Portfolio positioning** [08:08–09:25]: Host holds 250 shares (28% of 78% total GME allocation) and 50% short puts via [[covered-strangle]]. Only 20% capital remains undeployed; accumulation zone is attractive now that price has stabilized, but host avoids buying into rapid contraction.
- **Volatility convergence opportunity** [09:25–10:44]: 30-day implied vol has contracted from ~130% to ~93%, but realized vol (gray line) remains significantly below IV, creating excess premium for short-vol strategies. Convergence will compress this edge.
- **Bitcoin speculation catalyst** [10:44–11:56]: Photo of Ryan Cohen with MicroStrategy CEO Michael Saylor sparked internet speculation about GME Bitcoin involvement. No official comment; stock moved on the rumor. Institutions may be positioning if they believe in the pivot direction.
