# GME Background Deep Dive | GameStop Analysis Live Ep5

## Summary

Eric examines off-exchange order routing and dark pool mechanics through academic research, analyzes roaring Kitty's historical options positioning and RSI divergences on GME weekly charts, discusses the BCG lawsuit dismissal and upcoming BBBY 16b-6 insider trading case against Ryan Cohen, and addresses retail trader psychology around recent price pullbacks.

## Key takeaways

- **Off-exchange routing mechanics** [08:09–09:27]: Dark pools originated to allow large institutional orders to execute without front-running, but create a vicious cycle where reduced lit-exchange depth incentivizes further off-exchange routing, decreasing price discovery. Wider spreads and higher volatility entice off-exchange execution.

- **Price discovery vs. liquidity trade-off** [39:47–40:18]: Academic research shows traders balance possible price improvement against lower execution probability off-exchange; volatility increases the incentive to route away from lit exchanges. Retail traders are less impacted by this than institutions attempting multi-million-dollar fills.

- **Dark pool reporting and transparency** [48:45–53:16]: Dark pool trades are still disclosed to FINRA within ~10 seconds and to the public within seconds to minutes; they eventually affect price. Options have different regulatory routing and go through the OCC; dark pools primarily handle equities.

- **RSI and oscillator limitations** [20:14–42:08]: RSI divergences are easy to identify in hindsight but suffer from overfitting; oscillators peak when price moves highest (overbought at >70), making them poor standalone entry signals. Buying oversold conditions is a misapplication of consumer bargain-hunting psychology to markets.

- **Emotional discipline and position sizing** [17:39–28:39]: GME has doubled from mid-April lows despite recent pullback; emotional attachment to price action is wasted energy. Proper position sizing and a documented trading plan are the only sustainable ways to manage drawdowns without compromising long-term participation.

- **BCG lawsuit dismissed** [47:42–55:42]: BCG's $30M breach-of-contract suit against GameStop was dismissed with prejudice on 30 July; the court granted defendant's motion in part, finding insufficient written agreement on certain variable fee initiatives.

- **BBBY 16b-6 case upcoming** [59:04–02:03:04]: Hearing scheduled for 26 June on whether Ryan Cohen must return ~$60M profit from BBBY stake sale under short-swing profit rules; suit alleges board allies provided Cohen non-public information about strategy and financing before his exit.
