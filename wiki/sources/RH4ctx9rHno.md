# Options Trade Management Rules I Wish I Knew Earlier

## Summary

Effective options trade management requires treating each trade as part of a long-term process rather than a one-off salvage operation. The key is to start by analyzing the specific profit mechanism you're targeting—not by learning random structures—then test management approaches systematically before codifying them into rules. Generic advice (e.g., "never risk more than 1% per trade," "sell half at 50% profit") fails because different securities and volatility regimes demand different management styles.

## Key takeaways

- **Process over outcome** [00:00–01:04]: Treat trade management as a repeatable process you can control and measure, not as individual rescue attempts. Consistency in process is what matters long term, not the outcome of any single trade.

- **Reject generic rules** [01:04–02:25]: Blanket advice like "never risk more than 1%," "sell half at 50% profit," or "always roll for a net credit" is random and context-blind. These rules may work for some profit mechanisms but fail for others.

- **Profit mechanism drives management** [02:25–03:44]: Compare X (Elon Musk's platform) and Tesla: X moved ~8% over six months (16.76% high-to-low range), while Tesla moved 82.5% high-to-low. The same bullish strategy cannot be managed identically in both; volatility and range behavior dictate management rules.

- **Start with profit mechanism, not structure** [03:44–04:55]: Most traders learn structures first (verticals, iron condors) then try to manage them. Reverse this: identify the profit mechanism you want to capture, assess its behavior, then fit structures to it.

- **Quantify the behavior** [04:55–06:04]: Use standard deviation price-spike plots and volatility metrics (ATR, ADR) to understand how your target moves on your intended timeframe. For zero-DTE index strategies, match volatility analysis to zero-DTE straddle prices, not longer-dated IV.

- **Test management methodologies systematically** [06:04–07:13]: Once you've identified a candidate strategy (e.g., short straddles/strangles), test different management approaches—close for loss, roll, define risk, take to expiration—across different delta levels, entry times, and parameters over a broad timeframe, then shrink to smaller windows.

- **Codify, don't improvise** [07:13–08:31]: The correct sequence is: profit mechanism → fit structures → build strategies → test management → optimize → codify. Never build the plane while flying it; that leads to ad-hoc Reddit-style desperation trades.
