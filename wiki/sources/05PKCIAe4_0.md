# Don't default #optionstrading #trading #stockmarket

## Summary

A foundational lesson on matching options strategy structure to the profit mechanism you're trying to capture. The core mistake: selecting a strategy (e.g., short puts or put spreads) that caps upside when your directional thesis demands unlimited profit potential. Proper structure alignment is essential to avoid self-sabotage.

## Key takeaways

- **Structure must fit profit mechanism** [00:24–00:49]: If you expect a large directional move upside, selling puts or put credit spreads caps your profit potential—you're trading against your own thesis.
- **Directional breakout example** [00:00–00:24]: When playing an upside breakout, avoid strategies with capped upside; instead, use structures that benefit from large moves (e.g., long calls or call debit spreads).
- **Avoid self-sabotage through misalignment** [00:49]: Selecting the wrong structure undermines your edge; always verify that your chosen strategy's profit mechanism aligns with your market view.

## Notable quotes

> "The problem here is if you think about the profit mechanism in this instance being something going up a lot and then you sell a put or a put credit spread or you buy a call debit spread well you're capping your profit potential."
