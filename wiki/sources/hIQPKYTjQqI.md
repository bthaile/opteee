# Understanding Mean Reversion in VIX

## Summary

This educational segment contrasts mean-reverting and non-mean-reverting assets using VIX and SPX as examples. VIX exhibits true mean reversion—spikes are consistently followed by reversions toward a long-term average—while SPX demonstrates positive drift over decades despite short-term oscillations around moving averages. Understanding this distinction is foundational for volatility-based trading strategies.

## Key takeaways

- **VIX mean reversion is structural** [00:00]: Every spike in VIX is followed by reversion toward its long-term average; this pattern repeats consistently across decades.
- **SPX has positive drift, not mean reversion** [00:27]: While SPX oscillates around short-term moving averages, the long-term trend is clearly upward acceleration, not reversion to a fixed level.
- **Regime distinction matters for strategy selection**: Confusing a mean-reverting asset (VIX) with a drifting asset (SPX) leads to misaligned position sizing and exit logic.
