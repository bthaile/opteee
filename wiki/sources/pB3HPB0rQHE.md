# Trap Tasks And How They CAN Destroy Your Trading Career #optionstrading

## Summary

This video identifies "trap tasks"—activities that appear necessary to trading success but, when executed poorly, cause more damage than skipping them entirely. Research is highlighted as a prime example: biased analysis can create false confidence in flawed strategies. The core lesson is that depth, rigor, and skill demonstration matter more than going through the motions.

## Key takeaways

- **Trap tasks defined** [00:00] — Activities that *feel* productive but undermine your edge if done carelessly; research is the canonical example.
- **Bias as a research killer** [00:27] — Confirmation bias and favorable-path bias can make bad strategies appear good (or good ones appear bad), misleading your confidence.
- **Confidence requires demonstrated skill** [00:27] — Long-term edge builds from reliably executing a process with depth and detail, not from surface-level task completion.
- **Detail compounds over time** [00:27] — Attention to research rigor accumulates into genuine confidence; shortcuts create false confidence that eventually fails.

## Notable quotes

> "There's a entire slew of things. I refer to them as trap tasks where you're kind of doing the things that you have to do, but if you do them poorly, it literally can be more damaging than not even doing the things."
