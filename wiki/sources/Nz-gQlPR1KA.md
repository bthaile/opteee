# How I Grew My Trading Account from $3K to $1M+

## Summary

Eric walks through the four foundational principles that enabled him to grow a $3,000 initial account to over $1 million across 14+ trading years: aggressive saving, taking calculated risk, active trading with documented process, and diversification into alternative asset classes. The core thesis is that consistent contributions, compounded returns, and disciplined execution—not exceptional intelligence—drive long-term wealth accumulation.

## Key takeaways

- **Save aggressively early** [00:28–01:52]: Starting with $3K and adding just $100/month at 12% annual return yields ~$61K over 15 years; $1,000/month yields ~$463K; $2,500/month yields ~$1M. Savings rate should scale with career progression.
- **Risk-taking amplifies compounding** [03:50–04:43]: A 12% return more than doubles the outcome versus 6% on the same principal, even without additional contributions. Young traders should embrace appropriate risk exposure.
- **Document your process** [05:07–05:23]: Maintain a trading plan, trading log, and backtesting records to identify edge and improve systematically. This is non-negotiable for active traders.
- **Target 22% CAGR is achievable** [05:50–06:18]: Eric's 14-year compound annual growth rate is 22.8%. This is attainable through consistent, disciplined execution—not exceptional talent or market-beating returns.
- **Diversify into alternative assets** [06:40–07:05]: Real estate, angel investing, and rental property sales provide capital deployment opportunities and hedge against single-market conditions. Rebalance when opportunities arise.

## Notable quotes

> "If you half-ass it you will not even beat the market, you probably won't even be profitable, but if you take it seriously I am nothing special—I've done nothing special, I'm not that smart, so if I can get to something like this, other people most certainly can." [05:50–06:18]
