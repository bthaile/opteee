# Using Different Time Frames To Get More Context

## Summary

This educational segment demonstrates how multi-timeframe analysis reveals different market regimes and trend directions. By examining the same security across daily, 3-month, and 30-day timeframes using standard deviation channels, the instructor shows that longer timeframes may display uptrends while shorter timeframes show downtrends—illustrating the critical importance of aligning your analysis period to your trading timeframe.

## Key takeaways

- **Standard deviation channels provide trend context** [00:00] — Use them to visualize overall trend direction, even if they're not your primary trading indicator.
- **Longer timeframes show stronger uptrends** [00:00–00:17] — Daily and 3-month views both display strongly upward-sloping standard deviation channels.
- **Shorter timeframes can flip the picture** [00:17] — A 30-day timeframe on the same security shows downward-sloping channels, effectively reversing the trend narrative.
- **Match your timeframe to your trading horizon** [00:17] — Select analysis periods that align with the duration of your intended trades to avoid regime confusion.
