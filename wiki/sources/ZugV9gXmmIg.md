# Inside Citadel Securities $1M FINRA Fine

## Summary

Citadel Securities received a $1 million FINRA fine for failing to timely and accurately report approximately 42 billion equity and option order events to the Consolidated Audit Trail (CAT) system between June 2020 and August 2024. The violations included three primary error types: unreported zero values in canceled order fields (31.2 billion events), incorrect representative eligibility indicators (6.3 billion events), and missing time-and-force codes (4.3 billion events). The host contextualizes the fine's proportional impact relative to Citadel's assets and trading revenue.

## Key takeaways

- **CAT reporting failures spanning four years**: Citadel Securities failed to timely or accurately report data for approximately 42 billion order events from June 2020 through August 2024 [01:12]
- **Three primary error categories**: (1) unreported zero values in canceled order quantity fields (31.2 billion events), (2) incorrect representative eligibility indicators applied (6.3 billion events), (3) missing IOOC time-and-force codes (4.3 billion events) [02:23]
- **Remediation timeline**: By September 22, Citadel had remediated 33 error types identified through July 31, then discovered four additional issues [03:41]
- **Fine proportionality analysis**: The $1 million fine represents approximately 0.23% of Kenneth Griffin's personal net worth (~$43 billion) and 0.016% of Citadel LLC's assets (~$64 billion); equivalent to ~$156 for a typical US individual with $100,000 net worth [05:59–07:05]
- **Revenue context**: Citadel Securities posted $2.3 billion in quarterly trading revenue and $6.3 billion in annual net trading revenue (down from $7.5 billion prior year) [04:58]
