# How To Start Trading Your First $5000 The RIGHT Way #trading #optionstrading

## Summary

This video addresses the foundational approach to trading a small $5,000 account, emphasizing process-building over immediate profit-seeking. The core thesis is that with limited capital, the priority should be aggressive saving, core portfolio allocation, and long-term holdings rather than active trading—reducing the risk of self-inflicted losses while establishing sustainable habits.

## Key takeaways

- **Focus on process, not outcomes** [00:00] — Build a [[trading-plan]] and [[trading-log]] from day one; they won't be perfect initially, but iteration is the point.
- **Aggressive capital preservation** [00:24] — Save money aggressively alongside trading; this is the primary lever for account growth at small sizes.
- **Core allocation over active trading** [00:24] — Prioritize longer-term, core holdings you genuinely like rather than frequent trading; this reduces the probability of catastrophic losses.
- **Risk asymmetry at small account sizes** [00:47] — With $5,000, the downside risk of active trading far exceeds the upside; [[portfolio-first]] thinking is essential.

## Notable quotes

> "Focus on building the initial blocks that you need in order to grow. I would start writing things like a trading plan, trading log."

> "The whole point there is that if I focus on good core allocation and good core holdings, that means that the probability of me hurting myself really bad is low."
