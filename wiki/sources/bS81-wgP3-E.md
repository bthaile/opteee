# Rate Statement & GME Options Update

## Summary

Weekly market outlook covering Fed rate decision expectations (31 July), economic calendar, and in-depth GameStop options chain analysis. Futures show modest strength with small caps outperforming; market is pricing ~87% probability of no cut on 31 July, with three cuts expected by year-end. GME options show converging call/put open interest and layered near-OTM strikes suggesting potential mini gamma dynamics, though liquidity remains ample and a major squeeze is unlikely.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2024-07-28)

- **Futures tone:** E-mini S&P up ~0.18%, Russell 2000 up ~0.2%, NASDAQ up slightly; small caps showing relative strength [00:00–00:21]
- **Breadth divergence:** S&P 500 stocks above 50-day MA at 69.2%; NASDAQ falling but showing expanding gross highs and collapsing gross lows—sign that a handful of mega-cap names are dragging the index down [01:34–02:48]
- **Economic calendar:** McDonald's earnings (29 July); HPI, consumer confidence, JOLTS (30 July); Chicago PMI, Fed rate decision & press conference (31 July); manufacturing PMI, ISM, unemployment (1–2 August) [03:29–04:44]
- **Fed expectations:** Market pricing ~87.7% probability of no cut on 31 July (down from higher); three cuts expected by year-end (September, November, December) [04:44–06:08]
- **Risk:** If market reprices cut expectations downward, small caps likely to "chill out quite a lot" [06:27]

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Futures open vs. market open:** Futures open is not necessarily indicative of actual market open; only extremes matter [00:51]
- **Index composition risk:** When a few mega-cap names dominate an index, breadth indicators can diverge sharply from price action [02:24–02:48]
- **GME options structure:** Call OI (569k) and put OI (347k) converging—historically unusual; suggests quieter period ahead [07:06–07:31]
- **Expiration hierarchy:** 16 August expiration cycle has ~201k call OI + 109k put OI; front two weeklys (2 Aug, 9 Aug) combined are ~half that, reflecting normal liquidity concentration [08:09–08:39]
- **Strike distribution (16 Aug):** Largest concentration at 30 strike, then 40s, then 25s and 100s (nearly tied), then 50s; 125 strike still carries 40.5k OI [09:02–09:24]
- **Layered OTM calls:** Near-OTM strikes (24.5, 25, 25.5, etc.) show incoming volume and will build OI; creates potential mini gamma ramp as each falls ITM [09:48–10:08]
- **Mini gamma squeeze mechanics:** As front options fall ITM, market makers hedge by buying shares; layered strikes create sequential buying pressure, but ample borrow availability and ATM liquidity make a major squeeze unlikely [10:34–11:14]
