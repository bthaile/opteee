# Disposition effect in options trading

## Summary

The disposition effect—the tendency to close winning trades too early while letting losers run—is a pervasive behavioral trap for options traders. The core antidote is building a tested, researched trading plan; understanding the statistical path of your strategy through backtesting removes the emotional sensation that drives premature exits and hope-driven hold-outs.

## Key takeaways

- **Root cause of disposition effect:** emotional attachment to realized gains vs. unrealized losses [00:00]
- **The solution: backtesting and plan-building:** testing hundreds of trades in advance removes the sensation because you've already seen the statistical outcomes [00:25]
- **Execution becomes mechanical:** once you understand your strategy's path through research and testing, you can focus purely on execution rather than emotional decision-making [00:25]
- **Plan-first discipline:** taking time to build and validate a plan upfront is the foundational hedge against behavioral drift [00:25]

## Notable quotes

> "If you take the time to actually research, test, and build a strategy on your own, it completely removes the sensation because you fully understand the path of your trades because you've seen hundreds of them at that point in your testing."
