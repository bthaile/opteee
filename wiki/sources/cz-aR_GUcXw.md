# Complete Guide to Trading Options with $5K

## Summary

A foundational framework for trading options with a small account ($5,000), emphasizing that success depends on skill-building and disciplined process rather than rapid capital growth. The video addresses the structural challenges of options trading—complexity, low barrier to entry, lagged feedback, and misleading marketing—and outlines concrete goals (saving, learning, tracking, risk management) and strategy selection (verticals, ratio call diagonals, covered strangles) suited to capital constraints.

## Key takeaways

### Structural challenges of small-account options trading
- **Complexity + low barrier to entry = danger** [02:12–04:42]: Options require deep skill but anyone can trade them immediately (no certification, no vetting). Combined with slick marketing promising "$4,000 in 10 minutes," this primes new traders for unrealistic expectations and poor risk discipline.
- **Lagged feedback mechanism** [03:30]: Unlike basketball (immediate feedback on a jump shot), trading allows you to execute a sound strategy and lose money due to sequence of returns, or execute a terrible strategy and profit by luck. This breaks self-correction.
- **Multiple hats required** [04:42]: You must be researcher, trader, risk manager, and psychologist simultaneously—tasks that investment banks split across entire teams.

### Goals for a $5K account
- **Prioritize saving over returns** [08:43]: A $1,000 addition to a $5K account is a 20% boost; the same $1,000 on a $5M account is 0.02%. Early on, savings compound faster than trading returns. A 10% return on $5K is only $500—trivial compared to disciplined saving.
- **Commit fully or don't commit at all** [09:56]: Half-hearted trading wastes time and capital that could compound in a buy-and-hold index. Either accept the work required or skip it.
- **Learn the skill set first, not returns** [10:44]: Your goal is sustainable process, not a 10% or 50% return target. A consistent 10% annually, coupled with aggressive saving and income growth, yields real wealth over time.
- **Track everything** [11:56]: Without a trading log and plan, you cannot diagnose what works. This is non-negotiable.
- **Limit drawdowns ruthlessly** [11:56]: A 25% drawdown requires a 33.3% gain to break even. On a $5K account, a 25% loss ($1,250) is devastating. Target max 5% loss per trade; avoid portfolio-wide correlation.

### Risk management mechanics
- **Define exit points before entry** [13:57]: Know your stop-loss and profit target before you trade. This prevents emotional decisions and oversizing.
- **Allocation vs. utilization** [13:57]: Allocation = capital earmarked for a strategy; utilization = capital actually deployed. On a $5K account, you'll use most of it, but ensure trades don't all move together (e.g., don't put five $1K trades all in Tech).
- **Focus on percent returns, not dollar amounts** [14:53]: A 10% return is solid; $500 is trivial. Percent-based thinking keeps you disciplined on small accounts.

### Strategy selection for $5K
- **Verticals** [16:58]: Dollar-wide spreads let you access expensive underlyings (e.g., AAPL at $242) by capping risk to the spread width. Example: sell a $5-wide put vertical, risk $41 to make $59. Useful for learning but not ideal long-term.
- **Ratio call diagonals** [18:05]: Buy a 90-day call, sell shorter-dated calls against it. Actively managed. Example: buy SoundHound diagonal for $485 capital, but manage to a $17 stop-loss = ~$55 actual risk. Allows multiple positions within risk budget.
- **Covered strangles** [19:08]: Sell both a call and put against 100+ shares. Difficult on $5K (limits you to $5–7 stocks); better for long-term holdings.
- **Long singles** [20:09]: Buy calls without the short leg. Same management as ratio diagonals but simpler.

### Picker selection
- **Use scanners** [15:56]: Filter for optionable tickers trading $15 or less, with recent uptrend. This expands your choice set without requiring expensive underlyings.
