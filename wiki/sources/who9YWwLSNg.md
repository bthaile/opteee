# Trading in IRA Accounts

## Summary

This video argues for active trading within tax-advantaged retirement accounts (IRAs) as a way to compound skill-set development with tax efficiency. Eric demonstrates how he outperformed the S&P 500 by 24.7 percentage points in 2022 (up 5.3% vs. down 19.4%) using only 32 trades, primarily via the covered strangle strategy. The approach balances efficiency, long-term growth, and tax optimization without requiring excessive portfolio interaction.

## Key takeaways

- **Why trade your IRA:** Tax-advantaged growth compounds the effect of outperformance; if you've developed a profitable trading edge, deploying it in a Roth or traditional IRA amplifies after-tax returns [00:34–02:06].
- **Two approaches:** Passive dollar-cost averaging into index ETFs (SPY, IWM, QQQ) or active strategies like covered strangles and ratio call diagonals [02:25–03:46].
- **Dollar-cost averaging mechanics:** Regular monthly contributions smooth cost basis and reduce timing risk; buying the same dollar amount at different price levels yields a better aggregate entry [02:49–03:28].
- **Covered strangle in IRAs:** Bullish-biased, works well in neutral/up markets, and pairs naturally with dollar-cost averaging into index ETFs; short calls hedge downside when markets decline [05:32–06:17].
- **2022 case study:** Down market exposed the strategy's weakness in bearish conditions, but dollar-cost averaging in + short call income offset losses, yielding +5.3% return on the account [05:49–06:17].
- **Split portfolio option:** Allocate to both equities (covered strangle on index ETFs) and bonds (covered strangle on bond ETFs) to diversify objectives and hedge risk [06:38–07:13].
- **Broker constraints:** Check your broker's IRA rules; Schwab allows covered strangles, ratio call diagonals, and rolling options, but prohibits naked shorts, margin, and portfolio margin [04:45–05:11].
- **Efficiency focus:** Eric trades his IRA only ~12 times per year (vs. much more frequently in margin accounts) to optimize long-term performance without excessive time commitment [01:21–01:44].
