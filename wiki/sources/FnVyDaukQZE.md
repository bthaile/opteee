# Full Time Options Trading w/ @StockMarketOptionsTrading | The Outlier Podcast Ep73

## Summary

Eric, host of the Stock Market Options Trading YouTube channel and podcast, shares his 20-year journey from IT professional to full-time options trader. He discusses his evolution from directional single-option trades through credit spreads to a disciplined, systematic approach focused on SPX short-premium strategies with consistent duration and strike selection. The conversation emphasizes building runway, developing a repeatable process, and aligning trading strategy with personal life goals rather than chasing maximum returns.

## Key takeaways

### Background & motivation
- **Non-financial background**: Started as math teacher, musician, IT infrastructure manager for ~15–17 years before transitioning to full-time trading [01:48–03:03]
- **Initial exposure**: Attended a seminar featuring thinker swim instructor (circa 2004) who taught credit spreads and options mechanics; the "start with $2M to end with $1M" joke stuck with him [03:25–04:42]
- **2008 GFC catalyst**: Watched his 401k halved; realized he needed to learn income generation skills independent of employment [17:08–18:21]

### Evolution of strategy
- **Early phase (2004–2010)**: Directional single options, technical analysis (moving average crosses, three-green-arrows indicator), swing trading on 20–30 DTE [05:11–07:28]
- **Shift to credit spreads**: Found them more intuitive—"you don't have to be 100% right"—and capital-efficient [08:12–09:15]
- **2016 inflection**: SPX Monday/Wednesday/Friday expirations enabled consistent 1-DTE and 7-DTE strategies; later daily expirations allowed true frequency scaling [14:02–14:55]
- **Current approach**: Trades SPX exclusively with multiple fixed-duration strategies (1-day, 7-day, 14-day); at-the-money fixed risk/reward; minimal rolling or adjustment [47:22–48:08]

### The transition to full-time (t0 = ~4 years before interview)
- **Runway building**: Saved aggressively after paying off early debt; left job with ~1 year of expenses saved, plus company share buyout [27:28–28:12]
- **Trigger**: Friday incident with management over YouTube content; resigned Sunday; gave 30-day notice [34:25–37:45]
- **Pre-transition income**: Had Patreon, YouTube channel, and research partnership generating supplementary cash flow; confident in SPX zero-DTE strategies [45:03–45:49]
- **Post-transition**: Launched Alpha Crunching (analytics/backtesting tool) in November 2022, ~2.5 years after leaving corporate job [45:49–46:10]

### Core trading principles
- **At-the-money fixed risk/reward**: Sells spreads at ATM strikes; doesn't chase delta or IV; either wins or loses—no rolling [47:46–48:08]
- **Market regime alignment**: Primarily bullish put spreads because markets drift up over time; avoids selling call spreads in bull markets; sits out when market environment doesn't fit strategy [49:19–50:36]
- **Discipline over complexity**: Avoids trading both sides; doesn't adjust unless necessary (e.g., iron condors in strong uptrends); lets math play out [48:55–52:22]
- **Frequency + consistency**: Increased frequency (1-DTE daily) with same win rate and risk/reward allows smaller position sizing and more consistent execution [16:13–16:42]

### Advice for beginners
- **Start narrow**: Pick one ticker (or 2–3), one structure (verticals recommended for lower risk), one timeframe [58:27–59:11]
- **Timeframe alignment**: Daily chart for day-job traders; avoid 5-minute setups you can't execute consistently [59:11–59:29]
- **Duration**: 30–45 DTE is standard entry point; can compress to 1-DTE if you have time and backtesting [59:48–01:00:11]
- **Fractal nature**: Same principles apply across all durations; boring entry, exciting execution, discipline to do nothing [01:00:11–01:00:34]

### Personal philosophy
- **Freedom over wealth**: Prioritized time with family and flexibility over maximum income; willing to earn less for control of schedule [23:23–24:26]
- **Persistence & curiosity**: Blew out accounts early; learned from losses; ego (confidence) balanced with humility [55:39–56:47]
- **Continuous learning**: No finance degree; learned markets, Fed policy, economic cycles through trading and curiosity [57:43–58:03]
