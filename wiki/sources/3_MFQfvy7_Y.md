# Understanding Delta: Exposure to the Underlying

## Summary

Delta quantifies the directional exposure of an options contract to the underlying asset. A delta of 53 on a call option means the premium will increase by approximately 53 cents for every $1 move in the underlying, all else equal. This metric reveals that an option contract behaves like a fractional share position rather than the full 100-share notional exposure.

## Key takeaways

- **Delta as exposure metric** [00:00]: Delta tells you how much the option premium will change per $1 move in the underlying (ceteris paribus).
- **Fractional share equivalence** [00:30]: A 665 call with delta 53 behaves like owning 53 shares of SPY, not the full 100-share contract notional.
- **Baseline concept**: Every options contract represents 100 shares of the underlying, but delta reveals the actual directional sensitivity at any given moment.

## Notable quotes

"If spy goes up by a dollar, the premium here will increase by 53 cents. Ceteris paribus, meaning everything else remaining the same as a baseline concept."
