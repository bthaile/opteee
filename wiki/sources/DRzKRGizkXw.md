# GME Options: The Volatility Story Isn't Over Yet

## Summary

Post-shareholder meeting (June 17, 2024), GME implied volatility compressed from ~280% to ~204% — a 76-point drop — but remained elevated above 200% with 4 days to expiration, suggesting the market still prices significant price movement. Analysis of the options chain reveals a shift from yesterday's put-skewed volatility surface to a more normalized smile on the front expiration, while the June 28 cycle retains call skew, indicating mixed directional sentiment and substantial retail participation in near-term, out-of-the-money contracts.

## Key takeaways

- **IV compression post-event** [01:53–02:42]: GME IV fell from 280%+ to 204% after shareholder meeting close, but remained elevated; June 28 expiration still priced at ~180% vs. 150–160s elsewhere — unusual persistence suggesting market still expects large moves.

- **Volatility surface normalization** [04:27–05:19]: Front expiration (June 21) shifted from yesterday's depressed-put skew (bullish bias) to a standard volatility smile with slight call skew; June 28 retains the call-skewed structure seen previously, indicating different pricing regimes across expirations.

- **Order-flow interpretation** [05:42–07:21]: 36% of call volume traded at bid-or-below (likely sales/covered calls); 39% of put volume traded at ask-or-above (likely buys/protective puts). Net effect: more downside speculative activity than upside, despite elevated call open interest.

- **Open-interest vs. volume inference** [08:09–08:34]: June 21 $26 calls show 5,400 OI but 20,000 volume — implying ~15,000 net new buys. June 28 $30 calls similarly show net buying despite massive OI, indicating retail accumulation in far-OTM, short-dated strikes.

- **Moneyness and delta asymmetry** [08:55–09:15]: Stock at $25.22; first OTM put ($25) is closer to spot but carries lower delta than first OTM call ($25.50), signaling slight upside pricing bias despite proximity.

- **Retail participation risk** [11:04–11:32]: Majority of volume concentrated in front two expirations; heavy activity in $30 calls (far OTM, ~4 days to expiration) — a pattern flagged as dangerous for new traders due to theta decay and low probability of profit.
