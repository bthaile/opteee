# Effective Portfolio Management w/ Tom Basso | The Outlier Podcast Ep74

## Summary

Tom Basso discusses comprehensive portfolio construction and risk management for traders of all experience levels. The conversation covers position sizing methodology, the importance of trading plans with defined entry/exit rules, diversification across uncorrelated asset classes, and a dual-timeframe strategy combining long-term trend-following with short-term counter-trend trades. Basso emphasizes that robust, simple systems outperform complex, over-fitted approaches, and that most retail traders fail because they lack a plan before markets move against them.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2024-08-14)

- **Recent selloff context** [02:01–02:49]: After a prolonged rally concentrated in mega-cap tech (especially [[nvda]]), profit-taking triggered a sharp correction. Basso notes the selling was panic-driven but buying has been weak; recovery attempts show thin bars and low conviction. Risk of another leg down into a bear market remains if support breaks.
- **Market structure observation** [03:34–04:16]: Gap-down moves into strong support (e.g., Aug 5) reflect order-flow dynamics and computer-driven position adjustment rather than "magical" support levels. Once sellers exhaust, buyers hesitate to step in; current market is quiet with minimal new highs.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **The critical missing piece: trading plans** [06:28–07:46]: Most retail traders lack a complete plan (entry, exit, stop-loss, profit-taking method, position sizing). When markets reverse, they panic and ask for advice too late. Basso's response: "develop a plan and execute it; get it right next time."
- **Position sizing as risk control** [10:31–11:50]: Size positions based on **dollar risk per trade**, not notional cost. Calculate: (equity × 0.5%) ÷ (stop-loss in dollars per share) = number of shares. This ensures consistent risk across volatile and stable positions. Basso runs long-term retirement positions at 0.5% of equity per trade.
- **Diversification across uncorrelated assets** [11:50–13:45]: A long-only stock portfolio can be hedged or diversified by adding [[crude-oil]], [[gold]], [[natural-gas]], [[copper]], [[silver]], [[sugar]], [[cocoa]], crypto [[btc]]/[[ether]] futures, and other commodities. These move independently; blending them smooths equity curves and reduces drawdowns. Basso trades 35 futures markets to achieve this.
- **Dual-timeframe strategy design** [15:54–17:36]: Basso runs a long-term trend-following strategy (3+ weeks) and a short-term counter-trend strategy (3 days or less) on the same underlying. The counter-trend is sized ~2× the long-term position; it captures mean-reversion pops and pays for itself, buffering the long-term drawdowns. The two intentionally oppose each other, reducing net volatility.
- **Counter-trend entry mechanics** [24:18–26:01]: Use a 21-day RSI stochastic (overbought >80, oversold <20) as a **condition**, not a trigger. Only enter short when overbought *and* a trend-following model confirms reversal. This avoids whipsaw; you miss the extreme but catch the relief move. Basso does not use pure mean-reversion; he prefers counter-trend (short-term trend against long-term trend).
- **Position count guidance by account size** [27:09–28:42]: 
  - **$5k account**: ~10 positions (5 stocks + 5 micro futures for diversification and experience).
  - **$100k account**: ~20 positions (10 stocks + 10 futures).
  - **Larger accounts**: Scale up; Basso runs 35 markets and considers adding more.
  - Avoid concentration in one sector (e.g., all tech); spread across industries and asset classes.
- **Micro futures for small accounts** [28:42–30:21]: Liquid micro contracts include [[ether]] futures, lumber, [[corn]], [[soybean]], British pound, [[silver]] (1,000 oz, not 5,000), [[sugar]], [[natural-gas]], Australian dollar. These allow diversification without massive capital.
- **Robustness over complexity** [35:24–35:48]: Keep parameter sets low and robustness high. A simple system that works across major market runs (last 3–4 years) beats an over-fitted system that breaks in new regimes. Avoid over-modulation and over-complication.
- **Liquidity as a hard filter** [33:51–34:15]: Only trade markets that can handle your position size and trade with sufficient volume/open interest. Illiquid markets (e.g., milk trading 20 contracts/day for 5 minutes) are excluded. [[crude-oil]] (24/7 trading) is a natural fit.
- **Rolling long-term positions** [23:35–23:56]: Roll contracts when the new contract trades higher volume and open interest than the old one. A year-and-a-half trade might involve 15–18 rolls (e.g., crude oil).

## Notable quotes

> "To not have a plan is to fail."
