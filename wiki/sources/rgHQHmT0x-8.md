# Dialing it in #optionstrading

## Summary

Eric discusses the hallmark of a mature trader: the ability to anticipate trade outcomes before execution. Rather than predicting market direction, "dialing it in" means understanding the likely scenarios for each position—what success looks like and what failure looks like—and building a repeatable, scalable process around that clarity. This foundation enables consistent expected-value calculations and long-term profitability.

## Key takeaways

- **Knowing you're dialed in is a feeling, not a question** [00:00] — if you have to ask whether you've got it, you don't yet.
- **Anticipate trade outcomes, not market moves** [00:20] — before entering, you should know what winning and losing scenarios look like for that specific trade.
- **Consistency builds repeatable process** [00:20] — once you can predict your own trade outcomes reliably, you can scale and calculate expected return.
- **Expected return calculation follows clarity** [00:44] — only after understanding likely outcomes can you verify the trade makes money long-term.
- **Markets will always throw curveballs** [00:20] — the goal is not perfect prediction, but structured anticipation of your own position behavior.

## Notable quotes

> "If you have to ask the question you know it's not that point" — on recognizing mastery.

> "When you're putting on a trade and you already know if this works this is what it will look like if it loses this is what it will look like" — the core of dialed-in trading.
