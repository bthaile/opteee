# An Inside Look into the GameStop Unusual Options Activity

## Summary

Eric analyzes a significant wave of short put selling in GameStop across multiple strikes (95, 100, 105) and expirations (January 2026), totaling approximately 21.2 million in premium traded. The activity exhibits characteristics of a synthetic long stock position—effectively controlling ~200,000+ shares through deep in-the-money puts rather than outright equity purchase, likely for capital efficiency or regulatory reasons. The position's delta-one behavior means it will track the underlying dollar-for-dollar, with P&L driven by intrinsic value decay rather than volatility or time decay.

## Key takeaways

- **Massive put premium accumulation [00:00–01:22]**: The last two days show stacked put and call premium lines, indicating significant sold put volume. However, cannot conclude bullish intent in isolation—must consider counterparty's full portfolio context.

- **Strike progression and timing [01:22–02:31]**: Activity began yesterday at the 95 strike, then shifted to 105s today. The 125 January 2026 puts trade at 17 cents (cheap, low premium-adjusted size), while 105 puts at 79 cents represent larger premium-adjusted blocks despite lower contract volume (2,700 vs. 6,000).

- **Block size and fill distribution [02:31–03:51]**: Trades came through in blocks of 23–25 contracts at 95+ delta (effectively one delta), with premiums ranging 170–190 cents. Total premium traded today: ~21.2 million across 100s and 105s January 2026 expiries. Activity ran all day from ~7 a.m. onward.

- **Synthetic long interpretation [03:51–04:59]**: Current delta on 105 position exceeds 200,000, representing ~213,750 share equivalents. Maximum position size ~225,000 shares. Margin requirement ~$2,600 per contract (portfolio margin), nearly equivalent to buying shares outright at current prices. This is not a volatility play—it's capital-efficient share control.

- **Why one leg, not two? [06:05–07:19]**: The credit is overwhelmingly intrinsic value, not extrinsic premium. Likely driven by regulatory requirements or desire to build synthetic long, not yield optimization. If underlying stays near current levels, credit will be given back.

- **Delta-one mechanics and price movement [07:19–08:32]**: With delta ≈ 1, a $5 move in GameStop (e.g., $26 → $31) causes the option premium to decay by $5 (e.g., $74 → $69), not due to time or vol but because delta shows rate of change. Trader nets the underlying move through the option.

- **Not a loss-passing scheme [09:42–10:48]**: If this were a short position being hedged, P&L on short and long option would cancel; there's no "slick way" to avoid losses. The position is straightforward synthetic long.

- **Position completion signal [10:48–11:57]**: Yesterday's 95s activity ran to close; today's 100s/105s started near open but stopped before close. Suggests position may be complete. Continuation tomorrow would confirm terminal size. Call volume today was heavy (81,000 vs. 15,000 puts), centered in front expiration.

- **Volatility surface is flat [11:57–13:13]**: The vol curve is "relatively mellow" across the strikes. No relative volatility play evident. Confirms synthetic long thesis over any skew or term-structure arbitrage.
