# GME Livestream w/ @RichardNewton | History & Tin Foil Hats

## Summary

Eric and Richard Newton discuss the GameStop saga from multiple angles: Richard's journey from retail trader to deep researcher of cyclical patterns in GME, the mechanics of fails-to-deliver and ETF juggling, the impact of recent share offerings, and the predictable 34-trading-day cycles driven by options expiration settlement and ETF reconstitution. The conversation covers the bear thesis (legacy retail gaming business), the bull thesis (balance sheet recovery, Ryan Cohen's involvement, Roaring Kitty's position), and the technical mechanics that appear to suppress price while creating periodic volatility spikes.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2024-07-07)

- **GME price range and volatility**: Stock has traded from ~$10 to ~$80 in recent months; currently range-bound $22–$27 on light volume [02:26]
- **Recent share offerings**: Two dilutions totaling ~120M shares raised ~$3.1B; first at ~$20/share (May), second at ~$28/share (June) [28:16, 30:59]
- **Roaring Kitty position**: Holds ~9M shares (up from ~800k known earlier); bought heavily into June offering cycle [33:04]
- **Institutional ownership**: Dropped from ~99% to low levels; institutions largely exited; retail owns ~300M shares via DRS [51:16]
- **XRT (retail ETF) status**: Shorted 400%+; holds only 1.1% GME (down from major holding); Chewy now largest holding [01:02:41]
- **Expected August cycle**: Predicted cycle around Aug 8 due to June Opex settlement (34 trading days); July typically skipped due to holiday timing [01:12:37]

### Evergreen mechanics

- **The bear thesis (2020–2021)**: Declining revenue per store, shift to digital distribution, no customer stickiness, poor leadership [10:37]
- **The bull thesis (2024)**: Zero bankruptcy risk; $4B+ cash; profitable on balance sheet; Ryan Cohen (no salary, 8.8% stake) signaling transformation; collectibles/PSA cards and potential M&A [12:07, 13:54]
- **Fails-to-deliver juggling**: Market makers move FTDs between GameStop direct fails, ETF fails (especially XRT), and equity total-return swaps to avoid reporting and manage hedging costs [39:02, 40:06]
- **34-trading-day cycle theory**: Cycles appear ~34 trading days after monthly options expiration, driven by ETF authorized participant settlement windows and reconstitution demand [54:35, 56:22]
- **Why cycles happen**: Options expiration creates demand for GameStop shares; market makers fail ETF creation units (lacking GME) to avoid buying shares; 34 days later they must reconcile, creating volume and price movement [53:41, 54:15]
- **Why cycles stop**: Long-duration equity swaps (entered Aug 2022, expired May/June 2024) suppressed cycles; when swaps expired, cycles resumed [47:57, 48:18]
- **Seasonality**: June cycles hit ~66% of the time; July almost never; August consistent; December rare [01:00:56]
- **Share offerings into cycles**: Company has issued shares into relative highs (May 14 peak $80, June 6 peak $67) rather than lows, telegraphing awareness of cycles; Roaring Kitty's continued buying signals confidence [28:38, 30:59, 32:28]
- **Max pain dynamics**: Market makers have incentive to end weeks near max pain to scalp premium; however, order flow ultimately drives price, not max pain alone [09:01, 01:09:21]
- **Institutional lending loop**: Institutions lend shares, buy them back at lower prices, increasing holdings; this creates 100% institutional ownership in XRT basket except GME, Chewy, and one other stock [01:04:04]
