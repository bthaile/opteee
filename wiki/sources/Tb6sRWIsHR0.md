# David Hunter: A Detailed Look at Parabolic Melt Ups and Global Busts | The Outlier Podcast

## Summary

David Hunter discusses his updated equity and commodity targets in the context of a final parabolic bull-market leg, driven by potential Iran deal resolution, falling rates, and broadening market participation. He forecasts S&P 500 to 10,000, NASDAQ to 36,000, and precious metals to historic highs (gold 7,000, silver 200), followed by a historic bear market and "bust" (80% drawdown) driven by excessive leverage across the financial system. The interview covers timing uncertainty, the role of geopolitical catalysts, and the mechanics of leverage amplification on both the upside and downside.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2026-06-04)

- **Updated equity targets** [02:27–02:50]: S&P 500 raised to 10,000 (from 9,500), NASDAQ to 36,000 (from 32,000), Dow to 67,000 (from 65,000), Russell 2000 to 4,000 (from 3,800). Hunter estimates 30–35% upside from current levels over 3–4 months.
- **Iran deal as catalyst** [03:20–04:27]: Hunter assigns >50% odds to a near-term Iran resolution that would open the Strait of Hormuz, drive oil down, and trigger the final parabolic leg. Emphasizes this is a market-sentiment read, not a geopolitical prediction.
- **Market broadening underway** [05:40–06:24]: Russell 2000 outperforming; financials, materials, and basic industries beginning to catch up to tech. Miners and metals poised for inflection.
- **Precious metals targets** [48:00–51:20]: Silver raised to 200 (from 125 in January), gold to 7,000 (from 5,500). Expects another parabolic run; silver miners (GDXJ, SIL) could triple; gold miners (GDX) could double or more.
- **Copper and materials** [42:57–44:25]: Copper target $8 (possibly higher); COPX to 140. Materials benefiting from AI data-center buildout and power-plant construction.
- **Financials poised for catch-up** [40:53–42:27]: XLF target 90 (60–80% move from pre-June levels). Rates falling is bullish for financials (forward-looking stimulus signal); deregulation under Trump also supportive.
- **Dollar consolidation** [01:06:44–01:07:38]: DXY consolidating; expects next leg down to 89–90, then 82–83. Weaker dollar supports metals and equities.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Parabolic final leg** [12:42–13:28]: Each successive leg of the 44-year bull market (since 2009) has steepened; final leg will be steepest and most parabolic. Expects 30%+ moves in 3–4 months, similar to 2020–21 or recent semiconductor rallies.
- **Leverage amplification** [58:01–01:05:07]: Excessive leverage (debt, derivatives, margin, ETFs) on both economy (~$330 trillion global debt) and markets will accelerate downside when cycle turns. Bitcoin's move from 120k to 50k cited as precursor of leverage unwinding.
- **Fed policy lag** [01:03:32–01:05:27]: Fed reluctant to repeat 2008–2020 stimulus (zero rates, QE). Will respond slowly to bust, creating 2–3 month window of free-fall before policy catches up. Potential Fed balance sheet expansion to $30 trillion (vs. current ~$6.6 trillion).
- **Bust vs. bear market** [54:06–57:16]: Bust = economy + financial system crisis (worse than 2008), not just stock decline. Requires recession first; no imminent warning signs yet. Could begin late 2026, likely last 12–18 months.
- **Sentiment contrarianism** [31:10–32:22]: Fed funds futures showing 40%+ hike probability (March 2027) reflects current trader bearishness on bonds, not predictive. Hunter contrarian: expects rate cuts, not hikes; wall of worry in bond market.
- **Target-raising discipline** [11:57–14:12]: Hunter raises targets when sentiment is most bearish (e.g., January 2022 during correction). Acknowledges this creates perception problem—people skeptical at announcement, only believe in hindsight. Driven by macro analysis, not marketing.

## Regime / context

**Date context**: Recorded June 2026, six months after Hunter's previous appearance (January 2026). Market at ~7,600 S&P 500; oil elevated due to Iran tensions; bond yields consolidating after multi-year top.

**Cycle context**: Hunter frames this as the final leg of a 44-year cyclical bull market (2009–2026) that will end in a historic bear market and "bust" (80% drawdown, 12–18 months). Timing of bust uncertain but earliest late 2026; more likely 2027. Leverage (debt, derivatives) is the structural driver; geopolitical resolution (Iran) is the near-term catalyst for parabolic equity/commodity rally.

**Caveat**: Hunter's targets are macro-cycle calls, not trading calls. Timing has drifted 3+ years from original 2021–22 forecasts; direction and magnitude have proven accurate. Acknowledges he is "way ahead" of consensus and often early; expects to be early on bust timing as well.
