# 0DTE SPX Option Strategy Comparison

## Summary

This video compares zero-DTE SPX short-straddle strategies across different profit and loss management approaches, analyzing backtested results from January 2021 to August 2023. The analysis reveals that unmanaged positions lose money, but disciplined loss management at 25% max loss produces positive returns with acceptable drawdowns. The data highlights that transaction costs and bid-ask spreads are the primary headwind for retail traders in zero-DTE options, and that results improve significantly after May 2022 when daily SPX expirations launched.

## Key takeaways

- **Zero-DTE volume is rising exponentially, but retail profitability is negative after costs** [01:43–02:30]. Gross returns may look positive, but slippage and commissions (hidden in wide spreads) consistently wipe out retail edge.

- **Unmanaged short straddles lose ~$46k over 2.5 years (Jan 2021–Aug 2023)** [04:26–04:52]. Letting positions expire without management is a losing strategy; CAGR is negative 21%.

- **Managing profits at 25% max profit only is insufficient** [05:08–05:35]. This approach yields a small positive P&L but leaves losses to run unchecked, resulting in high drawdowns and low average win size.

- **Managing losses at 25% max loss is the single best approach in the first dataset** [05:35–05:59]. This produces +$14k P&L, 18.9% max drawdown, and 37.8% win rate. The key insight: let winners run, cap losers tightly.

- **Combining 25% profit cap + 25% loss cap reduces drawdown further** [06:53–07:13]. Although P&L is slightly lower than loss-management-only, max drawdown drops significantly, offering better risk-adjusted returns.

- **Results improve dramatically after May 13, 2022 (SPX daily expirations launch)** [08:57–09:45]. The same unmanaged short straddle becomes profitable (+$17.6k) with only 17.6% max drawdown. More occurrences and better market conditions both contribute.

- **Loss management is critical across all scenarios** [10:09–10:32]. Best CAGR figures consistently appear in strategies with explicit loss caps. Hard stops are problematic because they can exit trades that recover; iron flies or defined-risk structures are preferable for smaller accounts.

- **Time-frame-based management (e.g., 1-hour exit) underperforms percent-based rules** [08:14]. Random time exits do not outperform profit/loss percentage targets.

## Regime / context

This analysis spans two distinct regimes: (1) Jan 1, 2021–Aug 19, 2023 (full dataset, includes 2021 volatility spike and 2022 drawdown), and (2) May 13, 2022–Aug 19, 2023 (post-SPX daily expiration launch). The second period shows materially better risk-adjusted returns, suggesting that increased liquidity and daily roll opportunities improve retail profitability. The 2021–2022 period included significant market dislocations and elevated volatility, which may have penalized short-premium strategies more heavily. Results are specific to short straddles on SPX; applicability to other underlyings or strategies (e.g., iron flies, iron condors) is not directly addressed but is flagged as future analysis.
