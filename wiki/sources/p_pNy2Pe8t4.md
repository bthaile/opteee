# Options Tricks — Simulate Passage of Time Easily

## Summary

This video teaches a practical mental-modeling technique for simulating how option Greeks behave under different price and time scenarios without running full calculations. By flipping between expirations and adjusting strikes to match hypothetical price moves, traders can quickly estimate changes in delta, gamma, theta, and vega across their positions.

## Key takeaways

- **Surrogate-options method** [00:19]: To simulate a $5 price move, adjust your strike down by $5 (e.g., 70-strike becomes 65-strike) while keeping the expiration constant; this shows you what your position would look like deeper in the money.
- **Greek estimation via visual inspection** [00:43]: Once you've adjusted the strike to reflect your hypothetical price move, you can eyeball the new delta, gamma, theta, and vega without recalculating—this gives you a fast sense of position behavior.
- **Term-structure flipping** [00:19]: Comparing strikes across different expirations (within the same term) lets you mentally simulate passage of time and price movement simultaneously.
