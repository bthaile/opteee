# Who Trades Options?

## Summary

A brief educational segment exploring the psychological pitfalls of options trading, specifically the danger of cherry-picking short-term wins without understanding underlying risk. The discussion emphasizes that small, outsized gains (e.g., $4,000 in 10 minutes) can create a false sense of edge and lead traders astray from sound principles.

## Key takeaways

- **Beware of small-snapshot wins** [00:00] — A $4,000 gain in 10 minutes may feel compelling but is often a statistical anomaly, not evidence of a repeatable edge.
- **"If it seems too good to be true, it probably is"** [00:00] — A foundational principle for evaluating trading opportunities; outsized short-term returns warrant skepticism.
- **Process discipline over outcome bias** — Traders must distinguish between lucky outcomes and sound methodology to avoid overconfidence.
