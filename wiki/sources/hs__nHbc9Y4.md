# Trading Rules Don't Stop Winners

## Summary

Eric addresses the common complaint that trading rules (like pattern day trading restrictions) are rigged against retail traders, arguing that regulatory constraints are not the fundamental determinant of trading success. The core message: rules may limit *which* strategies you can execute, but they don't prevent you from developing and maintaining an edge.

## Key takeaways

- **Regulatory constraints ≠ edge killer** [00:00–00:19]: Pattern day trading rules and similar restrictions make certain strategies harder to execute, but they are not the difference between having an edge and lacking one.
- **Strategy-specific impact** [00:19]: Rules may prevent you from operating in specific markets or executing particular strategies, but this is a constraint on *execution*, not on edge development.
- **Focus on what you control** [00:00]: Rather than blaming systemic "rigging," traders should concentrate on building genuine edge through process and discipline.
