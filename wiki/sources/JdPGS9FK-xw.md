# How to NOT Mess Up Your Position Sizing | Options Trading for Beginners Ep4

## Summary

Episode 4 of the Outlier Options Trading Bootcamp focuses on position sizing as a critical trader mistake. The core lesson: position sizing is the only element of risk you can control before a trade executes; oversizing leads to ruin regardless of edge, while proper sizing allows positive-expectancy strategies to compound reliably over time. Kelly criterion provides an objective framework for sizing, though fractional Kelly (half or quarter) is safer than full Kelly for most traders.

## Key takeaways

- **Position sizing is the only controllable risk variable** [19:24] — You cannot control how a trade moves once live; sizing is your sole lever before entry.
- **Oversizing kills positive-edge strategies** [17:43] — Two traders with identical +EV systems: 2% risk survives; 25% risk goes bust after four consecutive losses (path dependency).
- **Risk ≠ premium spent or notional value** [23:03] — Risk equals loss at invalidation (stop level), not option premium or contract notional. Confusing these is the most common sizing error.
- **Kelly criterion maximizes growth but not safety** [40:08] — Full Kelly optimizes portfolio growth but creates extreme drawdown volatility; half or quarter Kelly maintains ~67% of growth while cutting drawdown risk nearly in half [54:13].
- **Bad paths are real and probable** [36:40] — Monte Carlo simulations show centralized performance, but you live in one universe; a low-probability tail path can still occur and blow up your account if you're oversized.
- **High win rate is a trap** [50:22] — High win-rate strategies often have large tail losses; win rate alone is indifferent to profitability and misleads many traders into oversizing.
- **Size relative to your system, not conviction** [42:35] — Conviction is subjective and emotional; size based on backtested/forward-tested system statistics instead. Newer traders should avoid conviction-based sizing.
- **Model your position lifecycle with Greeks** [01:02:06] — Use Greeks (delta, theta, vega) and days-to-expiration scenarios to estimate realistic P&L across the holding period, not just at entry.
- **Fractional Kelly drastically reduces ruin probability** [55:39] — Half Kelly reduces end-loss probability from ~13% to ~1.7%, a meaningful safety improvement with minimal growth sacrifice.
- **Compounding requires account survival** [47:40] — Modest, steady sizing allows compounding to work; aggressive early sizing chases quick returns but increases drawdown and ruin risk.

## Notable quotes
