# GME WAKING UP | GameStop Analysis Live Ep8

## Summary

Eric conducts a deep technical and options analysis of GameStop's recent volume expansion ahead of earnings (September 10), comparing current price-action patterns to historical precedent. The session explores the 8K filing announcing debt facility cancellation, examines roaring-kitty's historical trading methodology through archived posts, and analyzes a large 660k call position targeting the September 20 expiration—potentially signaling renewed activist interest. Volume scaling from ~5M to ~15M shares over two days is contextualized as distinct from typical earnings-driven spikes, suggesting additional structural factors at play.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2024-09-01)

- **Volume expansion precedes move** [08:00–09:45]: GameStop volume tripled from 5M to 15M shares over Aug 27–30, still a week before earnings (Sept 10). This differs from March earnings pattern where volume spiked only 1–2 days prior. Suggests non-earnings catalyst or broader structural shift.
- **Debt facility cancellation is major catalyst** [31:18–34:35]: GameStop's 8K filing canceling its credit facility removes restrictions on restricted payments, change-of-control provisions, and affiliate transactions. This grants full autonomy and hints at potential M&A activity rather than pure reinvestment.
- **Large call position on Sept 20 expiration** [57:18–02:01:35]: 660k call trade targeting 20 Sept expiration (through earnings) entered late Friday. Timing and size suggest institutional or high-net-worth retail positioning; potentially roaring-kitty given historical behavioral fingerprints (end-of-day entry, single-strike concentration, conviction-based sizing).

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Volume as leading indicator** [08:00–11:40]: Relative volume (vs. 5/20-day moving average) matters more than absolute figures. GameStop's recent 2–3× volume expansion on 5–20M baseline is meaningful; compare to 2021 squeeze peaks (200M+) only in context of prevailing regime.
- **Technical analysis has limits on GME** [05:49–07:33]: GameStop violates standard technical patterns unpredictably ("follows the rules until it doesn't"). Use technical tools as reference, not prediction; volume and order-flow are more reliable signals.
- **Roaring-kitty's trading style** [44:00–02:01:35]: Historical posts reveal options-first approach (not stock-first with options overlay). Early positions were 100% calls; scaling in over weeks/months; single-strike preference; no selling of calls or puts observed; extreme conviction (held through 175M+ daily gains without scaling). Suggests thesis-driven, window-based entry (not day-specific) and willingness to absorb drawdowns.
- **Earnings volatility windows vs. specific dates** [49:20–52:40]: Rather than pinpointing exact FTD/swap expiration dates, frame analysis as 5–10 day windows around catalysts. Roaring-kitty's early Jan–Feb 2021 entries predate the Feb 24 squeeze by weeks, implying he was trading a window, not a precise trigger.
- **Dark pools and off-exchange volume are regulated** [36:00–42:20]: Dark pool orders still report to consolidated tape (minutes delay, not days). Internalized orders do not report. Off-exchange volume is trackable via FINRA TRF; system is unfair to retail but not "totally rigged"—large entities would prevent GME moves if they had total control.
- **Merger arbitrage (Siri/Liberty) carries deal risk** [22:00–23:25]: Siri consolidation is internal reorganization, not traditional M&A, but still carries execution risk. Spread between Liberty Media and Siri series reflects arbitrage opportunity; if deal fails, spread collapses.
