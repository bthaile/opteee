# Portfolio Adjustments ZNGA, BAC, and XLF - Small Stacks Pt9

## Summary

Eric reviews tactical adjustments to the small-stacks portfolio, closing the ZNGA short put near expiration, liquidating the BAC diagonal to pivot into an XLF covered strangle for sector diversification, and adding a short call to a KO diagonal. The account has grown from $5,000 to $5,616 (~11% return) in three weeks, with credit spreads and diagonals outperforming covered strangles in the current regime.

## Key takeaways

- **ZNGA short put closure** [00:25–01:13]: Sold 8.5 put for 27¢, closed at 18¢ for ~$9 P&L (>1% return). Closed within 20 days to expiration to limit gamma risk exposure—a disciplined exit rule.
- **BAC diagonal → XLF covered strangle pivot** [01:55–02:48]: Liquidated BAC diagonal (+$26 P&L) to establish XLF covered strangle (~2% return on capital). Rationale: sector ETF reduces unsystematic risk vs. single-stock concentration.
- **KO diagonal scaling** [03:06–04:11]: Added short call to existing long call position, converting to covered call structure. Waiting for pullback to add long call leg; collecting premium upfront while maintaining flexibility.
- **Portfolio metrics** [04:11–04:39]: 64% capital utilization, $3,600 cash, $616 total P&L (11% return since Sept 1). Pfizer was an outlier winner; diagonals currently outperforming covered strangles.
- **Forward strategy** [05:56–07:05]: Seeking another diagonal candidate; will hold XLF as primary covered position. Planning modest scaling ahead of election (unlike margin account) to preserve exposure in small-capital account.
