# Strategy to Trade SpaceX IPO

## Summary

Eric outlines a two-phase approach to trading a SpaceX IPO debut: first, capturing the opening-day run to the high via trend-following on equities (no derivatives available), then assessing whether to hold a carryover position into the close based on price action. The strategy emphasizes disciplined exit on the initial trend and avoidance of the trade if price breaks lower.

## Key takeaways

- **Phase 1: Run to the high** [00:00–00:22] — Trade the initial trend following the IPO open, expecting a higher open than the offer price and a high within the first hour; exit on the completion of that trend.
- **Equity-only constraint** [00:40] — No derivatives available; must use stock directly, which limits position flexibility.
- **Phase 2: Carryover decision** [00:40–01:00] — Assess price action into the close to determine whether to hold a position overnight; decision depends entirely on how the stock looks in the final minutes.
- **Downside discipline** [00:22–00:40] — If the stock breaks lower and trends the opposite direction, abandon the trade entirely rather than chase.
- **Trend-following exit** [00:00–00:22] — Exit the initial position once the trend to the high completes; do not hold through a reversal.

## Notable quotes

> "I'm going to trade the run to the high, whatever that looks like, whenever that happens, and then I'm going to see if there's any trending component to it."

This is a pre-IPO strategy framework for a hypothetical SpaceX public debut. The approach is generic to IPO day-one trading: it does not depend on SpaceX fundamentals, but rather on the mechanics of opening-day momentum and range-finding. The constraint to equities (no options) is typical for newly listed securities in their first hours of trading.
