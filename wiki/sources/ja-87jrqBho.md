# Being Your Own Portfolio And Risk Manager #optionstrading

## Summary

This video breaks down the three core roles a self-directed trader must play: trader, portfolio manager, and risk manager. Each function has distinct responsibilities—from identifying individual trade opportunities, to ensuring positions work cohesively and reflect risk-reward preferences, to ruthlessly enforcing alignment between actual portfolio risk and stated risk tolerance. The emphasis is on integrating these roles to maintain discipline and long-term objective alignment.

## Key takeaways

- **Trader role** [00:00]: Identify individual trade opportunities and execute them; this is the most visible but only one of three critical functions.
- **Portfolio manager role** [00:00]: Ensure each position "plays nicely" with others; verify the aggregate portfolio reflects your stated risk-reward preferences and longer-term objectives.
- **Risk manager role** [00:29]: Take your documented risk preferences and ruthlessly enforce alignment—both in terms of total portfolio risk and reward—and determine when and how to realign positions.
- **Overlap and separation** [00:00]: While trader, portfolio manager, and risk manager functions overlap, calling them out separately forces discipline in each domain.
- **Realignment discipline** [00:29]: Risk management includes not just monitoring but actively deciding where and when to adjust positions to maintain stated risk tolerance.
