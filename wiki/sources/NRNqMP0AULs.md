# Analyzing Roaring Kitty's Trades Pt4 | GameStop Analysis Live Ep12

## Summary

Eric continues his line-by-line analysis of Roaring Kitty's Reddit position updates from early 2021, tracking his 50,000 shares and 500 April 16 $12 calls through February–April. The session reveals Kitty's management of deep ITM options held close to expiration, an early exercise of calls in mid-April to acquire 100,000 additional shares at ~$155, and a pivot away from pure value investing toward a hybrid approach blending technical analysis and leverage. Eric emphasizes the importance of exit strategies and contrasts Kitty's lumpy, time-based position management with traditional value-investing discipline.

## Key takeaways

- **Position tracking (Feb–Apr 2021):** Kitty held 50k shares (basis ~$14.89) and 500 April 16 $12 calls; by Feb 19 he doubled shares to 100k (new basis ~$26.80); by Apr 16 he exercised the calls early and bought 50k more shares at ~$155 [01:13:00–01:45:00]
- **Exit strategy by time, not price:** Unlike typical traders, Kitty appears to manage options by holding them close to or into expiration rather than taking profits at advantageous price levels; this suggests conviction in the underlying thesis rather than tactical timing [01:19:35–01:20:45]
- **Deep ITM options decay:** Once options are far in the money (e.g., underlying at $120+ vs. $12 strike), theta decay is minimal, making it rational to hold rather than close early [01:35:22–01:36:00]
- **Ratio call strategy:** Eric demonstrates why he sells calls at a ratio (e.g., 100 calls against 500 shares) rather than covered calls (1:1) to preserve unlimited upside while collecting premium [32:05–34:38]
- **Value investor pivot:** Kitty's early spreadsheets show traditional value-investing models (DCF, cash-flow analysis); his purchase of 50k shares at $155 contradicts value-thesis pricing, suggesting he abandoned pure value investing for a hybrid approach incorporating technical analysis and leverage [02:27:40–02:44:29]
- **Account volatility & lumpy returns:** Kitty's account swung from ~$33M peak to $7M trough and back to $20M+ by April 2021, consistent with his stated philosophy of accepting lumpy returns rather than optimizing for smooth equity curves [02:29:56–02:30:46]
- **Multi-account hypothesis:** Chat speculation and spreadsheet evidence suggest Kitty may have tracked positions across multiple accounts; his 2020 portfolio tracker shows only 16% GME while Reddit posts show concentrated positions, hinting at separate YOLO vs. value portfolios [02:34:42–02:44:29]
- **Volume as a squeeze precursor:** Before every squeeze Kitty observed, volume expanded massively; this is a necessary but not sufficient condition for identifying squeeze setups [01:00:08–01:04:24]
- **Inverse correlation break:** By late September 2024, GME broke its correlation with Russell 2000 small-cap index, suggesting independent strength post-ATM offering [21:22–21:46]
- **Covered calls cap upside:** Maximum profit on a covered call is achieved when the stock closes above the short strike; selling calls is not bearish if the trader holds shares [02:18:51–02:19:45]

## Notable quotes

- "Strong convictions loosely held" — Eric's trading philosophy: develop well-researched ideas but abandon them immediately if contradicting data emerges [44:07–44:30]
- "I don't attach my identity to these different ideas" — on why he can pivot trading approaches without ego attachment [44:53]
