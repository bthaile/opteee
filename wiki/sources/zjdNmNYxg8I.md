# Previous Squeezes | GameStop Analysis Live Ep7

## Summary

Eric conducts a deep forensic analysis of GameStop's historical price movements, identifying volume expansion as the most reliable predictor of large intraday moves (50%+). Using 24 years of daily data, he finds 392 instances of 100%+ day-over-day volume expansion and only 21 instances of 50%+ intraday price moves, establishing volume as a consistent pre-move signal. The session also explores roaring Kitty's recent pivot into Chewy, examining whether he liquidated his GME position to fund the ~$230M Chewy stake.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2024-08-25)

- **Volume as the primary signal** [01:42–05:46]: Before every major GME squeeze examined (Jan 13 2021, Mar 10 2021, May 13 2024), volume expanded meaningfully 1–3 days prior. On May 1–3, volume went 2.6M → 8.6M → 36M shares, preceding a major move. This is the cleanest, most objective predictor Eric has found across 17 years of trading.
- **Short interest data inconsistency** [18:50–20:29]: Fintel shows 11 days to cover (99.7% short float), but MarketBeat shows 0.8 days to cover (10.8% short interest). The discrepancy likely reflects the May 2024 at-the-money offering, which diluted short positions and raised ~$2B in cash.
- **GME consolidating, lagging Russell** [28:19–29:43]: From Aug 15–23, Russell 2000 rallied consistently while GME curled downward. GME showed resilience on down days but failed to participate in the index rally—a bearish divergence heading into Sept 4 earnings.
- **Earnings volatility anomaly** [32:46–33:38]: Sept 4 earnings expiration shows only 30 call contracts vs. 70,000 on Aug 30 and 63 on Sept 13. This extreme contraction is unusual and suggests either hedging unwinding or low conviction ahead of the earnings release.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Fingerprinting as a framework** [01:42–02:23]: Eric's approach to identifying unique behavioral patterns in a security before major moves—not a widely accepted term, but useful for building contextual understanding of how a stock behaves under stress.
- **FTD cycles and multivariate complexity** [15:47–18:12]: Failed-to-deliver data alone does not predict price moves reliably. Tested 34-day FTD cycles across multiple historical squeezes; results are mixed. The system is too confounded by other variables (swap structures, ETF redemption cycles, dealer positioning) to isolate FTD as a standalone signal.
- **Roaring Kitty's position transition** [50:13–02:02:04]: Based on account value (~$268M as of June 13) and the Chewy 13G filing (9,001,000 shares at ~$25.85 = ~$232M), the math suggests Kitty liquidated most or all of his GME position to fund Chewy. He retains ~$30M in cash, but no evidence of a large remaining GME stake. The Chewy move appears to be a tribute to Ryan Cohen (9M shares mirrors his original GME entry size) rather than a fundamental thesis.
- **Market maker incentives vs. conspiracy** [36:38–37:14]: Market makers profit from volume and liquidity provision, not from suppressing price. While they may engage in hedging or positioning that affects price, attributing all GME suppression to malice oversimplifies the mechanics. Shorts that remain are likely trapped in swaps or betting on bankruptcy.
- **Earnings guidance and investor confidence** [40:37–45:40]: GME raised $2B+ in cash but has not communicated a clear use-of-proceeds plan. Eric expects this to be addressed on the Sept 4 earnings call (or shareholder meeting). Without guidance, long-term investors will lose confidence, even if the apes remain loyal. Broad themes (store optimization, audience expansion) are necessary but insufficient.
