# Bayesian analysis in options trading

## Summary

Bayesian analysis in options trading is a mental-model shift away from predictive certainty toward probabilistic reasoning: integrating all available information at a given moment to form a probabilistic expectation of future outcomes. The core discipline is updating that expectation continuously as new information arrives, rather than becoming emotionally attached to an initial thesis and defending it against contradictory evidence.

## Key takeaways

- **Reframe from prediction to probability** [00:00]: Replace "the market will go up" with "I think the market is more likely to go up than down—maybe 55% edge"—a probabilistic lens that acknowledges uncertainty.
- **Information integration is the job** [00:50]: Your role as a trader is to continuously integrate new information into your view. If new data forces you to reconsider your original premise, reconsider it; ego attachment is detrimental.
- **Avoid entrenchment** [00:25]: Traders commonly become too attached to their initial idea and reluctant to abandon it. Sensitivity to what is *actually happening* in the market, not what you predicted, is what matters.
- **Update, don't defend** [01:15]: Being sensitive to current market conditions and willing to update your thesis based on new information is the outlier edge.

## Notable quotes

- "Your job is to integrate new information as it comes in into your idea and if there's information that forces you to reconsider the original premise just reconsider—let go of the ego and focus more on regularly updating the way you view things based on new information."
