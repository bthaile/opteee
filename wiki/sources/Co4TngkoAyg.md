# An Inside Look at War, Tariffs, and Energy with Doomberg | The Outlier Podcast

## Summary

Doomberg discusses Trump's aggressive policy rollout, the Ukraine war as an unwinnable position for the US, the strategic use of tariffs to address fentanyl trafficking, and energy market dynamics under the new administration. The conversation covers geopolitical leverage asymmetries, China's patient long-term positioning, and the reshaping of global power structures through spheres of influence.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2025-02-06)

- **Trump's policy velocity**: Trump is executing at unprecedented speed across multiple domains—Ukraine, tariffs, federal spending, energy—creating constant market-moving announcements [00:02–03:37]
- **WTI crude at low end of OPEC range**: Oil trading near $70.50, below Saudi Arabia's $95–105 budget break-even; OPEC on its back foot [42:28]
- **Tariff strategy as negotiation tool**: Within 24 hours of tariff announcement, Mexico and Canada pledged increased fentanyl enforcement; Trump delayed implementation to negotiate permanent settlement [26:47–27:06]

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Ukraine as inherited losing hand**: Russia has superior industrial capacity, proximity to battlefield, existential motivation; US/NATO have extended supply chains and no viable path to kinetic victory [10:17–13:31]
- **Leverage asymmetry—Canada oil**: Canadian exports represent 22% of Canadian GDP vs. 1.5% of US GDP; US can retool refineries for light crude; Canada has minimal actual leverage despite heavy-oil exports [30:39–40:14]
- **Tariffs as coercive diplomacy**: Tariffs work when tied to specific, achievable objectives (fentanyl reduction) rather than broad economic punishment; speed of response indicates effectiveness [25:51–29:30]
- **China's patient positioning**: China avoids urgency, operates on decade-long horizons, benefits from US–Russia cohesion, gradually consolidates Taiwan without kinetic conflict [44:42–48:16]
- **Technological disruption in warfare**: Fiber-optic-controlled drones, hypersonic missiles (Oreshnik), and autonomous systems are reshaping military calculus; old expensive weapons systems now obsolete [21:18–23:43]
- **Energy supply flexibility**: Global oil markets are fungible; US can source from Venezuela, Guyana, Argentina, or retool refineries; no single supplier has decisive leverage [40:14–41:18]
