# Roaring Kitty GME Option Position Update

## Summary

Analysis of Roaring Kitty's GME long-call position following unusual options-chain activity on June 12, 2024. The position comprises ~5 million shares at ~$21.27 and 120,000 long $20 calls (June 2024 expiration), totaling ~$29 million unrealized P&L plus ~$29.4 million cash. Anomalous volume of ~93,000 contracts in the $20 strike suggests possible position unwinding, cashless exercise, or new retail entry, though definitive attribution remains unclear.

## Key takeaways

- **Position snapshot [00:21–01:29]:** 5M shares (~$20.9M), 120K $20 calls (~$8.1M), total unrealized P&L ~$29M; mid-price quotes do not reflect actual execution slippage on full liquidation.
- **Volume vs. open interest distinction [02:44–03:12]:** Volume = daily contract trades; open interest = currently open contracts. The 93K volume in $20 calls is anomalous relative to typical daily activity.
- **Anchoring and round-number clustering [03:48–04:11]:** Retail traders cluster around round strikes ($30, $35); this behavioral pattern is tradeable and reveals market structure.
- **Possible mechanisms [05:52–06:13]:** Cashless exercise (trading half the calls for ~$4M to buy shares at spot) is plausible given limited free cash; rolling to later expirations or new retail entry are alternatives.
- **Liquidity hierarchy [06:58–07:38]:** Standard monthly expirations (June, July, August) have far wider bid–ask spreads and lower open interest than primary cycles; larger traders gravitate to tighter markets.
- **Ambiguity on attribution [08:47–09:43]:** Without intraday buy/sell breakdown, cannot confirm whether volume is Kitty unwinding, rolling, or new buyers entering; awaiting next official position update.
