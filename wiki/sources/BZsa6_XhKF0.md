# Delta Probability Shortcut #optionstrading

## Summary

This short educational segment explains how to use delta as a quick proxy for the probability of an option finishing in the money at expiration, and introduces a simple doubling rule to estimate probability of touch. While the delta shortcut works reasonably well for near-term, lower-volatility positions, the actual calculated probabilities diverge as time to expiration increases and implied volatility rises.

## Key takeaways

- **Delta as probability proxy [00:00–00:26]**: Delta can be used as a shorthand for probability of being in the money (ITM) at expiration; a 0.30 delta ≈ 30% probability ITM, though actual calculated probability may differ slightly (e.g., 32.85% vs. 30%).
- **Probability of touch shortcut [00:26]**: Double the delta to estimate probability of touch during the option's life (e.g., 0.30 delta → ~60% probability of touch; actual: 65.11%).
- **Accuracy degrades with time and volatility [00:45]**: The delta shortcut becomes increasingly inaccurate for longer-dated options and higher-volatility underlyings; platforms allow direct calculation of true probabilities rather than relying on approximations.
- **Platform calculation advantage [00:00]**: Modern trading platforms can calculate exact probabilities of ITM and probability of touch directly, removing the need to rely on shortcuts.
