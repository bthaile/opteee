# Goldilocks analysis

## Summary

Eric introduces "Goldilocks analysis"—the cognitive bias of visualizing only favorable trade scenarios while avoiding the mental work of stress-testing downside outcomes. The core lesson: traders must practice exposure therapy-style scenario planning, especially for assignments and gap-down moves, to avoid being blindsided when conditions change and the "discount" thesis collapses.

## Key takeaways

- **Goldilocks trap:** When placing a trade, it's easy to focus only on the "beautiful" scenarios where it works in your favor [00:00–00:21].
- **Assignment psychology:** Selling a put at a discount price sounds attractive until the stock crashes *below* that level and conditions fundamentally change [00:21–00:41].
- **Scenario stress-testing:** Visualize and think through the "goes terribly" case; this is exposure therapy that becomes easier with practice [00:00–00:21].
- **Avoid selective thinking:** Don't cherry-pick only the scenarios that sound good; build a complete mental model of downside risk [00:41].

## Notable quotes

> "Once you get used to doing it it's really not that bad but for a lot of people they'll say things like oh if I sell a put down here and I get assigned I would love that I'm getting it at such a discount until the stock goes to that goes below it keeps crashing below it because conditions have changed and now it's a completely different thing."
