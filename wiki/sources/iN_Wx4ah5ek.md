# Trading Psychology - Digging Deep w/ Richard Friesen | The Outlier Podcast Ep61

## Summary

Eric and Richard Friesen explore the psychological foundations of trading success, moving beyond discipline and willpower toward awareness, acceptance, and agency. Through live coaching, they examine how survival mechanisms formed in childhood—the "inner bitch" seeking immediate gratification—replay in trading decisions, and how reframing "lucrative" trades (process-based wins) rather than profitable ones rewires the brain to execute strategy consistently.

## Key takeaways

- **Awareness-Acceptance-Agency framework** [12:27]: Rather than suppressing emotions, traders should notice sensations (awareness), accept them without judgment (acceptance), and choose their response (agency). This replaces internal struggle with conscious choice.

- **Lucrative vs. profitable trades** [01:02:16]: A lucrative trade is one executed correctly per plan—regardless of P&L. Reframing wins this way rewires the brain away from the disposition effect (selling winners too fast, holding losers).

- **The inner impulse has positive intent** [21:31]: The part of yourself seeking immediate gratification emerged to protect you when powerless. Appreciation and dialogue, not suppression, allow it to expand its time horizon and support long-term goals.

- **Coin-flip exercise** [01:03:29]: Trading with emotional attachment to outcome mirrors irrational reactions to a fair coin flip. Practicing detachment from single-trade outcomes builds the neurological pattern needed for system execution.

- **Presence of a coach shifts behavior** [01:05:49]: Traders who go off-plan almost daily stop doing so when a coach is present, because they're invited to their highest self. New neural pathways form through repeated small wins, not willpower.

- **COVID's minimal impact on traders** [01:20]: Traders tend to be screen-focused and engineer-minded; isolation had less psychological impact than on other professions. However, the influx of retail traders during COVID may have increased herd behavior and social learning effects.

- **Process over single-trade outcome** [06:46]: Delayed gratification from martial arts and weightlifting taught Eric that effort precedes visible results. Applying this to trading—each trade is one data point in a multi-year system—prevents overemphasis on any single win or loss.

- **Disposition effect and prospect theory** [59:02]: Traders hold losers (avoiding the pain of realization) and sell winners (capturing the dopamine hit). This is neurologically wired; the solution is reframing what constitutes a "win" (execution, not profit).

- **Stoicism vs. cooperation** [51:26]: While logic and reason help, they don't eliminate the impulse. Offering the inner impulse an alternative gratification (e.g., "we'll go for a run after the trade closes") cooperates with it rather than fighting it.

- **Survival patterns repeat** [44:37]: Childhood coping mechanisms (self-sufficiency, immediate relief-seeking) become templates for adult behavior. Recognizing this pattern allows traders to update their strategy without shame.

## Regime / context

Recorded March 6, 2024. This is the second appearance of Richard Friesen on the Outlier Podcast. The conversation blends trading mechanics (zero-DTE short strangles, the disposition effect) with depth-psychology coaching, making it both a technical and psychological resource. Richard's framework—awareness, acceptance, agency—is presented as an alternative to pure discipline-based approaches, though Eric's Marine Corps background and stoic practice represent a complementary (not contradictory) path. The coin-flip exercise and "lucrative trade" reframing are practical tools for rewiring reward associations in the brain.
