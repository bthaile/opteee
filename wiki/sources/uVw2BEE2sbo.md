# Optimizing Your Losing Trades: Techniques for Better Outcomes

## Summary

Losing trades are inevitable in trading and investing; the goal is to lose intelligently in individual circumstances so you can improve your strategy and compound returns over time. This video walks through a real case study of a trader who turned $7,000 into $90,000 in seven months, then lost 99.56% of their account, and extracts actionable lessons on emotional discipline, honest risk assessment, trade management, and systematic optimization from losses.

## Key takeaways

- **Losing is part of the game** [00:00] — If you trade or invest, you will lose. The point is not to avoid losses entirely, but to lose in aggregate less than you win, and to extract learning from each loss.

- **Learn from others' mistakes, not just your own** [01:33] — The learning curve is too steep to only learn from personal experience. Study other traders' errors to avoid repeating them.

- **Unrealistic early returns set dangerous expectations** [02:36] — The case study trader achieved a 1,185% return (annualized ~8,000%) in seven months ($7k → $90k), which created an unsustainable expectation and likely contributed to overleveraging and subsequent 99.56% drawdown.

- **Acknowledge the contradiction between stated risk tolerance and emotional reality** [04:54] — Traders often say "I was willing to lose this money" but then suffer severe emotional distress when they do. Be honest: if losing 99% of your account is hard to swallow, you were not actually willing to lose it.

- **Emotional discipline requires pre-planning, not post-hoc coping** [04:32] — Waiting until losses are painful to figure out how to cope emotionally is too late. Visualize scenarios and genuinely assess your risk tolerance before entering trades.

- **Greed is not inherently bad; oppressive greed is** [07:13] — Greed motivates opportunity-seeking and generosity. The problem arises when greed is so excessive that you make poor decisions (e.g., 100% drawdown chasing 8,000% annualized returns).

- **Losing well means staying even-keel** [09:07] — After a losing trade, do not feel perturbed; after a winning trade, do not feel elated. Emotional neutrality is the goal.

- **Assess each loss against your plan and risk parameters** [09:50] — Ask: Did I maintain proper position sizing? Did I follow my trade management plan? Did I execute the right steps? If your 8% stop loss resulted in a much larger loss, something needs adjustment.

- **Treat losses as data points for system optimization** [11:12] — Every losing trade is an opportunity to feed your optimization cycle. Did you miss something (e.g., an earnings date)? Are there new ideas to test? The obstacle is the way.

- **Risk control comes first; everything else is optimization** [11:57] — You cannot invest zero money, so risk control is the foundation. Beyond that, both winning and losing trades provide data to refine your process over the long run.

## Notable quotes

- "If you get into this investing and trading world with the idea of never losing any money you're not going to make any money." [00:00]

- "The obstacle is the way — losing trades are difficult for our ego, we don't want to look at them, but all of that is an opportunity for you to get better as a trader and optimize your system." [12:17]
