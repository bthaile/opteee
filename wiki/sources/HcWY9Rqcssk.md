# Market Update: SPX Reversal, CPI Data, and Portfolio Adjustments

## Summary

Market opened soft on January 13th but reversed sharply into the close, though follow-through remains weak and the broader trend remains downward. CPI came in slightly light at 5.22% vs. 5.3% expected, with Fed rate expectations largely unchanged across near-term meetings. Portfolio adjustments made to TQQ and UPRO covered strangles, rolling in-the-money short puts to better strikes and reducing cash-secured requirements while maintaining defensive positioning.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2025-01-13)

- **Market structure remains bearish** [00:00–01:04]: SPX opened down, reversed into close but barely crossed Friday's close; developing successive lower highs and lower lows; still below key support level.
- **VIX contraction creates tactical opportunity** [01:04–02:16]: VIX opened above 20, fell below 19; historically, VIX above 20 lasts only 5–7 days; selling puts into high IV can work if IV contracts, protecting downside risk.
- **Sector breadth deteriorating** [03:09–04:21]: Materials consolidating (positive sign); Tech gapped down sharply; Staples showing sustained weakness over months; Energy and Healthcare relatively stronger; Utilities down; Discretionary downtrending.
- **Market rotation, not reversal** [04:21–05:36]: Advance/decline ratio improved today but market is still rotating over; too early to call direction; long inverse deltas (short beta-weighted deltas via long premium strategies) remain appropriate.
- **CPI and Fed expectations stable** [05:36–08:02]: CPI came in at 5.22% vs. 5.3% expected; Fed rate expectations show 97.3% probability of no change at January 29 meeting; slight edge on ease by June 18; underlying expectations shifting but near-term impact minimal.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Rolling options for basis improvement** [09:08–11:19]: Rolling in-the-money short puts to lower strikes improves basis and reduces cash-secured requirement; TQQ 83 puts rolled from 30 delta to 70 delta, improving basis by 3.6% and freeing $600 of capital; break-even tracking critical for prospective exit before expiration.
- **Covered strangle adjustments on leveraged ETFs** [11:19–13:31]: UPRO short calls taken down (102s to 95s, same size, same expiration) to increase premium without adding call-side size; short puts rolled down and out (90s/88s to 86s/85s, both 21 Feb, 50/46 delta) to near-the-money, reducing basis 4.4% and freeing $2,400 capital on 6-lot.
- **Defensive positioning in downtrend** [14:36–15:48]: Maintaining light allocation (sub-50% utilization) in leveraged positions; TQQ and UPRO already 3× levered, so notional exposure is high; staying defensive until trend reversal confirmed; no rate cuts near-term and earnings season ahead suggest more downside room.
- **Technical support levels matter** [14:36–15:48]: SPX trendline from recent highs; QQQ in clearly defined downtrend with descending channel; QQQ already below support, which is bearish for broader market given Tech's weight in indices.
