# Understanding Volume Profile

## Summary

Volume profile visualizes the density of transactions at each price level over a specified time frame, with the point of control marking the price where the most volume occurred. Price behavior around volume nodes—particularly gaps between high-volume shelves—reveals where price is likely to spend time and where support or resistance may form.

## Key takeaways

- **Point of control (POC)** [00:25]: The price level where the highest volume of transactions occurred; in the example shown, at the 160 handle.
- **Volume node gaps signal low-transaction zones** [00:44]: Large gaps between volume shelves indicate price previously did not trade much at those levels, which informs expectations for support and resistance.
- **Price behavior and volume density correlation** [00:44]: Price tends not to spend significant time in zones with sparse transaction history, but gaps do not guarantee price will "zoom through"—they simply reflect past trading patterns.
