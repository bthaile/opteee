# Vertical Spreads are More Affordable - But Are They Optimal?

## Summary

This video challenges the common assumption that vertical spreads are the default choice for traders with limited capital. While verticals do reduce upfront capital requirements, Eric argues that affordability should not drive strategy selection; instead, traders should identify the profit mechanism first, then select structures that optimally attack that mechanism, and only then apply capital constraints.

## Key takeaways

- **Profit mechanism first** [00:23] — Identify what you're trying to profit from before choosing a structure; overlay capital constraints last, not first.
- **Capital constraints are not strategy drivers** [00:00] — A small account doesn't preclude single-leg options; it simply means you need to think differently about position sizing and structure selection.
- **Vertical spreads as a fallback** [00:51] — Verticals are reasonable *if* they emerge naturally from your profit-mechanism analysis, not because they're cheaper.
- **Market indifference** [00:00] — The market doesn't care about your account size; structure selection should reflect edge and mechanics, not constraints.
