# Process vs outcome in options trading

## Summary

A trader's portfolio drawdown illustrates a critical failure mode: obsessing over individual position outcomes while neglecting process discipline. The core insight is that over a career spanning tens of thousands or millions of trades, process consistency—not outcome variance on any single trade—determines long-term profitability. Building a plan you trust and executing it through rough patches is the foundation of sustainable trading.

## Key takeaways

- **Trust your plan through volatility** [00:23] — The common saying "trade your plan, plan your trade" only works if you've done the foundational work to build a plan you actually trust; this is what allows you to stick through drawdowns.
- **Process > outcome on individual trades** [00:49] — Over a career of tens of thousands or millions of trades, fixating on the outcome of any single position while neglecting process is "missing the forest for the trees."
- **Prepare for market divergence** [00:10] — Many traders are unprepared when the market does something different from their expectations; this reinforces the need for robust contingency planning.

## Notable quotes

- "If you focus too much on the outcome on any one individual position and less on the process you are missing the forest for the trees."
