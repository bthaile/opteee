# ultimate goal for options traders

## Summary

The foundational principle for options traders is **edge**—the ability to consistently generate returns aligned with your objectives. While risk management and discipline are essential prerequisites, they are secondary to identifying and extracting a genuine market edge; without edge, disciplined risk management merely slows losses.

## Key takeaways

- **Edge is the prerequisite** [00:00–00:28]: Risk management and discipline are necessary but insufficient; they are tools to *preserve* edge, not substitutes for it.
- **The misconception trap** [00:28–00:50]: Many traders prioritize discipline or risk controls first, but these only matter in service of finding and executing a monetizable market effect.
- **The hierarchy** [00:50]: Edge → discipline → risk management. You must find the edge first, then apply discipline to follow the process, then manage risk to sustain it long-term.

## Notable quotes

> "Edge is the only thing that matters up front for an options trader."
