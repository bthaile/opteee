# Retail and Institutions Constraints Exposed

## Summary

Institutions and retail traders both operate under distinct structural constraints that shape their decision-making and execution. Institutions face mandate-driven risk limits and benchmark pressure, while retail traders contend with order routing latency via payment for order flow, informational disadvantages, and emotional volatility. Understanding these constraints is essential for realistic position sizing and strategy design.

## Key takeaways

- **Institutional constraints**: mandates, risk limits, and benchmark performance requirements govern decision cycles; large size creates liquidity friction [00:00–00:15]
- **Retail size paradox**: being very small constrains you to certain strategies, but also offers flexibility that larger players lack [00:15–00:20]
- **Order routing friction**: retail brokers typically route orders via payment for order flow, adding latency and execution slippage [00:25–00:35]
- **Informational gap**: retail traders face a persistent and unavoidable informational disadvantage versus institutions [00:35–00:40]
- **Emotional toll**: the psychological roller coaster of trading is a structural constraint many retail traders underestimate [00:40–00:45]
