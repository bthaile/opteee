# Getting Good Feedback Matters More Than Winning A Trade #optiontrading

## Summary

Accurate feedback on trade execution is more valuable than profit on any single trade. A winning trade executed for the wrong reasons creates a corrupted data point that undermines future decision-making. Correctly identifying *why* a trade succeeded or failed—whether the outcome matched your strategic expectation—is essential for building reliable edge over time.

## Key takeaways

- **Outcome vs. process alignment [00:00–00:51]**: A trade can be profitable yet strategically wrong if the price action diverges from your setup expectation. Buying calls that gain value due to unexpected market movement is a win for the wrong reasons.
- **Bad feedback from good P&L [00:23–00:51]**: Profiting without validating your thesis creates a corrupted data point. Unless you explicitly identify the mismatch, you'll carry false confidence into future trades.
- **Feedback as the real edge [00:00]**: Consistent, honest analysis of strategy performance—not individual trade wins—compounds into reliable edge.

## Notable quotes

> "Getting good feedback on what you're doing is more important than making money on any one individual trade."
