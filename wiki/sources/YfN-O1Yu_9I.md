# 0DTE #Options #stockmarket #trading

## Summary

Zero-DTE options volume has cooled from pandemic peaks, signaling that retail traders are exiting the strategy after realizing it does not reliably generate returns. However, for disciplined traders, pricing discrepancies between one-day straddles and realized moves still present edge—though that edge is narrower in current market conditions than during calmer regimes, requiring more careful trade timing.

## Key takeaways

- **Volume normalization** [00:00] — Zero-DTE options popularity peaked during COVID but is now leveling off as unsustainable traders exit the space.
- **Survivor's edge remains** [00:20] — Pricing discrepancies between one-day straddle prices and realized moves still offer edge for disciplined traders, but it is regime-dependent.
- **Regime sensitivity** [00:20] — The edge is more pronounced in mellow (low-volatility) markets; in current conditions, traders must be more selective with timing.
- **Timing discipline required** [00:39] — Success in zero-DTE requires careful entry timing and market-regime awareness rather than mechanical execution.
