# Enjoyment and trading #options #trading

## Summary

Eric argues that trading for entertainment or sensation-seeking is a path to financial ruin, and that the market will punish traders who approach it with casino-like expectations. The correct mindset is to treat trading as a business—a vehicle for earning income—and reserve enjoyment for what you do *with* the profits, not the trading process itself.

## Key takeaways

- **Separate business from pleasure** [00:00–00:39]: Trading is work; enjoyment comes from what you buy or do with the money you make, not from the trading itself. Sensation-seeking in markets is well-documented and leads to losses.
- **Casino analogy** [00:00–00:18]: A casino at least offers entertainment (shows, oxygen, drunk people); the market offers none of that—it just takes your money if you have the wrong expectations.
- **Treat it as a business, not entertainment** [00:39]: The path to profitability is to earn money through disciplined trading, then use that income for actual enjoyment (travel, purchases, financial security).

## Notable quotes

> "If you are doing this for your enjoyment just go to a casino literally a casino is at least more fun."

> "The way I look at it is I trade for business it's how I earn a living what I do with the money I make that's for enjoyment."
