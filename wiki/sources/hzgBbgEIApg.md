# Closing Short Puts

## Summary

This video addresses the mechanics and psychology of closing short put positions at a loss, arguing that taking a defined loss and moving on is often preferable to initiating a rolling campaign that can compound losses over time. The host emphasizes disciplined position management and the importance of evaluating each trade on its own merits rather than reflexively rolling underwater positions.

## Key takeaways

- **Accept defined losses on short puts** [00:00] — Buying to close a short put at a loss (e.g., sold at $10, bought back at $2 for a $100 loss) is a valid exit and often preferable to rolling.
- **Avoid reflexive rolling campaigns** [00:23] — The temptation to immediately roll a losing short put can compound losses by repeatedly opening new trades on positions that may not align with your thesis.
- **Evaluate each trade independently** [00:23] — Before rolling, carefully assess whether the underlying is behaving as expected; if not, closing and moving on preserves capital and mental clarity.

## Notable quotes

> "This is way more acceptable than people who sell puts tend to think. They more often think that they have to immediately go into this rolling campaign, which really can just compound your losses."
