# Information overload #optionstrading #trading #stockmarket

## Summary

The proliferation of information in modern markets creates a paradox: traders today have access to vastly more data than in 2007, yet this abundance becomes a liability without structure. The solution is to build a deliberate learning plan with clear progression, evaluation mechanisms, and personal accountability—mirroring the scaffolded approach of formal education but self-directed.

## Key takeaways

- **Create a structured learning plan** [00:00–00:24]: Treat your trading education like a formal curriculum with syllabus, homework, reading, quizzes, tests, and projects. Build skill sets progressively rather than consuming information randomly.
- **Embrace self-directed accountability** [00:24–00:49]: Unlike school, no one will tell you what to do or how to do it in trading. You must create your own structure and evaluate your own skill development.
- **Information overload is the modern problem** [00:00]: The shift from 2007 (scarce information) to today (ubiquitous information) requires intentional filtering and prioritization to avoid paralysis.

## Notable quotes

> "When I started trading in 2007 things were a lot different there wasn't information everywhere now today there's a whole other problem there's information everywhere it's actually information overload."

> "You have to figure it out for yourself so embrace that ambiguity and create your own structure."
