# Risk Management KILLS Retail Traders

## Summary

Institutional traders have dedicated risk-management roles and systematic processes that retail traders typically lack, leading to execution errors, poor position sizing, and cognitive biases. The core challenge for retail traders is finding the balance between under-managing risk (which prevents profitability) and over-managing risk (which creates excessive caution and missed opportunities).

## Key takeaways

- **Institutional advantage in risk infrastructure** [00:00] — Professional firms employ dedicated risk-management personnel whose sole responsibility is mitigation across all positions; retail traders rarely have this structural support.
- **Common retail execution errors** [00:28] — Mistakes include buying instead of selling, selecting wrong expirations, and missing earnings dates due to insufficient planning and oversight.
- **Position-sizing blind spot** [00:28] — Retail traders struggle to find the "sweet spot" for diversification: either too few positions to spread risk, or too many positions that create false diversification without understanding the underlying exposure.
- **Cognitive bias burden** [00:28] — New traders carry significant cognitive biases that compound execution errors and poor decision-making.
- **The risk-aversion paradox** [00:48] — Excessive risk-aversion prevents profitability; the solution lies in balance rather than extremes.
