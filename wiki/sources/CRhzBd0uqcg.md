# Three Extremely Useful Technical Analysis Indicators For Beginners

## Summary

Eric introduces three foundational technical analysis indicators suitable for beginner traders: moving averages, volume profile, and market breadth (net highs/lows). The focus is on practical application—slope, organization, price positioning, and multi-timeframe context—rather than exhaustive indicator stacking. These tools help traders avoid analysis paralysis while gaining a pulse on market structure and broad market health.

## Key takeaways

- **Moving averages: read slope, organization, and price position** [00:21] — examine how price sits relative to the averages, their width, and how they're organized; slope tells you momentum direction.
- **Volume profile reveals structure** [00:21] — look for nodes (price levels with high volume), shelves, and gaps; these are support/resistance zones that repeat across sessions.
- **Multi-timeframe analysis is essential** [00:21] — don't rely on a single timeframe; cross-reference signals across different periods to confirm bias.
- **Market breadth (net highs/lows) is a portfolio-level tool** [00:42] — this breadth indicator is not security-specific; it gives you a single-glance pulse on the broad market health, independent of any one stock.
- **Avoid indicator overload** [00:00] — even though a chart can display many tools, beginners should start with these three and build discipline before adding complexity.
