# 4 Steps to Better Options Trades | Outlier Trade Process

## Summary

Eric outlines a systematic four-step process for placing options trades that prioritizes process alignment over outcome luck. The framework emphasizes stacking multiple technical confirmations, articulating clear profit mechanisms, building trades that match portfolio needs, and conducting after-action reviews—ensuring that trades follow a tested plan rather than chasing individual wins.

## Key takeaways

- **Stacking strength** [01:00–02:00]: Evaluate multiple timeframes and technical factors together (6-month, 3-month, 30-day) rather than making decisions on a single element. Compare candidates (e.g., LININ vs. XLC) to ensure alignment across all aggregation periods.

- **Process over outcome** [02:01–02:55]: The perfect trade is not one that makes money by luck; it's one that follows your tested system. Most traders break their rules trying to salvage individual trades. Build a plan you trust, then focus on executing it consistently rather than obsessing over any single trade's P&L.

- **Look for "no" signals** [03:56–04:51]: Flip the screening logic: instead of finding reasons to say yes, identify reasons to say no. If too many technical or fundamental factors misalign, it's a no-trade. This eliminates confirmation bias.

- **The Big Four trade process** [04:51–08:41]:
  1. **Portfolio fit** [04:51]: Does the trade address a specific portfolio need (long delta, short delta, theta, gamma) or is it opportunistic?
  2. **Profit mechanism & signals** [05:53–06:45]: Clearly articulate how the trade makes money and what technical signals confirm it. Example: RDFN shows downward slope on 30-day but remains in broader uptrend—objective "no" across timeframes.
  3. **Build the trade** [07:44]: Match the strategy to the thesis (e.g., bearish direction → short calls or long puts; if expecting large move → prefer long puts for uncapped upside).
  4. **After-action review** [08:41]: Track decisions, outcomes, and plan adherence to refine the system over time.

- **Timeframe alignment** [06:45]: Define your trading window first, then use one-up-one-down aggregation (e.g., if trading 5-day thesis, use 1-day and 5-minute charts; if trading 30-day, use 3-month and 30-day).

## Notable quotes

> "The perfect options trade is not about whether or not the trade itself makes money… a good trade is not about luck, it's about precision, it's about timing."

> "One of the most common reasons for somebody to break a plan is that they don't trust the plan. Why would you follow a plan that you don't trust?"

> "I want to look for things that as I go through my screening criteria there's no logical reason for me to not place the trade."
