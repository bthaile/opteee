# SpaceX IPO - O/TOC

## Summary

Eric analyzes the SpaceX IPO scheduled for June 12, 2026, contextualizing it within a challenging market environment marked by weak breadth signals and elevated volatility clustering. The offering represents the largest IPO in history by 2.5× and carries a richly valued multiple (~94× trailing sales), but the compute contracts with Anthropic and Google materially reshape the valuation narrative. Eric emphasizes the tension between SpaceX's non-profit mandate (making humanity multi-planetary) and the need for massive capital deployment, and cautions against chasing the open while acknowledging significant long-term upside potential.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2026-06-11)

- **Weak breadth backdrop** [05:58–07:31]: Market showing conflicting signals—vol clustering above 20 handle, S&P constituents above 50-period MA struggling. IPO arriving in unfavorable tailwind environment, not necessarily a massive headwind but "starting at a bit of a tough spot."
- **IPO performance context** [08:21–09:40]: Tech sector historically performs well on IPO, but sample sizes are small. Current tech performance over the past year "not bad, but not as profound" as prior bull runs.
- **Largest IPO in history** [16:11–16:39]: SpaceX offering is 2.5× larger than previous record (Tesla, Meta). Books close same day; pricing finalizes; trading begins June 12.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Lockup asymmetry and sell pressure** [11:24–12:37]: Significant cohort of pre-IPO holders has no lockup period and will likely sell on open to realize generational wealth. Expect material sell pressure from day one, though not necessarily a complete dump.
- **Three-pillar business model** [18:07–20:24]: Space (government contracting, strong moat but dependent on non-prioritized ecosystem historically; now amplified by geopolitical shift to drone warfare); Connectivity (Starlink with massive upside, already deployed in remote areas with strong performance); AI (Grok competing but running deep losses—$3.2B revenue vs. $6.35B operating loss in 2025).
- **Compute contracts reshape valuation** [23:08–28:56]: Anthropic contract ~$1.25B/month for Colossus 1 (220k Nvidia processors); Google contract $920M/month (Oct–Jun) for 110k GPUs. These contracts disclosed 6–22 days before pricing and materially reduce the headline 94× trailing-sales multiple when included in forward analysis. Contracts have termination windows but remain critical to future value.
- **Musk control premium** [29:22–30:40]: Musk retains significant voting control by design. His track record of success across six major businesses (Tesla, X, Neuralink, etc.) is a material tailwind; not Dow-eligible but expected for immediate Nasdaq inclusion, which shifts demand dynamics.
- **Volatility and first-week dynamics** [33:51–35:43]: Expect extreme volatility on open due to competing forces (sellers locking in gains vs. buyers chasing upside). Halts possible. Over a week, slightly better odds of landing below the day's high than above. Nasdaq inclusion is ~52/50 conviction (essentially a coin flip). Most of a stock's annual return typically comes after the first week, not during IPO day.

## Notable quotes

> "The mandate here is to make humans multi-planetary, right? Like that's the mission for SpaceX. That itself isn't increased market cap, that isn't increased revenue, that isn't dominate sales. It's kind of a completely different mandate." [13:40–14:20]

And then you have a lot of people that are going to be clambering to get in order to potentially get something really cheap that ends up expanding." [33:51–34:13]
