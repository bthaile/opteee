# The Options Chain Doesn't Lie: SPCX Edition

## Summary

Eric analyzes the newly-opened SpaceX options chain one day after launch, examining volume distribution, open interest concentration, and volatility structure. The front-month term shows elevated implied volatility (~138%) with significant backwardation, while positioning reveals bullish speculative trades concentrated in July 235 calls and deep out-of-the-money lottery wings, with institutional intraday trading dominating the flow.

## Key takeaways

- **Volume vs. open interest split** [00:28–00:52]: Yesterday's options launch saw tremendous call-heavy volume, but only ~50% of contracts stuck as open interest, indicating heavy intraday institutional trading.
- **Front-month volatility richness** [02:02–02:35]: Front-term implied volatility dropped from ~170% to ~138% in one day, but remains significantly elevated relative to back months, creating a backwardated term structure ideal for calendar spreads.
- **Bullish positioning in July 235 calls** [03:52–04:27]: Largest open interest concentration at 17 July 235 calls (~36,000 contracts), with ~$10.2M in premium, indicating speculative directional bullish plays.
- **Call-heavy skew with light ITM volume** [05:22–06:22]: Calls built out at expected moderate out-of-the-money deltas and deep 300+ strikes (lottery wings), but surprisingly light in-the-money call volume; puts show minimal speculative downside beyond 200 strike.
- **Institutional intraday flow dominance** [09:14]: Only ~44% of call volume was retained intraday; the scale of trading rules out retail-only participation, confirming institutional market-making and hedging activity.
- **Volatility surface remains extremely hot** [09:38–10:03]: At-the-money implied volatility remains elevated; tail volatility is "unbelievably high," with speculative positioning visible in far out-of-the-money wings (300+ calls, 325 puts).

## Regime / context

SpaceX options chain opened 17 June 2026, one day before this analysis. The newly-launched options exhibit classic post-IPO characteristics: elevated front-month volatility, institutional participation, and speculative positioning in out-of-the-money strikes. The backwardated term structure and rich front-month premium create favorable conditions for calendar and volatility-mean-reversion trades.
