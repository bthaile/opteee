# GameStop Naked Shorting & Covered Strangle Management

## Summary

Eric walks through GameStop's current technical setup, business pivot thesis, and detailed management of a live covered strangle position. The stream includes a deep dive into dark pools and market structure via Dave Lauer's research, analysis of max pain mechanics, and practical position-sizing examples using both GME and TQQQ to illustrate how to adjust basis and manage leverage in volatile securities.

## Key takeaways

- **GameStop business thesis**: Ryan Cohen appears to be optimizing the existing brick-and-mortar business rather than pivoting to a holding company; the slow, targeted store closures and Canadian divestment suggest incremental restructuring, not radical transformation [09:20–13:15]
- **Covered strangle mechanics**: By rolling short puts down two strikes (25→23), Eric reduced cash-secured put requirement by $1,000 (~4% of portfolio), allowing flexible basis adjustment as the stock declines [37:11–43:06]
- **Max pain is dynamic**: Max pain moves as options are traded; it is not a fixed target and should be monitored throughout the week, not treated as a static price level [33:07–34:27]
- **Dark pools are not nefarious**: All trades—whether on-exchange or off-exchange—must be reported to the consolidated tape within 10 seconds; dark pools do not hide short positions or allow unreported trading [56:43–01:09:12]
- **Leverage ETF risk**: TQQQ has experienced 270%+ drawdowns over five years; a 30% decline is moderate; position sizing (62% utilization) is the primary risk control, not protective puts [01:25:12–01:35:07]
- **Rolling puts for basis**: When rolling short puts in leveraged positions, focus on decreasing strike to improve cost basis on assignment; this reduces total capital required and allows more units to be purchased at lower prices [01:39:16–01:42:18]
- **Consistency in analysis**: Maintaining consistent study of a security (like GameStop) over time is essential to pick up subtle signals that would be missed in an ebb-and-flow approach [08:05–09:20]
