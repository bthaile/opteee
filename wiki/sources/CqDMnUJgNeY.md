# My simple rules for trading breakouts #trading #daytrading #shorts

## Summary

Eric outlines a foundational breakout-trading framework using a gap-based setup with defined risk and profit targets. The approach establishes a base case using equity shares, with a stop-loss below the gap low and a near-term profit target at a predetermined resistance level, demonstrating position sizing tied to a fixed risk amount (~$6,000).

## Key takeaways

- **Gap-based entry logic** [00:00–00:26]: Identify a gap; set the stop-loss below the gap low (e.g., 66.5) to define maximum loss per share.
- **Fixed-risk position sizing** [00:00]: Decide on total risk capital upfront (e.g., $6,000); calculate share quantity from stop distance and total risk.
- **Near-term profit target** [00:26]: Place initial profit target at a moderately aggressive level above the gap (e.g., 75.5 handle).
- **Scenario analysis** [00:51]: Model downside loss (~$836 on shares) and upside gain (~$640) to validate risk/reward before entry.
