# Tender Offer Explained - What is the impact to investors?

## Summary

A tender offer is a conditional solicitation by an acquirer to purchase a specified percentage of outstanding shares at a fixed price from current shareholders. The transaction only completes if the acquirer receives commitments to sell sufficient shares to meet a predetermined threshold (e.g., 80% of outstanding shares). This mechanism is commonly used in hostile takeovers or when a firm seeks to take a public company private and gain control over decision-making.

## Key takeaways

- **Definition and mechanics** [00:24]: A tender offer is a conditional order to buy a certain number of shares at a specified price, designed to establish a large stake and influence within a company.
- **Threshold requirement** [01:45]: The acquirer sets a minimum percentage of shares they need to acquire (e.g., 80%). The transaction only proceeds if enough shareholders commit to sell at the offered price to meet this threshold.
- **Shareholder choice** [00:51]: Current shareholders receive the offer through their brokers and can choose to accept the price and sell, or reject it and retain their shares.
- **No transaction if threshold unmet** [02:11]: If the acquirer fails to secure commitments for the required percentage of shares, the tender offer is cancelled and no transaction occurs.
- **Purpose and control** [02:34]: Tender offers are used to solicit share sales so that an acquiring firm can take a large stake and gain influence or control over the target company's decision-making.
