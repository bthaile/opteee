# Charm AKA Delta Decay #optionstrading

## Summary

This video explains charm (delta decay), the rate at which delta changes as expiration approaches. The key insight is that in-the-money options become *more* likely to stay in the money as expiration nears, causing delta to increase. This mechanic influences position construction, particularly in ratio call diagonals where time-decay benefits are prioritized.

## Key takeaways

- **Delta expansion in ITM options** [00:00–00:24]: As expiration approaches, in-the-money options gain delta (become more likely to stay ITM). A 57-delta call at 680 strike becomes 63-delta at the same strike as expiration nears.
- **Charm's role in strategy selection** [00:24–00:42]: When holding positions for duration (e.g., ratio call diagonals), prefer at-the-money or slightly in-the-money strikes to preserve delta and avoid losing profitability from delta decay if the underlying moves favorably.
- **Time-decay vs. delta trade-off** [00:42]: Longer-dated positions benefit from theta decay, but delta decay can erode gains if the underlying moves; strike selection balances these competing forces.

## Notable quotes

> "If I'm going to hold it for some duration, I don't want to lose too many deltas in case I do get my move and then I lose on profitability."
