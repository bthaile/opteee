# Portfolio Adjustments INTC, F, MOS, KR - Small Stacks

## Summary

Eric reviews portfolio adjustments across four positions in the Small Stacks account: closing Ford entirely after hitting profit targets, trimming Kroger short calls while preserving upside, initiating an Intel diagonal call spread, and establishing a Mosaic short put ahead of earnings. The account sits at ~70% utilization with ~15.5% total return, demonstrating position-sizing discipline and tactical rebalancing around technical levels.

## Key takeaways

- **Ford closed for 13.7% ROIC** [00:29–01:43]: Exited the entire position after the stock made its expected move from mid-$7s to higher levels; took down short calls at profit and did not re-establish exposure.
- **Kroger: short calls trimmed, upside preserved** [01:43–03:32]: Closed short calls at profit (realized $288 on $1,357 outlay) but declined to re-sell at lower strikes ($35.50–$36) to avoid capping upside; watching for rebound to the 200-day MA and –2 standard deviations before adding ratio call structure.
- **Intel diagonal initiated** [04:04–05:49]: Bought June 2021 $37.50 calls for $10.35, sold December $47 calls for $1.65 (48 delta), netting ~16% ROIC; capped position size at $2,000 (~33% of account) to maintain portfolio discipline; willing to manage short call assignment if it falls ITM.
- **Mosaic short put for earnings vol** [06:34–07:18]: Sold $17 strike put, collected $0.90 premium; using earnings volatility expansion to boost return without treating it as a pure earnings play; comfortable owning the stock at $17 if assigned (would use $1,700 cash).
- **Account allocation and next moves** [07:40–08:43]: Small Stacks at ~70% utilization; next expected trade is adding short calls to Kroger on a rebound; Intel and Mosaic are "set and forget" until closer to expiration or earnings; account is proof-of-concept for strategy mechanics rather than wealth-building vehicle.

## Notable quotes

> "I don't want to prematurely close off the potential profit" — on leaving Kroger uncapped after trimming short calls.

> "This is working through mechanics and strategy and really proof of concept that we can implement a successful strategy" — on the Small Stacks account's educational purpose.
