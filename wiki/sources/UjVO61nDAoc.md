# GME Options Update - Broken Support

## Summary

GameStop fell ~5% to ~$22.50 after-hours, breaking below near-term support at $23. Volume analysis shows 96% seller-controlled activity with accelerating selling pressure. Put open interest has risen to 373k against 650k calls, and the options chain shows heavy concentration in out-of-the-money calls (125 strike) and near-term August expirations, with limited bullish conviction.

## Key takeaways

- **Support break and downside targets** [00:29–00:50]: GME broke below $23 support; next technical target ~$21 (volume node); further downside risk to ~$18.20 if buyers don't step in meaningfully.
- **Seller-dominated volume** [01:10–01:31]: 96% of today's volume was seller-initiated; volume graph shows acceleration in selling pressure (drifting upward), indicating more participants joining the sell-off.
- **Put OI rising; call concentration in OTM strikes** [01:55–02:42]: Put OI now 373k vs. 650k calls (approaching 1:2 ratio); 125-strike calls heavily built out; 16 August expiration shows more activity than near-term (112 calls vs. 49k puts on 2 Aug; 37k calls vs. 16k puts on 9 Aug).
- **Large premium sale on Aug 23 calls** [03:31–04:00]: 2,847 contracts of 2 Aug 23 calls sold (>$200k premium collected); indicates seller looking to cap upside and collect income.
- **Limited bullish options activity** [04:24–04:46]: Mostly small, out-of-the-money call purchases (33-strike nickels, 28-strike pennies); 392 contracts of 25-strike calls noted as worth monitoring.
- **Covered-call hedge offsetting downside** [05:09–05:30]: Short calls against long shares used to manage downside risk on days like this without capping full upside potential.
