# Market Update: Nike Earnings, Middle East Impact, Nvidia Trend Reversal

## Summary
Quick market recap covering three key drivers: Nike's earnings miss on guidance (down ~5% after-hours) despite EPS beat, geopolitical tension in the Middle East (Israel–Lebanon escalation, Iran missile strike) pressuring crude oil and gold, and a potential failed trend reversal in Nvidia showing lower highs. Mixed economic data (job openings up, manufacturing soft) keeps near-term volatility elevated.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2024-10-01)

- **Nike earnings trade setup** [00:00–03:22]: Beat on EPS (70¢ vs. 51¢ expected) but guidance disappointed; sales contracting year-over-year. Stock down ~5% after-hours from ~89 to ~84. Expect gap-down open with potential continuation lower; short-term trade opportunity (day-trade horizon). Leadership transition (Elliott Hill returning) signals turnaround narrative, but numbers don't support near-term recovery.

- **Geopolitical pressure** [04:18–06:41]: Israel ground assault into Lebanon + Iran ballistic missile strike driving risk-off. Crude oil spiking; gold showing renewed strength. Port strike (Maine to Texas, first since 1977) adds supply-chain uncertainty; workers sought 77% raise, granted ~50% over six years. Biden declined Taft-Hartley 80-day cooling-off period. Early-stage impact; Middle East risk is higher near-term concern.

- **Nasdaq/tech weakness** [07:36–08:54]: Nasdaq broke new high but now falling back into range. Nvidia down 3.66% intraday, further weakness after-hours (to ~116). Pattern shows lower highs, potential failed reversal—watch for breakout direction. Technology sector struggling to sustain recovery; near-term high not yet in place.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Earnings guidance vs. EPS beat**: Market often reprices on forward guidance miss despite current-quarter EPS beat; sales contraction is a red flag even with profitability.
- **Geopolitical vol**: Crude oil and safe-haven assets (gold) respond immediately to Middle East escalation; labor strikes add secondary supply-chain risk.
- **Failed trend reversal**: Lower highs after a brief recovery attempt signal potential breakdown; watch for range breakout (either direction) as confirmation.
