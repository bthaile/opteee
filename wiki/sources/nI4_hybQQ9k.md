# Failure rate of #options #trading #optionstrading

## Summary

The overwhelming majority of traders fail because they misunderstand the fundamental nature of trading as a business. Most enter with expectations of fast, easy money; those who survive the initial reality shock learn to treat trading as a serious profession with both rewarding and difficult elements. Understanding this challenge upfront significantly improves the probability of long-term success.

## Key takeaways

- **Expectation mismatch is the primary failure driver** [00:00–00:23]: Most traders begin with the belief that trading will be fast and easy money, then quit when reality contradicts this assumption.
- **Trading is a business, not a get-rich-quick scheme** [00:23–00:43]: Successful traders reframe trading as a profession with genuine challenges and rewards, similar to any other business.
- **Research and education reduce failure rate** [00:43]: Free resources like SSRN.com allow traders to study the landscape and understand trading mechanics before risking capital.
- **Survivor bias in trading**: The speaker's 18 years of experience reflects the minority who made the psychological transition; most do not.

## Notable quotes

> "The overwhelming majority of traders fail primarily for literally one reason and it's because they don't understand the reality of the game they're getting involved in."
