# You're Choosing Technical Analysis Indicators WRONG

## Summary

Technical indicators should not be chosen in isolation or based on popularity; instead, they must be selected relative to the specific [[profit-mechanism]] you're attempting to capture. The [[outlier-strategy-process]] framework prioritizes identifying the profit mechanism first, then overlaying signals that accurately identify and measure it—not the reverse.

## Key takeaways

- **Profit mechanism comes first** [00:00] — Analyze the profit mechanism you want to trade before selecting any indicators; signals are secondary to this analysis.
- **Indicators should serve a purpose** [00:27] — Choose signals and indicators that help you identify, measure, and extract profit from the specific profit mechanism, not generic tools like MACD or RSI used in isolation.
- **Avoid redundant indicator stacking** [00:00] — Many popular indicators (MACD, RSI, etc.) effectively measure the same thing; using multiple redundant signals adds noise rather than clarity.
- **Signal overlay is step two** [00:00] — In the outlier strategy process framework, signal overlay comes after profit mechanism analysis, not before.

## Notable quotes
