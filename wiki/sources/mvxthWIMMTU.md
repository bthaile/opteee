# Melt Up 2024 to Global Market Crash and $500 Oil - David Hunter | TOP

## Summary

David Hunter argues the market is in a sustained melt-up phase with the S&P 500 up ~68% and NASDAQ ~84% from October 2022 lows, targeting 7,500 by year-end or Q1 2025. He expects a parabolic final stage before a severe global deflationary bust in 2025, followed by a commodity-driven inflationary cycle where oil reaches $500, gold $20,000, and silver $500 by the early 2030s. Near-term catalysts include Fed easing, falling interest rates (targeting 2.5% on the 10-year), a weaker dollar, and institutional capitulation to the bull narrative.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2024-10-18)

- **S&P 500 melt-up thesis**: Up ~68% from October 2022 lows; NASDAQ up ~84%. Target raised to 7,500 from 7,000, achievable within 2.5 months if parabolic acceleration occurs [02:15].
- **10-year yield reversal**: Backed up from 3.60% to ~4.09% in recent weeks; Hunter views this as a correction within a broader bull market in bonds, not the end of the bond rally [07:57].
- **Fed 50 bps cut rationale**: Not panic-driven; reflects policy being too restrictive relative to inflation and economic data. Fed behind the curve, not ahead [10:09].
- **November FOMC expectations**: 25–50 bps cut likely; Hunter indifferent between the two, bullish either way. Bond market, not Fed decisions, dictates rate direction [15:46].
- **China stimulus rally**: Primarily short-covering and vacuum-driven, not fundamental. Expect orderly follow-up rally but not sustained bull market [22:57].
- **Crude oil bear case**: Target 60 near-term, 50s possible by year-end. Long-term: $30 in bust (2025), then $500 by early 2030s as demand surges post-bust money printing [28:44].
- **Gold targets**: Pre-bust target raised to $3,400 (achievable Q1 2025). Post-bust pullback to $2,100–$2,500 possible (35–40% decline), then $20,000 by early 2030s [37:02].
- **Silver outperformance**: Expect mid-$40s within months, then $75 pre-bust. Gold/silver ratio should compress from ~90 to ~40 in bull markets [41:44].
- **Dollar weakness ahead**: Counter-trend rally near completion. Expect decline from ~103 to mid-90s (3–5 months), ultimately to 82, then rally to 120 in bust [40:44].

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Bond market efficiency**: Collective wisdom of all bond investors more reliable than Fed forward guidance. Rates driven by supply, demand, inflation, jobs, and economic data—not Fed alone [19:12].
- **Melt-up warning signs**: Institutional targets rising (now 6,000+), narrative shifting to "beginning of cycle" rather than "end of bull market," all-in positioning, Goldilocks narrative (earnings up, rates down, inflation controlled) [53:49].
- **Bust transition signals**: Weaker economic data (ISM, retail sales, housing) initially received bullishly (more Fed cuts), then bearishly as deterioration accelerates. Housing inventory inflection likely early 2025 [57:18].
- **Bust-era asset allocation**: Treasuries and dollar only appreciating assets; everything else (equities, commodities, credit) declines 35–80%. Post-bust commodity cycle (2025–2030s) sees 25%+ inflation, commodities outpace equities [51:31].
- **Secular context**: 42-year secular bull market ending; 2-year cyclical bull within it. Valuations very high; parabolic moves cover ground in short time, not indicative of long-term sustainability [06:50].
