# I'm Short SPCX — Here's How Every Options Structure Reacts

## Summary

Eric walks through a directional short position in SpaceX (entered ~$169 on 22 June post-IPO) and compares how different options structures—synthetic short, 30/45/70 DTE puts at various deltas—perform against the same directional move. The trade illustrates the interplay of delta, theta, and vega across term structures in a high-volatility (~90% IV), newly-listed security with no historical volatility benchmarks.

## Key takeaways

- **Entry thesis & scaling** [00:57–01:42]: Entered short on 22 June after observing post-IPO downside breadth; scaled in to ~$169 average as velocity expanded below the open; stop placed above the day's high (support/resistance).

- **SpaceX volatility context** [02:34–03:02]: Implied vol ~90%, which is "unbelievably huge," but no historical lookback windows exist (less than one month post-IPO), so relative richness/cheapness cannot be assessed via IV percentile or IV rank.

- **Synthetic short vs. outright short** [07:40–08:05]: Synthetic short (short call + long put) flattens Greeks beyond delta; outright short is pure delta. Synthetic chosen to isolate directional exposure and avoid unwanted vega/theta drag from novel security.

- **Theta drag on near-DTE options** [08:33–09:01]: Shorter-DTE options (45 DTE) suffer more theta decay per day than longer-DTE (70 DTE), though longer-dated options cost more upfront. Both positive and negative vega impacts observed; vol contraction over trade life hurt short-premium positions.

- **Vega headwind from vol term structure** [09:23–10:18]: Entry vol ~87%; 45 DTE and 70 DTE terms moved differently. 70 DTE remained elevated relative to 45 DTE, creating asymmetric vega drag on the nearer term.

- **Normalized sizing & performance ranking** [10:45–11:55]: When scaled to equal dollar risk, 70-delta options outperformed 30-delta options despite smaller raw P&L, because 30-delta options require an 11%+ move to overcome theta/vega drag and activate convexity. Synthetic short and outright short tracked closely.

- **Max drawdown vs. peak P&L trade-off** [06:26]: 70 DTE 70-delta option showed $200 smaller max drawdown than synthetic short but $650 lower peak P&L—illustrating the ongoing tug-of-war between directional payoff and Greek decay.

- **Risk management & trailing stops** [12:46–13:29]: Partial stop at ~$158 (above prior high); hard stop below that. As downside moves materialize, new stop trails down; soft stop if reversal signals appear.

- **Position sizing philosophy** [07:15]: Trade sized at ~1% of portfolio—"really speculative, really high probability of losing, so really conservative sizing."
