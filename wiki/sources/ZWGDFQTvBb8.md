# Market Update: Google Unusual Options Activity, SoFi Setup, FOMC Expectations

## Summary

Market closes with large caps and tech (QQQ) near all-time highs while small caps (IWM) roll over despite near-certain FOMC rate-cut pricing (98.6% probability). Google shows massive unusual options activity—43,400 contracts of 190 puts opened in 13-day expiration, suggesting tactical fading of a 5.5% intraday rally. SoFi consolidates above key support, setting up potential breakout entry.

## Key takeaways

- **Market structure:** SPY at all-time highs (~6074), QQQ cracked 3-month highs (~530), IWM bucking trend lower. VIX sub-14, bonds rolling over, BTC above 100k. [00:00–01:04]
- **Sector divergence:** Energy strong, financials rolled over, tech/discretionary crushing. Discretionary decoupled from staples—unusual correlation breakdown. [01:04–02:14]
- **FOMC pricing dislocation:** Rate-cut probability jumped from ~65% one month ago to 98.6% for Dec 18 meeting. Messaging shift from Fed suggests slower, pragmatic approach; market now pricing quarter-point cut as baseline. [02:14–03:29]
- **Small-cap paradox:** IWM weakness despite rate-cut certainty signals "more going on underneath the surface"—suggests rate cut already priced in, other headwinds emerging. [03:29]
- **Economic calendar:** CPI came in light (315.49 vs. 315.34 consensus), moving in correct direction. PPI tomorrow—major release. Inflation holding steady. [04:38]
- **Google unusual activity:** 5.5% intraday move; 190 puts (13-day, OTM) saw 43,400 contracts opened vs. 137 prior OI—massive expansion. Traders fading the rally; 187.5 puts also heavy (22,407 contracts). Suggests tactical mean-reversion play, not fundamental bearishness. [05:53–07:09]
- **SoFi setup:** Exited earlier due to sideways rotation; waiting for close above 160.3 to confirm consolidation hold and base-building. Volume heavily bid today. Momentum/breakout entry strategy—depends on price action, not prediction. [08:31–09:32]
