# IWM Covered Strangle

## Summary

Eric demonstrates a basis-management approach to the [[covered-strangle]] strategy on [[iwm]], emphasizing careful cost-basis manipulation during downturns. The core thesis relies on the historical pattern that U.S. broad-market indices eventually make new highs, allowing traders to scale in and reduce average cost during drawdowns while maintaining premium income.

## Key takeaways

- **Basis manipulation during downturns** [00:00–00:26]: Rather than taking losses immediately, carefully scale positions to lower your average cost when the market is weak. This approach was profitable during the COVID recovery period.
- **Long-term index resilience** [00:26]: [[iwm]] and U.S. broad-market indices have a historical pattern of eventually making new highs; Japan is a notable exception. This regime assumption underpins the strategy's risk tolerance.
- **Psychological discipline** [00:00]: Holding losing positions is uncomfortable, but the process requires trusting the long-term thesis and avoiding panic exits.
