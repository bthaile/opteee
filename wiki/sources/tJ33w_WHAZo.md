# $10K SPY vs Tesla - Volatility Risk Nightmare

## Summary

Equal dollar allocation across assets with different volatility profiles creates asymmetric risk exposure that compounds over time. This breakdown demonstrates why position sizing must account for implied volatility and realized volatility, not just notional capital, whether trading equities, options, or futures.

## Key takeaways

- **Equal dollar ≠ equal risk**: A $10K allocation to SPY (30% IV) and $10K to Tesla (50% IV) have identical notional value at risk but vastly different actual volatility exposure [00:22–00:46]
- **Volatility context matters**: Position sizing must incorporate how much an asset moves; ignoring this compounds portfolio risk over time [00:00–00:22]
- **Cross-asset framework**: The principle applies uniformly across equities, options, and futures [00:22]
