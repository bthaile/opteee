# GameStop -10% Drop Analysis & Covered Strangle Adjustments

## Summary

GameStop experienced a rare ~10% single-day drop on January 15, 2025, with no identifiable news catalyst—a move that occurs roughly 1% of trading days historically. The analysis examines the unusual price action, elevated front-month volatility (136%), and gamma/dealer positioning, then walks through tactical adjustments to a covered strangle position designed to monetize the volatility spike while maintaining exposure.

## Key takeaways

- **Historical rarity of the move** [00:00–01:17]: 10%+ down days occur in ~1% of GameStop's trading history (75 instances since inception); today's move on no news is "exceedingly suspect."
- **Sector divergence** [01:17–02:56]: Retail ETF (XRT) down only 0.86%; small caps up; AMC showed sympathetic four-down-day pattern but different lead-in, suggesting GameStop's move is not sector-driven.
- **Technical breakdown** [02:56–04:18]: Stock punched through 10-day, 20-day, and 50-day moving averages; 200-day MA still positively sloped; no clear technical support until much lower levels.
- **Front-month volatility spike** [04:18–05:54]: Front expiration (this week) IV at 136%; 136k calls vs. 153k puts expiring Friday; over half of total call open interest in Friday expiration—"unequivocally a recipe for volatility."
- **Volume and supply/demand anomaly** [05:54–07:21]: Volume 11.77M (above 5-day avg of 8.8M, 20-day avg of 10.6M); move appears supply-driven but lacks normal news catalyst; GameStop "does not follow standard behavior of supply and demand."
- **Options flow analysis** [07:21–08:29]: Massive net change in OI in front expiration; no corresponding put buildup; no evidence of pre-positioned derivatives suggesting foreknowledge.
- **Gamma and dealer exposure** [08:29–09:46]: Heavy gamma concentration at 30s; dealers likely net long gamma; light gamma near current price (27–28), so dealer hedging not amplifying downside.
- **Position adjustments—equity side** [10:56–12:09]: Unwound 100 shares (from 300 to 200) at ~24 basis to reduce utilization and wait for consolidation; set alerts to re-enter on pullback or buy outright on strength; strategy is to "monetize movements" and compound position without adding capital.
- **Covered strangle roll** [12:09–13:15]: Closed 32 and 28 strike puts; opened new 26 puts (21 Feb); closed calls for 19¢; opened new 35 calls (21 Feb); maintains 1:2 call-to-put ratio; still well above cost basis and locking in profit if assigned.
- **Near-term outlook** [14:08–15:19]: Stock likely to either consolidate in current volume node or accelerate; second-largest volume node is current level; support below is sparse until much lower; after-hours tick up (+10¢) shows minor strength; expect binary outcome (consolidation or acceleration) without news catalyst.
