# 3 Ways to Stop LOSING Money Trading Options

## Summary

This educational video identifies three fundamental mistakes that cause retail options traders to lose money: chasing lottery-like asymmetric payouts without positive expectancy, misunderstanding the Greeks as causal forces rather than measurement tools, and obsessing over win rate instead of expected value. The host walks through each pitfall and provides concrete remedies grounded in proper strategy construction and risk awareness.

## Key takeaways

- **YOLO/lottery trading** [00:40–01:54]: Traders seeking low-probability, high-payout trades without building strategies with positive expectancy. Solution: scale into strategies with proven positive expectancy rather than throwing money at unvetted ideas.

- **Misunderstanding the Greeks** [02:12–05:12]: The Greeks (delta, gamma, theta, vega, rho) measure outcomes; they don't cause them. Theta decay isn't "killing" you—it's a symptom of not understanding your position's risk profile. Solution: map how each Greek impacts your specific strategy, understand what drives each Greek, and identify where the risk actually lives in your trade structure.

- **Chasing 99%+ win rates** [05:33–06:23]: High win rates correlate with tiny average wins (disposition effect). Sophisticated traders optimize for positive expectancy, not win rate. Solution: focus on expected value and accept that profitable strategies often have moderate win rates with larger average winners.

- **Copying institutional strategies as retail** [06:40–08:00]: Retail traders face structural disadvantages (bid-ask spread, slippage, commissions, no block-trade access) that institutions don't. Emulate institutional *discipline* (clear risk profiles, backtesting, strategy documentation), not their exact tactics.

## Notable quotes

> "The Greeks measure stuff they don't do anything. When people say 'theta is killing me,' theta is just a measure of time decay."

> "A sophisticated options trader is focused on positive expectancy, not win rate."
