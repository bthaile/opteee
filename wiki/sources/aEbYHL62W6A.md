# Understand The Whole Market with ONE Technical Indicator

## Summary

This video introduces a market-breadth indicator that tracks net highs and lows across the NYSE and NASDAQ separately, revealing whether the broader market is advancing or unwinding. By comparing gross highs/lows against net figures, traders can distinguish between genuine market surges and early signs of deterioration across the index.

## Key takeaways

- **Dual-exchange breadth histogram** [00:00]: The indicator plots NYSE (green) and NASDAQ (purple) net highs vs. lows, showing whether more companies are making new highs or lows.
- **Net vs. gross distinction** [00:26]: Dashed lines show gross highs and lows; solid lines show the net figure. This separation reveals whether the market is truly advancing or beginning to unwind.
- **Interpretation framework** [00:50]: A positive net (above zero) indicates more highs than lows; negative indicates unwinding. The divergence between gross and net signals whether momentum is slowing or reversing.
