# Stock Market Outlook - DJT, CRWD, GME

## Summary

Weekly market outlook covering three key events: Fed rate-cut probabilities (97% chance of 25 bps cut in September), the CrowdStrike outage impact on [[crwd]] (a content-update bug causing system-wide failures across industries), and [[gme]] positioning post-expiration with declining volume and near-balanced call/put ratios. The host reviews technical setups, options chain structure, and contrarian entry opportunities in event-driven dislocations.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2024-07-21)

- **Fed rate-cut trajectory** [02:25–03:10]: Market pricing 97% probability of 25 bps cut in September (down from 50 bps expectation), followed by potential 25 bps cuts in November and December. Host expects data-dependent pauses between cuts given Fed's focus on leads/lags in economic systems.
- **CrowdStrike outage severity** [07:55–12:45]: Stock fell from ~$369 to sub-$290 in three days (July 16–19). CEO George Kurtz confirmed a single content-update file (not a cyber attack) caused blue-screen-of-death across Microsoft environments; rollback underway. Widespread impact across airlines, news networks, and business operations.
- **DJT positioning controversy** [04:27–07:16]: Austin Private Wealth filed a bearish position (later revealed as clerical error with 10,000× contract multiplier). Stock shows lower highs since March, struggling sideways; bearish positioning not unreasonable from technical perspective, but filing timing coincidence overblown.
- **GME post-expiration state** [13:51–18:42]: 20% move from July 1 peak now fading; volume declining sharply vs. mid-June levels. Options chain lightening: 571k calls / 350k puts (much lower than typical). Call/put ratio near parity (historically 2–3× more calls), suggesting reduced bullish conviction. Next volatility concentration at August 16 expiration.
- **Overnight futures recovery** [19:06–19:46]: E-mini S&P 500 +0.3%, Russell 2000 +0.28%, Nasdaq +0.55% post-Biden withdrawal announcement. No strong directional signal; market showing standard consolidation behavior after prior week's uniform sell-offs.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Event-driven reversion plays** [13:08–13:30]: Tight stop-loss ranges around price extremes create asymmetric risk/reward when news-driven dislocations reverse; CrowdStrike exemplifies this setup.
- **Options chain as positioning gauge** [15:00–17:36]: Open interest distribution, call/put ratios, and volume migration across expirations reveal institutional conviction shifts and liquidity concentration; declining OI + balanced ratios = quieter period ahead.
- **Volume as confirmation** [14:32–15:00]: Declining volume in [[gme]] vs. mid-June baseline signals reduced participation; scans for volume expansion are key to identifying renewed interest.
- **Fed data sensitivity** [01:06–01:57]: PMI, PCE, GDP, jobless claims drive rate-cut probability updates; market reprices immediately on Fed Watch Tool shifts.
