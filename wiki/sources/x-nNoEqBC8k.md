# Vertical Spreads for Beginners

## Summary

An introduction to the four primary vertical spread structures: long call spreads, short put spreads, short call spreads, and long put spreads. The session explains how verticals function as defined-risk, defined-profit instruments by pairing long and short options at different strikes, making them capital-efficient for small accounts.

## Key takeaways

- **Vertical structure fundamentals** [00:00–00:23]: Verticals consist of long and short options at different strikes, creating defined profit and risk zones.
- **P&L diagram symmetry** [00:00–00:23]: Long call spreads and short put spreads share similar P&L shapes; short call spreads and long put spreads are likewise structurally analogous.
- **Capital efficiency for small accounts** [00:23]: Defined risk and profit zones allow verticals to be constructed at very low cost, making them accessible to traders with limited capital.

## Notable quotes

> "You have a defined profit zone, and you have a defined risk zone. And because of that, very frequently you'll find that these can be really alluring to small accounts because you can end up with really, really cheap positions."
