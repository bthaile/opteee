# Profit Mechanism Precedes Trade Management

## Summary

The foundational error in options trading is learning a structure first and then figuring out management. The correct sequence is to identify the profit mechanism that matches your market view, then select the appropriate structure and management approach. Volatility characteristics of the underlying—measured by metrics like average true range—directly determine how a trade should be managed.

## Key takeaways

- **Start with profit mechanism, not structure** [00:00] — Assess what profit mechanism you're trying to capture before choosing an options structure; trade management flows from this decision, not the reverse.
- **Volatility profile informs management** [00:20–00:44] — High-volatility names (e.g., Tesla) versus low-volatility names (e.g. Exxon Mobil) exhibit 2–3× differences in average true range and average daily range, directly shaping how you manage the same directional thesis.
- **Price direction + volatility analysis = structure selection** [00:20] — Once you know your directional bias and have analyzed the specific profit mechanism available in that name, you can select the appropriate structure and management rules.
