# Option liquidity #options #trading #optionstrading

## Summary
A quick practical tip for assessing relative option liquidity across different underlyings and strikes. By normalizing the bid-ask spread as a percentage of strike price and comparing it to the same delta and expiration in a liquid benchmark like SPY, traders can gauge how liquid an option contract truly is on a relative basis.

## Key takeaways
- **Bid-ask spread normalization** [00:00]: Divide the bid-ask spread by the strike price to create a comparable liquidity metric across different price levels.
- **Relative comparison method** [00:00]: Compare the normalized spread of your target option to the same delta and expiration in a liquid reference instrument (e.g., SPY) to assess relative liquidity.
- **Practical application** [00:22]: Use this technique when evaluating whether an option contract has sufficient liquidity for your trading needs.
