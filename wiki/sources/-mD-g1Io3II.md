# GME Surges Towards $30: Options Breakdown

## Summary

GameStop is consolidating above the $25 support level with sustained volume and organized moving averages, approaching earnings. The options market shows elevated implied volatility with strong call skew, a shift toward longer-dated positioning across multiple strikes, and net premium heavy to the buy side—indicating institutional and retail interest in the company's fundamental recovery rather than purely speculative squeeze dynamics.

## Key takeaways

- **Price consolidation & technical setup** [00:02–02:27]: GME consolidating above $25 handle since November 11; short-term and mid-term moving averages upward-sloping and organized; RSI hovering below 70 with momentum intact; volume sustained at 15–26M shares daily.
- **Implied volatility & skew structure** [02:27–06:49]: IV at ~131.8%; strong call skew across all primary expirations (June, etc.); OTM calls priced richer than puts, indicating market expectation of upside movement; call IV skew reflects both compensation for upside risk and genuine directional bias.
- **Open interest shift to longer expirations** [03:38–04:49]: Significant change in OI breakdown—activity spreading across 60-day (Jan 25) and longer expirations rather than concentrated in near-term; indicates positioning for company upside rather than short-term volatile pops.
- **Call volume & unusual activity** [05:46–07:58]: 348k calls vs. 52k puts traded today; most call volume on the buy side; $350k trade at $27 strike noted; activity in 20–26 strike range across Jan/Feb expirations; dark pool relatively quiet (no massive single transactions).
- **Premium distribution & participant breadth** [12:14–14:12]: Call premium built across multiple strikes ($10, $20, $25, $29, $30) and expirations; net premium heavy to buy side; flat distribution across strikes/expirations suggests multiple participants rather than single large actor; bullish signal for company valuation.
- **Earnings catalyst ahead** [14:12]: Company approaching earnings; focus on fundamental valuation improvement rather than squeeze mechanics; $30 target within sight as reflection of higher company valuation.
