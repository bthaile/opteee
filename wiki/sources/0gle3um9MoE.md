# Tech Sector, DG, NVDA, GME Analysis | Stock Market Recap

## Summary

Market recap covering three key movers: Dollar General crashed ~25% post-earnings on weak forward guidance despite acceptable net sales; the tech sector is trending down in a negative-sloping channel with the Nasdaq leading weakness while the S&P 500 remains more resilient; Nvidia sold off sharply on 89% sell-volume after a strong run, while GameStop showed strength with above-average volume and healthy rejection from support, ahead of earnings on September 10th.

## Key takeaways

- **Dollar General earnings miss**: EPS guidance of $5.50–$6.20 significantly missed the $7.12 consensus expectation; net sales expanded to $10.21B but forward sales growth guidance of 4.7–5.3% signals deceleration [00:20–01:17]
- **Tech sector weakness**: Nasdaq (QQQ) is trending down toward moving averages; S&P 500 has more room before rotation; Russell 2000 bucking the trend; VIX down despite market weakness—unusual but not infrequent [01:41–02:24]
- **Nvidia breakdown**: Fell more than expected with massive 89% sell-volume candle; emotional unwinding post-earnings; needed "perfect" results to sustain parabolic move; short-term weakness despite long-term promise [02:51–03:49]
- **GameStop positive action**: Rejected out of value area immediately; volume surged to 10.3M shares (2× 20-day average) with 53% buy-side; RSI still below 50 but price action encouraging [04:17–05:02]
- **GME options flow**: 211k calls vs. 47k puts traded; 576k call open interest vs. 288k puts; strong call skew; unusual distribution across expirations (Jan 2025 concentrated) rather than front-month concentration [06:07–06:53]
- **GME order flow diversification**: Healthy mix across expirations rather than hyper-concentration in front months—a shift from typical GME pattern; earnings cycle (Sept 10) may drive further build [07:38–08:18]
