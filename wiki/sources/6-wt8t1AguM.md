# Enhancing the Wheel Strategy Short Calls

## Summary

This video addresses a core limitation of the wheel strategy: capped upside when selling covered calls at a 1:1 ratio (one call per 100 shares). Rather than defaulting to this ratio, the host demonstrates how to calculate the breakeven point between collecting less premium upfront versus capturing additional upside if the stock continues to rise, enabling data-driven position-sizing decisions.

## Key takeaways

- **The 1:1 call-to-share ratio problem** [00:00]: Selling one call per 100 shares caps your profit potential; this is a design choice, not a requirement.
- **Ratio alternatives** [00:00]: Selling fewer calls (e.g., 2 calls against 300 shares instead of 3) reduces premium collected but preserves upside if the stock rallies.
- **Quantitative decision-making** [00:37]: Rather than guessing, calculate the price point at which selling fewer calls becomes more profitable than the 1:1 ratio, enabling logical position-sizing choices.
- **Premium vs. upside trade-off** [00:37]: The decision hinges on comparing the foregone premium against the additional profit from higher stock appreciation.
