# thinkorswim Volume Profile Setup

## Summary

A practical walkthrough of configuring volume profile in thinkorswim to visualize supply and demand at specific price levels. The video explains how volume nodes and the point of control reveal where the most trading activity has occurred, and demonstrates key settings (expansion mode, opacity, color customization) to make the indicator usable for chart analysis.

## Key takeaways

- **Volume profile visualizes supply and demand**: Volume nodes (peaks on the left side of the chart) show the number of transactions at each price level; the point of control (dashed line) marks where the most volume occurred [00:00–01:12]
- **Timeframe aggregation matters**: Volume profile automatically adjusts to match your chart's timeframe (daily, weekly, monthly); the same price level can show different volume concentrations across different periods [01:12–02:19]
- **Volume nodes indicate congestion zones**: High-volume price levels typically correspond to consolidation and support/resistance; gaps between volume nodes are areas where price moves quickly [02:19–03:28]
- **Setup: Studies → Edit Studies → Volume Profile**: Access the indicator via the Studies menu; the critical setting is **Expansion: No** (not Yes) to overlay the profile on the chart rather than displacing it [03:28–04:25]
- **Opacity and color tuning**: Set opacity to ~20% to avoid washing out the chart; customize point-of-control color in Globals if desired for visibility [04:25–05:39]
