# Technical Indicators I Use Daily: Moving Averages, Volume Profile & Net Highs/Lows

## Summary

Technical indicators are contextual tools, not predictive magic. This breakdown covers three core indicators—moving averages, volume profile, and net highs/lows—that apply across most trading timeframes. The key is aligning indicator selection to your profit mechanism, using multiple timeframes for context, and stacking strength objectively rather than confirming bias.

## Key takeaways

- **Multi-timeframe alignment** [01:11–01:54]: Pick timeframes one up and one down from your intended holding period. For a 5-day trade, examine 15-minute, 5-day, and 10–15-day charts; ignore 6-month charts that don't apply to your edge.

- **Indicators serve profit mechanisms, not vice versa** [03:26–04:13]: Choose signals relative to what you're trying to capture. A 5-day price-action trader doesn't need variance risk premium measures; a variance-premium trader does. Avoid redundant oscillators; test deeply and hone.

- **Stack strength objectively** [06:54–07:38]: Don't cherry-pick indicators to support your thesis. Evaluate all signals together. If they don't align, find another setup. The question is never "Can I trade this?" but "Is this the right place for my money?"

- **Moving averages: slope, organization, width** [17:21–18:27]: Assess price relationship to MAs, whether short-term is above long-term, and the width between them. Wide spacing often precedes consolidation or pullback. Strong upward-sloping MAs suggest upside momentum; use them as trend-following exit anchors.

- **Volume profile: point of control and nodes** [14:33–16:16]: The point of control marks highest-volume price level. Big volume nodes often act as support/resistance; gaps between nodes see less price activity. Use probabilistically—if price breaks a major node, there's likely a reason; that informs directional bias.

- **Net highs/lows: breadth divergence warning** [10:22–13:50]: Plot NYSE (green) and NASDAQ (purple) net highs minus lows as histograms and moving averages. When price makes new highs but net highs/lows flip negative or slow, that's a major warning sign preceding downside. Separate gross highs from gross lows to diagnose whether the move is driven by strength fading or weakness accelerating.

- **Avoid indicator overload** [08:20–08:38]: Use a clean chart for your primary timeframe; create separate profiles if needed. Mental filtering is a skill; beginners should simplify ruthlessly.

## Notable quotes

> "Technical analysis is grossly misunderstood. There is absolutely no magic. There is no specific indicator signal that always works."

> "The question is always not could I possibly place a trade in this. The question instead should always be, is this the right place for my money?"

> "If I'm trying to trade some sort of short-term price action to the upside and I see indicators that aren't stacking strength as I objectively analyze them, okay, then that's not going to be the thing. I'm going to go find something else that looks better."
