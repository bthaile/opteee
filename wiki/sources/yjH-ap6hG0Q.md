### Evergreen mechanics

- **Fingerprinting trader behavior**: Reconstruct entry patterns by overlaying position updates with historical price action; Roaring Kitty's 2019–2020 pattern shows scaling in on dips, averaging down hard, then averaging up as conviction grows [01:39:00–02:08:00]
- **Profit mechanism first**: Before trading options, define what you're trading (directional move, volatility, dividend, index inclusion) and measure it; options amplify an existing edge, they don't create one [00:08:00–00:11:00]
- **Position sizing discipline**: Roaring Kitty started with 50–100 lot entries, scaled to 200–300 as conviction grew and price fell; this is investor-grade conviction, not trader-grade churn [01:46:00–01:48:00]
- **Theta decay acceleration**: Options within 60 DTE show exponential theta decay; selling calls 132 DTE (like Sam's covered calls) ties up capital inefficiently; shorter cycles (30–60 DTE) recycle capital faster [00:23:00–00:24:00]
- **Covered call ratio management**: 2,000 shares with 9 short calls (900 shares at risk) leaves 1,100 shares of upside—good; but 132 DTE expiration means slow decay and extended capital lock-up [00:17:00–00:23:00]
- **Earnings volatility surface**: Call skew typically exceeds put skew on front expiration; 20 SEP shows inverted skew (calls > puts), suggesting market pricing upside bias post-earnings [02:37:00–02:39:00]
- **Unusual options activity scanning**: Filter by volume expansion (100%+ on single strike), persistent large trades, and relative volume vs. 20/5-day average; floor-executed block trades indicate institutional routing, not necessarily insider activity [00:28:00–01:01:00]
