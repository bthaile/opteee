# GameStop (GME) Analysis: Key Earnings & Options Expiration Insights

## Summary

GameStop's recent price action was driven by news coverage of a potential Bitcoin involvement (following Sailor/Cohen speculation), but the move appears overdone and likely to consolidate. With implied volatility at a 3-year low (74% IV, 3rd percentile), the options market is pricing in severe two-sided risk ahead of earnings on 25 Feb, making covered strangles attractive despite light overall open interest. The stock is consolidating in a wide 282–252 range with elevated call skew but declining volume and volatility.

## Key takeaways

- **Bitcoin news catalyst [02:17–03:26]**: The 13–14 Feb price spike was driven by news cycle pickup around potential GameStop–Bitcoin involvement, not new information. The move appears excessive and likely institutional unwinding rather than sustained accumulation.
- **Volatility at 3-year lows [03:26–04:40]**: IV at 74% sits at the 3rd percentile for the past year; 7-day realized vol has been declining for two months; 30-day IV contracted from 128% to 86%, creating a significant vol-crush environment.
- **Front expiration (21 Feb) is light [04:40–05:56]**: 166k calls vs. 40k puts in the upcoming expiration; total OI is sub-600k (normally 700k–800k). Call skew remains heavy (one of the largest call-to-put ratios in a while), but absolute depth is thin.
- **Call IV skew is extreme [07:08–08:12]**: Out-of-the-money calls show IV spikes to 89.7% (21 Feb) and remain elevated through 28 Feb, signaling the market prices equal severity in both directions—crash or moon. Contrast with Apple, where call IV is typically much lower than put IV.
- **Covered strangle remains attractive [08:12–09:19]**: The two-sided IV profile and elevated call skew make short strangles a good fit for holding GameStop; you're paid premium to hold a volatile stock.
- **Earnings (25 Feb) expected to move IV [09:19–10:26]**: IV jumps to 89.7% at the 21 Mar expiration, suggesting the market expects earnings to land within a defined range. Company still has cash to reinvent; outcome uncertain.
- **Call premium dominance [10:26–11:35]**: 130k calls vs. 23k puts in total OI, but call premium is 15.6M vs. put premium 1.9M. Many short calls are being sold by stock holders (covered calls), not pure bearish bets.
- **Consolidation zone [11:35–end]**: Stock is in a wide 282–252 consolidation; likely to narrow, but direction unclear. Needs to break out of this range to establish new trend.

## Notable quotes

> "The market is essentially pricing in severity to both directions—it doesn't know which way it's going to go, it's scared, and it's pricing in heavy vols to the upside because we all know GameStop can crack off to the upside big giant moves very quickly."
