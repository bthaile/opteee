# Deep Dive into GME Options

## Summary

Eric examines the GameStop options chain to assess volatility, open interest, and trading activity across multiple expiration cycles (5 July, 19 July, 16 August). He identifies predominantly small retail speculation in far out-of-the-money calls rather than institutional positioning, and discusses how option market structure—particularly implied volatility patterns—can signal future price movement. The analysis emphasizes the distinction between retail lottery-ticket buying and meaningful institutional accumulation.

## Key takeaways

- **Volatility structure across cycles** [02:13–02:37]: GME IV is running ~131%, notably steady across different expirations but suppressed versus historical levels; option markets are predictive of future price moves, making IV patterns worth monitoring.
- **Put/call IV skew and downside floor** [03:36–03:58]: Puts typically trade higher IV than calls (market fears downside), but GME's recent share offering and debt removal create a cash-value floor (~$13/share), flattening the usual skew.
- **Front expiration (5 July) activity** [01:25–01:52]: Mostly small buys across 5 July, 12 July, 19 July; one notable trade at 11 calls suggesting assignment intent; holiday (July 4) mutes volatility, flattening the usual front-expiration premium.
- **Retail vs. institutional positioning in OTM calls** [06:10–07:18]: Far OTM 95 calls show tiny individual trades (1–15 lots at 3¢); largest single trade ~$880; this pattern indicates retail speculation on cheap options, not institutional scale-in.
- **19 July primary expiration structure** [08:15–09:23]: Big round-number strikes (25, 30, 40, 100, 125 calls) show 19k–35k open interest; individual trades remain small (singles to low double-digits), again retail-driven; liquidity clusters at whole strikes.
- **Scanning for institutional entry** [10:04–10:21]: Eric monitors for sudden large block activity as a signal of institutional positioning; this is how he caught Chewy and GME moves early; current activity lacks that signature.
- **Cycle theory and option market timing** [04:18–05:00]: Richard Newton's cycle analysis is well-informed but may be too early; option markets are most active 2–3 weeks out, so front expirations must clear before mid-August moves materialize.
