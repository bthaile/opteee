# GameStop Earnings, Convertible Debt Offering & Covered Strangle Adjustment

## Summary

GameStop reported Q1 2025 earnings with a fourth consecutive beat on estimates (17¢ vs. 8¢ expected) and announced a $1.75 billion convertible senior notes offering (due 2032, zero coupon), triggering a 22% single-day decline. The debt issuance, while creating near-term delta-hedging selling pressure, positions the company with ~$8 billion in combined cash and Bitcoin holdings and no near-term dilution. The host adjusts his covered strangle position by rolling short puts from 28 strike (20 June) to 23 strike (18 July), reducing cash-secured requirements while maintaining upside exposure.

## Key takeaways

- **Earnings beat & revenue context** [03:49]: Fourth consecutive earnings beat (17¢ vs. 8¢ estimate); revenue declining QoQ (732.4M vs. 797M, 860M, 1.2B prior quarters) but earnings turning positive due to strategic cash positioning and impairment charges (35.5M related to international restructuring).

- **Convertible debt mechanics** [04:57]: $1.75B convertible senior notes at 0% coupon due 2032, plus up to $250M additional; creates ~$8B in combined Bitcoin + cash; no near-term dilution; delta-hedging component estimated at 20–25% of average daily volume, explaining much of the sell-side pressure.

- **Dark pool activity signals accumulation** [10:08]: Large block trades (10M–9M shares) concentrated on 12 June; reviewing 30-day history shows vast majority of large buy transactions occurred today despite the 22% decline, indicating institutional interest at lower prices.

- **Options market positioning** [11:28]: Call open interest exceeded 1M contracts (rare); most trades at bid or below (call selling); put buying dominated; front expiration (20 June) shows 424k call OI vs. 73k put OI; secondary pockets at 16 Jan and 17 Dec.

- **Volatility skew pricing** [12:35]: Steep call-side volatility skew on front expiration pricing severity to the upside, but surface shows market is "either way" on direction while pricing a large move; 15 Jan expiration shows more standard put-heavy skew by comparison.

- **Covered strangle adjustment** [15:01]: Closed 20 June 28 puts (sold at 34 delta for $1.74 credit, closed at $5.65) with net positive PNL despite 24% stock decline; rolled to 18 July 23 puts (46 delta) to reduce strike from 28 (above range midline) to 23 (within range); added 3 contracts, increasing cash-secured requirement from $16.8k to $20.7k while reducing per-share basis from 28 to 23.

- **Capital efficiency of adjustment** [16:20]: Adding 300 shares of put exposure increased cash-secured requirement by only ~$3k due to lower strike basis; covered strangle now running 8.4% better than underlying on total return on capital metric (conservative, excluding cash yield on box spreads).

- **Strategic context** [07:46]: Debt offering positions company well long-term; near-term price reaction immaterial; Cohen's moves are tactical cash acquisition for crypto/business investment, not a "quasi Ponzi scheme" like MicroStrategy; company simultaneously divesting unprofitable segments (Canada divestiture complete, assets held for sale indicate further unwind likely).
