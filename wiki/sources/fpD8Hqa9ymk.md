# Trading Indicators I Use Every Day #optionstrading

## Summary

Eric walks through his core daily trading indicators: moving averages (5-period and 20-period) for trend identification, relative volume analysis for breakout confirmation, a price-spike graph with standard-deviation bands to flag extremes, and RSI (Relative Strength Index) as an oscillator for relative strength context. These tools work together to identify trade setups and confirm directional bias.

## Key takeaways

- **Moving averages (5 & 20-period)** [00:00] — dual-period setup identifies trends and provides contextual reference; particularly useful for breakout strategies where trend confirmation matters.
- **Relative volume analysis** [00:00] — volume trends aren't absolute; compare current volume to historical baseline to confirm breakout strength.
- **Price-spike graph with standard-deviation bands** [00:26] — 20-period lookback flags daily moves relative to volatility; color-coded (gray = within 1σ, dark green = 1σ, light green = 2σ+) to spot price extremes.
- **RSI (Relative Strength Index)** [00:45] — oscillator that measures overall relative strength; complements price-spike data to contextualize momentum.
