# Roaring Kitty GME Options Trade

## Summary

Analysis of Roaring Kitty's ~120,000 GME $20 call position (21 June expiration) following the completion of GameStop's equity offering on 11 June 2024. The host examines the position's ~$67.9M unrealized gain, the mechanics of potential cashless exercise, and the gamma ramp dynamics driving call skew and accumulation into next week's expiration.

## Key takeaways

- **Position structure** [01:39]: Roaring Kitty holds 120,000 GME $20 calls (21 June) with notional exercise value of ~$240M; currently trading at $11.30 with ~$67.9M unrealized gain and $29.4M cash backing.
- **Equity offering completion** [02:27]: GameStop's secondary offering concluded on 11 June; massive selling volume (7–10 June) abated, replaced by DRS accumulation and buying above recent averages; GME rallied ~22% intraday to above $30 after-hours.
- **Deep ITM mechanics** [04:26]: Position is ~$10 ITM; extrinsic value compressed to ~$1; further price appreciation converts extrinsic to intrinsic, maximizing gain without theta drag concern for a CFA-level trader.
- **Cashless exercise opportunity** [06:05]: If total position gain exceeds $240M exercise cost, Roaring Kitty can execute cashless exercise using gains to cover full notional; currently very close or possibly already above threshold.
- **Call skew and gamma ramp** [07:30]: 21 June expiration shows massive call skew (456K puts vs 242K calls inverted); 600K call volume vs 260K put volume today signals market pricing massive upside; gamma ramp likely to accelerate into expiration.
- **Market maker dynamics** [09:25]: If Roaring Kitty attempts to unwind 120K contracts, sophisticated traders and market makers would immediately detect and fade the position; phased exit in 10K lots would still be picked up rapidly.
- **Execution optionality** [11:38]: Roaring Kitty can exercise part, sell part, or execute cashless exercise; unlikely to hit all 120K contracts at once due to spread haircut; phased approach over time more probable.
