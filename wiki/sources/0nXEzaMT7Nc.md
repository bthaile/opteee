# Captain Condor's 90,000 Contract Iron Condor

## Summary

A breakdown of a massive 90,000-contract iron condor position on SPX with $5-wide strikes, examining the trade's high-probability structure but unfavorable risk/reward ratio. The analysis highlights how tighter spreads reduce per-contract profit while maintaining similar loss exposure, illustrating a capital-efficiency trade-off common in large-scale premium-selling strategies.

## Key takeaways

- **Position structure** [00:00–00:24]: 90,000 contracts, $5-wide iron condor; long 6940 puts, short 6950 puts, short 6990 calls, long 7000 calls (approximate strikes).
- **Profit vs. loss asymmetry** [00:24–00:43]: Max profit ~$220 per spread, max loss ~$280 per spread—a risk/reward ratio unfavorable for the probability of success.
- **Capital efficiency trade-off** [00:00–00:24]: Tighter spreads ($5 vs. wider) reduce absolute profit per contract but require more contracts to achieve target notional exposure, increasing operational complexity and slippage risk.
- **High-probability trap** [00:24]: High-probability trades (typical for iron condors) come with the downside of capped profit and asymmetric loss potential relative to win rate.

## Notable quotes

> "The reason why people do this is because it's a pretty high probability trade, but there's a downside with anything that has high probabilities."
