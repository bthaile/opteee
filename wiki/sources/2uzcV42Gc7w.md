# Do NOT Sell Covered Calls On Auto Pilot

## Summary

Systematic covered-call selling—particularly as deployed in covered-call ETFs—underperforms because market makers front-run predictable selling patterns and adjust bids accordingly. A discretionary approach that selects entry points based on elevated implied volatility and fundamental context (e.g., earnings) preserves edge and avoids systematic underperformance.

## Key takeaways

- **Avoid mechanical covered-call programs** [00:00–00:23]: Covered-call ETFs perform poorly because their predictable selling schedules allow market makers to suppress bid prices in advance. Systematic approaches eliminate edge.
- **Pick your shots based on vol context** [00:23–00:45]: Sell calls selectively when implied volatility appears rich relative to baseline, not on a fixed schedule. High premiums warrant investigation—compare against historical norms before committing.
- **Investigate elevated premiums** [00:45]: When you see unusually high option premiums, dig into the reason (e.g., earnings, event risk). Decide consciously whether to sell through that event or wait for post-event normalization.

## Notable quotes

> "If you always sell calls against something, you are more likely than not to systemically underperform."
