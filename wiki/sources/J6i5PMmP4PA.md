# Why Backtesting Changes Everything - Project No Code

## Summary

Backtesting is the critical tool that eliminates years of trial-and-error in strategy development. Rather than spending extended time fumbling through market iterations, systematic testing of parameters—like candle timeframes and trade management rules—allows traders to rapidly validate edge and compress the learning curve from years to weeks.

## Key takeaways

- **Backtesting eliminates trial-and-error iteration** [00:22–00:44]: Testing ideas systematically prevents wasting years on parameter optimization (e.g., 15-minute vs. 5-minute candles, position management) that would otherwise happen live.
- **Edge requires systematic validation, not market fumbling** [00:00–00:22]: Building meaningful edge demands structured testing; you cannot reliably hit your desired outcome by experimenting in real time.
- **Backtesting compresses the strategy-development timeline** [00:44]: The ability to test ideas rapidly is what fundamentally changes the speed and reliability of strategy refinement.
