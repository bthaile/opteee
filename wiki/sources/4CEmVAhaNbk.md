# Option Greeks Simplified - Theta Decay Explained

## Summary

This video provides a foundational explanation of theta, the Greek that measures the rate of premium decay per day passing. The host demonstrates how theta behaves across different deltas and days-to-expiration, emphasizing the critical relationship between moneyness and time decay, and illustrates why buying cheap out-of-the-money options often results in significant losses due to accelerating theta bleed.

## Key takeaways

- **Theta definition and mechanics** [00:00]: Theta measures the rate of change of an option's premium per day passing; a negative theta value indicates how much premium the option will lose after one day.
- **Theta decays continuously, not discretely** [01:19]: Theta decay is not a step function applied at end-of-day; market makers apply smart acceleration and deceleration, pricing out weekend decay early to prevent arbitrage.
- **Raw theta vs. theta as percentage of premium** [05:21]: A low raw theta number can represent a high percentage of remaining premium; both metrics matter for trading decisions—the 10-delta 6-DTE option shows negative 0.09 theta but represents 30% of remaining premium.
- **Theta peaks at-the-money, declines out-of-the-money** [06:48]: Theta as a raw number is highest for at-the-money options (50-delta) because they have the most extrinsic value; deep in-the-money calls have very low theta because little extrinsic value remains.
- **Theta accelerates as expiration approaches** [08:06]: Theta generally declines as you move further out in time, but the relationship is not perfectly linear; anomalies (kinks) can appear when events like earnings are priced in.
- **Out-of-the-money options bleed value rapidly** [10:52]: A 6-DTE 10-delta call losing 19 cents per day means a $1 underlying move (worth ~10 cents profit) is offset by one day of theta decay; after one day, the same move requires a $1.90 move to break even.

## Notable quotes

> "Theta decays continuously. It's not just same value, end of day, Theta decay is now added. That's not it at all."

> "This is what makes these out of the money options like hot potatoes because the longer you hold on to them. You literally now need larger and larger moves to even get back to break even let alone to start making money."
