# Trade Management and Portfolio Plan - Small Stacks Pt8

## Summary

Eric reviews the Small Stacks $5k portfolio after two weeks of trading, highlighting a ~10% realized return ($581 profit) across closed positions in Pfizer, Kroger, and XLF. He establishes a new call diagonal in Bank of America and discusses position-sizing discipline, capital allocation, and the importance of building a consistent skill set that scales with account growth rather than chasing dollar returns on small accounts.

## Key takeaways

- **Goal-setting for small accounts** [00:26–01:33]: Target 8% annual return (~$34/month on $5k) as baseline; 15% as stretch goal. Focus on percentage returns and skill development, not absolute dollars, because meaningful dollar gains require larger capital bases.
- **Scaling capital thesis** [02:02–02:56]: A consistent 8% return on a $1.25M account yields ~$100k/year (~$8.3k/month). The small account is a training ground; real wealth compounds once capital exceeds $100k and savings accelerate growth.
- **Realized P&L breakdown** [04:52–05:49]: Pfizer closed for $402 profit; Kroger $155; XLF $24. Total realized P&L: $581 (~10% return in two weeks). Pending unrealized P&L: ~$109 (~2% of current account value).
- **Capital utilization efficiency** [06:16–06:40]: Account utilization just over $3k (54% of $5.6k). High return on invested capital (ROIC) achieved without maxing out buying power—a hallmark of disciplined position-sizing.
- **Bank of America call diagonal setup** [06:40–07:04]: Long 18-Jun-21 $18 calls (8 delta, paid $8); short 16-Oct-26 $16 calls (sold at $0.82). Expected ROIC >10%. Structured with no upside risk via delta management.
- **Kroger position patience** [07:27–07:52]: Holding long LEAPS without short calls because available premium insufficient to meet strike targets. Already captured $155 in short-call profit; willing to wait for better risk/reward.
- **Earnings volatility play** [08:36–08:59]: Bank of America earnings on 14-Oct; short calls expire 16-Oct. Plan to close shorts before earnings, then re-establish on earnings day to capture IV expansion.
- **Election risk management** [09:42–10:03]: Scaling out of all accounts ahead of election as a binary event; maintaining exposure but reducing position size to avoid unfavorable risk/reward.
- **Capital discipline** [10:27–10:47]: ~$2.5k dry powder remaining; not forcing trades; satisfied with current progress and return profile.

## Notable quotes

> "Focus on the skill set. Let this account continue to grow. This will help you modestly move towards that goal, but if you're first starting out with pretty much anything less than 100k, you really need to focus on saving money."

> "I don't see a good return on that risk [election exposure], so I will be following through with that as we continue along."
