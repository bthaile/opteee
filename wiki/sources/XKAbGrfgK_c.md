# Understanding Charm in Options Trading

## Summary

Charm is a higher-order Greek that measures how delta changes as time passes, independent of price movement. For the same strike, an option's delta can differ significantly depending on days to expiration—a critical dynamic that traders must account for to avoid being blindsided by time decay effects on position behavior.

## Key takeaways

- **Charm defined**: Delta changes over time for identical strikes at different expirations; charm quantifies this rate of change [00:00–00:24]
- **Same strike, different delta**: A 350 strike call can have 70 delta at one expiration but 90+ delta at another, purely due to time passage [00:24]
- **Binary choice**: Every option must eventually resolve to either zero delta (out of the money) or one delta (in the money); charm reflects the path of that transition [00:24–00:50]
- **Risk blind spot**: Traders can execute everything else correctly but still suffer losses if unaware of charm's impact on position dynamics [00:50]
