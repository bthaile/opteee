# GME Call Options Expanding

## Summary

GameStop closed at ~$24.97 on July 10, 2024, just shy of $25 resistance and near the point of control from the 3-month volume profile. Call options activity surged dramatically—over 300,000 calls traded versus 86,000 puts—with heavy concentration in the July 12 and July 19 expirations. Implied volatility expanded sharply in the front weeks while longer-dated August and September positions showed emerging conviction, signaling multi-cycle positioning distinct from typical short-term retail patterns.

## Key takeaways

- **Point of control resistance** [00:30–01:15]: The 3-month, 6-month, and 1-year volume profiles all cluster around $26.40–$26.60, where accumulated holders are unwinding positions near breakeven; this level acts as a natural supply zone.
- **Call volume surge** [02:46–03:11]: 300,000+ calls traded (vs. 86,000 puts) with 25,500 contracts in the July 12 and July 19 expirations alone; this represents a meaningful uptick from the prior week's mid-20s range.
- **July 19 $30 strike concentration** [03:39–04:27]: 41,700 contracts traded in the July 19 $30 calls; individual trade sizes ranged from $37k–$43k, smaller than recent $200k+ blocks but consistent volume building.
- **July 12 $26 strike open interest** [05:50–06:09]: 9,600 open interest with 2,636 volume on July 12 $26 calls (2 days to expiration); overwhelming opening activity suggests fresh positioning into imminent expiration.
- **Multi-cycle conviction** [06:40–09:31]: August 16 and September 20 expirations showing increased red squares (new open interest); this contrasts with prior weeks' front-month-only focus and indicates traders extending conviction beyond zero-DTE.
- **Volatility term structure** [08:19–10:14]: IV at 139–134 in the front weeks, dropping sharply thereafter; expect IV expansion as August 16 approaches, consistent with the observed expiration-cycle volatility clustering pattern.
- **Bullish lean in order flow** [08:41]: 33% of call transactions at the offer (buys); puts slightly at the bid (sells); net bullish skew in transacted volume.
