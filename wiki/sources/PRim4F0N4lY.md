# IBIT Covered Strangle #optionstrading

## Summary

Eric introduces the covered strangle strategy as deployed in the sample portfolio, distinguishing it from the wheel by maintaining simultaneous short puts and long equity without capping upside via call ratios. The strategy uses an allocation framework with utilization tracking to manage capital deployment during market declines, emphasizing the importance of cash reserves for scaling in.

## Key takeaways

- **Covered strangle vs. the wheel** [00:00–00:24]: The covered strangle differs from the wheel by not capping upside profit through call ratios; instead, short puts and long equity are held together, allowing full participation in rallies.
- **Allocation and utilization framework** [00:24–00:42]: The strategy operates within a defined total allocation, with utilization measuring how much of that allocation is deployed at any given time.
- **Cash management during declines** [00:42]: During market downturns, maintain moderate allocation (60–80% utilization) while keeping cash reserves available to scale in on weakness.

## Notable quotes

> "The way I think about covered strangles is they get what I refer to as an allocation. So it's a total amount of money that they get up to and then utilization is how much of that allocation are we're using at a given point in time."
