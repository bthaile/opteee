# How to Size Options Trades & Build a Trade Checklist | Options Trading for Beginners Pt7

## Summary

This session covers two core topics for options traders: position sizing methodology and the construction of a pre-trade and post-trade checklist. Eric walks through a systematic framework for determining trade size based on portfolio allocation, risk tolerance, and expected return validation. The checklist process emphasizes defining profit mechanisms, quantifying hypotheses, selecting appropriate strategies, and establishing predetermined exit rules before entering any trade.

## Key takeaways

### Pre-trade checklist framework
- **Profit mechanism first** [05:00]: Identify *how* the trade makes money before selecting options; options are a vehicle, not an edge themselves.
- **Broad product screening** [54:22]: Verify the security fits your criteria and check for upcoming events (earnings, etc.) that could impact the position.
- **Quantify your hypothesis** [55:06]: Move from "I want to be long GameStop" to specific targets: where do you think it goes, what's the probability, and in what timeframe?
- **Strategy selection** [56:12]: Match your thesis to your stable of strategies; narrow by expected move size and directional conviction.
- **P&L management plan** [57:30]: Define entry, stop-loss, and profit-taking rules *before* placing the trade; use alerts or stop orders to enforce discipline.
- **Trade log entry** [59:26]: Record strategy, strike, size, and thesis; this feeds your after-action review process.

### Position sizing methodology
- **Portfolio-first approach** [25:45]: Size is determined by portfolio structure (core vs. speculative allocation), not a fixed percentage rule.
- **Risk-based sizing** [28:01]: Define your maximum acceptable loss on the trade, then work backward to determine contract quantity. Example: $20k account, 2% risk = $400 max loss per trade.
- **Validate expected return** [38:19]: Calculate profit potential at your target price and compare to risk. Use probability of touch and expected move to assess risk/reward.
- **Capital allocation constraint** [36:30]: If capital is tight, your position size is capped by available funds; if unconstrained, risk becomes the limiting factor.
- **Avoid over-leverage on small accounts** [28:52]: Accounts under $15k are disadvantaged; focus on avoiding drawdowns rather than aggressive growth.

### After-action review (post-trade)
- **Track what happened** [01:03:33]: Log the outcome and context of the trade.
- **Evaluate strategy performance** [01:04:04]: Did the strategy behave as expected? Are there new data points to incorporate?
- **Bubble up to portfolio level** [01:06:04]: Did the trade fit and operate within the portfolio as designed?
- **Avoid emotional logging** [01:06:36]: Focus on objective trade mechanics, not emotional state (unless emotional discipline is a documented weakness).

### GameStop context & trade examples
- **Why GameStop** [14:49]: Combination of puzzle-solving appeal, market inefficiency signals, learning vehicle for derivatives mechanics, and tradeable price behavior.
- **Live sizing example** [28:01–36:30]: Walked through sizing a GameStop long call position: $20k account, 2% risk, support at $23.29, resulting in ~4 contracts at $615 each.
- **Expected return validation** [39:53–42:12]: Profit target $28.50 = ~$1,021 gain; loss at support = ~$400; missing piece is probability of touch (video coming tomorrow).

### Market context (2024-07-20)
- **Breadth deterioration** [48:31]: Net highs collapsed from 458 to 51 (NASDAQ); NYSE breadth barely holding. Headwind for small-cap momentum plays like GameStop.
- **Point of control** [46:38]: GameStop oscillating around $24–25 for weeks; volume concentrated here; limited support below.
- **Max pain dynamics** [44:36]: Post-max-pain reversion is typical, but small-cap weakness and market breadth are offsetting tailwinds.

### Three rules for trading options
- **Rule 1: Profit mechanism first** [05:00]: Understand how the trade makes money before using options.
- **Rule 2: Methodical approach** [07:48]: Test and validate strategies; don't randomly jump between approaches.
- **Rule 3: Take full responsibility** [10:19]: Brutally dissect your own decision-making; no excuses for poor execution.
