# What's the REAL Election Impact on Our Future? w/ Doomberg | TOP

## Summary

Doomberg and Eric discuss the 2024 U.S. presidential election outcome and its implications for markets, policy, and geopolitics. The conversation centers on Trump's decisive victory, the structural failures of the Democratic primary process, and the anticipated policy shifts—particularly deregulation, tariffs, energy production, and immigration enforcement. Doomberg argues that Trump's election represents a historically consequential realignment with inflationary consequences, reshoring momentum, and a fundamental reordering of global power dynamics.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2024-11-07)

- **Election outcome and market repricing** [00:02–07:28]: Trump won decisively; markets had largely priced this in via equities, bonds, and prediction markets. The race appeared closer in media narratives than in financial positioning or early voting data.
- **Democratic primary failure** [03:58–06:25]: Biden's reversal on his 2020 pledge not to run again, combined with late intervention and Harris's coronation without a primary, cost Democrats 2–3 percentage points—decisive in a razor-thin race. Harris never won a vote; Trump is a three-time primary winner and two-time general election winner.
- **Media ecosystem asymmetry** [12:08–13:18]: The right has built an alternative media ecosystem (podcasts, Substack, independent creators) that the left lacks. Trump's podcast strategy outperformed Harris's traditional ad spend; she declined or was unsuited for Joe Rogan.
- **Body language and campaign momentum** [09:44–11:02]: Harris showed visible despondency in final days; Trump ran through the tape. Early voting data and campaign granularity signaled outcome to both camps before election day.
- **Solar sector collapse** [28:31–29:38]: TAN (solar ETF) down ~15% on election morning—a 4–5 sigma move reflecting market belief in Trump's climate rollback. Pfizer notably flat/down, signaling pharmaceutical industry hedging against RFK Jr. influence.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Fiscal dominance and inflation** [21:11–22:23]: Trump is an inflationary force. He wants nominal numbers up (GDP, paychecks, stock market), debt and deficits up, and gas prices down. Tariffs, reshoring, and deregulation all add inflation; only abundant energy is deflationary. Long bond pricing reflects this.
- **Energy policy pivot** [16:42–20:04]: Trump views climate change as a hoax; EPA regulations on power plants will be rolled back. "Drill baby drill" returns; higher U.S. oil and gas production will lower prices, hurt oil majors, benefit service providers. OPEC faces pressure. COP29 attendance expected to drop 66% vs. COP28.
- **Tariff mechanics and currency** [24:38–25:54]: 60% tariffs on Chinese goods are a blunt instrument; passed to consumers until domestic supply comes online. Weak dollar needed to protect reshoring, but Trump also favors strong dollar—an inconsistency. Dollar initially rallied post-election; long-term weakness likely needed for tariff strategy to work.
- **Deregulation and investment boom** [28:31–40:23]: First 60 days will focus on energy deregulation, tariffs, and RFK Jr.'s health agenda. Regulatory certainty for 4 years attracts domestic capex. DOGE (government efficiency) harder than Elon thinks; firing federal employees faces union/legal barriers. Cutting $2T in spending would trigger recession—incompatible with Trump's growth goals.
- **Nuclear bullish, oil mixed** [37:53–39:15]: Nuclear power gains from collapse of environmental opposition. Oil initially bearish on Trump win (supply surge), then bounced on geopolitical uncertainty (Iran, Israel). Long-term: drill baby drill is bearish for oil prices, bullish for service providers.
- **Immigration as inflationary** [43:53–47:10]: Mass deportations will reduce cheap labor supply, raising costs in agriculture and services. Inflationary. Trump will enforce; Mexico will likely comply on border flow to avoid tariffs. USMCA renegotiation threatened if flow doesn't stop.
- **Recession probability fades** [52:02–54:31]: Hard to see 2025 recession given fiscal stimulus, cheap energy, and potential Chinese stimulus response to tariffs. Long bond would need to blow out to 6–8% to signal different regime. Fading recession call for 2025.

## Regime / context

**Date:** November 7, 2024 — election day and immediate aftermath. Trump declared winner by major networks; Senate control confirmed Republican; House control still ~85% probability Republican per prediction markets.

**Structural context:** This is a pivotal political realignment. Doomberg emphasizes that Trump's 2024 victory is more consequential than 2016 because he has four years of experience, no re-election constraint, and unified Republican control of Senate and likely House. The policy agenda (deregulation, tariffs, energy, immigration, RFK Jr. health initiatives) will execute rapidly in Q1 2025.

**Market implications:** Equities rallied ~1,500 points on election day; solar crashed 15%; pharma hedged. Long bond weakness (higher yields) reflects inflation expectations. This is not a typical post-election bounce but a regime shift in policy expectations.
