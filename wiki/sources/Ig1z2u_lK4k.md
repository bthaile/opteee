# The Best Way to Build a Diversified Options Portfolio (Step-by-Step)

## Summary

This video walks through a systematic process for building a diversified options portfolio tailored to individual market outlook and risk tolerance. Rather than applying generic diversification rules, the framework emphasizes aligning portfolio structure with current market disposition, explicit purpose (risk reduction vs. trading multiple regimes), and careful position sizing across strategies, expirations, and volatility levels. The host demonstrates how to use beta-weighted deltas, portfolio risk modeling, and multi-timeframe analysis to ensure the portfolio can achieve return targets while managing drawdowns.

## Key takeaways

- **Define your purpose first** [02:18]: Diversification serves two main goals—reducing portfolio risk through offsetting positions, or enabling you to trade multiple market conditions simultaneously. Your approach depends on whether you actively manage risk through sizing/stops or rely on non-correlated holdings.

- **Establish goals and market disposition before allocating** [01:08], [04:21]: Begin with a target return (e.g., 10% annually), then assess current economic/market conditions. This informs how much portfolio capital to deploy and in what mix. Adjust as market regime shifts (e.g., pivot from small-cap longs if rate-cut thesis fails).

- **Correlation is time-frame dependent** [06:25]: Bitcoin and E-mini S&P 500 may show 95% correlation on a 3-day basis but only 57% on a 30-day basis and 90% on a 360-day basis. Always check correlation across multiple time horizons relevant to your holding period.

- **Mix directional bias, premium type, and expiration** [07:25]: Combine long premium (defined risk, theta decay) with short premium (defined profit, undefined risk, theta collection). Vary expirations and volatility levels to avoid portfolio concentration. Selling premium only in high-IV tickers creates a volatile portfolio.

- **Avoid common mistakes: strategy concentration, position sizing blind spots, and ignoring trade lifecycle** [08:38], [09:36]: Don't stack only one strategy type (e.g., all short puts). Monitor both profit and risk sides. Account for gamma behavior over the trade's life—short puts can experience large swings as gamma increases.

- **Use beta-weighted deltas to size positions** [12:41]: Identify tickers by beta (e.g., QQQ at 1.3× S&P 500 volatility). Allocate core holdings (e.g., 60% of portfolio in covered strangles) and speculative/hedging trades (e.g., 40% split across momentum, volatility risk premium, and short-delta positions).

- **Model portfolio risk against a market move** [14:42], [15:47]: Use tools like thinkorswim's risk profile to test: "If SPX drops 10%, what is my portfolio loss?" Adjust position sizes until the drawdown aligns with your risk tolerance and monthly return target.

- **Maintain short-delta hedge proportional to market outlook** [13:43]: If bullish, hold minimal short positions. As market shows weakness (moving averages faltering, sectors trending down), increase short-delta holdings to 20%+ of portfolio to offset core long exposure.

- **Diversify across time frames and strategies** [16:44]: Different strategies and expirations behave differently over their lifecycle. Spread capital across weekly, monthly, and longer-dated positions to smooth returns and reduce concentration risk.

- **Validate diversification with beta-weighted deltas and portfolio modeling** [16:44]: Use a trade log or platform tools to confirm your portfolio can hit monthly return targets and absorb a standard 5–10% market drawdown without breaching your risk tolerance.

## Notable quotes

> "Diversification is not about reducing returns; it's about aligning your portfolio structure with your current market outlook and your actual risk management process."

> "Correlation is not static—you have to look at it across multiple time frames to understand whether two positions are truly non-correlated for your holding period."

> "If you build a portfolio where you're selling premium only in high volatility tickers, your portfolio is going to be super volatile—that's what volatility is."
