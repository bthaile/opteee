# Dave Lauer - Market Conflicts of Interest || Money Talks

## Summary

Dave Lauer discusses structural conflicts of interest in U.S. equity markets, focusing on payment for order flow (PFOF), wholesaler duopolies (Citadel and Virtue), and market fragmentation. He explains how retail order internalization damages price discovery, widens spreads economy-wide, and proposes the "We the Investors" petition to eliminate intermediation and restore direct market access. The conversation extends to analogous conflicts in ad-tech monopolies and touches on cryptocurrency as a potential disintermediation solution.

## Key takeaways

### Market structure & conflicts of interest
- **PFOF duopoly problem** [03:20–04:20]: Citadel and Virtue internalize ~90% of retail equity orders; brokers claim "price improvement" but academic evidence shows PFOF brokers deliver worse execution than non-PFOF brokers.
- **Spread widening cost** [08:28–09:41]: Wholesaler internalization widens spreads by ~25% economy-wide—a multi-billion-dollar hidden cost to all market participants; "price improvement" is theoretical, not real (doesn't account for hidden orders, midpoint orders, or odd-lot liquidity).
- **Price discovery damage** [22:51–25:38]: Internalized retail orders don't hit the NBBO; they print to tape but don't impact the national best bid/offer, hindering price discovery and potentially altering price direction.
- **Broker duty of best execution** [07:47–08:07]: Brokers accepting PFOF violate their fiduciary duty of best execution; the conflict is never disclosed to retail.

### "We the Investors" petition & solutions
- **Direct market access** [05:52–06:14]: Eliminate wholesalers; route retail orders directly to exchanges or transparent venues where hundreds of market makers can compete, not two.
- **Market simplification** [11:17–12:02]: Consolidate 16 stock exchanges, 40 dark pools, and numerous ATSs; reduce fragmentation; allow retail and institutions to interact directly.
- **Exchange subsidy problem** [17:17–18:27]: Three major exchange families (NYSE, Nasdaq, CBOE) receive ~$100M/year each in SIP (securities information processor) subsidies, allowing unprofitable exchanges to operate and preventing consolidation.
- **Petition status** [21:39]: ~71,000 signatures out of 75,000 target at time of recording; 24+ hours remaining.

### Dark pools & short selling
- **Dark pool conflicts** [13:34–14:48]: Dark pools operated by institutional brokers routing their own asset managers' orders create conflicts of interest; should require separation of broker and exchange operator roles.
- **Short selling & supply** [26:05–27:40]: Short selling is important for price discovery but excessive failures to deliver and synthetic shares increase supply, depressing prices; regulators underestimate this impact.

### Ad-tech monopoly analogy
- **Google/Facebook ad exchanges** [28:08–32:07]: Similar structure to PFOF—Google and Facebook operate exchanges, buy-side brokers, sell-side brokers, and trading desks with zero transparency; proposed bill would break up monopolies, require duty of best execution (order-by-order standard), and ensure data ownership.
- **Bipartisan support** [31:47]: Bill co-sponsored by Senator Mike Lee (R-UT) and Amy Klobuchar (D-MN).

### Cryptocurrency & blockchain
- **Bitcoin & inflation hedge** [37:13–38:28]: Bitcoin as potential long-term store of value; highly correlated to equities; appeals to those concerned about inflation.
- **Avalanche & proof-of-stake** [38:59–41:28]: Innovative consensus algorithm; subnets allow regulated systems on-chain; cheap, fast, decentralized; top-4 fee generation behind Bitcoin, Ethereum, Binance; potential basis for disintermediated financial system (mortgages, tokenization, settlement).
- **NFTs as art medium** [42:12–45:16]: NFTs are a new art form; dismissal of them is similar to early rejection of pop art and street art; crypto rediscovering securities law (Luna/UST, depository insurance).
- **Terminal platform** [47:46–48:04]: Dave's data platform opening to beta waitlist in weeks; aims to teach retail how to use datasets and build long-term wealth.
