# Selling Far Out Of The Money Options - Watch This

## Summary

This video challenges the common misconception that selling far out-of-the-money (OTM) options is a simple path to consistent profits. Using a 2021 case study with 5-delta puts and calls, the analysis reveals that while such trades can show a high win rate, cumulative P&L performance was poor—illustrating the critical gap between probability of success and actual profitability.

## Key takeaways

- **High probability ≠ high profit** [00:00–00:27]: Selling 5-delta puts and calls appears to offer "super high probability" of profit, but the case study showed three losing trades among many winners yet "did terribly" on cumulative P&L.
- **Win rate is not the full story** [00:27–00:52]: A portfolio of mostly winning trades can still produce negative returns if losses are large relative to gains—a classic asymmetry in short-premium strategies.
- **Manual backtesting reveals hidden costs** [00:27]: Clicking through 2021 data in Thinkorswim on-demand exposed the gap between theoretical edge and realized performance.

## Regime / context

This analysis is grounded in 2021 market data. The lesson—that high-probability trades can mask unfavorable risk/reward—applies across regimes but is especially relevant in low-volatility environments where far-OTM sellers face tail risk concentration.
