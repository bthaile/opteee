# High Probability of Profit Guaranteed to Make Money?

## Summary

High probability of profit (PoP) is a misleading metric that does not guarantee profitability. A strategy can have a very high PoP and still lose money over time. Understanding **expectancy**—how a strategy performs across a large sample size—is essential to evaluating true edge, not just the statistical likelihood of a small win.

## Key takeaways

- **PoP is incomplete [00:00]**: High probability of profit alone does not guarantee money-making; it is only one component of a larger profitability formula.
- **Expectancy over PoP [00:00]**: The critical metric is how a strategy performs when deployed repeatedly over a long period, building a statistically significant sample size.
- **High PoP + losses [00:00]**: You can sell high-PoP spreads on high implied volatility and still lose money overall if the strategy's expectancy is negative.
- **Sample size matters [00:00]**: A single trade or small sample of trades with high PoP is not evidence of edge; larger sample sizes reveal true strategy performance.
