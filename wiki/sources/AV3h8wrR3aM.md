# Stop Guessing: Master Trading Improvement with After Action Reviews (AAR)

## Summary

After Action Reviews (AARs) are a methodical framework for iteratively improving trading performance by comparing expected outcomes to actual results and identifying areas to sustain or improve. The process requires establishing clear goals, developing relevant metrics, identifying benchmarks, tracking performance via trade logs, and conducting regular reviews at portfolio, strategy, and macro-trend levels. This disciplined approach transforms vague feedback loops into actionable intelligence that drives long-term profitability.

## Key takeaways

- **AAR framework fundamentals** [00:00–02:44]: AARs provide a methodical structure to identify what to sustain and what to improve; most traders fail because they rely on half-hearted memory-based lookbacks instead of systematic review.

- **Establish goals first** [02:44–04:10]: Goals must be observable and relevant to your capability—not random targets. Anchor to benchmarks like the risk-free rate or S&P average return, then define whether you're optimizing for returns, consistency, or drawdown reduction.

- **Develop metrics tied to goals** [04:10–05:30]: Each goal generates specific metrics; use trade plans and trade logs as the backbone to document rules and quantitative/qualitative feedback. These become the control variables for iterative adjustment.

- **Set a review cadence** [05:30–06:43]: Commit to weekly, monthly, quarterly, or annual reviews as formal appointments with yourself. Frequency and depth scale with your trading commitment level.

- **Three-level AAR analysis** [06:43–09:10]: Conduct reviews at portfolio level, strategy level, and macro-trend level. Focus on statistical analysis (objective truth) while also monitoring execution and collating new research.

- **Population vs. sample segmentation** [09:10–10:30]: Split analysis between the entire lifespan of a strategy (population) and the recent period under review (sample) to identify performance deviations and regime-specific efficacy.

- **Visual performance tracking** [10:30–11:45]: Use rolling P&L plots, average P&L distributions, and P&L frequency histograms (built in Google Sheets or Excel) to spot inflection points where strategy behavior changes. This reveals the iterative tightening of a strategy over time.

- **Macro context via FRED** [11:45–13:00]: Monitor broad economic data (interest rates, inflation) to build a reference-point context window. This informs whether current market regimes match historical conditions and guides portfolio pivots.

- **Iterative lifecycle** [11:45]: Strategies typically start chaotic with fat tails, then tighten and stabilize through the AAR process—this is the entire purpose of disciplined review.
