# Options Trading Course Overview with Euan Sinclair | Money Talks Ep

## Summary

Euan Sinclair discusses the launch of a comprehensive options trading course co-created with James (a professional trader), designed to bridge the gap between theoretical option pricing and practical trading. The course teaches statistically valid trading strategies grounded in volatility phenomena, targeting traders who want to avoid both the "free money" trap of naive premium selling and the over-mathematization of academic approaches. Sinclair emphasizes that the course is expensive but offers genuine value comparable to professional MFE programs, with lifetime access to a closed Slack community for evidence-based discussion.

## Key takeaways

- **Course structure and cost**: $2,995 for 12–16 hours of video lessons, lifetime Slack access, and optional homework problems. First lesson is free to trial. [00:33–00:49]
- **Target audience and prerequisites**: No specific demographic; assumes basic familiarity with options nomenclature (Sinclair recommends reading Natenberg's *Option Volatility and Pricing* or his own *Option Trading* book beforehand). [47:02–47:49]
- **Core philosophy**: Avoid both naive edge-hunting (selling iron condors weekly) and over-reliance on mathematical models; focus instead on supply/demand dislocations and volatility characteristics. [23:35–24:47]
- **Practical vs. theoretical balance**: Course includes real trading examples and decision trees (e.g., "what do you do if this happens?"), not just backtests. Eight strategies covered, all of which Sinclair or James actively trade or would trade under their mandates. [36:05–37:29]
- **Volatility as the core edge**: Understanding implied vs. realized volatility, volatility regimes, and mean reversion is prerequisite to finding edges; theta is not an edge by itself. [28:36–28:58]
- **Backtesting caveats**: Volatility data is sparse (4 years of daily data ≈ 10 regime observations, not 1,000); easy to overfit; course addresses these dangers explicitly. [38:10–38:59]
- **Slack community**: Permanent, closed to course members only; focused on evidence-based trading discussion, not macro opinions or politics. Sinclair maintains old Slack channels indefinitely. [33:28–40:54]
- **SVB and macro context**: Sinclair avoids drawing strong inferences from singular events (SVB ≠ 2008); prefers to trade price action and volatility rather than causality. Regulations (Dodd-Frank) reduce systemic contagion risk. [01:37–05:06]
- **Fed and policy**: Sinclair respects Fed expertise and avoids overconfident macro calls; finds entertainment in Twitter outrage but acknowledges Powell has vastly more data and incentive alignment than retail traders. [17:39–21:06]
