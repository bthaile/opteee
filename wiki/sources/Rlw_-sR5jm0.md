# Tax Section 1256 and 60/40 Tax Treatment | Investor Dojo

## Summary

Section 1256 contracts receive preferential 60/40 tax treatment: 60% taxed at long-term capital gains rates and 40% at ordinary income rates, regardless of holding period. This creates significant tax efficiency for traders in higher brackets trading shorter time frames, particularly when using broad-based index options (SPX, NDX) or futures instead of ETF-based products (SPY, IWM).

## Key takeaways

- **60/40 tax treatment mechanics** [02:06]: For Section 1256 contracts, 60% of gains taxed at long-term capital gains rates (15–20%) and 40% at ordinary rates, resulting in blended rates as low as 27% for top-bracket traders versus 37% ordinary income tax.
- **Broad-based indices vs. ETFs** [05:30]: SPX and NDX (broad-based indices) qualify for 1256 treatment; SPY and IWM (ETFs) do not. SPX is 10× the contract size of SPY, requiring fewer contracts for equivalent exposure.
- **Eligible products** [05:30]: Futures, options on futures, and broad-based index options receive 60/40 treatment. Forex, single-stock options, and ETF options do not.
- **Cash settlement trade-off** [06:31]: Broad-based indices are cash-settled with no physical underlying, limiting assignment-based strategies but maximizing tax efficiency for directional trades.
- **Mark-to-market and wash sales** [08:35]: Section 1256 contracts are marked to market daily; unrealized P&L settles at year-end, and wash-sale rules do not apply.
- **Strategy alignment first** [09:15]: Tax efficiency should not drive strategy choice; focus on profitable strategies first, then optimize tax treatment within that framework.
