# Roaring Kitty Buying GME Calls?

## Summary

Eric analyzes a sharp expansion in GME call volume on June 24–25, 2024, investigating whether Roaring Kitty (DFV) is accumulating calls again. While the implied volatility surface shows meaningful kinks and a pronounced call skew, the transaction sizes and distribution patterns do not align with DFV's historical phasing behavior, suggesting the volume is likely retail-driven rather than a major position accumulation by the meme-stock figurehead.

## Key takeaways

- **Volatility expansion and surface kinks** [01:24–02:10]: Front-month IV jumped ~60 points (142% to 201%), with expected move rising from 2.79% to 3.6%. The IV curve shows unusual "bumpiness" across strikes rather than a smooth smile, signaling heterogeneous market pricing across expiration cycles.

- **Put-side expansion predictable; call-side lagging** [04:45–05:28]: Far out-of-the-money put volatility is expanding as expected, but far OTM call volatility is not keeping pace—a reversal of the earlier pattern where calls were outpacing puts.

- **$20 strike thesis does not hold under scrutiny** [05:50–08:23]: Despite social-media speculation about flooding at the $20 call for July 19, detailed time-and-sales data shows only three small tranches (16, 28, and 1 contract) across expirations. Overall call buying is 35% of volume vs. 35% selling—not centralized at the $20.

- **Transaction sizing inconsistent with DFV's historical pattern** [08:52–12:17]: DFV's last known position (June 10) held ~$6M cash. Even accounting for smaller stakes, the observed $80k in aggregate $20-call purchases today is fragmented across far-apart tranches, unlike his previous phased accumulation behavior.

- **Front expiration (June 28) dominates; unlikely DFV entry point** [12:35–13:22]: ~165,800 of the 277,000 total call volume is in the June 28 expiration (3 days to expiration). High IV makes this expensive; DFV would face rapid theta decay and no time for the position to work—inconsistent with his strategic approach.

- **At-the-money and far-OTM retail activity** [13:42–14:58]: Volume is concentrated at-the-money (24–25 strikes) and at extreme OTM levels (128 strike with 51,000 depth), suggesting retail YOLO activity and misunderstanding of option mechanics rather than institutional accumulation.

- **Gamma ramp building in near-term** [14:01–14:16]: At-the-money calls are not fully hedged by market makers yet; at least 10,000 new contracts opened today, creating potential gamma exposure if price moves.
