# The Institutional Way to Vol Sizing Your Portfolio

## Summary

This video demonstrates a volatility-normalized position-sizing formula used by institutional traders to allocate capital across multiple assets in a portfolio. Rather than splitting capital equally, the method scales position sizes inversely to each asset's realized or expected volatility, ensuring that each position contributes equally to portfolio volatility. The formula: `(target portfolio volatility / asset volatility) × base capital allocation` yields dollar amounts that align with a consistent risk profile across heterogeneous holdings.

## Key takeaways

- **The core formula** [00:00–00:20]: Multiply base capital allocation by target portfolio volatility, then divide by the asset's volatility. This yields the dollar amount to deploy in each position.
- **Practical example** [00:00–00:40]: A $20K portfolio split 50/50 ($10K each) between SPY and TSLA, with 40% target portfolio vol: SPY (30% vol) requires ~$13,333; TSLA (higher vol) requires ~$8,000. The total does not equal $20K because the formula optimizes for volatility parity, not capital parity.
- **Flexibility in implementation** [00:20–00:40]: The sizing is approximate and can be adjusted; the goal is volatility-normalized exposure, not rigid capital matching.
- **Institutional adoption**: This method is less commonly discussed in retail education but reflects how professional portfolio managers equalize risk contribution across uncorrelated or differently-volatile assets.
