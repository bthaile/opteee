# Trading a $25K Portfolio #optionstrading

## Summary

Eric demonstrates portfolio construction for a $25K account using ratio call diagonals as the speculative allocation, emphasizing the critical distinction between capital utilization and actual risk exposure. The strategy incorporates sector rotation principles, deliberately including healthcare exposure (CVS) to offset concentrated technology positioning and capture broad market drift.

## Key takeaways

- **Utilization vs. risk separation** [00:00–00:23]: A $3,500 utilization footprint carries approximately $1,660 in total risk; these are distinct metrics that must be tracked separately to avoid over-leveraging.
- **Ratio call diagonal mechanics** [00:00]: Two ratio call diagonals deployed as the speculative sleeve; requires careful fill management and slippage planning.
- **Sector rotation as portfolio hedge** [00:39–00:57]: Deliberately sizing a small healthcare position (CVS) despite its modest portfolio weight to capture sector outperformance and reduce concentration risk in technology-driven broad market exposure.
- **Position sizing discipline** [00:23]: Account for realistic slippage and fill variability; exact risk figures are approximate and depend on execution quality.
