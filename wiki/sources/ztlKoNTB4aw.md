# Trading Opportunities in Low Volatility

## Summary

Despite S&P 500 implied volatility reaching its lowest percentile (0%) over the past year, a profitable opportunity exists because implied volatility remains elevated relative to realized volatility. This spread represents a volatility risk premium that traders can capture by selling volatility, even in a low-vol regime.

## Key takeaways

- **IV percentile at 0% [00:00]**: Implied volatility in the S&P 500 has not been lower than current levels in the past year, marking an extreme low.
- **IV > RV spread persists [00:23]**: The yellow line (implied vol) remains elevated relative to realized vol, creating a profitable short-volatility opportunity.
- **Risk premium capture [00:23]**: Selling volatility when IV exceeds RV allows traders to harvest the volatility risk premium, even in absolute low-vol environments.
