# Take Smarter Exits On Wrong Trades

## Summary

This video teaches a framework for defining smarter exits by anchoring them to the underlying profit mechanism rather than arbitrary percentage-based rules. By identifying what you're actually trying to profit from—in this case, downside momentum following a technical breakdown—you can construct exits that align with the invalidation of your thesis, not your P&L.

## Key takeaways

- **Define your profit mechanism first** [00:00] — Identify the specific thing you're trying to make money from (e.g., price direction, momentum, mean reversion). This becomes the foundation for all subsequent decisions.
- **Exits should invalidate your thesis, not your account** [00:21] — If your trade idea is based on an index breaking below a 200-day moving average with downside momentum expected, your exit should trigger when price reclaims that moving average—not when you've lost an arbitrary percentage of premium.
- **Decouple exits from credit/premium received** [00:41] — Option value moves based on multiple factors (delta, gamma, vega, theta). Tying your exit to a fixed percentage of credit or premium ignores the actual mechanics driving profitability and leads to poor decision-making.
- **Price action validates or invalidates your edge** [00:21] — The profit mechanism is what matters most; use it as your north star for position management.

## Notable quotes

> "If my idea here is based on an index breaking below a 200-day moving average and then the expectation of momentum continuing to the downside, well, I could probably reasonably deduce that if it goes above that 200-day moving average, should probably get out."
