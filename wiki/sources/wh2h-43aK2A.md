# Initial Outlay - Small Stacks Pt4

## Summary

Eric outlines the initial positions and capital allocation for a small-account portfolio (~$5,000), featuring two active short puts (XLF and Zynga) and a planned LEAP diagonal in Ford. He emphasizes basis reduction, sector diversification, and patient position management as core principles for compounding returns in a capital-constrained environment.

## Key takeaways

- **Short put structure (XLF):** $50 credit collected; $2,400 cash secured; ~31 days to expiration [00:25]
- **Short put structure (Zynga):** $27.85 credit collected; 3.2% return on invested capital for 38 days; deliberate venture outside S&P 500 due to account constraints [00:51, 07:07]
- **LEAP diagonal (Ford):** Planned 1:1 ratio write to cap upside while collecting premium; chosen despite not being Eric's typical preference, driven by small-account position-sizing constraints [01:42, 02:08]
- **Portfolio allocation:** $77 total credit collected; $32.50 cash secured (1.5% return on capital in ~1 month); ~100% capital deployment intended [02:32]
- **Basis reduction priority:** In small accounts, rolling short puts for credit to reduce cost basis takes precedence over early assignment [03:27, 03:53]
- **Sector diversification strategy:** Intentionally building exposure across financials (XLF), tech (Zynga), consumer discretionary/autos (Ford or XLE), and energy to avoid concentration risk [07:50]
- **Position management philosophy:** Hold positions until they can be closed for minimal cost (pennies); avoid forced losses that small accounts cannot absorb [04:39, 06:26]
- **Compounding mindset:** Focus on steady, calculated growth rather than explosive returns; each profitable trade increases principal for the next cycle [05:46]

## Notable quotes

> "My goal is to not take this account and just knock it out of the park try to triple it. I'm trading this account as if I were starting again knowing what I know now, and that includes a very calculated and slow approach that is focused on a good foundation."
