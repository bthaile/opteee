# Move with a purpose #options #trading

## Summary

A foundational lesson on intentional market study and pattern recognition. The core principle: learn and explore markets to a standard, not to a timeline. Subconscious priming through repeated observation is powerful, but must be paired with deliberate, pre-defined analysis frameworks to identify dominant price trends.

## Key takeaways

- **Train to a standard, not a time** [00:00] — The markets don't care how long it takes you to master a concept; speed is irrelevant if the foundation is weak.
- **Leverage subconscious priming** [00:20] — Pattern recognition happens naturally as you observe charts over time; this is a genuine edge if you show up consistently.
- **Be intentional before you look** [00:20] — Define *what* you will analyze and *why* before opening a chart. Avoid random scanning.
- **Example framework: moving averages** [00:20] — Specify three moving averages, study their relationships, slopes, width, and price position relative to them to determine dominant trend direction.
- **Be an outlier through process** [00:41] — Discipline in your analytical approach separates you from undirected market observers.

## Notable quotes
