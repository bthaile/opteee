# What is Portfolio Margin?

## Summary

Portfolio margin is a broker-specific leverage system that calculates margin requirements based on overall portfolio risk rather than individual positions, allowing qualified traders to deploy capital more efficiently. Unlike Regulation T's fixed formulas, portfolio margin uses proprietary stress-test models that account for diversification, implied volatility, and product type—enabling significantly lower margin requirements for well-structured positions while imposing stricter account minimums and qualification standards.

## Key takeaways

- **Margin fundamentals** [00:24]: Margin is leverage—your broker allows you to trade notional value exceeding your account balance or requires a smaller cash reserve than a fully secured position would demand.

- **Initial vs. maintenance margin** [01:15]: Initial margin (typically 50% of purchase price for stocks under Reg T) is the upfront requirement; maintenance margin (SEC minimum 25%, but brokers typically enforce 30–40%) is the ongoing threshold to avoid margin calls.

- **Regulation T vs. portfolio margin** [02:48]: Regulation T uses fixed permutations for all positions; portfolio margin assesses the entire portfolio's risk, treating index products (e.g., [[spy]]) more favorably than concentrated single-stock positions.

- **Portfolio margin eligibility** [04:33]: Requires a standard margin account (not IRA), minimum $125,000 initial capital ($100,000 maintenance), and typically a written qualification test; Regulation T requires only ~$2,000.

- **TD Ameritrade portfolio margin ranges** [05:21]: Uses ±15% stress ranges for stocks/options and ±12%/+10% for broad-based indices; incorporates sticky-strike and sticky-delta IV calculations; wider stress parameters can be enforced at any time.

- **Capital efficiency example** [07:19]: Long 100 shares at $100 requires $5,000 initial margin (Reg T) vs. $1,500 portfolio margin—a 70% reduction.

- **Multi-leg position example** [08:05]: A short [[iron-condor]] shows $50,000 Reg T requirement vs. ~$31,896 portfolio margin, reflecting diversification benefit.

- **Best practices** [08:52]: Avoid "redlining" margin; maintain buffer for buying-power expansion during downturns; ensure solid trading plan before deploying leverage.
