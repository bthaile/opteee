# Using Long Call Options to Trade Nvidia Earnings

## Summary

Eric walks through a profitable long-call earnings trade on Nvidia ahead of the June 22 earnings release, analyzing the bullish call skew in the options market, historical earnings patterns, and the rationale for buying premium into an event expected to see volatility contraction. The position—six 21 June 945 calls purchased for ~$27,000—returned over $30,000 in realized profit through staged exits and post-earnings drift management.

## Key takeaways

- **Pre-earnings skew analysis** [01:09–02:41]: Nvidia showed unusual call skew (62k calls vs. 197k puts in the earnings expiration) despite typical put-heavy positioning, suggesting institutional bullish directional thesis.
- **Historical earnings pattern** [03:29–04:40]: Past 11 earnings showed persistent upside bias—largest down move was 7.6%, while upside moves were "pretty massive." Expected move was ~7.8%.
- **Why buy premium into volatility contraction** [05:40–06:50]: Thesis was that a large enough directional move (8%+ up) would overwhelm the post-earnings IV crush that typically occurs.
- **Delta selection rationale** [08:47–09:28]: Chose 55-delta calls (945 strike, 21 June expiration) to balance dollar-for-dollar move in the underlying with room to compound if the stock continued higher post-earnings.
- **Staged exit strategy** [09:49–11:06]: Sold 4 of 6 calls on May 23 at $8.05 for $47k credit (realized $29k profit on those legs); sold 1 more on May 24 at $11.24 for $112k credit (cumulative $37.4k profit).
- **Ratio diagonal setup** [11:32–12:32]: Kept 1 runner completely paid for; planned to set stop at 1020 (previous support) and potentially convert to ratio call diagonal if stock continued higher.
- **Portfolio context** [12:52–13:10]: Position was small allocation (~$26k on much larger portfolio); not recommended as 10% bet.

## Notable quotes

> "I was expecting a large enough move to overwhelm the contraction in volatility—that's what this trade was based on." [06:50]

> "I wasn't fully confident like our standard Wall Street bets degenerates that we necessarily would see the move immediately after earnings. Part of me was preparing for a reasonable earnings move but then might see some positive drift afterwards." [07:58]
