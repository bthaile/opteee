# Best Timing to Roll Options

## Summary

The optimal time to roll a challenged short option position is when it reaches at-the-money, where extrinsic value is maximized and intrinsic value is minimal. Rolling too late forces you to buy back deep intrinsic value with no credit, making adjustments progressively harder. The decision hinges on balancing thesis conviction, inventory tolerance, and the trade-off between capturing remaining extrinsic decay versus securing a better basis adjustment early.

## Key takeaways

- **Roll at-the-money for maximum optionality** [00:00] — ATM positions retain the most extrinsic value and offer the best roll mechanics; waiting risks the underlying moving against you and forcing a costly deep-ITM buyback.
- **Intrinsic value is a one-way cost** [00:18] — Once your short put falls deeper ITM, you accumulate intrinsic value on the buyback with no credit received; this makes rolling progressively harder the further ITM you go.
- **Balance recovery hope against liquidity decay** [00:40] — Weigh waiting for an underlying recovery (to avoid buying back extrinsic value) against rolling early to lock in a better basis adjustment and avoid running out of expiration liquidity.
- **Thesis and inventory drive the decision** [00:56] — The timing choice depends entirely on your conviction in the underlying's direction and your comfort with current position size; these factors determine whether you take the roll early or wait for a bounce.

## Notable quotes

> "The best time to roll in terms of the most choices you're going to have if your position is being challenged is when you are at the money because you're still at the most extrinsic value and you don't have a lot of intrinsic value." [00:00]
