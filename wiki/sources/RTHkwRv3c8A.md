# Rejected CEO Tom Sosnoff's Bold Move | The Outlier Podcast

## Summary

Tom Sosnoff recounts his pitch to run Interactive Brokers globally after the company's acquisition of Tastyworks, and his subsequent rejection by the board. After being told the acquirer wanted the company but not him in leadership, he negotiated a transition to focus on his own projects before eventually exiting four years into the deal.

## Key takeaways

- **Leadership ambition vs. acquisition reality** [00:00–00:20]: Sosnoff approached the IG board with a case to run the company globally, believing he understood the business better than existing leadership. The board rejected the proposal.
- **Negotiation outcome** [00:20–00:36]: After rejection, Sosnoff negotiated a reduced role, stepping back from US operations to pursue his own initiatives rather than accept a diminished position.
- **Exit timing** [00:36–00:53]: Four years into the post-acquisition period, Sosnoff decided to move on, having gained the ability to liquidate stock and transition cleanly.

## Notable quotes

> "I think I should run this company because I'm a better CEO than the people that you have and I understand this business better than anybody at this company."

> "We want your company, but we don't want you to run it."
