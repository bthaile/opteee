# Follow any Trend with Market Wizard Tom Basso | The Outlier Podcast Ep46

## Summary

Tom Basso, a 50-year veteran trader and author of *The All-Weather Trader*, discusses his multi-market trend-following approach across 26 futures markets and sector ETFs. He emphasizes position-sizing discipline, trailing stops scaled to volatility (ATR), and a nine-strategy portfolio that automatically transitions between long and short exposure without prediction. With markets showing early cracks—his NQ (Nasdaq futures) indicator already short while sector timing remains 27/30 long—Basso walks through his hedging mechanics, the Fed's policy bind, and thematic opportunities in energy, crypto, and commodities if the unwind accelerates.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2023-08-19)

- **Market transition underway** [00:27–01:17]: Stock market rally has surprised consensus bearish predictions; Basso removed hedges in January/February and ran nearly fully invested. Now seeing early cracks: nine-day NQ indicator already short, sector timing down to 27/30 long (from 30/30).
- **Treasury yields and bond headwinds** [05:54–06:43]: 30-year yields up ~500 bps from early July (~3.8% to ~4.3%). Bonds have had 40 years of tailwinds since 1980; now facing headwinds. Basso expects sticky inflation and rates to stay elevated or rise further; bonds unattractive except short-term (≤1 year) for cash parking.
- **Fed policy corner** [08:17–10:56]: Fed tightening cycle unprecedented; Basso expects them to be late on both sides—too slow to fight inflation, then too aggressive and forced to reverse. Simultaneously raising rates and shrinking balance sheet creates double headwind to growth. Congress/administration also anti-growth; recipe for eventual recession.
- **Economic resilience masking fragility** [11:48–13:15]: Wage and CPI data show continued strength, but Basso notes habitual spending patterns take time to break. A 40% stock market decline over two weeks could shock behavior rapidly; panic spreads instantly via social media.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Nine-strategy portfolio structure** [03:00–03:49]: Basso runs nine independent strategies with preset allocations. Each has its own risk budget (e.g., 0.5% entry, 1% max per position in futures). Strategies span different timeframes: two-to-three-day, nine-day (NQ), and longer-term sector timing.
- **Position-sizing discipline** [14:32–16:06]: Core risk control via position sizing, not market timing. Allows positions to grow to 1% of portfolio via profits, then peels off contracts to reset to 1% allowable risk. As markets transition, position sizes naturally shrink without forced liquidation.
- **Volatility-scaled trailing stops** [16:34–17:20]: Uses ATR (average true range) to set stops at 1–2× volatility away from price. As market rallies, stops move up; as volatility increases, stop distance widens. Stops reverse to short entry on breach. Keeps portfolio "mixed bag"—never all long or all short.
- **Sector ETF hedging** [25:51–28:24]: Rather than hedge individual stocks (tax/liquidity issues), uses 30 sector ETFs (energy, tech, bonds, international, small-cap, etc.). When exposure drops (e.g., 21/30 long), calculates net delta and shorts ES (S&P 500 futures) in one trade to neutralize. Avoids wash-sale complications and provides single-point liquidity.
- **Correlation creep and index dominance** [30:21–31:10]: Global stock correlations have risen from ~60% in late 1970s to 85%+ today due to proliferation of broad-based index funds. True diversification in long-only stock portfolios increasingly difficult; sector/index hedges more effective.
- **Profitability tracking** [24:37–25:01]: Maintains running spreadsheet of daily P&L over trailing year. Targets >50% profitable days; focuses on making winning days larger than losing days. Currently running ~51% profitable days.
- **Consistency over leverage** [23:45–24:13]: Deliberately controls volatility exposure to avoid stress and maintain 50-year consistency. Prefers "Mr. Serenity" approach (diversified, mixed daily outcomes) over high-leverage/high-stress trading.

- **Trend-following across 26 markets** [00:27–00:52]: Trades metals, currencies, ag products, meats, softs, and equities. Early 2023 saw commodity strength (inflation, drought); equity rally surprised consensus recession/rate-hike bears.
- **No prediction, just price action** [01:45–02:33]: Refuses to predict market direction. Consensus was wrong (expected recession or rate-hike pain); Basso simply followed price, removed hedges, and rode the rally. Applies same process daily regardless of macro narrative.
- **Thematic opportunities on unwind** [18:19–20:06]: If Fed unwind accelerates: crypto futures (high volatility = trader's edge), silver (inflation play), energy (unleaded, natural gas, crude—upside if inflation persists; downside if recession kills demand, as in COVID). Caveat: oil can crash to negative if storage fills.
- **Small-cap thesis** [33:19–35:54]: Small caps have agility but lack staying power of large-cap competitors. Best opportunities are niche/innovative small caps (e.g., Zoom during COVID) that become household names, not generic competitors. Larger companies preferred for defensive positioning.
- **Orange juice futures** [36:18–38:27]: Two structural headwinds: Florida winter freezes kill 10–20% of trees periodically; rising property values near Orlando incentivize farmers to sell land and retire rather than replant. Long-term supply uncertainty creates volatility; good trend-following vehicle both up and down.

## Notable quotes

When you can control your position sizes and risk and wash, rinse, and repeat over and over again for the last 50 years, it becomes about as easy as breathing." [02:33]

> "I don't have to predict it, it just sort of happens naturally." [22:05]
