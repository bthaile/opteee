# GameStop and eBay Acquisition What the Options Markets Say | The Options Trench

## Summary

Ryan Cohen's unsolicited bid to acquire eBay with GameStop—a smaller company with ~$11B market cap attempting to buy a much larger target for ~$56B—presents a fascinating case study in M&A mechanics and options market structure. The deal is half cash (from GameStop's $9B balance sheet and a $20B TD financing letter) and half stock, leaving a significant funding gap. The options markets reveal heavy call skew, steep volatility term structure, and unusual open-interest density at far out-of-the-money strikes, reflecting both the deal's uncertainty and GameStop's historical volatility premium.

## Key takeaways

### Deal structure & financing
- **GameStop market cap ~$11B; eBay target price ~$56B** [04:45–05:41]. Deal is 50% cash, 50% stock; GameStop has $9B cash on balance sheet, TD provides $20B "highly confident" (not committed) letter, leaving ~$16B gap to be filled via additional stock issuance and/or other financing.
- **Both stocks rallied on announcement (May 1)** [12:49–13:17]. eBay up ~10% (unusual for a 40 vol stock), GameStop down <3% (roughly one standard deviation). Atypical for M&A: normally acquirer sells off, target rallies. Suggests market pricing in combined-entity upside or skepticism about deal completion.
- **Cohen's activist thesis** [17:11–18:02]. Targets excessive sales & marketing spend, notes eBay leadership hasn't bought stock in years, positions acquisition as cost-cutting + revenue acceleration play leveraging GameStop's collectibles focus.

### Options market structure
- **Call-to-put open interest ratio 2–3:1** [19:07–19:31]. Unusually skewed toward calls; reflects GameStop's $9B cash buffer (downside truncated) and recent profitability flip, making puts "totally uninteresting" [20:20–21:08].
- **Steep call skew, 20 vol points OTM** [22:43–23:06]. 25-delta calls trade ~20 vols higher than ATM; steeper than typical but not as extreme as during Roaring Kitty episodes. Reflects tail-risk premium on upside moves.
- **Ascending volatility term structure (contango)** [26:46–27:46]. Front month ~40th percentile IV; December ~55 vol. Steep upward slope suggests longer-dated vol is rich relative to near-term; typical for names with event risk (deal uncertainty).
- **Dense OI at far OTM strikes** [34:34–37:30]. June 50-strike calls opened May 4 (day after deal announcement); January 2025 50-strikes carry 36K OI. Suggests speculative positioning on tail upside; benchmark expirations (June, January) have been listed longer and accumulated volume.

### Volatility & skew interpretation
- **Put skew is flat/normal** [28:12–28:37]. Puts are cheap relative to equity history; calls expensive. Reflects market consensus: downside limited by cash, upside optionality from deal/Cohen's next move.
- **Realized vol context** [26:46]. One-month IV ~40th percentile historically; stock 52-week range ~79%, suggesting current vol is not extreme on a relative basis.

### Trade mechanics & strategy appeal
- **Covered call / covered strangle** [38:45–42:26]. Selling 30-strike calls (16 delta, ~40¢ premium, ~2% monthly yield) against long stock at $24 offers 24% annualized return if stock stays ≥$20. Caps upside but compensates for truncated downside via cash buffer.
- **Call spreads are cheap** [24:22–25:08]. Steep skew makes vertical spreads attractive; e.g., buying 20/25 call spread for ~$2 when stock at $23.75 offers 2:1 payoff by expiration if stock holds.
- **Ratio considerations** [39:54–40:13]. Host runs covered strangle at sub-1:1 call ratio to preserve upside during GME surges; trend-following adjustments capture post-spike pullbacks.

### Relative value & framework
- **Absolute vs. relative vol lens** [45:21–47:08]. GME is small-cap, idiosyncratic; not traded for correlation. Evaluate skew/term structure vs. own history AND vs. broader market. GME's call skew and term structure are rich but not extreme; covered-call risk/reward "doesn't look bad" given cash buffer and fundamental stabilization.
- **Prediction market odds** [53:00–54:11]. Polymarket shows ~26% probability GameStop acquires eBay this year; variance between Polymarket and Kalshi suggests cross-exchange arbitrage opportunity (low volume).

## Notable quotes
