# July Options Expiration Week and GME Update

## Summary

Eric provides a broad market outlook for the week ahead, highlighting Powell's speech and retail sales data as key catalysts, then pivots to a detailed GameStop options analysis ahead of the July 19 expiration. He examines unusual call positioning in the 30-strike, discusses volatility skew dynamics, and speculates on potential correlation with Richard Newton's August thesis and historical Roaring Kitty positioning patterns.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2024-07-14)

- **Powell speaking tomorrow (July 15); retail sales on July 16** — Fed pivot expectations have shifted from March → end-of-year → now early September [05:36]
- **Broad market volatility muted** — implied vol at 11.5–12% for front expiration, 10–11% for next cycle; market not pricing this week as high-impact [06:38]

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Front expiration (July 19) is standard Opex, not weekly** — liquidity concentrates here; more impactful than weeklys [07:55]
- **Call/put open interest skew** — 762k calls vs. 456k puts; 333k of calls in July 19 expiration vs. only 142k in August 16 [08:49]
- **Unusual 30-strike call positioning** — ~5,000 contracts (≈$250k notional) built on Friday with 10 days to expiration; not Roaring Kitty scale but material [09:14]
- **Volatility smile behavior** — 25-delta OTM calls creeping up in IV; call IV still lower than put IV in near term (inverted from prior aggressive upside moves); August 16 call IV slightly elevated vs. OTM puts [13:19]
- **Downside bias in vol pricing** — far OTM puts gaining IV heavily; options market still pricing downside risk for this cycle [14:29]
