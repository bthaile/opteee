# Does technical analysis work? #options #trading #optionstrading

## Summary

Technical analysis is a valid probabilistic tool for assessing market behavior, not a magic predictor. It works because price action reflects the actual supply-and-demand footprint of market participants, and institutional money moves slowly enough that patterns become observable and actionable.

## Key takeaways

- **Technical analysis as probability, not prediction** [00:00] — A stock falling below its 50-day moving average is *more likely* to continue lower, but not guaranteed; the edge is probabilistic, not deterministic.
- **Price action = market footprint** [00:24] — Technical analysis observes actual supply-and-demand behavior; it's the recorded behavior of institutions and retail traders in real time.
- **Institutional constraints create exploitable patterns** [00:46] — Large money cannot move quickly; building positions takes multiple days, making their accumulation/distribution visible to technical analysts.
- **No magic, but real utility** [00:00] — Like any analytical framework, technical analysis has no predictive guarantee, but it provides a legitimate edge when applied with proper probabilistic thinking.

## Notable quotes

> "Technical analysis is very useful for a probabilistic assessment of things."

> "It's literally just observing price behavior—this is the actual supply and demand function of people going in the market and doing things; it's their footprint."
