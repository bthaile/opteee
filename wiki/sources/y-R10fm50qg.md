# Give Yourself Better Feedback To Improve Your Trades

## Summary

This video emphasizes the importance of separating process feedback from P&L outcomes when building a trading edge. By structuring your trade log to capture signals and thesis inputs separately from realized moves, you can generate meaningful feedback that P&L alone cannot provide—especially critical in short-term timeframes where luck can mask poor decision-making.

## Key takeaways

- **Isolate signals from outcomes** [00:00] — Create a dedicated section in your trade log for signals and thesis inputs, making it easy to run data analysis on the inputs themselves, not just the final P&L.
- **P&L is misleading in short timeframes** [00:28] — A profitable trade that resulted from poor process gives you no feedback on your actual ability; repeating that flawed process will eventually degrade performance.
- **Process feedback matters more than luck** [00:50] — Distinguish between "this trade made money" and "my decision-making was sound." The latter is what compounds over time.

## Notable quotes

"Your P&L is not the only feedback that matters. It's the one that we ultimately prioritize for sure, but in short-term time frames, it can be really, really misleading."
