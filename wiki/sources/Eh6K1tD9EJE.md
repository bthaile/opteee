# How Far to Roll Short Options?

## Summary

Eric discusses the tactical decision of how far forward to roll short options positions when managing adverse price moves. The core principle: minimize time-roll distance to preserve flexibility and avoid getting locked into distant expirations where capital sits idle and shorter-term opportunities are missed.

## Key takeaways

- **Minimize roll distance in time** [00:00–00:22]: When rolling short options, prefer rolling out the smallest number of days possible (e.g., 7 days rather than 28) to avoid cascading into far-dated expirations (76, 88, 105 DTE).
- **Opportunity cost of distant rolls** [00:22–00:44]: Rolling too far forward locks capital into a single position, forcing you to "sit and wait" rather than deploy into shorter-term opportunities.
- **The bridge-burning problem** [00:44]: Once you exhaust nearby expirations (e.g., rolling past 22 May), the next available roll may be weeks away (18 June), creating a forced holding period with no intermediate exit.
- **Delicate balance** [00:44]: Rolling strategy requires balancing the need to manage losses against the cost of being overcommitted to a single trade.

## Notable quotes

> "My goal is to go as little far in time as I can."

> "I much prefer to try and wait as long as I can. There's a delicate dance."
