# Market Rebounding | GME, CRWD, SIGA, CHWY

## Summary

Market indices show signs of recovery following strong retail sales data (1% MoM vs. 0.4% expected), with SPY and QQQ breaking above recent gap levels while Russell 2000 lags. Fed probability shifts toward 25bp cut in September. Individual stock analysis covers a small-cap news-driven loss (SIGA), GameStop consolidation with deepening options positioning, CrowdStrike's rubber-band recovery, and Chewy's bullish drift ahead of earnings.

## Key takeaways

- **Retail sales beat signals economic resilience** [00:41]: 1% MoM vs. 0.4% prior and −0.2% prior-prior; largest expansion in ~18 months; supports continued large-cap momentum.
- **Fed cut probability recalibrates** [02:22]: 100% probability of September 18 cut remains, but 25bp now favored over 50bp as economy shows less urgency for drastic action.
- **Index realignment underway** [01:26]: SPY and QQQ closing gaps and approaching previous highs; IWM (Russell 2000) still lagging, not yet closed initial drop gap.
- **Breadth improving** [03:23]: 66%+ of S&P 500 stocks above 50-day moving average; VIX contracting—fading volatility remains a reliable trade.
- **SIGA news-driven loss** [05:04]: Entered ~10.60, opened gap-down to ~7, closed ~9.62 (−$1 from entry, −19% from prior close). Flattened half position; thesis intact but illustrates market indifference to directional conviction.
- **GME consolidating with deepening options** [07:02]: Hovering ~$22; call OI 738k vs. put OI 350k (2:1 ratio typical for GME). New activity in Jan 2025 expirations suggests longer-term institutional positioning; front expiration (Aug 16) still dominant but rotation building.
- **GME volume and catalyst watch** [10:27]: Current volume quiet (104k vs. 29k short); 300k OI in front expiration expires tomorrow. Watch where it rotates—earnings on Sept 4 or next primary cycle. 13-step volatility surface shows disagreement (113 vol vs. 86 vol in further expirations).
- **CrowdStrike rubber-band recovery** [12:44]: News-driven selloff now reversing; breaking above $243 and $255 handles; moving averages catching up underneath. Earnings upcoming but downside likely priced in; looks like positive recovery play.
- **Chewy bullish drift** [14:04]: 276k calls vs. 194k puts; options spread across expirations (Aug 16 through Sept 20); earnings Aug 28 priced at 101% vol on 30-day cycle. Above all moving averages and point of control; next volume node serves as support.
