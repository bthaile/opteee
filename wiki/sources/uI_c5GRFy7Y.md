# Markets are NOT 100% Efficient

## Summary

While markets are generally efficient, clear inefficiencies exist that can be traced to structural reasons rather than random noise. These inefficiencies—such as window-dressing effects at month-end—create exploitable opportunities for traders who understand their underlying causes.

## Key takeaways

- **Market efficiency is not absolute** [00:00] — inefficiencies exist but are traceable to specific structural reasons, not randomness.
- **Window-dressing as a structural effect** [00:33] — hedge funds and institutional players liquidate losing positions at month-end to improve portfolio appearance, creating predictable price patterns.
- **Inefficiencies create edge** — understanding *why* inefficiencies exist is the foundation for identifying tradeable opportunities.
