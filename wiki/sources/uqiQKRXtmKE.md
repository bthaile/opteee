# Credit Doesn't Equal Income in Covered Calls

## Summary

A critical distinction in covered-call mechanics: premium received is not income available for withdrawal. When you sell a call against long shares, the credit sits in your cash account but represents a liability (the short call obligation). Withdrawing that premium while maintaining the position creates margin pressure and potential capital shortfalls.

## Key takeaways

- **Premium ≠ income**: The $623 credit from selling a call is a liability offset, not spendable income [00:00]
- **Liability side of the trade**: An open short call remains a live obligation even though it's covered by shares [00:28]
- **Margin implications**: Systematically selling calls against long equity while withdrawing premium can exhaust cash reserves and trigger margin requirements [00:50]
- **Intent matters**: Only if you plan to let shares be called away at expiration can you safely treat the premium as realized income [00:28]
