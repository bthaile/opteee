# Why a High Win Rate Can Make YOU Broke #optionstrading

## Summary

A high win rate alone does not guarantee profitability in options trading. This video demonstrates the critical distinction between win rate and expected return: a strategy with 95% winning trades averaging $100 gain can still produce negative P&L if the 5% losing trades average $2,000 losses. Expected return—the product of win rate, average win, and average loss—is the true measure of strategy viability.

## Key takeaways

- **Win rate is incomplete without loss magnitude** [00:00] — 95 winning trades at $100 average gain plus 5 losing trades at $2,000 average loss = –$500 total P&L, despite 95% win rate.
- **Expected return is the metric that matters** [00:31] — win rate by itself tells you almost nothing about profitability; you must factor in average win size, average loss size, and frequency.
- **Don't be seduced by high win-rate marketing** [00:31] — many trading systems advertise win rates without disclosing the asymmetry between average winners and average losers.
