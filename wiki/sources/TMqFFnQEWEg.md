# Understanding Gamma in #optionstrading

## Summary

Gamma is a nonlinear Greek that measures the rate of change of delta with respect to moves in the underlying. It is highest for at-the-money options and decreases as you move deeper in or out of the money. Gamma provides the convexity characteristic of options, determining how much delta changes per point move in the underlying, and its behavior varies significantly with time to expiration.

## Key takeaways

- **Gamma is nonlinear and moneyness-dependent** [00:00]: Gamma peaks at the money and declines as you move in or out of the money; deep ITM and deep OTM options have substantially lower gamma.
- **Gamma cuts in half moving ITM** [00:15]: At 4 days to expiration, the 50-delta ATM gamma is quite high, but drops roughly 50% as you move in the money, and continues to decline further as you go deeper ITM.
- **Gamma flattens with time decay** [00:25]: As you move further out in time (longer DTE), the gamma curve flattens; ATM gamma remains the highest point on the curve but the overall shape becomes less pronounced.
- **Gamma provides option convexity** [00:05]: Gamma is the mechanism that gives options their convex payoff—it determines how much delta moves per point move in the underlying.

## Notable quotes

- "Gamma is completely nonlinear. It's the highest for the at the money options. It gets lower when you're deep in the money or deep out of the money."
