# One MASSIVE Oversight With Covered Calls #optionstrading

## Summary

This video highlights a critical risk in covered-call strategies: loss of control over your underlying shares once calls are sold. The host emphasizes that while early assignment is technically low-probability absent dividends exceeding extrinsic value, retail traders often underestimate the psychological and operational cost of surrendering discretion over their position.

## Key takeaways

- **Never sell calls against stock you don't want to sell.** [00:00] Doing so sets up the trade for failure because assignment—including early assignment—is a real possibility and removes your control.
- **Early assignment risk is highest around dividends.** [00:23] Assignment becomes more likely when a dividend payment exceeds the remaining extrinsic value of the short call.
- **You lose full control of your shares the moment you sell the call.** [00:41] While defensive strategies exist to keep shares in most cases, the fundamental trade-off is surrendering discretion over exit timing and price.
- **Retail traders often exercise calls suboptimally.** [00:23] Random assignment can occur when traders don't realize the move is suboptimal, highlighting the importance of understanding counterparty behavior.

## Notable quotes

> "As soon as you sell that call, you are giving away control of your stock." [00:41]
