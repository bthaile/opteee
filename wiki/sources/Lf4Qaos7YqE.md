# Handling Criticism as a Public CEO Tom Sosnoff | The Outlier Podcast

## Summary

[[tom-sosnoff]] discusses his approach to handling public criticism as a prominent entrepreneur and CEO of an online brokerage. He emphasizes that negative feedback has minimal impact on him, and counters detractors by challenging them to name competing CEOs who are as accessible and responsive to customer concerns.

## Key takeaways

- **Accessibility as competitive advantage** [00:27] — Sosnoff positions direct CEO responsiveness to customer feedback as a differentiator; he challenges critics to find another online brokerage CEO willing to engage directly with complaints.
- **Emotional resilience to criticism** [00:00] — Despite a public profile, Sosnoff reports a ~95–98% approval rating and states that negative feedback does not affect him emotionally.
- **Turning critics into advocates** [00:48] — Sosnoff notes that when he responds to criticism with facts and engagement, many detractors acknowledge his point and retract their complaints.

## Notable quotes

> "Name me one other CEO of an online brokerage firm. Just name me one. You can't even name one."

> "I will write to you and you won't believe how many times people have written back and said, 'Okay, you win. You're right. Nobody else will ever write back to me.'"
