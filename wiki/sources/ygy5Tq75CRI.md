# SpaceX IPO: Why This Is Just the Beginning

## Summary

SpaceX completed the largest public offering in history on Friday, raising $75 billion at an offer price of $135, closing at $160.95 (up 19%) and immediately entering the $2 trillion valuation club. The IPO demonstrated orderly price discovery despite massive scale, with the company offering only 4.2% float while maintaining founder control. The key narrative shifts from scarcity-driven hype to fundamental supply-demand dynamics as secondary unlocks and options trading commence.

## Key takeaways

- **IPO mechanics & orderly execution** [00:47–01:34]: Market makers require time to assess supply/demand before open to prevent jagged, disorderly trading. SpaceX had an unusually orderly day with minimal limit-up/limit-down moves, contrary to expectations.

- **Valuation & scale context** [01:54–02:26]: At offer price, SpaceX valued at ~$1.7 trillion; by close, ~$2 trillion (just shy of Amazon). Only 4.2% float offered, yet raised 2.5× the prior largest IPO benchmark. Only seven US companies in the trillion-dollar club; SpaceX entered at IPO.

- **Scarcity as demand driver** [03:00–03:26]: Low float + high demand = extreme scarcity. Retail saturation (e.g., family members requesting shares) signals early-stage demand. Future secondary unlocks will be major supply events.

- **First-day performance vs. historical median** [04:13–06:08]: 19% first-day gain beat ~62% of 50 major US IPOs (2015–2026). Impressive given already-massive valuation; unlike FIG or other high-upside IPOs, SpaceX had limited room to run.

- **Governance & founder control** [06:33–06:59]: Elon maintained tight control of voting structure, learning from past governance disputes. Supply dynamics differ from typical IPOs due to concentrated ownership.

- **Next catalysts: supply unlocks, earnings, options** [07:29–08:23]: Hype will fade; fundamentals take over. Secondary unlocks and rebalancing will test supply-demand equilibrium. Options opening on June 16th will add new price-discovery mechanism.
