# Learning About Roaring Kitty & GME | GameStop Analysis Live Ep4

## Summary

Eric hosts a solo learning session on GameStop and the broader ape community, diving into Roaring Kitty's analytical methods, the mechanics of FTDs and ETF creation/redemption cycles, and the recent Chewy position acquisition. The stream emphasizes objective data analysis over pattern-seeking, examines options positioning ahead of the 16 August expiration, and explores the capital mechanics behind Roaring Kitty's reported positions.

## Key takeaways

- **Roaring Kitty's analytical toolkit** [13:04–23:30]: Roaring Kitty uses a multi-spreadsheet system to track fundamentals, universe data, and submissions; he manually inputs data but automates key inputs to remain close to information while maintaining efficiency—a common practice among professional traders.

- **FTD and ETF mechanics are community-specific knowledge** [02:08–02:28]: Most market participants don't understand FTDs or ETF creation/redemption cycles; these concepts are highly developed within the GameStop community and worth studying via objective sources.

- **Reddit DD has mixed quality; pattern-seeking is rampant** [03:57–05:36]: While Reddit due diligence contains valuable data points, it often exhibits overfitting, pattern-seeking, and cognitive bias. No single comprehensive DD fully connects all dots; people are incentivized to guess frequently because one correct call erases prior misses.

- **Options skew signals momentum readiness** [48:22–51:20]: Before the May move, GME showed ~4:1 call-to-put OI skew; currently at ~1.6:1, indicating less bullish positioning. Monthly expirations (16 Aug) are busier than weeklies due to liquidity; the 125 strike shows standard retail clustering.

- **Bid-ask spread widening during volatility is a real cost** [36:19–37:40]: Even when a long call position gains value during a sharp move, liquidity dries up dramatically (spreads widen from ~$0.30 to $1.45), making exit difficult at fair prices—a key reason to mix options with shares.

- **Chewy position capital mechanics remain unclear** [01:15:08–01:22:31]: If Roaring Kitty acquired 9.1M Chewy shares at ~$25.30 (≈$227.7M), the capital source is ambiguous given his reported $6.3M cash on 13 June. Options could theoretically generate the difference, but probability is low; retirement accounts or other holdings are speculative.

- **Correlation analysis requires objectivity; cost ≠ GME** [01:02:08–01:09:13]: While cost and GME are both consumer discretionary and may share ETF baskets, their price charts show sporadic correlation (3-day: 60%, 5-day: −5%, 10-day: 53%, 30-day: 3%). Sector overlap and ETF redemption cycles explain some movement, but claiming identical charts is pattern-seeking.

- **Emoji interpretation is low-probability speculation** [01:40:44–01:48:38]: Roaring Kitty's emoji posts (12 May) are unlikely to encode specific future trades (e.g., 16 Aug calls, Chewy entry on 24 June). While he does use memes intentionally, the combined intellect of the community can over-interpret simple symbols; Ryan Cohen's interview confirms not all details carry hidden meaning.

- **VWAP orders and institutional order types** [01:52:49–01:57:53]: VWAP (volume-weighted average price) and TWAP (time-weighted average price) are used by institutions to manage entry prices and avoid front-running in dark pools. The theory that a large VWAP order triggered the Chewy move is plausible but requires more specificity on timing and execution.

- **Siri merger has been public since December 2023** [02:07:31–02:12:03]: The Siri-Xm merger announcement predates Roaring Kitty's May emoji post by months; options activity in merger-arbitrage plays is predictable and not evidence of foreknowledge. Twitter's buyout cycle illustrates how merger uncertainty creates price volatility.
