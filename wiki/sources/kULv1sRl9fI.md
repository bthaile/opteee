# Wealth Through Real Estate w/ David Pere - Military to Millionaire | The Outlier Podcast Ep63

## Summary

David Pere, founder of Military to Millionaire, discusses his progression from military service to building a diversified real estate portfolio spanning residential, commercial, and alternative assets. The conversation covers creative financing strategies (seller financing, private money, lease options), market selection criteria, and the operational leverage available in commercial real estate compared to residential. Pere emphasizes that real estate's forgiving nature—combined with strategic use of leverage and off-market deal sourcing—makes it accessible even with minimal capital.

## Key takeaways

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Rent-to-price ratio as a baseline filter** [12:32]: Target markets where average rent equals at least 1% of average purchase price; look for landlord-friendly jurisdictions (generally red states with clearer tenant/landlord law).
- **Macro market criteria** [15:54]: Population growth trending upward (≥1% over 10 years), employment diversity across multiple industries (avoid single-industry dependency like pre-recovery Detroit), and accessible data via citydata.com.
- **Micro-level neighborhood research** [17:06]: Use local Facebook groups, real estate investor associations, and meetup.com to crowdsource neighborhood quality, crime, and property class insights; interview property managers and local lenders for ground truth.
- **Seller financing as a deal multiplier** [33:45]: Negotiate directly with sellers on terms rather than relying solely on bank financing; frame as "can I make payments to you?" rather than requesting a loan. This unlocks deals unavailable through traditional channels.
- **Private money and syndication** [35:02]: Raise capital from friends, self-directed IRAs, or other investors at 1–10% interest for 6–24 month terms; structure as first-position liens backed by property. Enables zero-money-down acquisitions after refinance.
- **BRRRR strategy (Buy, Rehab, Refinance, Rent, Repeat)** [37:10]: Purchase off-market, renovate, refinance at 85% LTV to recover capital plus profit, then rent. Repeat with recycled capital and private lender relationships.
- **Commercial vs. residential valuation** [48:33]: In commercial, every $1 of net operating income increase ≈ $16–20 in property value (depending on cap rate); residential is emotion-driven and market-dependent. Operational improvements directly multiply value in commercial.
- **Rate environment strategy** [27:02]: High rates lock in lower purchase prices; refinance when rates drop. Prefer paying more for rate than for house price; basis doesn't adjust until next transaction.
- **Off-market deal sourcing** [40:03]: Build relationships with wholesalers, agents, and other investors; prove you close deals consistently to get on "main buyers" lists and receive pocket listings before public MLS.
- **Network as prerequisite** [38:33]: Opportunities emerge only after you demonstrate capital and execution capacity; early stage requires proving you can close before being invited into larger syndications.

### Dated market read (2024-03-27)

- **Lock-in syndrome limiting supply** [52:31]: Homeowners reluctant to sell 3% mortgages to refinance at 7%; supply remains constrained despite demand.
- **Bullish 18-month outlook** [53:12]: Expect slow upward trajectory; rate cuts (forecast 3 in 2024, first likely June at ~50% probability) will accelerate real estate appreciation. No major crash expected unless led by banking, auto, or consumer debt sectors—not real estate.
- **Debt service capacity** [54:08]: ~60–70% of homeowners refinanced 2021–2022 at sub-4% rates; unlikely to default unless job loss or other debt accumulation occurs.
