# Iron condor option assignment

## Summary

A trader posted confusion about an iron condor assignment on r/options, revealing two critical gaps: lack of a pre-trade plan and misunderstanding of how short options behave within multi-leg strategies. The video clarifies that each short option in a spread is treated independently by the market and counterparties, making assignment a real risk even when the overall spread structure appears to hedge it.

## Key takeaways

- **Short options are standalone in the market's eyes** [00:44–01:07]: Even when a short option is part of a multi-leg strategy like an iron condor, a counterparty can exercise it independently. You may be randomly paired with an exercising counterparty, triggering assignment regardless of your other legs.
- **Assignment risk is navigable if understood** [01:07–01:28]: An assignment notice is "completely avoidable" only with a plan, but it is "navigable" if you understand the mechanics. The risk profile doesn't change as long as the other options remain in place.
- **Build your plan before the trade, not during** [00:00–00:26]: The trader was "trying to build the plan as they're flying it," which "never works well in trading." A pre-trade plan must account for assignment scenarios.
- **Verify spread risk tolerance includes assignment** [01:07]: Even with spreads in place, confirm that the risk from a single-leg assignment is tolerable to your portfolio.

## Notable quotes

> "If you have a short option, even if it has other legs as part of the trade, that short option is treated as a standalone option in the market's eyes."
