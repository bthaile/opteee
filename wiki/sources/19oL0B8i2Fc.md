# 3 Tricks to Survive as a New Options Trader

## Summary

New options traders often chase unrealistic returns (e.g., 10% daily on small accounts) without acknowledging the years of skill development required. This video outlines three foundational survival tactics: target returns commensurate with actual skill level, prioritize risk management over profit potential, and build a robust process rather than betting on rapid account scaling.

## Key takeaways

- **Target realistic returns early** [00:21–01:23]: Aim for "greater than zero" in your first two years. A $5,000 account generating $500/day (10% daily) is a statistical impossibility without exceptional skill; even 63 trading days at that rate would yield 4,000%+ returns. Avoid the trap of trying to rapidly grow a small account, which forces reckless position-sizing.
- **Spend more time on loss scenarios than profit scenarios** [01:46–02:07]: Most new traders focus on upside potential and ignore downside risk. Reverse this: analyze what happens if the trade goes against you *before* entering, not after.
- **Build income and savings rate, not just account growth** [02:30]: The path to wealth is a combination of maintaining a strong savings rate, investing consistently, and growing your income source—not scaling a small account into millions via trading alone.

## Notable quotes

> "Trading is the only skill I can think of where people regularly believe that you can get outsized returns with no skill."

> "My first two years of trading greater than zero is great—that's a great target to start."
