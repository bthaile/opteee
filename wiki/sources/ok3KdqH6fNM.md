# Earnings Season - Stock Market Update | Options Trading Live

## Summary

Live market recap during earnings season with focus on sector weakness (healthcare, energy), Trump tariff uncertainty, and bond selloff (TLT down to 85). Eric demonstrates expected-value mechanics using a 47% win-rate options-flow strategy (2.6× win/loss ratio) versus a 68.5% win-rate SPX premium strategy, illustrating why win rate alone is insufficient for profitability. Covers earnings trades (PNC, PGR, MS, JNJ, GS, BAC) and upcoming cycle entry.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Expected value vs. win rate** [17:26]–[23:19] — 47% win-rate strategy profitable because average win (2.6×) exceeds average loss; expected return = (win% × avg win) − (loss% × avg loss). Demonstrated with manual calculation: $100 loss, $115 avg win, 47% win rate = profitable.
- **Win-rate/win-size inverse relationship** [25:58]–[31:52] — Higher win rates typically paired with smaller average wins; lower win rates with larger average wins. Example: 95% win-rate short put requires 0.04 delta (5550 strike on SPX ~6015), needing $554k capital for $800 max profit.
- **Implied volatility → expected move** [42:40]–[44:17] — IV is forward one standard deviation range; expected move = IV scaled to underlying and time. JNJ example: 23.74% IV = ±$220 expected move. Straddle-based expected move uses deflator due to underlying mechanics.
- **Options flow as high-difficulty strategy** [16:02]–[16:52] — Not recommended for beginners; requires analyzing informed positioning beyond simple call premium positivity. ESBAT example: 67% gain over two days (14–16 July) from flow-based entry; reiterated conviction on 15th led to 29% gain on 16th.
- **Afteraction review (AR) process** [05:18]–[06:08] — Military-origin discipline applicable to trading and content creation; essential for extracting data to improve decisions.
