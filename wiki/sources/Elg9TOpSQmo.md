# Instant improvement #options #trading #optionstrading

## Summary

Define profit and loss targets before entering any trade, then size the position so that the risk relative to portfolio value makes sense. This simple discipline prevents the common trap of holding losing positions in hope, which locks up capital and compounds losses.

## Key takeaways

- **Pre-trade planning [00:00–00:28]:** Define profit and loss targets *before* putting on the trade based on where you think the underlying might go, accounting for your strategy direction (bullish or bearish).
- **Position sizing discipline [00:00–00:28]:** Size each trade so that the risk makes sense relative to your total portfolio value.
- **Avoid loss aversion trap [00:28–00:49]:** This simple step prevents the scenario where a losing trade goes against you, you become emotionally locked up, refuse to realize the loss, and then hold for hope as it deteriorates further—a destructive cycle.

## Notable quotes

> "Every single time before you put on a trade you define profit and loss targets… and sizing the trade so that if it does hit your risk control it makes sense relative to the portfolio value."
