# IV and Term Alignment #optionstrading

## Summary

This video addresses a critical alignment error in options trading: matching the implied volatility term used for analysis with the actual expiration term of the trade being constructed. While IV percentile is superior to IV rank for relative volatility assessment, traders often fail to account for the fact that volatility behaves differently across the term structure, leading to misaligned position decisions.

## Key takeaways

- **IV percentile vs. IV rank:** IV percentile is preferable because it is less skew-prone than IV rank, providing a cleaner relative volatility reading [00:00]
- **Term-structure mismatch is a common error:** Platform IV percentile defaults to 30-day volatility, but if you're trading a different expiration (e.g., weeklies, monthlies, or longer-dated), the volatility behavior will differ significantly [00:00–00:29]
- **Match your vol term to your trade term:** The critical discipline is ensuring the volatility metric you're analyzing aligns with the actual expiration of the position you're constructing [00:29]
