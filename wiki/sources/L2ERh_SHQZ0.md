# Retail Trader Evolution & Embracing Risk w/ Tom Sosnoff | Money Talks

## Summary

Tom Sosnoff, founder of thinkorswim and tastytrade, discusses his philosophy on risk-taking, retail trader education, and the evolution of options markets over the past two decades. He emphasizes that the key to long-term wealth creation is learning to appreciate, assess, and manage risk—not avoiding it—and argues that position sizing is the primary defense mechanism in trading. Sosnoff also shares his contrarian view on current market conditions, particularly on bonds and the US dollar, and previews a new Gen-Z focused trading app designed to teach traders how to lose money strategically.

## Key takeaways

- **Risk as DNA**: Sosnoff views risk-taking as partly innate but also developable; he advocates for teaching younger generations to embrace calculated risk rather than defaulting to passive, fee-heavy retirement vehicles [07:52–09:41].
- **Position sizing as defense**: The only real defense mechanism in trading is keeping position size in check; smaller positions reduce emotional volatility and allow for more strategic adjustments [15:31–16:20].
- **Emotional regulation through structure**: Sosnoff demonstrates calm during losing trades by relying on a logical, systematic approach to position management and rolling rather than panic-driven exits [14:23–15:06].
- **Retail trader evolution**: Over the past 20 years, retail traders have shifted from seeking charting studies to asking sophisticated questions about implied volatility, expected move, duration, and rolling mechanics [22:19–23:49].
- **Volatility normalization in 2022**: Realized volatility exceeded implied volatility for extended periods this year—a rare reversal of the historical trend—signaling mean reversion and normalization in the volatility surface [33:34–35:01].
- **Contrarian positioning on bonds and USD**: Sosnoff is unusually long bonds (second-most oversold asset class) and short the US dollar (most overbought), viewing both as mean-reverting extremes driven by emotional Fed-reaction rather than fundamentals [28:15–30:16].
- **Never buy premium**: Sosnoff's decision to sell premium is informed by realized-vs-implied analysis; he does not buy premium [35:01].
- **Legacy over accumulation**: At this stage of his career, Sosnoff is motivated by legacy—building businesses and methodologies that outlast him—rather than personal wealth accumulation [36:25–38:26].
- **New app philosophy**: Tasty is developing a Gen-Z trading app that teaches "how to lose money" strategically, emphasizing learning from losses and real risk-taking rather than passive index investing or gamified micro-investing [45:07–47:41].
- **Openness to new technology**: Sosnoff actively hires younger talent, invests in crypto and entertainment-tech ventures, and refuses to become entrenched in legacy systems, viewing adaptability as essential to long-term relevance [44:17–45:07].

## Regime / context

Recorded November 2022, during a period of elevated realized volatility exceeding implied volatility—a rare reversal of the historical regime where IV typically overstates realized moves. Sosnoff's contrarian positioning on bonds and the US dollar reflects the extreme dislocations of late 2022 (post-FOMC tightening cycle, yield-curve inversion, emerging-market stress). His remarks on retail trader sophistication and the evolution of options education span his 40+ year career from floor trading in Chicago (1979) through the 2008 financial crisis, the 2000 dot-com crash, and the 2022 volatility regime.
