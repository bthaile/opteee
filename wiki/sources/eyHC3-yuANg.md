# Tales of a Trader with Tom Sosnoff | The Outlier Podcast Ep72

## Summary

Tom Sosnoff discusses current market conditions following a down day with no two-sided trade, reflects on the overbought rally and small-cap normalization, and shares his framework for bond pricing and Fed policy. The bulk of the episode pivots to beginner trader guidance: capital requirements, position sizing, liquidity discipline, early trade management, and the realistic timeline for competence (30–60 days for engagement, longer for profitability).

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2024-07-31)

- **No two-sided trade today** [00:39] — Market opened down and stayed down all day; no rally or head fake, unlike most of 2024. This relentless selling was the most notable feature.
- **Tech earnings pressure** [01:36] — Tech must deliver nearly perfectly or exceed expectations to avoid selloffs; market has been hyperbolic and tightly concentrated.
- **Small-cap reversal** [06:06] — After months of underperformance, small caps (IWM) had a parabolic move in the last two weeks, recovering losses. Sosnoff was long but admits it was hard to hold through the drawdown.
- **Bond levels and rate cuts** [08:23] — Bonds at 118 suggest one rate cut; at 116, no cuts; at 120+, one or two cuts. Bond prices drive Fed policy, not vice versa.
- **SPX year-to-date** [07:21] — S&P 500 up ~14.6% YTD; Sosnoff expects modest pullback (maybe 5%) for the rest of the year given the magnitude of the first-half rally.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Bond market drives Fed** [12:02] — The Fed cannot lower rates if bonds are dropping or raise rates if bonds are rising. The marketplace drives the Fed; the Fed is in the passenger seat. Every two points in bond price puts different rate cuts back on the table.
- **Liquidity is your friend** [48:09] — Stay in the most liquid underlyings; penny-wide markets and commoditized fees make this easier than ever.
- **Trade small, trade often** [46:36] — Create enough occurrences to learn and achieve predictable outcomes via the law of large numbers. Emotional discipline and avoiding gamblers' fallacy are critical.
- **Manage trades early, not at expiration** [48:36] — Taking profits at 21 days (within a 45-day cycle) yields ~35% more P&L per day than holding to expiration, with the same probability of success. This reduces volatility and eliminates outlier risk.
- **Theta decay curve optimization** [50:35] — Dollar profit per day is significantly higher in the 45–21 day window than 21–0. The reason to exit early is risk-adjusted return, not to flip the directional thesis.
- **Zero-DTE and weekly risk** [51:29] — On volatile days (like today), zero-DTE and weekly options are uncontrollable; 45-day options allow risk management. Delta control is easy with time; hard without it.
- **Capital minimums** [58:13] — Minimum $25k to avoid pattern-day-trading rules; optimal $50–100k. Even $2k is viable for learning, though most people should expect to learn, not get rich.
- **Beginner strategy focus** [57:07] — Start with high-probability trades: short puts (80% prob. profit), covered calls (65–67%), or verticals (50–60%). Choose based on capital available.
- **Ramp-up timeline** [54:23] — 30–60 days to get comfortable and engaged (not profitable); one full 45-day cycle minimum to see adjustments and understand the game. Profitability takes longer.
- **Avoid the lottery mentality** [59:22] — Turning $2k into $100k is possible but rare; the realistic goal is $2k → $3k while learning everything. Optionality compounds when you have capital and skill.
