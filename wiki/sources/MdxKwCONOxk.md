# GME Options: Analyzing the Recent Volatility Drop

## Summary

Following GameStop's shareholders meeting on June 16, implied volatility contracted sharply from ~280% to 160%, with further decline to 160% by market close on June 18. The analysis examines the options chain structure, open interest skew, transaction flow, and volatility smile to assess market expectations for near-term and longer-dated expirations. Despite the volatility collapse, the market still prices in significant upside tail risk via elevated call premiums at far out-of-the-money strikes.

## Key takeaways

- **Volatility collapse post-event** [00:21–01:46]: IV expanded to ~280% ahead of the shareholders meeting (perceived as a non-event), then contracted 76 points to 204% immediately after, and fell further to 160% by close—a larger move than initially expected.

- **Call skew persists in near-term expirations** [02:05–02:55]: Open interest remains heavily skewed toward calls (~2:1 call-to-put ratio) in the 21 June and 28 June expirations, with ~818k of 1.2M total call OI concentrated in the current and next week—atypical for a hedging-focused market.

- **Parity in longer-dated expirations** [03:17–03:39]: Call and put OI approach parity and invert to normal put skew in July, August, and October expirations, suggesting the market is pricing near-term action within the October expiration window.

- **Call volume at the bid indicates covered-call sales** [04:18–04:42]: 39% of call transactions executed at or below the bid, consistent with sellers (likely covered-call writers) capitalizing on elevated premiums; put volume split evenly between buying and selling.

- **Elevated near-term call volume despite low catalysts** [06:29–07:05]: 42,000 contracts in the 21 June expiration (3 days to expiration, no market open tomorrow) traded despite no obvious catalyst; likely a mix of new speculative positions and premium capture via covered calls.

- **Volatility smile shows put-side tail risk, flattened call side** [07:25–08:47]: The 21 June IV smile now exhibits normal put skew (downside tail premium), while call-side IV has flattened; the 28 June smile remains flatter than expected, with residual call skew.

- **Catastrophic upside still priced in** [09:30–10:33]: Far out-of-the-money call premiums remain elevated across the chain, indicating the market has not fully discounted the possibility of a massive upside move, despite the overall volatility collapse.
