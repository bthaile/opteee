# How to Build an Options Portfolio: $5K vs $25K vs $250K

## Summary

This video outlines three distinct portfolio structures for options traders at different account sizes: $5,000, $25,000, and $250,000. The core principle is that smaller accounts must prioritize process, core allocation, and capital preservation, while larger accounts can introduce speculative strategies and naked risk. The key insight is that account size determines not just position sizing but the entire strategic approach and risk tolerance.

## Key takeaways

### $5,000 Account
- **Oversized by default** [00:00–01:12]: Any position in a $5K account is inherently oversized; accept this constraint and focus on process, not profits.
- **Prioritize delayed gratification** [01:12–02:25]: Build a trading plan and trading log from day one; expect slow initial growth that compounds exponentially over years.
- **Aggressive savings + core allocation** [02:25–03:33]: Focus 80%+ on core holdings you'd be comfortable holding long-term; avoid dollar spreads (low probability of meaningful return post-friction).
- **Covered strangles over ratio diagonals** [03:33–04:47]: Use covered strangles in leveraged products like SOXL to reduce timing risk; ratio diagonals require precision timing that newer traders lack.
- **Momentum-driven core** [04:47–06:00]: Favor securities moving aggressively; paper trade ratio diagonals and other timing-sensitive strategies extensively before live trading.

### $25,000 Account
- **Portfolio at scale begins** [06:00–07:07]: Now you can implement a true portfolio with multiple positions; take trading plan and logs seriously because capital is meaningful.
- **Core allocation shifts to covered strangles** [07:07–08:15]: Ratio call diagonals fall away from core; use covered strangles (e.g., in TQQQ) as the keel of the portfolio; upside bias is justified because markets trend up.
- **Introduce speculative allocation** [08:15–09:22]: Allocate ~20% core, ~5% speculative; use wings on spreads to manage capital requirements; avoid earnings plays (too bumpy for this size).
- **Sector rotation and diversification** [09:22–10:31]: Add positions in different sectors (e.g., healthcare via CVS) to hedge broad-market drift; notice the difference between utilization and risk.
- **Capital efficiency** [10:31–11:27]: You don't need to deploy all $25K; options are levered; leave dry powder for opportunities.

### $250,000 Account
- **Scaling and optimization** [11:27–12:39]: Take the $25K structure and enlarge it; speculative allocation can now absorb more capital depending on market regime.
- **Naked earnings risk** [12:39–13:52]: Now you can trade short straddles, short strangles, and naked single options on earnings to extract volatility risk premium; requires proper sizing.
- **Think about income and robustness** [13:52–end]: Begin modeling portfolio income and lifestyle sustainability; consider how to divvy up allocations for long-term viability.
