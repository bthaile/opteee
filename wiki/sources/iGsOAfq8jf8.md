# Matching strategy and personality

## Summary

A trader must identify their own behavioral tendencies and risk preferences, then deliberately match trading systems and strategies to those traits. Forcing a mismatch between personality and strategy produces poor results; the goal is to find profit mechanisms that naturally align with how you think and behave.

## Key takeaways

- **Identify your edge first** [00:00] — Find market forces and profit mechanisms you can monetize; this is the foundation before personality matching.
- **Know your risk tolerance** [00:24] — Determine whether you're comfortable with high risk or prefer lower-risk approaches; this is a core personality trait.
- **Match turnover to your preferences** [00:24] — Decide if you want to actively manage many positions or review your portfolio infrequently (e.g., quarterly).
- **Align system design with behavior** [00:43] — Build systems that naturally blend with your tendencies rather than forcing incompatible strategies; misalignment guarantees poor results.
- **Self-awareness is part of the job** [00:43] — Identifying your own trading tendencies is as critical as finding the edge itself.
