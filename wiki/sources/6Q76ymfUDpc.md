# GME Options Update 200k OTM Calls

## Summary

Eric reviews broad market weakness (S&P 500 down ~0.75%, NASDAQ ~0.5%, Russell 2000 ~1.9%) as a pullback after a strong April–July run, not capitulation. He covers his long GME options for profit but keeps short calls against shares; the market punishes the position anyway. The options chain shows elevated IV (155% front week, 131–129% further out) ahead of September earnings, with volume concentrated in front-month Opex and lighter activity in weekly/monthly expirations.

## Key takeaways

- **Market pullback, not panic** [00:51] — Indices down modestly after a sustained rally; breadth indicators (net highs/lows) turning down but not diving yet; lows expanding from ~39 to ~67 but still in "middle zone."
- **Covered-call trap didn't work** [02:01] — Sold long GME options at open, kept short calls against shares expecting the market to reverse; instead, market closed both for profit but punished the underlying shares.
- **Volume contracted, sale pressure high** [04:00] — Total options volume lighter than average; sale volume ~90% of mix, indicating broad unwinding across small caps (Russell 2000 heavily red).
- **IV term structure: front-month elevated** [06:55] — This week (1 day left): 155% IV; next week: 131–129%; further out: 125–131%. Elevated front-month IV is Opex-driven, not earnings-driven (September 4 earnings still ahead).
- **Big traders avoid short-term expirations** [08:46] — Large positions on July 16–17 were placed in July 26 and August 16 expirations, not Friday weeklies; signals sophisticated traders avoiding zero-DTE gamma risk.
- **Cautionary tale: OTM call buyers** [09:39] — 471 contracts of $25 calls bought at $2.90 (paid $137k) now trading at $1.10; illustrates velocity and drawdown risk in short-dated options.
