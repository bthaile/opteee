# Average Trader Performance

## Summary

This video examines real trader losses and the emotional cycles that drive poor decision-making. Using examples from retail trading communities, it highlights how traders often exit positions emotionally after taking stops, only to watch the market reverse in their favor—a classic manifestation of disposition effect and lack of emotional discipline.

## Key takeaways

- **Retail loss patterns** [00:00] — Portfolio losses of 91%+ are common in retail trading, particularly in speculative/leveraged positions; these reflect both strategy failure and emotional decision-making.
- **Stop-loss regret cycle** [00:22] — Traders frequently exit at stops, then experience FOMO when the market reverses; this emotional whipsaw is a primary driver of repeated losses.
- **Emotional recovery impulse** [00:22] — After a painful loss, traders ask whether they should "take a break for a few days," signaling emotional exhaustion rather than systematic review; this is a red flag for disposition-effect-driven trading.
- **Process vs. outcome** — The video implies that average trader underperformance stems not from a single bad trade, but from a pattern of emotionally-driven exits and lack of systematic discipline.
