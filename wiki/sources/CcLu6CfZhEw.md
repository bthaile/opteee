# Finding Big Moves with Unusual Options Activity

## Summary

Unusual options activity—abnormal trading patterns including high volume, large blocks, sweeps, and open-interest spikes—can signal informed speculative positioning ahead of directional moves or volatility events. The primary profit mechanisms are directional continuation (via call/put buying concentration) and volatility expansion. The video demonstrates a live example: a 266% return on ~$36.5k deployed across ESB August calls over 3 days (July 14–17), scaling out as the underlying rallied ~20% and options activity unwound.

## Key takeaways

- **What is unusual options activity** [00:00–01:29]: abnormal volume, large blocks, sweeps, open-interest spikes, and IV expansion relative to historical baselines; requires context to distinguish from routine hedging or rolling activity.

- **Profit mechanisms** [02:38–04:00]: (1) directional continuation if institutions/informed traders are buying calls/puts; (2) volatility expansion as aggressive transactions bid up option premiums; (3) leverage—options are preferred over stock for informed bets because of capital efficiency.

- **Identification via scans** [07:34–08:43]: filter for volume-over-open-interest ratio (e.g., >2.4x), out-of-the-money contracts (to exclude hedging), near-term expirations (30 days or less), and minimum premium thresholds ($50k+) to avoid noise; examine concentration by strike, term, and expiration.

- **Term matters** [10:02–11:14]: near-term unusual activity (2–4 weeks) signals expected directional move; far-dated activity may be general bullish/bearish positioning with less immediate impact.

- **ESB trade setup** [11:14–13:32]: on July 14, net call premium spiked to $6.67M (vs. $50–100k baseline); bought 75× Aug 30 calls + 50× Aug 41 calls for $36.5k total debit; sized small (<<1% of portfolio) because these are high-speculation trades.

- **Scaling out on confirmation** [14:48–17:17]: July 15 (+20% move): sold 50× 41 calls for $860 (bought $229), realizing $31.5k; July 16 (+20% more): sold 40× 30 calls for $1,239 (bought $1,000), realizing $36.1k; July 17 (momentum fading, RSI rolling over): sold remaining 35× 30 calls for $1,185, realizing $29.7k. Total P&L: ~$97.4k (266% ROIC).

- **Risk management** [12:26–13:32]: avoid front-month expirations (too much theta decay and whipsaw risk); use intermediate terms (4–6 weeks) to give the thesis time to play out while maintaining leverage.

- **False flags are common** [01:29–02:38]: unusual options activity is a discretionary, context-dependent signal; many volume/OI spikes are routine (expiration rolls, hedging, rebalancing); develop experience before relying on it as a primary strategy.

- **Confirmation signals** [17:17]: watch for unwinding in the options chain (closing volume) and price-action confirmation (RSI flattening/rolling over, momentum loss) to exit.

## Notable quotes

> "The broad idea behind unusual options activity is they do provide pretty significant risk-reward opportunities asymmetrically, meaning we're looking for bigger wins and pretty frequent but smaller losses." [00:00]
