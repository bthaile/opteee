# FOMC, CHWY, & GME Analysis

## Summary

Market update covering three topics: FOMC rate-cut probabilities ahead of the 18 September meeting (63% probability of 50 bps cut), technical analysis of Chewy post-earnings, and GameStop consolidation near support with elevated call open interest. Broad market showing sector rotation away from tech toward small caps and materials as rate-cut expectations build.

## Key takeaways

- **FOMC probabilities** [02:00–03:27]: 63% probability of 50 bps cut on 18 Sept; 87% probability of 25 bps in November; another 50 bps expected in December. This is more aggressive than anticipated (~125 bps total near-term vs. expected ~100 bps).
- **Sector rotation underway** [00:48–01:39]: S&P 500 at new all-time highs; small caps (IWM) catching up but still ~9% behind on year; materials (XLB) and real estate (builders, Home Depot) outperforming tech, signaling market is pricing in rate cuts.
- **Recession risk** [04:28–04:45]: 2025 recession probabilities are "not insignificant"; traders should prepare with a plan rather than panic.
- **Chewy technical setup** [04:45–06:27]: Post-earnings rally with expanding volume; support ~30, near-term target ~35. Options chain very quiet (250k calls vs. 206k puts open interest); no anomalous volatility or unusual activity detected.
- **GameStop consolidation** [08:14–10:36]: Price hugging perceived support; volume drying up (6.3M shares, mostly sell-side). Call OI at 660k vs. 300k puts (2:1 ratio); 89k calls vs. 13.7k puts traded today, mostly at offer. Longer-dated positions building.
- **Roaring Kitty trade analysis** [11:25–11:59]: Upcoming Saturday stream will analyze part three of Roaring Kitty's old trades; observed that he manages positions primarily by time rather than value, which is atypical.
- **Meme-stock options miniseries** [12:19–12:40]: Upcoming live stream will launch educational miniseries on trading meme stocks with options, focusing on profit mechanisms and thesis-aligned structures rather than cheap OTM lottery tickets.
