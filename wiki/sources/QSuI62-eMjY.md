# The Four Main Profit Mechanisms In Earning Releases

## Summary

Eric outlines a framework for trading around earnings releases by identifying four distinct profit mechanisms: implied volatility expansion into earnings, variance risk premium capture through the event, post-earnings breakouts/breakdowns, and post-earnings announcement drift. This taxonomy allows traders to systematically approach earnings as a single catalyst with multiple exploitable edges.

## Key takeaways

- **Implied volatility expansion into earnings** [00:00] — IV typically rises as the event approaches; traders can profit from this directional vol move independent of price direction.
- **Variance risk premium through earnings** [00:00] — The premium between realized and implied volatility creates a distinct profit mechanism during the earnings window.
- **Post-earnings breakouts/breakdowns** [00:00] — Price action immediately following the release often exhibits directional momentum that can be traded.
- **Post-earnings announcement drift** [00:00] — Price continues to move in the direction of the earnings surprise for days or weeks after the release, offering a separate edge from the immediate reaction.
