# Quick Rundown On Letting Short Puts Get Assigned

## Summary
This video covers the mechanics of allowing a short put position to be assigned rather than closing it before expiration. When assignment occurs, the trader realizes the premium collected upfront and takes on an open long stock position, typically at a loss since assignment signals the puts were in-the-money.

## Key takeaways

- **Assignment mechanics** [00:00]: Taking assignment on a short put means realizing the premium sold and opening a long stock position at the strike price.
- **Loss calculation at assignment** [00:24]: Unrealized loss post-assignment is calculated as spot price minus your cost basis (the strike price at which you were assigned).
- **Assignment signals ITM status** [00:00]: Assignment overwhelmingly occurs when short puts are in-the-money, indicating the underlying has moved below your strike.
