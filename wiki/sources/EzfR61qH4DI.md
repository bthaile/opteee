# WFC, ADBE, GME Stock Market News

## Summary

Quick market update covering three stocks: Wells Fargo (WFC) down 4% on regulatory issues; Adobe (ADBE) beat earnings but missed forward guidance, trading down despite strong revenue; and GameStop (GME) showing unusual dark-pool activity and noteworthy options structure with call-heavy skew and distributed open interest across expirations. The host emphasizes the importance of forward guidance in post-earnings moves and highlights GME's quiet accumulation phase with interesting volatility trading opportunities.

## Key takeaways

- **Wells Fargo regulatory pattern** [00:21–01:23]: WFC dropped 4% due to ongoing compliance failures; the host notes this is a recurring issue for the bank but respects its ability to remain in business despite investigations. Financial sector (XLF) showing signs of rotation after a period of strength.

- **Adobe earnings miss on guidance, not revenue** [01:44–03:48]: ADBE crushed revenue estimates (actual $4.65B vs. $3.63B estimate) but stock fell from $586 to $532 due to softer Q4 guidance ($5.50–$5.55B vs. market expectation ~$5.60B). Forward guidance is new information and priced heavily; tight margins leave little room for disappointment.

- **Rate-cut environment and tech uncertainty** [03:48–04:47]: Market is grasping for direction as rate cuts approach; no historical precedent exists for the current post-COVID fiscal/monetary environment. Host trades current market conditions rather than forecasting.

- **GME dark-pool activity spike** [05:11–10:41]: Nearly 100 transactions of 5,000-share blocks observed over the last 30 days, concentrated on certain dates (e.g., ~90 on Sept 12, ~45 on Sept 11, declining to near-zero on Sept 4–5). Pattern is undulating and difficult to explain; larger block sizes (87K–150K) flow without issue, making the repetitive 5K blocks anomalous.

- **GME options structure shift** [06:14–07:26]: 700,000 calls vs. 350,000 puts (2:1 ratio); majority of calls NOT concentrated in front expiration (146K of 700K). This represents a shift from typical front-heavy concentration; 20-step expiration has 135K calls, suggesting distributed positioning.

- **GME volatility term structure anomaly** [07:26–08:22]: Front week at 121% IV, dropping to 98–96–95–99, then plummeting to 69 before rebounding to 90. The 18-DTE spike (95→99) is noteworthy; soft volatility creates relative-value trading opportunities.

- **GME call-side IV skew** [08:45–09:08]: For the first time in some time, 20-step expiration shows heavy call-side implied volatility skew (normally puts are priced higher). This inversion is tradable across strikes.

- **GME support level** [05:33]: Price holding at 19.81 support level, which has been tested multiple times recently and appears meaningful.

- **Host position management** [12:45–13:22]: Host has paired down GME stock position at decent profit levels; treats it as accumulation zone. Emphasizes this is not a core holding for livelihood (unlike trading the broader market). Waiting for offering to abate and for capital-deployment guidance.
