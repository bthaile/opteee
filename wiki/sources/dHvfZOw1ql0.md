# CBOE State of the Options Industry Report | Live Stock Market Analysis

## Summary

Eric reviews the 2025 CBOE State of the Options Industry Report, highlighting record options volume (15.2 billion contracts, up 26% YoY) driven by retail and institutional participation, zero-DTE expansion, and structural market changes. The discussion covers volatility risk premium measurement, momentum trading mechanics, trailing stops, and the cost implications of frequent trading for retail accounts.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2026-01-25)

- **Record options volume**: 15.2 billion contracts in 2025, sixth consecutive record year; daily volume ~61 million contracts [19:41]
- **Zero-DTE dominance**: 32% of volume in zero-DTE contracts; 24% in single-stock zero-DTE [40:14]
- **Retail participation**: 50% of volume from self-directed retail brokers, particularly in single-stock and zero-DTE SPX options [20:41]
- **Index concentration**: SPY 41%, QQQ 20%, IWM 18% of equity options volume; SPX and VIX huge in index space [23:51]
- **Notional exposure**: ~4 trillion in notional value [23:14]
- **Venue expansion**: Miami trading floor opened; 19 venues, 7 operators; PDT rules easing [21:17]
- **ETF explosion**: ETF listings tripled over ~10 years; now thousands of options-eligible ETFs [34:44]
- **Effective spreads tighter than quoted**: Payment-for-order-flow price improvement visible, but masks underlying friction [38:20]

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Volatility risk premium (VRP) measurement**: No real-time indicator exists; edge lies in measuring implied vs. realized volatility yourself [06:45]. VRP is a generalized bet on whether implied volatility is rich relative to expected realized volatility; requires sampling and forecasting discipline [08:04]
- **Momentum trading framework**: Base factor is real and historically observable; success depends on matching strategy to time frame and asset class profile [17:15]. Volatility-adjusted momentum is a starting point, but total range and asset-class characteristics matter more [12:13]
- **Trailing stops and position management**: Balance between capturing most of the move and avoiding whipsaws; depends on underlying volatility profile and time frame [15:17]. Scaling in/out complicates backtesting but is essential for refined execution [15:47]
- **Backtesting and robustness**: Define strategy, test in-sample and out-of-sample, randomize samples, and check parameter sensitivity to avoid overfitting [16:32]. Momentum is a well-studied factor; the edge is in execution, not discovery [17:15]
- **Pattern recognition in breakouts**: Identify fake breakouts by studying thousands of examples and developing subconscious pattern recognition, similar to how elite athletes read defenses [44:02]
- **Transaction cost drag**: Average cost ~65 cents per contract; on small accounts, friction from entry, exit, and adjustments can consume entire edge [47:17]. Smaller traders especially vulnerable to cost bleed in short-term strategies [47:47]
- **Zero-DTE trade-offs**: Useful for short-term hedging without paying for longer-dated premium, but transaction-heavy and unforgiving; many retail traders miss longer-term opportunities [22:31]
- **24/7 trading impact**: Will smooth earnings volatility and likely reduce post-earnings drift premium; traders must evolve strategies as market structure changes [41:52]
- **Put skew and tail hedging**: Far out-of-the-money puts trade at elevated IV (e.g., 22% vs. 15% ATM); tail-hedge buyers overpay on absolute basis but fair value unknown for new products [31:27]
