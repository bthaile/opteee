# Expert Guide to Technical Analysis with Brian Shannon

## Summary
Brian Shannon (Alphatrends, author of "Technical Analysis Using Multiple Timeframes" and originator of the anchored VWAP tool) walks the Outlier Trading host through his full technical-analysis framework. His method is built on trend alignment across multiple timeframes: identify an uptrend candidate on the daily chart, wait for a real pullback, then buy strength only when a shorter timeframe makes its first higher high back into alignment with the primary trend, always with a defined stop under the most recent relevant higher low. Risk management is the foundation, and anchored VWAP is his signature edge — an objective, non-subjective read of who controls a stock from any chosen anchor point (an earnings date, a prior high/low). Highly usable for discretionary trend/swing traders.

## Key takeaways
- Risk management is the number-one job of any active market participant — comes before any forecast; you must build "I could be wrong" into the plan and cut losers when the trend definition breaks. [08:21] [09:05]
- Trend alignment is the core framework: use a 20- and 50-day moving average on the daily; if the 20 is above the 50 and both are rising, the stock is in an uptrend — "innocent until proven guilty." [38:16]
- Don't buy the initial FOMO move and don't buy dips blindly. A *true* dip is a stock in an uptrend that pulls back to a level, finds support, and resumes; if it breaks key support/moving averages that roll over, sellers have regained control and "you're buying into the abyss." [36:00-ish via clean text] [~1:18:56]
- Multi-timeframe entry: identify the candidate on the daily, then drop to a 30-min (last ~30 days), then 10-min / 2-min for timing. Enter as the shorter timeframe makes its first higher high out of the pullback — that's when momentum realigns with the longer trend. Stop goes under the most recent relevant higher low. [44:43] [40:03] [40:24]
- Confluence at a pullback zone is what he hunts: a rising 20-day MA, a 61.8% Fibonacci retracement, and an anchored VWAP off the prior low converging in the same area = three participant groups (longs, shorts, sideline cash) all reaching the same conclusion. [45:02] [1:15:38] [1:16:50]
- Anchored VWAP is his distinctive tool: anchor VWAP from any significant point (earnings, a 5-year-ago high, a prior low) to see with "100% certainty" whether buyers or sellers are in control from that point — "factual supply and demand," not subjective. He looks for a "VWAP pinch" where anchors from a prior high and prior low converge, marking the transition from supply- to demand-controlled. [1:10:47] [1:13:15] [1:14:30]
- Origin of anchored VWAP: discovered ~2003 on the RealTick platform by manipulating the date range on a VWAP line; asked RealTick to build an anchorable tool (they never did), TC2000 built it ~2016 as "VWAP by Alphatrends," now on ~a dozen platforms. [1:11:10] [1:12:53]
- Fibonacci and floor-trader pivots (R1/R2, S1/S2) are secondary/tertiary tools — Fibs to spot confluence at retracements (draws them high-to-low to encompass the whole range), pivots as first profit-taking levels. [1:15:17] [1:17:43]
- Anticipate, don't predict: he frames scenarios ("if-then") rather than forecasting outcomes — reacts to what the market is actually doing and lets the stop define wrong. [43:57]
- Scaling out is his winner-management engine: sell the first third into strength (often near daily R2), raise the stop on the remaining two-thirds toward breakeven, then trail the final third on a larger timeframe using the higher-highs/higher-lows definition of trend to hold multi-day/multi-week moves. "Winners don't take care of themselves." [1:04-1:07 region]
- Self-identifies as a swing trader; sweet spot is 3-8 days. Uncertainty/volatility naturally shrinks his timeframe — he listens to the market rather than forcing a fixed holding period. [~1:00-1:02]
- Over-researching fundamentals is a psychological trap: heavy DD makes you "married" to a position and prone to seek news to justify holding a loser — when price stops confirming your thesis, sell. He uses light fundamentals (rising revenue + rising earnings, real product) only for position-size confidence and overnight/earnings-hold decisions. [41:10] [44:43]

## Regime / context
- Recorded 2021-12-22 — the oldest video in the sample. Framing is heavily colored by late-2021 conditions: a strong post-COVID (2020 low) bull market punctuated by sharp 3-6 week bearish shakeouts, and the collapse of former leaders ("yesterday's winners") Peloton, Zoom, and PayPal, down 50-80%. His anti-dip-buying message is delivered explicitly against those broken-growth names.
- The "buy strength, not dips" and shrinking-timeframe (3-8 day) posture is a direct read of that choppy, growth-under-pressure late-2021 tape, not a permanent stance — he stresses letting the market dictate timeframe.
- Tool availability is date-stamped: anchored VWAP described as ~5 years old on retail platforms (TC2000 ~2016), now on ~a dozen platforms as of 2021.
- Anecdotes reference much older markets (early-1990s day-trading via 24k/48k modems, SOES/ECNs, quarter-and-eighth spreads) — historical color, not current market structure.
