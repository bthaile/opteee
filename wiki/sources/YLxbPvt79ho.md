# Effortless Trading: How to Manage Trades Systematically

## Summary

This video addresses the critical gap between trade entry and trade management: most traders place trades without a defined management plan, leading to reactive decision-making under pressure. Eric walks through a generalized trade management flow and nine pre-trade questions designed to force deliberate thinking about position sizing, profit/loss targets, scaling rules, and risk thresholds before capital is deployed.

## Key takeaways

- **Define total allocation before entry** [05:43–06:00]: Determine the total sum of capital willing to apply to a trade over its lifespan, plus initial outlay and scaling rules. This prevents the common mistake of adding money to losing trades.
- **Separate mental stops from hard stops** [10:56–11:16]: A mental stop signals you are wrong; a hard stop is where you must be completely out. Move your mental stop up as price moves favorably to lock in at least neutral P&L, then gradually lock in profits.
- **Use trend-following for scaling exits** [08:11–09:30]: For shorter-dated directional trades, apply a 9-day/21-day moving average cross or similar mechanical rule to scale out while trailing your stop upward as price moves in your favor.
- **Think about what can hurt you, not just what helps** [09:52–10:12]: Spend more time identifying risks and damage scenarios than upside scenarios. Define maximum acceptable risk and where you exit before reaching it.
- **Know how you are right and how you are wrong** [10:56–11:16]: Define the conditions that confirm your hypothesis (e.g., price moving up, stop moving up = correct) and the conditions that falsify it (e.g., price below mental stop = wrong).
- **Track metrics that matter to your strategy** [12:02]: For directional trades, track directional accuracy (win rate). For other strategies, track the specific edge that makes them work.
- **Keep the checklist portable** [12:26–12:42]: The nine questions are intentionally light enough to execute consistently. A 62-item checklist will be abandoned; a simple framework will be used.
- **Trade management flow has two versions** [02:31]: A generalized version (covered here) and a strategy-specific version (outlined in your trading plan). The generalized architecture prevents reinventing the wheel for each strategy.
