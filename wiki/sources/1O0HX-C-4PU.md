# Andrew Left Fraud and GME Options

## Summary

Eric analyzes the DOJ's indictment of Citron Research founder Andrew Left on securities fraud charges while discussing the irony of Left accusing Roaring Kitty of market manipulation. The video contextualizes the charges within the broader GME narrative, examines GameStop's quiet options expiration day, and explores emerging gamma positioning in near-the-money calls that could theoretically trigger hedging cascades.

## Key takeaways

- **Andrew Left indictment context** [00:00–06:51]: Left charged with 16–20 million stock manipulation scheme; faces up to 25 years on securities fraud counts. Eric emphasizes the irony of Left publicly accusing Roaring Kitty of manipulation while being charged himself; notes this validates GME community skepticism about market structure without endorsing blanket "everything is rigged" thinking.

- **Nuance on market efficiency** [02:12–04:30]: Markets are neither perfectly efficient nor entirely rigged; clear examples of manipulation exist alongside legitimate price discovery. Dismissing GME community as "tinfoil hats" ignores documented fraud cases and structural issues.

- **GME expiration day recap** [12:40–13:04]: Extremely tight range (66 cents, high 24.77 to low 24.11); 97% sell-side volume; stock held support/resistance without major move despite heavy volume.

- **Technical analysis caveat** [13:04–17:31]: Standard technical analysis breaks down on GME due to structural anomalies (e.g., 9.99 to 65.80 pre-market moves, Bollinger Band failures lasting weeks). Eric uses modified technical analysis with backtested indicators on large options+underlying dataset; standard tools (moving averages, support/resistance) fail here.

- **Options positioning: 2-Aug expiration** [18:11–19:44]: 161k calls vs 51k puts; 25-strike showing 22k volume (front) and 14.9k (next week). Surprised to see continued 25-call opening rather than pure rollout; net +500 OI on 24-strikes suggests new position building.

- **Gamma stacking at-the-money** [23:15–25:29]: 24-strike calls (50+ delta, 5 days to expiration) stacked with 2.1k, 10k, 1k, 3k, 1k, 2k OI across micro-strikes (24.00, 24.50, 25.00, 25.50, 26.00). This configuration could theoretically trigger gamma squeeze if market makers hedge sequentially, but requires much deeper chains and heavy volume to manifest; current positioning is interesting but not yet conviction-level.

- **Epistemic humility** [20:04–21:21]: Eric explicitly wrong on 25-strike rollout prediction; emphasizes traders are wrong constantly and warns against following analysts who only highlight correct calls while hiding failed predictions.
