# You Don't Have to Compete with Wall Street

## Summary

Retail traders cannot and should not attempt to compete directly with Wall Street institutions on their terms. Instead, the focus should be on identifying market inefficiencies and trading approaches where retail has structural advantages, allowing profitable trading without head-to-head competition against larger players.

## Key takeaways

- **Reframe the competition mindset** [00:00] — Retail traders don't need to compete with Wall Street; there are explicit ways to find profitable spots without attempting to match institutional scale or resources.
- **Identify retail-specific edges** — Focus on finding market conditions and strategies where retail structure, speed, or information asymmetries create an advantage rather than a disadvantage.
- **Avoid institutional battlegrounds** — Don't attempt to outcompete on capital, technology, or speed in markets where institutions dominate; instead, find niches where retail can thrive.

## Notable quotes

> "Retail can't compete with Wall Street. Like that's the fact. But more importantly and a better way to think of this is you don't want to."
