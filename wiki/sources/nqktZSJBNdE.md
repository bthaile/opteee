# How to Create an Option Strategy in 5 SIMPLE Steps

## Summary

Eric walks through a five-step framework for building self-sufficient, repeatable option strategies without relying on others' approaches. The process emphasizes structured documentation, rule-setting, systematic tracking via trade logs, backtesting, and progressive live testing—moving from initial rules through variants, backtests, paper trading, and finally small live positions.

## Key takeaways

- **Step 1: Outline the structure** [00:47] — Document the strategy's legs, general behavior, and components in a written plan (e.g., Word document with strategy outlines). This adds structure to a complex system and enables variable manipulation.

- **Step 2: Create initial rules** [02:11] — Make educated guesses about what matters (days to expiration, delta, etc.) even if you don't know yet. These assumptions will be tested and refined over time as you deploy the strategy.

- **Step 3: Define profit/loss and lifecycle management** [03:25] — Bake in max profit targets (e.g., 50% or 65% of max profit), max loss tolerance, and lifecycle rules (e.g., what to do if a short put is ITM with 20 days vs. 10 days left—roll, wait, or take assignment).

- **Step 4: Create a trade log with tracked variables** [05:39] — Use Excel or Google Sheets to log key variables: Greeks (delta, gamma, theta, vega), implied volatility percentile, realized volatility, days to expiration, and entry/exit conditions. Balance comprehensiveness with actually filling it out.

- **Step 5: Test variants in order of increasing fidelity** [07:13] — Progress from backtesting (least useful, but filters bad ideas) → paper trading / on-demand review → small live positions. Test multiple rule variants (e.g., 5, 7, 10 DTE) in parallel to identify what works.

- **Core principle** [05:18] — Structured, diligent work on rule-setting and tracking is what separates consistent traders from the 93% who fail within five years. The goal is to understand definitively which variables drive performance, then optimize off those.
