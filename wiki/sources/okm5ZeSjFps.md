# The Linear Component of Delta

## Summary

Delta exhibits a linear relationship with underlying price movement when normalized by delta itself, independent of time to expiration. A 50-delta option moves approximately $0.50 for every $1 move in the underlying, whether it expires in 4 days or 400 days. The nonlinearity emerges only when delta is normalized by strike price rather than by delta value itself.

## Key takeaways

- **Delta is linear across time frames** [00:00–00:31]: A 50-delta call has the same directional sensitivity regardless of days to expiration; a $1 underlying move produces ~$0.50 P&L in both 4-day and 400-day expirations.
- **Normalization method determines linearity** [00:31]: When delta is the normalization anchor, the relationship is linear; when strike price is the anchor, nonlinear (gamma) effects dominate.

## Notable quotes

"Delta is delta. But when we normalize it off of the strike, that's when we see the nonlinear function of delta." [00:31]
