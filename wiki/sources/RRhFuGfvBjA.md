# Tom Sosnoff on Market Volatility and Building a New Business | The Outlier Podcast

## Summary

Tom Sosnoff discusses recent market volatility—particularly the crypto selloff, silver/gold commodity moves, and subdued VIX despite macro uncertainty—while reflecting on his transition from Tasty to Lost Dog, a new software venture focused on portfolio optimization and career valuation. He emphasizes sizing discipline, contrarian entry signals, and the importance of focusing on controllable factors in trading and business.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2026-02-10)

- **Crypto capitulation signal [09:13–10:25]**: Made ~50–60 crypto trades yesterday, buying on oversold conditions after a ~50% six-month decline; viewed the move as a classic contrarian entry despite two-year bearish stance on crypto fundamentals.
- **Silver/gold volatility [24:18–28:07]**: Silver experienced extreme moves (7–10 standard deviation events), described as a "meme commodity" with unprecedented volatility; gold-silver pairs trade failed as a hedge when silver moved 10× faster; recovered in a single day (Friday).
- **Micro Strategies as single point of failure [17:33–19:49]**: Positioned MSTR as a structural risk to crypto markets due to flawed market structure and dividend obligations; down 75–80% from highs; believes this contributed to the broader selloff.
- **Robin Hood and Coinbase valuations [23:07]**: HOOD trading like a crypto stock despite crypto being only 25–30% of business; viewed as oversold; COIN also cheap on the move.
- **VIX sub-14 paradox [30:29–31:39]**: VIX drifting lower (16→15→14→13→12) despite macro uncertainty; noted VIX spends only ~30% of time in action, rest in "lull state"; outlier risk highest from low VIX levels; S&P near record highs creates disconnect.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Position sizing and tail risk [33:47–36:12]**: Core tension: size large enough to capture variance risk premium (e.g., short vol through earnings) but small enough to survive tail moves; consistent risk-takers solve this by balancing expected return vs. acceptable loss; bull markets hide poor sizing discipline.
- **Contrarian entry signals [20:53–22:03]**: Explosive volatility + underwater close + negative price action + "pukeage" = classic buy signal; never certain, but felt like capitulation yesterday; bought 50 different tranches throughout the day.
- **Holding duration spectrum [11:24–12:37]**: Ranges from minutes/hours (scalps, one-day trades) to weeks/months; currently ~40–45 positions (down from ~80 at Tasty); ~1/3 very short-term (<24h).
- **VIX behavior and risk [30:29–31:39]**: VIX drifts lower in lull state; all outlier moves originate from VIX <15; low VIX = hidden tail risk; focus on sizing, not macro prediction.
- **Stoic risk management [32:47–33:47]**: Focus on controllable factors (position size, trade discipline); ignore uncontrollable macro (3+ standard deviation moves, geopolitics); successful traders and entrepreneurs apply this principle universally.
