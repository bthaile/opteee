# YY0b7ShDies

## Summary

Weekly GameStop update analyzing near-term price action in the context of broader market correction. The host reviews the covered-strangle position currently underwater, discusses Ryan Cohen's public response to Bitcoin reserve asset proposal, and evaluates the quiet options market environment. Despite unrealized losses on the equity position, the strategy has generated realized P&L from prior short options rolls.

## Key takeaways

- **Broader market context** [00:00–01:02]: S&P 500 is approximately one-third of the way toward a 10% correction; GameStop weakness is partly symptomatic of this broader pullback, not purely company-specific.
- **Downtrend structure** [01:02–02:18]: Price has posted lower lows below prior support, confirming continuation of downtrend; consolidation levels suggest mixed market sentiment on the name.
- **Sentiment-driven near term** [02:18–03:34]: Near-term movement is driven by sentiment and short-term risk positioning rather than fundamentals; longer-term bullish on AI and growth catalysts despite near-term volatility.
- **Value-averaging approach** [03:34–04:36]: Strategy involves unwinding shares at profit before they turn to loss, then redeploying capital; position is currently trimmed and holding unrealized loss.
- **Volatility and options activity** [04:36–05:45]: IV is creeping back up (101% front month), partly from earnings expected 25 March; call/put ratio heavily skewed to calls (113k vs 25k), mostly sold below bid (covered calls).
- **Open-interest distribution anomaly** [05:45–07:03]: Unusual concentration in 17 April expiration (second primary cycle) rather than front month; overall options volume is quiet across all expirations.
- **30-day premium flow** [07:03–08:17]: Call/put premium flow has been subdued for weeks; spikes are minimal in context; options market reflects low activity environment.
- **Ryan Cohen Bitcoin response** [08:17–09:41]: Cohen's public "letter received" response to Strive Asset Management CEO's proposal to deploy $5B cash into Bitcoin is designed to build hype and signal shareholder recognition; suggests potential future positioning.
- **Volume bleed on news** [09:41–11:00]: Despite Bitcoin proposal news, volume did not spike meaningfully; average volume continues to decline, partly due to Bitcoin's own poor near-term performance.
- **Covered-strangle position review** [11:00–13:17]: Long 250 shares at $28.21 (underwater); short 25 puts and 35 calls both 21 March; 78% capital utilization; plan to potentially size down short puts by one contract to unlock capital for nibbling on dips during consolidation.
- **Realized P&L despite unrealized loss** [13:17–14:25]: Position has generated ~$1,000 in realized profit from prior rolls (0.45% return); unrealized loss on current short options will likely require rolling; strategy remains viable even in adverse price environment.
