# 2025 Stock Market ALERT with David Hunter | The Outlier Podcast

## Summary

David Hunter returns to discuss 2024 market performance and his multi-year thesis of a "melt-up" followed by a "global bust." He reviews the Japanese Yen carry-trade unwind, the December correction, and current sentiment shifts. Hunter maintains conviction that a parabolic rally phase lies ahead (potentially 30% S&P move in 2–5 months), driven by weakening economic data, lower inflation, and Fed easing—before a severe downturn rooted in systemic leverage. He also covers oil, precious metals, and energy policy under Trump.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2025-01-16)

- **2024 recap**: S&P rallied from ~3,500 to 6,000+; August Yen carry-trade unwind was sharp but brief; post-election rally stalled in December; Russell-2000 corrected ~10–11% while S&P fell <5%, NASDAQ ~6% [01:09–03:40]
- **Current sentiment reversal**: Bulls dropped to ~24%, Bears rose to ~40% in recent weeks; Wall Street strategists raised 2025 targets (6,500–7,100) just before the correction, showing lack of conviction [02:30–03:40]
- **VIX spikes**: December 18 VIX hit 28–32; August saw intraday spike to 65; recent spikes tied to Fed messaging and CPI/inflation data, not fundamental shock [09:49–10:59]
- **Yield curve moves**: 10-year moved from ~3.6% to 4.2–4.4%, then back down; December CPI and Fed meeting triggered sharp repricing [10:59–12:09]
- **Russell-2000 weakness**: Corrected to 200-day MA (~2,160–2,170); Hunter sees bottom and targets 3,300 (50% move) in parabolic phase [27:02–29:28]

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Melt-up vs. parabolic**: Melt-up is the entire rally from Oct 2023 to now; parabolic is the final steep stage, expected to deliver 30% S&P gain in 2–5 months [06:07–07:18]
- **Sentiment-driven corrections**: Small pullbacks (3–5%) now trigger outsized VIX spikes and sentiment swings because investors are conditioned to straight-up markets; each dip rebuilds skepticism needed for further gains [08:33–09:49]
- **Positioning and narrative**: Institutions are "one foot out the door"—bullish on tape momentum but fearful of being late into a potential "global bust"; this fear of systemic collapse is unusual and keeps conviction low [14:26–17:57]
- **Catalyst for parabolic**: Not necessarily Fed cuts (priced for June at ~50%); rather, weak economic data + lower inflation + Fed easing narrative will flip sentiment and drive euphoria [20:08–22:28]
- **Deflation → inflation cycle**: Hunter expects deflation first (weak economy, lower CPI), then massive fiscal/monetary response post-bust (~$20T Fed expansion) leading to 25% inflation by early next decade [35:39–36:52]
- **Trump policy narrative**: Initial post-election rally faded due to yield/dollar moves and tariff-inflation fears; Hunter disagrees with CBO/economist scoring (static models, supply-side skepticism); expects pro-growth narrative to re-emerge and boost markets [30:42–34:25]
- **Small-cap reversal**: Russell underperformance in Dec was positioning-driven—too many jumped on small-cap bandwagon after summer/election runs; correction "put them in their place"; recovery likely as sentiment rebuilds [25:56–28:11]

### Commodities & energy outlook

- **Oil**: Current high ~77–80; Hunter expects next leg down to 60 or high 50s as global demand softens (Germany negative GDP, China deflation); Trump's "drill baby drill" increases supply; long-term bust target: $30 [39:43–42:01]
- **Gold**: Breaking out of consolidation wedge; target 3,400 (first half 2025); may exceed without surprise [44:07–45:20]
- **Silver**: Lagging; corrected to ~30 from 35.5; Hunter targets mid-40s next, then 75 (longer timeframe); gold/silver ratio expected to compress from 80+ toward 40–45, favoring silver outperformance [44:07–46:28]
- **Metals miners**: Discouraged by repeated failed rallies; "show me" investors will enter later; Hunter's long-term targets: gold $20,000, silver $500 by early next decade [46:28–48:47]
- **Bitcoin**: No macro interaction with gold/silver; must "prove itself in the bust"; Hunter has no opinion on post-bust BTC price [47:39–48:47]
- **Technology metals & nuclear**: Silver benefits from EV, solar, tech; lithium demand rising; nuclear energy (small modular reactors) will play larger role under Trump; reshoring and commodity demand will spike post-bust [49:58–54:50]
- **Post-bust regime**: Interest rates will ratchet up from 0% to 20%+, unlike the 42-year decline from 15–20% to near-zero; growth stocks will underperform; commodities and resources will be the "name of the game" [51:18–54:50]

## Regime / context

Recorded 2025-01-16, one week after the December 18 market top and VIX spike. Hunter's multi-year thesis (melt-up → global bust) remains intact; he views the current correction as a healthy sentiment reset within the broader uptrend, not the start of the bust. The parabolic phase is expected to unfold over the next 2–5 months (Feb–Jun 2025), driven by weakening data and Fed easing narrative. Post-bust deflation and subsequent inflation cycle are forecast for later 2025 onward. Trump inauguration (Jan 20, 2025) is imminent; Hunter expects initial policy-driven rally but notes it has been overshadowed by recent yield/sentiment moves.
