# Roaring Kitty's Big CHWY Sale & Surprising Moves in GME and DJT!

## Summary

Eric analyzes Roaring Kitty's recent 13G filing showing a complete exit from Chewy (CHWY), examines the resulting GameStop (GME) volume spike and call open interest surge, and discusses his own speculative position in DJT amid a parabolic move driven by election sentiment rather than fundamentals. The video emphasizes the risks of selling premium in high-IV, high-delta environments and the distinction between IV-based and directional trading.

## Key takeaways

- **Roaring Kitty's Chewy exit [00:00–02:07]:** The 13G filing dated 30 September shows complete unwinding of ~9M shares; the actual exit likely occurred around 20 September. The position appears to have generated minimal profit or loss, suggesting it was a speculative play rather than a conviction trade.

- **GME volume and call OI spike [02:07–04:09]:** GME opened at ~20.83 and closed at ~22.73 on 28 October (a significant run). Call OI stands at 623,000 vs. 226,000 puts; 32% of today's 188,000 transactions were calls, concentrated in the 1 November expiration (3 days out). Eric bought April 2025 calls as a cheap tester position ahead of the volume expansion.

- **DJT parabolic move and speculation [05:13–08:30]:** DJT has moved from ~11.75 to ~55 in one month (24 September to 29 October), with today's volume at 167M vs. 5-day average of 85M. IV is 327% (100th percentile); open interest shows 68,000 puts vs. 436,000 calls. Eric has taken partial profits and holds runners; warns against selling premium in this environment because high IV does not eliminate directional (delta) risk.

- **Premium-selling pitfall [08:30–09:27]:** High IV tempts traders to "sell if IV is high," but in a stock moving 8–10% daily, short options with undefined risk remain dangerous regardless of IV level. Delta still matters; IV clustering does not guarantee mean reversion.
