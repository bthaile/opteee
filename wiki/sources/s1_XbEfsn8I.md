# Using PnL Management Plans For Better Trades

## Summary

This video emphasizes the importance of establishing a pre-trade P&L management plan before entering any position. Rather than relying on arbitrary price targets, traders should define their profit mechanism and identify specific technical or price-action conditions that signal when a trade has exhausted its thesis—such as a close below a key moving average—and when to exit.

## Key takeaways

- **Define your profit mechanism before entry** [00:00] — Know what you're trying to capture (e.g., breakout, near-term momentum) and plan exits accordingly, not just arbitrary price levels.
- **Use technical conditions as exit triggers** [00:22] — If your thesis is momentum-based on a 10-day moving average, plan to exit on a close below that MA, signaling the trend has reversed.
- **Identify when your thesis is wrong** [00:45] — Recognize that momentum will eventually slow; establish the specific condition (price action, technical level) that tells you the trade has run its course and it's time to move on.
