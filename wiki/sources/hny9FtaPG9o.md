# Losses that haunt me: options trading wiped out my savings

## Summary

This video dissects a Reddit post from a trader who lost approximately 88 million (likely in local currency) over two years through options trading, examining the psychological and structural failures that led to catastrophic losses. Eric outlines how retail traders lack the institutional risk controls that protect professional traders, and demonstrates concrete, implementable safeguards—circuit breakers and trade logging—that make such losses entirely avoidable.

## Key takeaways

- **Appeal to authority bias**: Professional status in finance does not translate to trading skill; institutional and retail trading operate under fundamentally different structural constraints [01:24].
- **Institutional vs. retail edge**: Institutions have dedicated risk management departments and firm-wide risk limits; retail traders must build these controls themselves [01:24–02:45].
- **Risk control at entry**: The only moment you can fully control trade risk is at entry, via position sizing and defined risk structures [02:45].
- **Sensation-seeking behavior**: Research (2006 paper on sensation seeking and overconfidence) shows traders frequently chase dopamine and winning trades rather than building solid foundational approaches [03:50].
- **Revenge trading and gambler's fallacy**: Small losses escalate when traders add capital to "make it back," a classic avoidant behavior pattern [04:00–05:07].
- **Dunning-Kruger effect in trading**: After initial learning, overconfidence leads to mistakes; the "valley of despair" is survivable only with basic portfolio controls [05:07].
- **Circuit breakers**: Set a portfolio loss threshold (e.g., stop adding capital if portfolio drops X%) based on strategy analysis, average drawdown, and risk tolerance [06:07].
- **Trade logging by strategy**: Maintain a detailed trade log organized by strategy with summary statistics (win rate, average PNL, drawdown); this feedback loop prevents blind capital-chasing [07:23–08:39].
- **Portfolio planning**: Define desired return and risk tolerance upfront; ensure composite trades fit within risk limits; validate strategy performance iteratively [09:50].
