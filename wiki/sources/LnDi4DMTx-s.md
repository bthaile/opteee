### Evergreen mechanics

- **Volume precedes price moves** [25:46]
  - Consistent across all analyzed securities; traders should prioritize volume analysis over narrative
  
- **Implied volatility percentile vs. rank** [27:11]
  - IV percentile better compensates for outlier moves via calculation method; IV rank remains skewed by historical extremes for ~1 year post-event

- **Options flow as positioning proxy** [01:03:22–01:23:56]
  - Large, repeated premium hits on same strike/expiration across days = informed scaling; single large trades = less conclusive
  - Longer-dated positioning (further out in time) healthier than front-month concentration

- **Volatility skew interpretation** [01:25:24–01:36:40]
  - Bullish skew: 50-delta strike sits above ATM (e.g., 31-strike at 51 delta vs. typical 50-delta ATM) → upside expectation priced in
  - Call/put ratio and premium distribution reveal market bias without requiring directional conviction

- **Covered-call mechanics on meme stocks** [01:29:20]
  - Target short strike above cost basis; adjust by rolling to maintain equity exposure if needed
  - High realized volatility clustering → avoid selling calls during volatility transitory phases; sell into normalization

- **Retail analysis pitfalls** [33:08–51:20]
  - Confirmation bias: analyzing to prove hypothesis rather than test it (black-vs.-color-counting analogy)
  - "Everybody's against us" narrative leads to skewed decision-making; objectivity preserves edge
  - Underestimating counterparty sophistication: shorts are smart; game-theory logic often contradicts retail narratives
