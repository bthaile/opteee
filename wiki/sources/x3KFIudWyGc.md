# Fed Rate Cut and Tech Stocks - Stock Market Update

## Summary

Eric reviews market conditions following a Fed rate cut, highlighting deteriorating breadth (41% of S&P 500 above 50-day MA) and unusual divergence between index highs and declining breadth metrics. He walks through a profitable zero-DTE short straddle trade executed around the Fed announcement, demonstrating gamma management and volatility sampling. The session covers earnings season mechanics, AI infrastructure plays (particularly nuclear energy), and a detailed framework for analyzing options flow and volatility skew using Black-Scholes-Merton pricing.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2025-10-30)

- **Fed cut delivered; December probability shifted** [23:49] — Fed cut as expected, but probability of no change in December ticked from ~0% to ~33%, signaling potential pause. Tariff uncertainty and government shutdown complicate forward guidance.
- **Breadth deterioration despite index highs** [07:29–11:01] — Only 41% of S&P 500 above 50-day MA; gross lows peaking on both NYSE and NASDAQ (top-3 worst on record). This divergence is "not normal" and warrants close attention, especially for leveraged strategies like covered strangles in QQQ.
- **VIX elevated but artificially suppressed** [14:38–15:08] — VIX remains slightly elevated vs. historic norms, but Eric argues nominal VIX should be higher if ancillary market activities (VIX-suppressing mechanisms) were stripped out. Volatility surface on longer-term SPX shows this tension.
- **Google Q3 revenue milestone** [27:40] — Alphabet announced first-ever quarter with >$100B revenue; 30% share surge created ~$770B stockholder wealth since September. AI tailwind driving tech sector.
- **Nuclear energy setup emerging** [39:13] — Uranium/nuclear plays (e.g., CCJ) moved from $15 to $50, now consolidating sideways. Infrastructure phase of AI energy demand may reignite these names; CCJ earnings on Nov 6.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Zero-DTE gamma management** [16:49–22:54] — Short straddle entered ~1:19 PM (post-Fed presser), initially priced at ~69.05, exited at 11:50 AM at ~68.75 for $1.22 loss despite ~0.5% adverse delta move. Realized volatility spiked above implied; Eric rebalanced deltas rather than stop-loss, then re-entered and held into expiration for healthy profit. Key: on zero-DTE, even 1-minute candle ranges (68.86–68.92) can knock profitable straddles out of zone; active management essential.
- **Volatility sampling and momentum** [41:21–41:44] — Eric samples realized volatility perpetually throughout the day, internalized differently by timeframe. Does not forecast volatility better than market, but recognizes volatility has momentum component; pays close attention to realized-vs-implied divergence.
- **Earnings season as forward guidance proxy** [06:01–06:26] — Earnings provide real-time company expectations; Eric trades earnings heavily and uses forward guidance to inform broader market thesis.
- **Breadth as momentum indicator** [08:01–10:31] — Gross lows (new 52-week lows) peaking while index makes new highs is a deviation signal, not a trade trigger. Overlay price action with breadth peaks to identify pullback zones; most useful when both NYSE and NASDAQ show synchronized peaks.
- **Options flow as supporting (not primary) factor** [46:01–46:42] — Unusual options activity is a strong supporting signal but rarely primary. Requires parameterization and backtesting over months to assess predictive power; eyeball test (visual pattern recognition) is a starting point, not a system.
- **Volatility skew visualization and pricing** [01:04:15–01:08:34] — Skew (smile) is easiest to see by plotting IV across strikes at a single expiration. To isolate skew premium: price option using ATM IV, then reprice using strike IV; difference is skew premium. Put skew common pre-earnings on institutional names (demand for downside protection); call skew (e.g., GME, TSLA) signals perceived upside tail risk. Use Treasury duration matching to back out risk-free rate for BSM.
- **AI adoption follows historical tech patterns** [31:40–35:02] — AI capacity is fundamentally different from internet (not just information gap), but adoption curve mirrors prior tech cycles. Still in infrastructure phase (chip supply, energy demand); many millionaires yet to be made. Latency reduction post-internet reduced bubble-formation lag, but leverage and order flow can still cause flash crashes.
- **Price action rejection as entry signal** [35:30–37:27] — Nokia day trade: rejected at prior resistance with 5.5% intraday drop despite strong gap-up. Eric exited most of position same-day based on price behavior near top, not on "missed upside." Confidence in process > regret over unrealized gains.
