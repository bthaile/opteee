# Covered Strangle in GameStop | Meme Stock Watch Ep51

## Summary
A Meme Stock Watch livestream centered on why a covered strangle is the host's favored way to trade GameStop. The core lesson: even when GME grinds sideways with a suppressed IV rank/percentile, its volatility risk premium stays fat (implied priced richer than realized), which is exactly the condition that rewards premium-selling structures like the covered strangle and the wheel. He shows his own GME covered strangle up ~28.8% since December while the underlying is down ~8.9% — both long-biased, with the difference driven entirely by volatility capture. The stream also covers a fixed-risk stop/position-sizing method and a long, statistically-minded breakdown of Roaring Kitty's GME returns.

## Key takeaways
- "Meme stock" is used descriptively, not pejoratively: a stock that is popular or gains popularity through social media. By that definition even Nvidia qualifies; it says nothing about quality or manipulability. [05:40]
- GameStop is far more volatile than it feels: its 20-day range (~23%) is about the same as Coca-Cola's entire 12-month range (~22%), making GME roughly 3x more volatile annually. GME only looks "quiet" relative to itself (IV percentile ~22%). [10:21]
- Why the covered strangle works here: risk premiums in GME have been fat since early July even though IV rank/percentile is low. Volatility can be low yet still rich — implied priced above realized — which is ideal for a covered strangle or the wheel. [15:02]
- Live P&L proof: his covered strangle is up ~28.8% since ~18 December (entry when GME was ~$29), while spot at ~$26.42 is down ~8.9%. Volatility capture, not direction, drove the gain. [16:43]
- The GME→GME1 warrant/ticker change (100 shares + 100 warrants per contract) does not change his approach; he is trading a plain foundational covered strangle. Original allocation ~$20–25k, now ~$35k purely from realized profits. [36:15]
- Roaring Kitty math: ~$34.5M to ~$210M over ~4 years is ~57% CAGR — very aggressive but not impossible given scale, a favorable path, and luck. It normalizes over a 20–30 year career and would not persist. [19:11]
- No evidence Roaring Kitty sold options (covered calls / cash-secured puts) — only long calls appear in what was posted, mostly moderately- to long-dated, averaged into, and usually managed before expiration. Claims of a predictable swap/cycle are unsupported. [32:36]
- Objective-study discipline: start from a neutral hypothesis and try to punch holes in it rather than hunting for a predetermined outcome ("poison fruit from the tree"); most perceived cycles are confirmation bias. [38:53]
- Stop / sizing framework: choose a portfolio risk budget (e.g. 1% of $100k = $1,000), define invalidation levels (below the volume node near $25.79, then below the breakout low ~$24), map the option's max loss to those levels, and size contracts to fit — splitting exits across levels. Prefers price alerts over resting stop orders. [49:40]
- For directional GME longs he'd favor slightly ITM (~65 delta), 90+ DTE calls over near-term, far-OTM options that "light money on fire." [50:31]

## Regime / context
- As of 2025-09-28, GME had been channeling sideways since mid-June, with a recent earnings-catalyzed gap up. IV percentile was ~22% (quiet only relative to itself), yet the volatility risk premium had been persistently fat since early July.
- The covered-strangle edge described here is regime-dependent: it works because implied vol stays richer than realized. If that premium compresses, the edge fades even if the chart looks the same.
- Meme-stock-specific mechanics are in flux: the GME options multiplier is changing (GME→GME1, 100 shares + 100 warrants per contract), though the host says it does not alter his covered-strangle plan.
