# Earnings Reports, DJT Move, & GME's Next Big Shift | What's Driving the Stock Market Today?

## Summary

Mixed sector rotation within the S&P 500 despite broad market pullback; technology (XLK) underperforming while financials and energy show strength. DJT exhibits extreme volatility (IV percentile at 100%) as a proxy for election outcome pricing. GameStop announces PSA trading-card grading partnership, signaling operational pivot; retail options activity remains heavily concentrated in front expirations, contrasting with [[roaring-kitty]]'s historical preference for longer-dated positions.

## Key takeaways

- **Broad market mixed despite pullback** [00:00–02:07]: S&P 500 down on the day, but XLF, XLI, XLC all hit highs; XLK (technology) had significant down day; most sectors up slightly, indicating rotation rather than universal selloff.

- **GameStop–PSA partnership announcement** [06:42–09:53]: GameStop becomes authorized PSA dealer for trading-card grading in select stores; provides physical retail purpose; no massive price move yet; structure and pricing details unclear.
- **GME options: front-expiration concentration vs. historical precedent** [10:51–13:22]: 712k calls vs. 290k puts; 338k calls in front expiration (47%); retail heavily weighted to near-term; [[roaring-kitty]] historical trades show preference for 52–137 DTE, with only one 3-DTE event-driven play (earnings, expired worthless).
