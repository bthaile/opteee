# GameStop Options Analysis & Post-Earnings Setup

## Summary

Eric analyzes GameStop's post-earnings options chain and price action, highlighting the shift in open-interest distribution toward the 17 January expiration (~35 DTE) rather than front-month contracts—a relatively new pattern for the stock. The stock closed at ~$27.99 post-earnings, consolidating in a tightening range that reflects healthy price discovery and rising support levels. Key observations include light overall volatility (sub-100 IV across most strikes), heavy call volume driven by position rolling, and a large anomalous trade in April 2025 $35 calls that likely represents profit-taking.

## Key takeaways

- **Open-interest distribution shift** [01:02]: Unlike typical meme stocks, GME's heaviest OI is now ~35 DTE (17 Jan) rather than front expirations—a month-long pattern worth monitoring as a baseline change.
- **Call-to-put ratio as GME-specific signal** [02:10]: Calls exceeding puts is normal for GME (inverse of SPY); the meaningful metric is the *ratio itself*—when calls exceed puts 2:1 or greater, it historically couples with higher volatility and movement expectations.
- **Post-earnings consolidation is healthy** [04:28]: Stock hovering in high $26–$29 range post-earnings (not driven by mania or short covering) reflects genuine repricing upward; tightening consolidation suggests price discovery and consensus-building.
- **Support floor rising over time** [06:41]: GME's support level has drifted from ~$19.32 to current ~$26–$27 range, indicating sustained higher consensus valuation.
- **Earnings beat but muted move** [07:41]: $0.95 EPS beat to $1.25 is solid, but most came from interest on cash hoard (not core business growth); explains lack of post-earnings announcement drift—expect PEAD once operational earnings strengthen.
- **Volume analysis: position rolling** [08:45]: Heavy call volume today was predominantly sales (13D data shows ~181k calls closing in front month, redistributing to 17 Jan and beyond)—standard practice to maintain exposure while extending duration.
- **Large anomalous trade: April $35 calls** [09:51]: 3,350 contracts at $5.40 (bid $5.35, ask $5.75) likely sold (profit-taking given recent run-up); transacted as bundle order indicating institutional liquidity.
- **Far-OTM calls: retail vs. institutional** [12:02]: Both retail and institutions populate far-OTM strikes, but for different reasons—retail faces bankroll erosion risk on low-hit-rate trades (expect 2 hits per 100 attempts); institutions amortize expected hits across large capital bases and size appropriately.
- **Volatility regime** [08:45]: IV sub-100 across all but front month (17 Jan at ~100%+) indicates quiet market; front-month always elevated post-earnings.
