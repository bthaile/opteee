# How Things Started — Origin Story & 3 Lessons That Changed Everything

## Summary

Eric recounts his YouTube origin story (sparked by a beekeeper's offhand comment in April 2020) and shares two formative trading experiences: a catastrophic $50k loss on a wide Russell iron condor that taught him the necessity of [[trading-plan]] and [[process-over-outcome]], and his best two trading years (with 31.6% compound annual growth rate) that demonstrated the power of consistency and downside protection. He distills three core lessons: prioritize process over individual trade P&L, moderate (not eliminate) ego, and think differently from the majority.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2020-05-22)
- YouTube channel genesis: May 4, 2020, during COVID lockdown; Eric began recording to help military friends understand investing basics rather than repeat phone calls [00:00–12:55]
- Beekeeper anecdote (April 21, 2020): A bee colony swarm incident and the beekeeper's casual question "Are you a YouTuber?" planted the subconscious seed, though Eric had previously disdained social media [12:55–17:11]

### Evergreen mechanics

- **The $50k loss (5 years into trading career):** Wide Russell iron condor with 10-delta shorts, targeting $500 profit on high probability; position moved $15–50k against him within days; Eric froze, unable to act, then closed for a loss that would have been profitable if held [24:17–30:49]
  - Root cause: "Goldilocks analysis" — obsession with the $500 target blinded him to lifecycle risk and behavioral drift
  - Forced a complete trading system overhaul: adopted formal [[trading-plan]] using military planning discipline, moved to [[paper-trading]], rebuilt from first principles [30:49–34:45]

- **Consistency beats volatility:** Portfolio 1 (steady 2–10% annual returns, 7.4% average) outperforms Portfolio 3 (−25% to +30%, 8.6% average) on compound annual growth rate (7.35% vs 6.87%) and ending value, despite lower average return [46:08–49:05]
  - Key insight: Outperforming the market in down years (2008: −37% market, −4.2% Eric; 2018: −4.4% market, +13.8% Eric; 2022: −19.4% market, +21.2% Eric) compounds more powerfully than chasing upside [49:05–50:26]

- **Three lessons that changed everything:**
  1. **Process over outcome:** Don't focus on individual trade P&L; reward yourself for following process. Thousands of trades compound; no single trade matters (within normal risk bounds) [01:08:01–01:09:31]
  2. **Moderated ego:** Ego drives ambition but unchecked ego manifests as timidity (avoiding trades) or rationalization (discounting losses). Control it, don't eliminate it [01:09:31–01:12:08]
  3. **Think differently:** Majority of traders fail; you must operate outside the consensus. Start by asking "How do others do this?" then pivot [01:12:08–01:13:22]

- **Wealth-building philosophy:** Multiple income streams (trading, side work, angel investing, real estate) create robustness; single W2 dependency is fragile [57:18–59:51]
- **Return targets:** Eric lowered annual targets from 15% → 12% → 10% → 8% → 5–6% as account grew; last two years (with lower targets) became best years ever, teaching that opportunity pursuit matters more than return chasing [41:38–43:31]
- **Giveaway:** One-on-one consultation offered to winner; emphasis on paying forward mentorship received [09:26–10:34]
