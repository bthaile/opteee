# Process Driven Trading Success

## Summary

Eric outlines the foundational principle of process-driven trading: building observable, quantifiable systems for market analysis, theme scanning, and decision-making. By applying Kaizen philosophy to cyclical trading tasks, traders can achieve slower initial setup but dramatically faster and more consistent execution over time.

## Key takeaways

- **Observable and quantifiable systems** [00:00] — Process-driven trading requires metrics you can measure and adjust, enabling continuous improvement rather than relying on intuition or outcome-dependent thinking.
- **Kaizen efficiency gains** [00:27] — Applying the Kaizen philosophy (continuous incremental improvement) to repetitive trading tasks like scanning and theme analysis yields compounding speed and consistency gains.
- **Upfront cost, long-run payoff** [00:27] — Developing a robust process is slower initially but dramatically accelerates execution and decision quality over the long run, especially for cyclical activities.

## Notable quotes

> "The reason for that is because I need things that are observable that I can quantify so that I can adjust and pivot over time."
