# Best Option Strike #optionstrading #options

## Summary

There is no universally "best" strike for an options trade—strike selection is entirely relative to your specific trading objective. Options trading introduces complexity (time decay, volatility exposure, delta movement) that must be managed deliberately; the primary benefit of options is the ability to structure a position precisely around your thesis rather than applying generic rules.

## Key takeaways

- **No universal best strike** [00:00] — strike selection depends entirely on what you're trying to accomplish as a trader; avoid broad platitudes that eliminate options' strategic specificity.
- **Options add multiple constraints** [00:20] — you must be correct on direction (delta), within a time window (theta), and account for volatility changes (vega); this complexity is a feature, not a bug.
- **Structure around your objective** [00:35] — define your trading goal first, then build the options position to match it; don't reverse-engineer a thesis from a "best" strike.
