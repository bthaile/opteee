# Ratio Call Diagonal Trade Recap - NEM

## Summary

Eric walks through a completed ratio call diagonal position on NEM (Newmont Mining) that returned 84.5% in under 40 days. The trade capitalized on a sector rotation into metals and mining driven by inflationary concerns, using a long June call position financed by short calls rolled higher as the stock moved in-the-money. The position was exited at profit after a strong breakout, with key management decisions centered on rolling short calls to preserve upside and using moving averages as a trailing exit.

## Key takeaways

- **Sector rotation thesis** [00:41–01:50]: Energy and materials (especially metals & mining) rotating into favor over communication services; NEM identified as a beneficiary of capital rotation in an inflationary environment.
- **Entry setup** [02:16–03:00]: Alert set at 31.31 resistance; entered on March 4 after volume expansion and breakout above 30.2 handle; speculative allocation (~$25k initial).
- **Ratio call diagonal structure** [04:30–05:51]: 45 long June 30 calls (70-delta, liquidity-constrained); initially sold only 10 short calls at 32 delta to maximize upside potential; collected $0.60 credit.
- **First roll management** [07:49–08:29]: March 20 roll: moved short calls from 34 to 35 strike, extended to April 19, collected $0.88 ($0.20 net credit); short calls fell deep ITM and dragged returns.
- **Scaling decision** [08:59–09:45]: Added 5 contracts on March 28 breakout above 35.22; raised stop from 31.70 to 34+ (locking profit), then to 35.26; acknowledged scaling mistake given time constraint.
- **Exit mechanics** [10:30–11:15]: Used 9–10 day moving average as trailing exit; exited all 50 long calls on April 11 at $9.10 average (bought $4.55); realized P&L: $22,765 long calls − $3,550 short calls = **$19,215 total (84.5% ROI)**.
- **Timing constraint** [06:37–07:02]: Originally planned to manage before April 25 earnings; travel and lack of sustained follow-through prompted earlier unwind; position ultimately gave enough time to capture the move.

## Notable quotes

## Regime / context

**Date:** April 12, 2024. Trade executed March 4–April 11, 2024 (39 days).

**Market backdrop:** Inflationary environment with "higher for longer" rate expectations; sector rotation from technology into energy and materials; earnings cycle approaching (NEM earnings April 25).

**Position lifecycle:** Entry on volume breakout; two rolls of short calls to manage ITM assignment risk and preserve upside; exit triggered by moving-average stop and timing constraint (travel). The short calls served as drag on returns despite the large underlying move, illustrating the trade-off between financing long premium and capping upside in a ratio structure.
