# What Indicators REALLY Work Simple Wins

## Summary

A foundational exploration of what makes trading indicators effective, emphasizing that simplicity and information generation matter more than complexity or "holy grail" systems. The video critiques the appeal of overpowered or perfect-setup indicators (Fibonacci, Ichimoku, Donchian channels) and reframes indicator use as a tool for market analysis and decision-making rather than a mechanical signal source.

## Key takeaways

- **Simplicity beats complexity** [00:49] — simple indicators tend to work better than elaborate multi-component systems; avoid the trap of seeking "overpowered" or "perfect" indicators.
- **Indicators are information tools, not signals** [00:26] — an indicator's job is to analyze the market and generate information to support decisions, not to replace judgment.
- **Zoom out on indicator selection** [00:26] — resist the YouTube algorithm's promotion of flashy indicator names (Fibonacci, Ichimoku, Donchian); focus on what actually works for your process.
- **Avoid the holy grail trap** [00:00] — the search for a perfect, mechanical setup is a common distraction; prioritize understanding market mechanics over indicator stacking.
