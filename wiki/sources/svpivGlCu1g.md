# HOW to Use AI as an Options Trader

## Summary

Eric walks through a three-step framework for integrating AI into options trading: landscape analysis (understanding available AI tools and their capabilities), execution (categorizing trading tasks and using AI for learning, research, strategy design, and process auditing), and practical application via prompt engineering. The video emphasizes that AI is an accelerator and decision-support tool, not a replacement for trader judgment, and demonstrates live examples of prompts for learning options fundamentals, building volatility surfaces, market analysis, and earnings strategy development.

## Key takeaways

- **Start with landscape analysis** [02:17]: Understand what AI tools exist (ChatGPT for reasoning, Perplexity for research, Grok for market sentiment, Claude for programming, Gemini for querying personal research files) and their specific strengths before deploying them.
- **Categorize your trading tasks** [04:33]: Use AI to assist with learning, news synthesis, research, strategy design, trading plan creation, and process auditing—not as a replacement for decision-making but as an accelerator.
- **Prime the AI with context** [10:02]: Begin every conversation by telling the AI your role ("I'm an options trader") and what functions you need it to perform; this dramatically improves response quality.
- **Use iterative prompt refinement** [07:53]: Treat prompting as a coaching process—if the first response isn't what you need, refine your language and ask follow-up questions to guide the AI toward your actual goal.
- **Always demand sources** [08:57]: Require the AI to cite sources for claims so you can fact-check; AI frequently makes confident errors and you remain the ultimate decision-maker.
- **Build learning scaffolds** [11:29]: Have AI create course outlines, teach specific topics, generate quizzes with answer keys, and verify your understanding—this accelerates competency building.
- **Create reusable templates** [19:55]: Use AI to generate trade log templates, pre-trade checklists (both standardized and strategy-specific), and strategy outlines that you can test and iterate on.
- **Use AI as a process auditor** [22:56]: Upload or describe your trades and ask for specific feedback on how to improve your process; ask "what questions should I ask you next?" to identify knowledge gaps.
- **Save effective prompts** [18:12]: Store prompts that work well so you can reuse them across tools; some AI (ChatGPT) remembers custom formats, others (Perplexity) require manual copy-paste.
- **Leverage Gemini's file integration** [06:51]: If you use Google Drive, Gemini can query your entire research library at once—a powerful advantage for backtesting and strategy validation.
