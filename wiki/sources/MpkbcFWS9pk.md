# GME Strategy Session: Analyzing the Stock and Options Chain

## Summary

Eric analyzes GameStop's current consolidation phase following the May squeeze, examining volume patterns, price structure, and his personal position management. He reviews the options chain in detail, noting a shift in open interest toward August 16 expiration and discussing unusual order flow activity, while emphasizing the importance of waiting for price confirmation before adding capital.

## Key takeaways

- **Volume baseline during consolidation** [01:06–01:28]: After the May pop, GME volume has contracted significantly below the recent regime high. This quiet period is actually useful for identifying clean triggers and baseline behavior before the next move.

- **Price structure: higher highs and higher lows** [08:45–09:42]: The key technical level to watch is the July 1 term low (closed at 23.33). If price holds above this, it signals healthy consolidation; breaking below signals weakness. Currently trading in a 23–28 range.

- **Personal position: scaled out meaningfully** [03:46–05:09]: Eric has reduced his core position substantially, maintains short calls for premium collection, and keeps a small speculative position to participate in the story. He scales out on turn days and when price falls below points of control to lock in profits.

- **Trading philosophy: wait for price confirmation** [06:28–07:35]: Eric does not pre-position based on expectations; he waits to see price behavior before committing capital. The opportunity cost of parking money in a consolidating stock is too high.

- **Options chain: call skew still bullish but lower than historical** [13:18–14:01]: 729k calls vs. 400k puts (roughly 1.8:1) is bullish but lower than typical GME ratios. Context matters—this is not as extreme as it has been.

- **Volume migration to August 16** [14:19–16:08]: Most new positioning is moving out to August 16 expiration (197k calls) rather than staying in front expirations (178k). This suggests traders expect the next significant move to occur around that Opex.

- **Unusual order flow: $200k call purchase in September** [17:58–20:39]: A single large buyer purchased 500 of the September 20 calls at the ask (~$200k position), making them the dominant volume in that illiquid expiration. The choice of this strike/expiration is puzzling given superior liquidity one week later.

- **Company fundamentals remain solid** [11:51–12:34]: Cohen's leadership, the at-the-money offering (which raised cash without massive dilution), and the roadmap for innovation position GameStop well operationally, despite meme-stock dismissal.

## Notable quotes

> "I'm very reluctant to let winning trades turn into losing trades, so when I see turn days like this I start scaling out." [04:31]

> "I have no problem not bottom-ticking it. I'm trying to catch the majority of moves and avoid parking money in something until it starts really exhibiting some behavior in a strong direction." [11:07]
