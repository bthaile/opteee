# Finding Opportunities with Relative Volatility

## Summary

This video explores how to identify trading opportunities by analyzing relative volatility across the volatility surface—both within a single expiration (the smile) and across expirations (term structure). Using SLV as an example, the host demonstrates how to spot anomalies where near-term volatility inverts relative to longer-dated volatility, creating potential setups for diagonal spreads and calendar trades.

## Key takeaways

- **Volatility surface analysis**: Compare implied volatility across strikes within an expiration (smile) and across expirations (term structure) to find mispricings [00:00–00:28]
- **Concrete example in SLV**: The 16-day expiration volatility between 60–70 strike was higher than 31-day volatility, suggesting a potential trade [00:00–00:28]
- **Flipping relationships**: Volatility term structure relationships flip back and forth; these reversals can signal trade ideas [00:28]
- **Deep troughs in the surface**: Look for peaky or anomalous areas in the volatility surface compared to surrounding levels [00:28–00:49]
- **Diagonal and calendar trades**: Once you identify relative volatility dislocations, run diagonal spreads or calendar spreads to exploit them [00:28–00:49]
- **Term volatility rotation**: Monitor when short-dated volatility (5–7 day) is declining while longer-dated volatility (60-day) is expanding—a common regime shift [00:49]
