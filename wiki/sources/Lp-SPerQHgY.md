# Erik's Trading Performance Review | 2026 H1 AAR

## Summary

Erik presents a detailed year-to-date performance review showing approximately 33% returns through mid-2026, driven primarily by speculative positions and a maturing skill set. The core allocation (covered strangles in TQs, IBIT, and GameStop) contributes modestly, while speculative upside, volatility, and futures strategies generate the bulk of returns. He discusses the evolution of his return targets from 15% annually (set in 2007) down to inflation-beating returns, and attributes recent outperformance to skill maturation, favorable regime conditions, and a lower return imperative that enables stricter trade selection.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2026-07-08)

- **Year-to-date performance: ~33% return** [01:19] — hovering around this level with core allocation contributing modestly and speculative strategies driving bulk of gains.
- **Market regime diversity in H1 2026** [14:17] — experienced stable sideways trending volatility, expanding volatility with spot down, V-shaped recovery with aggressive spot up, and distribution off highs; creates favorable environment for regime-following traders.
- **June market weakness** [02:47] — market down approximately 1.25%, impacting covered strangle positions that are sensitive to such moves.
- **Core allocation performance** [02:21] — TQs position and IBIT have been challenging; GameStop covered strangle earning capital and outperforming despite modest underlying move (9% YTD underlying, 21% strategy return).

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Return target evolution** [05:43–08:41] — started with 15% annual target in 2007; progressively lowered to 10%, then 8%, now targeting inflation-beating returns (~6%); lower targets enable stricter trade selection and better risk management.
- **Compounding vs. average returns** [07:17–08:15] — portfolio with consistent mid-range returns and controlled downside (25% down year) compounds better than high-volatility portfolio with larger average return; demonstrates why consistent 15% CAGR beats chasing 30% up years.
- **Skill maturation as performance driver** [10:24–11:18] — individual strategy performance, return clustering, and P&L volatility all show improvement; luck remains non-trivial factor but diminishes with consistent execution over extended duration.
- **Lower return imperative enables selectivity** [11:45–12:55] — trivial return target forces "no" stance on most opportunities; only positions with compelling risk-reward enter portfolio; creates natural filter against overtrading.
- **Core allocation structure** [13:50–14:17] — covered strangles (TQs, IBIT, GameStop) and box spreads for capital earmarking; no long-term holdings; all actively traded; no long-term stock positions.
- **Speculative bucket composition** [14:48–17:36]:
  - **Speculative upside**: breakouts, post-earnings drift, momentum, cross-sectional momentum, wide vertical spreads.
  - **Speculative volatility**: index ball (S&P, NDX, Russell 2000), zero-DTE, overnight plays, forced flow.
  - **Speculative futures**: hedging activity (market-close news, weekend news), weekend futures open, broad futures momentum with leverage.
  - **Speculative downside**: directional downside trades.
  - **Speculative V**: V expansion/contraction, long V into earnings, long V through catalysts, cross-ETP volatility trading.
- **Position count and sizing** [24:29–27:08] — typical book: 8–30 positions (one standard deviation range ~14 center); can spike to 100+ during earnings season; 6–15 trades per day average (including adjustments and rebalancing).
- **Low-touch vs. high-touch strategies** [22:18–24:04]:
  - **Low-touch**: earnings plays (work done in screening), most volatility ETP trades, index volatility; covered strangles (defined execution, minimal active decision-making).
  - **High-touch**: volatility ETP trades (background analysis and adjustments), active directional trades requiring monitoring.
- **Early trading history (2007–2011)** [27:58–30:24] — first five years: underperformed in 2007 (down 1.1% vs. market up 5%), 2008 (down 4.2% vs. market down 37.2%), beat in 2009 (up 18.3% vs. market up 27%), matched in 2010 (up 14.7% vs. market up 14.9%), beat in 2011 (up 14% vs. market up 2%); early years marked by capital shyness and risk aversion; first year beating market was year five.
- **Drawdown management** [18:42–19:44] — no negative months in last five years; estimated maximum intra-month drawdown 8–12% (conservative buffer: no more than 25%); typical monthly drawdown 3–6%; does not track Sharpe/Sortino at portfolio level (tracked at strategy level for sizing).
