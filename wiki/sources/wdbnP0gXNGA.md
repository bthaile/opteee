# Do All Your Work BEFORE Entering Trades #trading

## Summary

Eric argues that most traders focus excessively on entry and exit mechanics (stop-loss placement, profit targets) when they should instead complete all analytical work *before* entering a position. The core principle: if a stock reaches fair value with no remaining edge, exit—not because of a predetermined stop or target, but because the thesis is exhausted. This reflects a systematic, pre-planned approach rather than discretionary decision-making at the moment of trade.

## Key takeaways

- **Pre-trade work is the foundation** [00:00–00:40]: Complete your analysis, thesis validation, and risk calculus *before* entry. Once in the trade, execution should be mechanical, not reactive.
- **Fair value = exit signal** [00:19–00:40]: The primary exit trigger is not a stop-loss or profit target, but recognition that the stock has reached fair value and no edge remains. Holding beyond that point is "risk with no edge."
- **Systematic vs. discretionary** [00:40]: A non-discretionary, pre-planned approach removes the burden of real-time decision-making; all decisions are made during preparation, not during the trade.

## Notable quotes

> "Most of the time you should just be doing very boring trades that don't enter into any kind of big risk calculus. It's just okay, yeah, I think this stock is now at its fair value. There's no edge to holding it. It's just risk with no edge."
