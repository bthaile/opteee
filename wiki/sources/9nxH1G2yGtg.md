# BIG GME Call Option Volume

## Summary

Eric analyzes an unusual spike in GME call option volume on 2024-06-24, with ~27,740 call contracts traded versus ~92,666 puts. The bulk of activity concentrates in the 28 June expiration, particularly at the 25 and 30 strikes. While the volume itself is notable given the lack of obvious catalysts, the underlying trades (especially far OTM calls at 128 strike) appear speculative with minimal conviction and severe theta decay risk.

## Key takeaways

- **Call volume spike**: ~27,740 calls traded on 2024-06-24, concentrated in 28 June expiration [01:15–01:42]
- **Strike concentration**: 25 and 30 strikes show the heaviest activity; volume exceeds open interest, indicating net buying [05:25–05:47]
- **Far OTM speculation**: 1,110 contracts of 128 calls bought at 3 cents each (~$3,300 total position); theta decay of 2 cents per day means position loses value rapidly without a large move [02:39–03:22]
- **Gamma problem**: Far OTM calls have such low gamma that even a 10-point move provides minimal payoff; requires a "huge move" to become profitable [03:40–04:00]
- **IV context**: Put IV on far OTM strikes >52%; call IV showing "kinks" but not the primary driver of the unusual volume [00:49–01:15]
- **No clear catalyst**: No recent Roaring Kitty activity or major news; volume appears speculative rather than informed [06:26]
