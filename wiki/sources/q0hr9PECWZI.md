# PLAN #optionstrading #stockmarket #investing

## Summary

Retail traders cannot execute purely mechanical, rules-based systems like institutions; instead, the practical approach is to build a flexible trading plan that balances predetermined decision rules with real-time market analysis. Every trade must have a profit target, loss target, and time bound to remain disciplined while adapting to current conditions.

## Key takeaways

- **Mechanical trading doesn't work for retail** [00:20] — institutions have structural advantages that allow true rules-based execution; retail traders lack these capabilities and must remain engaged in the decision process.
- **Create a plan with a series of decisions** [00:20] — rather than full automation, design a framework of conditional decisions applied based on current market analysis.
- **Every trade requires three anchors** [00:43] — profit target, loss target, and time bound; these constraints enforce discipline while allowing flexibility.
- **Balance planning with market adaptation** [00:20] — the goal is to remain flexible to current market conditions without abandoning structure.
