# Analyzing Option Buys vs Sells

## Summary

This video examines how retail options traders can infer whether an option was bought or sold by analyzing order flow and bid/ask execution patterns. While trades executed at the bid typically signal sales and trades at the offer signal buys, the instructor emphasizes that these are probabilistic indicators rather than definitive proof, as edge cases and mid-trade shifts can create exceptions.

## Key takeaways

- **Bid/ask execution as a proxy** [00:17]: Options trading at the bid are conventionally associated with sales; those at the offer with buys. This is a useful heuristic but not conclusive.
- **Extraneous circumstances complicate inference** [00:40]: Bid/ask designation can shift during a trade execution, meaning an option that appears to trade at the bid may actually have been a buy. These exceptions are rare but real.
- **Order flow as a retail edge** [00:00]: Paying close attention to order flow patterns and volume distribution can reveal unusual activity worth investigating, as demonstrated by a live trade review.

## Notable quotes

> "We don't know conclusively if an option trades at the bid that it is a sale. We assume that it is, but there are extraneous circumstances where it could be a buy if it shifted during the course of the trade."
