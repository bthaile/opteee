# The Return - Stock Market Update

## Summary

Eric returns from a brief market absence to catch up on recent price action, Fed policy shifts, and earnings strength. The session focuses on supply-and-demand mechanics driving stock prices, the cascading effects of policy news (vaccine bans, tariffs) on sector rotation, and tactical opportunities in elevated volatility environments—particularly in furniture/home goods stocks and healthcare names facing regulatory headwinds.

## Key takeaways

- **Fed rate-cut expectations remain slow**: September cut priced at ~90%, October at ~34%, well below original 2025 guidance; Powell's Jackson Hole comments were less dovish than market interpreted [06:28–07:42]
- **Small-cap strength driven by earnings resilience**: Despite slow rate-cut timeline, market remains resilient due to strong earnings season and AI disruption tailwinds [06:00–08:35]
- **Palantir restructure**: Position flattened, then partially reopened at smaller size in different options chain; still bullish fundamentally but managing drawdown via profit-taking (cut ~25% decline to ~12% realized loss) [09:42–10:12, 25:10–25:56]
- **Valuation context matters for swing trades**: Valuations are part of supply-and-demand equation but not the whole story; stock price ultimately driven by who wants what at what price and quantity [12:34–17:16]
- **Vaccine ban policy cascades through healthcare**: Trump administration's potential vaccine ban triggers direct pharma selloff (PFE, JNJ, MRK, BMY down) plus secondary ETF rebalancing and tertiary biotech/peripheral impacts [29:51–41:45]
- **Tariff news on furniture/home goods**: RH, Wayfair, and similar names face tariff headwinds; creates immediate volatility expansion and put skew opportunities [43:05–47:41]
- **Volatility term structure distortions are tradable**: Front-month backwardation, elevated put skew, and call-put asymmetries in affected names present tactical short-premium and pairs-trade setups [44:36–50:17]
- **Earnings pipeline still robust**: OCTA, ANF, ASS among upcoming names; Eric prioritizes pricing out earnings vol for interesting risk-reward [50:56–51:22]
