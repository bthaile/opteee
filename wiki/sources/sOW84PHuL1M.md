# Analyzing Roaring Kitty's Trades Pt2 | GameStop Analysis Live Ep10

## Summary

Eric conducts a detailed forensic analysis of Roaring Kitty's historical options trades from June–December 2020, reconstructing his portfolio positions month-by-month to identify trading behaviors and risk management patterns. The session reveals that Roaring Kitty employed long-dated, far-out-of-the-money calls as a leveraged vehicle, averaged into positions over time, and appeared to manage exits based on time decay rather than profit targets—a methodology that worked spectacularly but carried extreme concentration risk and survivorship bias.

## Key takeaways

- **Fingerprinting via cost basis & cash flow** [05:34–06:28]: Eric's methodology tracks changes in total gain, cash, and portfolio value between YOLO updates to detect position adjustments and capital flows that aren't visible in snapshots alone.

- **Options-first strategy, not stock-first** [12:44–13:31]: Roaring Kitty's earliest positions (5 years prior) were entirely options-based, not stock trades with options overlays; he used leverage via calls from the outset, only adding a small 500-share position in June 2020.

- **Consistent use of monthly expirations, far OTM strikes** [23:27–24:28]: He avoided weeklies, preferring monthly expirations, and bought calls 6–12 months out at extreme strikes (e.g., $40 calls when stock was ~$5), paying pennies per contract (6–40 cents).

- **Averaging into positions across multiple tranches** [27:01–27:50]: Evidence of multiple fills at different prices (e.g., 17 July $20 calls at $0.1367 average) suggests he scaled in rather than taking single large fills, a disciplined accumulation approach.

- **Exited positions based on time, not profit targets** [33:15–34:11]: Despite a $600k unrealized gain in November, he held through drawdowns to $250k and back up to $314k, suggesting exits were keyed to expiration dates (e.g., exited 17 Oct calls ~51 DTE, 15 Jan calls ~46 DTE) rather than price targets.

- **Theta decay accelerates near expiration** [36:14–36:42]: By late November, his 15 Jan $20 calls (24 DTE) were losing ~$5k/day in time value despite being at-the-money, illustrating the peak of the theta decay curve.

- **Cash flow analysis reveals capital additions** [47:19–48:23]: Variance between portfolio value delta and total gain + cash delta indicates he added external capital to the account between updates (e.g., ~$22k variance in late September).

- **Survivorship bias & luck are critical** [40:58–41:52]: Eric emphasizes that Roaring Kitty's success, while built on solid research and conviction, required a "healthy dose of good fortune"—alignment of thesis, market conditions, no black-swan events, and no company scandals. For every successful concentrated bet, many blow up.

- **GameStop logo rebranding** [04:11–05:43]: GameStop rebranded in-house hardware accessories from "Atrix" to a new brand ~6 months prior; logo changed to all-black but the red-and-black flavicon remained.

- **13G filing rules & material change thresholds** [27:14–30:33]: Amended 13G filings for passive investors (>5% ownership) must be filed within 45 days after the end of the calendar quarter in which a material change (>5% ownership shift) occurs; recent SEC amendments (Sept 2024) accelerate this further. Roaring Kitty could theoretically unwind up to 4.5% of Chewy without triggering a new filing.

- **Dark pool 5K blocks are not evidence of manipulation** [02:12:04–02:15:54]: Dark pool trades still hit the consolidated tape (delayed by seconds to minutes) and volume; only internalized orders are invisible. The 5K block size originated from his options trades (5,000 contracts = 500k shares notional), not a coordinated scheme.
