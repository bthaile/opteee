# 10b. Erik's Favorite Strategy - The Covered Strangle (CS) Pt2 | Options Strategies

## Summary

This video details the management framework for the covered strangle strategy, focusing on decision trees for out-of-the-money and in-the-money scenarios. Erik outlines profit-taking rules, rolling mechanics (timing, credit requirements, size adjustment), and the transition to covered-call management upon assignment, emphasizing systematic but infrequent adjustment.

## Key takeaways

- **Out-of-the-money management** [03:22]: Close for 60% profit if available; consider closing at 80% max profit with >50% DTE remaining; take off if 30–40% profit realized in <2 days. Frequency depends on market regime.
- **Rolling timing and credit** [05:24]: Best roll opportunity occurs when underlying price equals short strike. Always require a credit on the roll; never roll for a debit.
- **Size adjustment on rolls** [06:14]: Reduce size (e.g., 5 lot → 4 lot) to lower risk, or increase size (e.g., 5 → 6 lot at lower strike) to move closer to the money and collect more credit.
- **Roll duration strategy** [08:34]: In bearish/bottom-heavy markets, let position decay to 14–15 DTE before rolling to minimize theta repurchase. Roll out 2–3 weeks if rolling both out and down; go further out (40–45 DTE) if targeting out-of-the-money reestablishment.
- **Assignment and covered-call transition** [10:17]: Once assigned, sell covered calls above cost basis. Simultaneously sell additional puts below the short strike to scale in and reduce average cost basis on further assignment.
- **Covered-call loop** [12:14]: Repeat covered-call sales until called away; when expiring worthless or near expiration, sell another and continue the cycle.

## Notable quotes

> "I always make sure I get a credit for the roll. Always. If you don't get a credit, you're gonna make this a very difficult trade to manage for the long term."
