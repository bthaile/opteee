# Market Divergence and Trade Opportunities

## Summary

Eric reviews Greek attribution mechanics through a live options question-of-the-day scenario, then pivots to broad market analysis highlighting significant divergence across sectors and indices. The market shows mixed signals with elevated IV (59th percentile) but near-zero risk premium, concentrated strength in tech and small caps, and FOMC expectations of rate hikes in July and September. Near-term positioning is favored given the conflicting technical and macro signals.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2026-06-30)

- **Regime deterioration**: Broad regime tool has flipped from ~0 to -1; risk premium is negative (-1) at 17th percentile [48:42]
- **Sector divergence**: 8 of 11 sectors above 5-day MA; 60–62% above 20/50/200-day MAs, but small caps (72–70%) vastly outpacing QQQ (51%) and SPX (65%) [49:11], [52:00]
- **Tech concentration**: QQQ nearly keeping pace with IWM year-to-date; tech has more than doubled SPX performance [50:17]
- **FOMC expectations**: Market pricing ~31% probability of July hike, 60%+ for September [50:46]
- **Net highs/lows**: Still printing net highs above 10-period MA, contracting slightly but showing sector flow strength [52:37]
- **Short-term technicals**: SPX in defined near-term downtrend; short-term moving averages "all broken"; key to watch SPX behavior near resistance [54:11]
- **Earnings calendar**: Next major earnings season begins ~July 14; post-earnings drift and announcement moves offer tactical opportunities [55:26]

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Greek attribution in long calls** [27:22–37:07]: Delta and gamma profit from underlying move; vega and theta decay hurt. In the example (30-delta call, 40→31% IV drop, +4% underlying move), vega loss was substantial despite directional correctness. Vega impact is smaller near-term (21 DTE) than far-term (90 DTE), where vega can exceed delta 2×.
- **Charm (delta decay)** [42:09–45:07]: In-the-money options gain delta over time (increasing probability of staying ITM); out-of-the-money options lose delta. Effect is more pronounced closer to expiration.
- **Sticky-strike delta behavior**: Same strike option's delta changes with time passage; at-the-money options show less rate-of-change than OTM/ITM [44:23–45:31].
- **Alternative bullish structures** [45:56–47:46]: Short put, short put spread, or synthetic long all express bullish thesis with different vega/IV exposure. Synthetic long removes undefined upside but matches long-stock risk profile.
- **Position sizing and context**: Absolute price moves (e.g., 4%) are relative to underlying price; a $4 move on a $500 stock ≠ $4 on a $10 stock in delta terms [37:31–38:32].
