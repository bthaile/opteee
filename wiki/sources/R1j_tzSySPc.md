# OPEN Deep Dive | Meme Stock Analysis Ep50

## Summary

A deep technical and fundamental analysis of OPEN (real estate tech platform) as a resurgent meme stock, examining why retail has developed intense followership despite weak fundamentals and limited short-squeeze mechanics. The session compares OPEN's extreme implied volatility skew and tail premium opportunities against [[gme|GameStop]]'s covered-strangle performance, highlighting how [[realized-vs-unrealized-pnl|realized volatility]] and [[risk-premium|risk premiums]] diverge from simplified IV-percentile trading rules.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2025-08-31)

- **OPEN resurgence driver**: Meme-stock narrative revival driven by retail social-media coordination and insider-buy PR (30k shares by president for ~$128k), not fundamental value [06:17–10:50]
- **Short-interest mechanics weak**: 24% short interest with 0.78 days-to-cover ratio is too low for meaningful [[short-squeeze|squeeze]] candidate; shorts can cover easily within a day [17:43–18:12]
- **Institutional positioning**: BlackRock scaled down; Vanguard underwater at $14 average; institutions unwound Q1, re-entered Q2 after price collapse [28:11–29:15]
- **Valuation disconnect**: DCF intrinsic value estimated ~$8.58 vs. stock trading ~$2–3 range; penny-stock suppression unexplained by fundamentals alone [30:38–39:31]
- **Earnings surprise**: Q2 revenue growth and profitability better than expected; net income negative $928M in 2022 (inventory/debt buildup), but recent quarters show recovery [34:40–37:17]

### Evergreen mechanics

- **[[Volatility-skew|Skew]] structure in cheap stocks**: OPEN exhibits extreme put skew at tails (194% IV at $1.50 strike vs. 172% at-the-money); call IV even richer (208% at 24-delta) [44:25–48:53]
- **Tail premium opportunity**: 3-delta short put on OPEN yields 2.7% return for 48 days (~22.5% annualized) vs. SPY 3-delta at $0.84 premium and TSLA at $15 — OPEN is "absolute blowout" [51:04–54:07]
- **Cash-secured put mechanics**: OPEN's $1.50 strike requires only $150 collateral, enabling small accounts to scale naked puts; commissions eat material % of premium on cheap names [54:07–54:45]
- **[[Iv-crush|IV contraction]] likely on downside**: Even if stock declines, high tail IV (242% at 1-delta) will contract faster than expand, favoring short-premium sellers [55:10–55:48]
- **[[Covered-strangle|Covered strangle]] on GME**: 19% total return on invested capital vs. 7.8% buy-and-hold loss; outperformance of 26.8% over 20-day period; call side up $940 on 350 shares at $23.61 cost basis [59:18–01:04:03]
- **[[Risk-premium|Risk premium]] vs. IV-percentile paradox**: GME shows fattest risk premiums in June–July when IV percentile is at floor; May shows high IV percentile but realized vol > implied (loss if sold) — simplified "buy low IV, sell high IV" rules fail [01:06:02–01:08:38]
