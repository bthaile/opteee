# GameStop Options Activity Spike: $660K Trade & Earnings Setup

## Summary

GameStop rallied 8% on elevated volume (15.5M shares vs. 5.7M 20-day average), with a notable $660K block trade in 21-call spreads at 20-Sep expiration. The trade's in-the-money strike (73 delta) and timing one week before earnings suggest a bullish positioning expecting either a move or guidance improvement, with lower theta decay than out-of-the-money alternatives.

## Key takeaways

- **Volume surge & price break** [00:00–01:09]: 15.5M shares traded (280% above 5-day average); price broke and held above $23, clearing previously cited Max Pain levels around $21.
- **Call/put skew & open interest** [02:16–03:24]: 621K calls vs. 294K puts; 316K call volume vs. 54K puts today; 39% of calls at-or-above offer (buying pressure), 35% of puts at-or-below bid (selling pressure).
- **$660K block trade in 21-call 20-Sep** [04:37–05:59]: ~2,000 contracts at $3.30 strike; 73-delta in-the-money calls; unusual size for retail; does not match historical Roaring Kitty fingerprint (typically OTM, ~1-month expiration).
- **Why in-the-money over OTM** [07:01–08:01]: Lower theta decay; better P&L on smaller moves; higher absolute cost but higher delta per dollar; trade-off is reduced compounding vs. OTM alternatives.
- **Earnings context** [09:05–10:08]: Earnings on 13-Sep; buying 20-Sep calls (one week post-earnings) reduces IV crush sensitivity while maintaining price exposure; suggests expectation of move or guidance beat.
- **Broader activity expansion** [10:08–11:12]: Multiple contracts now trading >100K volume; shift from 2–3 large trades per day to sustained elevated activity.

## Notable quotes

> "That's $660,000 in an individual trade. That's not small. That is unequivocally exciting to me because again, you can't, there's not a lot of retail traders that can move at that size."

> "Why would we go in the money? Lower theta decay and you make more money if it's a smaller move."
