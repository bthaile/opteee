# GameStop Analysis with @Peruvian Bull

## Summary

Eric interviews Peruvian Bull, a long-time derivatives trader and community analyst, on the structural anomalies of GameStop as a trading vehicle and the evidence of systemic naked shorting, swap abuse, and market infrastructure failures that emerged during the January 2021 squeeze. The conversation covers the role of total return swaps in masking short exposure, the Archegos collapse and its connection to GameStop, the conflict of interest in market maker–hedge fund hybrids, and speculation on Ryan Cohen's strategic use of accumulated capital.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2024-10-04)

- **GameStop cash position and floor:** With ~$4.6 billion in cash on hand (~$10.74 per share), the company has a hard floor at that cash value; current price stabilization around $20 suggests market is pricing in enterprise value above cash [01:42:32]
- **Timeline for capital deployment:** Expect material announcement on use of cash within 6–9 months; companies raising this level of capital typically deploy it within a year [01:58:34]

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Naked shorting and FTD loops:** Reported short interest of 22–28% masks historical evidence (140% FINRA-reported in Jan 2021, 220%+ from other brokers); naked shorting exemptions for market makers create systemic loopholes when combined with ETF creation/redemption abuse [29:12, 30:06]
- **Total return swaps as short-hiding mechanism:** Archigos and 70+ hedge funds used basket swaps to go long tech (Zoom, Tesla, Facebook) while shorting retail (GameStop, BBBY, Bed Bath & Beyond) starting March 2020; when Archigos collapsed (March 2021), Credit Suisse inherited toxic positions and required $30B bailout [51:48, 55:32]
- **Settlement system fragility:** January 28, 2021 buy button shutdown was triggered by Apex Clearing's detection of a $385M buy-sell trade by unnamed market maker (alleged Citadel); fractional sales executed at $2,600–$5,200 before halt, indicating price discovery was breaking [32:00, 01:16:41]
- **Volume expansion as pre-move signal:** Consistent pattern across all major GameStop moves is 3x+ volume expansion 2–5 days before price spike; only reliable technical correlation found in regression analysis of 162 indicators [01:52:31]
- **DRS impact on liquidity:** Direct registration of ~25% of float reduced trading volume and liquidity indicators; unknown whether 100% DRS is achievable or what happens if achieved [01:40:25]
- **Market maker conflict of interest:** Citadel Securities (market maker) and Citadel hedge fund share ownership/control; enables potential coordination (e.g., forced clearing errors to freeze buying) that pure market makers cannot execute [01:15:26, 01:18:47]
- **Liquidity vs. price discovery trade-off:** Current market paradigm maximizes liquidity at expense of price discovery; infinite liquidity justifies naked shorting, FTD kicks, and phantom shares; true price discovery would require hard share caps on derivatives and elimination of naked shorting [01:08:32, 01:24:06]
- **Ryan Cohen's strategic positioning (speculative):** Teddy Corp (Cohen's bank) funding loans to BBBY, Toys R Us, and alleged partnerships with Flexport (logistics) suggest potential conglomerate play to compete with Amazon; theory requires SEC coordination to prevent systemic collapse during squeeze [40:19, 41:39]
- **Earnings volatility pattern:** GameStop exhibits consistent pre-earnings grind lower, pop into earnings, then post-earnings slam; not guaranteed but documented across multiple earnings cycles [01:51:20]
