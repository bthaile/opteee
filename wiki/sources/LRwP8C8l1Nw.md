# Be prepared #optionstrading #trading

## Summary

A foundational message on why most retail traders fail: the stock market is highly competitive and accessible to everyone, yet most approach it without the rigor they'd apply to a business. The core insight is that successful trading requires a formal trading plan and trading log—treating markets like a business with documented processes, not a casino.

## Key takeaways

- **Low barrier to entry is a trap** [00:00–00:19]: Accessibility (platforms like Robinhood) creates false confidence; people are shocked when they lose money without a plan.
- **Apply business methodology to trading** [00:19–00:42]: Write a trading plan instead of winging it; maintain a trading log to test and model ideas, just as you'd use financial statements to validate a business.
- **Track performance like earnings** [00:42]: A trading log functions as your trading "earnings report"—the same rigor you'd apply to any serious venture.
- **Preparation separates outliers** [00:42]: The difference between retail failure and outlier success is treating trading as a discipline, not a shortcut.
