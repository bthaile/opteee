# Profit Mechanisms Come First

## Summary

The foundation of any trading approach must be a clearly defined profit mechanism—a robust, researched edge—rather than a collection of technical indicators applied arbitrarily. Technical and fundamental analysis tools serve only to identify opportunities aligned with that mechanism; signals without a profit mechanism are noise.

## Key takeaways

- **Profit mechanism first, tools second** [00:00–00:28]: Start by identifying a robust profit mechanism (e.g., momentum), then select analysis tools that directly support identifying candidates for that mechanism.
- **Momentum as a worked example** [00:28–00:55]: Momentum is well-researched and robust; if trading momentum, use moving averages (which measure momentum) to identify entry candidates—not moving averages for their own sake.
- **Avoid signal-chasing** [00:55]: Signals must directly enable identification and execution of the profit mechanism; signals divorced from a mechanism are a liability, not an edge.

## Notable quotes

> "Your profit mechanisms come first every single time."

> "The signals used should directly allow us to identify and trade the profit mechanisms, not signals for the sake of signals."
