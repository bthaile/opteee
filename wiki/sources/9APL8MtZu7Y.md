# tips for buying leaps

## Summary

LEAPS serve as effective leverage stock replacements but require careful attention to liquidity, bid-ask spreads, and Greek behavior across expiration cycles. The key is balancing gamma (high near-term, low far-out), vega (low near-term, high far-out), and extrinsic value to construct a position aligned with your directional conviction and expected move magnitude.

## Key takeaways

- **Liquidity is paramount** [00:23]–[00:50]: As you move further out in expiration cycles, liquidity drops and bid-ask spreads widen. This affects both entry and exit execution, making liquidity the single most important factor when selecting LEAPS.

- **Gamma is front-loaded** [00:50]–[01:14]: Gamma is high in near-term expirations and low in far-out cycles. This means front-month options respond more sharply to underlying moves.

- **Vega increases with time** [00:50]–[01:14]: Vega is low in near-term expirations and high in far-out cycles. Far-dated LEAPS carry more volatility risk but also more extrinsic value.

- **Match Greeks to your thesis** [01:14]–[01:32]: If you expect a modest move, you may need higher deltas (more expensive). If you're uncertain about magnitude, balance delta, gamma, and vega based on your conviction and time horizon.

- **Understand option behavior before building** [01:32]: The most important step is understanding how options behave across the expiration surface so you can construct a position that makes sense for your specific idea.
