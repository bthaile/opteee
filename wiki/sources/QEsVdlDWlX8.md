# The Best Trading Indicators That Actually Work (Most Are Useless)

## Summary

This video challenges the myth of a universal "best" trading indicator, arguing instead that indicator selection must be grounded in the underlying profit mechanism of your strategy. Eric walks through the relationship between strategy type and relevant signals, then demonstrates his personal indicator suite—moving averages, standard deviation channels, volume profile, RSI, volatility metrics, and breadth indicators—showing how each serves a specific analytical purpose tied to different trading approaches.

## Key takeaways

- **Indicator selection is strategy-dependent** [01:19–02:14]: Simple indicators directly tied to your profit mechanism outperform complex ones. The work is identifying the profit mechanism first, then overlaying signals that illuminate it.
- **Sector rotation example** [02:46–04:01]: Momentum-based sector rotation relies on relative strength and lookback periods (1-, 3-, 6-, 12-month), not on indicators like Ichimoku or Fibonacci that matter for other strategies.
- **Index variance risk premium vs. momentum** [04:53–06:17]: Risk premium strategies benefit from implied volatility percentile, IV rank, term structure, and skew; momentum strategies care about cross-sectional ranking and lookback periods. Minimal overlap—no universal "best."
- **Moving averages as trend and strength assessment** [07:06–07:31]: Multiple periods function as a MACD-like tool; weight them by your time frame (shorter-term strategies emphasize shorter-period MAs, longer-term strategies emphasize longer-period MAs).
- **Standard deviation channels for extremes** [07:54–08:16]: Plot mean ± 1 SD to identify trends and price extremes; does not imply mean reversion, just probabilistic context.
- **Volume profile for support/resistance** [08:16–08:46]: Shows where volume transacted; helps identify key price levels without predicting direction.
- **Volume breakdown and trends** [08:46–09:09]: Split volume into buy-side and sell-side; track 5-period and 20-period averages to spot volume trends, especially useful for breakout strategies.
- **Price spike graph and RSI** [09:37–10:01]: 20-period lookback identifies daily moves relative to recent volatility (gray = within 1 SD, darker green = 1 SD, lighter green = 2+ SD); RSI with 4-period MA detects relative strength for short-term trades.
- **Volatility metrics alignment** [10:27–10:50]: Implied vol (7-day, 30-day) includes weekends; realized vol (5-day, 20-day) does not—align them accordingly. Difference between periods shows vol term structure.
- **Breadth indicator** [10:50–11:13]: Net highs-lows for Nasdaq and NYSE with moving average; contextual for overall market health.
- **Options chain and term structure** [11:13–11:37]: Examine open interest, volume, order flow, liquidity, and volatility surface; importance varies by strategy (critical for term-structure trades, less so for breakouts).
- **Core principle** [12:04]: Spend more time analyzing the profit mechanism than selecting indicators. Indicator relevance is always relative to what you are trading.
