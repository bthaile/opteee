# realistic returns for options traders

## Summary

Eric addresses a critical mistake new options traders make: setting unrealistic return targets that force them into excessive risk-taking before they've developed the necessary skill. He contextualizes common goals (1% daily, 1% weekly, 1,000% returns) against their annualized equivalents and historical benchmarks, emphasizing that sustainable outperformance requires both reasonable expectations and proven edge.

## Key takeaways

- **Unrealistic goals drive excessive risk** [00:00–00:24]: New traders often pick ambitious return targets, then realize they must take unsustainable risk to achieve them—before they've proven they can trade profitably at all.
- **1% per week = ~69% annualized** [00:46]: Even "modest" weekly targets compound to world-record-breaking annual returns; consistency at that level is virtually impossible without proven edge.
- **Context prevents ruin** [01:05]: Understanding what reasonable performance looks like is essential to avoid blowing up accounts chasing fantasy returns.
