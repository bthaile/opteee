# Core Allocation on a $25K Portfolio #optionstrading

## Summary

This video outlines portfolio construction principles for a $25K account, emphasizing the shift from ratio call diagonals to covered strangles as the core allocation strategy. The host explains why downside trading is avoided in core positions due to the market's upward bias, and discusses position-sizing constraints imposed by capital limitations and instrument pricing.

## Key takeaways

- **Core allocation strategy shift** [00:00]: For smaller accounts, covered strangles replace ratio call diagonals as the preferred core allocation vehicle due to capital efficiency and price constraints.
- **Upside bias in position construction** [00:23]: Downside trading is viable mechanically but impractical for core allocation because markets and most securities trend upward over time; requires a separate, specialized playbook.
- **Multi-position scaling** [00:43]: A $25K portfolio should target 2–3 concurrent core trades depending on account size and the cost of the underlying instruments being traded.
- **Price efficiency constraint** [00:23]: Small accounts face hard limits on which strategies are executable; downside positions become prohibitively expensive relative to available capital.
