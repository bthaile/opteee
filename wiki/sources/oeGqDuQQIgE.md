# Doomberg on AI Power, Iran Risk, Tariffs, and the Collapse of Trust | The Outlier Podcast

## Summary

Doomberg discusses the Supreme Court's tariff ruling against Trump, the geopolitical fallout from recent file releases, cartel violence in Mexico, Iran military posturing, and the accelerating pace of AI development. The conversation spans constitutional law, energy markets, institutional trust, and the technical singularity, with emphasis on how political uncertainty and AI disruption are reshaping risk landscapes for traders and analysts.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2026-02-24)

- **Supreme Court tariff ruling**: Court ruled ~6–3 against Trump's tariff authority under existing law; Trump responded with 10% global tariff threat, then escalated to 15% via Truth Social [02:11–07:34]. Doomberg flagged the decision was "clearly priced in" via Poly Market (~80–85% probability pre-ruling); market treated it as "big nothing burger."
- **Trump's constitutional overreach**: Administration stretched statutory language beyond plain reading; Doomberg expects Congress to pass Lindsey Graham's sanctions bill, which would grant Trump unlimited tariff power via Russia-related business restrictions [03:23].
- **Files release and institutional erosion**: Massive document dump (3 million+) lacks coherent indexing; AI-generated clickbait and partisan interpretation are fracturing public trust in institutions [10:11–14:43]. Doomberg notes highest-level fallout in UK (arrests of royal family members); US examples include Goldman Sachs general counsel resignation.
- **Mexico cartel escalation**: Killing of cartel leader may trigger full-blown civil conflict; Doomberg views this as higher-priority military concern than Iran [21:03–28:15]. Fentanyl deaths (~100,000/year) represent "World War II level casualties"; Chinese control of precursor supply is proven by recent reduction in deaths post-Trump–Xi trade deal.
- **Iran military posture**: Poly Market still pricing ~50%+ probability of US strike by end of March [48:24]. Doomberg is "pessimistic" on swift regime change; risks include aircraft carrier loss, drone swarms (Ukraine precedent), and escalation to nuclear contingency if Israel's infrastructure is targeted [49:41–57:22].
- **AI singularity breached**: Doubling time of AI capability shrinking from years → months → weeks; AI now improving AI [59:42–01:06:19]. Doomberg expects "mega disruption" within ~1 year; big companies lack DNA to adapt; new startups with AI-native design will displace legacy software firms.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Major questions doctrine**: Courts scrutinize whether executive action requires explicit congressional authorization for major policy shifts; Trump's tariff strategy violated this principle [02:11].
- **Poly Market as price discovery**: Prediction markets reflect real-time probability assessment; useful for geopolitical risk modeling [03:23, 48:24].
- **Institutional capture and incentive misalignment**: Cancer treatment industry optimizes for recurring revenue (subscription model) rather than cure; same dynamic applies to pharma, energy, and defense [36:46–42:27]. Scientists defend failed theories due to career/funding sunk costs; NIH grant gatekeeping reinforces orthodoxy.
- **Exponential growth blindness**: Humans model linearly; exponential doubling (especially in AI) saturates systems faster than intuition predicts [01:06:19].
- **Cartel business model**: Cartels operate as rational economic actors; targeting US citizens increases law enforcement risk and disrupts profit flow; fentanyl supply control by China proves intentional policy, not accident [23:31–29:18].
- **Geopolitical risk and energy markets**: Energy analysts must track political viability of leaders and military posture; Trump's base fracturing over Iran/Ukraine contradicts campaign promises [48:24–54:46].
