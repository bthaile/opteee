# Agustin Lebron — Put Your Money Where Your Mouth Is

## Summary

Agustin Lebron discusses the disciplinary power of betting and prediction markets as mechanisms for forcing intellectual honesty. He argues that when traders are forced to put capital at risk behind their convictions, they become more rigorous about validating their beliefs, and that this principle extends broadly to markets and speculation as tools for improving decision-making.

## Key takeaways

- **Betting as a tax on speech** [00:00–00:24]: Putting money behind your words creates immediate accountability; traders learn that "saying words isn't free" and must validate their beliefs before committing capital.
- **Prediction markets as epistemic tools** [00:24–00:44]: Betting and prediction markets encourage more careful attention to what matters; they are valuable when focused on important, useful questions and potentially distracting when applied to trivial concerns.
- **Training effect of trading firms** [00:00]: Working at a trading firm teaches the discipline of aligning conviction with capital allocation more effectively than most other environments.

## Notable quotes

> "A bet is a tax on words. Anytime you are forced to put your money where your mouth is, you start to go like, 'Oh, actually, hang on a sec. Maybe I should actually figure out whether I believe the words I'm saying.'"
