# Option skew #trading #optionstrading

## Summary

This video explains volatility skew—the phenomenon where market participants systematically overpay for downside protection (put skew), creating a premium in out-of-the-money puts relative to calls. The asymmetry arises because downside moves are faster and more violent than upside moves, creating an unfavorable risk/reward for sellers of puts. Understanding skew allows traders to read market expectations about directional risk.

## Key takeaways

- **Put skew is the norm** [00:00–00:23]: Markets typically price puts at a premium relative to calls because participants value downside protection; this reflects the asymmetric nature of market crashes (elevator down, stairs up).
- **Risk/reward asymmetry drives the premium** [00:23–00:47]: Downside moves are faster and unlimited in magnitude, while upside profit potential is capped; this unfavorable risk profile for put sellers justifies the premium.
- **Call skew exists but is less common** [00:00–00:23]: While put skew dominates, call skew can occur in specific market conditions.
- **Skew as a directional signal** [00:47]: Once attuned to skew patterns, traders can use them to infer where markets are pricing in directional risk.
