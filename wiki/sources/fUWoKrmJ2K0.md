# Theta Relative to Time

## Summary

This educational segment examines how theta (time decay) behaves across different time horizons and strike prices. The key insight is that theta accelerates as expiration approaches and peaks at-the-money, where extrinsic value is highest, then declines as options move further out-of-the-money.

## Key takeaways

- **Theta magnitude increases near expiration** [00:00] — both in absolute dollar terms and as a percentage of option value, theta decay accelerates as you approach expiration.
- **At-the-money options have the highest theta** [00:00] — theta peaks when delta is around 50, because ATM options contain the maximum extrinsic value.
- **Theta declines out-of-the-money** [00:38] — as strike prices move further from spot, theta diminishes because OTM options are entirely extrinsic value with less total premium to decay.
