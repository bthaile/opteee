# Managing Catalysts in Trades

## Summary

Catalyst management is a distinct component of trade planning that addresses how to respond when unexpected events or timeline shifts emerge. Rather than treating catalysts as ad-hoc surprises, integrating them into a standardized process—such as earnings date changes or industry news—ensures consistent decision-making and reduces reactive trading errors.

## Key takeaways

- **Catalyst as a separate bucket** [00:00] — Treat catalyst management as its own process layer, distinct from but bundled with thesis development, to ensure it becomes a repeatable part of your trading workflow.
- **Earnings timeline shifts** [00:00] — When an earnings release date moves unexpectedly, explicitly ask whether that changes your original trade plan; do not assume your thesis remains valid.
- **Indirect industry impacts** [00:27] — Biotech and sector trades are vulnerable to industry news that indirectly affects your position; build a habit of reassessing thesis when external events surface.
- **Standardization over reaction** [00:27] — Formalizing catalyst checks prevents emotional or ad-hoc decision-making; make it a repeatable step in your process.
