# Top 5 Trading Psychology Mistakes You NEED to Avoid

## Summary

Eric outlines five critical psychological traps that undermine trader performance: cognitive biases (disposition effect, confirmation bias, hindsight bias), Goldilocks analysis (underestimating complexity and overweighting desired outcomes), detachment from trading capital, false attribution (misdiagnosing root causes), and undisciplined approach (lack of pre-trade documentation). The core fix is systematic awareness, external audits, detailed trade logging, and written trading plans executed before entry.

## Key takeaways

- **Cognitive biases are endemic** [00:00–01:08]: Disposition effect (cutting winners short, letting losers run), confirmation bias (seeking confirming information), and hindsight bias (clarity in retrospect) slowly siphon capital. Awareness is the primary defense.

- **Conduct landscape analysis and external audits** [01:08–02:11]: Study how traders behave on forums like Reddit; ask peers in a trading community to review your process; track your trades obsessively so biases bubble up visibly.

- **Goldilocks analysis: underestimating trade complexity** [03:13–04:14]: Traders spend disproportionate time analyzing the profitable side of a trade and ignore downside scenarios. Example: cash-secured put seller assigned on shares, down 15%, never analyzed the assignment risk upfront.

- **Detach from trading capital mentally** [05:19–06:24]: Assign money entering your trading account as "already gone" to reduce loss aversion. Roaring Kitty's ability to hold through $160k daily swings stems from mental separation of risk capital; most traders would lock in $1.5M gains immediately.

- **False attribution: dig deeper than surface answers** [07:27–09:22]: A trader blaming "greed" for giving back $2k of a $2.2k day is missing the real drivers: no written plan, no downside risk management, and loss aversion. Superficial diagnosis leads to wrong fixes.

- **Undisciplined approach: document before you trade** [09:22–10:28]: Define exit points, position sizing, and decision rules *before* entry. Pre-trade planning short-circuits psychological traps; post-trade analysis is too late.

## Notable quotes

> "If you don't control the sensation where it's like 'oh I just lost 40 hours worth of my money,' you'll never fully be able to detach from the capital." [05:19]

> "You don't just analyze the side of the trade that you're interested in, that you want to see happen—that's a primary driver of what you see here." [04:14]
