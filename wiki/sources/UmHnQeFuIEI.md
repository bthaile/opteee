# Indicators Built into the Options Chain #optionstrading

## Summary

The options chain itself contains embedded indicators—term structure, open interest distribution, volume concentration, and order flow—that reveal market microstructure and volatility regime. Which indicators matter most depends on your trading strategy: term-vol and volatility surface are critical for term-structure trades, while less relevant for breakout strategies. The key principle is aligning your analytical focus to your intended trade.

## Key takeaways

- **Options chain as a data source** [00:00] — Examine term structure, open interest location, volume concentration, and order transacting patterns to understand liquidity and market structure.
- **Volatility surface and term-vol** [00:23] — For term-structure trades, the volatility surface and term-vol structure are essential indicators; for breakout trades, they are less critical.
- **Strategy-relative indicator selection** [00:48] — The indicators you monitor must align with your trading objective; don't apply the same analytical framework to all strategies.

## Notable quotes
