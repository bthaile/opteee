# BIG GME Options Expiration

## Summary

On July 5, 2024, GME experienced heavy options expiration activity with ~239,000 calls and 75,000 puts traded—a significant acceleration from early June. Call skew dominated (37% at bid or below), while open interest showed ~200,000 calls expiring today with substantial call OI skew persisting into future cycles. The underlying consolidated sideways between $23–$27 with compressed moving averages, while implied volatility remained elevated in absolute terms but low on a relative basis (IV rank 23, IV percentile 73).

## Key takeaways

- **Expiration volume surge** [00:00–00:28]: 239,000 calls and 75,000 puts traded today; heavy call skew with 37% of call transactions at bid or below (sellers) vs. 29% at offer (buyers).

- **Open interest decay** [01:45–02:12]: ~200,000 calls and 84,000 puts expiring today; call OI skew remains heavy even after expiration, dropping from ~894k to ~700k calls.

- **Liquidity concentration in standard expirations** [02:38–03:28]: July 12 (non-standard weekly) shows only 79k calls vs. 40k puts; July 19 jumps to 256k calls vs. 141k puts; August 16 at 124k calls vs. 71k puts. Liquidity clusters in standard cycles.

- **IV metrics and relative context** [05:43–06:34]: IV at 127 (absolute), IV rank 23, IV percentile 73. IV rank artificially depressed due to skew; IV percentile more reliable. Volatility historically depressed in past year; better baseline from May 3 forward.

- **Volume distribution and retail behavior** [06:59–07:45]: Heavier volume at ATM strikes (25s, 30s) in July 19 and August 16; far OTM 125 calls still active. Mix of retail speculation (cheap lottery-like positions) and possible institutional activity, but retail dominates in small lot sizes (1–3 lots).

- **Position-building signals** [12:19–13:08]: $100,000 position in October 16 20-strike calls noted as meaningful; single-leg buys indicate conviction; no massive institutional positioning detected yet.

- **Technical consolidation** [04:15–05:00]: 3-month daily chart shows sideways range $23–$27; all moving averages (7, 14, 21, 50, 200) compressed into one line; MACD also compressed—supremely heavy consolidation.

- **Trading approach philosophy** [10:25–11:42]: Long-term success favors slightly ITM calls with better liquidity and delta exposure over far OTM lottery tickets; time-frame diversification and position sizing matter more than single-leg home runs.
