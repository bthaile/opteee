# SIMPLE Options Trade Checklist

## Summary

A practical pre-trade checklist framework designed to catch errors without becoming so burdensome that it won't be used. The checklist balances depth with usability, progressing from broad screening (liquidity, announcements, portfolio fit) through hypothesis generation and confirmation, to detailed strategy structuring and P&L management. The goal is to develop habits that eventually become internalized rather than requiring manual tick-box execution.

## Key takeaways

- **Broad screening filters** [02:14–02:53]: Apply big screening factors upfront to eliminate unsuitable trades efficiently. Liquidity is the primary exonerating factor—if a trade lacks liquidity, move on regardless of appeal.

- **Liquidity assessment** [02:53–03:57]: Check bid-ask spread (e.g., TLT weekly at ~3 cents is excellent), daily volume, open interest, and availability of weekly expirations. Note that liquidity deteriorates sharply during volatile periods, so assess baseline conditions.

- **Upcoming announcements** [04:17–04:58]: Screen for earnings dates and other binary events. High implied volatility scans often cluster around earnings; trading through an unrecognized earnings event is stepping into unnecessary risk.

- **Broad market events** [05:15–06:07]: Check the week ahead for FOMC statements, economic data releases, and other macro catalysts. Use tools like Financial Juice to stay informed. Relevance varies by market regime.

- **Portfolio-first thinking** [06:28–06:50]: Confirm the trade aligns with portfolio needs. Even the best trade doesn't go on if your portfolio doesn't need that risk.

- **Hypothesis generation** [07:07–08:18]: Clearly articulate whether the trade is opportunistic or targeting specific exposure. Define the hypothesis precisely (e.g., "bullish FedEx, up X points over Y timeframe") and ensure technical analysis confluence supports it.

- **Strategy selection and structure** [09:41–10:06]: Choose a structure that fits the hypothesis. For a two-month bullish bias, a ratio call diagonal might suit; ensure the strategy aligns with your edge.

- **Define invalidation conditions** [10:06–10:49]: Set clear price and time-based exit rules. Example: "If FedEx drops below 270, I'm wrong." For options, bake in a time element (e.g., manage at 60-day mark on long leg).

- **P&L management** [11:31–12:13]: Outline profit targets and loss limits before entry. Be strict on losses; adjust profit discipline by strategy type. This forces you to accept the possibility of being wrong.

- **Blind spot check** [12:13–12:32]: Before clicking the button, ask: "Did I miss anything?" This sanity check prevents fat-fingering and reinforces deliberate execution.
