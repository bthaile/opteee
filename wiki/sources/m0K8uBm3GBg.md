# Are You Managing Your Trades Too Much? Watch This #trading #optiontrading

## Summary

This video addresses the hidden costs and psychological pitfalls of overactive trade management. The core argument: frequent adjustments incur commissions that erode P&L, and more importantly, they prevent you from extracting meaningful lessons about your strategy's long-term efficiency. The host advocates for disciplined management when necessary, but always in service of building a repeatable, data-driven process.

## Key takeaways

- **Track commissions as a percentage of average trade P&L** [00:00] — this metric reveals whether your activity level is sustainable and profitable.
- **Hyperactive management creates two problems:** direct cost (commissions) and indirect cost (inability to replicate and improve the strategy) [00:00–00:29].
- **Use each trade adjustment as a data point for long-term optimization** [00:29–00:53] — ask "how does this management decision make my process more efficient over longer durations?" rather than focusing only on the immediate trade outcome.
- **Manage individual trades when necessary, but within a strategic framework** [00:29] — the goal is to build a sustainable, repeatable approach, not to react emotionally to every price move.

## Notable quotes

> "You should always think about it in the context of how can I use this as a data point to make what I'm doing more efficient, better over longer durations." [00:29–00:53]
