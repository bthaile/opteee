# Options Trading Think in Windows Not Exact Dates

## Summary

New options traders often make the mistake of selecting expiration dates based on a specific calendar date rather than thinking in terms of time windows and risk/reward trade-offs. This video demonstrates how longer-dated options, despite higher upfront cost, can offer superior risk characteristics (lower daily theta decay) compared to near-term expirations, allowing traders to hold positions with more flexibility while managing time decay.

## Key takeaways

- **Avoid calendar-date thinking** [00:22]: Don't pick an expiration simply because you think a move "might happen in 25 days." This locks you into a specific time window and forces you to pay accelerating theta decay.
- **Compare delta and theta across expirations** [01:32]: A near-term ATM call on Visa (21 March 25) had ~51 delta but lost $21.45/day to theta decay, while a longer-dated call (16 Jan 26, ~300 days out) had 61 delta but only lost $6.42/day.
- **Longer-dated options as a down payment** [02:19]: Think of longer-dated positions as a "down payment" you hold temporarily with the expectation of closing sooner. This reduces daily theta bleed and gives you flexibility if your thesis takes longer to play out.
- **Theta decay accelerates near expiration** [01:57]: The closer you get to expiration without a move, the larger the underlying move required to break even—because theta decay compounds daily.

## Notable quotes

> "The longer your idea doesn't come to pass, the more your options decay, so what that does is it requires a larger and larger move in order for you to subsequently make money." [01:57]
