# GameStop Warrant Analysis and Breakdown | Meme Stock Watch Ep50

## Summary

Eric walks through GameStop's warrant dividend distribution in detail, using source documents (8-K filings, OCC notices, broker policies) to separate fact from speculation. Holders of GME shares as of the October 3 record date will receive one warrant per 10 shares, exercisable at $32/share until October 2026, tradable under ticker GMEWS. The warrants are functionally similar to call options but issued as a separate security; they are not immediately dilutive but become dilutive only if exercised and settled via share creation.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2025-09-14)

- **Record date and settlement:** Shares must be owned and settled by close of business October 3, 2025 to qualify for warrants; T+1 settlement means purchase by October 2 [29:46]
- **Distribution mechanics:** ~59 million warrants to be distributed October 7; holders of convertible senior notes also receive warrants on an as-converted basis without requiring conversion [08:00]
- **Warrant terms:** $32 strike price, October 2026 expiration, tradable on NYSE under GMEWS starting day after distribution [09:01, 09:33]
- **Short interest snapshot:** 66 million shares short (~16% of float), 4–5 days to cover—anomalously high for the short interest level, suggesting unusual positioning [35:42]

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Warrant vs. call option:** Warrants function identically to call options (strike, expiration, extrinsic value) but are issued as standalone securities rather than resting in an options chain [20:42, 56:12]
- **Dilution timing:** Not immediately dilutive; becomes dilutive only upon exercise and share creation. Framing as "non-dilutive" is technically accurate only before exercise [21:43, 44:33]
- **Broker support variance:** Major brokers (Fidelity, Schwab, TD, Weeble, IB) will deliver actual warrants; discount brokers (Robinhood, Wealthsimple, Revolut) typically liquidate and pay cash equivalent [25:19, 27:01]
- **Short seller obligation myth:** Shorts are not obligated to deliver warrants; brokers handle warrant delivery to lenders of borrowed shares via standard locate mechanics [12:08, 18:04]
- **Exercise vs. trade decision:** Most warrant holders will trade rather than exercise because buying shares at spot price is more capital-efficient than exercising at $32 [48:41, 53:05]
- **Extrinsic value and time decay:** Warrants will trade with extrinsic value reflecting probability of finishing in-the-money; longer duration (13+ months) supports meaningful premium [56:12, 57:23]
- **OCC adjustment:** Existing GME options adjust to GME1 with warrant component built into deliverable (100 shares + 10 warrants) until warrant expiration [37:32, 38:47]
- **Liquidity as decision driver:** Initial illiquidity in GMEWS may push holders toward exercise despite inefficiency; market-making and bid-ask spread will be critical unknowns [30:41, 01:30:41]
