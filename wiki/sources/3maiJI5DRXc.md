# Volume over Open Interest

## Summary

Volume over open interest (V/OI) is a screening metric that identifies where options positions are being actively built across expiration cycles. A high V/OI ratio signals new position initiation rather than closing trades, enabling traders to spot unusual activity and generate trade ideas.

## Key takeaways

- **V/OI as a position-building detector** [00:00–00:31]: When volume significantly exceeds open interest, it indicates new positions being opened rather than existing positions being closed, since closed trades would draw down open interest.
- **Practical screening in HIMS** [00:31–00:53]: Real-world example showing V/OI ratios of 6×, 8×, and 859× across different strikes/expirations, each indicating varying degrees of position accumulation and potential trade opportunity.
- **Trade idea generation** [00:53]: High V/OI readings serve as a starting point for developing directional or volatility-based trade ideas around the identified strikes.

## Notable quotes

"When you see big volume over open interest, it tells you that it's not closing trades, at least most of it, because there's nothing to close."
