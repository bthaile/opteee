# GameStop eBay Deal Explained by Ryan Cohen

## Summary

Eric breaks down Ryan Cohen's Fox Business interview with Charles Payne regarding GameStop's proposed $56 billion acquisition of eBay at $125/share (50% cash, 50% equity). The deal structure involves immediate liquidity to eBay shareholders while combining operational efficiencies; Eric analyzes the financing mechanics, near-term dilution implications, and multiple paths to deal closure (negotiated, hostile proxy, or failed bid), noting current betting markets price ~20–25% probability of close within 1–2 years.

## Key takeaways

- **Deal structure mechanics** [01:38–02:39]: $28 billion cash (50%), equity rollover (50%); eBay shareholders receive immediate liquidity while owning 60% of combined earnings vs. 80% in pure stock deal; GameStop shareholders own 40% of combined entity.
- **Operational efficiency thesis** [07:21–08:17]: Cohen targets $2.5B in eBay sales/marketing cuts; combined entity earnings projected to grow from $40B (eBay alone) to $90B+ through cost reduction; eBay's 5.6B operating expense on 11B revenue is "asset-like" and restructurable.
- **Board/governance critique** [12:41–13:07]: GameStop board fees are zero with directors holding substantial personal capital; eBay board costs ~$4M/year with average director compensation ~$350K/year—Cohen frames this as systemic inefficiency.
- **Deal path scenarios** [13:33–14:48]: (1) Negotiated path: shareholder vote, exchange ratio negotiation, filings, close; (2) Hostile path: direct shareholder approach, proxy fight, tender offer; (3) Failed bid. Current betting markets ~20–25% probability of close within 1–2 years.
- **Position reconfiguration** [06:08–06:56]: Eric recently moved GameStop allocation to [[covered-strangle]] due to material near-term uncertainty; shrunk position size while remaining long; notes Burry recently exited for separate reasons.
- **Dilution vs. accretion** [07:50–08:17]: Cohen emphasizes this is *accretive* dilution (increases EPS despite share issuance) via operational leverage, not destructive dilution; combined entity earnings growth offsets shareholder count increase.

## Notable quotes

> "There's dilution that decreases earnings per share and there's dilution that is accretive to earnings per share. This is a transaction that I believe is accretive to both eBay shareholders and GameStop shareholders because we are going to increase earnings per share significantly by making the business more efficient."
