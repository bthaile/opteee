# Two BIG Things Holding Your Trading Back - Sizing & Inexperience

## Summary

This video addresses two critical failure points for options traders: improper position sizing and insufficient experience before deploying capital. Eric emphasizes that short-volatility strategies require rigorous backtesting, forward testing, and paper-trading cycles—particularly around earnings—before risking real money. Proper sizing must balance conservatism (avoiding blowups) with sufficient scale to generate meaningful returns.

## Key takeaways

- **Position sizing for short-vol plays** [00:00]: Target at least 5 trades per day for short-volatility strategies; exact sizing depends on selectivity and risk tolerance.
- **Mandatory testing before live capital** [00:23]: Paper trade any earnings strategy for a minimum of two full quarters before deploying real money; this informs conservative sizing that prevents account destruction while maintaining profitability.
- **Testing methodology hierarchy** [00:23]: Use backtesting, forward testing, or live paper-trading cycles to validate strategy mechanics and position-sizing assumptions.
- **Sizing sweet spot** [00:42]: Size positions conservatively enough to avoid blowups, but large enough to generate meaningful profit—this balance is discovered through testing, not guesswork.

## Notable quotes

> "Before you ever put any money behind an earnings strategy, you should paper trade whatever the effects are and the strategies that you build around those effects for at least two quarters."
