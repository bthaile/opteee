# I Risked $40,000 on RGTI Long Puts - Here's Exactly What Happened

## Summary

Eric walks through a live breakdown trade in RGTI executed on 4 November 2025, where he bought 95 long puts at the 30 strike for $4.15 per contract, targeting a downside continuation after a 275% five-week rally. The position was managed across four weeks using soft and hard stops tied to price action and consolidation behavior, with 85% closed on 24 November for $6.90 (realizing ~$22k) and the final 15 contracts exited on 4 December for a total realized P&L of ~$22.4k—a 57% return on risk capital in approximately one month. The trade exemplifies how strategy-specific profit mechanisms, win-rate expectations (45–55% for breakdowns), and disciplined scaling rules drive position management rather than arbitrary profit-taking or tight stops.

## Key takeaways

- **Breakdowns as a profit mechanism** [00:00–01:21]: Look for stocks with significant upside moves (275%+ in this case) that show consolidation behavior; watch for consistency through consolidation zones and how price behaves during pullbacks to identify downside continuation setups.

- **Price-action entry signals** [02:44–03:54]: Entry triggered at 12:30 PT on 4 Nov when RGTI showed accelerating downside after a red day and gap-down beginning; underlying at ~35.77, bought 95 × 30 puts @ $4.15 debit.

- **Soft stop vs. hard stop discipline** [05:13–06:19]: Soft stop at ~43.25 (85–90% exit probability unless strong reason to hold); hard stop ~44.75 (above recent highs). Newer traders must avoid the disposition effect of letting losers run; soft stops enforce this.

- **Dynamic stop management during downtrend** [06:19–07:23]: As price gaps down over first week, slide both soft and hard stops lower to reduce total risk while keeping position alive; no partial profit taken because position was undersized.

- **Consolidation breakout as exit signal** [08:36–09:56]: On 24 Nov, after 8-day consolidation (23.5–27 range), price shows upside strength; close 85% of position (80 contracts) for $6.90, realizing ~$22k. Leave 15 contracts with unchanged hard stop to test for further downside.

- **Final exit and P&L realization** [09:56–11:09]: On 4 Dec, price breaks above consolidation; close remaining 15 contracts, realizing $435 additional. Total: ~$22.4k realized P&L (~57% return on risk capital) in ~1 month.

- **Strategy-specific win-rate and position sizing** [11:09–12:28]: Breakdowns have 45–55% win rate; therefore require ≥2:1 reward-to-risk ratio to be profitable long-term. Manage exits and scaling to feed positive data samples into the strategy, not arbitrary rules.
