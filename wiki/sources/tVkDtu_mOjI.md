# GameStop Options Activity & Ryan Cohen HSR Penalty | Market Update

## Summary

Eric reviews quiet options activity in GameStop, noting heavy call skew and front-expiration volume being managed by traders attempting to salvage decaying premium. He also covers Ryan Cohen's ~$985k HSR Act civil penalty for failing to file required forms before acquiring Wells Fargo shares, and briefly discusses Chewy's shareholder offering structure and concurrent buyback program.

## Key takeaways

- **GME chart structure**: Longer-term moving averages still positive-sloping but turning negative on the daily timeframe; stock trading sideways in a range awaiting directional break [00:00–01:05]
- **Front-expiration volume management**: High volume in Jan 20 calls reflects traders rolling positions out in time rather than opening new trades; theta decay without catalyst makes holding front-month options untenable [01:05–02:12]
- **Deeper-dated call building**: Bulk of larger premium trades (300k–200k+) are in 2025-dated calls at $20 and $10 strikes—historically unusual concentration for GME [03:13–04:06]
- **Ryan Cohen HSR violation**: $985k civil penalty for acquiring 562k+ Wells Fargo shares without filing required antitrust forms; Cohen intended board influence, distinguishing this from passive investment exemption [04:06–07:51]
- **Chewy offering structure**: Buddy Chester sub LLC offering 500M shares; Chewy buying 300M concurrently while maintaining separate 500M repurchase authorization [08:04–09:04]

## Notable quotes

> "People are trying to salvage the value out of these options. And because they're going to decay away in the next day or so... it's just kind of holding something that's breaking apart in your hands that you can't grab a hold of once it's gone." [02:12]
