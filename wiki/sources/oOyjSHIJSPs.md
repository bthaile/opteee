# TSLA, DAL, and Earnings Season - Stock Market Update | Options Trading Live

## Summary

Live market update covering three key themes: Russell 2000 outperformance amid tariff sensitivity, Tesla's deteriorating fundamentals despite low implied volatility, and earnings season positioning in Delta Airlines and Kagra Group. The host analyzes volatility surfaces to assess market pricing of tariff risk, dissects Tesla's revenue contraction and capex expansion, and explores options activity patterns ahead of earnings catalysts.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2025-07-10)

- **Russell 2000 leading indices** [07:34]: IWM up ~1.08% on the day, outpacing SPY and QQQ. Small caps more sensitive to tariff news due to lack of pricing power vs. mega-cap dominance (e.g., Apple).
- **Tariff volatility surface flat at August event** [12:42–14:30]: One-August implied volatility shows no elevated concern relative to surrounding expirations; market not pricing in heavy put-side hedging for tariff announcement. Term structure comparison (18 July to 15 August) shows normal relationship, suggesting low near-term anxiety.
- **Tesla IV percentile at 25% despite 15% drop** [26:21]: Unusually low implied volatility given magnitude of move and news-driven chaos; suggests market may be underpricing idiosyncratic risk tied to Musk's political involvement.
- **Tesla revenue down YoY; operating income compressed** [34:18–35:03]: Automotive revenue contracting significantly; operating expenses flat but income down sharply. Capex expansion (property, plant, equipment) suggests investment phase, but topline deterioration is concerning.
- **Model 2 launch delayed; radio silence since June 28** [01:00:57–01:01:44]: Expected production start in H1 2025 has been pushed; no recent updates. Critical catalyst for next earnings; absence of guidance could be negative surprise.
- **Delta Airlines heavy call buying day-before-earnings** [01:03:18]: 5,400 55-strike calls (22 DTE) traded vs. 181 open interest; also unusual 2026 June 50-strike buying (343 DTE) suggesting directional bet with continuation move expected post-catalyst.
- **Kagra Group mixed options activity** [01:12:59–01:14:49]: Calls and puts both bought on the day; front expiration dominance indicates expectation of large move. Historical earnings misses modest (within 1–2% range).
- **Earnings calendar kicks off Monday PM** [01:15:49–01:16:44]: Monday and Tuesday cycles begin; Delta as front reporter will set tone for airline sector (UAL, etc.). Front expirations show elevated IV due to earnings concentration.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Volatility term structure as fact-check tool** [12:42–23:57]: Compare day-over-day IV changes across expirations using leapfrog method or Google Sheets time series; adjust for time decay to isolate event-driven vs. structural vol changes.
- **Gamma and convexity relationship** [01:18:11–01:24:46]: Gamma peaks at-the-money and flattens as you move OTM or ITM. Shorter-dated options have higher gamma (peakier curve); longer-dated options have flattened gamma across strikes. Buyers pay for convexity; sellers collect theta in exchange for capped upside.
- **Delta selection in long-dated earnings plays** [01:06:55–01:08:10]: Buying far-dated OTM calls (e.g., 343 DTE, 50 delta) reduces gamma but preserves delta compounding potential if directional move is large enough; lower vega sensitivity to front-expiration IV crush.
- **Short-term vs. long-term price predictability** [16:08–17:51]: Economic forecasts harder to predict near-term; stock prices easier to predict long-term but harder short-term due to positioning shifts. Opening range breakouts are one of few reliable short-term patterns (driven by overnight positioning and open auction dynamics).
- **Earnings positioning via weeklies** [01:15:49]: Trade front expiration containing earnings for purest earnings effect; monthly expirations mute the move. Weeklies also serve as liquidity proxy.
