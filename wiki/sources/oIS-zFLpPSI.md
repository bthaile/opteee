# Core vs Speculative - How To Balance A $25K Portfolio

## Summary

This video addresses portfolio construction for a $25K account, emphasizing the balance between a stable core allocation (the "keel of the ship") and a speculative allocation designed to deploy profit mechanisms and strategies. The host discusses how to implement speculative trades—particularly short-volatility strategies in indices—while managing capital requirements through the use of wings (spread structures) on smaller accounts.

## Key takeaways

- **Core vs. speculative split**: The core allocation provides stability and consistent returns; the speculative allocation applies advanced profit mechanisms and strategies [00:00–00:26].
- **Capital efficiency via wings**: On smaller accounts, add wings (spreads) to short-volatility strategies to reduce capital requirements and margin impact [00:26–00:48].
- **Profit mechanisms as the framework**: The speculative sleeve is where you deploy the full suite of strategies built on identified profit mechanisms, rather than ad-hoc trades [00:00–00:26].
