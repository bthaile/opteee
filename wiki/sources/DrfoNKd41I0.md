# Using Volatility to Create Trade Opportunities | Live Stock Market Analysis

## Summary

Eric analyzes the Iran–US conflict as a macro catalyst for volatility and identifies tradable themes across defense, energy, and shipping sectors. He emphasizes using volatility's relationship to itself and realized volatility to find edge, demonstrates how to read options skew and term structure in distressed names like airlines, and cautions against mechanical volatility-selling while showing how to operationalize opportunities in both large-cap and smaller mid-cap plays.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2026-03-08)

- **Conflict duration and regime**: Iran–US conflict likely to persist 6+ weeks, possibly several months; regime change objective complicates rapid resolution [16:50–18:29]
- **Defense sector leadership**: GE, RTX, LMT, Northrop Grumman, L3 Harris showing strong participation; massive breakdown in participation across defense names signals clear leaders and laggards [22:15–24:25]
- **Oil supply disruption**: Iranian oil production and refining capacity under direct attack; demand steady or rising, supply compressed → expect crude upside, but instrument selection matters (CL vs. Iranian light crude) [28:46–32:21]
- **LNG opportunity**: Qatar output down; Cheniere positioned to capture demand vacuum as long as conflict persists [45:57–47:06]
- **Airlines volatility distortion**: AAL, UAL, AAL showing haywire term structure (contango/backwardation flips); put skew not yet steep enough; market pricing floor ~$8.70–$8.80 [38:35–43:23]
- **Shipping headwind**: Strait of Hormuz transit friction; DHT and shipping plays less attractive near-term, but repricing opportunity post-conflict [49:41–50:08]

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Volatility ≠ sell signal**: High IV is not a notice to short; focus on IV's relationship to itself (vol-of-vol) and realized volatility [43:23–43:49]
- **Implied vs. realized lag**: Realized volatility lags by ~30 days; use lagging indicators to compare implied to realized for the same period; forward projections superior [44:17–45:24]
- **Skew and term structure reading**: Equidistant OTM plots less useful than delta-normalized plots; look for abnormal flips and asymmetry to spot where market disagrees on risk [39:32–42:08]
- **Disposition effect**: Traders take winners too fast, hold losers too long; overcome by focusing on conditions-based re-entry, not prior P&L [05:49–01:13:17]
- **Breakout whipsaw**: Getting kicked out and re-entering is normal; decision to re-enter should be agnostic to prior realized P&L, conditional only on setup persistence [01:11:29–01:12:58]
- **Broker execution quality**: SEC rules 605 (execution quality) and 606 (order routing) reveal hidden costs; IBKR standard for professional traders; avoid payment-for-order-flow traps [01:14:52–01:17:39]
- **Small-cap concentration**: Mid-cap and small-cap defense/energy plays move 1.5–2× large-cap moves; higher volatility = higher edge for traders [50:34–51:58]
- **Thesis persistence**: If hypothesis unchanged, don't exit; if conditions change, exit immediately and re-enter only when conditions return [01:05:49–01:09:11]
