# Roaring Kitty GME Options Update

## Summary

Eric analyzes Roaring Kitty's position change between June 10 and June 13, 2024, examining whether the increase in shares (from 5M to 9M) resulted from exercising deep ITM $20 calls or from selling the original position and rebuying. The analysis explores multiple scenarios with cost-basis reconciliation, noting that the exact mechanism cannot be determined definitively from public data. GME's options chain shows significant IV contraction and normalization of call/put open-interest skew following the position update.

## Key takeaways

- **Position scaling mechanics** [01:03–01:25]: Large positions (5M shares, 120k contracts) are scaled into over time to avoid market impact and achieve reasonable fills; the presence of multiple decimal places in average prices confirms this averaging behavior.

- **Cost-basis variance analysis** [01:49–02:10]: When determining whether options were exercised, the challenge is that premium paid varied across the scaled entry—some contracts above, some below the reported average—making exact reconstruction impossible.

- **Exercise scenario: 40,010 contracts** [02:37–05:21]: If ~40k of the $20 calls were exercised, the combined strike + premium basis would be ~$256.75, compared to the observed $260.91 cost per share. The $1.65M variance is plausible given this represents only ~one-third of the original 120k lot and depends on FIFO/LIFO accounting and relative premium costs.

- **Alternative scenario: sell-and-rebuy** [07:33]: GME traded near the $260.91 basis throughout the period, making it entirely plausible he sold all 5M shares and repurchased 9M at market prices rather than exercising options.

- **IV contraction and skew normalization** [08:23–09:54]: Implied volatility across expirations has fallen from >300% to <250%; call/put open-interest disparity (previously skewed heavily to calls) is normalizing toward typical put-heavy hedging patterns, suggesting some unwinding of speculative positioning.

- **$20 strike footprint** [08:23–08:46]: The $20 call still shows 64,718 open interest (down from 58,400 yesterday), remains 1-delta deep ITM, with lower volume today (12,468) but continued participation.

- **Volume and price action** [07:57–08:23]: GME showed an inside day with below-average volume (22-day average basis) and range ($11.86 vs. 5-day average $25.24), suggesting consolidation rather than directional conviction.
