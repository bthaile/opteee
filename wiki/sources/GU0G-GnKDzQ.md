# Analyzing Roaring Kitty's Trades Pt5 | GameStop Analysis Live Ep13

## Summary

Eric conducts a deep forensic analysis of Roaring Kitty's June 2024 position entry, reconstructing the 120k 21 June $20 calls purchased between 20–30 May 2024 using order-flow data. The session challenges the "magical dates" theory by examining position sizing, scaling patterns, and the relationship between GME price action and portfolio equity curves, concluding that leverage and options scaling—not date-specific timing—drove the account growth from $34M to $210M.

## Key takeaways

- **Position reconstruction via order flow** [02:37:00–03:03:00]: Eric manually reconstructs Roaring Kitty's 120k 21 June $20 calls by analyzing daily transaction data, average entry times (~11:25 AM), and premium paid (~$2.81 avg), revealing a scaling-in pattern over 20–30 May rather than a single large entry.

- **Scaling-in disproves "date certainty" thesis** [03:00:00–03:03:49]: The distribution of buys (light on 20–21 May, bulk on 22 May, trailing through 30 May) contradicts the hypothesis that Roaring Kitty knew specific catalyst dates; if he had date certainty, he would concentrate capital at the nearest expiration, not scale across a week.

- **Position sizing as a percentage of portfolio** [02:05:00–02:09:00]: The April 24–26 call purchases (~$500k total) represent only ~1.5% of his $34M portfolio at that time—too small to justify if he possessed high-conviction date knowledge; professional traders with certainty deploy 10–20%+ of capital.

- **GME price action during entry window** [02:59:00–03:02:00]: GME traded $23.14 (20 May) → $21.12 (22 May) → $22.61 (30 May); Roaring Kitty scaled in as price declined, consistent with opportunistic accumulation rather than pre-planned catalyst timing.

- **Equity curve vs. GME price correlation** [02:23:00–02:24:00]: Over the full 1143-day absence (16 April 2021–2 June 2024), GME appreciated ~1900%, yet the account grew from $38M to $210M—a 5.5× multiple suggesting options leverage and possibly trading in other securities (not just GME) drove the outsized return.

- **Survivorship bias and leverage as primary driver** [01:00:00–01:02:00]: Roaring Kitty's early trades ($100k → calls on GME) succeeded due to both conviction and luck; subsequent scaling used leverage to compound gains, not magical date knowledge. His own live streams reveal he adjusts positions dynamically and admits mistakes.

- **Indirect communication vs. hidden messages** [46:22–58:42]: Ryan Cohen and Roaring Kitty's tweets/memes are likely trolling and indirect communication for entertainment, not encoded trading signals; if they were communicating material information, the risk of SEC investigation would be prohibitive.

- **Chewy position as a test case** [09:06–09:28]: Roaring Kitty's Chewy trade (entry ~$24, exit ~$27) produced minimal return despite his 13G filing; this suggests he was testing market reaction and trolling, not executing a high-conviction thesis.

- **Volume expansion precedes large moves** [37:20–37:51]: Every major GME jump Eric has observed is preceded by volume expansion in the options chain; this is consistent with informed institutional activity, not retail speculation or swap expirations.

- **Swap expiration theory lacks statistical support** [39:20–42:29]: While swaps *could* drive moves, regression analysis of historic swap expirations shows non-correlation with subsequent price movement; the theory conflates correlation with causation and ignores dark-pool mechanics designed to obscure forced buying.
