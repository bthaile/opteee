# Money Manager and Futures Trader Tom Basso

## Summary

Tom Basso, retired commodity trading advisor and founder of Trendstat, discusses his 50-year evolution from chemical engineer to systematic multi-market trader. He emphasizes trend-following across 40–60 positions spanning futures, ETFs, and equities, with an "all-weather" portfolio designed to profit regardless of market direction. His core philosophy: attack risk through diversification and automation rather than avoid it, achieving serene, low-drawdown returns via position sizing and hedging strategies.

## Key takeaways

- **Career arc: engineer → retail trader → money manager → retirement trader** [02:15–09:16]. Started with low-priced stocks in 1974, moved to futures (~1976) after realizing need for non-correlated assets, built Trendstat to ~$600M AUM, then retired after 28 years when regulatory burden consumed 60% of time.

- **Trend-following discovery via point-and-figure charts** [27:08–29:59]. Manually plotted commodity charts from Wall Street Journal archives, laid them on the floor to identify big runs, circled top 10 trades, and reverse-engineered common characteristics—discovering that simple trend-following captured 9–10 of those high-conviction setups.

- **All-weather portfolio: 8+ strategies across 40–60 positions** [22:44–23:32]. Runs simultaneous trend-following (futures/ETFs), momentum (9-day, 21-day, 50-day timeframes), sideways-market premium selling (call/put spreads), and sector rotation. On any given day, ~60% of positions move favorably (big), 40% move against (small), yielding smooth equity curves.

- **Filling the "pothole": hedging drawdowns with uncorrelated strategies** [33:34–34:17]. When trend-following hits whipsaws in sideways markets, deploy index options (call spreads, put spreads) to exploit time decay and range-bound price action. Different strategies profit in different regimes; the goal is to smooth the equity curve by offsetting losses.

- **Position sizing as the cornerstone** [55:18–56:03]. Use spreadsheets or APIs (e.g., Interactive Brokers) to calculate shares based on risk per trade and account equity. Automate this calculation to remove emotion and ensure consistent risk allocation across all positions.

- **Automation mindset: eliminate routine tasks to free mental bandwidth** [56:53–58:48]. Started with spreadsheets, moved to fax-modem automation, then full order generation and transmission. Goal: reduce daily trading work from hours to 15–20 minutes, freeing time for strategy development and life outside markets.

- **Macro outlook for 2022: stay agnostic, prepare for volatility** [59:53–01:02:13]. "The market will do what the market will do." Maintain hedges for 60% S&P decline, long positions for continued rally, and diversified commodity exposure (lumber, oil, grains, meats). Volatility feeds profits if risk is managed well.

- **Enjoy the Ride (.world): asynchronous education platform** [01:06:08–01:12:39]. Built website to scale mentoring without working full-time. Hosts interviews, research papers, recommended reading, webinars, and trading-strategy video series. Designed for global audience (uses Google Translate for non-English inquiries).

- **Social media presence: Twitter (@_tombasso), Gettr, MeWe, LinkedIn** [01:13:20–01:17:01]. Prefers Gettr (777 characters) and MeWe (longer-form, image-friendly) over Twitter (288 characters, imposter problem). Facebook for family; Instagram not suited to data-driven content.
