# How to Use Linear Regression Channels to Make Better Trading Decisions

## Summary

Linear regression channels are a price analysis tool that combines a line of best fit with statistical bands to identify prevailing trends and price extremes. Unlike standard deviation channels, they use actual price extremes within a given aggregation period to construct the bands, making them useful for both long-term trend context and shorter-term reversal identification.

## Key takeaways

- **What linear regression channels are** [00:20–01:31]: A line of best fit (purple trend line) plus dashed bands (100% and 50% levels) derived from price extremes in the aggregation period, not standard deviations.
- **100% vs. 50% bands** [01:55–02:42]: The 100% band captures the widest price move in the dataset; the 50% band is half that distance from the line of best fit. Expect ~95% of closes within the 100% band and ~68% within the ±1 band.
- **Broader trend analysis** [04:16–05:24]: Use longer timeframes (3–5 year daily) to establish overall market sentiment and context; the slope of the line of best fit shows whether average price is rising or declining.
- **Identifying extremes for contrarian trades** [06:38–07:24]: When price touches the ±2 band (statistical extreme), it often signals a reversal opportunity; price typically cannot sustain at these extremes without significant volume support.
- **Shorter-term application** [08:10–09:13]: On 6-month or 3-month charts, watch how price reacts to the ±1 band; a break through suggests continuation; light touches suggest the move may not be complete.
- **Integration with other tools** [08:29–08:51]: Combine linear regression channels with price action patterns (higher highs/lows) and volume profile to confirm trend and support/resistance levels.

## Notable quotes
