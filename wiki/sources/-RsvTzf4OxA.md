# SpaceX IPO: Here's My Plan to Trade It

## Summary

SpaceX's IPO (priced at $135/share for Class A) represents the largest IPO by valuation (~$2 trillion) in history, launching on Nasdaq on June 12, 2026. The host outlines the business structure (space, connectivity, AI segments), historical IPO performance patterns, and a two-phase trading approach: capturing the initial run to the high, then assessing for any trending carryover into the close. No options will be available until June 16, limiting derivatives strategies on day one.

## Key takeaways

- **IPO valuation context** [00:20]: ~$2 trillion implied valuation; over 2.5× larger than the prior record IPO. Unprecedented scale and retail penetration.
- **Nasdaq listing mechanics** [01:09]: Delayed open expected (few hours after market open); market makers need time to gauge sentiment and set initial pricing.
- **Lockup exemptions** [01:57]: Small group exempt from lockup; potential insider selling pressure, though unlikely to overwhelm demand given scale of interest.
- **Options unavailable until June 16** [02:18]: No derivatives market on day one; proxy analysis via Rocket Lab and historical IPO data required.
- **Business segments** [03:27]: Three divisions—space (profitable), connectivity (Starlink growth), AI (data center demand). Only space currently profitable; AI at infancy with massive demand tailwinds.
- **Elon alignment incentive** [04:54]: Founder's mission to make humanity multi-planetary creates strong personal incentive beyond typical profit maximization; track record of execution.
- **Sector performance precedent** [08:25]: Historical IPO data (2015–2026) shows IT sector IPOs tend to outperform; expect opening above offer, high within first hour, then potential trailing off.
- **Two-phase trading plan** [09:18]: Phase 1—trade the run to the high (trend-following); Phase 2—assess for carryover position into close. Exit if price breaks lower; avoid trading if direction reverses completely.
- **Equity-only execution** [09:40]: No derivatives available; must use stock for day-one trading.
