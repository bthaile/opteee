# FINALLY Explaining My Trading Setup #optionstrading

## Summary

Eric walks through his core charting and analysis setup, detailing the specific technical tools he uses to assess price strength, identify trends, and locate support/resistance levels. The setup combines moving averages of multiple periods, standard deviation channels, volume profile, and order-flow volume analysis to create a multi-timeframe decision framework.

## Key takeaways

- **Multi-period moving averages function as trend and strength assessment** [00:00]: Different MA periods serve dual purpose—they act like a MACD to gauge price momentum, and the selection of periods aligns with the timeframe of the strategy being traded (shorter-term strategies weight shorter MAs more heavily).
- **Standard deviation channels identify extremes and trend boundaries** [00:22]: Dashed lines plot the mean plus/minus one standard deviation to help identify when price is trending versus when it's at an extreme.
- **Volume profile reveals support/resistance via price-level transaction history** [00:22–00:41]: The gray background visualization shows cumulative volume at each price level, providing an alternative price measurement for identifying key support and resistance zones.
- **Order-flow volume breakdown (bid vs. offer) adds directional context** [00:41]: Volume is further segmented by whether transactions occurred at the bid or the offer, clarifying whether buying or selling pressure dominated at each price level.

## Notable quotes
