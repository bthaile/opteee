# Profit mechanism first #options #trading

## Summary

Options traders, especially newer ones, often over-optimize for secondary inputs like implied volatility when the primary profit mechanism—directional accuracy—should be the focus. While volatility pricing matters, getting direction right is the foundational requirement; secondary and tertiary factors can be layered on only after the core edge is established.

## Key takeaways

- **Identify your primary profit mechanism first** [00:21–00:43]: For single-option directional trades, direction is the dominant factor. Volatility pricing is important but secondary.
- **Don't over-optimize for volatility** [00:21]: Buying or selling at theoretically perfect IV levels doesn't save a directionally wrong trade. Directional error dominates volatility edge.
- **Layer secondary factors after the core edge** [00:43]: Once you've optimized for direction, overlay volatility, Greeks, and other tertiary considerations—not before.
- **Options complexity can become a liability** [00:00]: The customizability of options is powerful, but newer traders often use it to chase optimization rabbit holes instead of focusing on the primary driver of P&L.

## Notable quotes

> "If you're trading single options with the directional disposition, your primary profit mechanism is direction. Then you can overlay those secondary, tertiary factors."
