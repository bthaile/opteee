# Spreads and Impact to Short Option Strategies

## Summary

This video examines the mechanical cost of adding protective wings (spreads) to short option strategies, particularly how bid-ask spread consumption erodes edge. The core argument: if your average win ($100) equals your average loss plus the spread cost, you've eliminated your edge entirely and must rely on directional skill to remain profitable.

## Key takeaways

- **Spread cost as edge killer** [00:00–00:23]: Adding wings to a short premium strategy consumes the bid-ask spread; if spread cost ≈ average win, the strategy becomes negatively expectant on its own mechanics.
- **Edge requirement shifts** [00:23–00:43]: A spread-constrained short strategy no longer has edge from the premium sale alone; profitability now depends entirely on directional accuracy or other external factors.
- **Blind selling + wings = poor setup** [00:43]: Mechanically adding protective tails to a short position without directional conviction or edge analysis is a high-risk approach.
