# GameStop Covered Strangle Live Trade Update

## Summary

Eric reviews the performance of his covered strangle strategy applied to GameStop, which despite representing only 4% of his core allocation has delivered 22% return (realized + unrealized P&L) since inception. The sideways price action around $27–28 creates ideal conditions for the three-leg covered strangle (long equity, cash-secured puts, ratio short calls), with current focus on short puts. Implied volatility at 84.5 with a 19th percentile rank indicates muted near-term expectations, supported by a massive call open-interest skew (913k calls vs. 292k puts) distributed across longer-dated expirations rather than front months.

## Key takeaways

- **Portfolio contribution paradox** [00:00–01:36]: GameStop is the second-largest contributor to covered strangle performance despite being the smallest 4% allocation; year-to-date return on invested capital is 22% (realized + unrealized), vs. 13% underlying move from $24.31 to $27.48.

- **Three-leg covered strangle structure** [03:04–04:33]: Long equity and cash-secured puts provide directional deltas; ratio short calls are secondary, deployed only when equity is held or sufficient put premium exists to allow reaction time if price rallies. Current positioning is primarily cash-secured puts.

- **Sideways price action as edge** [03:04–04:33]: GameStop has traded sideways around $27–28 since late December, creating the ideal environment for premium harvesting; allocation is 61% utilized of a $25k base (now $29k after realized P&L reinvestment).

- **Implied volatility context** [06:06–07:21]: IV at 84.5 is historically light for GME (19th percentile rank); no significant unusual options flow detected in the past week; high open-interest contracts are predominantly 250+ days out, suggesting longer-term structural positioning rather than near-term directional bets.

- **Volatility term structure normalization** [08:41–09:59]: Call skew has compressed significantly; put side now shows standard heavy skew (vs. historical call-heavy skew). Call OI skew of 3:1 (913k vs. 292k puts) is the largest in a long time but distributed across all expirations, not concentrated in front months. High call volume (192k) at the bid suggests institutional call selling against held shares.

- **Market expectation signal** [09:59–11:14]: Term structure and flow patterns indicate the market is not pricing in a massive near-term move; current environment supports continued volatility harvesting via covered strangle mechanics.
