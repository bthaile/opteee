# Is Roaring Kitty Buying SIRI Options?

## Summary

Eric analyzes a sudden spike in Sirius XM (SIRI) volume and options activity following cryptic emoji posts from Roaring Kitty, attempting to determine whether he is building a position. While the underlying stock shows genuine momentum and elevated volume, the options chain does not display the large, conviction-sized trades typical of Roaring Kitty's historical activity; instead, Friday's volume skewed toward put-buying and call-selling, suggesting net downside bias despite the stock's breakout.

## Key takeaways

- **Volume spike and breakout trigger** [01:27–02:18]: SIRI volume exploded from ~39M to 330M on June 21, triggering Eric's alert system; a subsequent break above resistance on July 2 with sustained elevated volume prompted a small, asymmetric long trade with a tight 295 stop.

- **Emoji speculation vs. data** [05:01–05:46]: Roaring Kitty's recent emoji posts sparked theories linking them to SIRI (via "American Broadcasting" interpretation), but Eric emphasizes this is entertainment-grade analysis; no portfolio filings or position updates have been disclosed since mid-June.

- **Options chain lacks conviction sizing** [06:34–07:50]: Despite ~820k calls and 570k puts in open interest, the chain is sparse (only 0.5–6 strike range); compared to GameStop's historical 5,000–10,000 lot sizes, SIRI options show no large, coordinated buying that would indicate a major player entering.

- **Abnormal IV term structure** [09:25–10:41]: The July 5 expiration (5 DTE) shows 195 IV, but the August 2 expiration containing earnings shows only 144 IV—inverted from normal; this suggests the front cycle's IV is driven by the recent move, not earnings anticipation.

- **Put-heavy skew and Friday volume bias** [11:30–12:22]: The volatility smile is inverted across expirations, with puts trading at the offer (buying) and calls on the bid (selling); Friday's transaction flow was ~60% put-buying, indicating net downside positioning despite the stock's breakout.

- **Largest options trade is far-dated leverage** [14:16–15:08]: The biggest single trade (~195k premium) is a long call far out in 2025 at a 3.5 strike with a wide 66–80 cent bid-ask; this signals leveraged upside exposure with time, not near-term conviction.

- **Equity vs. options signal mismatch** [15:56–17:04]: Stock volume is genuinely elevated and above average, but options activity does not corroborate large informed buying; Eric leans toward "no" on Roaring Kitty involvement from the options lens, though equity-side activity remains possible.
