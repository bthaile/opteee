# win rate is a lie

## Summary

Win rate alone is a meaningless metric for options traders. A strategy with a 95% win rate can still be unprofitable if the average loss on the 5% of losing trades exceeds the average gain on winning trades. The critical metric is **expected return** (expectancy), which combines win rate, average win size, and average loss size into a single equation that determines long-term profitability.

## Key takeaways

- **Win rate is not the goal** [00:00] — A 95% win rate means nothing if your losing trades lose $290 on average while your winning trades only gain $15.
- **Expected return is the equation that matters** [00:32] — Calculate: (win rate × average win) − (loss rate × average loss). This determines whether you'll profit or lose money long-term.
- **Focus on the math, not the feel-good metric** [00:32] — Traders often chase high win rates because they feel good psychologically, but a strategy with a 50% win rate and proper position sizing can be far more profitable.
