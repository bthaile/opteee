# Investing Goals in 5 Steps

## Summary

A five-step framework for creating a personal investing roadmap: begin with the end in mind (desired outcome), quantify your current state (liquid net worth, income, trading history), project a realistic timeline and growth targets, and establish tracking metrics to monitor progress. The approach emphasizes conservative planning, honest self-assessment, and continuous adjustment via the OODA loop (observe, orient, decide, act).

## Key takeaways

- **Begin with the end in mind** [00:32]: Define your desired end state—typically income replacement—and choose your retirement style (lean FIRE vs. fat FIRE). This shapes all downstream planning.
- **Quantify your current state** [03:01]: Assess liquid net worth (investable capital), current income, and at least five years of trading performance history to establish a realistic baseline.
- **Project forward with three levers** [06:46]: Adjust savings rate, time horizon, or expected return to reach your target. Use all three rather than relying on one alone.
- **Conservative estimates are critical** [09:27]: Underestimate returns and overestimate time; life disruptions will occur, so build slack into your plan.
- **Break long-term goals into tracking metrics** [10:10]: If your 10-year goal is defined, your 5-year checkpoint and annual/monthly targets follow logically. Monitor these continuously.
- **Prioritize savings first, then income growth** [10:48]: Increasing savings rate is the easiest lever early on; once maxed, focus on side income to expand margins.
- **Apply the OODA loop perpetually** [11:46]: Observe your current position, orient to your plan, decide on adjustments, and act. Review quarterly or annually to accelerate timelines or ease targets as needed.

## Notable quotes

> "Begin with the end in mind" — Stephen Covey's principle applied to investing: define what you're actually trying to achieve before you start.

> "Be conservative in your numbers and be realistic" — the most overlooked aspect of planning; most people avoid it because they dislike the answer they'll see.

## Regime / context

Educational framework applicable across market regimes. The five-year trading history requirement assumes sufficient market cycles to validate consistency; planning conducted during extended bull markets (e.g., 2012) may underestimate bear-market performance variance. All numerical examples are illustrative and pre-tax; actual outcomes depend on individual tax footprint and market conditions.
