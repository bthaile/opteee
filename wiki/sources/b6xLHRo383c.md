# SoFi, GameStop, and Bitcoin Treasury Analysis — Live Trading Session

## Summary

Live trading analysis covering three distinct themes: SoFi's near-term bullish call positioning versus weak price action; GameStop's strong call skew and FTD cycle research from the community; and critical examination of Strive Asset Management's Bitcoin treasury proposal to GameStop, with emphasis on volatility risk and the primacy of volume-driven price moves over timing theories.

## Key takeaways

### SoFi Analysis
- **Volume profile reveals overhead resistance** [24:55]: Major volume node at ~$15.64 handle; current price action shows moderate shelf but faces significant overhead. 27% drop over 74 trading days; near-term rally evident in higher local highs and lows, but price struggling to break above $13.75 resistance.
- **Institutional ownership context** [15:43–17:34]: SoFi ~6.5% institutionally held (vs. Microsoft 72.2%); lower institutional ownership correlates with higher volatility and more readily changing hands. Structural trading opportunity: less locked-up shares = more price movement potential.
- **Options positioning bullish but price lagging** [33:03–36:23]: IV skew 56% on both 39-delta and 41-delta calls (slight call skew); net call flow spiked ~April 23, but price has declined since. Demonstrates options flow is not omnipotent—timing matters. Longest OI concentration at 250 days (longer-term expectation, not near-term).
- **Trading setup** [34:48]: Resistance at $13.75; support/stop around $12.40. If bullish, scale in smaller with expectation of being stopped, then add on upside breakout. Price must support options positioning.

### GameStop Deep Dive
- **Call skew expansion on May 23 expiration** [01:07:56–01:09:34]: 33-delta calls at 51% IV vs. puts at ~25% (roughly double skew). If this persists, opportunity to take short puts down, buy shares, sell calls against for call skew capture. Protect downside with alerts near break-even on short calls.
- **Volume precedes every major move** [01:48:21–01:50:45]: Before every significant GameStop rally, massive volume expansion occurs *before* the price move (not during). April example: volume jumped from 2.6M to 8.6M to 36M. Use 5-day and 20-day trailing averages to spot these shifts. This is the simplest, most reliable signal—more useful than cycle theories.
- **FTD cycle analysis limitations** [01:25:04–01:26:20]: Community research shows statistical correlation but conflates timing with causation. Assumption that shorts wait full 35 calendar days is flawed; they optimize pricing over the window. "Green day" methodology too loose. Correlation ≠ predictability. Always include control names (Tesla, Microsoft) to isolate abnormal behavior.
- **Short interest and macro context** [01:38:57–01:40:13]: Short interest spike in 2019 coincided with low-rate environment and Fed balance sheet expansion. However, motivation for shorts is secondary; the finding that short interest exploded is the key observation. Avoid leading hypotheses; approach analysis open-ended.

### Bitcoin Treasury Proposal (Strive / GameStop)
- **Strive Asset Management merger** [39:11–41:14]: Strive announced definitive agreement to merge with digital marketing company; will operate as first publicly traded Bitcoin-focused asset management firm on Nasdaq. Proposes Section 351 tax-free exchange of Bitcoin for equity—massive potential capital draw for high-net-worth Bitcoin holders.
- **Activist letter to GameStop** [42:44–43:50]: Strive proposes GameStop allocate ~$5B cash reserves to Bitcoin as primary treasury asset. Rationale: Bitcoin maximalism, forward-thinking brand positioning. **Critical counterargument**: GameStop still operates brick-and-mortar stores; Bitcoin volatility (29% drop in recent ibit move = $1.45B asset decrease) is incompatible with operational stability. Negative real return on cash provides *stability*—a feature, not a bug.
- **Structural concerns** [47:13–48:36]: Stability of cash reserves matters more than chasing Bitcoin returns. Risk-adjusted returns, not raw returns. GameStop should focus on store closures (693 closed 2019–2020; 401 closed in 2024 alone—massive acceleration) and digital pivot. Bitcoin as reserve is a "big bet" on adoption that may not materialize at scale soon.

### Broader Themes
- **Correlation analysis pitfall** [01:16:08–01:20:21]: During 2020 market stress, *everything* became correlated (Chevron 0.4, Pfizer 0.58, Tesla 0.7 vs. GME). Meme stock correlation is not evidence of orchestration; it's a feature of crisis periods. Need benchmarks and controls to isolate signal.
- **Swaps and bespoke exposure** [01:44:18–01:45:35]: Total return swaps allow precise exposure design (e.g., variant swaps isolate vol-of-vol without delta hedging drag). Swaps are not inherently tied to low rates; their power is in customization. Expect sophisticated players to use them; this is structural, not conspiratorial.
