# How I'm Beating GameStop Returns with the Covered Strangle

## Summary

This video documents a live covered-strangle position in GameStop scaled to a $50,000 allocation, demonstrating how the strategy outperforms buy-and-hold despite the underlying declining 26% year-to-date. The host explains the mechanics of capturing variance risk premium and squeeze potential through a combination of long equity, short puts, and short ratio calls, achieving ~10% total PNL while the stock trades sideways in a consolidation zone.

## Key takeaways

- **Position structure** [00:00–01:19]: Covered strangle = long equity + short cash-secured puts + short ratio covered calls. Allocation doubled from $25k to $50k to show scaled version of personal portfolio position.

- **GameStop technical setup** [01:19–02:38]: Stock started 2025 at ~$31, hit lows near $27, rallied to mid-$30s, then cratered. Currently consolidating in $18–$22 range with support at $22 and $18. Recent earnings well-received; corporate activity adds volatility.

- **Why covered strangle works in GME** [02:38–03:52]: High implied volatility (IV percentile ~17%, but 70% vol still elevated) + strong variance risk premium (IV > RV most of the time) + squeeze potential. Calls trade rich due to parabolic upside risk, making both put and call legs profitable.

- **Variance risk premium display** [05:10]: Purple line on chart indicates variance risk premium present; red indicates inversion (realized move > implied). GME shows purple (premium available) most of the time over the past year.

- **Squeeze mechanics** [05:10–06:23]: Squeeze potential exists but diminished (inventory available, no longer hard-to-borrow). Liquidity in options chain evaporates during parabolic moves (bid-ask spreads widen dramatically), so long equity preferred over long calls for capturing squeezes.

- **Put-call skew advantage** [06:23–07:51]: Normally puts trade at premium to calls (put skew). In GME, calls trade rich due to parabolic upside, so both put and call legs generate decent returns—unlike typical names where only puts pay well.

- **Current position details** [09:58–11:30]: Long 600 shares at $22.90 basis. Two tranches of short puts/calls at $22 strike, 19 DTE (one rolled from $25 strike). Ratio call position leaves ~200 shares uncovered as hedge against gap risk. Realized PNL +$3,700 (7.41% return on capital); total PNL +9.8% despite underlying down 26%.

- **Options returns breakdown** [11:30–12:42]: Short puts returning ~6–8.5% (annualized ~42–69%); short calls returning ~4.4% over 45 days (annualized ~35%). Strategy monetizes downside while maintaining upside participation through active share trading.

- **Active share management** [12:42–13:55]: Position is not static. Host trades in and out of shares to amplify returns: dollar-cost-averaging on dips, taking profits on rallies (e.g., sold into peak above $34). This active approach generates capital gains tax but compounds gains significantly vs. buy-and-hold.

- **Ongoing monitoring** [14:55]: Covered strangle is base position; unusual options activity monitored separately. Meme stock stream runs every Saturday; GameStop updates slowed due to quiet period but can resume if community interest warrants.
