# Segment Intro and Approach - Small Stacks Pt1

## Summary

Eric introduces the "Small Stacks" segment, a real-money trading series using a $5,000 account to demonstrate how to approach options trading with minimal capital. The primary goals are capital preservation and skill development rather than rapid account growth; the strategy will focus on covered strangles and buy-and-hold positioning, deliberately avoiding active day-trading and intraday speculation.

## Key takeaways

- **$5k as minimum threshold** [00:00] — Eric identifies $5,000 as approximately the absolute minimum viable capital for options trading.
- **Capital preservation over growth** [00:48] — For accounts under $20–25k, the primary objective should be preserving capital and learning, not turning the account into "a bunch of money."
- **Risk management constraints acknowledged** [02:30] — Small accounts will frequently face over-allocation; this is accepted as a structural reality, not a failure.
- **Covered strangles as primary strategy** [01:59] — The account will trade primarily covered strangles; spreads will be used only for instructional/exploratory purposes, not as a core approach.
- **Deliberate buy-and-hold bias** [03:14] — The segment will model a realistic small-account approach: mostly buy-and-hold, not active intraday trading, because active trading on small accounts typically results in losses.
- **Watchlist development next** [03:50] — The follow-up video will build a watchlist of securities suitable for meaningful position sizing on $5k capital.
- **Monthly account updates planned** [04:28] — Updates will follow the same cadence as Eric's personal account reporting.

## Notable quotes

> "Small accounts have a lot of shortcomings that they have to work around; the ability to manage risk is a big one."

> "If you're lucky you'll break even doing [active intraday trading]; more probably than not you'll lose money."
