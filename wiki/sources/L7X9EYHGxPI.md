# How Does a Covered Strangle Work

## Summary

This video explains the mechanics of a covered strangle: buying shares while simultaneously selling an out-of-the-money put and call to generate premium without capping upside profit potential. The strategy distributes the aggregate exposure of a long stock position across three legs, and incorporates trend-following stops and rolling flexibility to manage risk dynamically.

## Key takeaways

- **Core structure** [00:00]: Buy 200 shares, sell one put, sell one call—same aggregate exposure as 300 shares but distributed across three legs.
- **Uncapped upside** [00:00]: Unlike a standard covered call, the ratio component preserves profit potential if the stock rallies significantly.
- **Rolling flexibility** [00:29]: If the short call is breached, you can roll it out and up, or add more size rather than being forced into a tight corner.
- **Trend-following risk management** [00:29]: Apply stops to avoid holding long shares unnecessarily during sharp declines; move in and out of the underlying and short puts based on price action.
- **Dynamic position management** [00:50]: Adjust the underlying and short-put exposure based on market conditions rather than holding static.
