# Market Outlook for the Week Ahead

## Summary

Eric reviews the week's market rotation across major indices (S&P 500, NASDAQ, Russell 2000), noting pocketed strength concentrated in large-cap names rather than broad breadth. The FOMC rate decision on the 18th is flagged as the week's critical catalyst, with market expectations shifting toward a higher probability of a 50 basis point cut. He discusses portfolio positioning, recent trade management (ratio put diagonals in energy), and the transition into the next earnings season.

## Key takeaways

- **Index positioning & breadth divergence** [01:04–02:18]: S&P 500 leading near term highs; NASDAQ at 19,872 (needs ~21,000); Russell at 2,209 (term high 2,320). NYSE showing strength even as IWM and QQQ roll over—indicates strength concentrated in large names, not broad market breadth.
- **NASDAQ technical setup** [02:18–03:33]: QQQ in negative-sloping 3-month standard deviation channel; volume profile point of control at 480–481. Critical decision point: break above = bullish resumption; rejection = lower high, setting up potential lower low (downtrend confirmation).
- **FOMC rate decision (18th) as primary catalyst** [04:44–08:10]: Market now pricing higher probability of 50 bps cut (shift from 50/50 yesterday). A 25 bps cut likely already priced in; 50 bps would signal Fed concern about economic resilience and could trigger sentiment shift despite near-term price support. Powell's methodical, communicative approach suggests caution about re-inflation risk post-cuts.
- **Secondary data releases** [04:44–09:14]: Retail sales (17th), industrial production, housing market index (18th), jobless claims (19th). Volatility expected around FOMC window; jobless claims gaining attention due to recent revisions.
- **Portfolio management & sector shorts** [10:27–12:33]: Closed ratio put diagonal on HAL (energy short) at near-target; still bullish on energy downside but waiting for pullback. XLV, XLF candidates for short deltas; XLE bid up, reducing short opportunity. Not initiating tech shorts. Maintaining core allocation with speculative overlays.
- **Earnings calendar transition** [13:41]: Current stragglers (Riley, GIS, FDX, UAL, CCL); banking earnings begin next month, signaling start of next earnings season. Prepping capital for earnings plays.

## Notable quotes

"I want to have as many factors in my favor as possible. Well, I can tell you one of those factors that's not here right now is all of the indices moving up uniformly." [02:18]

"Our job as traders is to come up with ideas, probabilistic assessments about what we think may or may not happen. But I'm going to wait until I can see some level of confirmation before I start committing large sums of capital." [03:33]
