# NVDA Earnings - Stock Market Update

## Summary

Nvidia reported Q2 earnings with a beat on EPS (delivered $1.05 vs. expected $0.93) and revenue growth of 56% year-over-year, but the stock declined after-hours as data center revenue came in soft relative to expectations. The company announced a major buyback and guided to ~$54B in next-quarter revenue with 73% gross margins. Despite strong fundamentals and dominant positioning in AI infrastructure, the market's reaction highlights the tension between exceptional growth and already-elevated valuations.

## Key takeaways

- **Earnings beat but data center miss** [01:00–01:31]: EPS came in at $1.05 vs. $0.93 expected; revenue up 56% YoY. However, data center revenue underperformed, driving the after-hours decline to ~$176 from prior close.
- **Volatility was not priced well** [01:57–02:31]: Expected move was ~6.2%; realized volatility (76%) nearly matched implied (77%), making the earnings play unattractive on a vol basis.
- **AI infrastructure dominance** [04:12–08:56]: Data center revenue up 56% YoY; Blackwell adoption accelerating across Disney, Foxcon, Hitachi, Hyundai. Nvidia positioned as central to global AI buildout—both hardware and software stack.
- **Agentic AI as next growth driver** [15:14–16:27]: Management emphasized reasoning/agentic AI requiring 100–1000× more compute than single-shot inference; long-term infrastructure opportunity estimated at $3–4 trillion by 2030.
- **China H20 sales uncertain** [19:07–19:45]: Potential $2–5B in H20 revenue this quarter contingent on geopolitical licensing; $180M inventory release to non-China customers provided partial offset.
- **Heavy call premium into earnings** [22:19–23:42]: $478M in call premium vs. $147M put premium on 2-day expiration; significant skew suggesting informed positioning ahead of release.
- **Volatility surface distortion** [33:24–37:40]: Front-term IV (7-day) at 89.5% vs. 60-day at 31%; pronounced kink in term structure creates tradable vol-of-vol opportunities (e.g., calendar spreads).
- **Positioning reflects long bias** [29:43–30:22]: Net long premium even far out in time; large stock holders selling calls into earnings (typical hedge behavior), not pure directional bets.
- **Post-earnings technical setup** [26:40–27:06]: Probability nodes at 171 and 157 suggest support; no-China-sales news already known, so market's near-ATH positioning implies limited downside surprise priced in.
- **Broader AI narrative intact** [16:27–17:46]: Despite near-term volatility, 56% YoY revenue growth and dominant infrastructure position support long-term thesis; smaller-cap AI names offer asymmetric risk/reward if positioned well.
