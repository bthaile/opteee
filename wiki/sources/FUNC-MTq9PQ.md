# Market Bottomed and Headed to Top in 6 Months Before 80% Global Bust with David Hunter

## Summary

David Hunter argues that the market bottomed in late October 2022 and is poised for a significant rally (melt-up) to 6,000+ on the S&P 500 over the next 6 months, driven by sentiment extremes and short covering. However, he maintains his longer-term thesis that the Fed's aggressive rate-hiking campaign has already sown the seeds for a global deflationary bust in the second half of 2023—worse than 2008–09 and accompanied by a financial crisis. The key driver of the bust will be the Fed's failure to understand leads and lags in monetary policy transmission, combined with Powell's adoption of Bill Dudley's thesis that the stock market must fall significantly to tighten financial conditions.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2022-10-24)

- **Recession confirmed; global bust ahead** [01:07]–[02:20]: Two consecutive quarters of negative GDP confirm technical recession; Hunter expects a "global deflationary bust" (worse than 2008–09, concentrated over ~1 year) in H2 2023, accompanied by bank failures and involuntary debt liquidation.

- **Fed policy error and Dudley thesis** [04:43]–[07:51]: The Fed is making "perhaps the largest policy error in history" by over-tightening at unprecedented speed. Powell has adopted Bill Dudley's thesis that the stock market must fall to ~3,000 to tighten financial conditions—a "false narrative" that Hunter believes is driving hawkish rhetoric and market suppression.

- **Market bottom likely in place** [16:24]–[16:39]: Hunter believes the market bottomed last Thursday (Oct 20) at ~3,490, forming a double bottom with the June low. This was a successful retest that scared weak hands out before reversal.

- **Melt-up to 6,000+ expected** [35:49]–[37:02]: The next leg of the secular bull market will run from ~3,500 to 6,000+ over 6 months or less, driven by short covering, institutional repositioning, and FOMO once new highs (4,800+) are decisively broken.

- **Housing market to contract 20–30%** [29:42]–[32:23]: Mortgage rates rising from 2.5% to 7%+ will cause housing prices to fall 20–30% over the next 6–12 months. Pent-up demand from rate dips has delayed the full impact, but affordability collapse will drive the decline.

- **Bond market capitulation** [50:22]–[53:26]: The 10-year yield's push to 4.30% may represent capitulation; Hunter expects yields to roll over and decline between now and year-end, similar to the stock market's reversal after the 3,490 spike.

- **Precious metals pivot point** [54:56]–[57:47]: Gold and silver are at a major pivot after rolling over with dollar strength in March–April. Hunter expects gold to 1,900+ and silver to 22–23 in the short term, with longer-term targets of gold 3,000 and silver 50+. Dollar weakness will be the key catalyst.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Leads and lags in monetary policy** [11:11]–[12:05]: The Fed conducts policy based on backward-looking CPI (already 1+ month old) and inflation that lags 1+ year. This guarantees over-tightening; the damage from rate hikes takes months to manifest, but the Fed doesn't pause to assess.

- **Fed credibility trap** [25:48]–[26:55]: Powell is over-tightening partly to restore credibility after being late to fight inflation in 2021. This personal credibility concern is driving policy error; the focus should be on correct policy, not Fed reputation.

- **Sentiment as a contrarian indicator** [39:43]–[40:31]: Sentiment is at 2008–09 / 1982 / 1987 / 2003 bear-market lows. Extreme bearishness indicates most bad news is already priced in; when sentiment reverses, the firepower to rally is enormous.

- **Psychology and momentum** [41:19]–[41:39]: Investors are most bearish at bottoms and most bullish at tops. As the tape (daily market movement) turns positive, psychology shifts; people chase momentum and put money to work, creating self-reinforcing rallies.

- **Institutional forward-looking behavior** [42:30]–[43:43]: Institutions don't buy/sell on today's earnings; they look 6–9 months ahead. Once convinced the Fed's tightening cycle is ending and the economy is troughing, they will buy in anticipation of 2024 earnings recovery—driving the 4,800–6,000+ move.

- **The final blow-off top** [45:15]–[46:51]: At the top (6,000–7,000), investors will be fully bullish and positioned for a "long cycle ahead." The Fed, seeing a speculative market and inflation still above target, will resume tightening—exacerbating the damage already sown and triggering the bust.

- **Dollar strength as a headwind** [57:47]–[58:59]: The dollar has surged from ~95 to ~115 DXY in 2022 due to Fed policy, straining global economies and currencies (e.g., yen). The Fed has ignored this externality; dollar weakness will be necessary for gold/silver and global relief.

## Regime / context

**Date:** October 24, 2022. This is the fourth interview between Eric (Outlier Trading) and David Hunter. The S&P 500 had just tested lows near 3,490 on October 20, 2022, following aggressive Fed rate hikes throughout 2022. The 10-year Treasury yield had recently spiked above 4%, mortgage rates had risen to ~7%, and recession fears were acute. Hunter's thesis—a near-term melt-up followed by a 2023 global bust—was contrarian at the time and reflects his macro cycle framework rather than a short-term trading call. The interview emphasizes the importance of understanding policy lags and long-term cycles over minute-to-minute market watching.
