# Trading in IRAs

## Summary

Eric outlines a practical framework for trading within Individual Retirement Accounts (IRAs), emphasizing Roth IRA contribution limits and income thresholds, and explaining the regulatory constraints that force conservative position structures. He demonstrates that covered strangles—his primary IRA strategy—have historically delivered ~15% annualized returns while respecting the account's margin restrictions.

## Key takeaways

- **Roth IRA contribution urgency** [00:27–01:27]: Max out Roth contributions as early as possible; once income exceeds $124k (single) or $196k (jointly), you lose contribution eligibility permanently and can only trade what's already in the account.
- **Combined IRA contribution cap** [02:08–02:30]: Total annual contributions across all IRA types (Roth + Traditional) are capped at $6,000; don't max both simultaneously or you'll exceed the limit.
- **Futures loophole for leverage** [03:03–03:50]: Options on stocks cannot be margined in IRAs, but futures and options on futures can be traded; futures require 125% of standard margin, forcing larger cash reserves than typical margin accounts.
- **Covered strangles as core strategy** [03:50–04:13]: Eric's primary IRA approach is covered strangles, which have averaged ~15.2% annualized returns (2007–2019)—modestly above market but below his margin-account performance.
- **Cash-secured positioning philosophy** [04:40–05:36]: Keep IRA trading simple and slow; use cash-secured positions only; reserve margin accounts for active trading; the IRA structure naturally enforces discipline.

## Notable quotes

> "Once you hit those thresholds and you move over, you cannot contribute to the account anymore—so what happens is you can get stuck with a certain amount of money in that account and you're going to essentially completely rely on your ability to develop the account for the accumulation of wealth in there."
