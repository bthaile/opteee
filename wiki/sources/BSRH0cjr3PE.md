# GME Options Chain Getting Active

## Summary

GME broke above the 26.2 point of control, closing at 27 (+3.65%), as the broader market digests post-weekend volatility and prices in political uncertainty. The options chain shows significant activity: 40,000+ contracts traded in the 19 July 30 calls, with a notable 7,800-contract block in the 27.5 calls (only 2,200 OI prior), suggesting deliberate position-building throughout the day. Implied volatility remains skewed toward puts, but emerging call-side anomalies and multi-expiration block trades signal growing conviction in upside scenarios.

## Key takeaways

- **Market context**: S&P 500 up 0.4%, Russell 2000 rallying ~3% over four trading days (from 20,482 to 22,190) on expectations of policy shifts; small caps benefiting from potential rate environment changes [00:29–01:35]
- **GME price action**: Broke above 26.2 support/control level, closed 27 (+3.65%); 30-day chart shows floor raising and moving averages aligning; caution advised on standard technical analysis given GME's non-normal behavior [02:18–03:26]
- **19 July 30 calls**: 40,000+ volume transacted; time-and-sales analysis shows mixed buy/sell ratio, predominantly small blocks (10–20 contracts), indicating broad retail/institutional participation rather than single whale [04:52–07:41]
- **Call/put skew**: 254,000 call volume vs. 58,000 put volume (massive call skew); however, implied volatility still heavily favors puts (far OTM puts at 550%+ IV), not yet pricing in sustained upside [07:41–08:56]
- **19 July 27.5 calls block trade**: ~$327,000 block (3,551 contracts) at open (06:39 PT), followed by 99 contracts at 08:08 and 101 at 12:24—phased position-building throughout day; only 2,200 OI prior, suggesting 4,000–5,000 contracts opened [11:01–13:09]
- **27.5 strike rationale**: Illiquid (7-point wide bid-ask vs. 5-point at round strikes), yet deliberately chosen; suggests conviction and willingness to sacrifice liquidity for specific strike targeting [13:09–13:32]
- **Exercise speculation**: 27.5 calls are all extrinsic value; exercising would forfeit $400,000–$500,000 in premium; unlikely unless intent is to drive demand, though calculus remains speculative [13:57–14:52]
- **Multi-expiration activity**: 16 Aug 40 calls ($378,000 block), 29 Aug 29 calls ($343,000), 26 Sept 26 calls ($232,000)—size rolling into multiple expirations, relatively new severity [16:42–17:10]
- **Open interest**: 85,000 total calls vs. 480,000 puts; gap narrowing (contraction phase); 29% of put volume at bid or below (sellers), 26% at offer or above; 70%+ of call volume at offer or above (buyers opening) [10:10–10:41]
- **Next catalyst**: Live stream Saturday 5 PM PT; Newton tracking August cycle as potential tailwind [02:39–03:03]
