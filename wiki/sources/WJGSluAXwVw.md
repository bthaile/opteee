# Analyzing Roaring Kitty's Trades Pt8 | GameStop Analysis Live Ep16

## Summary

Eric examines Roaring Kitty's (Keith Gill) options-based entry and subsequent unwind of his GameStop position in June 2024, focusing on whether he exercised his 21 June $20 calls or sold them to the market. Through detailed cost-basis and extrinsic-value analysis, Eric concludes Gill likely sold the calls rather than exercised them, preserving ~$920k in time value. The session also covers GameStop's current range-bound trading, collapsing volatility, and the systemic market-structure issues the saga has exposed.

## Key takeaways

- **Roaring Kitty's risk tolerance is exceptional**: Multiple 50%+ drawdowns while managing a portfolio that swung from $1.5M to $2.5M to $1.3M demonstrates willingness to endure volatility most traders cannot sustain [07:28–08:55]
- **He started with options, not stock**: Contrary to community assumption, Gill's earliest YOLO update was portfolio-heavy in options, not equities [05:17–05:46]
- **No evidence of "supreme timing"**: Eric finds no clear indication of a secret timing mechanism; Gill appears to trade on generally accessible information, making his edge primarily his risk tolerance and position-sizing discipline [06:08–07:00]
- **GameStop volatility has collapsed**: 30-day IV down from ~30% to single digits; 90-day average true range halved from 5.29% to 2.88%; options open interest now concentrated in Jan 2025 rather than front expirations [29:14–31:07]
- **The June 2024 unwind: likely a sale, not exercise**: Cost-basis math ($26.09 residual basis) and extrinsic-value preservation (~$920k foregone if exercised) suggest Gill sold his 21 June $20 calls rather than exercising them [57:05–01:16:47]
- **Systemic market structure remains broken**: Payment-for-order-flow, market-maker conflicts, and the fact that a single stock could threaten financial system collapse expose deep regulatory failures [10:27–10:54]
- **Community fatigue is real**: The GME community has quieted as the stock has ranged; many retail participants are "traumatized and tired" from false alarms and the 2021 buy-button shutdown [11:20–12:22]
