# Portfolio Update - Small Stacks Pt6

## Summary

Eric reviews the Small Stacks portfolio (part 6), highlighting a new long call diagonal position in Pfizer established despite near-term technical weakness, with the intent to scale in as capital frees up. He discusses active positions in XLF, Zynga, Kroger, and Pfizer, emphasizing capital efficiency, low correlation across holdings for diversification, and a disciplined approach to avoiding aggressive trading during market capitulation.

## Key takeaways

- **Pfizer entry rationale** [00:27–01:36]: Established a 1:1 call diagonal (long 28 call July 2021 / short 36 call Oct 2021) despite price approaching −2 standard deviation; willing to accept near-term downside (~$3 range) because longer-term outlook is positive and capital can be deployed to average basis once XLE short puts close.
- **Technical caution with conviction** [01:36–02:02]: Nine-day MA closing below 50-day and 200-day MA is typically bearish, but Eric maintains longer-term perspective and accepts near-term chop.
- **XLF and Zynga management** [02:28–03:24]: Both positions expiring early October; XLF near basis with no adjustment needed; Zynga just below $8.50 basis, allowing extrinsic value bleed before reassessment.
- **Kroger tactical adjustment** [03:24–04:14]: Short 36.5 call (Oct 9); considering closing before earnings for small profit, then re-establishing closer to current basis to capitalize on earnings volatility while keeping upside uncapped.
- **Pfizer trade structure** [04:40–06:00]: 1:1 ratio diagonal yields ~16% return on invested capital ($850 deployed, $138 collected); ratio structure caps upside but necessary for small account capital efficiency; plan to scale into additional LEAPS once capital frees up.
- **Portfolio correlation and diversification** [06:49–07:14]: Deliberately low correlation across holdings (Pfizer, XLF, Zynga, Kroger) to provide inherent diversification; account utilization at ~98.84%.
- **Capital discipline** [07:48–08:06]: Willing to take assignment on XLF or Zynga and hold long-term rather than aggressively trade into losses; avoiding reactive trading during market capitulation.

## Notable quotes

- "I view these accounts as puzzles—you have to have the right pieces that fit together to make sure that you aren't putting this account in a bad spot." [06:26]
