# Trading Earnings — Avoid These Two Common Mistakes #optionstrading

## Summary

This video identifies two critical mistakes traders make when selling options around earnings: (1) misidentifying the actual edge being traded (implied volatility vs. realized volatility, not theta collection), and (2) abandoning a tested approach prematurely due to adverse paths or drawdowns. The host emphasizes the importance of maintaining a rigorous trade log and building confidence through adequate sample size before modifying strategy.

## Key takeaways

- **Mistake #1: Confusing theta with the actual edge** [00:00] — Selling options through earnings collects theta, but the real effect being traded is implied volatility versus realized volatility. If you're not tracking this distinction in your trade log, you're not tracking what you're actually trading.
- **Mistake #2: Abandoning strategy due to bad paths** [00:20] — Earnings seasons can produce adverse outcomes; if you lack confidence built through testing and live experience, you'll prematurely change your approach, breaking your sample size and hurting long-term results.
- **Sample size is the foundation** [00:41] — The entire edge depends on accumulating enough trades. Changing things prematurely destroys the statistical foundation of your strategy.
