# Term Volatility

## Summary

Term volatility refers to the implied volatility specific to each expiration cycle. By examining the volatility term structure—how IV varies across different expirations—traders can identify relative value opportunities and trade cheap expirations against expensive ones, particularly when earnings or other events create skew.

## Key takeaways

- **Each expiration has its own IV** [00:00] — term volatility is the IV level unique to a given expiration cycle; plotting or reviewing these numbers reveals structural patterns.
- **Term structure shows relative value** [00:22] — when one expiration (e.g., 2M) trades at 80 IV while others sit in the 40s, that disparity signals opportunity, often driven by earnings.
- **Trade relative volatility across terms** [00:40] — compare cheap expirations to expensive ones; the information embedded in term volatility can be exploited for relative-value trades.
