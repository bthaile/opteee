# Quiet Market & GME Overview

## Summary

Market showed follow-through buying on 2024-08-13 with large-cap indices (SPY, QQQ) recovering most losses from the prior two days, but small-cap (IWM) lagging significantly. Despite near-100% probability of a Fed rate cut priced in, the market's parabolic recovery appears disconnected from the improving rate-cut expectations. GME consolidating sideways below point of control with elevated call open interest (700k calls vs 341k puts) ahead of September earnings.

## Key takeaways

- **Large-cap vs. small-cap divergence** [00:21–01:03]: SPY and QQQ have recovered ~90% of losses from Aug 1–2; IWM still down ~50% from those levels, signaling uneven breadth.
- **Net highs/lows trending negative** [03:06]: Despite green day, net advance/decline is negative, indicating gains concentrated in handful of names rather than broad participation.
- **Fed rate-cut expectations at 100%** [01:27–02:06]: FED watch tool shows quarter-point cut certain, half-point cut possible; yet market's parabolic move predates this clarity, raising timing questions.
- **Home Depot earnings signal consumer pause** [02:26–02:48]: HD beat EPS (467 vs. 454 expected) but commentary flagged "consumer paused in July"—soft signal worth monitoring.
- **GME consolidating, waiting for catalyst** [03:31–04:19]: Stock holding sideways, below point of control; healthy order flow but no directional conviction yet.
- **GME options: 16 Aug bucket dominates** [04:19–05:21]: 29k of 696k total call OI concentrated in Aug 16 expiration; Sept 20 (post-earnings Sept 4) showing minimal buildup so far; IV still low relative to event risk.
- **Covered-call activity detected** [05:47]: 349 Aug 16 23-calls sold, likely covered-call positioning; size notable but not extreme.
