# AMD, GME Warrants, Government Shutdown - Stock Market Update

## Summary

Market hits all-time highs (S&P ~6750) despite ongoing government shutdown, which historically shows minimal directional impact. AMD surges ~$60 on OpenAI news, reflecting profound AI transformation thesis. GME warrants launch with volatile first-day trading (opened ~$2.25, peaked ~$6), behaving as ~32-strike calls with ~45 delta; host emphasizes warrant decay and leverage mechanics to retail traders.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2025-10-09)

- **Government shutdown context** [05:03–08:19]: Day 7 of shutdown; historically coin-flip directional outcome, slight upward drift tendency. Longest prior shutdown was 35 days (2018). FOMC cut probability dropped from 99.4% to 94.7% for 25 bps—meaningful shift. Shutdown may suppress inflation data (CPI, PPI, jobless claims), complicating Fed decision-making.
- **AMD transformational move** [16:01–19:36]: Gap up from ~$168 to ~$230 on OpenAI partnership news. Host maintains 3–5 year conviction that AI-exposed names will be worth more; near-term hold/pullback uncertain. Tech and financials remain top-performing sectors into earnings season.
- **Pfizer reversion trade** [12:12–15:04]: Post-tariff-deal consolidation; host framed as rubber-band stretch likely to normalize, not revert to pre-catalyst lows. Pfizer down ~50% over 3 years from ~$54 peak; margin pressure from price concessions.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Warrant behavior & decay** [25:08–53:12]: GME warrants (GMEWS) function as ~32-strike calls expiring Oct 2026; currently ~45 delta, all extrinsic value. Decay mirrors Oct-26 32-strike options (~55% IV). As underlying rises, delta increases (gamma effect); intrinsic value only accrues above $32 strike. Leverage play: 10 warrants at ~$2.50 = $25 capital vs. 1 share at ~$25.
- **Options adjustment mechanics** [41:00–42:15]: Pre-warrant options chains auto-adjusted to include warrant deliverable; post-offering chains do not. As expirations roll off, warrant-adjusted chains expire; new weeklies have no warrant component.
- **Volatility & liquidity** [26:49–31:26]: GMEWS opened with 185k volume (light), rallied to ~6 intraday on algo activity. Bid-ask penny-wide despite first-day launch. Warrant-inclusive Oct-26 chain shows 57.54% IV vs. 57.88% standard chain—minimal premium yet. As warrants approach ATM, expect IV premium in warrant-adjusted chains.
- **Trading earnings** [19:36–24:06]: Successful earnings trading requires complete prep work, not guessing. Gap between perceived approach and actual execution is where most retail traders fail. Professional discipline (exit plans, position sizing) non-negotiable.
- **Volatility trading during shutdown** [11:19–12:12]: Intraday vol strategies (2–3 day expirations) outperform longer-dated index vol trades in current regime. Fat risk premiums in near-term justify focus there.
