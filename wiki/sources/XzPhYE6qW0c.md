# Building Companies, Not Running Them Tom Sosnoff | The Outlier Podcast

## Summary

Tom Sosnoff reflects on the sale of Tasty Works and his philosophy of building companies versus running them operationally. He discusses the decision to exit after decades in the options trading space, the appeal of serial entrepreneurship, and the importance of team continuity across ventures. The conversation centers on legacy, capital efficiency, and the distinction between creation and management.

## Key takeaways

- **Exit timing and optionality** [00:00–00:20]: When Tasty Works attracted five acquisition offers (cash and stock combinations), Sosnoff recognized the value of taking a win after 40+ years in the space rather than running the company indefinitely.
- **Building vs. running** [00:45]: Sosnoff and his core team (Christy, Woody, Scott) prefer building new products and companies over managing existing operations for others—a fundamental distinction in their entrepreneurial approach.
- **Team continuity as competitive edge** [00:20–00:45]: The same core team built both Thinkorswim and Tasty Works, suggesting that stable, long-term collaboration is a key ingredient in serial success.
- **Legacy and multiple wins** [00:20]: Creating multiple successful ventures (unicorns) and establishing a strong legacy is valued alongside the opportunity to attempt new challenges.

## Notable quotes

> "When you've been doing something in the same space for 40 some odd years, it's really cool to take it to the very end but it's also really cool to have to put a winner on the board."

> "We all love building stuff. Running stuff for other people. Not our thing."
