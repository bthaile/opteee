# Avoid Back Testing Pitfalls

## Summary

Backtesting is essential for long-term trading success, but most traders fail to recognize its inherent pitfalls. The session covers critical statistical and cognitive biases—particularly availability bias and desired-outcome bias—that poison backtesting results and lead to false edges.

## Key takeaways

- **Statistical rigor is non-negotiable** [00:00] — Most traders do not apply basic statistical approaches to backtesting, undermining the validity of their results.
- **Availability bias erodes edge** [00:24] — Information that is easy to access is widely known; easy-to-find edges are typically weaker than traders assume.
- **Desired-outcome bias poisons the entire process** [00:46] — Entering backtesting with a predetermined conclusion corrupts decision-making throughout the analysis.
- **Recognize cognitive biases in your workflow** [00:24] — Awareness of how biases distort backtesting is the first step to avoiding them.

## Notable quotes

> "Back testing is literally riddled with pitfalls."

> "If the information is easy to get, it means it's widely gotten."
