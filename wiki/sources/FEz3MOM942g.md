# Do High Win Rate Option Strategies Work?

## Summary

High win rate does not guarantee profitability in options trading. A system with 90% win rate but small average wins (~$20) and large average losses (~$200) will lose money overall. The market is competitive and will not offer pure edge through high probability of profit alone; context and expected value matter far more than win rate.

## Key takeaways

- **Win rate ≠ profitability** [00:00] — A 90% win rate with $20 average wins and $200 average losses results in net losses despite high frequency of winning trades.
- **Expected value is the metric** [00:00–00:29] — Systems must be evaluated on expected return, not probability of profit; the market prices in high-probability outcomes.
- **Market competitiveness prevents free edges** [00:52] — If high probability of profit were a pure edge, counterparties would not take the other side; the market is efficient enough to price away naive high-win-rate strategies.
- **Context is critical** [00:29] — High probability of profit is contextual; it depends on position sizing, risk-reward ratios, and the underlying market structure.
