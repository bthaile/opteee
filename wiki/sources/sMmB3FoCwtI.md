# GME Call Volatility Explained

## Summary

An in-depth analysis of GameStop's extreme implied volatility expansion on June 19–20, 2024, examining the volatility smile, skew dynamics, and order-flow patterns across the options chain. The host walks through why far out-of-the-money options are priced so rich relative to their intrinsic value, and addresses viewer questions about market-maker hedging behavior ahead of near-term expirations.

## Key takeaways

- **Massive volatility expansion across all expirations** [00:42–01:08]: Front-month (20 June) IV jumped from ~207% to 247%; 30-day IV rose from 161% to 189%. Volatility expansion extends far out the curve, indicating broad repricing of tail risk.

- **Far OTM options are priced extremely rich** [02:38–04:15]: 125-strike calls trading at >900% IV and 1,700% IV on far OTM puts. This reflects market-maker risk assessment and fat-tails pricing, not directional conviction. On a relative basis, these penny options are "mega rich" in implied volatility.

- **Volatility smile shows put-side skew** [04:52–05:36]: Put side is now richer than call side, a shift from earlier in the week when calls dominated. This suggests market repricing of downside risk relative to upside potential, though GME's historical behavior still permits significant upside moves.

- **Bullish order-flow skew despite high volatility** [06:13–06:32]: 39% of calls entered at offer or above (bullish); 33% of puts entered at bid (selling puts, also bullish). Traders appear to be exploiting elevated volatility rather than expressing directional fear.

- **Market-maker hedging capacity ahead of expiration** [07:37–08:16]: 20-strike and 25-strike calls already at 93–100 delta. Market makers likely fully hedged or have sufficient liquidity to cover positions into expiration; no expectation of dramatic after-hours moves if stock closes above strike.

- **No definitive explanation for volatility spike** [01:33–01:48]: Possible drivers include FTDs, ETF surrogate shorting, or other mechanisms, but the host emphasizes sticking to observable facts rather than speculation.
