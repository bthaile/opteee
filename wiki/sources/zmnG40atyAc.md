# Running A Covered Strangle On IBIT During a Bear Market #optionstrading

## Summary

Eric demonstrates a covered strangle position on IBIT (Bitcoin spot ETF) during a bear market, showing how to rotate from long equity with short calls into short puts while managing basis. The strategy illustrates how options allow traders to adjust exposure and basis without necessarily adding capital or risk.

## Key takeaways

- **Bear market positioning on IBIT** [00:00]: Started with long equity + ratio short calls, then flattened the position at a loss and rotated into short puts.
- **Mobile basis via options** [00:32]: Unlike equity positions where basis is fixed unless capital is added/removed, options allow traders to adjust exposure and basis dynamically without necessarily increasing risk.
- **Rolling short puts** [00:00]: Continuously rolling the short put position to manage duration and adjust strike selection as market conditions evolve.
- **Ratio call structure** [00:00]: Using ratio short calls (more calls than shares) to generate premium while maintaining long equity exposure.
