# Trading Bitcoin with Options

## Summary

Eric introduces a covered-strangle strategy applied to Bitcoin via the [[ibit]] spot ETF, combining a long equity base position with short calls and cash-secured puts to optimize entry pricing and capture volatility while maintaining a bullish directional bias. The strategy treats Bitcoin as a core portfolio holding and uses options to dollar-cost-average into the position and generate [[theta]] income across a $125k allocation.

## Key takeaways

- **Covered-strangle structure** [00:00–02:18]: Base position is long [[ibit]] shares; two optional components are [[ratio-write|ratio short calls]] and [[short-put|cash-secured puts]] that add [[volatility]] and [[theta]] exposure while enabling variable entry points via rolling.
- **Portfolio-first framing** [02:18–03:30]: The entire strategy assumes you want to hold Bitcoin long-term; options are optimization layers, not the primary profit mechanism. Allocation is $125k total; current utilization is $80k.
- **Bitcoin–equity correlation play** [03:30–04:52]: Bitcoin futures and S&P 500 futures show variable correlation (90-day strong, intra-day can invert), making [[ibit]] a portfolio diversifier that doesn't fully hedge but adds different composition to equity moves.
- **Volatility capture via options** [04:52–06:02]: [[ibit]] implied volatility is ~63% (high); options provide access to [[volatility]] trading independent of directional price movement, unlike futures.
- **Why [[ibit]] over mining stocks** [06:02–07:23]: [[ibit]] holds Bitcoin directly (plus minimal cash/USD), avoiding exposure to mining operations, electricity costs, and facility overhead; cleaner directional exposure.
- **Live position tracker** [07:23–09:45]: 1,000 shares at $54.55 basis; two tranches of short puts (52 strike, 49 strike, Feb expiry); six short calls at 60 strike (Feb). Despite ~4.2% underlying decline, position shows $20.8k unrealized P&L via [[scaling-in]] and option credits ($1k from puts, $1.1k from calls).

## Notable quotes
