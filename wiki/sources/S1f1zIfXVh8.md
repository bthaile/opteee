# GME Livestream with @RichardNewton - GameStop Analysis Ep3

## Summary

Eric and Richard Newton conduct a deep-dive analysis of GameStop's recent price action, focusing on max-pain mechanics, ETF settlement cycles, and the structural factors driving periodic volatility spikes. They examine the June 21 options expiration and its connection to subsequent ETF creation/redemption flows, mapping a potential August 8 settlement event. The discussion covers the historical context of the 2021 squeeze, Roaring Kitty's recent position disclosure, and the regulatory landscape surrounding short selling and market manipulation.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2024-07-21)

- **Max-pain mechanics and price control** [04:24–07:28]: Richard argues that while market makers can move price 3 cents easily, larger moves (e.g., $3–5) require specific market conditions. The June 21 opex saw broad weakness and slowing options chains, creating conditions for downward pressure. However, incoming order flow constrains their ability to suppress price significantly.
- **Recent price action (July 17–21)** [24:37]: Eric flattened options positions on July 17 after a ~20% move in GameStop; he retains core "participation shares" for solidarity with the movement but actively trades derivatives for profit.
- **Correlation breakdown** [11:27–12:13]: 3-day correlation to SPY is 98%, but 30-day is negative 32%. GameStop shows higher correlation to IWM (Russell 2000) than SPY, though historical negative beta has shifted; current fast beta is ~2.11.
- **ETF settlement cycle hypothesis for August 8** [50:16–53:14]: June 21 opex saw large ETF creation/redemption and fails to deliver. Six trading days forward + 35 calendar days points to August 8 as a potential settlement event, consistent with historical patterns (May 3 → June 6, April 19 → June 6).
- **Quarterly capital-gains washing** [01:03:15–01:07:51]: Vanguard, State Street, and BlackRock conduct massive creation/redemption cycles on March, June, September, and December opex dates. These cycles create demand for underlying shares (including GameStop) and can be followed by settlement-driven price moves 35 days later.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Roaring Kitty's position and narrative** [37:10–38:40]: Roaring Kitty disclosed a large position and held through volatility; his live streams correlate with stock weakness, proving he is not pump-and-dumping. His actions may be building a record of market dynamics rather than pursuing explicit manipulation charges.
- **Squeeze mechanics and MOASS thesis** [25:54–31:45]: Thomas Peterffy's 2021 CNBC interview highlighted that more calls were in-the-money than shares existed, which would have forced market makers to deliver shares and potentially collapsed the system. The buy-button shutdown prevented this. Subsequent rule changes increased margin requirements, allowing the system to absorb larger moves without systemic risk. Volkswagen's 2008 squeeze (reaching thousands per share) is cited as a historical precedent.
- **Relative market-cap calculation** [34:24–35:42]: If GameStop briefly became the largest company by market cap (like Volkswagen in 2008), with current top-cap companies at ~$3.5 trillion and GameStop's ~700 million shares outstanding, the math suggests ~$5,000–$8,000 per share at peak. However, this would immediately face short selling, puts, and liquidation cascades.
- **ETF flows and settlement mechanics** [08:34–09:09]: Market makers can route buys off-exchange and use ETF creation/redemption to manage short positions. If they internalize buys and delay settlement via ETF cycles, they can later cover at better prices. The Evans paper documents that ETFs are often more liquid than their underlying securities, forcing delays in settlement.
- **Seasonal patterns in settlement cycles** [54:08–01:02:08]: August hits ~50% of the time, June ~67%, July rarely. September and February are also common. The pattern correlates with quarterly capital-gains washing on opex dates, followed by 35-day settlement windows.
- **Earnings and cycle timing** [01:00:34–01:02:08]: GameStop earnings expected September 4. If a September opex cycle occurs, settlement would land near earnings day, potentially creating a "rocket" move the day after earnings (historically GameStop tanks on earnings calls, then reverses).
- **Hardware and console-cycle thesis** [15:30–20:49]: Hardware sales have grown from 15% to 57% of GameStop's revenue. Nintendo Switch 2 is expected in 2025, PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X successors in 2027. Roaring Kitty's original thesis was that console cycles drive cash-flow positivity, allowing debt repayment. GameStop repaid senior notes in 2020–2021 using hardware-cycle cash.

## Regime / context

**Date:** July 21, 2024. GameStop had recently moved from ~$17 to ~$29 (a ~70% move) in early July, then pulled back to ~$24 by the livestream date. Broader market had sold off mid-week (July 17–19), with VIX spiking to 47 and 67.

**Part 3 of GME-saga series.** Previous analysis covered the 2021 squeeze mechanics and Roaring Kitty's position disclosure (June 2024). This episode focuses on settlement cycles and the August 8 hypothesis. Subsequent episodes will track whether the predicted cycle materializes and how earnings (expected September 4) interact with September opex.

**Key regulatory context:** Rule changes post-2021 increased margin requirements for market participants, reducing systemic risk but also allowing larger price moves without triggering circuit breakers. The SEC and FINRA have not pursued explicit manipulation charges against retail traders or Roaring Kitty, though congressional attention (John Stewart) and public scrutiny remain high.
