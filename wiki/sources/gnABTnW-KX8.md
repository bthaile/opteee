# Election Impact on Markets and AI - Tom Basso | TOP

## Summary

Tom Basso discusses post-election market positioning and volatility management across a diversified 38-market portfolio using algorithmic position-sizing. He outlines his trend-following approach to trading in the present moment rather than forecasting, explores Trump administration policy implications (tariffs, reshoring, energy, education), and cautions traders against AI over-optimization of historical data while acknowledging its potential for efficiency gains across the economy.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2024-11-20)

- **Post-election volatility and positioning** [00:02–02:39]: Extreme pre-election volatility forced position-sizing algorithm adjustments; post-election rally broke sideways consolidation patterns. Out of ~38 markets, only 7 showing upside moves; short-term reversal strategies already shifting downside on equities while longer-term strategies remain mixed.
- **Commodity strength** [02:39–03:54]: Cocoa and coffee rallying; grains (corn, soybeans, wheat) net short and profitable on downside. Portfolio risk aggregation required peeling off contracts to stay within tolerance limits.
- **Bond market sideways** [31:09–32:15]: Treasury and ultra-bond positions flat for ~1 month around election; potential rally could unlock real estate and M&A activity if rates drop and economic optimism increases.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Trading in the now vs. forecasting** [05:17–08:06]: Basso uses Bayesian/snapshot approach—analyzing 38 markets across 7 indicator strategies to rate direction *at this moment*, then sizing positions accordingly. No prediction of future; instead, continuous re-evaluation each day. Large runners (multi-month trends) pay the freight; small whipsaws are losses to minimize.
- **Position-sizing discipline** [00:02–01:20]: Algorithms measure volatility and risk as % of equity; when portfolio risk exceeds tolerance, peel off one contract across all positions to rebalance. Prevents catastrophic drawdowns and enforces emotional discipline.
- **Diversification across uncorrelated markets** [15:07–17:32]: Adding markets like iron ore (Singapore, non-correlated, time-zone diversification) and Canola (Canada) to hedge currency and geographic risk. Careful vetting before adding; each new market requires ~1–2 weeks of spec work and integration.
- **Trend-following robustness** [01:08:07–01:09:27]: Long-term 21-day indicators catch big moves reliably; short-term indicators flip faster. Ignoring noise and over-optimization allows consistent capture of quarterly/semi-annual runners that pay all freight.

- **Policy-driven market themes** [10:16–12:34]: Trump's pro-oil stance, tariff negotiations, and 15% corporate tax for domestic production create speculative opportunities (e.g., crude oil, energy). Basso trades crude, natural gas, and UNL gas; avoids Brent (currency complexity, data fees, integration overhead).
- **Tariffs and reshoring economics** [20:02–21:21]: Lower US corporate tax (15% domestic vs. 21% current) + tariff pressure incentivizes multinational factories in US (closer to market, skilled labor, automation, legal certainty). Capital inflow and supply-chain localization are anti-inflationary long-term (6–12 months+), though short-term tariff pass-through may inflate.
- **Fed policy risk** [23:36–24:58]: Pre-election 50 bps cut seen as excessive; potential inflation re-acceleration in January could force Fed to overtighten, risking recession. Trump's proposals (no tax on tips/overtime, tariffs, energy production) could offset if executed, but timing and sequencing matter.
- **Recession probability (24 months)** [26:18–27:31]: Low-to-moderate if Trump's pro-growth policies execute; higher if Fed over-responds to inflation spike. Basso leans cautiously optimistic on cabinet picks and detailed proposals.

- **Real estate outlook** [31:09–35:42]: Currently frozen by high rates; potential thaw if rates drop and deal-flow increases (anti-monopoly deregulation). California exodus to Texas/Arizona/Denver creates supply/demand imbalance; expect softness in CA, strength in Sun Belt.
- **Department of Education elimination** [46:31–50:12]: Federal DoE (~$80B/year) has not improved student outcomes; direct state funding (bypassing federal bureaucracy) would increase per-pupil dollars and local accountability. School choice and homeschooling expansion likely if funding follows student.
- **AI in early stage** [57:13–01:02:05]: Current AI = "fancy Google search" + Alexa-like interpretation. Intermediate AI = analyzing vast datasets (e.g., DOGE identifying government waste). Advanced AI = AI designing AI (existential risk if not aligned with human values). Positive near-term: efficiency, manufacturing, economy-wide optimization.

- **AI over-optimization risk for traders** [01:05:42–01:08:07]: Backtesting and AI naturally over-fit to historical data; parameter optimization across 1,000 cases yields case #537 that will never repeat. Traders should use AI for screening, scenario analysis, and data aggregation, not for buy/sell signals. Edge historically came from information scarcity; now drowning in data risks over-analysis paralysis.
