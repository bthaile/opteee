# GME Put Option Activity

## Summary

GameStop's options chain shows a notable shift in put-to-call open-interest ratio (360k puts to 64k calls, approaching 2:1), with unusual activity concentrated in far-dated puts (Jan 2025–Jan 2026) at elevated strikes (115–125). Near-term put buying (9 Aug 22-strike, 5,000 contracts) signals bearish positioning, while longer-dated put selling appears portfolio-hedging rather than speculative.

## Key takeaways

- **Open-interest ratio shift** [00:49]: Put-to-call ratio now ~5.6:1 (360k puts vs 64k calls), approaching the 2:1 threshold historically associated with upside activity reversals.
- **Near-term put accumulation** [04:57]–[05:51]: 5,000 contracts of 9 Aug 22-strike puts bought at ~$0.30; existing open interest was only 363, signaling concentrated bearish intent 11 days out.
- **Far-dated put activity** [07:03]–[07:25]: Significant volume in Jan 2025–Jan 2026 125/120/115-strike puts; small repeated bids (10–15 contracts) suggest portfolio hedging or skew capture rather than speculation.
- **Expiration disparity** [01:15]–[01:40]: Calls concentrated in 16 Aug; puts spread across multiple expirations (9 Aug, 16 Aug, 18 Oct, 17 Jan 2025), indicating different strategic intent.
- **Spread construction** [08:12]–[08:39]: Some far-dated activity appears to be calendar spreads (same strike, same expiration, bid/ask pairs within seconds), not outright directional bets.
- **Margin efficiency** [10:05]–[10:24]: Deep-in-the-money put sales (125/120 strikes) require lower margin than long calls; likely portfolio-offset trades rather than naked speculation.
- **Volume context** [00:24]–[00:49]: Overall GME volume remains anemic (93% sell-side); options activity is the primary signal, not underlying price action (down ~2.2%).

## Notable quotes

> "When you see instances where the current open interest is so low and the volume is so high, somebody's interested in this at this level for a very specific reason." [05:24]
