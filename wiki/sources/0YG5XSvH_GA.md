# An Important Pitfall To Avoid In Technical Analysis #optionstrading

## Summary

This video addresses a critical cognitive bias in technical analysis: the tendency to selectively cherry-pick indicators that confirm your existing thesis while ignoring contradictory signals. Eric emphasizes the importance of objectively evaluating all indicators in conjunction and aligning your timeframe analysis with your intended holding period, rather than allowing confirmation bias to distort your market read.

## Key takeaways

- **Confirmation bias in indicator selection** [00:00–00:22]: When you want to be long, your mind naturally gravitates toward supporting evidence (upward-sloping long-term moving averages, positive standard-deviation channels, price above volume nodes) while filtering out contradictory signals.
- **Objective multi-indicator synthesis** [00:22–00:47]: You must carefully and objectively examine all indicators together and summarize what the breadth of evidence says collectively, rather than cherry-picking favorable ones.
- **Timeframe alignment matters** [00:22–00:47]: If you're trading a 5-day price action in the S&P, the long-term moving average slope is irrelevant; you need to check shorter-term moving averages instead. Misalignment between your analysis timeframe and your trade timeframe creates false confirmation.
- **Guard against thesis-supporting bias** [00:47]: You must actively and heavily guard against the propensity to support your idea; this is a foundational discipline in technical analysis.

## Notable quotes
