# How to Manage a Trading Portfolio

## Summary

Eric walks through a systematic framework for portfolio management applicable to both passive and active traders. The core thesis: risk management is the primary job, followed by alignment with quantified objectives, portfolio-level correlation analysis, and individual trade mechanics (stops, profit targets, thesis validation). He splits his portfolio into core (high-probability income strategies like covered strangles and ratio diagonals) and speculative (event-driven, earnings plays, single options) allocations to maintain control and flexibility across market regimes.

## Key takeaways

- **Begin with the end in mind** [01:42] — Define quantified long-term and short-term objectives (e.g., 12% annual return, income generation). Vague goals like "grow the account" lack the fidelity needed to guide position sizing and strategy selection.

- **Risk management is job #1** [03:34] — Profits and return targets are secondary. Your number one responsibility is to manage risk; without capital preservation, there is no capital to compound.

- **Desired risk is dynamic** [04:25] — Assess current standing against year-to-date goals and current market conditions. On 2023-03-13, Eric was ~98% utilized due to SVB bank failures and perceived opportunity; in 2022 downturns, he ran 10–20% utilization. Risk tolerance should flex with both portfolio progress and market regime.

- **Portfolio-level checks precede trade-level checks** [07:25] — Monitor beta-weighted deltas, correlation between positions (e.g., two financials when not bullish on the sector), and alignment with market hypothesis. Avoid unintended concentration.

- **Individual trade management checklist** [08:27] — (1) Risk metrics hit? (mental stops, resistance levels). (2) Profit target reached? (3) Time-sensitive events (expiration, earnings)? (4) Thesis still valid? If the original hypothesis breaks, exit regardless of P&L.

- **Core vs. speculative allocation split** [10:55] — Core (up to 100% of portfolio): covered strangles, ratio diagonals, cash-secured puts—high-probability income strategies targeting the annual return goal. Speculative (up to 40% currently, historically 80%): single options, straddles, earnings plays, event-driven trades around FOMC, SVB-type dislocations.

- **Scaling and sub-allocation within buckets** [14:30] — Within core, a covered strangle on IWM might be 50% of the core bucket, but only 50% of that is deployed at any given time. This layered capital management allows ebb-and-flow as opportunity emerges, enabling outperformance in both bull and bear regimes (e.g., +20% in 2022 when SPX was −19%).

- **Systematic process beats intuition** [16:14] — Document objectives, current tracking, desired risk, portfolio correlation, and market hypothesis. Validate decisions against this framework; without it, portfolio management becomes chaotic and impossible to audit.

## Notable quotes

> "Your number one job as a portfolio manager is manage risk. You cannot make money if there's no money to make money on."

> "Begin with the end in mind—what is the objective of your portfolio? Are you trying to aggressively grow your account or manage income?"
