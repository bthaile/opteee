# Roaring Kitty? Ryan Cohen Buying more eBay? GME Proxy Statement | Ep62

## Summary

Eric investigates a suspected hack of Roaring Kitty's X account (featuring shitcoin promotion), analyzes Ryan Cohen's performance-based option award structure in GameStop's proxy statement, and examines Proposal 5—a request to increase authorized shares from 1 billion to 2.5 billion to enable potential M&A flexibility, likely for an eBay acquisition. The episode covers the mechanics of Cohen's compensation, the dilution implications, and the strategic rationale behind the capital raise.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2026-05-12)

- **Roaring Kitty account hack [04:26–06:40]**: A tweet promoting a shitcoin appeared on the account at ~14:45 PT; the account was subsequently restored to its standard state (last tweet 22 Jan 2025). GME rallied ~10% in after-hours (~23 to ~26) on the fake tweet, then sold off immediately when the hack was identified. This is consistent with a compromised account, not a genuine return.
- **eBay dark pool activity [09:45–15:22]**: Elevated but not extreme dark pool prints on eBay since Cohen's 5% disclosure (1 May). No clear evidence of a single entity building a massive position; likely multiple participants responding to the catalyst.
- **GME options market structure [18:38–20:56]**: Open interest is unusually distributed away from front-month contracts and heavily skewed to calls. This reflects GME's proximity to cash value per share, which limits perceived downside and inflates right-tail skew. The volatility surface is pricing in the possibility of a Roaring Kitty return, even though the hack suggests he is not currently active.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Cohen's performance award [22:06–33:04]**: 71.5 million options with a strike of $20.66, vesting in nine tranches tied to market-cap milestones (first tranche at $20B, subsequent eight at $10B increments) and cumulative EBIT hurdles ($2B first, $10B for the rest). The grant-date fair value is estimated at ~$2.5B. Cohen does not have to exercise; he can sell the options via cashless exercise or broker-assisted settlement once they have intrinsic value. This is standard for large equity awards.
- **Proposal 5: authorized shares increase [24:39–49:12]**: GameStop seeks shareholder approval to increase authorized common shares from 1 billion to 2.5 billion. This is authorization only—no immediate dilution. If fully deployed for an eBay acquisition, it would result in ~1.44 billion new shares, representing ~71.8% dilution to current holders (post-deal ownership ~17.94%). The board frames this as enabling "transformational acquisitions" and maintaining flexibility; it is widely understood as preparation for the eBay deal.
- **Dilution vs. value creation [49:38–01:06:09]**: Dilution is a tool, not inherently negative. Short-term ownership percentage decline is offset by the potential for a much larger combined entity (GME + eBay) generating higher revenue and profitability. The bet is on long-term value creation, not on squeezes. Shareholders must evaluate whether they believe the acquisition thesis; if not, they can reduce or exit their position.
- **Board alignment [01:02:16–01:03:04]**: Board members are wealthy and willing to accept dilution risk because they have significant personal wealth at stake and believe in the combined-entity thesis. They do not draw salaries, only equity compensation, which aligns them with shareholders.
