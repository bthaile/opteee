# Size Positions Taking the SAME Risk Use Vol

## Summary

This video demonstrates how to allocate capital across multiple positions so that they carry equivalent risk exposure despite different underlying volatilities. By weighting position sizes inversely to volatility, traders can ensure that two trades with different instruments behave as equal-sized risk bets from a portfolio perspective.

## Key takeaways

- **Volatility-weighted allocation formula** [00:22]: Allocate capital to each position by multiplying total capital by the other instrument's volatility, then dividing by the sum of both volatilities. This ensures equal risk contribution.
- **Practical example with SPY and Tesla** [00:42]: On a $20,000 allocation, if SPY has lower volatility and Tesla has ~50% volatility, SPY receives $12,500 and Tesla receives $7,500 to equalize risk exposure.
- **Risk parity principle** [00:00]: Rather than equal dollar amounts, equal *risk* allocation requires inverse weighting by volatility—higher-volatility positions get smaller capital allocations.
