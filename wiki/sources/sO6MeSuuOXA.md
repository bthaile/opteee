# Market Decline & GME Update

## Summary

Broad market selloff driven by weak non-farm payroll data (unemployment rising to 4.3% vs. expected 4.1%) and manufacturing weakness, pushing Fed rate-cut expectations to 75% probability for a 50 bp cut in September. SPY down ~10 points from highs but not yet in correction territory; NASDAQ down >10% from peak. GameStop remains in downtrend with mixed volume signals; notable large put purchase (1,600 contracts, ~$552k) on 20 September 22 puts suggests bearish positioning.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2024-08-02)

- **Broad weakness, not panic yet** [01:19–01:51]: Non-farm payrolls "cratered"; unemployment 4.1→4.3%; market pricing 75% probability of 50 bp Fed cut in September (up from 22% day prior). SPX up ~100 pts year-to-date (466 low to 565 high), now at 532—not yet a correction.
- **Tech overbought, sector rotation underway** [06:22–06:47]: NASDAQ moved 395→530 (massive move), now selling off hard; high-flying tech getting "burnt" more in earnings season + broader weakness.
- **Lagging indicators compound uncertainty** [02:20–02:46]: Economic weakness is lagging; even if Fed cuts now, next month's data likely still weak. Market pricing in uncertainty about magnitude and timing of policy response.
- **GME volume split 54% buy / 46% sell** [10:27–10:47]: Positive signal that buyers are interested at current levels; volume node building around $20 and $17.98. Still in downtrend from 17 July peak.
- **Large GME put purchase: 1,600 contracts, ~$552k** [12:09–13:05]: 20 September 22 puts bought at $3.45 each; suggests trader expects further downside (possibly to $18 handle). Puts remain expensive despite stock decline.
- **Small-cap headwind for GME** [12:09]: Russell 2000 / small caps being "hammered"; this structural weakness impacts GME outlook.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Portfolio stress-test** [04:31–05:22]: If current 10% pullback causes anxiety, your position sizing / risk structure is wrong. "Worst day is always ahead of us"—position accordingly, not for comfort.
- **Rotation near highs** [05:41–05:59]: Standard practice: become more cautious as market approaches highs; rotate out of positions; watch closely but don't exit entirely (never know when capitulation starts).
- **Pockets of strength in broad weakness** [09:17–10:03]: Even in selloffs, sectors like XLU show strength. Always opportunity if you look—long, short, or neutral positions available.
- **Waiting for directional confirmation** [15:00–15:20]: For options, wait for price to cross above thresholds *on the way up* rather than buying on the way down; set alerts. Alternative: dollar-cost average on regular schedule.
