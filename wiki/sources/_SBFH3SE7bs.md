# Don't Let High Win Rates Fool You - Learn About Expected Returns

## Summary

A foundational lesson on the critical distinction between win rate (frequency of profitable trades) and expected return (average profit per trade accounting for both wins and losses). The video traces a common trader progression—from directional options to vertical spreads to far-OTM short premium—and explains why high win rates can mask poor expected value, a trap that derails many retail options traders.

## Key takeaways

- **Win rate ≠ expected return** [00:19–00:48]: Traders often conflate how frequently they win with how much money they make on average. High win rates on far-OTM short premium can obscure negative or marginal expected value.
- **The vertical spread trap** [00:00–00:19]: Many traders discover vertical spreads, run them for a while, and realize they win often but don't make much money—prompting a search for the "next logical conclusion."
- **Far-OTM short premium logic** [00:19–00:48]: The intuitive but flawed reasoning: "If I sell options far enough out of the money, I'll win so frequently that infrequent losses won't matter." This ignores the magnitude of those losses.
- **Expected value formula** [00:48]: Expected return = (win probability × average win) + (loss probability × average loss). Both frequency *and* magnitude matter; high frequency alone is insufficient.

## Notable quotes

> "You realize that you might win a lot, but you don't necessarily make a lot of money."

> "People first confuse the idea of win rate, how frequently you win, with your expected return, how much money you make on average over time, including your wins and losses and their corresponding probabilities."
