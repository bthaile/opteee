# 4 Common Mistakes to Avoid AFTER Placing an Options Trade

## Summary

This video identifies four critical post-entry mistakes that undermine otherwise sound options trades: failing to set P&L management rules before entry, over-emphasizing individual trade outcomes at the expense of system feedback, conflating realized P&L with strategy validation, and hyperactive position management that destroys replicability. The core thesis is that trading performance emerges over thousands of trades and decades; each trade is a data point, not a verdict.

## Key takeaways

- **Set P&L management before entry, not after [00:00–02:21]**  
  Define exit conditions tied to your profit mechanism (e.g., close below 10-day MA for trend trades) rather than arbitrary price targets. This removes emotion and ensures consistency.

- **Individual trades are samples, not outcomes [03:34–06:09]**  
  Your long-term performance depends on thousands of trades, not one win or loss. Prioritize accurate feedback on whether your thesis played out, even if the P&L was positive. A profitable trade executed for the wrong reason is a bad data point.

- **Separate realized P&L from strategy validation [06:09–08:46]**  
  A trade can be profitable by accident (e.g., selling puts when IV is high but making money because the underlying rallied, not because volatility compressed). Track thesis vs. realized move in your trading log to detect these false signals.

- **Quantify commission drag relative to average trade P&L [10:11–11:31]**  
  Calculate total commissions as a percentage of average P&L per trade. A trader making $250k over 1,000 trades faces ~25% friction per trade; one making it over 10 trades faces ~1%. This reveals whether you're overtrading.

- **Hyperactive management teaches you nothing [11:31–12:56]**  
  Manually nursing a losing position to breakeven may work once, but it doesn't improve your system. Each trade should inform your strategy; if you're constantly ad-hoc managing, your P&L will remain volatile and your edge will erode.

## Notable quotes

> "Your performance as a trader is going to come down not after an individual trade, 10 trades, 100 trades… it is going to be done over thousands of trades, over hopefully decades."

> "Each individual trade serves as a sample in the data collection of a strategy."
