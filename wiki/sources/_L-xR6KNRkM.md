# Beware Market Paths Can Blow Up Your Account

## Summary

This video examines the critical risk that market *paths*—the sequence of price moves over time—pose to options traders, even when individual moves appear statistically improbable. Using a 6-month standard deviation analysis, the host demonstrates how multiple extreme moves (3.1σ and 3.8σ) can occur within a 60-day window far more frequently than naive probability calculations suggest, with direct implications for iron condor and other defined-risk strategies.

## Key takeaways

- **Standard deviation moves over 6-month windows:** Calculate the joint probability of multiple extreme moves (e.g., 3.1σ and 3.8σ) occurring within a 60-day period [00:00–00:25].
- **Path risk vs. point probability:** A move that should occur once every ~249 years in isolation can happen much more frequently when you account for the *sequence* and *duration* of price action [00:25–00:54].
- **Iron condor vulnerability:** Defined-risk strategies like iron condors are exposed to path blowups; the strategy's theoretical edge can evaporate if the underlying makes multiple extreme moves in quick succession [00:25].
- **Fat-tail reality:** Unexpected extreme moves occur more frequently than standard normal distribution models predict; "if it sounds too good to be true, it probably is" applies to backtested edge [00:54].
