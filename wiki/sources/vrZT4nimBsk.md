# Know Your Greeks: The Non Linearity of Delta #optionstrading

## Summary

This educational segment explores the nonlinear relationship between delta and time to expiration. Using a 670 call as an example, the video demonstrates how delta changes across different expiration dates—ranging from 28 delta at 4 days to expiration up to 59 delta at 459 days—illustrating that delta decay is not uniform as expiration approaches.

## Key takeaways

- **Delta varies nonlinearly with time:** The same strike (670 call) exhibits 28 delta at 4 DTE but 59 delta at 459 DTE—roughly double the delta further out [00:32]
- **Delta decay accelerates near expiration:** As time to expiration decreases, delta changes become more pronounced for the same underlying move [00:32]
- **Time-to-expiration is a critical delta driver:** Understanding how delta shifts across different calendar expirations is essential for position management and strike selection [00:00]
