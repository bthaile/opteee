# Why Tom Sosnoff Left tastylive & What's Coming Next | The Outlier Podcast

## Summary

Tom Sosnoff discusses his departure from tastylive after nearly 15 years and IG Markets' ownership, explaining his serial-entrepreneur mindset and loss of operational control post-acquisition. He announces Lost Dog (launching November 10), a streaming network and AI-powered platform designed to address worker fairness and wealth inequality by helping people understand their true market value through salary analytics, real-time job data, and educational content—applying 40+ years of business-building expertise to a new domain.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2025-10-22)
- Short positions in gold, silver, S&P 500, and Nasdaq; gold and silver finally profitable after extended drawdown [56:07]
- Awaiting broader market selloff to realize gains on equity shorts [56:07]

### Evergreen mechanics
- **Serial entrepreneurship as identity** [00:57–02:22]: Sosnoff has worked for himself for 44 years; only received paychecks twice (TD Ameritrade acquisition, IG Markets acquisition). Loss of control post-acquisition is the primary driver of departure, not financial need.
- **Pattern across ventures** [02:22–05:22]: Left Thinkorswim after 12 years (CEO Fred Tomzack told him "not over my dead body" when asked about running TD); left tastylive after ~15 years despite loving the work, citing the Theo Epstein principle ("after ten years, it was just time").
- **Why sell if you don't need to?** [07:31–08:44]: When five companies bid for tastylive, the team (Sosnoff, Scott, Christy, Woody) decided to "put a winner on the board" and pursue new challenges rather than extract maximum value. Framed as a trade, not a retirement.
- **Blocking out noise and criticism** [21:03–24:56]: 20 years on the trading floor taught Sosnoff to ignore negative feedback; he maintains ~95–98% approval rating and focuses only on people who care about him. Does not read social media criticism.
- **Rapid response culture** [26:06–27:01]: Expects instant email replies (30 seconds to 1 minute); maintains inbox zero; responds to all customers regardless of account status. Views slow response as disqualifying in trading/business context.
- **Lost Dog platform mechanics** [32:00–37:18]: 
  - Streaming show launching November 10 (daily, live + produced)
  - Co-hosted by Sosnoff, Scott, and 30-year friend Saul
  - Core thesis: average person leaves $2–3 million on the table over lifetime due to not knowing their market value (compounded at risk-free rates)
  - AI-powered resume analyzer shows industry salary averages, lifetime wealth impact, and personalized recommendations
  - Real-time job quotes (like stock quotes) showing market movement
  - Subscription model: $100/year
  - Planned ecosystem: stablecoin purchases, tokenized investments, equity stakes for users
- **Addressing CEO pay disparity** [42:45–43:57]: Sosnoff and Scott will take zero compensation from Lost Dog; all profits go to employees and investors. Criticizes CEO multiples (e.g., Citigroup CEO $300M vs. lowest-paid employee $65K) as "complete garbage"; claims he has met only 2 CEOs in his lifetime he would hire out of hundreds.
- **Differentiation from Glassdoor/Zillow** [49:02–50:05]: Lost Dog provides not just salary ranges but "know-how"—content, analytics, AI tools, and mechanisms to make users more valuable. Analogous to how tastylive changed trading education by providing actionable trading information, not just data.
- **Breaking salary taboo** [51:12–52:33]: Younger generation (Gen Z) already discusses compensation openly on LinkedIn; Lost Dog formalizes and quantifies this transparency. Older generation (Boomers) treats salary discussion as taboo.
- **Still actively trading** [54:48–56:07]: Made 77 trades on the day of interview; maintains short positions in gold, silver, and equities. Trading is non-negotiable; describes it as an addiction with no rehab.
