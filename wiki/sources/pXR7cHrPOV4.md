# +346% Return Pro Trader Matt Caruso | Money Talks

## Summary

Matt Caruso, a professional trader and Chartered Market Technician, discusses his path from retail investor to market maker to independent trader, culminating in a +346% return in the 2020 U.S. Investing Championship. He emphasizes technical analysis, institutional flow reading, disciplined risk management, and a fundamental-technical synthesis for identifying high-growth equities positioned for multi-month runs.

## Key takeaways

### Background & philosophy
- **Early influence of William O'Neill's *How to Make Money in Stocks***: O'Neill's emphasis on controlling risk and chart-based entry timing resonated far more than value-investing approaches; became foundational to Caruso's trading DNA [01:39–02:10]
- **Market maker experience (6 years at a major Canadian bank)**: Learned to read institutional order flow, recognize broker codes, and understand that institutions—not retail—drive trends; also gained macro awareness through commodity/resource stock exposure [06:48–11:04]
- **Discipline over luck**: Never experienced a major drawdown because he internalized O'Neill's cardinal rule of limiting risk; treats trading as a business, not gambling [01:00:43]

### 2020 Championship approach & COVID pivot
- **Cash positioning in February 2020**: Recognized distribution days (increased selling on down days) and deteriorating breadth (more 52-week lows than highs) as signals to move to 100% cash before the crash [26:24–27:14]
- **Fed confirmation + technical follow-through**: Waited for the Fed's emergency accommodation (zero rates, QE, reserve requirement elimination) and then a William O'Neill "follow-through day" (market up 1.25%+ on high volume, 4+ days after lows) before re-entering [32:47–34:11]
- **Sector thesis**: Identified stocks benefiting from stay-at-home economy (Levongo, Fastly, The Trade Desk) by studying historical parallels (1914 WWI steel stocks) and fundamental earnings growth [25:08–25:26]
- **Position building**: Scaled into winners over weeks, not days, to avoid gap-down risk and build a profit cushion; used short-term technical patterns to time additions to longer-term growth positions [45:20–51:06]

### Risk management & position sizing
- **Fixed-dollar stop-loss ("crucial dollar stop")**: Initial position sized as a percentage of account, but the dollar stop does not increase as position grows; allows aggressive scaling without increasing total risk [46:31–47:19]
- **5% advance rule**: Waits for initial purchase to advance ~5% before adding, ensuring breathing room for normal volatility and profit cushion [47:43–48:15]
- **Asymmetric payoff structure**: Willing to risk 3–4% per trade for potential 50–200%+ upside; can be wrong 7 out of 10 times and still have a profitable year [57:14–57:50]

### Stock selection & fundamental-technical synthesis
- **Earnings or sales growth as anchor**: Looks for record earnings or highest earnings in 8–10 years, OR record/highest sales with aggressive quarter-over-quarter growth [56:20–56:55]
- **Market recognition**: Identifies stocks at or near 52-week highs with strong fundamentals; uses this as confirmation that institutions are accumulating [53:22–53:40]
- **Macro + micro integration**: Combines macro thesis (e.g., Fed tightening favors current-earnings stocks over high-P/E growth) with sector rotation (energy over tech in 2022) and individual stock technicals [01:04:14–01:08:43]

### Technical tools & market reading
- **William O'Neill bases**: Specific criteria for consolidation patterns (depth, duration, minimum requirements) that precede institutional accumulation and explosive runs [01:12:46–01:13:34]
- **50-day moving average**: Simple filter; stocks below it are in correction or downtrend [01:13:54]
- **Breadth indicators**: Tracks new 52-week highs vs. lows; three consecutive days of more lows than highs signals institutional selling [26:57–27:14]
- **Real-time volume context**: Compares intraday volume to typical volume for that time of day (e.g., first 30 min is most active); above-average volume at breakout confirms institutional participation [01:17:19–01:17:51]
- **Simplification over time**: After years of study, Caruso's charts now contain only candlesticks, moving averages, and breadth; focus is on reading what the pattern tells you, not imposing a pattern [01:15:25–01:16:03]

### 2022 market outlook & oil thesis
- **Fed tightening as headwind**: Rising rates make future earnings less valuable; growth stocks already topped in Feb 2021; general market gains limited [01:04:14–01:09:18]
- **Sector rotation to energy**: Oil companies printing record earnings; OPEC and U.S. shale reluctant to increase supply (post-2020 restructuring, climate pressure); tight supply + recovering demand (travel, driving) = asymmetric upside to $100+ oil [01:05:05–01:08:43]
- **Time frame**: Typical holding period 3–9 months, occasionally up to 12 months; does not hold for multi-year theses due to opportunity cost and personal need for liquidity [01:11:27–01:11:58]

### Psychology & trader mindset
- **Competitive pressure in championship**: Balancing personal capital preservation with competitive drive; chose discipline over risk-taking to protect livelihood [34:33–36:07]
- **Trader vs. part-time investor**: Full-time traders face higher taxes, capital constraints, living expenses, and drawdown impacts on future earning power; requires different psychology than side trading [35:03–36:07]
- **Disposition effect & the dress analogy**: O'Neill's parable of selling non-performing inventory to buy more of what works resonated deeply; internalized early, making it "impossible" to hold losers [01:00:43–01:02:22]
- **Independent, self-reliant mindset**: Active trading builds confidence, risk assessment skills, and resilience to adversity—similar to military training [38:09–40:29]

### Teaching & course development
- **University course (2000s)**: Taught "How to Build a Profitable Trading System" using CMT curriculum + Bloomberg terminals; students built simple moving-average or breakout systems [13:27–14:37]
- **Active Growth Investor course**: Video-based, A-to-Z approach covering mindset, fundamentals, technicals, and position management; all five-star reviews [01:18:46–01:19:23]
- **Upcoming subscription service (Feb 2022)**: Twice-weekly video calls, daily markup roundups [01:19:41–01:19:57]
