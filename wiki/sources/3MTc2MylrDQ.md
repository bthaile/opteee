# buying otm options

## Summary

Far out-of-the-money (OTM) options are cheap and allow you to buy more units for the same capital, but require a larger underlying move to generate profit because delta is low far from the strike. The trade-off is lower theta decay (favorable) against the need for bigger moves to activate gamma gains.

## Key takeaways

- **Capital efficiency via unit count** [00:41]: OTM calls cost ~10 cents vs. ~$1 for ATM, allowing you to buy 10× the contracts for the same capital outlay.
- **Gamma acceleration in near-term OTM** [00:19]: Shorter-dated OTM options gain gamma quickly as expiration approaches, even if they start with little gamma.
- **Lower extrinsic value = lower theta bleed** [01:19]: OTM options have less extrinsic value than ATM, so you lose less to daily theta decay—a structural advantage.
- **Move requirement is the catch** [01:19]: Because delta is low far OTM, you need a *big* directional move to turn on gamma and start profiting; small moves won't move the needle.
- **Use case: high-conviction, large-move scenarios** [00:41]: Buy far OTM when you expect a really big move and want to maximize unit exposure on a tight budget.
