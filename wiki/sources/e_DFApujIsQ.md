# Covered Call Volatility Myth Exposed #optionstrading

## Summary

The video challenges the conventional wisdom that options should only be sold when implied volatility is at extreme percentiles. The host argues that selling covered calls and ratio call diagonals can be profitable across a range of volatility regimes, with the key principle being to avoid extremes rather than chase peaks. Optimal entry points often occur at moderate IV levels (15–20 percentile) where compensation is still attractive.

## Key takeaways

- **Avoid the IV-percentile trap** [00:00] — selling options exclusively at high IV percentiles (85+) is not a requirement for profitability; the real rule is to avoid extremes on both ends.
- **Mid-range IV can be lucrative** [00:18] — IV at 15–20 percentile can offer excellent risk-reward, provided you're still being adequately compensated for the eventual volatility expansion.
- **Compensation vs. extremes trade-off** [00:36] — when IV is very low, you risk insufficient premium to justify the risk when volatility eventually spikes; balance entry timing with expected payoff.
- **Ratio covered calls thrive in moderate regimes** — the strategy (selling short calls at a ratio to the long position) works best when you avoid both IV extremes and focus on fair pricing.

## Notable quotes

> "You want to avoid extremes is effectively what it boils down to."
