# How to Improve Short Puts Using Risk Adjusted Returns

## Summary

Rather than selecting short puts based solely on credit received, traders should evaluate candidates using risk-adjusted return metrics. The video introduces two primary frameworks: return on invested capital (premium ÷ margin requirement) and return on risk (premium ÷ value at risk), with guidance on annualizing these figures for meaningful comparison across different expirations and strikes.

## Key takeaways

- **Move beyond credit alone** [00:00] — Most traders focus only on the highest credit available; risk-adjusted returns provide a more complete picture of trade quality.
- **Return on invested capital (ROIC)** [00:00–00:27] — Divide premium by margin requirement to measure efficiency of capital deployed.
- **Return on risk (ROR)** [00:27] — Divide premium by your defined value at risk; you can customize the denominator (margin requirement, cash-secured put requirement, or your intended management level).
- **Annualization formula** [00:54] — A quick method exists to annualize these returns for fair comparison across different time frames.
