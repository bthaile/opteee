# The ITM Short Put Decision Tree

## Summary

A framework for managing short puts that move in-the-money, presenting four core decision paths: close the trade, roll it, take assignment, or pursue unassignment. The decision hinges on whether your original thesis—that the underlying will appreciate—remains intact, with additional nuance when the short put is part of a broader multi-leg strategy like a covered strangle.

## Key takeaways

- **Four management choices for ITM short puts** [00:00]: close, roll, take assignment, or pursue unassignment (the last being underutilized).
- **Thesis validation is the decision anchor** [00:26]: ask whether your original bullish view on the underlying still holds when the put moves ITM.
- **Context matters for multi-leg strategies** [00:49]: the decision framework shifts when the short put is embedded in a broader structure (e.g., covered strangle), not a standalone position.
- **Typical entry is OTM, but ITM entries exist** [00:26]: even if you sell an ITM put initially, the same thesis-first logic applies.
