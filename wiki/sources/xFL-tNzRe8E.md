# Selling Options Premium | Options Hacks #optionstrading

## Summary

The conventional wisdom to "sell premium when volatility is high" is only partially correct because high implied volatility correlates with large stock moves, increasing assignment risk. The optimal premium-selling opportunity occurs when implied volatility is elevated but realized volatility remains low—a gap known as the volatility risk premium—allowing sellers to capture contraction without fighting adverse price action.

## Key takeaways

- **High IV ≠ best selling environment** [00:00] — When implied volatility spikes, realized moves often follow, challenging short positions; the stock moves a lot, increasing the probability of being tested on the trade.
- **Volatility risk premium as the real edge** [00:18] — The gap between implied and realized volatility (variance risk premium) is the true opportunity; selling when IV is elevated but realized vol remains low captures this spread without fighting large directional moves.
- **Outlier approach** [00:18] — Successful premium selling requires identifying regimes where the market is pricing in more volatility than will actually occur, not simply selling into spikes.
