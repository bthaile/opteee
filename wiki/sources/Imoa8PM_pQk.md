# Improve your trading #options #trading #optionstrading

## Summary

The core principle for recovering from losing trades—and sustaining winning ones—is to shift focus from individual trade outcomes to the underlying process and system. Over a multi-decade trading career, no single trade should make or break a portfolio; consistent adherence to a proven process in aggregate is what generates long-term returns.

## Key takeaways

- **Process over outcome** [00:00–00:27]: The best recovery mechanism from losses is identical to managing wins—stop obsessing over individual trade results and focus on the repeatable process behind them.
- **Decades-long perspective** [00:27–00:49]: Your job as a trader is to deploy a skill set across decades, not days or weeks. Starting early (e.g., age 17) compounds the edge over time.
- **Portfolio-level thinking** [00:49]: No single trade should make or break your portfolio. Reward yourself for following the process; punish yourself for outcome-obsession, not for losses that follow good decisions.

## Notable quotes
