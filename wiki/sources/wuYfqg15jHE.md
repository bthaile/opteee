# GME Stock and Options Analysis

## Summary

GameStop is consolidating sideways after the recent offering, with volume contracting from a peak of ~62M shares (Sept 20) to ~5.7M. The options market shows massive call skew (700k+ calls vs. <300k puts), with most call open interest concentrated in October 18 expiration and longer-dated puts reflecting hedging positions. Eric maintains a covered-call position (long shares + short calls at ratio) while monitoring dark-pool 5,000-share block trades and analyzing a viewer's potential volatility play across two call expirations.

## Key takeaways

- **Consolidation phase post-offering** [01:12–01:57]: Volume has contracted significantly from 62M (Sept 20) to 5.7M; GameStop is leveling up from the 200-day moving average and consolidating sideways, which Eric views as healthy.
- **Extreme call skew** [02:17–03:01]: Over 700,000 calls vs. fewer than 300,000 puts—well above the standard 2:1 ratio Eric monitors. Most calls are in October 18 expiration; longer-dated puts (Jan 17, 2025) suggest hedging positions.
- **Implied volatility remains low** [03:23–03:48]: IV is below 100%, which is relatively low for GameStop; this is becoming the new norm across products.
- **Covered-call position management** [03:48–04:41]: Eric maintains long shares + short calls at a ratio (>100 shares per call sold) to avoid being short GameStop in a squeeze while extracting value during consolidation.
- **Dark-pool 5,000-share blocks** [04:58–06:00]: Recurring 5,000-share block trades visible since May; Eric notes these are worth monitoring but cautions against over-interpreting them as evidence of a single actor.
- **Options activity quiet** [06:24–07:06]: Weekly options flow is subdued; largest single trade was ~42k premium. Eric emphasizes that changes in this activity are what signal interesting moves.
- **Viewer trade analysis: likely volatility play** [07:06–12:23]: Paul's identified trades appear to be 550 long Jan 17, 2025 $18 calls paired with 550 long Sept 27, 2024 $17 calls—likely a volatility expansion play. Eric cautions that inferring trader intent from order flow is inherently speculative and difficult to monetize as a primary strategy.
