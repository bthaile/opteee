# Trading failure rate #options #trading

## Summary

The video addresses why trading failure rates are cited as high as 98%, attributing the lack of precise data to brokers' financial incentive to encourage trading volume. The core reason most traders fail is approaching markets with unrealistic expectations of quick, easy profits rather than treating trading as a learnable skill requiring disciplined study and execution.

## Key takeaways

- **Brokers suppress failure data** [00:00] — Brokers are disincentivized from publishing trader failure statistics because they profit from transaction volume; free education often encourages multi-leg strategies to generate more commissions.
- **Wrong mental starting point** [00:23] — Most traders fail because they begin with the assumption that trading is fast, easy money, when it is actually a challenging environment requiring serious skill development.
- **Trading is a learnable skill** [00:46] — Like any other skill set, trading can be learned if you understand what you're trying to do and commit the requisite work; if that commitment doesn't appeal to you, passive investing is a viable alternative.
