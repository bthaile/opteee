# Trading GameStop with the Covered Strangle Options Strategy Pt1

## Summary

Eric demonstrates a practical covered strangle position on GameStop, combining 200 long shares with short puts and a short call to generate premium while maintaining upside participation. The strategy exploits GameStop's exceptionally high implied volatility (110% vs. Apple's 26.6%) and unusual call skew to capture volatility risk premium. The position is designed for holders who intend to own the stock long-term and want to optimize returns during sideways or consolidation periods.

## Key takeaways

- **Covered strangle structure** [01:16–04:59]: Long shares + cash-secured short puts + ratio short calls (1 call per 200 shares to avoid capped upside and preserve management flexibility).
- **Why GameStop fits the strategy** [07:40–09:03]: High realized volatility (110% IV vs. 26.6% for AAPL) and elevated IV percentile (53%) mean short options capture significant premium; volatility risk premium is the edge.
- **Unique call skew advantage** [10:36–13:28]: GameStop exhibits reverse skew—calls priced at 143.37% IV vs. puts at 87.2% IV (opposite of typical put skew). This makes short calls exceptionally lucrative relative to short puts, a structural anomaly in this ticker.
- **Position allocation and risk** [14:39–15:50]: $115,000 allocation with defined risk equal to total capital (willing to lose entire amount if GME → $0). Risk is managed via allocation size, not stops, because GameStop can consolidate for extended periods.
- **Management plan—upside** [16:18–17:08]: Do not unwind shares unless price doubles (target ~$50–60 from current levels), signaling a squeeze. Unwind is opportunistic profit-taking to redeploy capital into more puts and shares on pullbacks.
- **Management plan—direction-based entry** [17:08–18:27]: Sell puts during sideways/down periods; buy shares outright during strong uptrends. Maintain 70%+ invested at all times, with mix of shares and cash-secured puts adjusting by price action.
- **Current position snapshot** [19:46–20:57]: 200 shares @ $24.31 basis; 2× Feb 21 $28 puts; 1× Jan 24 $29 put; 1× Jan 17 $35 call. From Nov 8 entry, GME returned 26%, strategy returned 29.2% (realized gains included).
- **Weekly tracking** [20:57–22:03]: Eric commits to weekly updates on position management, rolls, and adjustments as the trade evolves.

## Notable quotes

> "The purpose of this strategy is to optimize an existing holding position of stock so the base position in the coverage triangle is long shares and then the options are just to amplify the position." [07:40]

> "GameStop uniquely has exceedingly high volatility on the call side rather than what you typically get in the coverage strangle and like an index ETF the calls don't really help you that much because you collect a little bit on them but there's not a lot of V in them on a relative basis to the puts and GameStop it's the opposite you make a lot of money on a relative basis by selling the calls."
