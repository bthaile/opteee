# Doomberg - Is the Economy About to Change Big Time? | The Outlier Podcast

## Summary

Doomberg discusses macroeconomic shifts under Trump's administration, focusing on tariff policy, energy markets, manufacturing renaissance, and geopolitical realignment. The conversation covers Trump's strategy to catalyze US manufacturing through tariffs, tax cuts, and foreign direct investment; OPEC's apparent coordination with US interests; the Ukraine conflict's trajectory; and the critical role of nuclear energy in powering AI infrastructure. Doomberg emphasizes lateral thinking, process-based analysis, and the importance of reading multiple information sources to navigate an increasingly complex macro landscape.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2025-05-28)

- **Trump's economic gamble**: Combination of tariffs, tax cuts, FDI, and cheap energy aimed at manufacturing boom; success depends on bridging unsustainable old state to disputed new state [02:31]
- **Oil target**: Trump has declared desire for ~$50 crude; Saudis appear willing to cooperate; matters less to Russia now than three years ago [03:44]
- **OPEC coordination**: Deal likely cut between Russia, Saudi Arabia, and US; OPEC masking cooperation as punishment of Kazakhstan cheating [19:08]
- **Ukraine stalemate**: Russia escalating territorial demands with each advance; NATO/Ukraine demanding ceasefire while Russia refuses (winning side doesn't demand ceasefire) [26:25]
- **Energy infrastructure risk**: Terrorism against choke points (Ras Tanura, Turkstream pipelines) represents underappreciated systemic risk; Nordstream destruction normalized critical infrastructure sabotage [49:09]
- **AI-grid incompatibility**: Texas needs to double installed capacity in 8 years; distributed SMRs with natural gas preferred over grid-dependent AI deployment [54:20]

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Lateral thinking framework**: Make wild assumption, play it forward, measure against data/evidence; discard model moment it stops working [06:16]
- **Doomberg's editorial process**: Show homework, cite sources, present evidence that would disprove thesis; let intelligent readers form own conclusions [07:25]
- **Forecasting humility**: Nobody bats a thousand; publish quarterly retrospectives on call accuracy; confidence vs. arrogance distinction [08:33]
- **Information synthesis**: Read both sides' propaganda (e.g., Kiev Independent + TASS); wait 3–4 days for ground truth to emerge; weight credibility of sources over time [31:04]
- **Accountability-control alignment**: Match decision authority with accountability; use RACI framework (Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed) to prevent mission failure [45:32]
- **Distributed energy architecture**: SMRs + natural gas on-site superior to centralized grid for high-demand applications; avoids grid congestion and price inflation [55:33]
