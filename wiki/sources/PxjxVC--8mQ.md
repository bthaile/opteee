# The Future of Markets & Investing in AI - Matt Caruso | The Outlier Podcast Ep67

## Summary

Matt Caruso discusses the structural constraints of capital deployment at scale, the concentration risk in mega-cap tech (particularly the "Magnificent Seven"), and the AI revolution as a multi-decade infrastructure and application wave comparable to the internet boom. He outlines his trend-following methodology using net highs/lows as a liquidity indicator, recent earnings season challenges, and positioning for potential rate cuts and economic slowdown in 2025.

## Key takeaways

### Market structure & scale
- **Capital deployment limits** [00:20–01:29]: Warren Buffett's observation that even billionaires struggle to deploy capital into small-cap opportunities; consistent 30–50% annual returns exist but don't scale beyond tens of millions.
- **Liquidity as a natural constraint** [02:04–02:25]: Derivatives markets have a naturally limited universe of tradeable securities once you reach institutional scale; this is why mega-cap tech (especially [[nvda]]) captures disproportionate flows.
- **Market concentration at extremes** [11:26–11:59]: The S&P 500 is now ~25–26% concentrated in three stocks; this is historically unprecedented and distorts traditional index-based analysis.

### Technical analysis & breadth
- **Net highs/lows as liquidity proxy** [05:31–07:10]: Unlike advance/decline lines, net highs/lows measure actual 52-week extremes and reflect net money flow; useful as an "easy money vs. hard money" indicator rather than a binary bull/bear signal.
- **NASDAQ vs. NYSE divergence** [05:48–06:24]: NASDAQ weakness (apart from large caps) reflects speculative sector struggle; NYSE relatively stronger; the gap is informative but not predictive on its own.
- **Avoiding false divergence signals** [07:27–07:41]: Advance/decline divergences can persist for years (topped 1998, market topped 2000); net highs/lows divergences are typically short-lived in healthy markets.
- **Contextual interpretation** [08:14–08:50]: Breadth indicators are tools, not binary signals; overlay with price action and sector charts to avoid forced trades in weak groups.

### Current market regime (June 2024)
- **Hard dollar environment** [10:39–11:10]: Money flowing exclusively into AI and large-cap tech; everything else in correction or sideways chop for months.
- **Earnings season headwinds** [04:14–04:45]: Nine out of eleven positions gapped down on earnings; hedges (puts) protected downside; forced repositioning difficult in strong market.
- **Trend-following challenges** [05:16–05:31]: Past 2–3 months deadly chop; net highs/lows helped sidestep noise; waiting for next momentum ignition.

### AI as a multi-decade cycle
- **Infrastructure wave (current)** [15:22–15:55]: Similar to 1990s internet; Microsoft Windows, Intel, Nvidia are foundational but already large; percentage gains capped.
- **Next wave: applications** [16:15–17:10]: Yahoo, Google, and new upstarts delivered outsized returns; 95% of Y Combinator startups now AI-related; biotech, machinery, call centers all disrupted.
- **Capital commitment validates thesis** [17:24–17:42]: Elon/Tesla spending $4–5B/year on Nvidia chips alone; combined industry spend in hundreds of billions; no company invests that without real monetization path.
- **Metaverse vs. AI** [18:40–19:16]: Metaverse required behavioral shift (digital living); AI is tool-agnostic, applicable to drug discovery, vehicle efficiency, countless domains; broader applicability = easier scaling.

### Historical precedent & timeline compression
- **Internet as best comparison** [25:04–25:26]: Internet provided a base-layer platform that mutated in unforeseen directions; cars remained cars, but internet enabled countless new services.
- **Multi-layer infrastructure evolution** [25:49–26:07]: Early 1990s winners (Cisco, 3Com) different from late 1990s (Nortel, fast ethernet); technology moves fast, infrastructure rebuilds every 10 years.
- **Compressed timelines** [26:25–26:45]: 1990s–2000s internet cycle took ~20–25 years; AI cycle may compress to 7–10 years due to faster iteration.
- **Pattern persistence across cycles** [27:54–28:31]: Nvidia, ARM, Taiwan Semi all show classic 6-month–1.5-year leading-stock cycles; volatility compression, trend, then breakout; patterns repeat despite compressed timeframes.

### Position management & entry discipline
- **Build-in protocol** [31:24–32:02]: Accumulate over 1–2 weeks across multiple stocks; ideal entry after market correction; hold 3–5 months for wave cycle; sell into strength.
- **ARM case study** [33:14–36:42]: IPO base at ~$46.50; first entry at 50-day MA (~$55); added on consolidation breakouts; sold into 115–120 strength pre-earnings; post-IPO lockup break typical; re-entry on next base setup.
- **IPO lockup rule** [35:50–36:08]: Sell into strength if stock doubles from base midpoint; insiders liquidate post-lockup expiry; expect nasty break; re-entry on next structure.
- **Position sizing flexibility** [30:12–30:59]: Typical 10% initial entry; if uncertain, start 5–6% but force way to target; avoid staying small; adjust for chop but maintain discipline.

### Fed, rates & 2025 outlook
- **Two-year yield as policy anchor** [44:30–45:02]: Federal funds should follow US 2-year yield; currently ~0.75 points below; too-low rates create asset bubbles, too-high create weakness.
- **Economic weakness building** [45:23–46:01]: Canadian, European, equal-weight US markets soft; large-cap tech distorts; ECB cut 25bp recognizing inflation near 2% and growth risk.
- **Fed likely slow to cut** [46:36–47:10]: Powell prefers to revive weak economy than fight sticky inflation; willing to let things break slightly; QE easier than inflation control.
- **2025 catalyst stack** [52:53–53:13]: Potential tax cuts + falling rates + AI revolution + inflation control = "really promising 2025"; expect September–October correction, then rally into year-end.

### Dividend & bond proxies
- **High-dividend positioning** [48:29–49:02]: If economic slowdown arrives, dividend payers will get bid as rates fall; TLT (bond ETF) making higher lows; bond proxy play.
- **Speculative upside from rate cuts** [49:02–49:19]: When rates fall, speculative growth gets another tailwind; currently under pressure from high rates; watch for next leaders post-correction.
