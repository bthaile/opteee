# Economic Recession Avoided? w/ Doomberg | The Outlier Podcast Ep60

## Summary

Doomberg discusses why the US avoided the widely-expected 2023 recession despite the Fed raising rates from 0% to 5%. The core thesis: the Shale revolution added ~2.5 Saudi Arabias' worth of hydrocarbon production to the US energy mix, creating a manufacturing cost advantage that insulated the economy from rate shock. The conversation pivots to geopolitical risk, border security, and the potential for escalating conflicts (Ukraine, Middle East, China) to derail the energy-abundance thesis.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2024-02-28)

- **Recession avoided (for now)** [00:45–02:17]: The consensus view entering 2023 was that the Fed's aggressive rate hikes would break the economy. Neither a recession nor a Shale production collapse materialized; instead, the US set record oil, NGL, and natural gas production months.
- **Energy abundance pivot** [17:25–18:48]: In November 2023, Doomberg shifted from energy-scarcity to energy-abundance thesis after deep-diving the global natural gas market. This realization—that the world is now swimming in natural gas—explains why the US economy remained resilient.
- **Natural gas pricing advantage** [02:41–03:05]: At ~$1.65/MMBtu, US natural gas is priced at the energy equivalent of ~$10/barrel oil, giving US manufacturers a massive cost advantage vs. global competitors.
- **Political tailwind for incumbents** [20:33–22:54]: The Biden administration understands commodity dynamics (SPR draining, export pause signaling) and is actively managing energy policy to avoid recession before the election.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Energy is the economy** [04:13–04:58]: Energy is not merely an input; it is the foundation of economic activity. Standard of living correlates directly with per-capita energy consumption. Thermodynamically, order requires constant energy dissipation (heat waste).
- **Hydrocarbon-powered manufacturing** [02:41–03:24]: The US has integrated downstream manufacturing (chemicals, automotives, fertilizers) fed by cheap domestic energy. This structural advantage is unlike petro-states that rely solely on commodity exports.
- **Recession catalysts remain** [23:16–24:24]: China property collapse, European de-industrialization, and geopolitical escalation (Ukraine, Middle East, Taiwan) could still trigger recession despite energy tailwinds.
- **Analyst vs. advocate mindset** [01:59–02:17]: Doomberg's philosophy: when you get a call wrong, understand why and learn. Avoid advocacy (defending a thesis) in favor of analysis (seeking truth). Publish mistake reviews every 30–40 pieces.

### Geopolitical risk (secondary thread)

- **World War III framing** [35:34–43:55]: Doomberg argues the US is already in a multi-front conflict: proxy war in Ukraine (NATO vs. Russia), kinetic war in Middle East (arming Israel), and rising tensions with China. This mirrors 1941 pre-Pearl Harbor, when the US was supplying multiple sides before formal entry.
- **Border security as national defense** [52:43–57:04]: An uncontrolled southern border (10M+ crossings) is a failure of the government's primary duty. Doomberg contrasts TSA security theater with open borders, arguing both or neither should exist.
- **Ukraine situation as 2024 wildcard** [01:01:11–01:02:29]: The most interesting question for 2024 is how fast Ukraine resolves. Open-source intelligence (drone footage, battlefield maps) shows a very different picture than Western media portrayal; rapid Ukrainian collapse or Zelenskyy ouster is possible and underpriced.

## Regime / context

**Date:** 2024-02-28 (Q1 2024). The US has just completed 2023 without a recession despite a 500 bp rate hike cycle. Energy markets are in a glut phase (natural gas abundant, oil production at record levels). Geopolitical tensions are elevated: Ukraine war ongoing, Israel–Gaza conflict active, China property sector in distress, and US border crossings at historic highs.

**Doomberg's thesis:** The Shale revolution's energy abundance is the primary reason the US economy avoided recession. However, multiple black-swan risks (China collapse, Ukraine escalation, Middle East spread, Taiwan conflict) could reverse this tailwind. The 2024 election cycle creates political incentives to maintain economic stability through energy policy management.
