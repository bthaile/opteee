# The Risks of Naked Shorting VIX

## Summary

A cautionary breakdown of why naked short calls on VIX are structurally dangerous: the index can spike violently (to 80+) in short windows, triggering forced liquidations and broker margin calls. The session also explains why buying puts on elevated VIX appears profitable on paper but underperforms expectations due to mean-reversion pricing already embedded in the options surface.

## Key takeaways

- **Naked short calls on VIX are a liquidation trap** [00:00–00:24]: VIX can rip 80+ points in short periods, defying gravity temporarily and forcing you into a dangerous broker margin situation.
- **Put buying on VIX looks logical but underperforms** [00:24–00:41]: When VIX is elevated, the 50-delta put strike sits far out-of-the-money (e.g., 20.5 when spot is higher) because the market has already priced in mean reversion.
- **Mean reversion is baked into the options surface** [00:41–00:56]: The market knows VIX will not stay elevated forever; that conviction is reflected in skewed delta placement, so your P&L on a put sale won't match your intuitive expectation.

## Notable quotes
