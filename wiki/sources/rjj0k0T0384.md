# AVGO Earnings, Market Breadth Scan, and GameStop Options Setup

## Summary

Eric reviews Broadcom's mixed earnings (beat on EPS but stock down ~10 points), examines sector rotation weakness across financials and utilities while tech struggles, and scans optionable stocks to assess market breadth. He notes S&P 500 breadth has contracted to 66% above the 50-day MA and flags a tentative market environment near all-time highs. GameStop remains in a sideways consolidation with a 2:1 call-to-put ratio; he plans a dedicated Saturday stream to analyze Roaring Kitty's historical options positions.

## Key takeaways

- **AVGO earnings miss on guidance despite EPS beat** [00:00–01:02]: Broadcom beat EPS (124¢ vs. 95¢ expected) but fell ~10 points to 142; mixed earnings and operational weakness are weighing on the stock.
- **Tech sector distribution phase** [01:02–02:09]: QQQ consolidating but tech (XLC) showing weakness; broader market rotation out of strength sectors (XLF, XLV down; XLU, XLRE, XLP holding highs; XLB, XLE continuing downtrend).
- **Market breadth contraction warning** [02:09–03:22]: S&P 500 stocks above 50-day MA dropped to 66%; Eric dislikes seeing this below 75% during aggressive downmoves; indicates subsurface rotation.
- **SPY technical setup** [03:22–04:25]: Shorter-term moving averages rotating over; about to test 50-day MA; rounding top pattern; VIX not confirming the down move, but momentum indicators flattening.
- **Optionable stock scan: highs vs. lows** [04:25–06:34]: ~1,719 stocks within 5% of 52-week highs vs. ~426 near 52-week lows; energy, materials, industrials dominate the lows; financials leading the highs; tech (only 2 optionable names near highs) in distribution.
- **GameStop sideways consolidation** [07:42–08:40]: Downtrending from recent peak but building a higher low; 2:1 call-to-put ratio with call side growing; earnings upcoming.
- **GameStop options expiration structure** [08:40–10:47]: Current non-earnings weekly expiration quiet (3:1 calls-to-puts); Eric monitoring whether earnings expiration (13 DTE) will see volume expansion above 100k contracts, signaling genuine earnings plays vs. simple rollovers.
- **Current trading posture** [10:47–11:44]: Taking premium opportunities; light trend-following in futures (indices not trending); using ratio call diagonals for directional plays in smaller groupings; avoiding heavy capital deployment near all-time highs with stalling momentum.
