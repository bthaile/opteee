# Moving Averages For Technical Analysis

## Summary

A technical analysis education on using moving averages as a primary tool for assessing price strength and momentum across multiple timeframes. The session covers how to interpret moving average organization, spacing, and price relationship to identify trend direction, momentum quality, and potential consolidation or pullback zones.

## Key takeaways

- **Timeframe alignment matters**: Only reference moving averages relevant to your trading timeframe—a 5-day trader ignores longer-term MAs [00:00]
- **Organization reveals structure**: The arrangement of short-term vs. long-term moving averages (which is above/below) tells you the directional bias [00:21]
- **Width signals extension**: When the spacing between moving averages widens excessively, price often becomes overextended, frequently leading to consolidation or pullback before continuation [00:21]
- **Slope = momentum quality**: Strongly upward-sloping moving averages typically indicate good upside momentum [00:47]
- **Exit framework**: Moving average relationships form the basis for many trend-following exit strategies [00:47]
- **Balance principle**: Always evaluate price in the context of balance between multiple moving average levels [00:47]
