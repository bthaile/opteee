# Trading Vol & Spreads #optionstrading

## Summary

This video contrasts pure volatility trading (via straddles and strangles) with spread-based strategies (iron condors, iron flies). The core insight: spreads flatten the Greeks and convert volatility trades into directional range-bound bets, fundamentally changing the profit mechanism and risk profile. Rebalancing is essential for maintaining delta-neutral volatility exposure.

## Key takeaways

- **Straddles/strangles isolate volatility initially, but require active rebalancing** [00:00] — as the market moves, unmanaged delta exposure creeps in; rebalancing the position restores volatility isolation.
- **Spreads flatten Greeks and shift the trade from volatility to range-bound directional** [00:22] — iron condors and iron flies are not volatility trades; they bet on price staying within a range, with max loss built in.
- **Spread max loss is a structural disadvantage in volatile markets** [00:22] — if the underlying moves sharply, you hit max loss quickly, whereas a rebalanced straddle can adapt.
- **Conflating volatility trading with spread trading is a common mistake** [00:46] — many traders believe they're trading implied volatility when using spreads, but they're actually trading containment.

## Notable quotes

> "The whole point of spreads is to flatten out the Greeks, diminish them. It turns them more into directional bets."
