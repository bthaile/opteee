# Managing Early Assignment in Covered Calls #optionstrading

## Summary

Early assignment on short calls in a covered-call position is avoidable through active monitoring and management. The risk escalates when calls move deep in-the-money with minimal extrinsic value remaining, especially ahead of dividend dates. Rather than passively holding to expiration, traders can roll positions or add shares to preserve inventory while maintaining the primary profit driver: the underlying equity position.

## Key takeaways

- **Early assignment risk triggers** [00:00]: Deep ITM calls with little extrinsic value, particularly when a dividend payout exceeds remaining time value, create strong incentive for counterparty exercise.
- **Rolling as a management tool** [00:24]: If you want to keep your shares, rolling the short call to a later expiration or higher strike is an active alternative to sitting idle until expiration.
- **Profit mechanism clarity** [00:44]: The primary profit in covered calls comes from the long equity position, not the short call premium—this reframes how to think about assignment risk and position management.
- **Proactive inventory preservation** [00:24]: You can buy additional shares or adjust the position dynamically rather than accepting whatever outcome expiration delivers.

## Notable quotes
