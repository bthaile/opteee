# Andrew Mack: Creating Edge in Options Trading & Sports Betting | The Outlier Podcast

## Summary

Andrew Mack discusses the deep parallels between sports betting and options trading, emphasizing that both are fundamentally about identifying positive-expectancy edges through market distortions. He walks through the process of discovering, testing, and validating trading strategies—from effect identification through walk-forward testing to live execution—and stresses the importance of understanding market regimes, payoff ratios over win rates, and the psychological discipline required to give strategies adequate runway before judgment.

## Key takeaways

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Edge is the foundation** [01:10]: Trading and betting both rest on positive expectancy; the Kelly criterion and risk management determine sustainable growth without ruin.

- **Distortions vs. efficiency** [02:34]: Look for market distortions (inefficiencies, mispricings) rather than assuming markets are perfectly efficient. Smaller, less-watched markets often have wider bid-ask spreads but can harbor 20% of tradeable edge if you can overcome the cost headwind [07:27].

- **Liquidity paradox** [09:44]: Highly liquid markets are highly efficient; illiquid markets have wider spreads. The sweet spot is finding edge first, then filtering for tradeable liquidity—not the reverse.

- **Market effects as starting point** [14:43]: Begin by building a mental repertoire of market effects (VRP, equity risk premium, momentum, mean reversion), then investigate subsections and timing within those effects.

- **Counterparty dynamics** [15:52]: Understand what other market participants are doing and when they might be forced to buy or sell at unfavorable prices. This reveals "pinch points" where edge emerges.

- **Testing sequence: Monte Carlo → walk-forward → live** [18:11]: Use Monte Carlo simulation to validate effect strength; walk-forward testing to assess current-regime performance (better than backtesting for distortions); then fire on the strategy live to collect real market feedback.

- **Risk premia vs. distortions** [18:11]: Backtesting works well for risk premia (structural, persistent); walk-forward testing is safer for distortions (environment-dependent, may fade).

- **Environmental conditioning** [20:23]: Track whether a strategy performs better/worse above the 200-day SMA, in contango vs. backwardation, when VIX is above/below historical levels. This reveals regime dependence and improves timing.

- **Payoff ratio over win rate** [25:06]: Prefer strategies with healthy payoff ratios and lower win rates (especially with options' convexity) over high win-rate, low-payoff strategies. Win rates are environment-dependent and can collapse (e.g., short-vol players in March–April).

- **Rolling metrics in live trading** [28:59]: Monitor both cumulative sample size and rolling metrics (last month vs. last year) to spot divergences early. Small sample sizes (2–3 trades) are meaningless; expect hundreds to thousands of trades before drawing conclusions.

- **Fractional Kelly for portfolio sizing** [37:37]: Use quarter-Kelly or similar fractional sizing to avoid over-betting estimated edge. Full Kelly with estimated parameters risks ruin.

- **Complementary strategies by regime** [38:53]: Build a toolkit of strategies that work in different volatility/market environments (long straddle for high-vol, momentum for trending, etc.) so you always have positive-expectancy trades available.

- **Catalyst vs. inefficiency** [55:18]: Catalysts (tariffs, wars, supply shocks) are unpredictable events; inefficiencies are persistent mispricings. Fragility (unbalanced positioning) creates payoff opportunity even if catalyst probability is low.

- **Intraday vs. overnight volatility** [52:32]: Track intraday/overnight realized volatility ratios to understand where moves are coming from and position accordingly (e.g., long straddle overnight if overnight vol is elevated).

- **Sports betting ↔ options crossover** [01:03:05]: NFL point spreads map to ATM strikes; alternate lines map to OTM strikes; vig increases with distance from the main line, just as implied volatility increases with moneyness. Market makers and sportsbooks use similar logic.

### Dated market read (2025-07-16)

- **Trump tariff environment** [48:54]: Mack identified overconfidence in an "up-only" market narrative despite minority voices warning of downside fragility. He positioned long puts on indices (SPY, QQQ, ES) ahead of tariff announcements, capturing realized volatility spikes and achieving two of his best trading months during that period.

- **Market positioning into known catalysts** [56:26]: Despite advance notice of tariff announcements, the market was heavily positioned for upside, creating asymmetric payoff for long-volatility trades even with low probability of the downside event.

His approach blends sports-betting frameworks (player props, market-based modeling) with options trading, emphasizing regime-adaptive strategy selection and the primacy of effect identification over backtesting confidence.
