# Rolling the Covered Strangle

## Summary

This video explains the tactical differences between rolling short puts versus short calls within a covered strangle framework. The core principle: short puts are rolled aggressively to manage cost basis and avoid being trapped at unfavorable entry prices, while short calls are rarely rolled unless the profit opportunity is substantial—allowing assignment to free up capital for reinvestment in new positions.

## Key takeaways

- **Short put rolling discipline** [00:00]: Roll puts proactively to manage basis and prevent being locked into a top; this is a core risk-management tactic in the covered strangle.
- **Short call assignment as feature, not bug** [00:00]: Allow short calls to be assigned when profitable; assignment frees up long equity capital that can then be redeployed into new cash-secured put sales.
- **Asymmetric rolling approach**: Short calls are rolled only when the profit opportunity is clearly substantial; otherwise, assignment is preferred to reset the capital cycle.
