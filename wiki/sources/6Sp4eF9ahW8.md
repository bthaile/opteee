# David Hunter on Navigating Tariffs & Global Economic Chaos | The Outlier Podcast

## Summary
Macro strategist David Hunter argues that despite the violent April 2025 tariff sell-off, his long-standing "melt-up into a bust" thesis is intact — and he actually *raised* his targets in the middle of the panic. He frames the tariffs as a negotiating tool that resolves toward freer trade, sees rates and inflation falling, and treats extreme bearish sentiment plus forced liquidation as a contrarian buy signal. The trading implication: a sharp final melt-up in equities, bonds, and precious metals into a summer/H2 top, followed by a deflationary global bust (possibly starting Q4 2025) and then a massive central-bank reflation.

## Key takeaways
- Thesis unchanged, targets RAISED during the sell-off. Made the revisions "right smack in the middle" of the sell-off with the S&P below 5,000 [1:16:33].
- S&P 500 pre-bust / melt-up top target raised to **8,000** (from 7,500), expected this year [09:21], [1:16:33].
- NASDAQ target raised to **27,000** (from 25,000) [1:16:33].
- Gold target raised to **4,000** (from 3,400), "very doable this year"; silver target held at **75** and expected to outperform gold from here [1:16:33], [1:17:40].
- SMH (semis) target **400** vs ~190 at the time — "more than a double"; expects semis to lead the next leg [1:16:33].
- 10-year yield: sub-3% by summer, possibly 2.5% by Aug/Sep. Insists the April rate spike to 4.5%+ was a "round trip" panic, not a trend change; rates topped near 5% months ago and are still in a downtrend [07:04], [08:11], [12:49].
- Claims the S&P bottomed on the violent sell-off at ~4,800–4,900; expects the next leg up, NOT a retest / new lows; could be "back at the high 5000s" within weeks [10:32].
- Recession call: "in recession if we're not already… before this quarter is out" — but views a *containable* recession as bullish (market rises into it). A much harder landing "may end my thesis" [07:04], [36:13].
- Bust timing: still a possible Q4 2025 event, could slip to 2026; timing "not really changed," maybe lost a month [10:32].
- Tariffs are NOT inflationary — one-time price bump offset by a big demand dip; expects inflation to fall toward/below the 2% target ("true inflation" 1.3%), helped by oil ~$55 and rolling-over housing [38:36], [39:49].
- Fed: bond market (vigilantes), not the Fed, controls rates. Thinks the Fed *should* cut in May and *could*, even though Powell signals otherwise. Explicitly NO "Powell put" — no 2020-style money pump to save the market [08:11], [1:08:19], [1:10:40].
- Tariff resolution: 40–50 countries coming to the table; expects deals over "the next several weeks" and a net move to freer / lower tariffs within ~a month to a month and a half; China and the EU the hard cases [15:04], [19:45].
- The bust scenario (falsifiable, longer-dated): global deflationary bust triggers a ~$20T Fed response (balance sheet toward ~$30T) and ~$60T worldwide; commodities crater first, then explode next decade [1:02:18], [1:03:32].
- Bust-then-boom commodity path: copper $6 this cycle → down to ~$1–2 in the bust → ~$25 next decade; oil ~$60 now → $30 in the bust → possibly $500; natural gas from ~$3 range toward ~$50 [59:44], [1:01:04], [1:04:49].
- Reshoring is a multi-year story (real output ~2027–2030); helps the recovery/melt-up as a *concept* that markets discount, not a near-term economic driver [25:42].
- Falsification watch-list (Hunter's own): (1) tariff/negotiation progress over the next month or two; (2) Fed timing / jobs / broad economic data; a hard landing or China refusing to negotiate ("we just continue to ratchet up tariffs") would break the setup [1:07:10], [1:08:19], [1:10:40].

## Notable quotes
