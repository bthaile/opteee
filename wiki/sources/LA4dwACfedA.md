# AI Can't Kill Retail Edge Yet

## Summary

Retail traders retain exploitable edge in markets where institutional operators face capacity constraints, despite AI expansion making institutional attacks on smaller opportunities increasingly cost-effective. Portfolio managers' time allocation toward higher-impact positions leaves gaps that smaller traders can still profitably access. The structural advantage of retail—ability to deploy capital in lower-capacity markets—persists, though it is gradually eroding.

## Key takeaways

- **Capacity constraints protect retail edge** [00:00–00:33]: Larger operators cannot profitably deploy meaningful position sizes in smaller markets; this structural reality remains despite AI advancement.
- **Time allocation favors institutional focus on high-capacity trades** [00:33–00:52]: Portfolio managers optimize for higher-impact operations that absorb more capital and generate greater absolute returns, leaving smaller opportunities uncontested.
- **AI is raising the bar, not eliminating retail opportunity** [00:00]: Institutional adoption of AI makes attacks on retail-accessible markets more cost-effective over time, but the transition is gradual—retail edge is not yet extinct.
