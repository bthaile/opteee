# Is Roaring Kitty Buying GME Calls?

## Summary

Analysis of a reported block trade in GME 15 November $25 calls (~5,000 contracts at ~$0.33 delta) attributed speculatively to Roaring Kitty. The host examines the trade's characteristics—position size, days-to-expiration, delta selection, and entry timing—against Roaring Kitty's documented historical trading patterns to assess likelihood of attribution. Conclusion: mixed signals, but the short duration and modest position size diverge from his typical phasing-in behavior.

## Key takeaways

- **Block trade details** [00:00–01:14]: ~5,000 GME 15 Nov $25 calls purchased at offer around 14:30 ET; volume spike to 20,000+ contracts on the day triggered unusual-activity scans.
- **Gamma ramp mechanics** [02:28–03:48]: Gamma ramps require depth across multiple strikes; individual 5,000-contract blocks insufficient alone. Market makers hedge by buying shares as options move ITM, pushing price into next strike if sufficient OI exists.
- **Strike concentration pattern** [04:04–05:04]: Primary expirations (strikes ending in 0 or 5) naturally accumulate higher volume; $25 strike had 18,000 OI pre-trade, $30 had 13,000—normal distribution, not necessarily bullish signal.
- **Roaring Kitty's historical DTE preference** [06:09–07:24]: Overwhelming majority of his entries use much longer days-to-expiration (30+ days); only one documented sub-30-DTE trade (3 DTE in 2020, tied to console-release thesis). 15 Nov calls (~9 DTE at time of trade) would be anomalous.
- **Position-sizing and phasing pattern** [08:42–10:00]: His prior large positions scaled in over multiple days; $350k notional is tiny relative to his account size; lack of multi-day entry pattern suggests this is not a phasing-in move typical of his style.
- **Support level** [02:28]: GME respecting ~$19.50–$20.50 range as new floor; prior technical support drawn at $20.50 from longer-term price action.
- **Verdict** [10:00]: Strike selection and 5,000-lot size match his habits; timing (end-of-day entry) fits; but short duration, small position, and absence of multi-day scaling diverge significantly. Host leans toward "not him" but acknowledges mixed signals.
