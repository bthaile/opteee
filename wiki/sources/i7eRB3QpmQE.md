# Are your spreads actually safe? #optionstrading #investing

## Summary

Risk-defined spreads protect against catastrophic single-trade losses but do not guarantee profitability. A trade with risk defined can still erode capital slowly over time if it lacks a solid underlying profit mechanism and positive expected return. The mental model that "risk defined = safe" is a dangerous misconception that can lead to systematic losses.

## Key takeaways

- **Risk-defined ≠ safe** [00:00] — Risk-defined strategies prevent catastrophic blowups on individual trades but do not prevent slow capital erosion through repeated unprofitable trades.
- **Death by a thousand paper cuts** [00:00] — Even with defined risk, you can systematically lose money if the underlying strategy lacks a solid profit mechanism.
- **Profit mechanism is foundational** [00:45] — A risk-defined approach applied to a strategy without positive expected return will still lose money; it just loses more slowly.
- **Mental model matters** [00:24] — Conflating "risk defined" with "safe" is a critical error that can lead traders to execute unprofitable strategies with false confidence.

## Notable quotes

> "Risk defined equals safe. It's literally the same thing I just showed you. Risk defined is safe from catastrophic moves, which is fantastic. It's a very actually important function to something like vertical spreads, but it doesn't equal safe."

> "If you're trading something that isn't a solid profit mechanism that doesn't have positive expected return long-term, it doesn't matter if you trade it risk defined. You're just going to lose money more slowly, but you're still going to lose money."
