# Portfolio Update 12% ROC - Small Stacks Pt11

## Summary

Eric reviews the Small Stacks account (started 1 September) showing a 12% return on capital deployed. He closed an XLE short put at ~20% capture, established a ratio-call diagonal in Ford, adjusted a Kroger diagonal by adding a second short call, and maintains 40% cash reserves. The portfolio is structured for capital efficiency in a small account, relying on diagonals and short premium strategies rather than cash-secured puts due to sizing constraints.

## Key takeaways

- **XLE short put closed** [00:21–01:09]: Took down the 29 puts at 58¢ capture (~20% return in ~8 days); preferred not to hold XLE long-term despite typical hold duration.
- **Ford ratio-call diagonal established** [02:22–04:07]: Bought 3× Jan 2022 21 calls at $3.02 (88 delta), sold 2× 30 Oct 7 calls at 36¢ (53 delta); uncovered structure targeting high probability of close above strike; ~8% ROIC; current Ford price ~$6.98.
- **Kroger diagonal adjusted** [04:29–04:55]: Added second short call when premium spiked above $1.25 on the 35 Nov call; now 1:1 ratio with $185 credit outstanding; maintains zero upside risk.
- **Portfolio metrics** [05:17–07:16]: $5,682 total capital; ~$2,300 deployed (40% utilization); 12% return since 1 September; $124 in outstanding credit (~2.2% additional return potential); majority of gains from diagonals.
- **Capital deployment strategy** [05:43–06:26]: Difficulty finding cash-secured puts that fit account size; shifting toward diagonals for cost efficiency; considering long VXX calls if volatility capitulates.
