# Market Update: Powell Comments, Rate Cut Expectations & Sector Rotation

## Summary

Jerome Powell's mid-session comments on 2025 rate cuts triggered a ~2% intraday dip in the S&P 500, resetting market expectations from 50 bps to 25 bps cuts at a measured pace. The host reviews sector rotation dynamics—strength in communications (XLC) and utilities (XLU) versus weakness in technology—and outlines a framework for identifying tradable opportunities across both bullish and bearish setups using price action and moving-average alignment.

## Key takeaways

- **Powell's measured-pace messaging [00:59–04:45]**: Fed signaled 25 bps cuts (not 50 bps) at a data-dependent, methodical pace; market had front-run expectations and repriced accordingly. Recession probability remains elevated (25–50% for 2025).
- **Sector strength hierarchy [05:48–08:03]**: XLC and XLU making new highs for weeks; XLK consolidating near highs but showing tentative reversal; XLV and XLP weakening or consolidating at key levels.
- **Tech (QQQ) setup [06:54–08:03]**: Higher-low pattern suggests potential reversal, but failed breakout above prior high; moving averages consolidating; host is "tentatively long" QQQ futures pending conviction above resistance.
- **Trade both sides [08:03–09:01]**: Identify strength (higher highs, higher lows) and weakness (lower highs, lower lows) independently; options traders benefit from bidirectional exposure.
- **Data dependency ahead [04:45]**: Employment, inflation, and economic data will be "hot button items" until recession clarity emerves; watch for broad market data releases.

## Notable quotes
