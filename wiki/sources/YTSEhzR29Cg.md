# Structured Options Trade Management

## Summary

This video outlines a systematic framework for managing options positions before entering a trade. Rather than improvising management decisions mid-trade, the approach emphasizes defining the profit mechanism first, testing different management styles (closing for loss, rolling, defining risk), and codifying the approach that works best. The core principle is to build the strategy before flying the plane, not during.

## Key takeaways

- **Define profit mechanism first** [00:24] — Start with the core profit driver (e.g., theta decay, directional move), then fit option structures around it, not the reverse.
- **Test management approaches systematically** [00:00] — Before trading live, test how closing for a loss, rolling, or defining risk performs across different market conditions.
- **Codify before trading** [00:44] — Once you understand what management style works for your profit mechanism, turn it into a repeatable strategy with clear rules.
- **Avoid reactive management** [00:24] — The common mistake is entering a trade and then asking "what should I do now?" This is building the plane while flying it.
- **Optimize after testing** [00:44] — Only after testing and understanding robustness should you optimize position sizing, entry timing, and other variables.

## Notable quotes

> "It's not building the plane as you're flying it where you're in the trade and then you're like, 'Oh, what should I do now?' It's so common, but it's the exact wrong way to do this." [00:24]

> "You start profit mechanism, then you fit structures to see what might make sense. You turn those into strategies. You test those strategies and you tinker with different management approach. Then you codify." [00:44]
