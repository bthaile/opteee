# Election Goldmine: How to Make Money from the Election

## Summary

Following Trump's 2024 election victory with Republican control of the presidency, Senate, and likely House, Eric outlines the policy landscape and its trading implications across two distinct timeframes: 90-day momentum plays (deregulation, energy, defense) and 6+ month thematic positions (tariffs, crypto, healthcare). The video maps sector rotation opportunities and emphasizes pragmatic long-equity positioning in a high-conviction political regime shift.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2024-11-09)

- **Political control landscape** [00:00–01:14]: Trump won 295 electoral votes; Republicans hold Senate (53 seats) and likely House (211 seats). Full Republican control enables rapid legislative execution in first two years.
- **Deregulation as immediate catalyst** [10:49]: Financial sector (XLF) gapped from ~46 to 49 on Nov 6; deregulation is already pricing in and will drive profitability.
- **Energy sector positioning** [12:05]: Oil & gas producers (KMI, Exxon) bounced on Nov 6 following decreased renewable emphasis; still in downtrend but gaining momentum.
- **Defense/Space Force** [12:05]: Lockheed Martin and Raytheon bounced on Nov 6 but remain weak post-earnings; defense sector prefers outcome but lacks near-term strength.
- **Crypto rally** [06:05]: Bitcoin hitting all-time highs on favorable crypto regulation expectations; crypto markets reacting very positively to election outcome.
- **Pharma weakness** [03:36–05:01]: Johnson & Johnson and pharma sector falling post-election due to RFK's expected scrutiny of healthcare; biotech and life sciences less impacted.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Two-timeframe framework** [10:49–13:05]: 90-day window for momentum trades (deregulation, energy, defense); 6+ months for thematic positioning (tariffs, policy clarity, sector rotation).
- **Sector rotation approach** [13:05]: Identify expanding sectors, find leaders within them; long equities remain strong trade given S&P 500 e-mini strength above 6,000.
- **Inflation headwind** [07:18]: Trump's growth-oriented policies are inherently inflationary; tariffs (especially China) pose systemic shock risk; phased implementation likely to avoid catastrophic disruption.
- **Tariff impact on consumer discretionary** [07:18]: Walmart, Target, and consumer staples vulnerable to Chinese tariff pass-through; utilities and real estate under pressure from rate expectations.
- **Policy clarity pending** [02:18]: Most Trump policies still at talking-point stage; detailed outlines coming soon will add clarity for positioning.
