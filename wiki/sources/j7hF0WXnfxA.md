# Market Recap: FOMC, META, CRWD & GME Position Adjustment

## Summary

Market rallied sharply on Powell's FOMC messaging suggesting rate cuts remain on the table if inflation continues to fall, with probability-weighted expectations favoring 25 bps cuts in September and November. [[meta]] posted strong earnings and AI guidance, [[crwd]] continued its breakdown with slowing sell volume, and [[gme]] showed early signs of recovery with volume node building at support. Eric adjusted his short call positions on GME by rolling them down and further out in time to optimize theta decay while maintaining upside exposure.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2024-07-31)

- **FOMC messaging & rate expectations** [01:35–03:31]: Powell reiterated "getting closer but not quite there" on rate cuts; market is pricing ~87.5% probability of 25 bps in September, ~68% in November, and ~99.3% probability of at least one cut by year-end. Eric suspects a pause in December to assess feedback rather than three consecutive cuts.
- **Russell 2000 volatility** [04:59–06:30]: [[iwm]] opened at ~2262, rallied to ~2320 (+58 pts), then closed at ~2273 (+11 pts)—a 100-point intraday swing driven by Powell's talk. Year-to-date +12.7% gain concentrated since July 11th as small caps front-run rate-cut expectations.
- **S&P 500 breadth** [06:56–08:03]: 72% of [[spy]] constituents above 50-day MA; [[spx]] broke through 50-day resistance with expanding net highs/lows and accelerating highs—bullish breadth configuration.
- **META earnings** [08:26–10:15]: Closed at $474.83, after-hours ~$508.50 (+$35). Raised 2024 capex guidance to $37–40B (from $35–40B); expects "significant" 2025 capex growth for AI. Meta AI on track to be most-used assistant by year-end. Options: 1.2M calls vs 900K puts; 103K calls vs 87K puts isolated to earnings.
- **CRWD breakdown & volume analysis** [11:01–13:48]: Continued lower; volume node building at current levels. Sale volume: 9.8M (July 29), 17M (July 30), 6.8M (July 31)—deceleration suggests potential consolidation. Eric managing short with trailing stops; expects inverse opportunity (rally) once news settles.
- **GME recovery setup** [14:17–22:08]: +1.21% close; volume node building in gap zone (bullish signal for future interest). Options: 676K calls vs 374K puts (2:1 ratio, bullish). August 16 expiration active; September 6 shows 103% IV (earnings on Sept 4 pre-market). 110K calls vs 37K puts transacted today. Eric rolled short 20-SEP calls down to ATM and further out (51 DTE) to reduce gamma while maintaining theta decay (~3–4 cents) and higher delta for consolidation/downside profit potential.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Zero-DTE volatility trading** [00:42–01:02]: Eric profited from [[zero-dte]] [[spx]] options during Powell's speech by managing positions mid-talk as volatility spiked.
- **Volume profile & support identification** [11:25–12:04]: Volume nodes mark prior point-of-control and current accumulation zones; absence of volume (gaps) often becomes future support/resistance as price drifts toward them.
- **Options chain liquidity & expiration cycles** [16:53–19:07]: Primary expirations (16 AUG: 220K calls vs 107K puts) show 2–3× the liquidity of secondary expirations (6 SEP: 48K calls vs 19K puts); traders migrate to next cycle as current expires.
- **Theta decay vs. gamma trade-off** [20:10–21:32]: Rolling short calls further out in time (51 DTE vs. 37 DTE) preserves theta decay (~3–4 cents daily) while reducing gamma sensitivity, allowing consolidation/downside profit without violent price swings.
- **Covered-call position management** [19:33–21:53]: Long shares + short calls is not a bearish bet; it's a premium-collection strategy. Rolling down and out extends duration, lowers delta, and allows tactical profit-taking on both legs during price movement.
