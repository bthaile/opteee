# High Win Rate Options Trap #optionstrading

## Summary

A direct critique of the common trader obsession with win rate as a primary performance metric. The video argues that expected return and strategy quality matter far more than win-rate percentage, and that chasing high win rates often leads to poor risk-adjusted outcomes and misallocated effort.

## Key takeaways

- **Win rate is a vanity metric** [00:00–00:31]: High win rate does not correlate with profitability; low-win-rate strategies with strong expected returns often outperform high-win-rate strategies with poor payoff ratios.
- **Focus on expected return, not win rate** [00:00]: Allocate effort to identifying strategies with good expected value, regardless of whether they win 30% or 80% of the time.
- **Avoid idolizing win rate** [00:31]: Treating win rate as the primary success criterion is "a massive, massive mistake that's really easy to prevent."
- **Strategy quality over frequency** [00:00]: A single high-expected-value trade beats many low-expected-value trades, even if the latter have higher win rates.

## Notable quotes
