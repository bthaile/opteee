# Being right vs Making money #investing #trading #stockmarket

## Summary

Eric argues that traders should prioritize profitability over being correct about market narratives. Understanding every economic relationship and headline is unnecessary noise; the focus should be on capital efficiency and execution rather than comprehensive knowledge of interdependencies.

## Key takeaways

- **Separate curiosity from profit motive** [00:00–00:21]: You don't need to know everything to make money. Learning about economics and markets is fine as a passion interest, but differentiate it from your trading edge.
- **Ignore daily news narratives** [00:21]: Media attempts to bubble everything into convenient daily narratives. This is noise that doesn't improve trading outcomes.
- **Capital and time efficiency** [00:00]: Chasing complete understanding of every market relationship is a poor return on your capital and time as a trader.
- **Focus on execution first** [00:00]: Always prioritize making money; curiosity and deeper learning come second.

## Notable quotes

> "Do you want to be right or do you want to make money?"

> "You don't need to know everything… the understanding of every single interdependency and relationship actually takes a ton of effort and it's not a great return on your capital or your time."
