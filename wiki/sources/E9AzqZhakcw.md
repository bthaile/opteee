# Enhancing the Wheel Strategy Puts

## Summary

This video presents a tactical refinement to the wheel strategy's short-put leg: replacing arbitrary profit-target management with **delta-band rebalancing**. Instead of closing puts at fixed profit percentages, traders maintain a target delta range (e.g., 25–30), rolling or closing positions when delta drifts below the lower band. This approach captures more upside during rallies while managing strike creep and assignment risk.

## Key takeaways

- **Delta-band management vs. fixed profit targets** [00:00–00:30]: Replace "close at X% profit in Y% time" with a rule like "roll back to 30 delta whenever the put falls below 25 delta." This is more responsive to market regime.
- **Upside capture during rallies** [00:30]: When the S&P rallies, a delta-managed short put generates significantly more premium than a position closed at a fixed profit target, because you stay in the trade longer.
- **Strike creep trade-off** [00:30]: The downside of delta-band management is that your short strike drifts progressively closer to spot price as you roll, increasing assignment risk if the market reverses.
