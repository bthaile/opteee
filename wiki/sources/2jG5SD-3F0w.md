# GameStop Covered Call ETF - Income ETFs Explained

## Summary

The IGME ETF is a covered-call income fund that holds synthetic long positions (deep ITM calls) in GameStop rather than shares outright, selling calls against them to capture volatility premium. The fund uses leverage and paired short puts to create a delta-neutral, capped-upside structure—a professional-grade strategy common among institutional income funds like JPMorgan's JEP and JEPQ, but rarely accessible to retail traders at this cost efficiency.

## Key takeaways

- **Synthetic long via deep ITM calls for leverage** [01:29–02:57]: IGME holds 351 call contracts (35,100 share equivalents) worth ~$63k in current market value, versus $87,651 to buy shares outright—demonstrating capital efficiency similar to a ratio call diagonal, but without the ratio component.

- **Paired short puts lock in delta-neutral structure** [04:21–05:38]: The fund pairs long calls with short puts at specific strikes to eliminate upside risk and cap profit potential. Without these puts, the position would have unhedged upside exposure—a critical risk management detail.

- **GameStop IV percentile at historic lows despite high raw IV** [05:38–06:55]: GME shows 73.56% implied volatility but only 4% IV percentile (meaning volatility is near its lowest relative level in history), compared to Tesla at 68% IV / 54% percentile and Apple at 29.8% IV / 62% percentile. This richness justifies the premium-capture strategy.

- **Flex options and synthetic pairings are institutional standard** [08:13–09:14]: JPMorgan's JEP and JEPQ use identical synthetic structures with equity-linked notes and paired positions. Regulation requires clear pairings to prevent risk-reward distortion; this is not unique to GME.

- **High fees (0.98%) reflect complexity but remain accessible** [06:55–08:13]: The fund's expense ratio is above average but justified by the institutional-grade synthetic construction and daily rebalancing required to maintain delta neutrality.

- **Retail traders can replicate cheaper but with operational friction** [09:14]: Building this position manually is simpler and cheaper than the ETF wrapper, but the fund offers simplicity and regulatory compliance for institutional mandates.
