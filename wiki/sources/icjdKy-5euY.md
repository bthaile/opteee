# The Real Reason Tom Sosnoff Left TastyLive

## Summary

Tom Sosnoff discusses his departure from TastyLive after 15 years building the company and 4 years post-acquisition by IG. A serial entrepreneur who has spent 44 years working for himself, Sosnoff explains that loss of operational control following the 2021 sale, combined with IG's refusal to let him run the company globally, prompted his decision to leave on his own terms and pursue new ventures as a lead investor.

## Key takeaways

- **Entrepreneurial cycle**: Sosnoff has been self-employed for 44 years; the only paychecks he received were from TD Ameritrade (post-thinkorswim acquisition) and IG (post-TastyLive acquisition) [00:00–01:18]
- **Loss of control as dealbreaker**: After acquisitions, Sosnoff loses operational autonomy; he explicitly asked TD Ameritrade's CEO Fred Tomzack if he could become CEO and was told "not over my dead body" [01:18–02:24]
- **TastyLive origin**: Built as a streaming network starting in 2010 with initial backing from TD Ameritrade (Fred Tomzack invested $20M on Sosnoff's vision alone) [02:24–04:16]
- **12-year tenure at thinkorswim, 15 years at TastyLive**: Sosnoff stayed through 2025, lasting 4 years post-2021 IG acquisition despite loving the work [04:16–05:35]
- **Attempted to run IG's company**: Approached the IG board proposing he run the company globally; they declined and offered him a US-only role, which he rejected [05:35–06:32]
- **Exit timing**: Decided to leave after 4 years post-sale when he could finally liquidate stock and pursue a new company where he is a lead investor [06:32]

## Notable quotes

> "After you get bought out, you lose control. And it's kind of a weird thing."

> "I'm getting older, I got to build some stuff."

> "It's probably the best brokerage firm on the planet. You know, I still use the platform 50 to 100 times a day. But it was just time for me to go out on my own terms."
