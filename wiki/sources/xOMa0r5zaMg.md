# Economic Disarray with Doomberg | The Outlier Podcast Ep53

## Summary

Doomberg returns to discuss three major geopolitical flashpoints—the Israel-Hamas conflict and risk of Middle East escalation, the Ukraine war and potential regime collapse, and US-China tensions over Taiwan and semiconductors—and their second- and third-order consequences for energy markets and commodity pricing. The conversation emphasizes how market prices (particularly oil) may be underestimating tail risks, and explores the structural vulnerabilities the US has created by ceding manufacturing and supply-chain control to China.

## Key takeaways

### Geopolitical risk & energy markets
- **Middle East escalation**: Oil markets may be underpricing the tail risk of conflict spreading beyond Gaza to Iran via Hezbollah or direct US intervention; WTI in the high 70s suggests contained scenario, but narrow Strait chokepoints create catastrophic downside [09:57–10:16]
- **Ukraine war trajectory**: Western media overstates Ukrainian progress; Zelenskyy's team is panicked; 20 months in, neither side achieved expected outcomes; US military supply constraints now split between Israel and Ukraine [42:04–44:27]
- **China-Taiwan semiconductor vulnerability**: Taiwan's dominance is built on decades of institutional knowledge and artisanal technician expertise that cannot be quickly replicated; US timelines for domestic chip production are vastly optimistic [30:57–35:49]

### US strategic positioning
- **Supply-chain capture**: China controls ~100% of rare-earth processing, 97%+ of solar ingot/wafer/mounting; this stems from allowing domestic pollution as an undeclared subsidy, not inherent advantage [37:14–38:54]
- **Undeclared wars & constitutional erosion**: Modern US military actions bypass Congressional declaration; this breaks the post-WWII precedent and removes accountability [25:38–26:54]
- **Impeachment as partisan tool**: Overuse has desensitized the public; no crime would now convince a president's party to support removal [27:37–29:44]

### Content & platform strategy (Doomberg)
- **Twitter/X exodus**: Elon Musk's algorithmic throttling of Substack links caused 90% impression collapse; Doomberg pivoted to Substack ownership of email list and direct subscriber relationship [01:20–02:58]
- **Email as asset**: Direct email access to ~200k subscribers is far more valuable than social-media follows; Substack's 10% take is justified by infrastructure, discoverability, and comment-section engagement [04:12–06:00]

### Energy focus going forward
- **Peak ESG & nuclear renaissance**: Doomberg's primary lens is second- and third-order energy consequences of geopolitical events; AI/computing power race may normalize nuclear in the West [50:39–52:12]
