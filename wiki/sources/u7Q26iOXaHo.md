# Market Update: September 11th Reflection, Tech Rally, and Chewy Earnings

## Summary

A broad market update covering three topics: a September 11th reflection on geopolitical stability, a technical analysis of the NASDAQ rally and Nvidia consolidation, and an examination of Chewy's sympathy move following Petco's strong earnings beat. The host emphasizes cautious positioning in tech despite recent strength, noting the market remains emotionally driven and dependent on external economic factors.

## Key takeaways

- **September 11th context** [00:00–02:48]: Reminder of geopolitical fortune and the importance of remembering that stability is not guaranteed; context for understanding broader market sentiment and risk.

- **DJT technical breakdown** [02:48–03:57]: DJT has collapsed to near IPO lows in a vicious downtrend; downside appears exhausted near the 14 handle, but not a primary focus.

- **QQQ six-month structure** [03:57–06:08]: Key support level is critical; compression between lower low and higher low is tighter (~1 month) than prior move (~4 months), suggesting caution. Host added NASDAQ futures position today but remains sensitive to failed breakouts above near-term resistance.

- **Nvidia consolidation** [07:06–08:24]: Despite brutal August selloff (128 → 101), Nvidia is consolidating well with higher lows; still dependent on external economic factors; host setting alerts to monitor upside performance.

- **Sector rotation** [08:24–09:34]: Energy fades continue to work; XLF caught a bite and sold off; XLU, XLR, XLP holding; XLK (tech) rallying but not out of the woods.

- **Chewy earnings sympathy** [09:34–11:58]: Petco beat EPS expectations (−2¢ vs. −8¢ expected) and rallied 32.9%; Chewy moved in sympathy. Roaring Kitty's post timing is notable but unclear if he caught the move; no refiled 13G yet. Petco's strong guidance and capex investment drove the move despite slight revenue decline YoY.
