# The First Law of Trading by Agustin Lebron

## Summary

Agustin Lebron introduces a foundational principle of trading psychology: traders are structurally unhappy with position sizing regardless of outcome. If a trade loses, the trader wishes they had sized smaller; if it wins, they wish they had sized larger. This paradox reveals a core tension in trading that no mechanical system can resolve.

## Key takeaways

- **The First Law paradox** [00:21–00:38]: Bad trades create regret over not sizing to zero; winning trades create regret over not sizing larger. This dissatisfaction is inevitable and structural to trading.
- **Book genesis as training material** [00:00–00:21]: The book emerged from principles-based teaching for interns and new hires, prioritizing conceptual depth over mechanical profit signals.
- **Audience awareness** [00:00]: Lebron consciously wrote for a small audience interested in principles rather than obvious money-making tactics.

## Notable quotes

> "If it was a bad trade you should have done zero and if it was a good trade you should have done it bigger right so no matter what happens you're going to be unhappy."
