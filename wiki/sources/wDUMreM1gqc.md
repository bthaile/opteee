# Trade More Methodically Or Friction Will Kill Your Gains

## Summary

This video examines how excessive trading frequency erodes profitability through commission drag, even when gross P&L appears strong. The core insight: a trader generating $250k over 1,000 trades faces ~25% friction per trade, while the same $250k over 10,000 trades incurs 10× higher total commissions. Methodical trade selection and position sizing are essential to ensure edge survives transaction costs.

## Key takeaways

- **Commission-to-P&L ratio as a diagnostic** [00:27] — Calculate total commissions divided by individual trade P&L to expose whether you're trading too frequently relative to per-trade profitability. A 25% friction ratio signals overtrading.
- **Scale matters more than frequency** [00:00] — Two traders with identical $250k gains can have vastly different commission burdens (250 vs. 2,500) depending on trade count; fewer, larger trades preserve edge.
- **Friction context informs strategy design** [00:58] — Understanding your total friction relative to per-trade basis immediately reveals whether your process is sustainable or self-defeating.
