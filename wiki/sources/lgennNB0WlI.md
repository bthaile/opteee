# Sizing speculative options trades

## Summary

Options add leverage but require the same disciplined position-sizing methodology as equities. Size speculative trades by calculating the loss at your stop level, then determine position quantity such that the total risk aligns with your portfolio risk tolerance—typically 0.5–3% for most traders, occasionally up to 10%.

## Key takeaways

- **Apply equity sizing logic to options** [00:00–00:22]: Set a stop below near-term support (e.g., $216 on Tesla), calculate the loss per contract at that stop, then size accordingly.
- **Typical speculative allocation: 0.5–3% of portfolio** [00:45]: The overwhelming majority of speculative trades should risk between half a percent and 3% of total portfolio value; occasional outliers may reach 10%.
- **Size before entry, not after** [01:09]: Determine position size based on your strategy and risk tolerance upfront; do not enter a trade and "see how it goes."
