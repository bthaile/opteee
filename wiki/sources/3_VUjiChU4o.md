# 0DTE Options Trading Performance

## Summary

Eric walks through zero-DTE SPX options trading results for 2023, comparing historical performance (2015 short straddles) against current-year data and his actual trading. The key insight: market regime changes require strategy pivots. He demonstrates that 20-delta short strangles with 300% max-loss management outperformed unmanaged positions, and he personally traded 121 contracts (89 winners) for ~$6,000 profit mid-year onward, emphasizing the importance of continuous optimization via detailed trading logs.

## Key takeaways

- **Historical baseline (2015):** 50-delta short straddles captured variance risk premium effectively when daily moves were small; 20-delta short strangles underperformed by ~$700 per lot [01:45–03:11]
- **2023 regime shift:** Daily price movements are significantly larger than 2015; this structural change broke the old short-straddle playbook [03:30–03:53]
- **Winning 2023 setup:** 20-delta short strangle with 300% max-loss management produced 152–153 winning trades (out of ~154 total) and capped max loss at ~$3,360 vs. $5,623 unmanaged [05:20–06:36]
- **Tail-cutting mechanics:** Applying loss limits removes catastrophic outliers without eliminating most winners; only ~5 trades were artificially stopped out [06:14–07:02]
- **Personal execution (May–Nov 2023):** 5-lot position sizing, 121 total trades, 89 winners, ~$6,000 net profit; large losses still occurred but infrequently [07:27–08:13]
- **Core lesson:** Continuous market monitoring and willingness to pivot strategy mid-year prevented drawdown; systematic traders who never adapt often fail [00:24–00:42, 08:29]

## Notable quotes

> "What we did before might not work now and that's the point." [00:42]

> "Variance risk premiums is simply the tendency for implied volatility to trend over realized volatility—it's a sort of systematic alpha and one of the best possible things retail traders can expose themselves to because it is really persistent." [02:07–02:26]

> "You might have your typical way of deploying which is great, however you have to stay sensitive to the current market conditions because what worked before doesn't always continue to work in the future." [08:29]
