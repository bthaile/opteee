# Placing The Trade Is Easy If You Do Your Homework #optionstrading

## Summary

Execution becomes effortless when the preparatory work—research, scoping, and intuitive understanding of the instrument—is done thoroughly beforehand. The trader's emotional discipline and decision-making quality are determined by the quality of homework, not by willpower at the moment of entry. If you struggle with greed, discipline, or position management, the root cause is incomplete pre-trade analysis, not a character flaw.

## Key takeaways

- **Homework precedes execution** [00:00–00:25]: All the hard cognitive work happens before you place the trade. When you understand *why* the trade exists and how the instrument behaves, execution becomes automatic.
- **Emotional discipline is downstream of preparation** [00:25–00:42]: Struggles with greed, taking winners too early, or letting losers run signal that the research phase was incomplete—not that you lack discipline.
- **The trader is the biggest variable** [00:00]: Your psychology and execution quality are the critical control points; mastering them requires mastering the setup first.

## Notable quotes

> "If you do the rest of this well, execution literally becomes easy."

> "When you know the thing that you're trading is well researched, you've scoped it and you have an intuitive understanding of how it behaves, you just do it."
