# GameStop to Buy eBay? | Ep60

## Summary

Eric analyzes the rumored acquisition of eBay by GameStop, examining the strategic rationale, financial mechanics, and options market positioning. While assigning low probability to deal closure, he notes the market's serious reaction via volatility spikes and unusual options activity, particularly a concentrated 91,000-contract position in 30-strike June calls. The analysis focuses on tradable catalysts driven by order flow rather than deal outcome.

## Key takeaways

- **Deal structure & probability** [07:32–09:16]: GameStop (~$11B market cap) pursuing eBay (~$46–50B) would require a premium (10–30%), likely $58–60B total consideration. Offer probability estimated ~50%+; deal closure probability single-digit percent. A 13D filing and meaningful stake accumulation would signal seriousness.

- **Strategic rationale** [11:03–13:51]: eBay is profitable, growing 6–19% YoY, with 135–136M active buyers and 2.5B listings. GameStop's loyal shareholder base and PSA grading collectibles expertise could complement eBay's scaled marketplace; synergy exists but eBay has no current incentive to sell.

- **Volatility spike & market seriousness** [28:41–30:19]: GameStop IV percentile jumped to 67%; 30-day realized vol spiked from 39 to 59 on Friday. Term structure across entire curve shifted. Market reaction signals genuine catalyst, not noise.

- **Unusual options positioning** [31:13–42:40]: 934k calls vs 292k puts; 91,000 contracts concentrated in 30-strike June expiration (10% of total call OI). Day-over-day OI expansion of 10,000 in that strike. 34% of Friday transactions at offer or above (aggressive buying). Sustained accumulation pattern (not day-trading) suggests informed positioning.

- **Order flow analysis** [43:27–49:59]: 30-strike June calls traded throughout Friday in 200–250 contract blocks, likely same entity. Positions split across day starting 6:30 AM. Sweep activity concentrated in 30-call. Position sizes in millions but not at Roaring Kitty scale.

- **Current position management** [52:20–52:52]: Covered strangle still active; short-term trading around Friday's catalyst; no evidence of cat-like (Roaring Kitty) involvement based on position size and timing.
