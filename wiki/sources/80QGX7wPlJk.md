# Building Market Awareness & How to Start Trading Options w/ Tom Sosnoff | The Outlier Podcast

## Summary

Tom Sosnoff reflects on a flat 2024 marked by contrarian positioning in bonds, currencies, and range-bound trades, while discussing his worst trade (an ego-driven scale into [[nvda]]) and the dangerous precedent of political influence on markets. He outlines a framework for beginner traders—start small, participate actively, and prove repeatability—and warns of asymmetric downside risk (4–5:1) in current market conditions despite unanimous bullish consensus.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2024-12-26)

- **Contrarian year**: Long bonds, short dollar, range-bound positions paid all year; equities were "flat" overall [00:02–01:13]
- **Tesla and Nvidia stumbles**: Post-election Tesla gap-up reflects political loyalty pricing, not policy forecasting; Nvidia was ego-driven "angry size" scaling [01:13–04:21]
- **Broadcom (AVGO) earnings shock**: ~60-point move vs. ~13–15 expected; a $750B company gaining $350B in two days signals speculation/complacency [23:49–27:15]
- **Unanimous bullishness red flag**: Every analyst projects +10–20% for 2025; such unanimity historically precedes reversals [29:24–30:42]
- **Skew and put premium collapse**: Volatility skew at 170, no put skew (happens ~10–12% of the time); market pricing outlier risk that won't materialize [44:34]
- **Asymmetric risk**: Upside to ~6,300 SPX; downside to ~5,100 "almost overnight"—4:1 to 5:1 risk/reward favoring shorts [42:07–43:24]

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Ego in trading**: Scaling into losing positions on emotion ("angry size") is the fastest way to blow up; accept losses to worthy competitors [01:13–03:19]
- **Lump-sum vs. dollar-cost averaging**: Sosnoff is a "lump summer"—rarely adds to losers; scaling in is ego-driven and dangerous [02:21–03:19]
- **Fed number vs. earnings**: Both are binary events with no edge for outsiders; earnings premium collapses post-announcement, but bond premium does not [19:17–20:21]
- **Implied volatility and earnings**: Prefer selling premium when IV is high and expected moves >10%; low-vol, high-market environments make earnings plays unattractive [21:20–22:39]
- **Market efficiency**: Markets are more efficient today than 5–10 years ago; risk premiums have contracted [21:20]
- **Proof of concept for intermediate traders**: Must demonstrate repeatability and scalability; backtesting helps stress-test ideas across unseen market regimes [39:00–41:06]
- **Beginner framework**: Start with tiny size (1–2 shares, pennies on options), participate actively, and accumulate sample size; learning happens through doing, not watching [34:48–39:00]
- **Market regime awareness**: 2022 and 2024 saw bear markets; most traders since 2020 have only seen one downturn; lack of regime diversity creates blind spots [41:06–42:07]

## Notable quotes

> "I'm a contrarian so when you have a straight one-dimensional, one-directional market, sometimes that gets a little tough for me." [00:02]

> "I'm purely a lump summer—I don't dollar-cost average, I don't roll in over time. For me to add to a losing trade is incredibly rare." [02:21–03:19]

> "There's $4 or $5 of risk for every $1 of upside, and when I get my head in that space it's very difficult to think objectively." [42:07–43:24]
