# Inventory Preference - ITM Short Put Management

## Summary

This video covers the critical decision framework for managing in-the-money short puts: first validating that your original thesis remains intact, then determining your inventory preference—whether you actually want to take on shares at the current price or would prefer assignment at a lower level. This preference directly shapes how you manage the position through expiration.

## Key takeaways

- **Thesis validation comes first** [00:00]: Before making any management decision on an ITM short put, objectively evaluate whether your original thesis for the underlying is still valid. If it's broken, close the position and move on.
- **Inventory preference is the second input** [00:21]: Once thesis is confirmed, ask yourself: do I want to take on shares at this price, or would I prefer to own them at a lower price? This preference directly determines your management approach.
- **Preference shapes management** [00:21]: If you already own shares and would rather not add at the current level, your inventory preference is to avoid assignment—which changes how you handle the ITM short put.
