# "Long" Options #optionstrading #trading #stockmarket

## Summary

This educational segment clarifies the distinction between being "long" an option in transactional terms (having bought it) versus the directional disposition of the trade. A trader can be long a put option while maintaining a bearish market outlook, illustrating that transaction direction and market bias are independent concepts.

## Key takeaways

- **Long vs. short as transaction:** "Long" means you *bought* the option; "short" means you *sold* it [00:00]
- **Disposition vs. transaction:** Being long a put is a bullish *transaction* but bearish *disposition*—you profit if the market falls [00:25]
- **Avoid conflating terms:** New traders often confuse whether "long" refers to how you entered the trade or your market outlook [00:00]
