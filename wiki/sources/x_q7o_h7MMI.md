# The VIX Explained in 60 seconds #shorts

## Summary

The VIX is a forward-looking volatility index derived from S&P 500 options that measures expected volatility over the next 30 days. Unlike equities, VIX cannot be bought or sold directly, but options on the VIX can be traded. Volatility products exhibit parabolic price behavior and require careful risk management.

## Key takeaways

- **VIX construction**: Derived from SPX options; measures expected 30-day volatility [00:00]
- **No direct ownership**: VIX has no underlying stock; cannot be bought or sold as a security [00:31]
- **Options trading available**: VIX options can be traded for those interested in volatility exposure [00:31]
- **Parabolic behavior**: Volatility products move in parabolic fashion, requiring disciplined risk management [00:31]
