# GOOGL, TSLA, & Earnings - Stock Market Update | Options Trading Live

## Summary

Live market update covering earnings season with deep dives into Tesla and Google earnings releases, plus tactical analysis of recent high-volatility trades in KSS and OPEN. The host demonstrates how to extract edge from earnings-driven volatility moves, emphasizing profit mechanisms over directional bias and the importance of volatility pricing in options strategies.

## Key takeaways

- **Earnings as uncorrelated return source** [07:16–08:13]: Options traders can remove directional element and focus on volatility behavior around earnings (pre-release, during, post-release), with clear price behavioral trends to capitalize on.
- **Tesla fundamentals deteriorating** [22:01–24:18]: Stock down ~4% on the day; technical setup shows lower highs and higher lows (weak pattern). Earnings reveal 16% YoY decline in energy generation/storage revenue, 13% YoY decline in vehicle deliveries, and margin compression from competitive pricing pressure (Powerwall prices cut 10–15%).
- **Energy business headwinds** [29:28–36:43]: Tesla's energy ASP declining 15–20% due to strategic pricing and competition from BYD and CL; Megapack facing supply chain delays. Gross profit growth insufficient to offset revenue decline.
- **Google earnings solid** [56:10–01:01:05]: Net income up 19% YoY, operating income up 14%; cloud expenditures surging. Position (ratio call diagonal + bullish earnings play) performing well post-earnings.
- **Bloom Energy (BE) consolidating** [08:39–17:55]: 89% YoY return but options chain quiet; call skew present but positions modest (~30k–17k across expirations). Consolidating above 24 handle; not compelling vs. stronger momentum names.
- **KSS and OPEN as volatility gifts** [05:35–06:00]: Extreme moves (2.6–3.6 standard deviations) create tradeable opportunities; can sell puts to profit from V collapse even as stock drops if vega exceeds delta impact. Front-month IV collapsed from ~300% to ~170% on relative basis.
- **Strategy refinement via logging** [01:01:29–01:02:45]: Cutting losing tails more aggressively than winning tails yields net positive impact; track standard deviation moves to refine screening and reduce tail frequency.
- **Portfolio risk allocation** [01:08:41–01:09:09]: Speculative trades typically 0.5–2% portfolio risk max; maintain sliding scale between core and speculative buckets.
- **HIMS gap down** [01:10:27–01:13:40]: Massive gap down on 23rd (partnership deal crush); 81.8M volume on close; insider selling at higher levels; not informed positioning, mostly reactive trading.

## Notable quotes
