# Strategy Outlines - Small Stacks Pt3

## Summary

Eric outlines his core strategy framework for small-account trading: cash-secured puts, covered calls, covered strangles, diagonals, and selective verticals. He emphasizes delta selection (0.2–0.3 for puts), minimum 1% return on invested capital, disciplined rolling to avoid net liquidating value erosion, and replacement-trade requirements before closing profitable positions.

## Key takeaways

- **Put-side mechanics** [00:26]: Target 0.2–0.3 delta on short puts to balance assignment probability and rolling frequency; require minimum 1% return on invested capital.
- **Covered call discipline** [00:49]: Sell calls only above cost basis; hold unless downside management triggers (>10% drawdown).
- **Profit management** [01:13]: Close at 55% max profit or within 17–18 days to expiration; avoid rolling excessively on small accounts to prevent net liquidating value decay.
- **Replacement-trade requirement** [02:01]: Only close a profitable trade if a suitable replacement offering larger dollar credit is identified; otherwise hold to expiration.
- **Rolling cost warning** [02:44]: Each roll books a loss against net liquidating value; repeated rolling can hollow out account equity despite positive cash sweep.
- **Diagonal structure** [03:48]: Long leg minimum 90 days to expiration (target 6 months or LEAP); long strike 80–90 delta to eliminate upside risk and enable aggressive short-call selling.
- **Diagonal scaling** [05:20]: Buy at least two long options; establish half upfront, add second leg opportunistically.
- **Diagonal selection** [05:48]: Target securities trading near or below 3–6 month mean with strong fundamentals.
- **Verticals** [06:10]: Use sparingly for strong directional conviction; target 30+ cents credit on dollar-wide spreads; expect good expectancy before entry.
