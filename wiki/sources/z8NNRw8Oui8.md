# Ambiguity #optionstrading #trading #stockmarket

## Summary

Trading rarely offers discrete answers; success requires clarity about your objective and selecting the strategy that fits it. Buying premium makes sense when you expect large directional moves (profit mechanism scales with volatility), while selling premium suits different regimes. The key is intentional strategy selection rather than defaulting to a single approach.

## Key takeaways

- **Clarity of intent is foundational** [00:00–00:24]: Define what you're trying to accomplish (directional move, income, hedge) before choosing a strategy.
- **Match profit mechanism to market view** [00:24–00:45]: If you expect a large move, buying premium (despite higher cost) preserves upside; selling caps profit potential and may not fit your thesis.
- **Avoid default thinking** [00:45]: Don't default to "I buy options" or "I sell options"—evaluate each trade on its merits and regime.
- **Context determines tool selection** [00:45]: There is a time for both buying and selling; the best traders adapt strategy to their edge and market conditions.
