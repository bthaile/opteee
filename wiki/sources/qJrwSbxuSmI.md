# Selling Options is NOT an Edge

## Summary

The video challenges the widespread misconception that selling options or collecting theta automatically constitutes a trading edge. While selling options *can* be profitable, the statistical foundation commonly cited—that 90% of options expire worthless—is factually incorrect. The majority of options are traded before expiration, and among those reaching expiration, more expire worthless than in-the-money, but this does not guarantee profitability for sellers.

## Key takeaways

- **The 90% myth is wrong** [00:00–00:27]: The Options Clearing Corporation data shows the overwhelming majority of options are traded before expiration, not held to worthlessness. This invalidates the most common justification for selling options as an edge.
- **Theta alone is not an edge** [00:00]: Confusing a mechanism (theta decay) with an edge is a critical error; many sellers lose money despite time decay working in their favor.
- **Selling options requires edge beyond the mechanism** [00:27]: Simply executing short-premium strategies without a documented edge or risk framework is not a viable approach.
