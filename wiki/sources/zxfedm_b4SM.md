# The Assignment Trap in Options Trading

## Summary

This video examines the psychological pitfall of pre-committing to assignment acceptance without stress-testing actual emotional and capital responses. Traders often rationalize assignment ("I'd love to own it lower") but fail to account for how they'll behave when the underlying continues to deteriorate, leaving them unable to execute follow-up strategies like call selling.

## Key takeaways

- **Goldilocks analysis trap** [00:00–00:28]: Traders overemphasize the upside scenario of assignment while underweighting the practical reality of how they'll react if the stock continues falling after they take assignment.
- **Put-selling example** [00:28–00:49]: Selling puts on a stock you like (e.g., SMCI) because you expect upside, then taking assignment as it drops—only to find the stock falls further, leaving you unable to sell calls against the position.
- **Capital and optionality loss** [00:49]: Once assigned and underwater, you lose the flexibility to execute secondary strategies (e.g., covered calls), trapping capital in a deteriorating position.
- **Process discipline over wishful thinking**: Separate your *stated* assignment tolerance from your *actual* behavioral response under stress; test this gap before committing to short-premium strategies.

## Notable quotes
