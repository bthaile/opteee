# Using Probability Cones To Predict Movements

## Summary

This video explains how to construct probability cones using implied volatility to forecast price movements across different time horizons. By scaling volatility according to the square root of time, traders can estimate the range of expected moves at one standard deviation (68% probability) and use these bands to speculate on directional outcomes over specific durations.

## Key takeaways

- **Probability cone construction** [00:00]: Create bands around spot price by multiplying IV by the square root of time (in years), giving wider ranges for longer durations.
- **Volatility scaling formula** [00:44]: Expected move = Spot Price × IV × √(Duration / 365). Use 365 days (not trading days) because IV is annualized across all calendar days.
- **One standard deviation band** [00:00]: Within a 68% probability band, you can speculate on how frequently price will touch different parts of the cone over your chosen time frame.
- **Time-horizon dependency** [00:25]: Longer durations produce wider cones; volatility scales with √time, not linearly.
