# Option probabilities

## Summary

Options are probabilistic tools that encode market consensus about future price movement. While delta is often used as a shorthand for probability of expiration in-the-money, the actual mathematical relationship between delta and probability diverges significantly beyond 20 days to expiration. Options chains provide direct access to these probability estimates for any target price level.

## Key takeaways

- **Delta as probability proxy works only short-term** [00:00–00:26]: Delta approximates probability of finishing in-the-money for options under ~20 days to expiration; beyond that window, the relationship breaks down materially.
- **Options encode consensus price targets** [00:26–00:42]: You can query an options chain to answer "what is the probability this stock reaches 105?" — the market's collective view is embedded in the option prices themselves.
- **Options are fundamentally probabilistic instruments** [00:00]: The entire options market functions on probability; understanding this unlocks their utility beyond directional speculation.
