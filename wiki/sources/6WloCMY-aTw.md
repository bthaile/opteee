# Nvidia Earnings, Tesla Positioning, GameStop Bitcoin & Market Tariffs

Discussion includes tariff court ruling implications, earnings-driven positioning mechanics, and volatility term structure inversions across major tech names.

- **Nvidia earnings context** [00:00–02:00]: Revenue beat at $44B (440 pallets of $100 bills for scale); data center segment up 73% YoY to $39.1B, slightly below analyst expectations of $39.36B yet market reacting positively. Call-heavy positioning with some unwind; net premium shows downside spikes but overall flat-to-bullish bias. Consolidation pattern suggests appetite for move to all-time highs.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Earnings forward guidance vs. past metrics** [20:19–21:44]: Past earnings (revenue, EPS) are largely recounting what happened and pass if "close enough" to expectations. Forward guidance is net-new information and primary driver of post-earnings moves; more speculative than backward-looking metrics.
- **Implied volatility percentile vs. rank distinction** [34:39–36:23]: IV percentile at 71 indicates vol is objectively high; IV rank would incorrectly suggest vol is low. Calculation methodology makes material difference; high percentile = high vol environment.
- **Equity holder hedging vs. speculative positioning** [26:57–28:29]: Long-term equity holders buy puts near earnings to reduce risk without betting direction; speculative traders buy calls for upside. These are distinct cohorts with different risk profiles and time horizons.
- **Volatility term structure inversion** [39:09–41:08]: Abnormal inversion (peak at 23 DTE, then decline) signals near-term event risk; tail skew heavier on put side but not dramatically. Inversion typically resolves post-event.
- **Risk premium persistence despite realized moves** [39:09–41:08]: Tesla maintains aggressive risk premiums even with larger-than-expected realized moves; short premium strategies remain profitable from theta decay despite adverse price movement.
