# Trade Management as a Process #optionstrading

## Summary

This video emphasizes that options traders must adopt a systematic, process-driven approach to trade management rather than treating each trade as an isolated event. The core insight is that consistent adherence to a defined management process—applied uniformly across all trades—is what generates long-term edge, not ad-hoc salvage attempts on individual losing positions.

## Key takeaways

- **Process over outcome** [00:00–00:46]: Treat trade management as a repeatable process applied consistently across all positions, not as one-off tactical fixes. This consistency is what allows you to isolate what actually works over time.
- **Resist the salvage impulse** [00:25]: When a trade moves against you, the temptation to deploy different management tactics to "fix" it undermines your ability to measure what your strategy actually does. Stick to your plan.
- **Long-run context** [00:46]: Frame every management decision in the context of a strategy you expect to execute repeatedly over years, not a single trade you're trying to squeeze profit from.

## Notable quotes

> "When we trade options, it's very easy to get sucked into each individual trade. We need to treat the way that we manage trades in the context of this is a process that we're expecting to do over the long run, not something that we're just doing this one-off instance to make a few bucks."
