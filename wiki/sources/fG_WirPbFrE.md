# Ratio Call Diagonal Options Trade in AAPL Explained

## Summary

Eric executes a ratio call diagonal in AAPL using deep in-the-money long calls (130 strike, November expiration) paired with near-term short calls (175 strike, June expiration), risking $10,000 with 95% capital allocation. The trade captures directional upside while collecting premium from short calls, demonstrating superior risk-adjusted returns compared to a covered-call equivalent on the same capital base.

## Key takeaways

- **Trade structure & entry** [01:20–02:26]: Long two 130 calls (Nov expiry, ~90 delta) at $47.45; short two 175 calls (June expiry, ~42 delta) at $2.38. Entered May 10 after confirming support at a key volume node; initial stop at 164.47.
- **Directional setup** [02:56–04:44]: Post-earnings gap held; linear regression channels and moving averages aligned bullish. Waited four days to confirm price action before entry; used stock-level stops (not premium-based) tied to the directional hypothesis.
- **Adjustment & lot reduction** [06:35–08:03]: On May 26, when short calls went ITM but volatility contracted and theta decayed, rolled from two-lot June 175 calls to one-lot June 9 175 calls, collecting $3.27 and unlocking additional upside potential.
- **Comparison to covered call** [08:49–10:40]: Same $94.90 capital buys only 54 shares in a covered call (insufficient for 100-share requirement); ratio diagonal P&L is $900 vs. $340 for covered call equivalent, demonstrating leverage advantage when directional bias is correct.
- **Exit management options** [11:00–14:49]: Three choices: (1) roll short calls out in time at same or higher strike; (2) close short calls at loss and sell one long call to lock profit; (3) close entire position. Leaning toward rolling to extend max profit potential and capture remaining extrinsic value.
