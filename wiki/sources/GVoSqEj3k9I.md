# Matt Caruso on Finding Huge AI Stocks | The Outlier Podcast

## Summary

Matt Caruso, a growth-oriented trend follower, discusses his 2025 portfolio positioning around AI infrastructure plays, his process for identifying market leaders, and his tactical approach to navigating volatility. He emphasizes the importance of staying in cash during corrections to avoid drawdowns, using relative strength to confirm leadership, and maintaining disciplined position sizing with defined risk parameters. The conversation covers his framework for differentiating genuine AI revenue drivers from hype, his use of liquidity proxies like net highs/lows and high-yield bond spreads, and his view that the current market cycle mirrors the 1990s internet infrastructure phase.

## Key takeaways

- **Position structure**: Start each position at ~10% of portfolio, build to 25–30% with subsequent 10% purchases; typically hold 8–12 stocks early in a trend, narrowing to 5–6 core winners. Risk per position averages 4–5%; cumulative stop-loss exposure kept under 2% [08:06, 09:49].
- **Bimodal holding periods**: Most trades last ~2 weeks (chop/losers); smaller cohort of 3–4 month holds generates majority of annual profit [13:27].
- **Leader identification**: Leaders must outperform on upswings *and* fall less on downswings (not just high-beta volatility). Confirm with 2–3 week performance review; funnel capital into stocks satisfying both criteria with biggest gains [11:34, 12:25].
- **Trimming laggards**: Use time-based and performance filters; even 8–12% gains offset multiple 3–5% losers; build profit cushions in core names [12:25].
- **Risk/reward entry rule**: Require $5 profit per $1 risk; as stocks extend, entries become harder to justify [14:28].
- **Correction playbook**: Best entries occur when market breaks but leaders hold—strongest signal of institutional demand. Corrections free mental bandwidth from loss-management [14:28, 27:17].
- **February–March 2025 execution**: Held well early, forced out as better names broke, went full cash by late February, stayed cash through "liberation day" tariff rollout, repositioned late April into core names [16:25, 17:11].
- **Current holdings** (as of early June): Palanteer ($93 cost basis), IBIT, GEV ($37.50 cost basis), Hood ($47 cost basis), Meta, NBIS; most with significant profit cushions [17:58].
- **AI stock filtering**: Start with ~50–60 names showing real AI revenue (not just marketing); filter for liquidity, relative strength vs. market, and institutional sponsorship signals [43:58, 45:47].
- **AI revenue validation**: Examine what % of sales is genuinely AI-driven; look for 100%+ YoY growth in AI segments even if overall earnings are negative; require actual product sales, not pre-revenue [42:14, 43:06].
- **Liquidity proxies**: Track net highs vs. lows (correlates with NFCI from Chicago Fed); monitor high-yield bond spreads (ICE BofA US High Yield Index effective yield) as risk/liquidity indicator; these confirm significant market moves [52:05, 53:54].
- **Market regime view**: Current cycle mirrors 1994 internet infrastructure phase (Cisco, not Yahoo); AI is still in power/electricity demand phase; autonomous vehicles and robotics will be first mass AI products [40:26, 41:19].
- **Tariff volatility & positioning**: Trump's deal-making style (massive threat, then walk-back) creates worst-case volatility for directional traders; V-shaped recovery driven by forced buying after extreme positioning; shallow consolidation likely before next leg [35:44, 38:23, 51:11].
- **FOMO indicator**: Tracks % of NASDAQ stocks above 5-day average; use to actively trim into strength (not weakness) at cycle tops [28:13].
- **Trend-end signals**: Climax tops are obvious; slow grinding tops require watching internal rotation (e.g., money flowing into Apple/safety) and P&L stalls despite new index highs [29:02, 30:50].
- **February 20–21 warning**: One day off all-time highs with more 52-week lows than highs = rare internal divergence; preceded sharp downside [31:46].

## Notable quotes
