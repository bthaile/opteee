# DJT Dumps While GME Surges: Stock Market Recap

## Summary

Market-wide euphoria post-election with broad sector strength across S&P 500, though notable divergence between consumer staples and discretionary. DJT exhibits extreme volatility (290% IV) and inverted put/call skew (875k puts vs 515k calls) following post-election gap-up and subsequent crash, signaling downside positioning. GME shows volume-driven breakout behavior with resistance at $25, supported by technical volume nodes and emerging call IV skew suggesting renewed upside expectations.

## Key takeaways

- **Broad market strength**: All primary S&P 500 sectors up; XLI (Industrials), XLF (Financials), XLE (Energy) near all-time highs; QQQ retakes 6-month summer high [00:00–01:12]
- **Russell 2000 catching up**: Small caps up ~19.4% YTD vs S&P 500 up ~26.7%; e-mini futures +0.15% on Sunday open despite bank holiday lighter volume [01:12]
- **DJT post-election collapse**: Gap-up Nov 6 (election known) followed by crash to lows; treated as election proxy play, now unknown is known [02:21]
- **DJT options skew extreme**: 875k puts vs 515k calls; IV 290%→260%→227% with inverted term structure (210→197→204); 38% of Friday call volume at offer or above, suggesting some expect follow-through [03:25–04:35]
- **GME volume expansion precedes moves**: 2.6M → 5.9M → 13.6M volume progression; every major parabolic move preceded by volume expansion [05:36]
- **GME $25 resistance critical**: Volume nodes at $25–26 from prior buyers seeking break-even exit; closed $24.88; sellers eager but not in mass; larger volume node $20–23 below [06:53–08:00]
- **GME options normalization**: Near-term chain was "all sorts of messed up" on Saturday; already normalizing; 23-strike ~104.6 IV vs 15-Nov ~9.8; massive call IV skew returning, pricing upside [09:07–10:11]
- **Position management**: Host holds GME shares, short calls now underwater, added near-dated Jan calls vs longer-dated April calls; sliding stops up [09:07]
