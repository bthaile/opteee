# Improving Your Decision Making in Trading

## Summary

This video explores how to build robust decision-making frameworks in trading by layering contextual information around statistical patterns. The core tension is between adding meaningful context (e.g., post-earnings drift combined with consecutive green days) and avoiding overfitting to individual circumstances—a balance achieved through large sample-size validation.

## Key takeaways

- **Post-earnings drift + multi-day confirmation**: If you observe a gap-up on earnings followed by two consecutive green days, the historical propensity is for the stock to be up ~5% over the next five trading days [00:00]. This illustrates how to combine multiple signals into a single decision rule.
- **Robustness requires large sample sizes**: Adding context to your decision-making is valuable, but you must validate across a large sample to avoid overfitting to individual circumstances [00:28].
- **The overfitting trap**: Going too far down the path of contextual specificity leads to rules that work only for narrow, individual scenarios rather than generalizable edge [00:28].

## Notable quotes

> "You have to make sure what you're doing is robust. What that means is if I go too far down that path, then all of a sudden I'm just really overfit for individual circumstances, which is why it's important to look at things across a large sample size." [00:28]
