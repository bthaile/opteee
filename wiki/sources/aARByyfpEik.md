# Don't Wait Until Assignment #optionstrading

## Summary

Eric demonstrates a tactical shift from holding underwater equity to converting a position into short puts, allowing for active basis adjustment without additional capital deployment. The core insight: short puts offer flexibility in managing cost basis that static equity positions lack, enabling traders to avoid prolonged drawdowns through active management rather than passive assignment.

## Key takeaways

- **Convert equity to short puts for basis management** [00:00–00:27]: When holding shares at a loss, convert the entire position to short puts to unlock adjustability. Account for both the prior realized loss and the equity loss in the new position basis.
- **Short puts require no additional capital to adjust basis** [00:27–00:50]: Unlike dollar-cost averaging in equity (which requires fresh money), short-put adjustments can be made without adding capital, preserving liquidity.
- **Active management beats passive assignment** [00:50]: Don't wait for assignment to force a decision. Proactively buy and sell shares, and roll puts, to avoid being trapped in extended downtrends.
- **Basis was ~56 per share** [00:27]: The original equity position had accumulated a cost basis around that level before the conversion.
