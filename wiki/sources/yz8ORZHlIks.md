# Trade the Small Fish Edge - Live Like a King

## Summary

A foundational lesson on retail trading identity: the core edge for small traders lies in accepting structural differences from institutions rather than attempting to replicate their strategies. Success comes from building a sustainable, profitable lifestyle within the constraints and advantages of retail scale, not from competing directly in institutional game theory.

## Key takeaways

- **Embrace retail constraints as advantage** [00:00] — Retail traders operate in a fundamentally different game than institutions; attempting direct emulation leads to failure. The edge is in accepting and leveraging this difference.
- **Position sizing and lifestyle alignment** [00:00] — Build a trading approach that sustains your desired lifestyle without requiring institutional scale or capital. Profitability at retail size is achievable when expectations are calibrated correctly.
- **Avoid the trap of scale ambition** [00:30] — The moment a small trader tries to become a big fish, competitive disadvantages (capital, infrastructure, information speed) become fatal. Stay in your lane.

## Notable quotes

> "As soon as you're a little fish trying to be a big fish, that is when you get eaten. Don't get eaten."
