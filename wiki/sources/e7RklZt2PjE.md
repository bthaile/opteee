# Master 0DTE Options

## Summary

A comprehensive guide to zero-DTE (same-day expiration) options trading, covering the mechanics, retail trader pitfalls, and Eric's personal integration strategy. The video emphasizes that while zero-DTE options offer high gamma and theta, retail traders lose approximately $358,000 daily on SPX zero-DTE contracts due to transaction costs and fees consuming 40–60% of gross profits. Success requires selling premium in high IV–HV variance environments or around catalysts, not scalping or treating them as lotteries.

## Key takeaways

### Mechanics & Greeks [00:51–02:41]
- **Gamma on expiration day is extremely high** [01:08]: all options must resolve to either zero or one delta (OTM or ITM), benefiting long-option buyers and hurting sellers.
- **Theta decays very fast** [01:32]: OTM options approach zero bid-ask spreads (e.g., bid 0, offered 5 cents), creating massive relative spreads compared to later expirations.
- **No overnight risk** [03:03]: zero-DTE trades close by end of day, but this is not a reason to trade them exclusively; overnight risk is not the primary risk driver.

### The Retail Trader Problem [06:16–14:41]
- **Massive volume concentration** [06:16]: zero-DTE and 1-DTE options now dominate monthly trading volume, especially among retail traders (SLIM data).
- **Retail buys, institutions sell** [09:05]: retail traders centralize in buying single contracts; institutions favor selling, which is properly deployed.
- **Fees destroy profitability** [11:21]: even in profitable gross-profit periods, fees (commissions, slippage, payment-for-order-flow, non-competitive pricing) consume 40–60% of gains; in losing periods, net losses are catastrophic.
- **Four profitable months vs. nine gross-profitable months** [12:26]: after fees, aggregate profitability is cut in half month-over-month.
- **Retail loses ~$358,000/day on SPX zero-DTE** [14:41]: over 60% of daily losses come from transaction costs.
- **Scalping doesn't work for retail** [12:46]: intraday scalping requires sub-second execution (HFT domain); retail cannot compete on speed, and small profits are erased by fees.

### When to Trade Zero-DTE [15:04–17:03]
- **Prefer simple single options over spreads** [15:22]: avoid iron condors and verticals in zero-DTE; use short calls, short puts, short straddles, or short strangles to minimize bid-ask bleed.
- **Sell premium when IV > HV** [15:44]: trade when implied volatility is overstated relative to realized volatility (variance risk premium), not just when IV percentile or IV rank is high.
- **Limit frequency to catalysts** [16:44]: trade zero-DTE around Fed announcements, earnings releases, or other clear catalysts that offer a larger edge.
- **Two conditions only** [17:03]: (1) large IV–HV variance, or (2) event-driven catalyst. Do not trade zero-DTE for speed or to avoid overnight risk.
