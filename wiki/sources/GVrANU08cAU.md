# David Hunter Final Warning The Melt Up Before the Global Bust | The Outlier Podcast

## Summary

David Hunter presents a contrarian macro thesis: a final parabolic melt-up in equities (S&P 8,700, NASDAQ 30,000) over the next 3–6 months, followed by a severe global bust and deflationary cycle in 2026. He argues the bond market—not the Fed—will drive rates lower, sentiment remains subdued despite momentum, and precious metals will outperform alongside equities into the top. The key catalyst is a wall of worry that will fuel the final advance.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2025-07-23)

- **Melt-up targets revised upward** [04:36]: S&P 8,700 (from 8,000), NASDAQ 30,000 (from 27,000), Dow 60,000, Russell 3,400+. Hunter has raised targets each quarter as analysis confirms upside.
- **"Liberation day" (20% S&P drawdown) was a surprise** [00:55]: Hunter expected 5–7% pullback but the sharp decline actually strengthened his bullish case via sentiment reset.
- **Near-term pullback expected, then parabolic run** [17:46]: Likely pullback to 6,000–6,100 on S&P (6% correction), then final vertical advance to 8,700 in 3–6 months—potentially 40%+ in 3 months.
- **Bond market rolling over; rates heading lower** [12:48]: 10-year rates forming a two-year top; expect sub-3% this year, possibly 2.5%, then down to near-zero in the bust. Fed will follow, not lead.
- **Dollar DXY near critical 14-year trendline** [35:04]: Expect break below to 90 in 1–3 months, then target 82. Weaker dollar supports earnings translation and export competitiveness.
- **Oil rolling over; expect 50s–60s** [35:04]: Gasoline price pressure will ease inflation concerns; Fed should worry about deflation, not inflation.
- **Sentiment remains subdued despite momentum** [21:21]: Institutions cautious (BofA survey shows caution); strategists not raising targets aggressively. Long runway to top because "it doesn't top until they're all in."

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Bond vigilantes drive rates, not Fed** [08:03]: The Fed controls only the overnight fed funds rate. Bond market participants (institutional traders) set longer-term rates via supply/demand. Fed will ultimately follow market signals.
- **Sentiment as primary driver** [15:18]: Hunter uses technical, fundamental, macro, and cross-market analysis, but sentiment is the "overlying driving thing." Conviction rises when all pieces confirm the same view.
- **Contrarian positioning** [03:16]: When sentiment hit rock-bottom in April (down 20%, equal to 2020 lows), Hunter got very bullish. Wall of worry = fuel for advance.
- **Sector rotation: semis, financials, precious metals** [29:18]: Semiconductors remain leaders into the top; financials (especially broker-dealers) have significant upside; precious metals (gold, silver, miners) are "ready to run" and will move up alongside equities.
- **AI as productivity tailwind, not primary driver** [42:14]: AI helps inflation control and productivity but is not the core thesis. Hunter's targets have risen partly due to AI, but the melt-up was already in the cards.
- **Crypto not yet macro-significant** [47:04]: Bitcoin needs to be tested in a major downturn before Hunter assigns macro importance. Currently too momentum-driven; lacks systemic relevance.
- **Consumer resilience masks underlying weakness** [38:39]: Delinquencies rising; consumers trade off vacations vs. big purchases. Top half of income still spending; bottom half struggling. Housing rolling over. Soft landing narrative will drive final leg.
