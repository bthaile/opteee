# Unusual Options as a Market Effect

## Summary

Research from McGill University demonstrates that unusual options activity precedes material corporate events—M&A announcements, takeovers, earnings releases, and product launches. Informed traders systematically use options over stock to gain leverage when they possess advance knowledge or high conviction about upcoming catalysts, creating a detectable market signal.

## Key takeaways

- **M&A and takeover signals**: Academic research confirms statistically significant options activity ahead of M&A announcements and takeover events [00:00–00:15]
- **Earnings and product release patterns**: Unusual options activity also precedes earnings releases and product announcements, suggesting informed positioning [00:15–00:32]
- **Leverage as the mechanism**: Informed traders prefer options to stock because options provide superior leverage when conviction is high [00:32–00:45]
- **Market-readable signal**: The systematic preference for options leverage by informed traders creates a detectable pattern that can inform broader market analysis [00:45+]
