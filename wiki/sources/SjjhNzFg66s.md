# DJT Stock Volatility: An In-Depth Analysis

## Summary

DJT (Trump Media & Technology Group) is trading as a direct proxy for 2024 U.S. presidential election outcomes, with implied volatility at 300%+ and IV percentile at 98%. The stock has rallied from ~$17.75 (Sept 24) to ~$34+ in three months, driven entirely by election probability shifts rather than fundamental valuation. Prediction markets show Trump favored at ~64% vs. Harris at ~36%, creating a tradable mismatch between stock price and underlying probabilities.

## Key takeaways

- **Extreme volatility metrics** [03:24]: IV percentile at 98% (247 of 248 days lower); IV itself over 300%; 5-day average daily range 9.3%, ATR 8.47%. This is not normal equity behavior.
- **Volatility rising with price** [04:40]: Unusual pattern—normally stock appreciation contracts volatility. Here, rising price + rising volatility signals market uncertainty about direction, not conviction.
- **Election-driven proxy** [08:14]: DJT has no fundamental earnings story; it is purely a bet on Trump victory. Stock moves with election probability shifts, not company operations.
- **Options positioning** [07:08]: 388k calls vs. 468k puts; 156k calls vs. 160k puts on Nov 15 expiry (election date). Most open interest clustered around election period.
- **IV skew imbalance** [08:14]: Put-side IV richer than call-side IV, suggesting hedging demand and downside protection pricing.
- **Prediction market alignment** [05:56]: Polymarket shows Trump at 64% vs. Harris 36%; another market 61% vs. 39%. Discrepancies are arbitrageable; Trump clearly favored.
- **Hedge opportunity** [07:08]: If bullish on Trump + broad market, can go long equities and short DJT as a hedge to cap downside if Harris wins.
- **Pullback expectation** [07:08]: After massive run, reversion likely; some of current put positioning may price this in.
