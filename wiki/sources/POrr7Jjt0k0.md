# Return on Invested Capital (ROIC) in Vertical Spreads

## Summary

This video examines the tension between capital efficiency and absolute profit in options trading, specifically in the context of vertical spreads. While high return on invested capital (ROIC) is desirable, obsessive pursuit of efficiency metrics can lead to suboptimal trading decisions and opportunity cost. The core lesson: a 1,000% return on $1 is still only $1 profit.

## Key takeaways

- **ROIC vs. absolute profit trade-off** [00:00–00:29]: High percentage returns on small capital deployments can mask the reality of small absolute dollar gains; efficiency metrics should inform but not dominate decision-making.
- **Efficiency as a trap** [00:29]: Over-prioritizing capital efficiency can drive traders toward suboptimal position sizing, entry/exit timing, and risk management choices in pursuit of a "perfect" metric.
- **Portfolio-first thinking**: Vertical spreads and other defined-risk strategies should be evaluated on their contribution to total portfolio return and risk, not isolated ROIC percentages.

## Notable quotes

> "I still made a dollar. The point that I'm making here is that efficiency is important, but when people become too wrapped around efficiency, it can lead you again to making a lot of suboptimal decisions in the pursuit of efficiency."
