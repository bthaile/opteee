# Unusual Options as a Market Effect #optionstrading

## Summary

This video examines academic research from McGill University on unusual options activity preceding major corporate events—M&A announcements, takeovers, earnings releases, and product launches. The core insight is that informed traders systematically use options rather than stock to gain leverage when they possess material non-public information or high-conviction theses, creating detectable market signals.

## Key takeaways

- **Academic evidence of informed trading via options** [00:00] McGill University research documents statistically significant unusual options activity prior to M&A announcements and takeovers, indicating preemptive positioning by informed traders.
- **Leverage as the primary driver** [00:32] Traders with insider information or high-conviction views on earnings or product releases prefer options over stock because options provide superior leverage on the same capital.
- **Options as a market-efficiency signal** [00:00–00:32] The systematic use of options by informed traders before major events reveals that options markets can serve as a leading indicator of material information flow.
