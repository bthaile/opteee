# These 4 Options Trading Mistakes are Destroying Your Profits

## Summary

This video identifies four critical execution errors that undermine otherwise sound options trading ideas: poor P&L management discipline, overemphasis on individual trade characteristics at the expense of portfolio context, fixation on realized gains while ignoring unrealized positions, and excessive position management that erodes edge. The core thesis is that most trading work happens before entry, yet many traders sabotage good setups through post-trade behavior.

## Key takeaways

- **P&L management discipline** [00:00] — Set profit targets and stop-loss levels *before* entry; most traders have good ideas but execute them poorly after placing the trade.
- **Portfolio-first thinking** [00:26] — Avoid overemphasizing individual trade traits in isolation; context the position within your overall portfolio and risk allocation.
- **Realized vs. unrealized distinction** [00:26] — Focusing only on closed P&L creates blind spots; track both realized and unrealized positions to understand true portfolio health.
- **Hyperactive management** [00:26] — Excessive adjustments and re-management of positions erode edge; trust your plan and avoid emotional tinkering.
