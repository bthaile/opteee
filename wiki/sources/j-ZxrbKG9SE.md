# Trade Adjustments & Diag Prospects

## Summary

Eric reviews the Small Stacks portfolio (part 12), which is up ~13% with 22.6% capital utilization. He walks through recent adjustments to Ford and Kroger positions—converting them to 1:1 call ratios to lock in premium while managing downside risk—and analyzes a prospective Intel call diagonal entry, waiting for a pullback to the 48 handle before committing.

## Key takeaways

- **Ford adjustment [01:25–02:46]**: Sold a third short call (7 Oct expiry) against three long calls to create a 3:3 ratio. Rationale: expects modest downside or sideways action into earnings; collects premium upfront and profits if Ford stays flat or rises modestly, but caps gains if it rallies >20¢.
- **Kroger adjustment [03:08–04:46]**: Sold a second short call (6 Nov 35.5 strike) on 7 Oct, then closed it the same day for 58¢, realizing 75 bps P&L (5.53% ROIC). Uncovered the leap again to preserve upside potential.
- **Intel diagonal prospect [05:11–07:05]**: Eyeing 18 Jun 21 40 call diagonal (one lot, ~1400 bps capital). Wants pullback to 48 handle; currently too extended. Bullish on tech exposure; willing to wait for better entry rather than chase.
- **Portfolio health [00:38–01:25]**: Total P&L ~757 bps (13%+ return since 1 Sept); credit outstanding 182 bps (3.16% portfolio return). Still room to add positions without over-leveraging.
- **Position-sizing discipline [06:16–07:26]**: Avoids oversizing; Intel position would be on par with Kroger. Small Stacks portfolio intolerant of long waits; margin account allows scaling in, but Small Stacks requires upfront conviction.

## Notable quotes

> "I generally like to keep the leap uncovered which I did for a while but now that I see Ford kind of making a move up I perceive more room to the downside at this point."

> "I uncovered the max potential gain on Kroger again so I will continue to do this same exact system where I'll either allow it to move up until I perceive more downside risk then add that short call to collect more money."
