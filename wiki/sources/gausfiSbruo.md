# Unassigning Short Puts

## Summary

This video demonstrates how to manage a short put position after assignment by closing the assigned shares and rolling back into options. The mechanics show the interaction between realized losses on equity and realized gains on the option premium, with the flexibility to re-enter the options trade at a new strike or expiration.

## Key takeaways

- **Assignment mechanics**: When assigned on a short put, you become long the underlying at the strike price [00:00–00:21].
- **Closing assigned shares**: You can sell the assigned shares at market price to realize the equity P&L, separate from the option credit already captured [00:21–00:43].
- **Rolling as accounting**: Rolling is a system of accounting—closing the equity position and re-entering options at a different strike/expiration with no inherent rules [00:43].
- **Net P&L calculation**: Total P&L combines the realized loss on equity (e.g., long at $10, sold at $8 = $200 loss) plus the realized option credit (e.g., $100 gain), yielding net P&L [00:21–00:43].
