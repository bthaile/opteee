# Are Covered Calls A Good As A Hedge? #optionstrading

## Summary

Covered calls are commonly misunderstood as a hedge, but they provide minimal downside protection while significantly capping upside profit potential. In a 5% move scenario, selling calls reduces downside loss by only a few thousand dollars while cutting upside gains roughly in half—a poor risk-reward tradeoff for hedging purposes.

## Key takeaways

- **Covered calls provide negligible hedge value** [00:00–00:35]: In a 5% down move, selling calls reduces losses from ~$31k to ~$27k—only ~$4k of protection.
- **Upside is severely capped** [00:35]: In a 5% up move, unsold stock gains ~$31k while covered calls cap gains at ~$15.5k—a 50% reduction in profit.
- **The hedge-to-upside tradeoff is unfavorable** [00:00–00:35]: The slight downside cushion does not justify sacrificing half of potential upside gains.

## Notable quotes

> "You're very slightly hedging the downside by a few grand in this scenario, but your upside you are absolutely crushing."
