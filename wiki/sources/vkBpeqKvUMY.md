# GME Top Theories Analysis Pt5 | GameStop Analysis Live Ep22

## Summary

Eric reviews GameStop's price action and consolidation patterns, then dives deep into a comprehensive DD library analyzing naked shorting mechanics, fail-to-deliver cycles, and the use of deep in-the-money calls and married puts to perpetuate short positions. The analysis examines how market makers and hedge funds may exploit options strategies and dark pool activity to defer regulatory obligations while maintaining short exposure.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2024-12-08)

- **Price consolidation as positive signal** [41:17]: GameStop showing healthy consolidation after recent volatility; consolidation itself is bullish regardless of recent down days, indicating normalization of price activity.
- **Algorithmic front-running on social media posts** [14:54–17:01]: When Roaring Kitty posted a meme, price spiked dramatically on a single 1-minute candle with massive volume; analysis suggests algorithms are programmed to front-run his posts and their expected market impact.
- **10:45 AM volume anomaly** [25:18–36:40]: Statistical analysis of intraday minute-level data reveals 6+ instances where 10:45 AM candles showed 2x+ average 15-minute volume; suggests programmatic activity at that time, though not conclusively causal.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Deep in-the-money calls and married puts** [55:29–01:07:55]: Market makers and short sellers use deep ITM calls (buy-write) and married puts to create synthetic long positions that can reset fail-to-deliver obligations without actually covering; these are "reset transactions" that perpetuate short positions indefinitely.
- **Fail-to-deliver perpetuation cycle** [56:05–01:14:13]: When shares are hard to borrow, pricing differentials between stock and options create arbitrage opportunities; shorts can use synthetic positions to satisfy regulatory closeout requirements on paper while maintaining actual short exposure.
- **Options market maker exemption abuse** [01:12:54–01:13:49]: Options market makers claim Bonafide Market Making exemption to locate requirement, but subsequent transactions designed to "give the appearance" of satisfying closeout obligations while actually extending fail-to-deliver positions may violate Reg SHO.
- **35-calendar-day FTD cycle** [01:26:14–01:27:12]: Persistent spikes in price and volume every ~35 days correlate with forced covering cycles; however, other variables (options expirations, underlying movement) also feed into this pattern—causality is multivariate, not discrete.
- **Dark pool settlement mechanics** [01:28:54–01:29:39]: Dark pool volume can hide FTDs by allowing buy/sell pairs to settle old FTDs while shorts unwind to maintain original positions; the issue is not dark pools themselves (which report with delay) but the transactional strategy to defer obligations.
- **Synthetic shares vs. counterfeit shares** [01:34:25–01:37:09]: Synthetic positions (long or short) are legitimate trading tools; the problem is their use by market makers to defer FTD obligations without actual delivery, not synthetics themselves.
