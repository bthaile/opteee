# Tom Sosnoff on Volatility, Risk, and Why Most Traders Misunderstand Options | The Outlier Podcast

## Summary

Tom Sosnoff discusses the 2025 market environment, the April volatility spike, and his philosophy on risk management within two standard deviations. He reflects on market integrity concerns amid policy-driven destabilization, shares his personal recovery strategy from the April crash, and emphasizes that risk-taking and process—not outcome—are the true drivers of long-term wealth creation. He also previews upcoming software designed to help traders monetize trading knowledge beyond P&L.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2025-05-15)

- **Market positioning**: Expected S&P range of 5400–6000 for the remainder of 2025; April's ~1000-point drop was a "once every seven years" volatility expansion and likely the year's low [03:38]
- **Volatility regime**: VIX at ~18 is 10% elevated; prefers 20–22 but 18 is acceptable; expects lower volatility in H2 2025 [54:28]
- **Bond market**: TLT at 113; sees this as a constraint on new highs [53:17]
- **Tariff pause**: 90-day Chinese tariff negotiation window; treat as perpetual baseline, not a temporary reprieve [25:11]

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Two standard deviations rule**: Prepare for moves within ±2σ; beyond that (3σ+), outcomes become unquantifiable and uncontrollable [07:00]
- **Short premium approach**: 45 days to expiration, manage close to 20–21% profit target; strategy remains consistent regardless of market condition [16:07]
- **Contrarian positioning**: In destabilized markets, stay small and contrarian; easier to sell into strength than buy into weakness [15:06]
- **Account utilization**: Pre-April ~40–45%; spiked to low 70s during crash; now back to ~30% [22:52, 24:06]
- **Nibbling on the way down**: Do not increase size during drawdowns; reduce or hold steady to stay in the game [21:51]
- **Recovery management**: Target 30–60% retracement to close positions; lock in losses on rolls rather than hold to zero [20:49]
- **Risk control**: Never put yourself in a position to blow out; control your own investments to manage correlation risk [21:51, 32:53]
- **Earnings plays**: Short premium on earnings has been strong in 2025; contrarian directional plays (e.g., short USD) also profitable [17:10]
- **Market integrity**: Free markets require integrity; policy-driven destabilization (tweets, tariffs) undermines trust and capital flows [11:21, 28:16]
- **Risk-taking as wealth driver**: 50% of risk-takers outperform the bottom 50% of non-risk-takers; experience and knowhow matter more than initial account size [39:22, 40:39]
- **Process over outcome**: Focus on understanding *what happened* in a trade (e.g., selling an OTM put), not just P&L; this builds long-term edge [50:47]

## Notable quotes

## Regime / context

**Date:** May 2025, ~6 weeks post-April crash. Markets have recovered from ~4800 lows to ~5900 SPX. Tariff negotiations with China paused for 90 days. VIX elevated but off crisis levels.

**Key context:** Tom's April experience (short delta + short premium, account utilization spiked to 70s) illustrates the limits of two-standard-deviation risk models during policy-driven tail events. His recovery strategy (nibble, target 30–60% retracement, lock losses on rolls) reflects pragmatic adaptation rather than heroic doubling down. The broader concern—market integrity under tweet-driven policy—frames his skepticism about new highs and preference for range-bound trading.

**Upcoming:** Tom is building software (expected later in 2025) to help traders monetize trading knowledge and experience beyond P&L, addressing the "holy grail" question of how a $5k account becomes long-term wealth through process and differentiation.
