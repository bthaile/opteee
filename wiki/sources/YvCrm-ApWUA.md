# Money is Stupid with Tom Sosnoff

## Summary

Tom Sosnoff reflects on the role of money in his life and trading career, arguing that money itself has no intrinsic utility beyond enabling experiences and comfort. He emphasizes that his primary motivation has shifted from wealth accumulation to building a community of like-minded traders and leaving a meaningful legacy through education and mentorship.

## Key takeaways

- **Money as a tool, not a goal** [00:00–00:19]: Sosnoff articulates that money has no purpose for him personally—he owns his house, his businesses, and has everything he needs. The utility of money is limited to enabling nice dinners, hotels, and travel, but it doesn't "turn him on" as a motivator.
- **Legacy through community building** [00:19–00:38]: The most interesting part of his life now is helping a like-minded community build wealth. He frames this as the ultimate legacy—leaving something special behind through education and mentorship rather than pure wealth accumulation.
- **Reframing success** [00:38]: Money can only do so much; the real value lies in the impact you have on others and the community you build.

## Notable quotes

> "Money has no purpose for me… it doesn't turn me on."

> "If that's your legacy then you really left something special behind… money can only do so much."
