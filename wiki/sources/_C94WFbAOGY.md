# The SPCX Problem Nobody's Talking About | Unhedged w/ Tom Sosnoff

## Summary

Tom Sosnoff and Eric discuss SpaceX's IPO execution, valuation concerns, and the broader tech concentration problem in markets. The conversation spans SpaceX's three business verticals (Starlink, government contracts, data infrastructure), the sustainability of mega-cap tech valuations, and macro positioning around bonds and Fed policy under Kevin Warsh. Tom argues SpaceX is priced to perfection despite being early-stage as a business, and explores whether recent mega-deal announcements represent real capital or circular money shuffling among AI/tech giants.

## Key takeaways

### SpaceX IPO & valuation
- **IPO execution was tamer than expected** [07:50]: Opened at $150 (vs. anticipated ~$195), traded to $170, closed first day at $161. Subsequent rally to $215 followed by pullback to $150 made sense to Tom.
- **Polarizing bet on two fronts** [08:29]: (1) Does Elon have more magic left? (2) Is this a glorified Starlink play? At $2T valuation, betting heavily on Starlink sustainability and government contract durability.
- **Three distinct business verticals** [35:57]: Starlink, government/defense contracts, and data-center-in-the-sky ambitions. Each has a moat; each can grow independently.
- **Single point of failure: Elon** [36:57]: Tom acknowledges Elon as existential risk to SpaceX. If he leaves for Mars, markets crater. But long-term (3–5 years), Tom sees upside from current $147 level.
- **Short puts as entry strategy** [35:36]: Tom already has short puts in SpaceX; volatility is rich and outside expected move. If bullish, sell the $100 puts.

### Tech concentration & pairs trading
- **Nasdaq dominance unsustainable long-term** [15:46]: Tech is >30% of S&P 500 and priced to perfection. Will eventually revert, but leadership may not change—just repricing.
- **Russell/Nasdaq basis hasn't mean-reverted in 10 years** [12:39]: Small-cap underperformance is cyclical. Pairs trades (Qs vs. Russell, Qs vs. S&P) have been brutal; Tom avoided them this year except gold/silver.
- **Gold/silver pairs trade** [14:42]: Sold silver/bought gold early year (finally profitable). Now flipped: buying gold/selling silver. Both on lows; requires futures for capital efficiency.
- **Mega-cap AI deal skepticism** [10:01]: Tom questions whether $20B "deals" are real or just the same capital shuffled between OpenAI, Nvidia, Google, Microsoft, Meta, SpaceX. Clawbacks and NFL-contract-style guarantees make headline numbers misleading.

### Nvidia & Micron blow-ups
- **Micron: $40 → $2,300 in <1 year** [17:10]: Tom started selling at $545 two and a half months ago; it hit $1,200 yesterday with no downtick until today. "Most ridiculous thing I've ever seen."
- **Nvidia precedent** [18:14]: Nvidia went $95 → $995 before split. Tom has been run over many times; it's part of trading.

### AI & disruption
- **AI is 10 years old in innovation, 40–45 in price** [19:36]: Closer to infancy than maturity. But priced like a mature, peak-earning business. Same pattern as internet in 1999–2000.
- **Speed is the gamma** [24:00]: AI's real edge is velocity (million lines of code in 1.5 days vs. 1.5 years). Not creative; it's a machine. Allows creators to move faster.
- **Job displacement is real but necessary** [24:29]: ~150k jobs lost to AI this year. Tom sees it as paradigm shift; people must adapt via education, networking, upskilling. No alternative.
- **Energy tax needed** [26:25]: Tom argues trillion-dollar AI companies shouldn't be subsidized by consumers' tax dollars. Proposes consumption/energy tax on token usage.
- **Curriculum disruption via AI** [29:45]: AI can write a curriculum in 3 hours vs. 1 year. But risk: AI may just dust off old curricula rather than innovate.

### Fed & bonds
- **Kevin Warsh in "can't lose" spot** [46:02]: Bonds at 113 handle; nowhere to go but up. Inflation sticky but manageable. Warsh has leash from Trump (unlike Powell).
- **No rate hikes if bonds stay >112.5** [48:19]: Market decides, Fed follows. If 10-year (ZN) stays ~110 or above, no hikes. If bonds rally 2 more points to 115, might see cuts instead.
- **Basis arb: notes vs. bonds** [49:43]: If notes drop to 108 (10-year yield too high), Fed must raise. If bonds stay 113+, no pressure. Currently in "no man's land."

### Commencement speeches & tone-deafness
- **Tech billionaires shouldn't lecture grads on job losses** [41:01]: Google CEO walkout at Stanford (second recent incident) shows tone-deaf messaging. Bring comedians (Harvard model) or inspiring speakers, not CEOs warning of disruption.
- **Universities need better judgment** [42:14]: Don't chase big-name donors for commencement. Bring funny, uplifting speakers. Will Ferrell example cited as excellent.
