# GME Livestream | GameStop Live Analysis Ep6

## Summary

Eric conducts a live analysis of GameStop and related securities, diving into the Bed Bath & Beyond litigation against Ryan Cohen to understand the broader context of activist investing and market dynamics. The stream covers volume analysis as a leading indicator for price movement, examines Cohen's deposition testimony, and explores emerging opportunities in Chewy and GameStop's current consolidation phase.

## Key takeaways

- **Volume as a leading indicator** [01:01–01:07]: Buy volume spikes often precede major price moves by days; on May 2nd, GME's buy volume (8M shares) was 2× the 20-day average total daily volume while price was still sub-$15, well before the May 13–14 rally.
- **Bed Bath & Beyond case context** [00:24–00:46]: Plaintiffs allege Cohen and Gustavo engaged in a pump-and-dump scheme; however, large-float stocks are difficult to manipulate, and the case may be settlement-focused rather than trial-ready.
- **Deposition tactics** [01:26–01:52]: Opposing counsel uses repetitive questioning and null-hypothesis framing to box in testimony; Cohen's responses show increasing fatigue and belligerence as the deposition progresses.
- **Cohen's philosophy on short-selling** [01:28–01:29]: Cohen states short-selling is "unamerican" because it profits from failure; he advocates for transparency parity between long and short disclosures.
- **Chewy breakout** [02:05–02:06]: Chewy broke above $25.24 with 93% buy volume (4.8M shares) on a single day, exceeding the 10-day average total daily volume; earnings on Aug 28 may be driving options activity.
- **GME consolidation** [01:15–01:21]: Despite low recent volume, GME is holding support with positive buy-side pressure; intrinsic value estimated ~$12 (conservative floor); options chain is anemic ahead of Sept 4 earnings.
- **Market efficiency debate** [01:37–01:41]: Payment for order flow has made markets more efficient since 2007, but concentration of power (Citadel, Virtu) creates monopolistic risks; better systems exist but adoption is slow.
