# Options Alpha Founder - Kirk Du Plessis | The Outlier Podcast Ep65

## Summary

Kirk Du Plessis, founder of Options Alpha, discusses his evolution from M&A analyst at Deutsche Bank to options educator and automated trader. The conversation centers on capital efficiency, the transition from discretionary "hunter-gatherer" trading to mechanical bot-driven strategies, and the critical importance of maintaining portfolio neutrality to avoid catastrophic drawdowns. Kirk emphasizes that most retail traders lack a systematic plan and advocates for simplicity, position sizing, and awareness of trading friction (bid-ask spreads, payment for order flow) as the true levers of long-term profitability.

## Key takeaways

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Options as capital-efficient leverage** [07:25]: Options allow rapid directional changes and strategy re-engineering without the capital drag of stock ownership or the settlement friction of real estate.
- **The F1 steering wheel metaphor** [10:01]: Options have many dials and modes, but most traders press every button. Success comes from identifying the few "big rocks"—position sizing, diversification, staying neutral—and ignoring the rest.
- **Neutral portfolio bias prevents catastrophic drawdowns** [45:39–49:32]: If you sell a put spread and the stock tanks, adding another put spread digs a deeper hole. Instead, hedge with opposing positions (e.g., call spreads) to work toward delta-neutral and avoid the 60–70% drawdown that forces years of recovery.
- **Mechanical trading beats discretionary hunting** [15:42–17:31]: Most traders "gunslinger from the hip" looking for trades. Kirk's evolution: from active hunter-gatherer to setting bot criteria and letting automation find qualifying trades, reducing emotional recalibration and improving consistency.
- **Bid-ask spread and payment for order flow are real costs** [36:09–41:26]: Free commissions mask the true friction. A 50-cent wide market on a $1 stock is a 50% cost. Kirk's team quantifies this via a "scalp" metric (bid-ask spread × 100 shares) and fill-percentage tracking to show traders the actual dollar drag.
- **Beta-weighted delta exposure as a guardrail** [50:11–54:03]: Kirk targets ~1% beta exposure on a 30-day basis; if the portfolio drifts beyond that, he adds offsetting positions. This prevents one-directional leverage and keeps the portfolio resilient across market regimes.
- **Duration and awareness alignment** [23:06–23:45]: Zero-DTE traders must track every Fed announcement; 35–60 day traders can ignore daily noise. Match your situational awareness to your time frame.

### Dated market read (2024-05-22)

- **VIX under 12, retail over-leveraged to one direction** [51:08]: Market is risk-averse and compressed; Kirk observes traders are insanely levered long and penny-stock chasing is rampant—a sign of complacency.
- **GME resurgence on Roaring Kitty return** [32:16]: Retail piled in opportunistically; stock crashed again. Illustrates the danger of discretionary "opportunity" trading without a systematic framework.
