# Palantir (PLTR) Stock Analysis: Valuation vs. Trading Lens

## Summary

Eric analyzes Palantir's 3x move over six months (from ~$21 to $66) through both a value-investing and trading lens, examining critical analyst downgrades and reconciling the apparent contradiction between high PE ratios and continued price strength. The stock shows strong post-earnings drift, bullish options positioning, and no alarming insider selling, though RSI is heavily overextended. From a pure price-action perspective, the stock remains bullish until technicals deteriorate.

## Key takeaways

- **Valuation vs. price action divergence** [00:00–03:44]: High PE ratios (177× forward) look "psychotic" by value metrics, but many tech stocks maintain elevated multiples for extended periods. PE ratios are relative, not absolute; what matters to traders is whether price continues higher, regardless of the valuation narrative.

- **Post-earnings drift is real** [08:32–09:32]: Palantir beat earnings significantly (expected $0.05, delivered $0.10; expected $0.04, delivered $0.09). Positive post-earnings announcement drift is well-researched and has been a dominant effect here, supporting continued strength.

- **Technical setup remains bullish** [09:32–10:37]: One-year move from $15 to $67, six-month tripled, three-month still in aggressive growth. RSI is heavily overextended (redlining) on a 7-day moving average, which is unusual and warrants caution, but price action itself shows no breakdown yet.

- **Options positioning slightly bullish** [10:37–11:55]: 1.7M calls vs. 1.4M puts; near-term (20 DTE) mixed (308K calls vs. 272K puts); April 17 roughly balanced (129K vs. 125K); September inverted (55K puts vs. 51K calls); January 2026 bullish (165K calls vs. 109K puts). Volatility is elevated but not extreme.

- **Insider selling is structural, not alarming** [13:16–14:25]: Recent insider sales are heavy on the sell side, but mostly tax-withholding obligations tied to RSU vesting. No evidence of mass executive exit; selling appears driven by compensation mechanics rather than conviction.

- **Bayesian trading approach** [14:25–15:37]: As new information emerges, update your thesis. Don't make year-ahead predictions; trade the moves as they happen. Until price action deteriorates (volume expansion, consolidation, loss of trend), remain bullish. Value investors and traders operate on different time horizons and risk frameworks.

## Notable quotes

> "I don't care if it goes up because of momentum, drift, value, because your grandma likes it and bought it and she has a bunch of money and drives the price up. I couldn't care less what makes it go up as long as these candles keep going up, that's what I ultimately care about." [03:44]

> "Just because something has a high PE ratio does not mean that it can't continue to go up, but it does mean for somebody that's a value investor, they're probably going to try to look other places where they can get better value." [08:32]
