# Analyzing Top Meme Stocks | Meme Stock Watch Ep46

## Summary

Eric conducts a landscape analysis of current meme stocks by examining charts, options flows, and fundamentals across a curated list including GameStop, AMC, SoFi, Palantir, Rivian, and Hims. He demonstrates how to evaluate risk premium opportunities, decode options skew and net flows, and assess whether stocks merit directional or volatility-based trades. The episode emphasizes the importance of not overpaying for volatility in high-IV names and using technical structure to time entries.

## Key takeaways

- **Orientation phase & landscape analysis** [07:41] — Eric applies a military-derived framework to stock market analysis: establish orientation first, then conduct terrain/landscape analysis to understand the environment before trading decisions.

- **Meme stock definition challenge** [08:05] — Meme stock lists vary by source with unclear population methodology; Eric uses multiple resources to cross-reference and identify the most relevant names for analysis.

- **SoundHound: AI pivot story** [11:28]–[15:42] — SoundHound demonstrates strong revenue growth (nearly 3× year-over-year) and is successfully pivoting into agentic AI with penetration across restaurants, voice commerce, and enterprise sectors; options show heavy call skew with 4M in net call premiums on 30 June.

- **Volatility percentile vs. raw IV** [50:46]–[52:12] — IV percentile matters more than absolute IV level; SoFi September calls at 44% IV percentile are expensive relative to 365-day calls at <5% IV percentile, making far-dated calls economical despite higher dollar cost.

- **Vega across expirations** [41:18]–[46:49] — Vega increases with DTE but the percent change in Vega is smaller going from 195 to 440 days than from 13 to 41 days; this justifies buying far-dated calls in high-volatility names to avoid vega crush.

- **SoFi breakout setup** [30:16]–[39:07] — SoFi shows strong upside net call flows, institutional sponsorship, and 136% YoY revenue growth; Eric identifies a consolidation entry with a stop around the 17 handle, treating it as an asymmetric upside play despite 67× forward PE.

- **AMC covered strangle** [25:05]–[26:30] — Eric holds a covered strangle in AMC based on a multi-year viewer bet; the stock shows basing activity (higher lows, higher highs) suggesting a trend reversal, though still in a broad downtrend.

- **Rivian risk premium** [23:04]–[24:34] — Rivian displays persistent risk premiums and earnings on 5 July after market; the volatility surface suggests a strong risk-premium candidate for short-volatility strategies.

- **Boeing case study: timing entries** [58:17]–[01:01:10] — ZXBorg's Boeing 230 call (12 June, 43 DTE) lost money despite directional correctness; the trade was hurt primarily by theta (–0.072 initial, –0.035 at exit) and delta (stock moved against entry), not vega. Lesson: wait for consolidation and strength before entering, avoid catching falling knives.

- **Palantir: strong fundamentals, insider selling** [01:09:16]–[01:20:41] — Palantir shows strong US revenue growth (55%+), expanding commercial segment alongside government revenue, and new enterprise partnerships (Heineken, Wendy's, AIG, Archer); however, heavy insider selling and slowing call-side premiums suggest growth expectations may be moderating.

- **Hims: partnership termination red flag** [01:21:07]–[01:38:39] — Novo Nordisk terminated partnership citing "illegal mass compounding and deceptive marketing"; put-side premiums are expanding abnormally (nearly equal to call premiums), and insider selling is heavy. Revenue growth is solid (136% YoY) but margins are not expanding as expected for an early-stage company. Near-term sentiment risk is high.

- **Scanning for risk premium** [27:24]–[29:42] — Implied > realized volatility is a simple initial filter; Eric uses a composite volatility score based on multiple calculation methods and then plots individual option risk premium using the volatility surface to refine candidates.

- **Options flow interpretation** [16:44]–[17:51] — SoundHound shows 4M in net call premiums with a large unwind on 30 June (stock up 9%); disaggregated trades suggest retail/speculative activity rather than institutional positioning.

- **Fractional shares internalization** [24:10]–[24:34] — Fractional shares are broker-internalized orders; the broker holds inventory and lets retail hold a piece. Availability varies by broker and ticker; not all brokers offer fractionals on all names.
