# The Best Buy and Hold Portfolio

## Summary

Eric presents two market-beating buy-and-hold portfolios designed for both passive investors and traders learning to trade. The core strategy splits capital between equity ETFs (SPY, IWM, QQQ) and bonds (TLT) in various allocations (80/20, 60/40, and equal-weight), with backtested performance from 2003–April 2022 showing that a 80% QQQ / 20% TLT allocation significantly outperforms the S&P 500 while a 60/40 split offers better risk-adjusted returns with lower drawdown.

## Key takeaways

- **Why buy-and-hold matters** [00:39–01:02]: Most traders fail statistically; buy-and-hold should anchor most portfolios. It can also serve as a learning vehicle while paper trading.
- **Portfolio structure** [01:25–01:43]: Split capital into two buckets—equity (SPY, IWM, QQQ) and debt (TLT)—and adjust the ratio to match your risk tolerance.
- **Key performance metrics** [02:33–03:37]:
  - **CAGR** (compound annual growth rate): target higher; shows annualized return.
  - **Standard deviation**: target lower; indicates consistency and lower volatility.
  - **Max drawdown**: worst peak-to-trough loss; lower is better.
  - **Sortino ratio**: risk-adjusted return focusing on downside risk; higher is better.
- **80/20 QQQ/TLT results** [04:00–04:27]: Nearly doubles the S&P 500 return over the study period, but has a worse worst-year than some alternatives (e.g., IWM/TLT splits).
- **60/40 QQQ/TLT trade-off** [05:07–06:02]: CAGR ~12% vs. 13.8% for 80/20, but max drawdown improves from −36% to −22%, and worst year improves by ~6% (at cost of ~6% worse best year).
- **Equal-weight "25s" portfolio** [06:48–07:13]: 25% each of SPY, IWM, QQQ, and TLT; beats the market with lower standard deviation and better worst-year/max-drawdown than 80/20 QQQ/TLT, though lower absolute return.
- **Regime consideration** [07:13–07:36]: Tech (QQQ) has been exceptionally hot; the 25s portfolio offers diversification if tech leadership doesn't persist.

## Regime / context

Backtest period: 2003–April 2022 (with some comparisons from 2001 onward). This analysis predates the 2022–2024 AI/mega-cap rally and subsequent market shifts; the dominance of QQQ in this study reflects the tech strength of the 2010s–early 2022 period. Allocations and risk profiles should be reassessed in different market regimes.
