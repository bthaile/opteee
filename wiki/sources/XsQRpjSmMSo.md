# Retail vs Wall Street Be a Successful Small Fish

## Summary

This video examines the structural differences between retail traders and institutional market participants (sell-side liquidity providers and buy-side operators), emphasizing that retail's disadvantage is not insurmountable if they avoid competing directly with larger players. The core thesis: success for small traders requires accepting their position in the market ecosystem and trading accordingly, rather than attempting to match institutional scale or speed.

## Key takeaways

- **Market structure fundamentals** [00:00–00:30]: Sell-side participants are liquidity providers; buy-side includes most retail traders; some operators span both roles. Understanding where you sit in this hierarchy is foundational.
- **Small-fish survival principle** [00:30]: Being retail (small) is not a death sentence, but attempting to trade like an institution (big fish) will result in being "eaten." Success requires accepting structural constraints.
- **Avoid direct competition with institutions** [00:30]: Retail traders must not try to outspeed, out-capital, or out-information institutional players on their home turf.

## Notable quotes

> "Just because you're a small fish does not mean you can't be a successful fish. But what it does mean is if you're a small fish and you try to act like a big fish, you're probably going to get eaten."
