# Breaking Down Trends from Roaring Kitty's GME Trades

## Summary

This episode synthesizes four prior deep-dives into Roaring Kitty's Reddit yellow updates and trading history, focusing on his options-based approach from 2019–2021. The host identifies uncommon behavioral patterns: conviction-driven position sizing, time-based (not price-target-based) trade management, and willingness to tolerate extreme portfolio volatility (swings from $7M to $50M to $17M). The analysis rejects the "perfect timing machine" narrative and instead reveals a trader managing concentrated, leveraged bets with multi-month holding periods and no profit targets.

## Key takeaways

- **First position was options, not stock** [04:24] — Roaring Kitty's earliest 2019 yellow update shows ~$100k in calls across multiple expirations and strikes, contradicting the assumption he was primarily a stock trader.

- **Portfolio volatility reveals conviction, not timing precision** [07:22–08:15] — The account swung from $2.8M (Jan 2021) → $22M → $48M → $17M, exhibiting massive drawdowns without exits. This pattern suggests either (a) other accounts fund his livelihood, or (b) he has no profit targets and is riding conviction.

- **Time-based management, not value-based exits** [12:29–13:28] — Evidence shows he holds options to expiration, closes positions >45 days out, and states an average holding period of 3–24 months. He does not appear to manage by return, price target, or new catalyst.

- **Leverage + conviction without standard risk controls** [14:45] — Unlike traditional full-time traders who set profit targets to avoid volatility, he allows portfolio swings of $40M+ without apparent concern, suggesting a fundamentally different risk tolerance and capital structure.

- **Options knowledge is deep, not accidental** [11:31–12:13] — He discusses liquidity siphoning, market-making, and option pricing (finding them "too expensive"), indicating sophisticated understanding despite occasional trolling about his competence.

- **Lumpy returns philosophy** [09:32] — He explicitly accepts "up big, down big" returns, which aligns with his observed behavior of riding volatility rather than smoothing it.

- **2024 analysis pending** [13:58–15:32] — The host plans to analyze 2024 updates in the next episode to determine whether his methodology has changed given the much larger account size.

## Notable quotes

> "I see somebody who is not setting profit targets and again for most of us traditional full-time traders we absolutely would set profit targets and it's so that we don't go through this kind of volatility." [09:55]

> "He has a very strange way to manage these options... he seems to be managing these options based on time not by value not by return not by price target not by new catalyst by time." [12:29]
