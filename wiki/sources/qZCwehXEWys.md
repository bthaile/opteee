# Covered Strangle Strategy for Leveraged ETFs: A High-Reward Approach

## Summary

This video demonstrates how to apply the covered strangle strategy to leveraged ETFs (specifically TQQQ) to amplify returns while managing the excessive volatility and drawdown risk inherent in 3× daily leverage. The core approach combines long shares, short cash-secured puts, and ratio short calls within a 50 DTE window, using moving averages and 5% scaling triggers to navigate volatility decay and avoid catastrophic drawdowns.

## Key takeaways

- **Leverage amplifies both gains and losses**: TQQQ (3× QQQ) dropped 80%+ during the 2022 bear market vs. QQQ's 36% decline; notional sizing is critical [00:59–02:10]
- **Volatility decay is the hidden cost**: Consecutive down days compound losses in leveraged ETFs; a sequence requiring 8.5% recovery in QQQ needs 29% in TQQQ due to daily rebalancing [02:10–03:16]
- **Covered strangle structure for hands-off management**: Long ≥100 shares + short cash-secured puts + ratio short calls, all within 50 DTE to maximize theta decay without constant adjustment [06:26–07:22]
- **Risk management via position sizing and scaling**: Use 5% drawdown windows to scale in/out; avoid top-ticking by maintaining dry powder to average down during crashes [07:22–08:19]
- **Moving averages as entry/exit filters**: 50-day MA reduces max drawdown vs. buy-and-hold; 200-day MA is slower but avoids whipsaws; hybrid approach balances sensitivity and protection [10:19–12:11]
- **Profit mechanism**: Capital gains via trend drift and beta; the strategy aims to capture TQQQ's 3,270% historical gains while avoiding 82%+ drawdowns [05:22–06:26]
