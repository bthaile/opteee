# The Mistake of Selling Options for Income

## Summary

Selling options for income is a common but flawed approach to options trading. While selling options itself is a valid tactic, treating the upfront credit as "income" is a critical mistake because the position remains open and exposed to market risk until expiration or closure. True income must account for the full P&L of the trade, not just the initial credit received.

## Key takeaways

- **Credit ≠ income**: Collecting an upfront credit from selling options does not constitute income; it is still tied to an open position with unrealized risk [00:00–00:28]
- **Open position risk**: The credit received is contingent on the trade closing profitably; marking it as income before resolution creates a false sense of earnings [00:28]
- **Selling options is valid**: The strategy itself is not inherently flawed; the mistake lies in the mental accounting and framing of the trade [00:00]

## Notable quotes

> "Just because you collect a credit upfront does not mean that is your income because it's still tied to an open trade."
