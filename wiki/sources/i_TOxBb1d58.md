# Breaking Down My DJT Long Call Trade – Key Takeaways on Gamma

## Summary

Eric walks through a $45,000+ profit trade on DJT long calls structured as a gamma play around the 2024 US presidential election. The trade deviated from his typical ratio-call-diagonal approach, using a unit structure (500 contracts of 15-Nov $115 calls) sized to a $155K max-risk allocation. He phased out 400 contracts at $1.85 for a $42.8K gain on 28-Oct, then closed 98 of the remaining 100 at $1.25 on 30-Oct, leaving 2 contracts as a speculative remainder.

## Key takeaways

- **Election proxy thesis** [01:03–02:12]: DJT became a pure proxy for Trump election probability (tracked via PolyMarket), with no fundamental business change driving the 360%+ rally. This short-term event structure required a different approach than fundamental growth plays.

- **Max-risk allocation over position sizing** [03:18–04:24]: Used a $155K max-risk cap (less than 1% of account) rather than fixed capital allocation, appropriate for a highly speculative, short-term election-event play. Expected binary outcome: either compound via long gamma or lose the risk.

- **Unit structure for gamma compounding** [04:24–05:30]: Built the trade as a gamma play (long gamma = units), not a typical ratio diagonal. Chose 15-Nov $115 calls (OTM) over ATM for lower delta (0.09) and better liquidity, allowing gamma to rapidly build deltas if the move materialized while buying time if directional move delayed.

- **Entry: 500 contracts at $0.78** [07:32–08:38]: Phased in over 24–25 Oct at ~$35 DJT price. 500 × 15-Nov $115 calls @ $0.78 = ~$39K outlay. Delta 0.09, gamma 0.01. Left room to scale within the $155K risk envelope.

- **First exit: 400 contracts at $1.85 on 28-Oct** [08:38–09:51]: Sold 400 of 500 for $1.85 after the big gap-up rally and topping day. Realized $42.8K profit on that leg, leaving $7.8K residual risk. Rationale: took profits on the outsized move before overstaying a short-term momentum play; also managed risk ahead of personal obligations (medical appointment on 29-Oct).

- **Final exit: 98 of 100 remaining at $1.25 on 30-Oct** [10:53–12:02]: Closed 98 contracts for $1.25 after consolidation, netting $4.6K and bringing total profit to ~$47.4K. Left 2 contracts open "because I literally couldn't care less"—a speculative remainder with minimal capital at risk.

- **Risk management via phased exits** [12:02]: First leg removed all directional risk while preserving gamma upside; second leg maximized value from residual position. Demonstrates balancing systematic rules with life constraints (unavailable to monitor on 29-Oct).
