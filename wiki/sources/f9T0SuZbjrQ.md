# Agustin Lebron And The Bologna Sandwich of Trading

## Summary

Agustin Lebron, a veteran of Jane Street, uses the "bologna sandwich" analogy to explain market making and ETF/ADR arbitrage: buying components cheaply, assembling them, and selling the finished product at a markup. The core insight is that market making is a service business with value proportional to volatility, but faces no structural barriers to entry, making it intensely competitive.

## Key takeaways

- **The bologna sandwich model** [00:00–00:15]: Buy bologna, bread, and cheese for $3, assemble, sell for $4—that's a business. This directly maps to ETF market making, ADR arbitrage, and options market making: understanding mechanical relationships between securities and providing liquidity.
- **Market making as a service** [00:15–00:41]: The service of always being willing to buy and sell has real value, proportional to the volatility of the underlying security.
- **No structural moat** [00:41]: The bologna sandwich business has no real barrier to entry, making it incredibly competitive despite being profitable in principle.
