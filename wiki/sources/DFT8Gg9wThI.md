# Create a Profitable Options Approach in 4 Steps | Outlier Strategy Process

## Summary

This video outlines a four-step framework for learning options trading: (1) define profit mechanisms, (2) identify signals and scans to find them, (3) learn options mechanics and Greeks, and (4) build strategies and track performance. The core thesis is that options are vehicles to express an opinion on a profit mechanism—not inherently profitable—and that most traders fail by learning Greeks and structures before understanding what actually makes money.

## Key takeaways

- **Define profit mechanisms first** [00:56–03:11]: Options are vehicles, not profit sources. Identify what makes money (e.g., post-earnings drift, variance risk premium) before learning Greeks. Knowing intricate details about theta decay or strangle mechanics is useless if you sell a strangle into a breakout.

- **Post-earnings announcement drift (PEAD) as a profit mechanism** [02:03–03:11]: Example: TAA exhibited a breakout on earnings followed by positive drift and momentum over the next quarter. This is a repeatable phenomenon worth trading.

- **Variance risk premium as a profit mechanism** [03:11–04:19]: Implied volatility (yellow lines) consistently exceeds realized volatility (white lines). This structural mispricing is tradeable regardless of option structure chosen.

- **Build scans and signals to find conditions** [04:19–05:24]: Once you identify a profit mechanism, create tools to reliably spot it. Example: a scan that sorts stocks by variance risk premium magnitude, allowing you to see which names exhibit the largest IV–RV spread.

- **Learn options mechanics only after steps 1–2** [05:24–06:27]: Only after defining profit mechanisms and building signal systems should you study Greeks, basic structures, and option behavior. This prevents learning in a vacuum.

- **Build strategies that fit the profit mechanism** [06:27–07:27]: Use a trading plan template to quantify your approach, track performance, and assess your ability to reliably find and exploit profit mechanisms within your portfolio context.

- **Free syllabus available** [07:27–08:31]: Outlier's website offers a free options trading beginners guide with a structured syllabus, reading materials, and subtopics for each concept.
