# The Best Indicators Depend on Your Edge #optionstrading

## Summary

The "best" trading indicator is not universal—it depends entirely on your profit mechanism. Eric contrasts two fundamentally different edges: index variance risk premium (requiring IV percentile and VRP measurement) and momentum (requiring lookback-period optimization and cross-sectional ranking). The key insight is that indicator selection must align with the specific edge you're trading.

## Key takeaways

- **No universal best indicator** [00:47] — indicator utility is profit-mechanism-dependent; what works for variance risk premium fails for momentum and vice versa.
- **Variance risk premium edge signals** [00:22] — focus on [[implied-volatility-percentile]] and actual VRP measurement; these are the relevant metrics for short-volatility strategies.
- **Momentum edge signals** [00:22] — lookback-period optimization and cross-sectional ranking matter far more than IV metrics; different time-frame windows drive the edge.
- **Find the best for your edge** [00:47] — the process is to identify your [[profit-mechanism]], then reverse-engineer which indicators actually correlate with that specific edge.

## Notable quotes

> "There genuinely is no best. But there are ways to find the best trading indicators relative to specific profit mechanisms." [00:47]
