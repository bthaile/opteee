# GameStop's Ryan Cohen Ambitions Acquiring eBay

## Summary

This analysis examines the strategic logic and financial implications of a potential GameStop acquisition of eBay under Ryan Cohen's leadership. While the transaction presents genuine synergies—combining GameStop's brick-and-mortar footprint and collectibles pivot with eBay's established marketplace—the execution complexity and aggressive earnings targets (projected EPS increase from ~$4.25 to ~$779 in year one) require careful scrutiny of the underlying assumptions.

## Key takeaways

- **Synergy rationale** [00:00–00:23]: GameStop's brand recognition, physical retail presence, and collectibles focus align naturally with eBay's marketplace infrastructure; this is not a random transaction but a strategically coherent combination.
- **Earnings projection skepticism** [00:23–00:46]: Year-one diluted EPS expansion from approximately $4.25 to $779 is aspirational and requires substantial operational delivery; such targets are typical in M&A pitches but demand verification.
- **Execution complexity** [00:00–00:46]: Cohen's track record supports confidence in turnarounds, but the scale and integration challenges of combining two distinct business models (retail + marketplace) remain material risks.
