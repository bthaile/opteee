# Smooth Out Your Emotional Cycle #optionstrading

## Summary

Emotional discipline in trading requires decoupling outcome from process. Traders should maintain equanimity whether a trade profits or loses, recognizing that individual results reflect prior research and strategy quality rather than trading skill. The focus should shift from outcome obsession to systematic data collection and continuous optimization.

## Key takeaways

- **Emotional neutrality on wins and losses** [00:00] — Maintain relative indifference to both profitable and losing trades; reserve emotional response only for glaring execution mistakes.
- **Outcome ≠ strategy quality** [00:26] — Good strategies sometimes lose money; bad strategies sometimes win. Individual trade results are noise; focus on the broader process and data collection.
- **Process-first optimization** [00:49] — The sooner you numb yourself to individual outcomes and focus on systematic data collection and optimization, the better your long-term results.

## Notable quotes

> "Whether a trade makes money or doesn't is really just a result of everything that preceded it. All the research, all the homework."
