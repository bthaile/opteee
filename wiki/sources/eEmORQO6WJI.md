# Roaring Kitty Massive Options Position | GME Squeeze

## Summary

Eric analyzes Roaring Kitty's (DFV's) massive 12,000 long-call position in GME 21 June calls, purchased at ~$5.67 average, representing ~$68 million in premium outlay and notional exposure equivalent to 12 million shares. The position sits deep in-the-money with 80+ delta, but the critical constraint is capital availability for exercise; with only ~$29.5 million cash remaining, DFV cannot fully exercise without additional funding. The analysis explores three scenarios—expiration worthless (unlikely), sell-to-close (liquidity-challenged), or exercise (potentially triggering a gamma squeeze)—and examines how a gamma squeeze could cascade given GME's low float and elevated short interest.

## Key takeaways

- **Position structure** [01:51–02:21]: 5 million shares + 12,000 21 June $21 calls at $5.67 average cost; $68 million premium paid upfront; $29.5 million cash remaining; notional value ~12 million shares (would make DFV 4th largest shareholder if exercised).
- **Capital constraint** [05:32–05:52]: DFV lacks sufficient cash to exercise the full 12 million shares; this is the binding constraint on the position's resolution.
- **Deep ITM status** [06:16–06:40]: Calls trading at 80+ delta with stock down from highs; expiration worthless is extremely unlikely; position remains highly profitable.
- **Call skew and IV structure** [06:40–07:51]: Massive call skew in the 21 June series (driven by DFV's size); near-term expirations show lower IV than 21 June, possibly signaling market expectation that DFV lacks exercise capital.
- **Sell-to-close friction** [08:13–08:34]: If DFV attempts to exit, he faces 120,000 shares of open interest; limited ape-army selling pressure means he bids against himself; market makers widen spreads on exit liquidity.
- **Gamma squeeze mechanics** [08:56–09:19]: If DFV exercises (full or partial), market makers must locate 12 million shares of low-float stock; this creates cascading demand and potential gamma squeeze, distinct from short squeeze (driven by dealer hedging, not short covering).
- **Short interest context** [10:12–10:36]: Short interest at ~68 million shares; 3.3 days to cover; as shorts become scarcer, covering pressure intensifies, compounding gamma squeeze risk.
- **Direct registration impact** [09:48–10:12]: Ape community's DRS (direct registration) locks shares out of circulation, reducing available float and amplifying scarcity if exercise occurs.
- **Dilution risk** [10:36–11:07]: GameStop's potential secondary offering could dilute shares and reduce gamma squeeze severity; timing of announcement relative to DFV's position disclosure noted as curious.
- **Partial exercise scenario** [12:11–12:35]: DFV could exercise a subset of calls, still triggering gamma squeeze but at reduced magnitude; multiple resolution paths exist.
