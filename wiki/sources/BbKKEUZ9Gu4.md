# Meme Stock Watch #41 — GameStop, HIMS, SOFI options analysis & covered strangle update

## Summary

Live Saturday analysis of three meme-adjacent securities: HIMS (post-earnings volatility collapse and recovery), SOFI (trend reversal signals and long-dated call skew), and GameStop (covered strangle position update, Bitcoin balance-sheet impact, and options flow interpretation ahead of earnings). Emphasis on reading options chains through premium and open-interest splits to infer institutional positioning and directional bias.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2024-05-25)

- **HIMS volatility cycle**: 200% rally into earnings, 68% crash post-release, 175% recovery. Parabolic structure with mean reversion to prior consolidation. [26:03]
- **HIMS options skew**: Huge put premium skew (6.8M puts vs 3.6M calls on near-term expiration); far OTM puts indicate directional downside expectation but low probability. [27:29]
- **SOFI trend reversal**: Higher highs and higher lows emerging from downtrend; volume nodes at ~10 and ~16 levels critical for accumulation vs. unwinding signals. [35:49]
- **SOFI long-dated call skew**: 1.1M calls vs 102k puts in 2026 June; consistent bullish skew across all far-dated expirations signals institutional upside expectation. [49:54]
- **GameStop volume expansion**: Volume jumped from 8.76M to 21M; 16% two-day rally. Precedes significant price moves historically; now is critical observation window. [01:14:33]
- **GameStop earnings**: Scheduled for June 6 (unconfirmed); vol curve suggests possible later date given higher vol in 20 June vs. near-term. [01:22:15]
- **GameStop options flow**: 26M call premium vs 1M put premium in 20 June expiration; deep ITM synthetic puts (likely institutional), plus directional 40-strike call bets (26 delta). [01:24:16]
- **GameStop Bitcoin exposure**: ~20% of balance sheet (~$1.48B of $7.75B cash + debt) in Bitcoin; creates correlation sensitivity to BTC moves; $880M unrealized gains at $110k BTC. [01:29:09]

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Premium vs. OI split**: High OI with low premium = far OTM positions (directional, high leverage); high premium with moderate OI = ATM/ITM (higher probability, lower leverage). [27:29]
- **In-the-money vs. out-of-the-money trade-offs**: ITM options more expensive per lot but require smaller moves to profit; OTM cheaper per lot but need larger moves and suffer faster theta decay. [40:40]
- **Delta and gamma in small moves**: Delta provides base move; gamma accelerates delta as price moves. ITM options cap delta at 1.0; OTM options have room to gain delta. [42:07]
- **Rolling covered calls**: When short calls go ITM, roll out and up by collecting enough premium to offset the loss. High volatility (like GameStop) provides abundant roll choices. [01:06:16]
- **Volume profile and resistance**: Volume nodes show where transactions occurred; light volume at resistance levels = fewer bag-holders incentivized to sell; heavy volume = potential supply. [56:34]
- **Institutional positioning via options**: Deep ITM puts = synthetic long (capital-efficient stock replacement); far OTM calls = speculative directional bet; short calls = covered call or risk reversal. [01:25:59]
- **Volatility surface interpretation**: High tails on OTM puts = higher probability of larger downside move but lower probability it happens; muted upside = higher probability of smaller move. [31:21]
