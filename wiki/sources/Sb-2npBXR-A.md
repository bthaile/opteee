# Analyzing Roaring Kitty's Trades Pt7 | GameStop Analysis Live Ep15

## Summary

Eric continues his deep forensic analysis of Roaring Kitty's historical options trades, focusing on a critical shift in trading approach observed in April–May 2024. The session examines a series of 17-May expiration call purchases (10 and 20 strikes) initiated on 24 April with small test entries that scaled dramatically over three days, followed by partial exits on 6 May and full unwinding on 15–16 May. The analysis suggests a departure from long-dated value plays toward shorter-dated, market-impact-aware position testing, with evidence of tactical entry/exit sequencing rather than predetermined date-based triggers.

## Key takeaways

- **Test-and-scale pattern on 24–26 April** [07:45–54:28]: Roaring Kitty entered 1,000 contracts on 24 April at ~$124.29, scaled to 4,000 more on 25 April, then 6,000 on 26 April—all in-the-money 10 and 20 strike calls. This mirrors a hypothesis-testing approach: small entry, observe market reaction, then commit if conditions align.

- **Dramatic shift from long-dated to 30–32 DTE entries** [39:06–43:20]: Historical trades averaged 180 DTE; these April entries were 32 DTE when initiated. Only two prior sub-50 DTE trades exist in the record (one earnings play, one 3 DTE). This represents a material departure from the value-investor playbook.

- **Partial exit on 6 May, full unwind 15–16 May** [01:09:18–01:12:12]: 5,000 contracts sold on 6 May (when stock moved ~$1 from prior close), then 1,000-lot exits on 15–16 May. Exit timing was not optimal relative to the 14–15 May peak, suggesting reactive rather than pre-planned date-based execution.

- **Market-impact awareness hypothesis** [55:43–58:04]: The scaling pattern and subsequent exits suggest Roaring Kitty is now aware of his own price-moving capacity and is testing market response to position size, liquidity absorption, and stock reaction—not simply playing a predetermined catalyst.

- **Volume and dealer hedging correlation** [56:13–57:07]: The 1,000-lot entry on 24 April coincided with volume spike from 2.5M to 4.8M shares. Likely dealer hedging of the call purchases triggered secondary buying activity, which other market participants detected as a signal.

- **No evidence of specific date targeting** [02:01:20–02:02:07]: Despite community theories about 7 November or other "hype dates," the exit pattern (spread over 15–16 May rather than concentrated on a single date) contradicts the idea of a pre-planned catalyst. Eric interprets this as reactive profit-taking and market-condition assessment.

- **Comparison to historical long-dated plays** [01:37:05–01:38:28]: Earlier trades (Jan 2020: 158 DTE, 198 DTE; July 2020: 137 DTE) show consistent long-duration value positioning. The April 2024 shift to 30 DTE represents a fundamental change in time-frame strategy, possibly reflecting confidence in near-term volatility or market-moving capacity.

- **Aggregate P&L on April–May trades** [01:25:04–01:26:29]: Rough calculation suggests ~$1M+ realized gain on the test-and-scale sequence, though exact attribution across expirations is difficult due to overlapping lot sizes and partial exits.

- **Dealer hedging and market-maker behavior** [01:43:22–01:44:18]: Market makers are now more calculated with risk on meme stocks. Unhedged call exposure is unlikely; hedging is probable. This shifts the focus away from "gamma squeeze" mechanics toward broader institutional positioning and order-flow detection.

- **No evidence of share accumulation preceding options** [01:54:54–01:57:11]: Dark pool data shows minimal share buying in April before the 24 April options entry. The May 20 options entry (120k of 21 June 20 calls) also shows no massive share pre-positioning, suggesting options are the primary vehicle, not a secondary wave-riding tactic.
