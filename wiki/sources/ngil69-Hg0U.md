# GME Options Update: IS Roaring Kitty Buying Calls?

## Summary

Eric analyzes GameStop's options activity for late June 2024, examining whether Roaring Kitty is accumulating call positions. He finds no clear evidence of large, coordinated call buying matching Roaring Kitty's historical trading fingerprint. The bulk of call volume concentrates in near-term expirations (28 June) and far out-of-the-money strikes, which Eric characterizes as speculative lottery-like positions rather than informed institutional accumulation.

## Key takeaways

- **Volatility spike then reversal [01:57–02:45]**: IV jumped ~50 points on 25 June, then collapsed the next day—unusual moves that typically signal news or positioning shifts. Near-term vol moved more than midterm, consistent with short-term event expectations.

- **Call volume concentration [03:07–03:30]**: Three consecutive days of 220k+ call volume across all strikes/expirations; 130k of today's 226k volume is in the 28 June expiration (3 days out). Puts remain static in the 50k range.

- **Standard expirations dominate [03:57–04:44]**: Liquidity naturally clusters at round-number strikes (20s, 25s) and standard expirations (not weeklys). Open interest at 20-strike is 8,971 vs. 775 at 21-strike—human nature and market-maker behavior create observable support/resistance.

- **Far OTM calls are lottery tickets [06:45–07:13]**: 1,306-lot at 128-strike (28 June) = ~$2,600 notional; 1,000-lot at same expiration 10 minutes apart. These are the largest observed positions but still small in absolute terms.

- **128-strike calls will expire worthless [08:44–09:25]**: Already decayed to 1.5¢ mid-price with zero gamma and 2¢ daily theta decay. Unless GME rallies dramatically, these positions lose money daily. Most retail buyers appear to be chasing Roaring Kitty theory rather than executing informed trades.

- **19 July 30-strike shows more structure [14:06–14:34]**: 21,600 OI vs. 6,400 volume today; 36-delta calls will capture gamma if GME moves up. This is a "decent trade" but still not hyperactive accumulation. Largest single trade was 772 contracts.

- **No Roaring Kitty fingerprint detected [00:25–01:08]**: Historical Roaring Kitty positions showed consistent strike selection, size, and frequency patterns. Current activity lacks that signature—mostly small, scattered transactions at extreme strikes rather than coordinated accumulation.

- **Theta decay accelerates into expiration [12:37–12:58]**: 30-strike (28 June) worth 17¢ today, losing 14¢ daily. Traders closing positions to salvage remaining extrinsic value before expiration.
