# A More Flexible Wheel — The Covered Strangle Strategy

## Summary

The covered strangle is a flexible variant of the wheel strategy that begins with a combination of cash-secured puts and long shares rather than pure short puts. This dual-leg approach allows traders to dynamically adjust their equity exposure based on market conditions—favoring puts during sideways phases and scaling into shares during rallies—before transitioning to a ratio covered call in the terminal state.

## Key takeaways

- **Leg 1: Flexible entry with puts + shares combo** [00:00–00:30]
  - Start with cash-secured puts, long shares, or a mix (not pure short puts like the wheel)
  - Adjust the ratio dynamically: favor more puts in choppy/sideways markets; scale down puts and buy more shares during strong rallies
  - This flexibility is the core advantage over the standard wheel

- **Leg 2: Ratio covered call** [00:53]
  - Terminal state mirrors the wheel's covered call phase
  - Transition to a ratio covered call structure once assigned or at target price

- **Decision framework** [00:00–00:30]
  - Assess market regime (trending vs. ranging) to decide put/share weighting
  - Maintain combo position until assignment or profit target
  - Capital efficiency improves by choosing equity over short puts when conviction is high
