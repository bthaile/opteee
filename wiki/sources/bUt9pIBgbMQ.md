# Core of a $25K Portfolio #options #trading

## Summary

This video demonstrates a portfolio construction framework for a $25,000 account using a covered strangle on leveraged QQQ (TQQQ) as the core holding. The strategy allocates approximately 63% of capital to the core position (combining long shares and short puts) while reserving the remainder for speculative trades, with a 20/5 split between core and speculative allocation.

## Key takeaways

- **Core allocation strategy** [00:27]: Divide a $25K portfolio into core ($20K) and speculative ($5K) buckets to balance capital efficiency with risk management.
- **Covered strangle on TQQQ** [00:00]: Use a leveraged ETF as the core holding because it provides built-in diversification while allowing concentrated options strategies.
- **Capital utilization** [00:27]: A covered strangle combining long shares and short puts uses approximately 63% of portfolio capital, leaving dry powder for opportunistic trades.
- **Timing and execution** [00:00]: Acknowledge that timing, entry mechanics, and position management are critical components alongside the structural allocation.
