# The Ultimate Guide to Scanning for Profitable Options Trades | Outlier Scanning Process

## Summary

A systematic three-step framework for scanning and structuring options trades: portfolio review (utilization, risk assessment, portfolio fit), scanning methodology (portfolio-driven vs. opportunity-driven), and trade structuring. The process integrates market disposition, sector analysis, technical inputs, and position management to ensure trades align with portfolio needs and risk tolerance before execution.

## Key takeaways

- **Portfolio review first** [00:39–01:46]: Before scanning, assess utilization (capital deployed), risk assessment (beta-weighted deltas, expiration diversification), and portfolio fit. Adjust selectivity based on annual performance pace: ahead of target → very selective; on pace → moderate selectivity; behind pace → slightly relaxed limits but never accept poor trades.

- **Two scanning modes** [03:50–04:57]: Portfolio-driven scanning starts with a defined need (e.g., "need short deltas, 2+ weeks duration"); opportunity-driven scanning searches for attractive setups first, then validates fit. Both follow the same blueprint but differ in entry point.

- **Sector-level filtering** [08:29–09:57]: Use multi-timeframe sector analysis (6-month, 3-month, 1-month) to identify weak sectors, then drill into sub-sectors and individual laggards. Stack factors in your favor: if bearish, find sectors already rolling over; if bullish, find top performers.

- **Technical overlay** [10:23–11:54]: Apply technical filters (moving averages, price action) to create a short list of candidates. For short-term trades (2–90 days), focus on shorter-term moving averages; for longer theses, layer in fundamental analysis.

- **Opportunity-driven example: VIX trade** [13:21–14:04]: When VIX dipped below 13, this was an opportunity-driven trigger. Validate portfolio fit, then apply in-depth analysis and structure accordingly. Same blueprint, different entry point.

- **Trade structuring informs scanning** [05:23–06:27]: Market disposition, product preference, exposure duration, and management frequency all feed back into which scan parameters and strategies make sense. It's a symbiotic relationship.
