# How a Market Wizard KEEPS His Returns with Tom Basso | The Outlier Podcast Ep57

## Summary

Tom Basso, a legendary trend-following trader and author of *Successful Traders*, reflects on 2023's anomalous market strength—particularly the stock market's resilience despite economic headwinds—and shares his systematic approach to preserving capital through hedging, position sizing, and multi-market diversification. He emphasizes that the key to long-term trading success is not prediction but preparation: maintaining balanced portfolios, using trend-following indicators to exit positions before major drawdowns, and deploying hedges as "fire insurance" rather than profit engines.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2023-12-30)

- **Stock market strength despite economic weakness** [01:15–02:25]: Basso was surprised the S&P 500 reached near all-time highs despite his expectation of a hard landing. His overbought/oversold indicators failed to peak despite consecutive daily highs, suggesting strength without conviction.
- **Commodity volatility as a profit driver** [02:50–03:36]: Orange juice, coffee, and soft commodities experienced violent moves; Basso's all-weather strategy (26 futures markets) captured both upside and reversal trades.
- **Early-year whipsaw and recovery** [05:15–06:15]: The market's failed rally in early February (from open to highs, back to open by March) stopped out ~50% of his positions; hedges engaged briefly before being whipsawed out as the market resumed its uptrend through year-end.
- **Tech sector concentration and risk** [07:57–11:06]: Basso draws parallels to the 1999–2000 tech bubble, noting that while mega-cap tech (Amazon, Meta, Google) survived and thrived, current valuations lack earnings support. AI hype is unproven; competition, EU fines, and advertising headwinds pose risks if the economy softens.
- **Energy and geopolitical tail risk** [13:23–15:42]: Crude oil sideways; natural gas volatile. Ukraine war, if it continues into a harsh winter, could disrupt energy supplies given the short lead time from extraction to consumption.
- **Nuclear energy and policy** [16:07–20:48]: Basso advocates for nuclear as the most cost-effective, environmentally friendly baseload power, but regulatory burden and anti-nuclear sentiment block new capacity. Solar and wind are unreliable; fusion energy (recently achieving net-positive results) could be transformative but remains years away.
- **Bonds no longer a reliable hedge** [46:03–49:14]: The 40-year bull market in bonds (1980–2020) ended when rates hit zero. Rising rates in 2022–2023 caused simultaneous drawdowns in stocks and bonds, breaking the 60/40 diversification myth. Stocks and commodities are now the only liquid markets for large capital flows.

### Evergreen mechanics

- **Trend-following as the core edge** [03:36–04:52]: "If you have a trend, you can make money; if you don't have a trend, you can't." Basso's primary driver of returns is identifying and riding trends in stocks, commodities, and futures; early-year stops and reversals are part of the process.
- **Hedging as risk reduction, not profit** [39:21–42:16]: A well-deployed hedge is a drag on returns by design. It should balance a portfolio to near-zero market exposure, not speculate. Over-hedging (e.g., $200k short on a $100k portfolio) is speculation masquerading as hedging. Hedges are "fire insurance"—you hope never to collect.
- **Position sizing and rebalancing** [35:37–37:20]: After a strong year, winners often grow to outsized portfolio weights. Rebalancing—trimming winners and reallocating to maintain balance—smooths drawdowns and prevents concentration risk. This is why Basso wrote *Successful Traders Size Their Positions*.
- **Trend-following indicators and time frames** [33:19–34:06]: Basso uses 21-day (one-month) and 50-day parameters for trend identification. He avoids frequent trading (long/short three times per month) and prefers monthly-scale decisions. Indicators can be moving averages, Keltner channels, Bollinger bands, or point-and-figure charts.
- **Hedging small accounts** [43:54–44:56]: For accounts under $10k, hedging is premature. Instead, use tight stops on every position based on trend indicators, keep positions small, and focus on adding capital. Diversification and cost management are harder at small scale.
- **All-weather diversification** [12:34–12:59]: When stocks are hedged or in drawdown, look for opportunities in bonds, gold, silver, platinum, aluminum, softs (coffee, cocoa), and energy. This multi-market approach allows profit generation even when equities stall.
- **Preparation over prediction** [06:15–07:06]: Basso does not predict market direction. Instead, he maintains tight stops, keeps hedges ready, and reacts quickly to signals. Volatility and tail risk (tax selling, geopolitical shocks, wars) are inevitable; the goal is to be positioned to react, not to forecast.
- **Drawdown management** [57:57–58:20]: The goal is not to eliminate drawdowns (impossible) but to reduce their magnitude and duration. Basso has improved this over 30+ years through systematic risk management, allowing him to approach uncertain years with confidence.
- **Election-year catalysts** [59:53–01:03:34]: Election years introduce political catalysts that can trigger trend reversals. Media amplifies sentiment (bullish or bearish), perpetuating trends. The Fed's ability to fine-tune the economy is limited; Basso worries about rates staying too high too long, risking a medium-to-hard landing.
