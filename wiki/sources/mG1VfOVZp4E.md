# 2022 Market Crash with David Hunter

## Summary

David Hunter presents a macro forecast combining a near-term "melt-up" to S&P 6000 (raised from 5300) and Nasdaq 20,000 by mid-to-late 2022, followed by an 80% bear market and deflationary bust beginning in Q4 2022 and extending through 2023. The thesis rests on 40 years of declining rates ending, massive leverage (300 trillion in global debt, quadrillions in derivatives notional), and systemic fragility exacerbated by pandemic-era monetary expansion. Ukraine/Russia geopolitical risk is treated as a near-term catalyst already priced in; the real driver is the reversal of the rate-driven bull market since 1982.

## Key takeaways

### Dated market read (2022-03-31)

- **Melt-up target**: S&P 6000, Nasdaq 20,000, Dow 45,000 [12:11]. Likely to occur over 3–6 months (summer 2022 or beyond) [43:49].
- **Subsequent bust**: 80% bear market peak-to-trough, deflationary phase, 10-year yields to 0%, then to 1.5% by decade-end [38:26, 39:09].
- **Near-term pullback risk**: 5–10% correction possible in next 2–3 weeks before melt-up resumes [35:51, 43:25].
- **Ukraine/Russia impact**: Market has already discounted the invasion; rally began the day Russia invaded ("sell the rumor, buy the news") [00:54]. Resolution would be bullish; NATO escalation would be bearish [02:31, 03:22].
- **Sanctions risk**: Unintended consequence of SWIFT exclusion and sanctions is accelerating de-dollarization; Saudi Arabia discussing yuan-denominated oil contracts [07:15]. Hunter estimates 5+ years before material impact on USD reserve status [08:04].

### Evergreen mechanics

- **40-year bull market thesis**: Driven by 40-year decline in rates (from ~20% T-bills in early 1980s to near 0% in 2021). Melt-up is the final parabolic leg; rates bottoming marks the inflection [16:04, 16:31, 18:07].
- **Leverage equation**: Massive leverage + fragility = bust. Global debt rose 50 trillion in 2 years (250T → 300T); derivatives notional ~1 quadrillion [25:19, 25:46, 26:11].
- **Fragility concept**: Pandemic-era monetary expansion and leverage have made the economy less resilient to rate hikes. Even modest tightening can trigger rapid unwinding [23:34, 24:01].
- **Fed balance sheet**: Grew from 875B (Oct 2008) to 9T (March 2022). Hunter expects 30T by 2024 if major bank failures occur [22:39, 29:45].
- **Monetarism vs. rates**: Hunter emphasizes money supply (balance sheet) as the key lever, not interest rates alone. Fed officials discussing balance-sheet reduction is the real tightening risk [20:50, 21:33].
- **Sector rotation**: Melt-up will be broad-based (growth, value, large-cap, small-cap, cyclicals, semis, commodities). Bust will be broad too, but tech/FANG will underperform in the next cycle [34:21, 35:24, 36:37].
- **Post-bust cycle**: Inflation cycle favoring value, energy, metals, agriculture; tech/growth will lag. Oil forecast to 400/bbl by decade-end; gold to 10K–20K; silver to 400 [39:58, 40:25].
- **Yield curve**: Already inverted; 10-year peaked near 2.5%, will fall to 0% in bust, then rise to 1.5% by decade-end [38:26, 39:09].
- **Sentiment as contrarian signal**: At 6000 S&P, sentiment will flip to euphoria; that will mark the top [44:28].

## Regime / context

**Date context**: March 31, 2022. Russia invaded Ukraine ~4 weeks prior (late Feb 2022). Fed had begun signaling rate hikes; 10-year yields near 2.5%. S&P 500 had rallied ~10% off early-March lows. Inflation (CPI) running 6–7% YoY.

**Forecast timeline**: Hunter's melt-up target (6000 S&P) expected by mid-to-late 2022 (3–6 months from interview date). Bust to begin Q4 2022, extend through 2023. This is a multi-year macro call, not a short-term trade.

**Caveats**: All price targets and timelines are approximate; ASR transcription of live speech introduces numeric uncertainty. Hunter's 80% bear-market forecast is contrarian and depends on cascading bank failures and policy response. Geopolitical escalation (NATO involvement) could accelerate timeline.
