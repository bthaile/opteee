# Greek Attribution

Greek attribution is the practice of decomposing options P&L into contributions from individual Greeks—delta, gamma, theta, and vega—to understand which market factors (directional move, volatility change, time decay, or acceleration) drove actual trading outcomes. Rather than viewing an options position as a single bet, Greek attribution isolates how each Greek's exposure interacted with market conditions to produce the final result, enabling traders to diagnose whether their thesis was correct but their structure was wrong, or vice versa. This framework is essential for learning from trades because it reveals whether losses came from being wrong on direction, overpaying for volatility, poor strike selection, or time decay working against the position—distinctions that raw P&L alone cannot show. By aligning position construction (strike, DTE, structure) with the Greeks that match the actual thesis, traders can avoid the common mistake of choosing strikes based on price targets rather than the risk and leverage profile needed to profit from their directional or volatility view.
