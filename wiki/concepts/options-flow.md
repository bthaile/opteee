# Options Flow

Options flow refers to the volume and positioning of options contracts being traded, used by traders to gauge market sentiment and identify potential price moves or reversals. Analyzing flow involves examining call versus put volume, open interest concentration across expiration cycles, and skew patterns to infer whether institutional or retail traders are positioning bullishly or bearishly. Flow analysis becomes more challenging in large-cap, well-developed names with diverse trader motivations, requiring traders to supplement flow signals with volatility surfaces, term structures, and volume-driven price action rather than relying on timing theories alone. Successful flow-based trading depends on expected value mechanics—combining win rate with risk-reward ratios—rather than win rate in isolation.
