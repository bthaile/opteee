# Capital Allocation

Capital allocation in options trading refers to how traders distribute their trading capital across different strategies, positions, and risk levels to optimize returns while managing drawdowns. The channel discusses allocation both at the individual trader level—where early-stage traders should prioritize aggressive capital accumulation before transitioning to skill-based compounding, and where core positions (such as covered strangles) may generate modest returns while speculative positions drive outsized gains—and at the macro level, where structural constraints on capital deployment at scale create concentration risks and opportunities in markets. Effective capital allocation requires treating trading as a business with defined runway and risk management, and involves strategic decisions about position sizing across trend-following, volatility, and speculative strategies based on market regime and trader skill maturity.
