# High Implied Volatility

High implied volatility represents elevated option prices that attract premium sellers seeking to capture that excess premium, but it often signals underlying risk or market stress that can make selling at extremes dangerous. The practical approach is to measure IV percentile relative to a security's historical range, avoiding both undercompensated extremes (sub-10 percentile) and potentially mispriced highs (above-90 percentile). According to the channel's philosophy, the only reliable edge for premium sellers is selling in very high implied volatility environments where compensation justifies the risk taken.
