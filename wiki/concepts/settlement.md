# Settlement

Settlement refers to the process and timeline by which options and other derivative contracts are finalized and obligations are fulfilled, governed by regulatory rules such as Regulation SHO which establishes different settlement cycles (T+1, T+3, T+6, C+35). In options trading, settlement mechanics are critical to understanding market structure, as delays or failures in settlement can create opportunities for price manipulation through mechanisms like naked shorting, swap abuse, and market maker hedging behavior. Traders use settlement knowledge to analyze how market infrastructure failures and regulatory exemptions may drive observable price movements and to understand the mechanics of gamma squeezes and forced buying that occur when options expire and positions must be settled.
