# Directional Trading

Directional trading in options involves taking a position based on a predicted move in the underlying asset's price, but success depends critically on how the Greeks—particularly delta, theta, and vega—interact with strike selection, time-to-expiration, and implied volatility rather than on being correct about direction alone. A trader can be right about direction yet lose money if they choose strikes poorly, pick the wrong expiration, or fail to account for how volatility changes will affect the position's value. Directional options traders should size positions by defining their thesis breakpoint, calculating cumulative loss at that level, and limiting risk to 1–3% of portfolio capital, while selecting from structures like long calls, synthetic shorts, or ratio diagonals based on their volatility outlook and risk tolerance.
