# Vertical Spreads

Vertical spreads are directional options strategies that combine a long and short option of the same type at different strike prices, offering defined risk and reduced capital requirements compared to naked options. However, they are fundamentally directional bets rather than volatility plays and carry inherent negative expected value due to their structure and transaction costs, making them suitable only when a trader has a strong directional thesis. The strategy's affordability should not drive selection; instead, traders should first identify their profit mechanism and only then choose vertical spreads if they optimally attack that mechanism. Retail traders are often advised to trade spreads as wide as possible or avoid them entirely in favor of single-leg options, since narrow spreads eliminate most optionality while incurring double commissions.
