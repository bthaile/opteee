# Sharpe Ratio

The Sharpe ratio is a key performance metric used to evaluate whether a trading strategy's returns adequately compensate for the risk taken, calculated as return divided by volatility or standard deviation. Retail traders should target a Sharpe ratio in the range of 1.5–2.5 as a realistic expectation when building systematic strategies, and this metric should be tracked alongside other statistical measures like Sortino ratio, maximum drawdown, and expected return to honestly assess edge and strategy viability. The Sharpe ratio helps traders distinguish between strategies that generate profits through genuine edge versus those that succeed through luck or favorable market conditions, making it essential for the iterative process of strategy evaluation and refinement.
