# Compounding

Compounding in options trading refers to the reinvestment of profits and disciplined capital management to grow an account exponentially over time, enabled by consistent contributions, positive-expectancy strategies, and proper position sizing. The concept is foundational to long-term wealth accumulation: traders compound returns by maintaining strict risk discipline, executing a documented process reliably, and allowing skill-based edge to work across many trades rather than seeking outsized returns on individual positions. Proper position sizing—informed by frameworks like Kelly criterion—is essential to compounding because it preserves capital during drawdowns while allowing profitable strategies to work repeatedly, whereas oversizing ruins accounts regardless of edge.
