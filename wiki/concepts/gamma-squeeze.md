# Gamma Squeeze

A gamma squeeze occurs when rapid price acceleration forces market makers to hedge their short call positions by buying shares, creating additional buying pressure that further accelerates the price move—a self-reinforcing cycle distinct from a short squeeze driven by short covering. The mechanism is rooted in options market structure: as a stock rises, the delta of out-of-the-money calls increases, requiring market makers to purchase more shares to remain hedged, which can cascade into explosive price moves in stocks with low float and elevated short interest. Gamma squeezes are analyzed through implied-volatility skew patterns, call-to-put ratios, and options chain positioning to identify when market-maker hedging dynamics could amplify price moves beyond fundamental drivers.
