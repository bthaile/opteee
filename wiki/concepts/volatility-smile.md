# Volatility Smile

A volatility smile is a pattern in an options chain where implied volatility is higher at both out-of-the-money and in-the-money strikes compared to at-the-money strikes, creating a U-shaped curve when plotted across strike prices. The smile reflects market pricing of tail-risk hedging and supply-demand dynamics, with far out-of-the-money options commanding outsized premiums relative to their intrinsic value—a "puncher's chance" pricing model that compensates market makers for hedging extreme price moves. Volatility smiles can shift between expirations and over time; for example, a put-skewed smile may normalize or shift to call skew as market sentiment and expiration dates change, revealing mixed directional sentiment and the level of retail participation in near-term contracts.
