# Call Skew

Call skew refers to an asymmetry in implied volatility across the options chain where out-of-the-money calls trade at elevated premiums relative to puts, indicating market participants are systematically paying more for upside exposure than downside protection. This pattern emerges when traders and institutions expect or position for directional bullish moves, contrasting with the more common put skew where downside protection commands a premium due to faster, more violent downside price action. In practice, traders monitor call skew to identify periods of institutional or retail bullish positioning, assess whether premium collection strategies remain attractive, and gauge whether the market is pricing in directional upside risk rather than downside hedging demand.
