# Vertical Spread

A vertical spread is an options strategy that combines long and short positions in the same underlying to neutralize Greek exposure and define maximum risk, with the spread's width determining the magnitude of residual delta and the flatness of gamma, vega, and theta curves across price levels. The strategy's exit and profitability depend entirely on the underlying profit mechanism and strategy design rather than the structure itself, and traders must validate that the position has positive expected value before entry rather than relying on risk definition alone to ensure safety. Vertical spreads are a foundational tool in a well-rounded options toolkit, but their effectiveness requires proper position sizing based on actual strategy data and a clear understanding of when and why the trade should be exited.
