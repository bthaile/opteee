# Short Call

A short call is an options position where a trader sells a call contract, obligating them to deliver shares at a predetermined strike price if the buyer exercises. In covered-call strategies, short calls are sold against owned stock to generate premium income, though this caps upside potential and typically underperforms buy-and-hold approaches; effective execution requires selective strike selection (often 20–35 delta), delta rebalancing as the underlying moves, and awareness of opportunity costs relative to expected appreciation. Short calls can be rolled or allowed to expire depending on profit opportunity and capital redeployment goals, but they function poorly as hedges due to the minimal downside protection their premium provides.
