# Delta Decay

Delta decay, also called charm, is a second-order Greek that measures how an option's delta changes with the passage of time rather than underlying price movement. As expiration approaches, out-of-the-money options lose delta rapidly and non-linearly, with the decay accelerating sharply in the final weeks, while in-the-money options gain delta as they become more likely to stay in the money. This time-driven delta erosion is critical for position management because even favorable price moves can be offset by reduced delta near expiration, making strike selection and time-to-expiration essential to matching your Greeks profile to your directional thesis.
