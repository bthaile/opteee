# Volatility Trading

Volatility trading in options involves profiting from changes in implied volatility rather than directional price movements, using strategies such as short straddles, short strangles, and ratio spreads that benefit when volatility contracts or expands. Traders access volatility as a distinct asset class by selling options when implied volatility is elevated and buying when it is depressed, allowing them to isolate volatility exposure from directional risk. The approach requires understanding how vega (sensitivity to volatility changes) and theta (time decay) interact across different market regimes, and successful volatility traders adapt their positioning based on market conditions—such as selling volatility into earnings season spikes or adjusting hedges when institutional positioning signals upcoming moves.
