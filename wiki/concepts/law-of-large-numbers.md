# Law Of Large Numbers

The law of large numbers in options trading refers to the principle that consistent profitability emerges from executing a defined, repeatable trading process across many independent decisions and positions over an extended timeframe, rather than from short-term luck or individual trade outcomes. Retail traders succeed by embracing this concept—accepting that early results are noise, focusing on process discipline and proper position sizing across diversified, uncorrelated trades, and building sustainable habits that compound over a career. The framework requires realistic expectations about the timeline to competence, strict risk management to cut losses, and the understanding that wealth accumulation in trading is a function of decision volume and longevity, not of waiting for certainty or chasing quick returns.
