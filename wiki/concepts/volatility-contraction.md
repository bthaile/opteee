# Volatility Contraction

Volatility contraction occurs when implied volatility decreases, typically after a significant market event or earnings announcement resolves uncertainty. Traders exploit volatility contraction by buying options premium before events expected to see IV decline, capturing gains as the market reprices risk downward following the catalyst; conversely, short premium positions benefit when volatility remains elevated or expands rather than contracts as anticipated. The concept is particularly relevant in earnings trades and event-driven strategies, where the timing and magnitude of volatility collapse directly impact option profitability independent of directional price movement.
