# Number Of Decisions

Number of Decisions refers to the principle that trading success is fundamentally driven by the volume of decisions made over time rather than the quality of any individual trade outcome. This concept, central to Tom Sosnoff's trading philosophy, posits that wealth accumulation in options trading results from maximizing the number of reasonable risk-taking opportunities and maintaining longevity in the markets, since success emerges as a statistical byproduct of decision volume rather than waiting for perfect setups. The approach emphasizes that traders should focus on executing a systematic process repeatedly—tracking trades, maintaining discipline, and building a robust toolkit—rather than obsessing over individual wins or losses, as consistent profitability compounds through many decisions made with proper risk management and emotional detachment.
