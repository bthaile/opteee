# Trade Management

Trade management is the systematic approach to monitoring, adjusting, and closing positions after entry, and its effectiveness depends entirely on aligning decisions with the specific profit mechanism of the trade rather than following generic rules. Proper trade management requires establishing a deliberate plan before deploying capital—including position sizing, profit targets, loss thresholds, and scaling rules—rather than making reactive decisions under pressure after entry. The foundation of sound trade management is first identifying the profit mechanism that matches your market view and volatility assessment, then selecting the appropriate structure and management rules that fit those conditions, rather than learning a structure first and improvising management afterward.
