# Soft Landing

A soft landing refers to an economic scenario in which the Federal Reserve successfully slows inflation and moderates growth without triggering a recession, typically supported by controlled rate cuts and stable employment. In options trading, market participants monitor soft-landing probability through economic data releases (PMI, jobless claims, consumer confidence, GDP) and Fed communications to gauge the likelihood of continued equity gains versus deflationary busts. The concept is central to sector rotation decisions, with soft-landing expectations favoring tech and growth stocks, while deteriorating soft-landing odds prompt shifts toward defensive sectors like utilities and financials. Traders use soft-landing narratives to assess whether rate-cut cycles will support equity valuations or precede credit contractions and drawdowns.
