# Allocation

Allocation in options trading refers to the framework for deploying capital across positions and managing how much of available funds are committed to active trades versus held in reserve. Traders use allocation tracking to monitor utilization rates, which becomes especially important during market declines when maintaining cash reserves enables scaling into weakness and adjusting losing positions. The allocation approach supports disciplined position-sizing and risk management, allowing traders to navigate drawdowns methodically while preserving flexibility for future entries and adjustments rather than being forced into suboptimal decisions due to insufficient capital.
