# Discretionary Trading

Discretionary trading refers to making trade decisions based on real-time market analysis and judgment rather than following a predetermined mechanical system, though the channel emphasizes that successful discretionary trading requires completing all analytical work before entry and maintaining discipline around thesis-based exits rather than arbitrary stops. The material suggests discretionary trading amplifies existing trader skill and edge but does not create edge on its own, and that most retail discretionary traders fail because they lack systematic planning, proper position sizing, and awareness of trading friction costs. The channel contrasts discretionary approaches with mechanical, bot-driven strategies that maintain portfolio neutrality and remove emotional decision-making, positioning discretionary trading as viable only when paired with rigorous pre-trade analysis and strict risk management rather than reactive entry-and-exit mechanics.
